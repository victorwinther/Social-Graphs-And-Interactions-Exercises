'''Joanna Cotten''' (born in [[Memphis, Tennessee]]<ref name="whitburn">{{cite book|last=Whitburn|first=Joel|title=Hot Country Songs 1944 to 2008|publisher=Record Research, Inc|page=105|year=2008|isbn=978-0-89820-177-2}}</ref>) is an American [[country music]] singer. Cotten's debut single, "The Prize," peaked at number 60 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs]] chart.<ref>{{cite news|last=Morris|first=Edward|title=Luke Bryan, Eagles Bound Onto the Charts|url=http://www.cmt.com/news/country-music/1567860/luke-bryan-eagles-bound-onto-the-charts.jhtml|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080511021711/http://www.cmt.com/news/country-music/1567860/luke-bryan-eagles-bound-onto-the-charts.jhtml|url-status=dead|archive-date=May 11, 2008|accessdate=September 2, 2011|newspaper=[[Country Music Television]]|date=August 25, 2007}}</ref> Cotten recorded 21 songs for an unreleased album for [[Warner Bros. Records]]. She split from the label in December 2007 after the release of a digital-only [[extended play]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Joanna Cotten splits with Warner|url=http://www.countrystandardtime.com/news/newsitem.asp?xid=1241|accessdate=September 2, 2011|newspaper=Country Standard Time|date=December 20, 2007}}</ref> Cotten has continued to record and tour.<ref>{{cite news|last=Hight|first=Jewly|title=Joanna Cotten has no label, but she's got verve|url=http://www.nashvillescene.com/nashville/joanna-cotten-has-no-label-but-shes-got-verve/Content?oid=1200253|accessdate=September 2, 2011|newspaper=[[Nashville Scene]]|date=March 12, 2009}}</ref> From 2013 to 2022, she was a member of [[Eric Church]]'s band, until she quit in July 2022.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.savingcountrymusic.com/end-of-an-era-joanna-cotten-leaves-eric-churchs-band/ | title=End of an Era: Joanna Cotten Leaves Eric Church's Band | date=August 2022 }}</ref>
