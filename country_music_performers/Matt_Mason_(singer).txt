'''Matt Mason''' (born {{circa|1985}}) is an American [[country music]] [[singer/songwriter]] from [[Fairland, Indiana]]. He was the winner of ''CMT's Next Superstar'' in 2011. Prior to that, he competed on ''[[Nashville Star]]''.

== Early life and career ==
Mason was born in [[Fairland, Indiana]] and attended [[Triton Central High School]] in [[Shelby County, Indiana]].<ref name=indy>{{cite news|title=Hoosier musician Matt Mason lands label, U.S. tour|url=http://www.indy.com/posts/hoosier-musician-matt-mason-lands-label-u-s-tour|newspaper=Indy Star|date=June 17, 2011}}</ref> He grew up listening to traditional country music, and started playing guitar at age eleven, and from that time onward wanted to sing professionally. At age 16, he opened for [[Charlie Daniels]] in western Massachusetts. Six months after graduating from high school in 2004, he moved to [[Nashville, Tennessee]] to pursue his career, and found work as a session musician. Within three weeks, he was performing in live shows.<ref name=JournalGazette>{{cite news|author=Penhollow, Steve|date=February 26, 2006|title=Rants & Raves: Indiana supplies fifth of new 'Nashville Star'|newspaper=[[The Journal Gazette]]|page=E1}}</ref>

== Reality Television ==
In 2006, at age 20, he appeared on ''[[Nashville Star]]'',<ref name=JournalGazette /> finishing in fourth place.<ref>{{cite news|author=Wooley, John|date=August 4, 2006|title=Star power: 'Nashville Star' champ is hanging on for the ride|newspaper=[[Tulsa World]]|page=S12}}</ref> Afterwards, his productivity was affected by a struggle with alcohol and drug addiction, from which he recovered.<ref name=conaway>{{cite news|last=Conaway|first=Allana|title=Matt Mason Is Ready to Be Country Music's 'Next Superstar'|url=http://tasteofcountry.com/matt-mason-cmt-next-superstar-interview/|newspaper=Taste of Country|date=May 2, 2011|accessdate=October 2, 2014}}</ref> He subsequently performed live as often as four times a week in venues on [[Lower Broadway]].<ref name=conaway /> In 2011, he won ''CMT's Next Superstar''.<ref name=harr>{{cite news|last=Harr|first=Dan|title=Matt Mason From CMT's Next Superstar Performs at Warner Music Nashville|url=http://www.musicnewsnashville.com/matt-mason-from-cmts-next-superstar-performs-at-warner-music-nashville|newspaper=Music News Nashville|date=June 24, 2011}}</ref><ref name=tucker>{{cite news|last=Tucker|first=Ken|title=Matt Mason Wins CMT's Next Superstar|url=http://www.countryweekly.com/news/matt-mason-wins-cmts-next-superstar|newspaper=Country Weekly|date=Jun 12, 2011}}</ref>

== Post-''Superstar'' ==
Following his ''Next Superstar'' win, Mason was signed to [[Warner Records]]. Anticipating a slow process of creating and releasing a major-label album, Mason continued to tour and self-release recordings.<ref name=Thrasher>{{cite news|author=Thrasher, Don|date=June 22, 2012|title=Mason Mason: 'Next superstar' invests in himself|newspaper=[[Dayton Daily News]]|page=AC4}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|author=Thrasher, Don|date=July 26, 2013|title=The Top|newspaper=Dayton Daily News|page=AC3}}</ref> He released the EP ''America's Favorite Pastime'' in 2012.<ref>{{cite news|author=Thrasher, Don|title=Matt Mason: Country singer survives by catering to fans: Musician combines online social media posts with touring|newspaper=Dayton Daily News|date=November 1, 2013|page=AC5}}</ref>

Mason lives in [[Nashville]].<ref name=Thrasher />
