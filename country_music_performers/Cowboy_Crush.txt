'''Cowboy Crush''' was an American [[country music]] band signed to [[Curb Records]]' Asylum/Curb division. The group is composed of Trenna Barnes ([[lead vocals]]), Debbie Johnson ([[bass guitar]], vocals), Becky Priest ([[Keyboard instrument|keyboards]], vocals), and Renaé Truex ([[fiddle]], [[mandolin]]). Until 2006, Darla Perlozzi ([[Drum kit|drums]]) was also a member of the group. Perlozzi co-wrote [[Big & Rich]]'s 2007 single "Loud".

All five founding members of Cowboy Crush had previously worked throughout [[Nashville, Tennessee]] before meeting at [[Belmont University]].<ref name="cmt">[https://web.archive.org/web/20050716075122/http://www.cmt.com/artists/az/cowboy_crush/bio.jhtml CMT.com : Cowboy Crush : Biography]</ref> They held their first practice together in a garage on March 18, 2003.<ref name="cmt"/> Cowboy Crush is also known for its high-energy concerts and backstage antics.<ref>[http://www.countrymusicfestival.com/ccrushinfo.htm Cowboy Crush] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070707210902/http://www.countrymusicfestival.com/ccrushinfo.htm |date=2007-07-07 }}</ref> The group's debut single, "Nobody Ever Died of a Broken Heart", was previously recorded by [[Trick Pony]]. Cowboy Crush's debut album was released to digital retailers on July 14, 2009.
