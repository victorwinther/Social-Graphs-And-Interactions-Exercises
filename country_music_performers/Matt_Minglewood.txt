'''Matt Minglewood''' (born '''Roy Alexander Batherson''', 31 January 1947) is a Canadian musician whose style can be described as a blend of [[country music|country]], [[blues music|blues]], [[folk music|folk]], [[Traditional music|roots]] and rock. The name "Matt" was borrowed from his brother, Matt Batherson.

== Career ==
Born in [[Moncton, New Brunswick]], Minglewood later relocated to [[North Sydney, Nova Scotia]] with his family. He later moved to nearby [[Glace Bay, Nova Scotia]] where he resides to the present day. His nephew [[Norm Batherson]] played hockey professionally, and his grand-nephew [[Drake Batherson]] is currently a [[Winger (ice hockey)|right winger]] for the [[Ottawa Senators]] of the National Hockey League.<ref>{{cite web |title=Drake Batherson |url=https://www.eliteprospects.com/player/195192/drake-batherson |website=eliteprospects.com |access-date=20 December 2020}}</ref>

Minglewood joined his first band, the Rockin' Saints, when he was in his late teens, playing local high school dances, and even tavern gigs before he was old enough to get into bars. He played organ and rhythm guitar.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Dillon |first1=Charlotte |title=Artist Biography |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/matt-minglewood-mn0000970507 |publisher=AllMusic|access-date=20 December 2020}}</ref>

When he was in his early twenties he was part of a band called 'Sam Moon, Matt Minglewood & The Universal Power'. The group was formed in 1969 with Sam Moon<ref>{{cite web|url=http://nsclassicrock.8m.com/sammoon.html/|title=Sam Moon|publisher=Nova Scotia Classic Rock|first=Peter|last=Crowe|accessdate=19 March 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090511121042/http://nsclassicrock.8m.com/sammoon.html|archive-date=11 May 2009|url-status=dead}}</ref> and Minglewood sharing vocal duties. The band soon changed their name to Moon – Minglewood and performed regularly in the [[Halifax Regional Municipality|Halifax/Dartmouth metro area]], becoming a staple at local high school dances in the area.{{citation needed|date=December 2020}}

Minglewood briefly sang lead vocals and played guitar for Pepper Tree, then formed Cold Duck, and then in 1974 formed [[The Minglewood Band]], a band which toured Canada extensively and achieved a measure of national success. The Minglewood Band recorded their first album with Solar Records in 1975. Soon after the album was released they signed a [[Major label#Major labels|major label]] deal with [[RCA Records]]. The group received a 1980 [[Juno Award]] nomination for the [[Juno Award for New Group of the Year|Most Promising Group of the Year]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Past nominees and winners |url=https://junoawards.ca/awards/past-winners-nominees/?search_year_val1=1970&search_year_val2=1981&search_category_val=&award_name=&search_wins_val=no&tag_search_val=Minglewood+Band&submit=submit |website=junoawards.ca |access-date=20 December 2020}}</ref>

From 1984 to 1985, Minglewood took a sabbatical before launching a solo career. In the mid 1980s to early 1990s he was also part of an annual seasonal [[Dance troupe|troupe]] called the Cape Breton Summertime Revue, consisting of about 12 members performing musical and comedy skits for all ages throughout the [[Maritimes]].{{citation needed|date=February 2023}}
.
Over his career he has won and been nominated for numerous [[East Coast Music Awards]],<ref>{{cite web |last1=Young |first1=Norma |title=2018 East Coast Music Awards Rundown |url=https://canadianbeats.ca/2018/05/07/2018-east-coast-music-awards-rundown/ |website=Canadianbeats |date=7 May 2018 |access-date=21 December 2020}}</ref> [[Canadian Country Music Awards]] and [[Juno Awards]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Minglewood to be inducted into hall of fame |url=https://www.capebretonpost.com/lifestyles/local-lifestyles/minglewood-to-be-inducted-into-hall-of-fame-400305/ |website=Cape Breton Post |access-date=20 December 2020}}</ref> In 2013 he received the Blues With a Feeling Lifetime Achievement Award at the 16th annual [[Maple Blues Awards]] in [[Toronto]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Grant |first1=Jean |title=An award of a lifetime for Matt Minglewood |url=https://www.capebretonpost.com/lifestyles/an-award-of-a-lifetime-for-matt-minglewood-19083/ |website=Cape Breton Post |access-date=20 December 2020}}</ref> In January 2020, Music Nova Scotia and Casino Nova Scotia announced his induction to the Casino Nova Scotia Music Hall of Fame.<ref>{{cite web |title=Casino Nova Scotia Music Hall of Fame |url=http://musicnovascotia.ca/content/casino-nova-scotia-music-hall-fame |website=Music Nova Scotia |access-date=21 December 2020}}</ref>

On 4 July 2019, Minglewood suffered an eye injury which left him temporarily blind in his left eye.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://atlantic.ctvnews.ca/despite-eye-injury-minglewood-vows-to-keep-rockin-the-blues-1.4504172 |title=Despite eye injury, Minglewood vows to keep 'rockin' the blues' |date=11 July 2019 |website=atlantic.ctvnews.ca |access-date=11 August 2019}}</ref>
