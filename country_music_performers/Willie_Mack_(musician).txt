'''Willie Mack''' is an American [[country music]] singer-songwriter born in [[Tulsa, Oklahoma]] and raised in [[Chico, Texas]].<ref name="cmt">{{Cite web|url=http://www.cmt.ca/Artists/All/williemack/Bio.aspx|title=Willie Mack's Bio |website=CMT.ca}}</ref> He has had his songs recorded by [[Sara Evans]], [[Collin Raye]], [[The Oak Ridge Boys]], and [[Mark Wills]] among others.<ref name="ole">{{cite web|url=http://olemm.mobi/artist17.html|title=ole|website=Olemm.mobi}}</ref> His single "Don't Waste Your Pretty" charted on the [[Canadian Hot 100]].

==Biography==
Mack attended [[Belmont University]], and signed a publishing deal with [[Famous Music]] after graduating.<ref name="paquin">{{cite web|url=http://paquinentertainment.com/agency-roster-bio.php?art_id=286|title=Paquin Entertainment Group, a full-service diversified entertainment company|website=Paquinentertainement.com}}</ref> In 2004, Mack released his first album, ''Average Guy''. He had a hand in writing every song on the project.<ref name="pla">{{Cite web|url=http://plamedia.com/willie.htm|title=PLA Media: Willie Mack|website=Plamedia.com}}</ref> The first single, "We Can't All Be From Texas," reached the top 10 on the Texas Music Chart<ref name="newsletter">{{cite web|url=http://www.olemm.com/enewsletter/BrowseroleNewsJune07P2.cfm?storyid=177|title=ole – Welcome!|website=Olemm.com}}</ref> and was used by [[George W. Bush]] during his 2004 election campaign.<ref name="plapress">{{Cite web|url=http://plamedia.com/pressdocs/wille-bush.htm|title=Willie and President Bush|website=Plamedia.com}}</ref> It was also played in the stadium during the stadium of the 2004 college football game between Oklahoma Sooners and the University of Texas Longorns (the Red River Shootout.)

When Canadian artists such as [[George Canyon]], [[Adam Gregory]] and [[Brad Johner]] began recording Mack's songs, he started making trips to Canada and writing with Canadian songwriters.<ref name="bio">{{Cite web|url=http://www.openroadrecordings.com/pdf/bios/willie_mac_bio_wma.pdf|title=Willie Mack Bio at Open Road|website=Openroadrecordings.com}}</ref> One of those songwriters was [[Jason McCoy]], who would become the co-producer of Mack's second album and the co-writer of its first single, "Gonna Get Me a Cadillac."<ref name="paquin"/> The album, ''[[Headlights & Tailpipes]]'', was recorded in [[Toronto]], [[Nashville, Tennessee|Nashville]] and [[Texas]].<ref name="cmt"/> It was released in July 2007 in Canada on [[Open Road Recordings]].<ref name="allmusic">[{{AllMusic|class=album|id=r1111682|pure_url=yes}} AllMusic ((( Headlights and Tailpipes > Overview )))]</ref> Once again, Mack wrote or co-wrote all twelve tracks.<ref name="paquin"/> The album's second single, "Don't Waste Your Pretty," peaked at No.&nbsp;94 on the all-genre [[Canadian Hot 100]]. Mack released his third album, ''[[The Journey (Willie Mack album)|The Journey]]'', in 2009.

In 2012, Mack formed the group Friday Night Satellites.<ref>{{cite news|last=Morin|first=Brandi|title=Get one-on-one with Willie Mack at up coming house concert|url=http://www.sprucegroveexaminer.com/2012/10/11/get-one-on-one-with-willie-mack-at-up-coming-house-concert|accessdate=January 18, 2013|newspaper=[[Spruce Grove|Spruce Grove Examiner]]|date=October 11, 2012}}</ref>
