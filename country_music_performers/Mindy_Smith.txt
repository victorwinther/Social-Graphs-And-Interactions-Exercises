'''Melinda Leigh Smith''' (born June 1, 1972, [[Long Island, New York]]) is an American singer-songwriter. Her first record deal came after she sang a cover version of the song "[[Jolene (song)|Jolene]]" by [[Dolly Parton]].

==Music career==
Smith was adopted at birth by a non-denominational Protestant minister and his wife, who was choir director at the church. She grew up on [[Long Island]], New York. After her mother died of cancer in 1991, Smith attended Cincinnati Bible College for two years.<ref name="ND">{{cite web|title=Mindy Smith - On the inside|url=http://nodepression.com/article/mindy-smith-inside|website=No Depression|accessdate=26 January 2017|date=31 October 2006}}</ref>

Smith and her father moved to [[Knoxville, Tennessee]], where she began listening to [[folk music|folk]] and [[bluegrass music]], [[Alison Krauss]], and [[the Cox Family]]. In 1998, she moved to [[Nashville, Tennessee|Nashville]] to pursue a career in music. Two years later, she reached the finals of a contest at the [[Kerrville Folk Festival]]. This led to a contract with [http://www.bigyellowdogmusic.com/ Big Yellow Dog Music].<ref name="AM bio">{{cite web|last1=Deming|first1=Mark|title=Mindy Smith {{!}} Biography|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/mindy-smith-mn0000493015/biography|website=AllMusic|accessdate=22 January 2017}}</ref>

Smith attracted attention in 2003 when she sang a cover version of "[[Jolene (song)|Jolene]]" by [[Dolly Parton]] for the tribute album
''[[Just Because I'm a Woman: Songs of Dolly Parton|Just Because I'm a Woman]]''. Soon after, she signed a contract with [[Vanguard Records]], who released her debut album, ''[[One Moment More]]'' in 2004.<ref name="AM bio" /> In addition to Dolly Parton, she has expressed admiration for [[John Prine]], [[Alison Krauss]], [[Patty Griffin]], [[Shania Twain]], [[Kris Kristofferson]], [[Buddy Miller]], and [[Bill Gaither (gospel singer)|Bill Gaither]].<ref name="ND" />

"Come to Jesus" was her biggest hit, receiving airplay on [[country music|country]], [[Christian music|Christian]], [[adult album alternative]] (AAA), and [[adult contemporary music|adult contemporary]] radio. The song charted at No. 32 on the [[Adult Top 40]] chart of ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' magazine.{{citation needed|date=January 2017}} In 2004 Smith appeared at the [[Cambridge Folk Festival]] in the U.K., which was broadcast nationally on [[BBC Radio]].

In October 2006, Smith released "Out Loud", the first single from her second album ''[[Long Island Shores]]''. The song was well received by AAA rock radio and Country Music Television ([[CMT (U.S. TV channel)|CMT]]). On January 10, 2007, she performed "Please Stay" on ''[[The Tonight Show with Jay Leno]]''.

In October 2007, Smith released a Christmas album, ''[[My Holiday]]''. She wrote six original songs, including "I Know the Reason" with [[Thad Cockrell]].<ref name="pop_matters_mindysmith">{{cite web|url=http://www.popmatters.com/pm/review/mindy-smith-my-holiday/|title=Mindy Smith: My Holiday|last=Gilstrap|first=Andrew|work=[[Popmatters]]|accessdate=2009-10-24}}</ref>

In August 2009, Smith released her fourth studio album, ''[[Stupid Love (album)|Stupid Love]]''. She appeared on ''[[The Early Show]]'' on August 15, 2009, to perform the first single, "Highs and Lows". On September 29, 2009, while promoting the album on the syndicated radio show ''[[World Cafe (radio program)|World Cafe]]'', she disclosed that she had [[obsessive–compulsive disorder]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=113306795|title=Mindy Smith: A Chanteuse's Emotional Return|work=NPR|date=September 29, 2009|accessdate=April 2, 2017}}</ref>

In June 2012, Smith released an [[Mindy Smith (album)|eponymous independent studio album]] on her own Giant Leap label, in conjunction with TVX. In October of the same year, Vanguard Records released a compilation album of her songs, "The Essential Mindy Smith".

On October 29, 2013, Smith released a holiday EP entitled ''Snowed In'' on Giant Leap/TVX. The release contained both original [[Christmas songs]] and cover material.

Between 2015 and 2018, Smith contributed (as sole writer or co-writer) several songs to the musical television drama series ''[[Nashville (2012 TV series)|Nashville]]''. She is credited on four of the songs performed by cast members and appearing [[Nashville discography|on the soundtrack]] in the series' final three seasons.

==Charity==
In March 2013, Smith worked with [[Anthropologie]] during an in-store performance to raise money and awareness for the [[Captain Planet Foundation]], a non-profit organization. Anthropologie donated fifteen percent of sales made in the first hour after Smith's performance to CPF.<ref name="Charity Work">{{cite web|title=Mindy Smith joins forces with lifestyle brand Anthropologie and Captain Planet|url=http://www.mindysmithmusic.com/news/mindy-smith-joins-forces-lifestyle-brand-anthropologie-and-captain-planet-176|website=Mindy Smith|accessdate=14 October 2015|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150924121433/http://www.mindysmithmusic.com/news/mindy-smith-joins-forces-lifestyle-brand-anthropologie-and-captain-planet-176|archivedate=24 September 2015}}</ref>
