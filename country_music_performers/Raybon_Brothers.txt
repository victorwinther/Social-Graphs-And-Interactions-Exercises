'''Raybon Brothers''' were an American [[country music]] duo from [[Sanford, Florida]], consisting of brothers [[Marty Raybon]] and Tim Raybon. Prior to the duo's inception in 1997, Marty Raybon was the lead singer of the country music band [[Shenandoah (band)|Shenandoah]], having left in 1997, before rejoining in 2014.<ref name="gibson">{{cite web |url=http://www.gibson.com/player/apr03/raybon.html |title=Marty Raybon scratches his bluegrass itch |accessdate=2008-07-28 |last=Nikolai |first=Michelle |work=Gibson Guitar Corporation |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080719161854/http://www.gibson.com/player/apr03/Raybon.html |archivedate=2008-07-19}}</ref>

The Raybon Brothers charted in 1997 with a cover of [[Bob Carlisle]]'s pop hit "[[Butterfly Kisses (song)|Butterfly Kisses]]". Their version was a top 40 hit on both the ''Billboard'' country music charts and the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. A second single, "The Way She's Lookin'", reached the lower regions of the country charts. Marty recorded a number of solo projects before rejoining Shenandoah.
