'''Erika Jo Vastola''' (née '''Heriges''', born November 2, 1986, in [[Angleton, Texas]]), known simply by the [[stage name]] '''Erika Jo''', is an American [[country music]] recording artist who was declared winner of the 2005 season of the ''[[Nashville Star]]'' television program. Eighteen years old at the time, Erika Jo is both the youngest person to win the competition and the first female winner. She was signed to [[Universal South Records]] in 2005 and released her [[Erika Jo (album)|self-titled debut album]], which produced a chart single in "I Break Things", a No.&nbsp;53 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs]] charts. A second single from the album, a cover of the [[Jessi Colter]] hit, "[[I'm Not Lisa]]", failed to chart, and she was dropped from Universal South in 2007.

==Early life==
Erika Jo is the daughter of Bo and Paige Heriges. Erika Jo grew up in [[Mount Juliet, Tennessee]], 15 miles east of [[Nashville, Tennessee|Nashville]], and is a 2005 graduate of Wilson Central High School in [[Lebanon, Tennessee]].

==Career==
Erika Jo was also a national finalist in the 1999 [[Colgate Country Showdown|True Value Country Showdown (now called the Colgate Country Showdown)]], a talent competition consisting of regional winners from across the United States. She also competed on the singing competition ''[[Nashville Star]]'' in 2005 and won first plce.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/erika-jo-mn0000376806/biography | title=Erika Jo biography | publisher=AllMusic | accessdate=March 26, 2022}}</ref>

Her first single, "I Break Things", was released immediately upon her ''Nashville Star'' victory. The [[music video]] received heavy rotation on [[Country Music Television|CMT]] and [[Great American Country|GAC]], but the song peaked at No.&nbsp;53. One subsequent single ("[[I'm Not Lisa]]") was released; it failed to reach the charts. Her only professional album, ''[[Erika Jo (album)|Erika Jo]]'', was released by [[Universal South Records]] on June 14, 2005 and debuted at No.&nbsp;5 on [[Billboard magazine|Billboard]]'s country album chart, but the album, like its first single, enjoyed very limited success after its initial launch, selling just 118,829 copies through its first 11 months in release.<ref>[http://www.tennessean.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/20060503/ENTERTAINMENT12/605030432] {{Dead link|date=July 2022}} ''Tennessean.com'' Retrieved on 06-07-07</ref> She was later dropped from Universal South.

==Personal life==
Erika Jo married Stephen Vastola in 2013. They reside in suburban Nashville.
