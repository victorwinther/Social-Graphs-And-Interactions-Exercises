'''Chad Brownlee''' (born July 12, 1984) is a Canadian [[country music]] artist, songwriter, actor, and former [[ice hockey]] [[defenceman]]. He has one #1 [[Canada Country]] hit with "[[Forever's Gotta Start Somewhere]]".

Brownlee was a draft pick for the [[Vancouver Canucks]] in 2003, going in the sixth round No.&nbsp;190th overall.<ref name="hockey">{{cite web |last=Kennedy |first=Ryan |title=The Straight Edge: When pro hockey is no longer the dream |url=http://www.thehockeynews.com/articles/25876-The-Straight-Edge-When-pro-hockey-is-no-longer-the-dream.html |work=[[The Hockey News]] |access-date=November 19, 2009 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090504085041/http://www.thehockeynews.com/articles/25876-The-Straight-Edge-When-pro-hockey-is-no-longer-the-dream.html |archive-date=May 4, 2009 |url-status=dead }}</ref> After four years of playing for the [[National Collegiate Athletic Association|NCAA]]'s [[Minnesota State Mavericks]] (located in Mankato, Minn.), Brownlee made his professional debut with the [[ECHL]]'s [[Idaho Steelheads]] playing a lone season with the club in the [[2007-08 ECHL season|2007–08]] season before ending his playing career.<ref name="hockey" />

==Music career==
Following a series of injuries, he turned his attention to music the following year.<ref name="hockey" /> His first single, "The Best That I Can (Superhero)", was released in November 2009. Brownlee's [[Chad Brownlee (album)|self-titled]] debut album, produced by [[Mitch Merrett]], was released in August 2010 via [[MDM Recordings Inc.|MDM Recordings]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.cmt.ca/Artist/ChadBrownlee |title=Chad Brownlee Biography |access-date=November 20, 2009 |archive-date=November 21, 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101121041855/http://www.cmt.ca/Artist/ChadBrownlee |url-status=dead }}</ref> He followed it up with ''[[Love Me or Leave Me (Chad Brownlee album)|Love Me or Leave Me]]'' in 2012. Brownlee was nominated for a [[Juno Award]] for [[Juno Award for Country Recording of the Year|Country Album of the Year]] on February 19, 2013. The awards took place on April 21 in [[Regina, Saskatchewan]].<ref>{{cite news|title=JUNO nominees include The Sheepdogs, Justin Bieber|url=http://ckom.com/story/announcing-juno-award-nominees/97119|access-date=February 19, 2013|newspaper=[[CKOM]]|date=February 19, 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130928052415/http://ckom.com/story/announcing-juno-award-nominees/97119|archive-date=September 28, 2013}}</ref> Brownlee's third album, ''[[The Fighters (Chad Brownlee album)|The Fighters]]'', was released on June 3, 2014.<ref name="fighters">{{cite web |title=Chad Brownlee to release The Fighters June 3 |url=http://www.universalmusic.ca/press-releases/chad-brownlee-to-release-the-fighters-june-3/ |work=[[Universal Music]] |access-date=April 28, 2014}}</ref>

Brownlee landed his first No. 1 hit with "[[Forever's Gotta Start Somewhere]]" in April 2019.<ref name="Canada Country"/> It was included on the EP ''[[Back in the Game (Chad Brownlee album)|Back in the Game]]'', which was released on June 21, 2019, via [[Universal Music Canada]].<ref name ="AllMusicBG">{{cite web|title=''Back in the Game'' – Chad Brownlee|url= https://www.allmusic.com/album/back-in-the-game-mw0003292951|work=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=April 26, 2020}}</ref> Brownlee extended that to become his fourth album on January 10, 2020.<ref name="UMusicBG">{{cite web|title=CHAD BROWNLEE RELEASES DELUXE ALBUM, BACK IN THE GAME, OUT NOW|url=http://www.umusic.ca/press-releases/chad-brownlee-releases-deluxe-album-back-in-the-game-out-now/|work=UMusic.ca|publisher=[[Universal Music Canada]]|date=January 10, 2020|access-date=January 17, 2020|archive-date=February 8, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200208094859/http://www.umusic.ca/press-releases/chad-brownlee-releases-deluxe-album-back-in-the-game-out-now/|url-status=dead}}</ref> A July 2020 [[Nielsen Music]] study found Brownlee to be the eighth-highest played Canadian artist on domestic radio in the first half of 2020, ahead of [[Brett Kissel]] and [[JP Saxe]], and behind [[Dallas Smith (singer)|Dallas Smith]] and [[Drake (musician)|Drake]].<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/9417354/nielsen-music-mrc-data-midyear-charts-canada-2020|first=Karen|last=Bliss|title= The Weeknd Tops Nielsen Music/MRC Data Midyear Charts Across Canada|date=13 July 2020|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=6 August 2020}}</ref>

In 2023, Brownlee ended a prolonged hiatus and released the single "The Country Kind".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://canadianbeats.ca/2023/09/22/chad-brownlee-unveils-new-single-the-country-kind-interview/|title=CHAD BROWNLEE UNVEILS NEW SINGLE, "THE COUNTRY KIND" (INTERVIEW)|date=September 22, 2023|first=Jenna|last=Melanson|access-date=October 18, 2023}}</ref>

==Acting career==
Brownlee's first role as an actor came in 2010 film ''[[Tooth Fairy (2010 film)|Tooth Fairy]]'' as an unnamed hockey player with no lines.<ref name="PCA">{{cite web|title=Exclusive Interview: Pop-Culturalist Chats with Range Roads' Chad Brownlee|url= http://pop-culturalist.com/exclusive-interview-pop-culturalist-chats-with-range-roads-chad-brownlee/|work=Pop-Culturalist|date=March 31, 2021|access-date=April 27, 2021}}</ref> He appeared in the 2021 film ''[[Range Roads]]'' as Bruce, the ex-boyfriend of the main character Frankie.<ref name="PCA"/>
