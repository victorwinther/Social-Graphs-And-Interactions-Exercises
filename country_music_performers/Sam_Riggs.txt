'''Sam Riggs''' is an American [[country music]] singer-songwriter.

==Biography==
Sam Riggs was born in [[St. Cloud, Florida]].<ref name="newsok">{{cite news|last1=McDonnell|first1=Brandy|title=Interview and video: Texas country-rocker Sam Riggs makes an impression with "When the Lights Go Out," plays tonight at Grady's 66 Pub|url=http://newsok.com/article/3887302|accessdate=May 13, 2016|work=[[The Oklahoman]]|date=September 27, 2013}}</ref> He moved to [[Austin, Texas]] in August 2007 when he was 19 years old.<ref name="newsok" /> He worked in [[manual labour]] but also performed his songs in local cafes and bars.<ref>{{cite web|last=Erlewine|first=Stephen Thomas|title=Sam Riggs {{!}} Biography & History|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=mn0003180318/biography|pure_url=yes}}|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|accessdate=May 13, 2016}}</ref> Riggs released his first [[extended play]], ''Hairpin Trigger Heart'', in 2010.<ref name="newsok" /> He met [[Ray Wylie Hubbard]] in 2011, who taught him his [[fingerstyle guitar|fingerpicking]] style.<ref name="newsok" /> Riggs sold all of his possessions to finance the release of his second extended play, ''Lighthouse'', released in 2012.<ref name="sanangelo">{{cite news|last1=Martinez|first1=Federic|title=Newfound success leaves Sam Riggs "Breathless"|url=http://www.gosanangelo.com/entertainment/music/newfound-success-leaves-sam-riggs-breathless-2d795f6a-986d-2cd1-e053-0100007f78f2-371568071.html|accessdate=May 13, 2016|work=[[San Angelo Standard-Times]]|date=March 9, 2016}}</ref>

Riggs' full-length debut album, ''Outrun the Sun'', was released on October 29, 2013 by Vision Entertainment.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Whitaker|first1=Sterling|title=Sam Riggs and the Night People, 'Outrun the Sun' – Exclusive Album Stream|url=http://theboot.com/sam-riggs-and-the-night-people-outrun-the-sun-stream/|accessdate=May 13, 2016|work=The Boot|date=October 23, 2013}}</ref> The album was released under the moniker Sam Riggs and the Night People.<ref name="wideopen">{{cite news|last1=Burchard|first1=Jeremy|title=Sam Riggs Steps Towards National Success with 'Breathless'|url=http://www.wideopencountry.com/breathless-sam-riggs-takes-big-step-towards-national-success/|accessdate=May 13, 2016|work=Wide Open Country|date=February 2, 2016}}</ref> The first two singles reached the top 10 on the Texas Music Chart.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Webb|first1=Pennylynn|title=Sam Riggs: A star on the rise|url=http://www.palestineherald.com/community/sam-riggs-a-star-on-the-rise/article_3fa0daf8-c09f-11e5-814f-9bbd65c8954e.html|accessdate=May 13, 2016|work=[[Palestine Herald-Press]]|date=January 21, 2016}}</ref> The video for the single "When the Lights Go Out" peaked at number one on [[CMT (U.S. TV channel)|CMT]]'s fan-voted Pure 12 Pack Countdown.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Clark|first1=Rob|title=Country music singer Sam Riggs excited for upcoming show at Hurricane Harry's|url=http://www.theeagle.com/news/local/country-music-singer-sam-riggs-excited-for-upcoming-show-at/article_fe0e5936-8f6f-52a5-9138-c41dd0b81f19.html|accessdate=May 13, 2016|work=[[The Bryan-College Station Eagle]]|date=January 23, 2014}}</ref> One of the album's songs, "Collide", was featured in the TV show ''[[Nashville (2012 TV series)|Nashville]]''.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Wenger Watson|first1=Julie|title=Sam Riggs: A Journey to Texas Country|url=http://nodepression.com/interview/sam-riggs-journey-texas-country|accessdate=May 13, 2016|work=[[No Depression (magazine)|No Depression]]|date=August 31, 2015}}</ref> The album sold over 10,000 copies<ref>{{cite news|last1=Horton|first1=Greg|title=Country artist Sam Riggs to play Wormy Dog|url=http://okgazette.com/2015/07/22/country-artist-sam-riggs-to-play-wormy-dog/|accessdate=May 13, 2016|work=[[Oklahoma Gazette]]|date=July 22, 2015}}</ref> and earned him Artist of the Year at the first Texas Magazine Texas Music Awards.<ref name="sanangelo" />

In February 2015, Riggs released "Second Hand Smoke", the first song from his second studio album.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Rodgers|first1=D. Patrick|title=5 Minutes in Texas With Sam Riggs|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/5-minutes-in-texas-with-sam-riggs-20150220|accessdate=May 13, 2016|work=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=February 20, 2015}}</ref> The album, ''Breathless'', was released on February 19, 2016.<ref name="wideopen" /> Riggs produced the album with Eric Herbst.<ref>{{cite news|title=First Listen: New Artist Sam Riggs|url=http://www.countryweekly.com/music/first-listen-new-artist-sam-riggs|accessdate=May 13, 2016|work=[[Nash Country Weekly]]|date=February 11, 2016}}</ref> It was released on his own record label, Deep Creek Records, and funded by a [[Kickstarter]] project.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Hoover|first1=Carl|title=More than just 'Lucky': Sam Riggs returns to Waco with hit album, single|url=http://www.wacotrib.com/entertainment/accesswaco/music/more-than-just-lucky-sam-riggs-returns-to-waco-with/article_b45c4adb-b115-5114-a76a-9187f42de799.html|accessdate=May 13, 2016|work=[[Waco Tribune-Herald]]|date=March 17, 2016}}</ref> ''Breathless'' debuted at number 12 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' Country Albums chart<ref name="Country Albums" /> and number 168 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]],<ref name="Billboard 200" /> selling 3,800 copies in its first week of release.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Bjorke|first1=Matt|title=The Country Album Sales Chart: February 29, 2016|url=http://roughstock.com/news/2016/02/39856-the-country-album-sales-chart-february-29-2016|accessdate=May 13, 2016|work=Roughstock|date=February 27, 2016}}</ref> On February 1, 2019, Sam released his first single of 2019, "Obsessed" and on March 1, 2019, he released his current single, "Until My Heart Stops Beating".

==Personal life==
Riggs is currently married and has a son.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mysanantonio.com/entertainment/entertainment_columnists/john_goodspeed/amp/Rocking-country-singer-songwriter-Sam-Riggs-at-11949928.php|title=Rocking country singer-songwriter Sam Riggs at Floore's|date=August 22, 2017|work=My San Antonio}}</ref>
