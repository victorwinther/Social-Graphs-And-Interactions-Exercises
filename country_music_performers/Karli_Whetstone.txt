'''Karli Whetstone''', born in [[Ohio]], United States, is an American country music writer and singer.

==Career==
Whetstone began dancing at the age of three and continued dancing through high school.  She won championships and competed at the national level for her dancing.  Whetstone began classical vocal training at the age of 12, soon entered singing contests and, by the age of 14, was performing with area bands in Ohio.{{citation needed|date=August 2021}}

After graduating high school, Whetstone began to open shows for big-name acts in Nashville, including [[Taylor Swift]], [[Phil Vassar]], [[Darryl Worley]], and [[Joe Nichols]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Power 105.1|url=http://www.power1051fm.com/new2/artists/i/150488|accessdate=October 25, 2012|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130131200301/http://www.power1051fm.com/new2/artists/i/150488|archive-date=January 31, 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Whetstone released her self-titled four-song EP, as well as the regional hit single "Ohio" in 2011.<ref>{{cite web|title=Karli.com {{!}} Music|url=http://www.karli.com/music/|accessdate=2012-10-25}}</ref>  "Ohio" was adopted by Ohio State head football coach [[Jim Tressel]] being played for over 100,000 people in [[Ohio Stadium]] during every home game.

She is also often featured in local media in the Nashville area presenting her recipes, such as her Leprechaun Cake.<ref>{{cite web|title=WSMV.com NBC 4|url=http://www.wsmv.com/story/17166882/karli-whetstones-leprechaun-surprise-cake-31512|accessdate=October 25, 2012}}</ref>

In 2015, Whetstone released her music video for "Frozen," directed by Roman White.<ref>{{cite web|title=CountryWeekly.com|url=http://www.countryweekly.com/music/karli-whetstone-premieres-frozen-video|accessdate=March 16, 2016}}</ref>

In 2016, she released the Country Enough – EP, produced by Tone Def. It features six songs including "Blow It Up."<ref>{{cite web|title=iTunes|website=[[iTunes]] |url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/country-enough-ep/id1079719934|accessdate=March 16, 2016}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Whetstone is currently married to [[Doug Datish]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Tribune Chronicle|url=http://www.tribtoday.com/page/content.detail/id/506136.html|accessdate=October 25, 2012}}</ref>
