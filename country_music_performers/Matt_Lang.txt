'''Matt Lang''' is a Canadian [[country music|country]] singer and songwriter. He has released three albums ''More'', ''Moonlight Sessions'', and ''All Night Longer'', as well as multiple singles, including "[[Getcha]]" and "[[Only a Woman (song)|Only a Woman]]".<ref>{{cite web|title=More - Matt Lang|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/more-mw0003398534|work=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=September 10, 2021}}</ref>

==Early life==
Lang is from [[Maniwaki, Quebec]]. He recalls [[Johnny Cash]]'s "[[Folsom Prison Blues]]" as the first song he ever performed live.<ref>{{cite web|title=Matt Lang shows Québec's take on country music|url=https://www.queensjournal.ca/story/2020-01-24/arts/matt-lang-shows-quebecs-take-on-country-music/|date=January 24, 2020|work=[[The Queen's Journal]]|first=Brittany|last=Giliforte}}</ref> While primarily playing [[ice hockey|hockey]] as a teenager, Lang realized his true passion was writing and performing music. He cites Cash, [[Dwight Yoakam]], and [[Merle Haggard]] as artists he listened to growing up, in addition to regional Quebecois acts [[Kaïn]], [[Les Colocs]], and [[Okoumé (band)|Okoumé]].<ref name="UW">{{cite web|url=http://uwimprint.ca/article/matt-lang-canadas-next-country-star/|title=Matt Lang: Canada's next country star|first=Sharanya|last=Karkera|date=January 28, 2020|work=UW Imprint}}</ref>

==Career==
Lang began his career as a Francophone singer on Quebec reality show ''[[La Voix]]'', North America's [[French language]] counterpart to ''[[The Voice (franchise)|The Voice]]''.<ref name="UW"/> He subsequently released a French album before deciding to pursue his passion for country music.<ref name="UW"/> When he travelled to [[Nashville, Tennessee]] in 2018, Lang stated he was then only learning to speak the [[English language]],<ref name="EF">{{cite web|url=https://entertainment-focus.com/2021/02/25/interview-matt-lang-opens-up-about-learning-to-speak-english-and-his-album-more/|title=Interview: Matt Lang Opens Up About Learning To Speak English And His Album 'More'|first=Pip|last=Ellwood-Hughes|date=February 25, 2021|work=Entertainment Focus}}</ref> while working on his self-titled extended play.<ref>{{cite web|title='I want to sound like an American country man'|url=https://www.saultstar.com/entertainment/local-arts/i-want-to-sound-like-an-american-country-man|work=[[The Sault Star]]|date=January 24, 2020|first=Jeffrey|last=Ougler}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.socanmagazine.ca/features/matt-lang-and-laurie-leblanc-heading-west/|title=Matt Lang and Laurie Leblanc: Heading West|date=June 4, 2020|first=Catherine|last=Genest|work=Words and Music|publisher=[[SOCAN]] Magazine}}</ref>

Lang won the second annual [[SiriusXM]] "Top of the Country" competition in September 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.siriusxm.ca/matt-lang-is-canadas-next-big-name-in-country-music-winning-siriusxm-top-of-the-country-and-25000/|title=Matt Lang Is Canada's Next Big Name in Country Music, Winning Sirius XM's Top of the Country and $25,000|date=September 6, 2019|work=[[SiriusXM Canada]]}}</ref> He released his debut [[country music]] album, ''More'', on Jayward Artist Group in June 2020, which contains the singles "Water Down the Whiskey", "[[Getcha]]", "[[Only a Woman (song)|Only a Woman]]", and "In a Bar".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.canewsottawa.ca/matt-lang-more-album-now-also-available-in-germany-country-d/|title=Matt Lang – More: Album Now Also Available In Germany - Country D|first=Alec|last=Woods|work=CA News Ottawa|date=August 24, 2021|access-date=September 9, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://entertainment-focus.com/2021/06/06/matt-lang-releases-new-single-in-a-bar/|title=Matt Lang Releases New Single 'In A Bar'|date=June 6, 2021|work=Entertainment Focus|first=Pip|last=Ellwood-Hughes|access-date=September 9, 2021}}</ref><ref name="Jayward">{{cite news|title=Jayward Artist Group - Matt Lang|url=https://www.jaywardartistgroup.com/mattlang|work=Jayward Artist Group|access-date=September 23, 2021}}</ref> Lang noted influence of [[Bakersfield sound]] and the [[Western music (North America)|western]]-influenced Franco-country music of [[Quebec]] in his music.<ref name="EF"/> In 2022, Lang collaborated with [[Tyler Joe Miller]] on the single "[[Never Met a Beer]]", which received a nomination for Musical Collaboration of the Year at the [[2023 Canadian Country Music Awards]].<ref name="Dicaire">{{cite web|url= https://www.lacliqc.com/videoclip-never-met-a-beer/|title=Le vidéoclip de "Never Met a Beer" maintenant disponible!|date=November 9, 2022|first=Christopher|last=Dicaire|work=La Cliqc|access-date=July 30, 2023}}</ref>
