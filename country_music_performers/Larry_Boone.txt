'''Larry Eugene Boone''' (born June 7, 1956) is an American [[country music]] artist and songwriter.  Between 1985 and 1993, Boone recorded five major label studio albums, in addition to charting several singles on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs|Hot Country Singles]] charts. His highest-charting single, "Don't Give Candy to a Stranger", reached No.&nbsp;10 in 1988. Boone has also co-written several singles for other country music artists, including a Number One single for [[Kathy Mattea]], and Top Ten hits for [[Don Williams]], [[Tracy Lawrence]], [[Rick Trevino]] and [[Lonestar]].

==Musical career==
Larry Eugene Boone was born in [[Cooper City, Florida]], on June 7, 1956. He is a distant relative [[Daniel Boone]], the famed frontiersman.<ref name="google">{{cite book|title=The insider's country music handbook|author1=Flint, J.H.|author2=Nelson, J.A.|date=1993|publisher=Gibbs-Smith|isbn=9780879055639|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=pin7Ya25sGkC|accessdate=April 14, 2015}}</ref> He attended [[Florida Atlantic University]] and moved to Nashville in 1981.

His first cut as a songwriter was [[Marie Osmond]]'s 1985 single "Until I Fall in Love Again".<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web |url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p1530|pure_url=yes}} |title=allmusic ((( Larry Boone > Biography))) |accessdate=November 23, 2007 |last=Loftus |first= Johnny |work=[[Allmusic]]}}</ref> Boone was signed to a recording contract with [[Mercury Records]] in 1986. Boone's debut single "Stranger Things Have Happened" was released that year, reaching a peak of No.&nbsp;64 on the ''Billboard'' Hot Country Singles charts. It was the first of seven singles from his self-titled debut album, released in 1987. The album's last single, 1988's "Don't Give Candy to a Stranger", was Boone's highest charting single, peaking at No.&nbsp;10.<ref name="allmusic"/>

1988 saw the release of Boone's second album, ''Swingin' Doors, Sawdust Floors'', which produced Top 20 hits in "I Just Called to Say Goodbye Again" and "Wine Me Up". Meanwhile, he continued to write songs for other artists, including "[[Burnin' Old Memories]]", a Number One single for [[Kathy Mattea]] in 1989.<ref name="allmusic"/><ref name="oldies">{{cite web |url=http://www.oldies.com/artist-biography/Larry-Boone.html |title=Larry Boone biography |accessdate=November 23, 2007 |work=Oldies.com}}</ref>

Boone's third and final album for Mercury, 1990's ''Down That River Road'', produced only one single before he was dropped from Mercury's roster.<ref name="oldies"/> In 1991, Boone signed to [[Columbia Records]]. His first album for the label, 1991's ''One Way to Go'', was released that year, followed by ''Get in Line'' two years later. Both albums produced minor hit singles before he left Columbia as well.<ref name="oldies"/> Boone continued to compose songs for other artists throughout the 1990s and into the 2000s, including singles for [[Shenandoah (band)|Shenandoah]], [[George Strait]], [[Lonestar]], [[Rick Trevino]], [[Tracy Lawrence]] and [[Doug Stone]].
