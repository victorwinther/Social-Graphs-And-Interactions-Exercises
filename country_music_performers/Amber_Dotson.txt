'''Amber Dotson''' (born in [[Garland, Texas]]<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web |url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p675686|pure_url=yes}} |title=Amber Dotson biography |accessdate=2008-06-14 |last=Leggett |first=Steve |work=[[Allmusic]]}}</ref>) is an American [[country music]] artist. Initially a songwriter for Sony/Tree Publishing, Dotson soon began singing demos as well. She also made an appearance on [[Travis Tritt]]'s 2004 album ''[[My Honky Tonk History]]'' as a background vocalist.<ref name="liner">{{cite AV media notes |title=My Honky Tonk History |others=Travis Tritt |year=2004 |publisher=[[Columbia Records]] Nashville |id=CK 92084}}</ref> [[George Strait]] heard some of the singer's demos, and asked her to join him on his 2005 tour, which also included [[Dierks Bentley]].<ref name="cmt">{{cite web |url=http://www.cmt.com/artists/az/dotson__amber/bio.jhtml |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080128184558/http://www.cmt.com/artists/az/dotson__amber/bio.jhtml |url-status=dead |archive-date=January 28, 2008 |title=Amber Dotson biography |accessdate=2008-06-14 |work=[[Country Music Television]]}}</ref> Dotson was signed to [[Capitol Records]] Nashville in 2005, and although she released two singles for the label, she did not release an album.
