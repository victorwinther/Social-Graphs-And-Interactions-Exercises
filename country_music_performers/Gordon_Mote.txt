'''Gordon James Mote'''<ref name="BMI">{{cite web|title=Songwriter/Composer: Mote Gordon James |url=http://repertoire.bmi.com/writer.asp?blnWriter=True&blnPublisher=True&blnArtist=True&page=1&fromrow=1&torow=25&querytype=WriterID&keyid=452000&keyname=MOTE+GORDON+JAMES&CAE=243319290&Affiliation=BMI |publisher=[[Broadcast Music, Inc.]] |author=[[Broadcast Music, Inc.]] |access-date=January 5, 2016 }}{{dead link|date=March 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> (born October 25, 1970) is an American [[Christian country music|Christian country]]/[[southern gospel]] singer, piano virtuoso, and [[Contemporary worship|worship leader]]. He was born blind. He has released eight studio albums. His album ''Don't Let Me Miss the Glory'' (2007) was his breakthrough on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' charts.

==Early life==
Mote was born, on October 25,<ref name="Day">{{cite web |title=@HamblenMusic: @GordonMote We were a day early on the birthday wish, weren't we? (Your birthday is tomorrow, correct?)You are correct... |url=https://twitter.com/GordonMote/status/525779717572808704 |author=Gordon Mote (GordonMote)|publisher=Twitter|access-date=January 5, 2015|date=October 24, 2014}}</ref> 1970, in [[Gadsden, Alabama]], as a blind person,<ref name="AllMusic Biography">{{cite web|title=Gordon Mote : Biography|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/gordon-mote-mn0000776983/biography|author=[[AllMusic]]|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=January 5, 2016}}</ref> where he grew up in nearby [[Attalla, Alabama|Attalla]]. He attended both [[Jacksonville State University]], where he spent the first three years of his music education, while he transferred to [[Belmont University]] in [[Nashville, Tennessee]], where he graduated [[Latin honors|with honors]] in music.<ref name="AllMusic Biography"/>

==Music career==
Just after graduating, [[Lee Greenwood]] asked Mote to join his band. Since then, he has toured with artists such as [[Trisha Yearwood]], [[Tanya Tucker]], [[Porter Wagoner]], the [[Gaither Vocal Band]], and the [[Gaither Homecoming]] Tour. In 2001, when a pianist was needed for [[Alan Jackson]]'s "[[Where Were You (When the World Stopped Turning)]]" recording, Mote was recommended. After that, he became a very sought-after studio musician, playing on numerous country and gospel artist's albums.<ref>{{cite web|title=Gordon Mote Biography|url=http://www.gordonmote.com/bio.html}}</ref>

His studio album, ''Don't Let Me Miss the Glory'', was his debut as a vocalist.<ref name="AllMusic Biography"/> It was released on October 23, 2007,<ref name="AM Review">{{cite web|title=''Don't Let Me Miss the Glory'' – Gordon Mote : Review|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/dont-let-me-miss-the-glory-mw0000485414 |author=[[AllMusic]] |publisher=[[AllMusic]] |access-date=January 5, 2016|date=October 23, 2007}}</ref> with Spring Hill Records.<ref name="CR Review">{{cite web|title=Review: Don't Let Me Miss the Glory – Gordon Mote |url=http://www.crossrhythms.co.uk/products/Gordon_Mote/Dont_Let_Me_Miss_The_Glory/32961/ |first=Dave |last=Brassington |publisher=[[Cross Rhythms]] |access-date=January 5, 2016|date=February 14, 2008}}</ref> This album was his breakthrough, appearing on the [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'' magazine]] charts, peaking at No. 170 on the [[Billboard 200]]<ref name="Billboard 200 Albums">{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/303025/gordon-mote/chart?f=305 |title=Gordon Mote Album & Song Chart History (Billboard 200)|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|publisher=[[Prometheus Global Media]] |access-date=January 5, 2016}}</ref> and No. 10 on the [[Christian Albums]] chart.<ref name="Christian Albums">{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/303025/gordon-mote/chart?f=310 |title=Gordon Mote Album & Song Chart History (Christian Albums)|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|publisher=[[Prometheus Global Media]] |access-date=January 5, 2016}}</ref>
