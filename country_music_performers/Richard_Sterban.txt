'''Richard  Anthony Sterban''' (born April 24, 1943) is an American singer. He was born in [[Camden, New Jersey]]. In 1972, he joined the [[country music|country]] and [[gospel music|gospel]] quartet [[The Oak Ridge Boys]], in which he sings [[bass (voice type)|bass]].

Sterban was inducted into the [[Country Music Hall of Fame and Museum|Country Music Hall of Fame]] in 2015 as a member of the Oak Ridge Boys.

==Personal life==

Born in [[Camden, New Jersey]], Sterban grew up in [[Collingswood, New Jersey]],<ref>DeLuca, Dan. [http://articles.philly.com/1994-04-28/entertainment/25861569_1_oak-ridge-boys-hall-oates-plaques "Today's Walk Of Fame Honorees Include 2 Oak Ridge Boys Just A Couple Of Philly-area Country Boys"], ''[[The Philadelphia Inquirer]]'', April 28, 1994. "Bonsall grew up in the Harrowgate section of Philadelphia, near the Tioga Street el stop; Sterban was born across the river in Camden and grew up in Collingswood."</ref> After graduating from [[Collingswood High School]], Sterban attended Trenton State College (now [[The College of New Jersey]]).<ref>Lloyd, Jack. [http://articles.philly.com/1991-02-22/entertainment/25775974_1_bonsall-country-music-country-boy "Sturdy Oaks And A Country Boy From Phila."], ''[[The Philadelphia Inquirer]]'', February 22, 1991. Accessed November 13, 2013. "The remaining member of the Oaks, Richard Sterban, is a native of Camden who graduated from Collingswood High School and attended Trenton State College."</ref>

==Career==

Prior to joining The Oak Ridge Boys, Sterban toured with [[J. D. Sumner]] and the [[Stamps Quartet]], who were singing backup for [[Elvis Presley]] at that time. Sterban ultimately became best known for his "oom-pa-pa-oom-pa-pa-moww-moww" bass solo in the Oak Ridge Boys' 1981 single "[[Elvira (song)|Elvira]]" and sang lead vocals on a select few of the group's songs, including a cover of The Righteous Brothers' hit "[[Dream On (The Righteous Brothers song)|Dream On]]", which was a top-ten hit.

Sterban has recorded [[public service announcement]]s for [[NOAA Weather Radio]]. He served as the voice of ''[[The Roadhouse]]'', the [[classic country]] [[Sirius Satellite Radio]] [[List of Sirius Satellite Radio stations|channel]]. Sterban was a minority owner of the [[Nashville Sounds]] [[minor league baseball]] team from 1978 to 2008, along with entrepreneur [[Larry Schmittou]] and other country music stars, such as [[Conway Twitty]], [[Larry Gatlin]], and [[Cal Smith]].<ref>{{citation|first=Larry|last=Woody|title=Schmittou: A Grand Slam in Baseball, Business, and Life|publisher=Eggmann Publishing Company|location=Nashville|year=1996|ISBN=1886371334 |pages=64–65}}</ref>

Since 2023, Sterban has taken prolonged absences from The Oak Ridge Boys' tours. Aaron McCune, the bass vocalist for [[Dailey & Vincent]], serves as Sterban's designated fill-in.<ref>[https://www.savingcountrymusic.com/aaron-mccune-keeps-bass-singing-alive-with-oak-ridge-boys-dailey-vincent/ Aaron McCune Keeps Bass Singing Alive with Oak Ridge Boys, Dailey & Vincent]. ''Saving Country Music''. February 3, 2023. Retrieved September 20, 2023.</ref> As of September 2023, the band has stated that the issues that led to Sterban's absences have been resolved.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Watts |first=Cindy |date=September 19, 2023 |title=The Oak Ridge Boys Announce Farewell Tour |url=https://www.cmt.com/news/hkt8uf/the-oak-ridge-boys-announce-farewell-tour |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230928110644/https://www.cmt.com/news/hkt8uf/the-oak-ridge-boys-announce-farewell-tour |url-status=dead |archive-date=September 28, 2023 |access-date=2023-09-21 |website=[[Country Music Television|CMT]] |language=en}}</ref>
