The '''James Barker Band''' is a Canadian country group formed in [[Woodville, Ontario]], in 2015. The band consists of James Barker, Taylor Abram, Connor Stephen, and Bobby Martin.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Whitaker|first1=Sterling|title=James Barker Band Get 'Lawn Chair Lazy' in New Video [Exclusive Premiere]|url=http://tasteofcountry.com/james-barker-band-lawn-chair-lazy-video/|access-date=March 25, 2017|work=[[Taste of Country]]|publisher=[[Townsquare Media]]|date=June 29, 2016}}</ref> In Canada, they have four number one country singles with "[[Chills (James Barker Band song)|Chills]]", "[[Keep It Simple (James Barker Band song)|Keep It Simple]]", "[[Over All Over Again]]", and "[[New Old Trucks]]".

==Career==
In 2015, they won the Emerging Artist Showcase at the [[Boots and Hearts Music Festival]] and the following year, they were signed to [[Universal Music Canada]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Doolan|first1=Susan|title=Band catches a big break at festival|url=http://www.thebarrieexaminer.com/2016/07/15/band-catches-a-big-break-at-festival|access-date=March 25, 2017|work=[[The Barrie Examiner]]|publisher=[[Postmedia Network]]|date=July 15, 2016}}</ref> The band's debut [[extended play]], ''[[Game On (EP)|Game On]]'', was released April 21, 2017.<ref>{{cite news|title=James Barker Band: Video + EP Release Date|url=http://www.iheartradio.ca/new-country-94/news/james-barker-band-video-ep-release-date-1.2493419|access-date=March 25, 2017|work=[[iHeartRadio]]}}</ref> The band received two [[Juno Award]] nominations for [[Juno Award for Breakthrough Group of the Year|Breakthrough Group of the Year]] and [[Juno Award for Country Album of the Year|Country Album of the Year]] at the [[Juno Awards of 2018|2018 Juno Awards]],<ref>[http://www.cbc.ca/music/junos/junos-news/arcade-fire-and-daniel-caesar-lead-2018-juno-award-nominations-1.4520940 "Arcade Fire and Daniel Caesar lead 2018 Juno Award nominations"]. [[CBC Music]], February 6, 2018.</ref> winning the second.<ref>[https://www.cbc.ca/music/junos/news/junos-2018-the-complete-list-of-winners-1.4579405 "Junos 2018: the complete list of winners"]. ''CBC News'',  · March 25, 2018</ref> In 2018, the band's song "[[Chills (James Barker Band song)|Chills]]", cowritten by Barker, Gavin Slate, Travis Wood and [[Donovan Woods (musician)|Donovan Woods]], received a [[SOCAN Songwriting Prize]] nomination<ref>[http://exclaim.ca/music/article/2018_socan_songwriting_prize_nominees_revealed "2018 SOCAN Songwriting Prize Nominees Revealed"]. ''[[Exclaim!]]'', May 24, 2018.</ref> and won the award for Single of the Year at the [[Canadian Country Music Awards]].<ref>[https://www.cbc.ca/news/entertainment/2018-canadian-country-music-awards-shania-twain-hamilton-1.4816712 "Host Shania Twain is big winner at 2018 Canadian Country Music Awards"]. [[CBC News]], September 9, 2018.</ref><ref>[https://www.thechronicleherald.ca/living/the-best-nova-scotia-concerts-of-2018-270134/ "The best Nova Scotia concerts of 2018"]. ''Chronicle Herald'', Stephen Cooke, Dec 20, 2018</ref>

one.<ref name="Canada Country"/> In October 2021, they released the song "[[New Old Trucks]]", which features American country singer [[Dierks Bentley]].<ref>{{cite Trucks"|date=October 8, 2021|access-date=October 12, 2021|work=The Country Note}}</ref> They followed that up with "[[Wastin' Whiskey]]" in 2022 on Starseed Records.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://canadianbeats.ca/2022/03/29/james-barker-band-delivers-what-could-be-another-1-single-with-the-release-of-wastin-whiskey/|title=James Barker Band delivers what could be another #1 single with "[[Meet Your Mama]]".<ref name="REC">{{cite web|url=https://musicrow.com/2023/01/james-barker-band-joins-records-nashvilles-growing-roster/|title=James Barker Band Joins Records Nashville's Growing released on October 20, 2023.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://musicrow.com/2023/09/james-barker-band-pops-champagne-for-upcoming-ep-ahead-of-our-time/|title=James Barker Band Pops 'Champagne' For Upcoming
