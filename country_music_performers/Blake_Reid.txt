'''Blake Reid''' (born and raised in Cremona, Alberta) is a Canadian [[country music]] and roots singer-songwriter.  To date he has released 2 albums as a solo artist.

==Career==

Reid's first album 'Against the Grain' was released independently in 2012. Reid released 'Rust' in 2015, distributed by [[Sony Music Canada]].  In 2017, he formed the 'Blake Reid Band' and is featured in the award-winning full-length film/music documentary 'No Roads In' with respective 'No Roads In' album set to be released 2018.<ref>{{cite web |title=No Roads In |url=http://www.cbc.ca/documentarychannel/docs/no-roads-in |website=CBC CA |accessdate=5 August 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web   |title=Five Questions with Blake Reid|url=http://canadianbeats.ca/2017/11/02/five-questions-with-blake-reid-band/ |website=Canadian Beats |date=2 November 2017 |accessdate=5 August 2018}}</ref> Reid has been touring throughout 2018.<ref>{{cite web |title=Country singer Blake Reid charms Cold Lake |url=http://coldlakesun.com/2018/01/22/country-singer-blake-reid-charms-cold-lake |website=Coldlake Sun |accessdate=5 August 2018 }}{{Dead link|date=July 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> Their film and album were created in High River Alberta, Canada (in an old, unused farm house).<ref>{{cite web |last1=Collie |first1=Doug |title=Singer returns to his roots in Olds |url=https://www.oldsalbertan.ca/article/singer-returns-to-his-roots-in-olds-20180220 |website=Olds Albertan |accessdate=5 August 2018}}</ref>

==Style==

Some of Reid's music reflects his Alberta roots, particularly the song 'Fuel, Fertilizer and John Deere'<ref>{{cite journal |title=Corb lund and the Albertan Fossil Fuel energy Debate |journal=American Music |volume=34 |issue=1 |pages=43–86 |jstor=10.5406/americanmusic.34.1.0043 |doi=10.5406/americanmusic.34.1.0043 |year=2016 |last1=Watson |s2cid=192383186 }}</ref>
