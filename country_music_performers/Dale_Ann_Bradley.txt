'''Dale Ann Bradley''' is an American [[bluegrass music]]ian. She is a six-time (2007, 2008, 2009, 2011, 2012, and 2021) Female Bluegrass Vocalist of the Year, a distinction given by the [[International Bluegrass Music Association]].<ref>{{cite news | last =Halsey | first =Derek | title =Five-time award-winning vocalist Dale Ann Bradley brings all-star band to the Mountaineer Opry | newspaper =The Herald Dispatch| date =March 13, 2014 | url =http://www.herald-dispatch.com/features/x1313365424/Five-time-award-winning-vocalist-Dale-Ann-Bradley-brings-all-star-band-to-the-Mountaineer-Opry | accessdate =March 19, 2014 }}</ref> She has released music both as a solo artist and as part of the group New Coon Creek Girls.<ref name="CMTBio">{{cite web | title =About Dale Ann Bradley | publisher =CMT | url =http://www.cmt.com/artists/dale-ann-bradley/biography/ | archive-url =https://web.archive.org/web/20140116074706/http://www.cmt.com/artists/dale-ann-bradley/biography/ | url-status =dead | archive-date =January 16, 2014 | accessdate =March 25, 2014 }}</ref>

==Early life==

Bradley was born in [[Bell County, Kentucky|Bell County]], Kentucky. Her father was a coal-mining Baptist minister.<ref name="NPRDaABrad">{{cite news | title =Dale Ann Bradley On Mountain Stage | newspaper =NPR | date =June 9, 2009 | url =https://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=106420668| accessdate =March 19, 2014 }}</ref> She grew up without running water or electricity until she was a senior in high school.<ref name="SouthMaryNews">{{cite news | title =A 'Women in Bluegrass Spectacular' will feature Dale Ann Bradley, Most Recent Female Vocalist | newspaper =Southern Maryland News | date =February 18, 2014 | url =http://smnewsnet.com/archives/93464/ | accessdate =March 25, 2014 }}</ref> She also lived with heavy religious restrictions with her father being a minister. She received her first guitar at the age of 14, making a guitar pick out of a plastic milk carton to play.<ref name="SouthMaryNews"/>

As a junior in high school, Bradley met a childhood friend of her mother who was also her new band director at school.<ref name="SouthMaryNews"/> He and his wife sang at [[F. D. Roosevelt State Park|Pine Mountain State Park]], located in Pineville, Kentucky, in the summers and invited Bradley to perform with them. She played with the band (Backporch Grass) and recorded a few singles and had the opportunity to perform in front of live audiences.<ref name="SouthMaryNews"/>

==Career==

Bradley auditioned for the New Coon Creek Girls in 1988 but was unsuccessful.<ref name="CMTBio"/> For the next couple of years, she worked as a solo artist before finally joining the group in 1991.<ref name="CMTBio"/> She released her first solo album, East Kentucky Morning, in 1997. The album was top ten on both the Bluegrass Unlimited chart and the Gavin Americana chart.<ref name="CMTBio"/> In early 2015, Bradley left her record label [[Compass Records]] for Pinecastle Records, with whom she recorded her first solo album.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.daleann.com/ |title=Dale Ann Bradley & Coon Creek |website=www.daleann.com |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/19991104050852/http://daleann.com/ |archive-date=1999-11-04}}</ref> In late 2015, her 7th album, Pocket Full of Keys received a Grammy nomination for Best Bluegrass Album  <ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.grammy.com/nominees?genre=59|title=Awards Nominations & Winners|date=April 30, 2017}}</ref>

In 2019, Bradley joined a new bluegrass band known as Sister Sadie and their self-titled debut album received a [[Grammy Award for Best Bluegrass Album]] nomination.
