'''Hal Willis''' (born '''Léonald Francis Gauthier'''; July 15, 1933 – September 4, 2015) was a Canadian country singer who lived in [[Nashville, Tennessee]], USA. He was born in [[Rouyn-Noranda|Rouyn-Noranda, Quebec]] the youngest son of Alfred and Evelina Gauthier.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.musicrow.com/2015/09/lifenotes-canadian-country-star-hal-willis-passes/ |title=LifeNotes: Canadian Country Star Hal Willis Passes |work=MusicRow – Nashville's Music Industry Publication – News, Songs From Music City|date=September 9, 2015 }}</ref>

==Career==
His famous country single was "[[The Lumberjack (Hal Willis song)|The Lumberjack]]," an international hit that sold over 1.5&nbsp;million copies. In 1965, it peaked at number 5 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' country charts in the USA. This success was such a phenomenon that he was nicknamed "Mr. Lumberjack."

His popularity started rising in 1956 when he and his wife, Ginger Willis, were hired by [[Colonel Tom Parker]] to tour with the music phenomenon of the hour, [[Elvis Presley]]. At this time, Hal and Ginger were also [[rock and roll]] singers recording songs like "My Pink Cadillac" and "Bop a Dee Bop a Doo." Hal and Ginger, along with [[Hank Snow]], were the only Canadian performers to tour with [[Elvis Presley]]. He also performed and toured through his career with many American [[Country Music Hall of Fame and Museum|Country Music Hall of Fame]] singers such as [[Patsy Cline]], [[Webb Pierce]], [[Loretta Lynn]], [[Dolly Parton]], [[Porter Wagoner]], [[Johnny Cash]] and [[Willie Nelson]].

Hal and Ginger Willis were inducted into the [[Canadian Country Music Hall of Fame]] in 2010.<ref name="ccma">{{cite web |url=http://www.ccma.org/halloffame/hofprofile.cfm?AwardID=809 |title=Hal & Ginger Willis biography |work=[[Canadian Country Music Association]] |access-date=November 1, 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110929074708/http://www.ccma.org/halloffame/hofprofile.cfm?AwardID=809 |archive-date=September 29, 2011 }}</ref>

On May 27, 2012, Hal Willis was also inducted to the Quebec Museum of Rock & Roll through a ceremony held in Rouyn-Noranda, Quebec, Canada at the international ''Festival des guitares du monde de l'Abitibi-Témiscamingue''.

In March 2013, at the age of 79 years, Hal Willis released a new single "Working in the Goldmine".

==Death==
Willis died in [[Nashville, Tennessee]] on September 4, 2015.<ref>{{cite magazine |last1=Oermann |first1=Robert K. |title=LifeNotes: Canadian Country Star Hal Willis Passes |url=https://musicrow.com/2015/09/lifenotes-canadian-country-star-hal-willis-passes/ |magazine=MusicRow |accessdate=September 13, 2018 |date=September 9, 2015}}</ref>
