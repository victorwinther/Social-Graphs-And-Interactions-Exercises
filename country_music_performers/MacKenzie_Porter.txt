'''MacKenzie Lea Porter''' (born January 29, 1990<ref>{{cite tweet|user=MacKenziePMusic|author=MacKenzie Porter|number=817896236346961920|date=January 7, 2017|title=Love all the birthday wishes but it's actually not my birthday!! jan 29th}}</ref>) is a Canadian [[country music|country]] singer, songwriter, and actress. She has released two albums (''MacKenzie Porter'' and ''[[Nobody's Born with a Broken Heart]]'') and achieved four [[Canada Country]] number ones with "[[About You (MacKenzie Porter song)|About You]]", "[[These Days (MacKenzie Porter song)|These Days]]", "[[Seeing Other People (song)|Seeing Other People]]", and "[[Pickup (song)|Pickup]]". She is featured on [[Dustin Lynch]]'s number one [[Country Airplay]] hit "[[Thinking 'Bout You (Dustin Lynch song)|Thinking 'Bout You]]". As an actor she is best known for her leading role as a future-sent medic in the television series ''[[Travelers (TV series)|Travelers]]''.

==Early life==
MacKenzie Porter was raised on a cattle and bison ranch near [[Medicine Hat|Medicine Hat, Alberta]].<ref name="ottawa">{{cite news|last=Pape|first=Hendrik|title=MacKenzie Porter almost ready to take off|url=https://www.soundcheckentertainment.ca/mackenzie-porter-almost-ready-to-take-off/|newspaper=Sound Check Entertainment|date=August 10, 2011}}</ref> She began studying piano, violin, and voice at age four.<ref name="ottawa" /> While growing up she toured in a family band with her brother, 2004 ''[[Canadian Idol]]'' winner [[Kalan Porter]].<ref name="ottawa" /> Their grandfather [[Robert Harold Porter|Bob Porter]] was [[Member of Parliament (Canada)|Member of Parliament]] for [[Medicine Hat (federal electoral district)|Medicine Hat]] from 1988 to 1993.

==Film and TV career==
Porter has appeared in numerous films and television productions, including ''[[Dinosapien]]'' (2007), ''[[Hell on Wheels (TV series)|Hell on Wheels]]'', and ''[[Travelers (TV series)|Travelers]]'' (2016). She earned a Best Actress in Alberta award for her role in the 2008 television film ''The Other Woman''.<ref name="ctv">{{cite news|last=Block|first=Sheri|title='The Horses of McBride' a perfect fit for Alberta actress MacKenzie Porter|url=http://www.ctv.ca/2012HolidayProgrammingGuide/article/e2809cThe-Horses-of-McBridee2809d-a-perfect-fit-for-Alberta-actress-MacKenzie-Porter|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121215214552/http://www.ctv.ca/2012HolidayProgrammingGuide/article/e2809cThe-Horses-of-McBridee2809d-a-perfect-fit-for-Alberta-actress-MacKenzie-Porter|url-status=dead|archive-date=December 15, 2012|access-date=August 21, 2013|newspaper=[[CTV Television Network|CTV]]|date=December 14, 2012}}</ref> She was also named as one of the Top 11 Hottest Vancouver Actors to Watch for in 2011 by ''BC Living'' magazine.<ref>{{cite news|last=Leary|first=Joe|title=Top 11 Hottest Vancouver Actors to Watch for 2011 Recap|url=http://www.bcliving.ca/entertainment/top-11-hottest-vancouver-actors-to-watch-for-2011-recap|access-date=August 21, 2013|newspaper=BC Living|date=February 16, 2012}}</ref>

===2018–2022: ''Drinkin' Songs'', Big Loud Records, five number ones===
In 2018, Porter signed with [[Nashville]]-based [[Big Loud]] and became their first Canadian artist.<ref>{{cite news|title=MacKenzie Porter Returns with Announcement of New Music|url=https://bigloudrecords.com/mackenzie-porter-returns-with-announcement-of-new-music/|date=November 30, 2018|work=[[Big Loud Records]]|access-date=March 20, 2021}}</ref> She released the single "[[About You (MacKenzie Porter song)|About You]]", co-written by [[Hardy (singer)|Hardy]]. The song would hit number one on [[Canada Country]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Bickell|first=Dutch|title=Mackenzie Porter celebrates her first number one song "About You"|url=https://canadianbeats.ca/2019/05/23/mackenzie-porter-celebrates-her-first-number-one-song-about-you/|date=May 23, 2019|work=Canadian Beats Media|access-date=January 7, 2021}}</ref> Porter is featured on the [[Dallas Smith]] song "Friends Don't Let Friends Drink Alone" with [[Dean Brody]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.completecountry.ca/article.php?article_id=4562|title=Dallas Smith Releases 'The Fall'|date=22 March 2019|website=Complete Country|access-date=20 March 2021|archive-date=6 August 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200806192928/https://www.completecountry.ca/article.php?article_id=4562|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In March 2019, Porter released the single "[[These Days (MacKenzie Porter song)|These Days]]", which charted on Canadian country, pop, and adult hits charts as well as on the US [[Hot Country Songs]] and [[Country Airplay]] charts.<ref name="FYI">{{cite news|first=Kerry|last=Doole|title=Music News Digest, June 15, 2020|url=https://www.fyimusicnews.ca/articles/2020/06/15/music-news-digest-june-15-2020|access-date=July 10, 2020|work=FYI Music News|date=June 15, 2020}}</ref> It also reached number one on [[Canada Country]], making Porter the first Canadian woman to land back-to-back Number Ones since [[Shania Twain]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thecountrynote.com/in-the-news/mackenzie-porter-makes-her-u-s-country-radio-debut-with-these-days/|title=Mackenzie Porter Makes Her U.S. Country Radio Debut with "These Days"|work=The Country Note|date=April 27, 2020|access-date=July 10, 2020}}</ref> She then opened for [[Dallas Smith]] and [[Dean Brody]] on their co-headlining cross-Canada ''Friends Don't Let Friends Tour Alone Tour''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Dean Brody's New Single, New Album and New Tour|first=Randy|last=Owen|url=https://www.country1011.com/2019/08/21/dean-brodys-new-single-new-album-and-new-tour/|work=101.1 Country|publisher=[[Rogers Media]]|date=August 21, 2019}}</ref> In February 2020, Porter released "[[Seeing Other People (song)|Seeing Other People]]" which would become her third consecutive number one, making her the first woman (Canadian or American) to land three consecutive Number Ones on Canadian country radio since Twain.<ref>{{cite web|first=Jason|last=Scott|url=https://americansongwriter.com/dustin-lynch-mackenzie-porter-thinking-bout-you-song-interview/|title=Dustin Lynch Revamps "Thinking 'Bout You" With MacKenzie Porter|work=American Songwriter|date=March 19, 2021}}</ref>

Porter's EP ''[[Drinkin' Songs: The Collection]]'' was released in November 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://vancouversun.com/entertainment/5-things-to-know-about-drinkin-songs-the-collection-by-mackenzie-porter|title= 5 things to know about Drinkin' Songs: The Collection by MacKenzie Porter|work=[[Vancouver Sun]]|first=Stuart|last=Derdeyn|date=November 18, 2020}}</ref> The EP was her debut American release and included her three chart-toppers in addition to her top five hit "[[Drinkin' Songs]]".<ref>{{cite web|title= REVIEW: Mackenzie Porter – Drinkin' Songs – The Collection|url=https://www.offtherecorduk.com/review-mackenzie-porter-drinkin-songs-the-collection/ |work=Off the Record UK|date=November 6, 2020}}</ref> It received a nomination for Country Album of the Year at the [[2021 Juno Awards]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://medicinehatnews.com/news/local-news/2021/03/12/mackenzie-porter-nominated-for-best-country-album-at-junos/|title=Mackenzie Porter nominated for best country album at Junos|work=Medicine Hat News|first=Collin|last=Gallant|date=March 12, 2021}}</ref> In March 2021, she featured on the [[Dustin Lynch]] single "[[Thinking 'Bout You (Dustin Lynch song)|Thinking 'Bout You]]",<ref name="TBY">{{cite news|title= Dustin Lynch Teams With MacKenzie Porter for a Conversational New Version of 'Thinking 'Bout You' [Listen]|first=Carena|last=Liptak|url=https://tasteofcountry.com/dustin-lynch-mackenzie-porter-thinking-bout-you/|work=Taste of Country|date=March 20, 2021}}</ref> and then joined Canadian pop artist [[Virginia to Vegas]] on the single "[[This Sucks.]]" in September 2021.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.fyimusicnews.ca/articles/2021/09/19/virginia-vegas-mackenzie-porter-earn-strong-radio-support|title=Virginia To Vegas & MacKenzie Porter Earn Strong Radio Support|work=FYI Music News|date=September 19, 2021}}</ref> She released the song "[[Pickup (song)|Pickup]]" later that year; it became a single in early 2022.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.discovermoosejaw.com/country-100/fresh-music-friday-song-of-the-day-mackenzie-porter-pickup|title=Fresh Music Friday Song Of The Day: MacKenzie Porter- "Pickup"|first=Courtney|last=Fielder|work=Country 100|date=February 4, 2022}}</ref> "[[Thinking 'Bout You (Dustin Lynch song)|Thinking 'Bout You]]" spent six weeks atop the [[Country Airplay]] chart, marking Porter's first number one in the U.S., and spent a record-breaking 28 weeks in the top ten.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/chart-beat/country-radio-chart-slowdown-dustin-lynch-tops-country-airplay-1235062380/|title=Country Radio's Chart Slowdown: 'We Must Still Take Chances … or We Will Become Irrelevant'|author=Jim Asker|date=April 22, 2022|publisher=Billboard|access-date=April 24, 2022}}</ref> Meanwhile, "[[Pickup (song)|Pickup]]" would go on to reach number one on Canada Country,<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/canada-country/2022-06-18|title=Canada Country chart for June 18, 2022|magazine=Billboard |access-date=June 14, 2022}}</ref> and achieve gold-certification.<ref name="MC certs"/> Later that year, she joined fellow Canadian country artist [[Dallas Smith]] on the single "[[One Too]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://countrynow.com/exclusive-premiere-dallas-smith-releases-music-video-for-one-too-with-mackenzie-porter/|title=Exclusive Premiere: Dallas Smith Releases Music Video For 'One Too' With MacKenzie Porter|first=Melinda|last=Lorge|date=June 22, 2022|work=Country Now|access-date=August 15, 2022}}</ref>

===2023–present: ''Nobody's Born with a Broken Heart''===
In March 2023, Porter participated in an all-star recording of [[Serena Ryder]]'s single "[[What I Wouldn't Do]]", which was released as a charity single to benefit [[Kids Help Phone]]'s Feel Out Loud campaign for youth mental health.<ref>Brent Furdyk, [https://web.archive.org/web/20230302170512/https://etcanada.com/news/972900/feel-out-loud-alessia-cara-serena-ryder-more-canadian-artists-collaborate-on-new-single-promoting-youth-mental-health-initiative/ "Feel Out Loud: Alessia Cara, Serena Ryder & More Canadian Artists Collaborate On New Single Promoting Youth Mental Health Initiative"]. ''[[Entertainment Tonight Canada]]'', March 2, 2023.</ref> In May 2023, she released "[[Chasing Tornadoes]]", the second single off her upcoming full-length album.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://entertainment-focus.com/2023/05/06/mackenzie-porter-releases-the-lainey-wilson-penned-chasing-tornadoes-from-her-upcoming-debut-album/|title=MacKenzie Porter releases the Lainey Wilson-penned 'Chasing Tornadoes' from her upcoming debut album|date=May 6, 2023|first=James|last=Daykin|work=Entertainment Focus|access-date=May 7, 2023}}</ref> In the fall of 2023, Porter embarked on her first tour named "The Canadian Headlining Tour".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://canadianbeats.ca/2023/09/24/mackenzie-porter-shares-upcoming-tour-dates/|title=MACKENZIE PORTER SHARES UPCOMING TOUR DATES|first=Nanci|last=Dagg|work=Canadian Beats Media|date=September 24, 2023|access-date=January 26, 2024}}</ref> While on tour, she released the single "[[Bet You Break My Heart]]".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.circleallaccess.com/circles-release-of-the-week-mackenzie-porters-bet-you-break-my-heart/|title=Circle’s Release of the Week: MacKenzie Porter’s "Bet You Break My Heart"|work=Circle All Access|first=Megan|last=Grisham|date=November 17, 2023|access-date=January 26, 2024}}</ref> Porter also joined Canadian pop singer [[Elijah Woods x Jamie Fine#Elijah Woods|Elijah Woods]] on a new version of his song "Last Girl".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://canadianbeats.ca/2023/11/07/elijah-woods-unveils-new-version-of-last-girl-ft-mackenzie-porter/|title=ELIJAH WOODS UNVEILS NEW VERSION OF "LAST GIRL" FT. MACKENZIE PORTER|first=Jenna|last=Melanson|work=Canadian Beats Media|date=November 7, 2023|access-date=January 26, 2024}}</ref>

In January 2024, Porter announced the release of her second full-length studio album, ''[[Nobody's Born with a Broken Heart]]'' on Big Loud Records.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.countrystandardtime.com/news/newsitem.asp?xid=13514|title=Porter finds "Nobody's Born With A Broken Heart,"|work=Country Standard Time|date=January 26, 2024}}</ref> The nineteen-track album was released on April 26, 2024, and includes the singles "[[Pickup (song)|Pickup]]", "[[Chasing Tornadoes]]", and "Bet You Break My Heart", as well as four songs that were released concurrently with the album's announcement, including "Easy to Miss".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://int-www.breakfasttelevision.ca/videos/canadian-country-singer-mackenzie-porter-performs-her-new-song-easy-to-miss/|title=Canadian Country Singer MacKenzie Porter performs her new song "Easy to Miss"|date=January 26, 2024|work=[[Breakfast Television]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://countrytown.com/news/mackenzie-porter-announces-debut-album-nobody-s-born-with-a-broken-heart/9IRK5uno6-o/27-01-24|title=MacKenzie Porter Announces Debut Album 'Nobody’s Born With A Broken Heart'|first=Mary|last=Varvaris|work=Countrytown|date=January 27, 2024|access-date=January 31, 2024}}</ref> Porter received six nominations at the [[2024 Canadian Country Music Awards]], and won Female Artist of the Year.<ref name="CCMAXXIV"/> She also co-hosted the award show alongside [[Thomas Rhett]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ca.billboard.com/music/country/american-country-star-thomas-rhett-will-host-2024-ccma-awards-with-mackenzie-porter|title=American Country Star Thomas Rhett Will Host 2024 CCMA Awards with MacKenzie Porter|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] Canada|date=April 9, 2024|access-date=September 11, 2024}}</ref>
