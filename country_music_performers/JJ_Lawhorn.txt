'''Jonathan Wayne "JJ" Lawhorn''' (born September 1993) in [[Hanover County, Virginia]]) is an American [[country music]] singer-songwriter. Lawhorn was discovered online by producer [[Jeremy Stover]] in 2010.<ref name="theboot">{{cite news|last=Whitaker|first=Sterling|title=JJ Lawhorn Interview: Newcomer Talks Debut Album, 'Original Good Ol' Boy'|url=http://theboot.com/jj-lawhorn-interview-2013/|access-date=March 4, 2014|newspaper=The Boot|date=July 17, 2013}}</ref> He signed with [[EMI Music Publishing]] within a year.<ref name="gac">{{cite news|last=Doerschuk|first=Bob|title=New Artist Spotlight: JJ Lawhorn|url=http://blog.gactv.com/blog/2013/07/24/new-artist-spotlight-jj-lawhorn/|access-date=March 4, 2014|newspaper=[[Great American Country]]|date=July 24, 2013}}</ref> He signed a recording contract with [[Average Joes Entertainment]], a record label owned by [[Colt Ford]], in June 2011 when he was 17 years old.<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|last=Leggett|first=Steve|title=JJ Lawhorn {{!}} Biography|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=mn0002738497/biography|pure_url=yes}}|work=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=March 4, 2014}}</ref>

Lawhorn's debut album, ''Original Good Ol' Boy'', was released by Average Joes on July 16, 2013.<ref name="theboot" /> The album was produced by Stover.<ref name="gac" /> Lawhorn wrote or co-wrote all thirteen tracks.<ref name="allmusic" /> Matt Bjorke of ''Roughstock'' gave the album four stars out of five, calling Lawhorn "an artist well worth seeking out."<ref>{{cite news|last=Bjorke|first=Matt|title=Album Review: JJ Lawhorn – Original Good Ol' Boy|url=http://www.roughstock.com/reviews/album-review-jj-lawhorn-original-good-ol-boy|access-date=March 4, 2014|newspaper=Roughstock|date=July 16, 2013|archive-url=https://archive.today/20140304190936/http://www.roughstock.com/reviews/album-review-jj-lawhorn-original-good-ol-boy|archive-date=March 4, 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> The album sold 4,000 copies in its first week of release, debuting at number 20 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Top Country Albums]] chart and number 91 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Bjorke|first=Matt|title=Country Album Chart News: The Week of July 24, 2013: Travis Tritt, Luke Bryan, Florida Georgia Line, Jason Aldean, Blake Shelton, Hunter Hayes, Mud Digger 4|url=http://www.roughstock.com/blog/country-album-chart-news-the-week-of-july-24-2013-travis-tritt-luke-bryan-florida-georgia-line-jason-aldean-blake-shelton-hunter-hayes-mud-digger-4|access-date=March 4, 2014|newspaper=Roughstock|date=July 24, 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131005103309/http://www.roughstock.com/blog/country-album-chart-news-the-week-of-july-24-2013-travis-tritt-luke-bryan-florida-georgia-line-jason-aldean-blake-shelton-hunter-hayes-mud-digger-4|archive-date=October 5, 2013}}</ref>

The single "Good Ol' Boys Like Us" was released to country radio on February 17, 2014.<ref>{{cite news|last=Thompson|first=Gayle|title=JJ Lawhorn Releasing New Single|url=http://theboot.com/jj-lawhorn-good-ol-boys-like-us/|access-date=March 4, 2014|newspaper=The Boot|date=February 13, 2014}}</ref> The song received a favorable review from Taste of Country, which called it "a great lyric and a strong vocal performance."<ref>{{cite news|title=JJ Lawhorn – 'Good Ol' Boys Like Us' [Listen]|url=http://tasteofcountry.com/jj-lawhorn-good-ol-boys-like-us/|access-date=March 4, 2014|newspaper=Taste of Country|date=February 17, 2014}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Lawhorn graduated from [[Hanover High School (Mechanicsville, Virginia)|Hanover High School]] in [[Mechanicsville, Virginia]].
