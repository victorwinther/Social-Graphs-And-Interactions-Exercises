'''Caylee Anna Hammack''' (born March 19, 1994<ref>{{cite tweet |title=I can't explain the sheer excitement I am tingling with while we drive to our show at the @CocaColaRoxy tonight with @JonPardi & @949TheBull ATL HERE WE COME! I've been waiting to celebrate my birthday tonight in my home state | url=https://twitter.com/Cayleehammack/status/1372946933770248196 | user=Cayleehammack | number=1372946933770248196 | via=[[Twitter]] | date=March 19, 2021}}</ref>) is an American [[country music]] singer-songwriter. She is signed to [[Capitol Records Nashville]] and released her debut album ''[[If It Wasn't for You]]'' on August 14, 2020. She is signed to Red Light Management and is managed by Mary Hilliard Harrington.

==Early life==
Hammack started playing music at age 13.<ref name=Know>{{cite web | last=Liptak | first=Carena | title=Who Is Caylee Hammack? 5 Things You Need to Know |url=https://theboot.com/who-is-caylee-hammack/ | website=The Boot| date=February 28, 2020 }}</ref> While not getting much of a music education or vocal lessons at a young age, she self-taught by listening and singing songs on the country radio.<ref name="allaccessinterview">{{cite web |title=10 Questions with ... Caylee Hammack | url=https://www.allaccess.com/country/10-questions/archive/29911/10-questions-with-caylee-hammack | website=All Access}}</ref> The first time she had to turn down potential tennis scholarships was at age 15, after a surgery to remove a tumor. She wrote her first song, "Addictive", about the pain pills she had to take after the surgery.<ref name=Emerging>{{cite magazine | title=Emerging Country Artist Spotlight: Caylee Hammack | url=https://www.billboard.com/music/country/caylee-hammack-rising-country-artist-spotlight-8503871/ | last=Reuter | first=Annie | magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | date=March 25, 2019}}</ref>

While her brother played [[Southern rock]] and her sister [[Britney Spears]] songs, she discovered the country genre for herself and counts [[the Chicks]] and [[SHeDAISY]] as her early inspirations.<ref name=Unwavering>{{cite news | title=Who Is Caylee Hammack? An Unwavering Artist Forged in Fire | url=https://tasteofcountry.com/who-is-caylee-hammack/ | first=Cillea | last=Houghton | work=[[Taste of Country]] | date=November 26, 2019}}</ref>

She received a full music scholarship to [[Belmont University]] in Nashville at age 18, but declined because her boyfriend persuaded her to stay in Ellaville with him. After she broke up with her boyfriend and was noticed by [[Luke Bryan]],<ref>{{cite magazine | title=Caylee Hammack, Cindy Mabe & More Featured as Speakers at 2019 CMA EDU Executive Summit | url=https://www.billboard.com/music/country/caylee-hammack-cindy-mabe-2019-cma-edu-executive-summit-8523979/ | last=Reuter | first=Annie | magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | date=July 25, 2019}}</ref> she moved to Nashville at the end of 2013 with about $1,000 of savings, a high school diploma, and her clothes in trash bags.<ref name=Strength>{{cite magazine | title=Country Singer-Songwriter Caylee Hammack on Her Love of David Bowie, Finding Strength as an Artist | url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/caylee-hammack-country-singer-morning-sessions-868494/ | magazine=[[Rolling Stone]] | date=August 7, 2019 | url-access=limited}}</ref> She ran out of money and slept in her car. With a fake ID, she entered the music venues and bars and, after asking a bass player at the Honky Tonk Central on [[Broadway (Nashville, Tennessee)|Broadway]], she started to sing [[cover version]]s there weekly.<ref name=Unwavering/><ref name=Strength/> Before signing a [[recording contract]], she performed several original songs, including "Redhead".<ref>{{cite news | title=Caylee Hammack's Best Songs Are All Originals | url=https://tasteofcountry.com/caylee-hammacks-songs/ | last=Dukes | first=Billy | work=[[Taste of Country]] | date=January 4, 2020}}</ref>

==Career==
After about two years of playing and writing songs, she was introduced to [[Universal Music Publishing Group]] by Robert Filhart, where she started as a staff writer.<ref name=Landing/> During a writers' retreat, she found out that her house in Nashville had burned down due to an electrical fire that destroyed about 70% of her belongings. [[Tenille Townes]] was at the retreat with her and helped her during this time which was the start of their friendship.<ref name="allaccessinterview" />

In 2018, she signed a recording contract with Capitol Nashville.<ref name=Landing>{{cite magazine |last=Reuter | first=Annie | title=How Caylee Hammack Went From Sleeping In Her Car To Landing a Record Deal | url=https://www.billboard.com/music/country/caylee-hammack-capitol-records-nashville-album-interview-9344053/ | magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | date=March 27, 2020}}</ref>

Her debut song "Family Tree" was released on January 18, 2019, and was the most-added debut song by a female artist at country radio in the previous three years.<ref>{{cite news | title=Caylee Hammack Earns Highest Female Country Radio Debut In Three Years | url=https://musicrow.com/2019/04/caylee-hammack-earns-highest-female-country-radio-debut-in-three-years/ | last=Nicholson | first=Jessica | work=Musicrow | date=April 15, 2019}}</ref> On May 29, 2019 she performed the song on the ''[[Today (American TV program)|Today]]'' show in her national television debut.<ref>{{cite magazine | title=See Caylee Hammack's Swaggering 'Family Tree' on 'Today' | url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/caylee-hammack-family-tree-today-show-841574/ | last=Freeman | first=Jon | magazine=[[Rolling Stone]] | date=May 29, 2019 | url-access=limited}}</ref>

In 2019, she was the [[opening act]] for [[Lanco (band)|Lanco]], [[Dierks Bentley]], [[Trisha Yearwood]], and [[Miranda Lambert]].<ref>{{cite news | last=Thompson | first=Gayle | title=Miranda Lambert Praised by Tour Opener, Caylee Hammack: 'She's a Saint' (Exclusive) | url=https://popculture.com/country-music/news/miranda-lambert-praised-tour-opener-caylee-hammack-shes-saint/ | work=[[PopCulture.com]] | date=November 15, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news | title=Trisha Yearwood Praises Caylee Hammack Ahead of Every Girl Tour | url=https://tasteofcountry.com/trisha-yearwood-praises-caylee-hammack/ | last=Dukes | first=Billy | work=[[Taste of Country]] | date=November 27, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine | title=Caylee Hammack Joins Dierks Bentley On Tour |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/caylee-hammack-joins-dierks-bentley-on-tour-908689/ | magazine=[[Rolling Stone]] | date=November 6, 2019 | url-access=limited}}</ref>

Hammack wrote "Small Town Hypocrite" while still being a staff writer, together with Jared Scott, about the breakup with her boyfriend who cheated on her, which eventually brought her to Nashville. Later she found out that he lived in a double-wide trailer, which made its way into the song's final verse.<ref>{{cite news | title=Caylee Hammack Explains the Writing Behind "Small Town Hypocrite" | url=https://americansongwriter.com/caylee-hammack-small-town-hypocrite-behind-the-song/ | last=Beviglia | first=Jim | work=[[American Songwriter]] | date=April 20, 2020}}</ref> The song was released on February 21, 2020 <ref>{{cite news | title=Caylee Hammack Releases New Single, 'Small Town Hypocrite' | url=https://www.bellesandgals.com/2020/02/21/caylee-hammack-releases-new-single-small-town-hypocrite/ | last=Roberts | first=Megan | work=Belles and Gals | date=February 21, 2020}}</ref> and received praise from Billy Dukes of Taste of Country calling it "a product of courageous songwriting".<ref>{{cite web |last1=Dukes |first1=Billy|title=Caylee Hammack's 'Small Town Hypocrite' Is Courageous Songwriting |url=https://tasteofcountry.com/caylee-hammacks-small-town-hypocrite-a-product-of-courageous-songwriting/ |website=Taste of Country |access-date=14 April 2023 |language=en |date=3 May 2020}}</ref> She sent a recording to [[Chris Stapleton]], who agreed to sing vocals on the song for a re-release in 2021<ref>{{Cite news | url=https://tasteofcountry.com/caylee-hammack-chris-stapleton-small-town-hypocrite/ | title=Caylee Hammack Taps Chris Stapleton for a Turbo-Charged 'Small Town Hypocrite' [Listen] | first=Carena | last=Liptak | work=[[Taste of Country]] | date=April 29, 2021}}</ref>

In February 2020, Hammack learned about her first ACM nomination for New Female Artist of the Year for the [[55th Academy of Country Music Awards|2020 ACM Awards]] while waiting for a plane at the airport.<ref name=cried>{{cite news | title=Caylee Hammack "cried in an airport bathroom" when she found out about her first-ever ACM nomination | url=https://www.ktlo.com/2020/02/27/caylee-hammack-cried-in-an-airport-bathroom-when-she-found-out-about-her-first-ever-acm-nomination/ | work=[[KTLO-FM]] | date=February 27, 2020}}</ref>

Hammack was scheduled as the opening act for [[Reba McEntire]] and Luke Bryan in 2020 but the tour was shortened due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]]. The opening acts for McEntire were postponed, which left Hammack and her band with no income. During this time they offered a landscaping service to make up for the lost income.<ref>{{cite news | last=M. Hall | first=Kristin | title=Caylee Hammack's band went from concerts to mowing lawns | url=https://apnews.com/article/entertainment-ap-top-news-music-concerts-united-states-e45919cac564fa11a38e4df4d0779f82 | work=[[Associated Press News]] | date=April 1, 2020}}</ref> After the pandemic prevent her from opening for Reba McEntire and Luke Bryan in 2020, she got the chance to open for Luke in 2021 <ref>{{cite web |last1=Black |first1=Lauren Jo |title=Luke Bryan Announces Proud To Be Right Here Tour with Direct Support From Dylan Scott |url=https://countrynow.com/luke-bryan-announces-proud-to-be-right-here-tour-with-direct-support-from-dylan-scott/ |website=Country Now |access-date=14 April 2023 |date=23 April 2021}}</ref> and in 2022 for [[Brad Paisley]] for some of the US stops during his [[List of Brad Paisley concert tours#World Tour 2022 (2022)|World Tour]].<ref>{{cite magazine |last1=Hudak |first1=Joseph |title=Brad Paisley Announces 2022 World Tour Dates |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/brad-paisley-2022-tour-dates-1329341/ |magazine=Rolling Stone |access-date=14 April 2023 |date=29 March 2022}}</ref>

Hammack hosted a Facebook event together with [[Ashley McBryde]] called ''CMT Next Women of Country Goes Live'' on April 7, 2020<ref name="wincountry">{{cite news | title=Ashley McBryde joins "CMT Next Women of Country Goes Live" today |url=https://www.umgnashville.com/news/tune-in-caylee-hammack-hosts-cmt-next-women-of-country-goes-live-w-guest-ashley-mcbryde/ | work=Universal Music Group Nashville | date=April 7, 2020}}</ref> and appeared on ''Country Outdoors LIVE'' special from [[Outdoor Channel]] on May 8.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://musicrow.com/2020/05/the-outdoor-channel-umg-nashville-team-to-present-country-outdoors-live/ | title=The Outdoor Channel, UMG Nashville Team To Present 'Country Outdoors LIVE' | first=Lorie | last=Hollabaugh | work=Music Row | date=May 6, 2020 }}</ref>

Her debut album ''If It Wasn't For You'' was released in August and contains 13 songs. She co-wrote 12 of them and wrote one by herself. The album features duets with Reba McEntire, Ashley McBryde, and Tenille Townes.<ref name=Upcoming/>

On August 13 a new version of "[[On the Road Again (Willie Nelson song)|On the Road Again]]" with [[Willie Nelson]], Hammack, and nine other [[Academy of Country Music]] nominees was published as "On the Road Again (ACM Lifting Lives Edition)" with proceeds from downloads to benefit a COVID-19 fund from ACM.<ref>{{cite magazine |title=Willie Nelson Teams With ACM New Artist Nominees for 'On the Road Again' Remake to Aid COVID-19 Fund | url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/country/9431783/willie-nelson-acm-on-the-road-again |last=Grein |first=Paul | magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |date=August 10, 2020}}</ref>

In February 2023 Caylee announced a second album when releasing the first single "All or Nothing" which she wrote together with Tofer Brown and [[Thomas Finchum]] and produced with [[Dann Huff]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Iahn |first1=Buddy |title=Caylee Hammack drops fierce 'All or Nothing' |url=https://themusicuniverse.com/caylee-hammack-drops-fierce-all-nothing/ |website=The Music Universe |access-date=14 April 2023 |date=3 February 2023}}</ref>

In March, she was part of [[C2C: Country to Country#C2C: Country to Country 2023|C2C: Country to Country 2023]] and had her debut performance in Europe.

==Influences==
Hammack cites Kacey Musgraves, [[David Bowie]], and [[Tom Waits]] as influences in her adult life.<ref name=Strength/>

==Personal life==
In November 2020, Hammack purchased a home in Nashville that needed to be renovated.<ref>{{Cite news | url=https://people.com/country/caylee-hammack-finds-refuge-inspiration-new-nashville-home/ | title=Caylee Hammack Finds a Refuge — and a Flood of Inspiration — Within the Walls of Her New Nashville Home | first=Tricia | last=Despres | work=[[People (magazine)|People]] | date=May 7, 2021}}</ref>
