'''Maggie Marie Sajak''' (born January 5, 1995) is an American television personality and [[country music]] singer. She is the daughter of former ''[[Wheel of Fortune (American game show)|Wheel of Fortune]]'' host [[Pat Sajak]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://todayscountrymusicvideos.com/2012/01/17/new-music-video-from-newcomer-maggie-sajak-watch-the-official-music-video-for-first-kiss/|title=New Music Video From Newcomer Maggie Sajak – Watch The Official Music Video For 'First Kiss|publisher=Todayscountrymusicvideos.com|date=January 17, 2012|accessdate=January 28, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120309085737/http://todayscountrymusicvideos.com/2012/01/17/new-music-video-from-newcomer-maggie-sajak-watch-the-official-music-video-for-first-kiss/|archive-date=March 9, 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref> and photographer Lesly Brown Sajak. She is the on-air social media correspondent for the game show.

==Early life==
Maggie Marie Sajak was born on January 5, 1995, to television host [[Pat Sajak]] and his wife, Lesly. She grew up near [[Severna Park, Maryland]]. She began playing the guitar at age 12.{{cn|date=February 2024}}

==Career==
In 2011, she released her first single, "First Kiss".<ref name=":0">{{cite web |url=http://www.fanlala.com/news/exclusive-interview-maggie-sajak |title=EXCLUSIVE INTERVIEW: Maggie Sajak |publisher=Fanlala.com |date=January 17, 2012 |accessdate=January 24, 2012 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131109144414/http://www.fanlala.com/news/exclusive-interview-maggie-sajak |archivedate=November 9, 2013 }}</ref> The music video for "First Kiss" was directed by [[Trey Fanjoy]] in [[Nashville, Tennessee|Nashville]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cGq3RBxzRmY|title=Maggie Sajak – First Kiss (Official Music Video)|publisher=YouTube.com|accessdate=January 28, 2012}}</ref> Sajak's video was featured on the January 3, 2012, episode of ''[[Wheel of Fortune (U.S. game show)|Wheel of Fortune]]'' on her 17th birthday. In 2013, Sajak recorded a song in Nashville called "Live Out Loud" to honor Muriel Walters, a teenage girl in [[Maryland]] who was battling cancer.<ref>{{cite web |last=Allers |first=Hannahlee |date=October 19, 2013 |title=Maggie Sajak Releases 'Live Out Loud' for Cancer Awareness |url=http://theboot.com/maggie-sajak-live-out-loud/ |accessdate=September 30, 2018 |website=The Boot |language=en}}</ref> Soon afterward, one of her songs was featured in the season finale of a TV show, ''Sweet Home Alabama''.<ref name=":0" />

In 2013, Sajak began her freshman year at [[Princeton University]],<ref name="tdfr2013">{{Cite web |date=2013-08-21 |title=Talking Princeton, AlaÃ¯a, And Country Music With Maggie Sajak |url=https://fashionweekdaily.com/talking-princeton-alaa%C2%AFa-and-country-music-with-maggie-sajak/ |access-date=2023-12-30 |website=Daily Front Row |language=en}}</ref> and was featured in a fashion shoot for ''[[Teen Vogue]]''.<ref name=tdfr2013/> She graduated from Princeton University and did an additional postgraduate program at Columbia University. In May 2023, she graduated from Georgetown University Law School. <ref>{{Cite web |title=MSN |url=https://www.msn.com/ |access-date=2024-06-09 |website=www.msn.com}}</ref>

For a week of episodes of ''[[Wheel of Fortune (American game show)|Wheel of Fortune]]'' which aired in January 2020, Maggie Sajak took over for [[Vanna White]].
