'''Amber Hayes''' is an American [[country music]] singer-songwriter. In 2008, she was cast as Kathy Twitty in the [[Conway Twitty]] production, ''Conway Twitty: The Man, The Music, The Legend...''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theboot.com/2008/10/15/conway-twitty-comes-alive-in-new-musical|title=Conway Twitty Comes Alive in New Musical|work=The Boot|date=October 15, 2008 |access-date=December 8, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gactv.com/gac/nw_headlines/article/0,,GAC_26063_5938588,00.html|title=News : Headlines : Conway Twitty Gets Own Musical : Great American Country|publisher=Gactv.com|access-date=December 8, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141129012812/http://www.gactv.com/gac/nw_headlines/article/0,,GAC_26063_5938588,00.html|archive-date=November 29, 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> She was signed to FUNL Music in 2010 and released her debut album ''C'mon'' and video later that year.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.musicrow.com/2011/03/signing-news-from-ray-stevens-amber-hayes/|title=Signing News From Ray Stevens, Amber Hayes|work=MusicRow – Nashville's Music Industry Publication – News, Songs From Music City|access-date=December 8, 2014|date=March 15, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://digitalrodeo.com/news/funl-music-double-feature-event-showcases-artists-amber-hayes-ty-herndon|title=FUNL Music Double-Feature Event Showcases Artists Amber Hayes & Ty Herndon|publisher=Digitalrodeo.com|access-date=December 8, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141129083353/http://digitalrodeo.com/news/funl-music-double-feature-event-showcases-artists-amber-hayes-ty-herndon|archive-date=November 29, 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> Also in 2014, Hayes signed with [[AristoMedia]] for publicity and [[GMV Nashville]] for digital distribution.

"C'mon" was the first of two singles off the album and went to 38 on the [[Music Row]] Country Breakout Chart.<ref name="Check1">{{cite web|url=http://www.countryhound.com/amber-hayes-on-the-rise|title=Amber Hayes: On the Rise|publisher=Countryhound.com|access-date=December 8, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141215070227/http://www.countryhound.com/amber-hayes-on-the-rise|archive-date=December 15, 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> "Wait" was the second single and was released in February and went to number 35 on the [[Music Row]] Country Breakout Chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.roughstock.com/blog/new-artist-spotlight-amber-hayes|title=New Artist Spotlight: Amber Hayes|author=Chuck Dauphin|work=RoughStock|access-date=December 8, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121013124235/http://www.roughstock.com/blog/new-artist-spotlight-amber-hayes|archive-date=October 13, 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://tasteofcountry.com/amber-hayes-wait/|title=Amber Hayes, 'Wait' – Song Review|work=Taste of Country|date=July 8, 2011 |access-date=December 8, 2014}}</ref> It premiered on Yallwire on May 13, 2011.<ref name="Check1" /><ref>{{cite web|url=http://digitalrodeo.com/news/new-single-amber-hayes-worth-%E2%80%9Cwait%E2%80%9D|title=New Single From Amber Hayes Worth the "Wait"|publisher=Digitalrodeo.com|access-date=December 8, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160204112832/http://digitalrodeo.com/news/new-single-amber-hayes-worth-%E2%80%9Cwait%E2%80%9D|archive-date=February 4, 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.amberhayesmusic.com/home/25-wait-music-video-to-world-premiere-on-yallwirecom-may-13|title="Wait" Video to Premiere on May 13|publisher=Amberhayesmusic.com|access-date=December 8, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141209200150/http://www.amberhayesmusic.com/home/25-wait-music-video-to-world-premiere-on-yallwirecom-may-13|archive-date=December 9, 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Hayes' ''C'mon'' album release party was held at the [[Station Inn]] and was broadcast on [[WSM (AM)|WSM]] 650AM in late 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.free-press-release.com/news-amber-hayes-celebrates-release-of-c-mon-with-legendary-650am-wsm-1283895499.html|title=Culture shock News: Amber Hayes Celebrates Release of C'mon with Legendary 650AM WSM|work=Free Press Release|access-date=December 8, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allaccess.com/net-news/archive/story/79489/amber-hayes-celebrates-debut-ep-release-with-wsm-a|title=Latest Radio News, Talk Shows, Sports, Hosts, Personalities - AllAccess.com|work=All Access|access-date=December 8, 2014}}</ref>

Much of 2010 and 2011 was devoted to touring with label mate [[Ty Herndon]] and included international shows in China, Japan, and Sweden.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allaccess.com/country/artist-info/q/id/2510/amber-hayes|title=Country Music Artist Info – New Information, Facts, Interviews, and Bios About ...|work=All Access|access-date=December 8, 2014}}</ref>

In 2012, Hayes' music ("C'mon", "Right As Rain", "Always There For Me") appeared in the major motion picture and soundtrack for ''[[Cowgirls 'n Angels]]'', which starred [[Bailee Madison]], [[James Cromwell]], [[Frankie Faison]], among others. In addition to Hayes' music being featured in the movie, she had a minor part in the film, dueting with [[Lonestar]]'s [[Richie McDonald]] during one the scenes.<ref name="Check2" >{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-615/474162/615-spotlight-amber-hayes-any-day-is-a-good-day|title=615 Spotlight: Amber Hayes' 'Any Day Is a Good Day'|magazine=Billboard|access-date=December 8, 2014|date=November 14, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1894561/fullcredits?ref_=tt_cl_sm#cast|title=Cowgirls 'n Angels : Full Credits|publisher=IMDb.com|access-date=December 8, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://blog.gactv.com/blog/2011/06/02/amber-hayes-to-star-in-cowgirls-n-angels/|title=Amber Hayes to Star in Cowgirls N' Angels|work=GAC News & Notes|access-date=December 8, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://newsok.com/oklahoma-singer-amber-hayes-contributes-three-songs-to-the-movie-cowgirls-n-angels/article/3679608|title=Oklahoma singer Amber Hayes contributes three songs to the movie 'Cowgirls N' Angels'|work=NewsOK.com|access-date=December 8, 2014|date=May 30, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://social.entertainment.msn.com/music/blogs/one-country-blogpost.aspx?post=98a7c849-ab1f-41c6-b72c-e4c05f8b3c20|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130411100510/http://social.entertainment.msn.com/music/blogs/one-country-blogpost.aspx?post=98a7c849-ab1f-41c6-b72c-e4c05f8b3c20|url-status=dead|archive-date=April 11, 2013|title=msn|publisher=Social.entertainment.msn.com|access-date=December 8, 2014}}</ref>

Also in 2012, Hayes' music "Wait", "C'mon", "Easy on Me" and "Right As Rain" appeared in the [[CW Network]]'s ''[[Hart of Dixie]]'' which stars [[Rachel Bilson]] and [[Scott Porter]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.misssouthtexas.org/2013-Entertainer---Amber-Hayes.html |access-date=April 2, 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131005054918/http://www.misssouthtexas.org/2013-Entertainer---Amber-Hayes.html |archive-date=October 5, 2013 |title=Miss South Texas Scholarship Organization - 2013 Entertainer - Amber Hayes }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allaccess.com/country/artist-info/q/id/3709/amber-hayes|title=Country Music Artist Info – New Information, Facts, Interviews, and Bios About ...|work=All Access|access-date=December 8, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://musiccityencore.com/kelly-clarkson-uncle-kracker-amber-hayes-new-albums|title=Kelly Clarkson, Uncle Kracker, Amber Hayes – New Albums|author=|work=Music City Encore|access-date=December 8, 2014}}</ref>

On October 22, 2012 Hayes released ''Any Day Is A Good Day'' on [[GMV Nashville]] and A-OK Entertainment.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.roughstock.com/blog/independent-lens-amber-hayes-talks-any-day-is-a-good-day-and-dreams-coming-true|title=Independent Lens: Amber Hayes Talks "Any Day Is A Good Day" And Dreams Coming True – RoughStock|author=Ben Foster|work=RoughStock|access-date=December 8, 2014|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130411184731/http://www.roughstock.com/blog/independent-lens-amber-hayes-talks-any-day-is-a-good-day-and-dreams-coming-true|archive-date=April 11, 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://jbcultureshock.wordpress.com/2012/10/28/amber-hayes-through-the-grapevine-album-released/|title=Amber Hayes Through the Grapevine (Album Released!)|work=CultureShock|access-date=December 8, 2014|date=October 28, 2012}}</ref>

In January 2013, [[Billboard Magazine|''Billboard'' magazine]]'s "The 615" named Hayes an "Artist to Watch" in 2013.<ref name="Check2" /><ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-615/1484124/615-picks-10-artists-to-watch-in-2013|title=615 Picks: 10 Artists to Watch in 2013|magazine=Billboard|access-date=December 8, 2014|date=January 8, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://blog.gactv.com/blog/2013/01/16/billboards-top-10-country-americana-artists-to-watch-in-2013/|title=Billboard's Top 10 Country Artists to Watch in 2013 – GAC News & Notes|work=GAC News & Notes|access-date=December 8, 2014}}</ref> Also in early 2013, she signed with noted booking agent Tony Conway of Conway Entertainment Group.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://musicconnection.com/amber-hayes-signs-booking-agreement/|title=Amber Hayes Signs Booking Agreement|work=Music Connection|access-date=December 8, 2014|date=March 18, 2013}}</ref>

In April 2013, Hayes released single "Any Day Is A Good Day" co-written by Hayes, Bill DiLuigi and JP Williams. Also in 2013, Hayes’ released a lyric video for her single "Any Day Is A Good Day", which was shot and edited by Thomas Newton and illustrated by Christen Cole.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CsgNbLcSv0Q|title=Amber Hayes – "Any Day Is A Good Day" [official lyric video]|work=YouTube|date=May 17, 2013 |access-date=December 8, 2014}}</ref>

Hayes' song "Right As Rain" is featured in the documentary film ''Music City USA'', which took home the Golden Drover for Best Feature Documentary at the 2014 Trail Dance Film Festival.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.musiccitymovie.com/|title=MUSIC CITY MOVIE|publisher=Musiccitymovie.com|access-date=December 8, 2014}}</ref>

Hayes makes an appearance in the 2014 ''Cowgirls 'n Angels'' sequel, ''[[Cowgirls 'n Angels: Dakota's Summer]]'', for a performance of her rendition of country classic "[[Cotton Eyed Joe]]." The track was produced and co-written by Charlie Kelley, and is featured on the ''Cowgirls 'n Angels: Dakota's Summer'' soundtrack along with her song “Home.”<ref>{{cite web|url=http://trailers.apple.com/trailers/fox/cowgirlsnangelsdakotassummer/|title=Cowgirls 'n Angels: Dakota's Summer|publisher=Trailers.apple.com|access-date=December 8, 2014}}</ref> Later in 2014, Hayes released a music video for "Cotton Eyed Joe.”

In April 2014, ''Cowgirls 'n Angels: Dakota's Summer'' premiered at the 2014 Dallas International Film Festival. In the movie, [[Academy Award|Oscar]] winner [[Keith Carradine]] (''Cowboys & Aliens'') stars alongside a young, popular cast that includes [[Haley Ramm]] (''[[X-Men: The Last Stand]]''), [[Jade Pettyjohn]] (''[[An American Girl: McKenna Shoots for the Stars]]'') and [[Emily Bett Rickards]] ([[Arrow (TV series)|''Arrow'']]).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://diff2013.dallasfilm.org/|title=Dallas International Film Festival|access-date=December 8, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141129031139/http://diff2013.dallasfilm.org/|archive-date=November 29, 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

At the June 2014 [[CMA Music Festival]], Hayes presented Sweet Sunday Singin’ with Amber Hayes and Friends, which was hosted by Philip Gibbons and sponsored by Gigi's Cupcakes and J. Alexander Home.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://digitalrodeo.com/news/inaugural-“sweet-sunday-singin’”-take-place-sunday-june-8-listening-room|archive-url=https://archive.today/20141119010931/http://digitalrodeo.com/news/inaugural-%E2%80%9Csweet-sunday-singin%E2%80%99%E2%80%9D-take-place-sunday-june-8-listening-room|url-status=dead|archive-date=November 19, 2014|title=INAUGURAL "SWEET SUNDAY SINGIN'" TO TAKE PLACE SUNDAY, JUNE 8 AT THE LISTENING ROOM|publisher=Digitalrodeo.com|access-date=December 8, 2014}}</ref>

In November 2014, Hayes released a 5-song EP, titled ''Running Out of Memories'' through GMV Nashville/A-OK Entertainment, which features Hayes’ rendition of "Cotton Eyed Joe," a remix of her previously released "Me & Loretta," as well as three additional songs that were produced by Bobby Terry.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hngn.com/articles/48966/20141110/amber-hayes-new-ep-is-both-poignant-and-downright-foot-stompin.htm|title=Amber Hayes' New EP Is Both Poignant And Downright Foot Stompin'|work=Headlines & Global News|access-date=December 8, 2014|date=November 10, 2014}}</ref>
