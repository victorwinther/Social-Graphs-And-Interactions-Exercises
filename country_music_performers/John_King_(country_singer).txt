'''John King''' is an American [[country music]] singer and songwriter.

King was born and raised in [[Demorest, Georgia]].

He worked construction jobs as a teenager and was heavily influenced by Country, Rock and [[Southern rock]] artists. King later graduated from [[University of Georgia]] before signing his first record deal with [[Black River Entertainment]] (2014-2016).<ref name="bio">{{cite web|url=https://www.johnkingcountry.com/|title=John King|website=John King}}</ref>

His debut single, "Tonight Tonight", was released in mid-2014 and featured on the [[NFL|NFL's]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nfl.com/|title=NFL.com - Official Site of the National Football League|website=www.nfl.com|language=en-US|access-date=2018-06-14}}</ref> Thursday Night Football on [[CBS]]. [[Taste of Country]] reviewed it favorably, saying that it is "a great hook over a strong beat, all built sturdy country voice. King solidly follows in the footsteps of many modern country hit-makers, unveiling a debut single that is fun and catchy and will immediately have one hitting repeat for a second listen."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tasteofcountry.com/john-king-tonight-tonight/|title=John King, 'Tonight Tonight' [Listen]|first=Taste of Country|last=Staff|website=Taste of Country|date=5 June 2014 }}</ref> It entered Top 40 on [[Country Airplay]] for the chart dated September 13, 2014.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/biz|title=Billboard biz|last=alex|date=21 April 2018|magazine=Billboard}}</ref>

King co-wrote [[Randy Houser]]'s single "[[We Went]]" with Justin Wilson, Matt Rogers, the song reached #1 on Mediabase<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mediabase.com|title=Mediabase 24/7 - Logon|website=www.mediabase.com|access-date=2018-06-14}}</ref> and Billboard<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts|title=Charts {{!}} Billboard|magazine=Billboard|access-date=2018-06-14}}</ref> [[Country Airplay]] Charts.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/360710/Randy+Houser/chart?f=1226|title=Randy Houser - Chart history - Billboard|website=www.billboard.com}}</ref> It also scored him his first [[American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers|ASCAP]] Songwriter Award in 2016. King also penned the first [[Hootie & the Blowfish|Hootie and The Blowfish]] song to be released since 2005. “Rollin” is featured on their new album, "Imperfect Circle" that was released November 1, 2019.<ref>{{Citation|title=Rollin' by Hootie & The Blowfish|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/imperfect-circle/1478791238?i=1478791723|language=en-us|access-date=2019-11-11}}</ref>

The video for King's 2017 "I Still Pick Up" was voted Top 10 on the [[CMT (U.S. TV channel)|CMT]]countdown.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.cmt.com/shows/hot-20-countdown|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170621000401/http://www.cmt.com/shows/hot-20-countdown|url-status=dead|archive-date=June 21, 2017|title=CMT Hot 20 Countdown {{!}} Episodes (TV Series)|website=CMT|access-date=2018-06-14}}</ref> King signed [[Sony/ATV Music Publishing|Sony/ATV]] Nashville in April 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sonyatv.com/|title=Sony/ATV|website=www.sonyatv.com|language=en|access-date=2018-06-14}}</ref>

King’s single, “Try Saying Goodbye” pulls from his storytelling prowess. It has already garnered more than 15 million streams<ref>{{Citation|title=Try Saying Goodbye|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/try-saying-goodbye/1448487412?i=1448487420|language=en|access-date=2019-11-11}}</ref> and 30 major market radio adds including Atlanta, Boston and Seattle. It was named one of Rolling Stone’s Top 10 Country Songs,<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/best-country-songs-aaron-watson-im-with-her-785273/|title=10 Best Country, Americana Songs to Hear Now: Aaron Watson, I'm With Her|last=Crawford|first=Robert|date=2019-01-28|magazine=Rolling Stone|language=en-US|access-date=2019-11-11}}</ref> earning features from Amazon, Whiskey Riff, Taste of Country<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://tasteofcountry.com/john-king-try-saying-goodbye/|title=John King Strikes Universal Chord in 'Try Saying Goodbye'|last=Whitaker|first=Sterling|website=Taste of Country|date=24 January 2019 |language=en|access-date=2019-11-11}}</ref> and more.

On his debut album '''‘Always Gonna Be You,’''' King shines light on the most important things in life: family and faith. ‘Always Gonna Be You’ is available everywhere. '''''American Songwriter''''' says the album “establishes the Georgia native as a boundless artist with an undeniable talent for truth-telling."

John recently broke 50 Million All Time Streams and tours countless dates with the likes of Blake Shelton, Jason Aldean, Sam Hunt and Travis Tritt.
