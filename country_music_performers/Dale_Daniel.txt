'''Lisa Dale Daniel''' is an American country music artist. She has recorded one studio album, ''Luck of Our Own''. Daniel is the daughter of songwriter Naomi Martin, whose credits include the Grammy nominated "Let's Take the Long Way Around the World" by [[Ronnie Milsap]] and "My Eyes Can Only See as Far as You" by [[Charley Pride]].<ref name="oldies">{{cite web |url=http://www.oldies.com/artist-biography/Dale-Daniel.html |title=Dale Daniel biography |accessdate=2008-09-03 |work=Oldies.com}}</ref> Daniel and Martin also co-wrote the track "Someone to Call Me Darling" on [[Lorrie Morgan]]'s 1992 album ''[[Watch Me (album)|Watch Me]]''She also co-penned a Barbara Mandrel and Lee Greewood duet, “I’d Give Anything to be Everything to You”.<ref name="oldies"/> which also featured Daniel on background vocals.

Daniel made her first recording at age 14, and by 1991, she had joined [[Moe Bandy]]'s band.<ref name="oldies"/> Signed to [[RCA/BNARecords]] (then known as BNA Entertainment) in 1993, she released her [[Jerry Crutchfield]]-produced debut album ''Luck of Our Own'' in 1994 and Daniel still continues to write and live in Nashville, and plans on putting out more music. Daniel's title track on her album “Luck of Our Own” was covered by Keith Urban.[[Keith Urban]]'s 1999 [[Keith Urban (1999 album)|self-titled album]].
