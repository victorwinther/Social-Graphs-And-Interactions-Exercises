'''Michael James Henderson''' (July 14, 1953 – September 22, 2023)<ref name="BGTobi">{{cite web |url=https://bluegrasstoday.com/mike-henderson-passes/|first=Richard|last=Thompson|title=Mike Henderson passes |date=September 29, 2023|accessdate=September 30, 2023|website=Bluegrass Today}}</ref><ref name="KCSobi">{{cite web |url=https://www.legacy.com/us/obituaries/kansascity/name/michael-henderson-obituary?id=53184107|title=Michael Henderson Obituary|date=September 26, 2023|accessdate=September 30, 2023|publisher=The Kansas City Star}}</ref> was an American singer-songwriter. In addition to his solo career, which included five studio albums, Henderson was a member of the country band [[The SteelDrivers]] from 2005 to 2011 and was a songwriting collaborator of his former SteelDrivers bandmate [[Chris Stapleton]].

==Biography==

===Early life and career===
Henderson was born in [[Independence, Missouri]], in 1953.<ref name="Gray">{{cite encyclopedia |last=Gray |first=Michael |author-link=Michael Gray (author) |editor-first=Paul |editor-last=Kingsbury |encyclopedia=The Encyclopedia of Country Music |title=Mike Henderson |year=1998 |publisher=Oxford University Press |isbn=978-0-19-977055-7 |oclc=707922721 |pages=236
}}</ref><ref name="whitburn">{{cite book|last=Whitburn|first=Joel|title=Hot Country Songs 1944 to 2008|publisher=Record Research, Inc|page=186|year=2008|isbn=978-0-89820-177-2}}</ref> He was an original member of [[blues]] group the Bel Airs when they formed in [[Missouri]] in 1981.<ref name="belairs">{{cite web|title=The Bel Airs Bio|url=http://www.belairs.com/bio.html|work=The Bel Airs|access-date=February 11, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160401190848/http://www.belairs.com/bio.html|archive-date=April 1, 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Himes|first=Geoffrey|title=MIKE HENDERSON & THE BLUEBLOODS "Thicker Than Water" Dead Reckoning|url=http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P2-570184.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140611061743/http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P2-570184.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=June 11, 2014|access-date=February 11, 2014|newspaper=[[The Washington Post]]|date=March 5, 1999}}</ref> They released an album, ''Need Me a Car'', on [[Blind Pig Records]] in 1984.<ref name="belairs" /><ref name="houston">{{cite news|last=Sherman|first=Jim|title=Aw, Twern't Nuthin'|url=http://www.houstonpress.com/1997-01-16/music/aw-twern-t-nuthin/full/|access-date=February 11, 2014|newspaper=[[Houston Press]]|date=January 16, 1997}}</ref> Henderson left the band in 1985 and moved to [[Nashville]].<ref name="belairs" /><ref name="bio">{{cite web|title=Mike Henderson Bio|url=http://www.mikehenderson.com/bio.htm|work=Mike Henderson|access-date=February 11, 2014}}</ref> The following year, he joined the [[roots rock]] band The Roosters.<ref name="bio" /> He was also a member of spin-off band The Kingsnakes.<ref name="bio" /> The Kingsnakes began playing weekly at the [[Bluebird Cafe]] in July 1986.<ref name="bluebird">{{cite web|title=About {{!}} The Bluebird Cafe|url=http://www.bluebirdcafe.com/about/|work=[[Bluebird Cafe]]|access-date=February 11, 2014}}</ref> They shortened their name to The Snakes when they were signed by [[Curb Records]].<ref name="bluebird" /> An album, ''The Snakes'', was released by Curb in 1989.<ref>{{cite web|title=''The Snakes'' – The Snakes|url={{AllMusic|class=album|id=mw0000653677|pure_url=yes}}|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=February 11, 2014}}</ref>

In 1988, [[The Fabulous Thunderbirds]] covered "Powerful Stuff", a song Henderson had written for The Snakes, for the soundtrack to the film ''[[Cocktail (1988 film)|Cocktail]]''.<ref name="bio" /><ref>{{cite news|last=Galipault|first=Gerry|title=Mike Henderson & The Bluebloods swing the blues|url=http://www.pauseandplay.com/mike-henderson-the-bluebloods-swing-the-blues/|access-date=February 11, 2014|newspaper=Pause & Play|date=January 10, 1999}}</ref> Henderson later became a staff songwriter for [[EMI Music Publishing|EMI]].<ref name="houston" /> His songs have been recorded by the [[Dixie Chicks]], [[Trisha Yearwood]], [[Gary Allan]] and [[Patty Loveless]], among others.<ref name="tennessean">{{cite news|last=Cooper|first=Peter|title=Peter Cooper on Music: A mandolin player ZZ Top can love|url=http://blogs.tennessean.com/tunein/2011/11/17/peter-cooper-on-music-a-mandolin-player-zz-top-can-love/|access-date=February 11, 2014|newspaper=[[The Tennessean]]|date=November 17, 2011}}</ref><ref name="credits">{{cite web|title=Mike Henderson {{!}} Credits|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=mn0000486733/credits|pure_url=yes}}|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=February 11, 2014}}</ref> Henderson also found work in Nashville as a [[slide guitar]]ist.<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|last=Skelly|first=Richard|title=Mike Henderson {{!}} Biography|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=mn0000486733|pure_url=yes}}|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=February 11, 2014}}</ref> He played on albums by [[Emmylou Harris]], [[John Hiatt]], [[Joy Lynn White]] and [[Kelly Willis]].<ref name="bio" /><ref name="credits" />

===''Country Music Made Me Do It''===
Henderson's demos drew the attention of [[country music]] label [[RCA Records Nashville|RCA Nashville]].<ref name="houston" /> RCA signed Henderson and released his solo debut album, ''Country Music Made Me Do It'', in March 1994.<ref>{{cite web|title=''Country Music Made Me Do It'' – Mike Henderson|url={{AllMusic|class=album|id=mw0000110086|pure_url=yes}}|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=February 11, 2014}}</ref> Bob Cannon of ''[[Entertainment Weekly]]'' gave the album an A− grade, writing that Henderson's "enthusiastic field holler and his guitar's riveting twang give off enough sparks to ignite [the songs]."<ref>{{cite news|last=Cannon|first=Bob|title=Country Music Made Me Do It Review|url=http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0,,302122,00.htm|archive-url=https://archive.today/20140212221757/http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0,,302122,00.htm|url-status=dead|archive-date=February 12, 2014|access-date=February 11, 2014|newspaper=[[Entertainment Weekly]]|date=May 6, 1994}}</ref> Dan Kening of the ''[[Chicago Tribune]]'' gave the album three and a half stars, saying that "Henderson downplays his guitar chops on his first solo album in favor of his songwriting and strong vocals and acquits himself admirably."<ref>{{cite news|last=Kening|first=Dan|title=Mike Henderson Country Music Made Me Do It (RCA)|url=https://www.chicagotribune.com/1994/05/12/mike-hendersoncountry-music-made-me-do-it/|access-date=February 11, 2014|newspaper=[[Chicago Tribune]]|date=May 12, 1994}}</ref> The album also received a favorable review from Peter Cronin of ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', who declared that "Henderson comes to the party with plenty of attitude and a distinctive point of view."<ref>{{cite news|last=Cronin|first=Peter|title=Album Reviews|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=NQgEAAAAMBAJ&q=%22mike%20henderson%22%20%22if%20the%20jukebox%20took%20teardrops%22&pg=RA1-PA68|access-date=February 11, 2014|newspaper=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=March 26, 1994}}</ref>

The album's first single, "Hillbilly Jitters", peaked at number 69 on the ''Billboard'' [[Hot Country Songs|Hot Country Singles & Tracks]] chart.<ref name="whitburn" /> When subsequent singles "The Want To" and "If the Jukebox Took Teardrops" failed to chart, Henderson was dropped by the label.<ref name="reader">{{cite news|last=Margasak|first=Peter|title=Mike Henderson|url=http://www.chicagoreader.com/chicago/mike-henderson/Content?oid=890203|access-date=February 11, 2014|newspaper=[[Chicago Reader]]|date=April 11, 1996}}</ref> "If the Jukebox Took Teardrops" was later a minor chart hit for [[Danni Leigh]] in 1998.<ref>{{cite web|title=''29 Nights'' – Danni Leigh|url={{AllMusic|class=album|id=mw0000042159|pure_url=yes}}|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=February 11, 2014}}</ref>

===''Edge of Night''===
After being dropped by RCA, Henderson founded the label [[Dead Reckoning Records]] with [[Kieran Kane]], [[Kevin Welch]], [[Tammy Rogers]] and [[Harry Stinson (musician)|Harry Stinson]].<ref name="reader" /> His second album, ''Edge of Night'', was released by Dead Reckoning in January 1996.<ref name="houston" /><ref>{{cite web|title=''Edge of Night'' – Mike Henderson|url={{AllMusic|class=album|id=mw0000183441|pure_url=yes}}|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=February 11, 2014}}</ref> The video for the first single, a cover of [[Eddy Clearwater]]'s "I Wouldn't Lay My Guitar Down", was added by [[Country Music Television|CMT]] in February 1996.<ref>{{cite news|title=Billboard Video Monitor|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Og8EAAAAMBAJ&q=%22mike%20henderson%22%20%22lay%20my%20guitar%20down%22&pg=PA76|access-date=February 11, 2014|newspaper=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=March 2, 1996}}</ref> Tony Scherman of ''Entertainment Weekly'' gave the album a B+ grade, writing that Henderson is "a good songwriter, even if he wears his influences a little too plainly."<ref>{{cite news|last=Scherman|first=Tony|title=Edge of Night Review|url=http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0,,291012,00.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081203141202/http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0,,291012,00.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=December 3, 2008|access-date=February 11, 2014|newspaper=[[Entertainment Weekly]]|date=January 19, 1996}}</ref> Parry Gettelman of the ''[[Orlando Sentinel]]'' gave the album five stars, stating that Henderson's "strong, slightly sandpapery voice is as soulful as it is twangy."<ref>{{cite news|last=Gettelman|first=Parry|title=Mike Henderson|url=https://www.orlandosentinel.com/1996/01/26/mike-henderson/|access-date=February 11, 2014|newspaper=[[Chicago Tribune]]|date=January 26, 1996}}</ref> Chet Flippo of ''Billboard'' also reviewed the album favorably, saying that "Henderson manages to sound at once world-weary and exuberant in a solid lineup of original material and country chestnuts."<ref>{{cite news|last=Flippo|first=Chet|title=Reviews & Previews|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=7g4EAAAAMBAJ&q=%22mike%20henderson%22%20%22edge%20of%20night%22&pg=PA105|access-date=February 11, 2014|newspaper=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=January 27, 1996}}</ref>

===''First Blood''===
Later in 1996, Henderson formed the blues band Mike Henderson & the Bluebloods with [[Reese Wynans]] on piano, [[Glenn Worf]] on [[bass (instrument)|bass]] and John Gardner on drums.<ref name="houston" /><ref name="allmusic" /> They released the album ''First Blood'' in October 1996 on Dead Reckoning.<ref>{{cite web|title=''First Blood'' – Mike Henderson & The Bluebloods|url={{AllMusic|class=album|id=mw0000613452|pure_url=yes}}|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=February 11, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Margasak|first=Peter|title=Mike Henderson & the Bluebloods|url=http://www.chicagoreader.com/chicago/mike-henderson--the-bluebloods/Content?oid=892419|access-date=February 11, 2014|newspaper=[[Chicago Reader]]|date=January 9, 1997}}</ref> [[Mark Knopfler]] wrote the album's [[liner notes]].<ref name="nodepression">{{cite news|last=Ray|first=Linda|title=Mike Henderson & The Bluebloods – First Blood|url=http://archives.nodepression.com/1997/03/mike-henderson-the-bluebloods-first-blood/|access-date=February 11, 2014|newspaper=[[No Depression (magazine)|No Depression]]|date=March 1997|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140222013118/http://archives.nodepression.com/1997/03/mike-henderson-the-bluebloods-first-blood/|archive-date=February 22, 2014}}</ref> [[Alanna Nash]] of ''Entertainment Weekly'' gave the album an A grade, writing that "''First Blood'''s blistering, seamless blues covers prove [Henderson]'s a remarkable guitarist and frontman."<ref>{{cite news|last=Nash|first=Alanna|title=First Blood Review|url=http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0,,294614,00.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090425114608/http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0,,294614,00.html|url-status=dead|archive-date=April 25, 2009|access-date=February 11, 2014|newspaper=[[Entertainment Weekly]]|date=October 18, 1996}}</ref> A review in ''[[People (magazine)|People]]'' stated that "when the combination of piano, bass, drums and electric guitar is as neck-snappingly strong as it is on the Bluebloods' first album, you don't need other instruments, original compositions or even many original ideas to deliver a knockout blow."<ref>{{cite news|title=Picks and Pans Review: First Blood|url=http://www.people.com/people/article/0,,20142941,00.html|access-date=February 11, 2014|newspaper=[[People (magazine)|People]]|date=December 9, 1996}}</ref> Linda Ray of ''[[No Depression (magazine)|No Depression]]'' also gave the album a positive review, praising Henderson's "masterful guitar and vocals" and saying that "the way he plays that slide is likely illegal in several states."<ref name="nodepression" /> The song "Pay Bo Diddley" received some radio airplay.<ref name="allmusic" />

===''Thicker Than Water''===
Mike Henderson & the Bluebloods released their second album, ''Thicker Than Water'', in January 1999 with [[John Barlow Jarvis]] replacing Reese Wynans on piano.<ref name="thicker">{{cite web|last=Byrkit|first=Becky|title=''Thicker Than Water'' – Mike Henderson & The Bluebloods|url={{AllMusic|class=album|id=mw0000046781|pure_url=yes}}|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=February 11, 2014}}</ref> Becky Byrkit of [[AllMusic]] gave the album four stars out of five, writing that "Henderson contributes a particularly clear vocal style with plenty of simultaneous character from both the blues and true-blue country music."<ref name="thicker" /> The album received a mixed review in ''People'', which praised Jarvis' "richly layered, hard-driving solos" but compared Henderson's vocals to "the white-guy-trying-to-sound-soulful desperation of [[Dan Aykroyd]] and [[John Belushi]] in their [[The Blues Brothers|Blues Brothers]] mode."<ref>{{cite news|title=Picks and Pans Review: Thicker Than Water|url=http://www.people.com/people/article/0,,20127411,00.html|access-date=February 11, 2014|newspaper=[[People (magazine)|People]]|date=January 25, 1999}}</ref> Ed Kopp of ''[[All About Jazz]]'' gave the album a positive review, saying that "leader Mike Henderson is a highly capable slide guitarist, harpist, and singer, but the guy who makes this CD extra special is John Jarvis."<ref>{{cite news|last=Kopp|first=Ed|title=Mike Henderson and the Bluebloods: Thicker Than Water|url=http://www.allaboutjazz.com/php/article.php?id=4098|access-date=February 11, 2014|newspaper=[[All About Jazz]]|date=August 1, 1999}}</ref> Tim Steil of the ''Chicago Tribune'' also gave the album a favorable review, stating that "whether playing [[Hound Dog Taylor]]-ish slide, or blowing harp lines that would make [[Little Walter]] smile, Henderson deftly conjures the sound of '50s Chicago."<ref>{{cite news|last=Steil|first=Tom|title=Mike Henderson and the Bluebloods Thicker Than Water|url=https://www.chicagotribune.com/1999/01/17/mike-henderson-and-the-bluebloodsthicker-than-water/|access-date=February 11, 2014|newspaper=[[Chicago Tribune]]|date=January 17, 1999}}</ref>

===Later career===
Henderson toured with Mark Knopfler on his 2001 [[Sailing to Philadelphia Tour]].<ref name="bio" /><ref name="tennessean" /> In 2008, Henderson was one of the founding members of [[bluegrass music|bluegrass]] group [[The SteelDrivers]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Lupton|first=John|title=The SteelDrivers put blue in bluegrass|url=http://www.countrystandardtime.com/d/article.asp?xid=1044|access-date=February 11, 2014|newspaper=[[Country Standard Time]]|date=January 2008}}</ref> He played [[mandolin]], [[Resonator guitar|resophonic guitar]] and harmonica and co-wrote most of the band's original songs.<ref>{{cite web|last=poet|first=j.|title=''Reckless'' – SteelDrivers|url={{AllMusic|class=album|id=mw0002021612|pure_url=yes}}|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=February 11, 2014}}</ref> The SteelDrivers' 2010 album ''Reckless'' was nominated for a [[Grammy Award for Best Bluegrass Album]] at the [[53rd Annual Grammy Awards]] in 2011.<ref>{{cite news|title=Five artists under the radar at the Grammys|url=http://usatoday30.usatoday.com/life/music/awards/grammys/2011-02-07-fiveradar07_ST_N.htm|access-date=February 11, 2014|newspaper=[[USA Today]]|date=February 7, 2011}}</ref> Henderson left The SteelDrivers in December 2011.<ref>{{cite news|last=Lawless|first=John|title=Mike Henderson moves on|url=http://bluegrasstoday.com/mike-henderson-moves-on/|access-date=February 11, 2014|newspaper=Bluegrass Today|date=December 21, 2011}}</ref>

[[Adele]] performed Henderson's song "If It Hadn't Been for Love" for her 2011 DVD ''[[Live at the Royal Albert Hall (Adele video)|Live at the Royal Albert Hall]]''.<ref name="tennessean" />

Henderson continued to play weekly shows at the Bluebird Cafe with the Mike Henderson Band.<ref name="bluebird" /><ref name="tennessean" />

===Death===
Mike Henderson died unexpectedly in his sleep on September 22, 2023, at the age of 70.<ref name="BGTobi"/><ref name="KCSobi"/><ref>{{cite web |last1=Hudak |first1=Joseph |title=Mike Henderson, Chris Stapleton Co-Writer and the SteelDrivers Founder, Dead at 70 |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/mike-henderson-steeldrivers-dead-obit-1234830058/ |website=Rolling Stone |access-date=23 September 2023 |date=22 September 2023}}</ref>
