'''Gregory Scott Reeves''' (born May 16, 1966)<ref name="about">{{Cite web |title=About the Actors: Scott Reeves |url=https://www.soapcentral.com/theactors/reeves_scott.php |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230610000000/https://www.soapcentral.com/theactors/reeves_scott.php |archive-date=June 10, 2023 |access-date=August 15, 2024 |website=Soap Central}}</ref> is an American actor, musician, and songwriter. He is known for playing the roles of [[Ryan McNeil]] on the [[CBS Daytime]] soap opera ''[[The Young and the Restless]]'' (1991 to 2001), [[Steven Webber]] on the [[ABC Daytime]] soap opera ''[[General Hospital]]'' (2009 to 2013), and Noel Laughlin on the ABC series ''[[Nashville (2012 TV series)|Nashville]]'' (2014 to 2016). For his work on ''The Young and the Restless'', Reeves was nominated for two [[Daytime Emmy Awards]] for [[Daytime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Drama Series|Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Drama Series]]. He has received two [[Soap Opera Digest Awards|Soap Opera Digest Award]] nominations and won the award for Outstanding Younger Leading Actor in 1994.

As a musician, Reeves co-founded the country music duo [[Blue County (duo)|Blue County]] with [[Aaron Benward]]. Their self-titled debut album was released on [[Curb Records]] in 2006. They had four songs reach the [[Billboard charts]]. Reeves also co-wrote the song "[[Made in America (Toby Keith song)|Made in America]]", which was recorded by [[Toby Keith]] and charted at number one. 

== Early life ==
Reeves was born in [[Santa Monica, California]] and raised in the [[San Fernando Valley]]. His father was a Beverly Hills police officer and his mother owned a publishing company. He attended [[West Valley Christian School]], where he played baseball and football.<ref name="soapbook">{{Cite book |last1=Rout |first1=Nancy E. |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=yzQlimPTTEMC |title=The Soap Opera Book: Who's Who in Daytime Drama |last2=Buckley |first2=Ellen |publisher=Todd Publications |year=1992 |isbn=978-0-915344-23-9 |pages=225}}</ref> After graduation, he studied acting at the Renee Harmon Commercial Workshop and the [[Beverly Hills Playhouse]]. He also worked with acting coach Carole D'Andrea.<ref name="about" /><ref name="soapbook" />

==Personal life==
He met actress [[Melissa Reeves]] (then known as Melissa Brennan) when he played the role of Jake on ''Days of Our Lives''. They became engaged on April 1, 1989 in Vancouver, where he was filming ''[[Friday the 13th Part VIII: Jason Takes Manhattan]]''. He proposed to her at [[Stanley Park]].<ref name="golden" /> 

After experiencing seizures in 1989, he sought medical help. He was diagnosed with an [[abscess]] on his brain, caused by [[sinusitis]] that had traveled to his forehead. Shortly before his wedding, he underwent surgery to have the abscess removed. The Reeves' were married on March 23, 1990 at Lake Manor Chapel in [[Chatsworth, Los Angeles|Chatsworth]], [[California]]. They are [[Christians]].<ref name="golden">{{Cite journal |last=Byron |first=Ellen |date=June 25, 1991 |title=Golden Girl |journal=[[Soap Opera Digest]] |volume=16 |issue=13 |pages=6-10}}</ref> They have a daughter, born in 1992, and a son, born in 1997.<ref name="about" />

In late February 2017, Reeves had to postpone concert dates with his band, Port Chuck, when he was suddenly hospitalized. He was initially thought to have a heart condition, but after a brief hospital stay, he announced that he was fine.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Clifford |first=Kambra |date=March 4, 2017 |title=Scott Reeves posts update video after hospital scare |url=https://www.soapcentral.com/general-hospital/0304-scott-reeves-hospitalized-update |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240815192820/https://www.soapcentral.com/general-hospital/0304-scott-reeves-hospitalized-update |archive-date=August 15, 2024 |access-date=August 15, 2024 |website=Soap Central}}</ref>
