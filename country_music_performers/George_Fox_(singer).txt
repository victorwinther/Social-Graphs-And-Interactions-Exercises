'''George Fox''' (born March 23, 1960) is a Canadian country/western music singer/songwriter raised in [[Cochrane, Alberta|Cochrane]], [[Alberta]], the son of cattle ranchers Bert and Gert Fox.{{citation needed|date=July 2021}}

==Career==

His first single, "Angelina", reached No.&nbsp;8 on the Canadian country music charts. In subsequent years, "Goldmine", "No Trespassing", "Mustang Heart", "I Give You My Word", "[[What's Holding Me]]" and "Breakfast Alone" also became top 10 singles and signature songs. He has written a book of short stories depicting ranch life entitled ''My First Cow''.{{citation needed|date=July 2021}}

==Honours==

Awards include the [[Canadian Country Music Awards]] (CCMA) Male Vocalist Of The Year (three times) and the [[Juno Award]]s Country Male Vocalist Of The Year (three times). Fox was host of the CCMA Awards television show from 1991 to 1994.

In 1995, his home town of Cochrane named a new street George Fox Trail in his honour.<ref name="HALLOFFAME">{{cite news| url=https://www.thespec.com/entertainment/music/opinion/2022/08/18/george-fox-canadian-country-music-hall-of-fame.html| title=A look back on George Fox's Hall of Fame career| last=Rockingham| first=Graham| work=The Hamilton Spectator| date=August 18, 2022}}</ref>

In September 2022 he was inducted into the [[Canadian Country Music Hall of Fame]].<ref name="HALLOFFAME" />

==Personal life==

Fox and his wife Monica live near [[Ancaster, Ontario|Ancaster]] in southern [[Ontario]] with their two daughters.<ref name="HALLOFFAME" />
