'''Alan Gordon Anderson''' (born July 26, 1947) is an American guitarist, singer, and songwriter. In the 1960s, Anderson was the frontman of the band [[the Wildweeds]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.wildweeds.net/bio.html|title=The Wildweeds – Biography|website=Wildweeds.net|access-date=March 5, 2020}}</ref> which had success with the song "No Good To Cry",<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g52IEEhAnSk |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211213/g52IEEhAnSk |archive-date=2021-12-13 |url-status=live|publisher=YouTube|title=No Need to Cry|date=June 6, 2013 |access-date=March 5, 2020}}{{cbignore}}</ref> which he wrote. Between December 1971 and December 1993, he was the lead guitarist in the rock band [[NRBQ]], also releasing several solo albums. He also played electric guitar on [[Jonathan Edwards (musician)|Jonathan Edwards]]'s 1973 album ''[[Have a Good Time for Me]]''.

In the 1990s, Anderson shifted his focus to country music, writing hit songs for such artists as [[Carlene Carter]], [[Vince Gill]], [[Diamond Rio]] and [[Trisha Yearwood]], as well as [[Tim McGraw]]'s number 1 hit "[[The Cowboy in Me]]" and several album cuts. Anderson has also released six solo albums.<ref>{{cite web |url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p3541/biography|pure_url=yes}} |title=Al Anderson biography |access-date=June 5, 2008 |last=Deming |first=Mark |website=[[AllMusic]]}}</ref>
