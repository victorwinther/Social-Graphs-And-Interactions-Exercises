'''Sonya Melissa Isaacs''' (born July 22, 1974) is an American [[country music|country]], [[Bluegrass music|bluegrass gospel]] and [[Christian music]] singer. Isaacs grew up near [[Morrow, Ohio]], and graduated from [[Little Miami High School (Ohio)|Little Miami High School]] in 1992. Her maternal  grandparents are [[The Holocaust in Poland|Polish Jewish Holocaust]] survivors and were liberated from a concentration camp in Germany in 1945.<ref name="lufkin">{{cite news |last1=BARCLAY |first1=BRITTANY |title=The Isaacs to bring bluegrass gospel to The Pines |url=https://lufkindailynews.com/news/community/article_94d60ad4-b88c-5651-a672-7ce01b2b7c36.html |access-date=12 July 2021 |work=The Lufkin Daily News |language=en}}</ref><ref name="people">{{cite news |last1=Etienne |first1=Vanessa |title=The Isaacs Premiere 'The American Face' Music Video: 'United We Stand' |url=https://people.com/music/isaacs-premiere-the-american-face-music-video-united-we-stand/ |access-date=12 July 2021 |work=PEOPLE.com |language=en}}</ref>

She has released one album on [[Lyric Street Records]], and has charted five singles on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs]] charts. Three were included on her self-titled debut album, released in 2000. A fourth was a Christmas single included on the label's multi-artist collection ''No Wrapping Required: A Christmas Album''. Her highest-charting single, "No Regrets Yet", peaked at number 36 on the country charts but did not appear on an album.

She parted ways with Lyric Street in 2004.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.allbusiness.com/transportation-communications/communications-radio/4361802-1.html |title=On the Row: Compadre Records Signs Three Artists |access-date=January 4, 2008 |last=Stark |first=Phyllis |date=March 12, 2004 |work=Airplay Monitors}}</ref> Isaacs, along with several of her family members, also comprise a [[gospel music]] band called [[The Isaacs]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.cmt.com/artists/az/isaacs/bio.jhtml |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20050225200135/http://www.cmt.com/artists/az/isaacs/bio.jhtml |url-status=dead |archive-date=February 25, 2005 |title=The Isaacs biography |access-date=January 4, 2008 |work=[[Country Music Television]]}}</ref> Sonya recorded the song "The Battlefield" for the soundtrack to the 2006 film ''[[Broken Bridges]]''. She and [[Vince Gill]] contributed guest vocals to a cover of "[[Misty (song)|Misty]]" on [[Cledus T. Judd]]'s 2007 tribute album ''[[Boogity, Boogity – A Tribute to the Comedic Genius of Ray Stevens]]''.

She also sang a duet with [[Dolly Parton]] to "The Angels Rejoiced".

Isaacs married singer-songwriter [[Jimmy Yeary]] on December 20, 2009, with whom she co-wrote [[Martina McBride]]'s 2011 single "[[I'm Gonna Love You Through It]]".

Isaacs and Yeary have two sons born in 2011 and 2015, and a daughter born in 2017.<ref>{{cite web|author=Brad Schmitt |url=http://www.tennessean.com/story/entertainment/2016/08/26/sonya-isaacs-healing-stillborn-birth/89225046/ |title=Sonya Isaacs healing from stillborn birth |publisher=Tennessean.com |access-date=August 26, 2016}}</ref>

As a member of ''The Isaacs'', Isaacs became a [[List of Grand Ole Opry members|member]] of the [[Grand Ole Opry]] in 2021.<ref>{{cite news |title=Ricky Skaggs invites family bluegrass group The Isaacs to become Grand Ole Opry members |url=https://www.tennessean.com/videos/entertainment/2021/08/11/ricky-skaggs-invites-family-bluegrass-group-isaacs-become-grand-ole-opry-members/5566500001/ |access-date=8 March 2022 |work=The Tennessean}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Opry in the News: The Isaacs Inducted as Newest Members |url=https://www.opry.com/story/opry-in-the-news-the-isaacs-inducted-as-newest-members/ |website=Grand Ole Opry |access-date=8 March 2022 |language=en}}</ref>
