'''Blackberry Smoke''' is an American country rock band formed in [[Atlanta]], [[Georgia (U.S. state)|Georgia]], in 2000. The lineup consists of Charlie Starr (lead vocals, guitar), Richard Turner (bass, backing vocals), Paul Jackson (guitar, backing vocals), and Brandon Still (keyboards).  Richard's brother Brit Turner was the band's drummer before his death in March 2024. In 2018, they added touring members Benji Shanks (guitar) and Preston Holcomb (percussion). They have released eight studio albums, two live albums and five extended plays.

== History ==
Blackberry Smoke was formed in Atlanta, Georgia, in 2000. They are the first independently released artist to hit No. 1 on the [[Billboard Country Albums|Billboard Country Album]] charts in modern history. Their debut album, ''Bad Luck Ain't No Crime'', was released in 2003. Their second album released in 2009, ''Little Piece of Dixie''. has been featured by ''[[Paste (magazine)|Paste]]'' magazine and other publications.<ref name="pastemag">{{cite web |url=http://www.pastemagazine.com/blogs/whitman/2009/05/blackberry-smoke----little-piece-of-dixie.html |title=Blackberry Smoke – Little Piece of Dixie :: Blogs :: Andy Whitman on Music :: Paste |publisher=Pastemagazine.com |date=May 14, 2009 |access-date=January 9, 2013 |archive-date=March 5, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130305010734/http://www.pastemagazine.com/blogs/whitman/2009/05/blackberry-smoke----little-piece-of-dixie.html |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Phillips |first=Fred |url=http://somethingelsereviews.com/2012/08/12/blackberry-smoke-the-whippoorwill-2012/ |title=Blackberry Smoke – The Whippoorwill (2012) |date=August 12, 2012 |publisher=Somethingelsereviews.com |access-date=January 9, 2013}}</ref>

In 2015 Blackberry Smoke became the first small independent outfit to have an album, ''Holding All The Roses'', go to No. 1 on the Billboard Country Albums chart. This was the first time a non-mainstream-supported artist or band was able to achieve this feat in the modern era. Blackberry Smoke's 2016 album ''Like An Arrow'' did the same.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.savingcountrymusic.com/blackberry-smoke-goes-1-in-album-sales-with-you-hear-georgia/ |title=Blackberry Smoke Goes #1 in Album Sales with "You Hear Georgia" |work=Saving Country Music|date=June 7, 2021 }}</ref> They have performed throughout the United States, Europe, Australia, and South America. They have performed alongside numerous artists such as [[Tedeschi Trucks Band]], [[Guns n' Roses]], [[Zac Brown Band]], [[ZZ Top]], [[Eric Church]] and [[Lynyrd Skynyrd]].

[[File:Blackberry Smoke in 2012.jpg|thumb|upright=1.1|Blackberry Smoke performing in 2012]]
The band had their first chart success with their third album, ''[[The Whippoorwill]]'', released in August 2012, reaching Top 40 on [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref>{{cite web |url= http://rockrevoltmagazine.com/blackberry-smoke-video-pictures/ |title=Watch Blackberry Smoke's new video & check out live pics! |work=Rock Revolt Magazine|date=January 18, 2013 }}</ref> The album was released under the Southern Ground record label.<ref name="sga-label">{{cite web |url=http://www.southerngroundrecords.com/2011/05/12/zac-brown-launches-southern-ground-artists-label/ |title=Zac Brown Launches Southern Ground Artists Label « Southern Ground |publisher=Southerngroundrecords.com |date=May 12, 2011 |access-date=January 9, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120624075433/http://www.southerngroundrecords.com/2011/05/12/zac-brown-launches-southern-ground-artists-label/ |archive-date=June 24, 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref> On August 26, 2012, they performed at a charity benefit called the Boot Ride with the cast of ''[[Sons of Anarchy]]'', partnered with The Boot Campaign at the Happy Ending Bar and Grill in [[Hollywood, Los Angeles|Hollywood]], [[California]].<ref name="boot-ride">{{cite web |url=http://bootride.com/home.html |title=Boot Ride — Get Your Tickets NOW! |publisher=Bootride.com |access-date=January 9, 2013 |archive-date=August 17, 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120817235718/http://www.bootride.com/home.html |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://somethingelsereviews.com/2012/08/12/blackberry-smoke-the-whippoorwill-2012/|title=Blackberry Smoke – The Whippoorwill (2012) – Something Else!|work=Something Else!|date=August 12, 2012|access-date=February 13, 2015}}</ref>

On November 12, 2014, they appeared among many others – [[Jamey Johnson]], [[Trace Adkins]], [[Warren Haynes]], [[Peter Frampton]], [[Gregg Allman]], etc. – in a show in tribute to Lynyrd Skynyrd at the Fox Theatre in Atlanta.

They moved to [[Rounder Records]] in 2014,<ref name="BS-Rounder-release">{{cite web |url=http://www.blackberrysmoke.com/entry/blackberry-smoke-leave-zac-brown-s-label-ink-rounder-deal-for-new-album.html/ |title=Blackberry Smoke Leave Zac Brown's Label, Ink Rounder Deal for New Album |publisher=blackberrysmoke.com |date=November 6, 2014 |access-date=November 12, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141112155510/http://www.blackberrysmoke.com/entry/blackberry-smoke-leave-zac-brown-s-label-ink-rounder-deal-for-new-album.html/ |archive-date=November 12, 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref> and released ''[[Holding All the Roses]]'' produced by [[Brendan O'Brien (record producer)|Brendan O'Brien]] in early 2015.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.loudersound.com/reviews/blackberry-smoke-holding-all-the-roses-1 |title=Blackberry Smoke: Holding All The Roses |work=Louder Sound |first=Polly |last=Glass |date=January 23, 2015}}</ref> The album reached No. 1 on Billboard's Country Albums chart. The band spent much of the summer of 2016 on tour with [[Gov't Mule]].

Blackberry Smoke released their fifth studio album, ''[[Like an Arrow]]'', featuring Gregg Allman, on October 14, 2016, via their own record label, 3 Legged Records.<ref>{{cite web|title=Album of the Month (October 2016): Blackberry Smoke, 'Like an Arrow' Read More: Album of the Month (October 2016): Blackberry Smoke, 'Like an Arrow' |url=http://theboot.com/blackberry-smoke-like-an-arrow-october-2016-album/|website=The Boot|date=October 3, 2016 |publisher=Taste of Country Network|access-date=October 27, 2016}}</ref> The album landed at No. 1 on the US ''[[Billboard charts|Billboard]]'' Country<ref>{{cite magazine|title=Top Country Albums|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/country-albums|magazine=Billboard|publisher=Billboard|access-date=October 27, 2016}}</ref> and Americana/Folk<ref>{{cite magazine|title=Americana/Folk Albums|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/americana-folk-albums|magazine=Billboard|publisher=Billboard|access-date=October 27, 2016}}</ref> charts as well as the UK Rock<ref>{{cite web|title=Official Rock & Metal Albums Charts|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/charts/rock-and-metal-albums-chart/|website=Official Charts|publisher=The Official UK Charts Company|access-date=October 27, 2016}}</ref> and Independent Albums<ref>{{cite web|title=Official Independent Albums Chart Top 50|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/charts/independent-albums-chart/|website=Official Charts|publisher=The Official UK Charts Company|access-date=October 27, 2016}}</ref> charts during release week. The band released its sixth studio album, ''Find A Light'', on December 19, 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.blackberrysmoke.com/find-a-light|title=Blackberry Smoke – Find a Light|access-date=October 8, 2019|archive-date=October 8, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191008173715/https://www.blackberrysmoke.com/find-a-light|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In 2019, the band released via [[Earache Records]] a live album and a film ''Homecoming: Live in Atlanta'', recorded at their annual Brothers And Sisters Holiday Homecoming event at the [[Tabernacle (concert hall)|Tabernacle]] in Atlanta in November 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.loudersound.com/news/blackberry-smoke-announce-homecoming-live-in-atlanta-live-album-and-film |title=Blackberry Smoke announce Homecoming: Live In Atlanta live album and film|first= Fraser |last=Lewry |date= September 9, 2019 |work=Louder Sound}}</ref> In 2021 the band released its seventh studio album, ''You Hear Georgia.''<ref>{{Cite web|last=Ehrenclou|first=Martine|date=February 24, 2021|title=Blackberry Smoke To Release New Album 'You Hear Georgia' Out May 28th, Share Title Track|url=https://www.rockandbluesmuse.com/2021/02/24/blackberry-smoke-to-release-new-album-you-hear-georgia-out-may-28th-share-title-track/|access-date=October 13, 2021|website=Rock and Blues Muse|language=en-US}}</ref>

On May 28, 2021, their album ''[[You Hear Georgia]]'' was released. It debuted at No. 1 on the Billboard Americana/Folk Chart.<ref>{{cite magazine|url= https://www.billboard.com/charts/americana-folk-albums/2021-06-12/ |title=Blackberry Smoke Americana/Folk Charts|date= June 12, 2021 |magazine=Billboard}}</ref> It sold over 33k copies the first week, 12,239 copies in pure album sales. This was good enough to put it also at No. 1 in album sales in both country and rock, and No. 3 in all of music. The album also racked up another nearly 1 million streams its debut week. The album was produced by [[Dave Cobb]] and also features appearances by [[Jamey Johnson]] and [[Warren Haynes]].

In March 2024, the band announced drummer Brit Turner had died after a battle with [[glioblastoma]]. He was 57, and was diagnosed with the cancer in 2022.<ref>{{cite news |title=Blackberry Smoke Drummer Brit Turner Dies at 57 |url=https://therockrevival.com/rock-news/blackberry-smoke-drummer-brit-turner-dies-from-brain-cancer/ |access-date=4 March 2024 |publisher=The Rock Revival |date=4 March 2024}}</ref>

On April 5, 2024 Preston Holcomb announced via Facebook his last show would be April 27th and he would no longer be touring with the band.
