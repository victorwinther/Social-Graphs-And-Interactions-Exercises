'''Devin Dawson Durrett''' (born January 30, 1989) is an American [[country music]] singer and songwriter. He became known after filming a mashup of [[Taylor Swift]] songs with his fellow friend Louisa Wendorff on her [[YouTube]] channel, which gained popularity on YouTube.<ref name=people>{{Cite news|url=http://people.com/country/devin-dawson-all-on-me-ones-to-watch/|title=From Death Metal to Country, Why Devin Dawson Moved to Nashville to Get Away from Himself|work=People.com|date=October 10, 2017|accessdate=October 13, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.cmt.com/news/1787464/the-before-and-after-of-devin-dawson/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171012033348/http://www.cmt.com/news/1787464/the-before-and-after-of-devin-dawson/|url-status=dead|archive-date=October 12, 2017|title=The Before and After of Devin Dawson|work=CMT News|accessdate=October 13, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/country/7824548/devin-dawson-all-on-me-debut-single-interview|title=Former YouTube Phenom Devin Dawson Puts It 'All' on the Line in Debut Single|author=|date=June 7, 2017|website=billboard.com|accessdate=November 16, 2017}}</ref> His debut single "[[All on Me]]" is also his first hit song.<ref name=people /> With the help of producer [[Jay Joyce]], he signed to [[Warner Bros. Records]] in 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/devin-dawson-mn0003592778/biography|title=Devin Dawson biography|last=Monger|first=James Christopher|work=Allmusic|accessdate=October 22, 2017}}</ref> Dawson's "All on Me" has charted on the [[Hot Country Songs]] and [[Country Airplay]] charts.

Dawson was born in [[Orangevale, California]]. Before launching his solo career, he was the bass player for the [[Heavy metal music|heavy metal]] band Shadow of the Colossus.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/how-devin-dawson-went-from-heavy-metal-to-country-musics-dark-horse-628219/|title = How Devin Dawson Went from Heavy Metal to Country Music's 'Dark Horse'|magazine = [[Rolling Stone]]|date = May 16, 2018}}</ref>

Dawson has also toured with [[Brett Eldredge]], [[Tim McGraw]] and [[Faith Hill]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cmt.com/news/1787464/the-before-and-after-of-devin-dawson/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171012033348/http://www.cmt.com/news/1787464/the-before-and-after-of-devin-dawson/|url-status=dead|archive-date=October 12, 2017|title=The Before and After of Devin Dawson|author=|date=|website=[[CMT (American TV channel)|CMT]]|access-date=November 16, 2017}}</ref>

Dawson's debut album ''[[Dark Horse (Devin Dawson album)|Dark Horse]]'' was released on January 19, 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/business/8014773/warner-music-nashville-southwest-live-35-music|title=Warner Music Nashville and Southwest Formalize Partnership Bringing Live Music to the Skies: Exclusive|author=|date=October 26, 2017|website=billboard.com|accessdate=November 16, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://tasteofcountry.com/devin-dawson-dark-horse-album/ |title=Devin Dawson Announces Debut Album 'Dark Horse' |publisher=[[Taste of Country]] |date= October 26, 2017|accessdate=November 14, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.countrystandardtime.com/news/newsitem.asp?xid=9845 |title=Dawson goes with "Dark Horse" |publisher=[[Country Standard Time]] |date=October 23, 2007 |accessdate=November 14, 2017}}</ref>

Dawson's band includes Austin Taylor Smith (guitar, vocals), Nick DiMaria (lead guitar), Sam  Rodberg (bass guitar), and Kip Allen (drums).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/dark-horse-mw0003130823|title=Dark Horse credits|author=|date=|website=[[AllMusic]]|accessdate=August 3, 2019}}</ref>
