'''Justin Townes Earle''' (January 4, 1982{{spnd}}August 20, 2020) was an American singer-songwriter and musician. After his debut EP, ''Yuma'' (2007), he released eight full-length albums. He was recognized with an [[Americana Music Award]] for Emerging Artist of the Year in 2009 and for Song of the Year in 2011 for "[[Harlem River Blues]]". His father is [[alternative country]] artist [[Steve Earle]].

==Early life==
Earle grew up in [[South Nashville]], [[Tennessee]], with his mother, Carol Ann Hunter Earle. His father, [[Steve Earle]], named him in honor of his own mentor, singer and songwriter [[Townes Van Zandt]].<ref name="wsj24Nov2010">{{cite news|last=Dollar|first=Steve|date=November 24, 2010|title=Riding That Country Wagon|newspaper=[[Wall Street Journal]]|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB10001424052748703904804575631293749147382|access-date=December 28, 2010}}</ref> When Justin was two, his father left the family, but after Steve Earle got sober in 1994 he returned.<ref>{{cite book

==Career==
Earle played in two [[Nashville]] bands: the rock band the Distributors and the [[ragtime]] and [[bluegrass music|bluegrass]] combo the Swindlers.<ref name="CMT"/> He spent some time as guitarist and keyboardist for his father's touring band the Dukes.<ref name="CMT">{{cite web

=== Albums ===
In 2007, Earle released a six-song EP called ''Yuma''.<ref name="CMT" /> He signed a contract with Chicago's [[Bloodshot Records]] and released an album called ''The Good Life'' in 2008.<ref name="CMT" /> In 2009, he released the album ''[[Midnight at the Movies]]''.<ref name="CMT" /> In 2010, he released the album ''[[Harlem River Blues]]'', followed by the album ''[[Nothing's Gonna Change the Way You Feel About Me Now]]'' in 2012.<ref name="CMT" /> From 2014 to 2017, Earle released a "family trilogy" of albums, comprising ''[[Single Mothers (album)|Single Mothers]]'' (2014), ''[[Absent Fathers]]'' (2015), and ''[[Kids in the Street (Justin Townes Earle album)|Kids in the Street]]'' (2017). His last album, ''[[The Saint of Lost Causes]]'', was released in May 2019. Earle produced [[Wanda Jackson]]'s album ''[[Unfinished Business (Wanda Jackson album)|Unfinished Business]]'' in 2012.<ref>{{cite magazine|title=Wanda Jackson 'In the Studio'|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/videos/wanda-jackson-in-the-studio-20120710?stop_mobi=yes|access-date=February 13, 2015|magazine=Rolling Stone}}</ref>

== Honors and distinctions ==
In September 2009, Earle received an [[Americana Music Award]] for New and Emerging Artist of the Year.<ref name="Paste">{{cite web|author=Photo by Ray Kennedy|date=September 21, 2009|title=Buddy Miller, John Fogerty, Justin Townes Earle Amongst Notable Winners at Americana Music Awards|url=http://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2009/09/americana-music-awards-honor-buddy-miller-john-fog.html|work=[[Paste Magazine]]|access-date=May 11, 2017}}</ref>

In 2011, Earle received the [[Americana Music Award]] in the Song of the Year category for "Harlem River Blues".<ref name="Seattle 2012">{{cite news|author=DeBarros, Paul|date=July 12, 2012|title=Justin Townes Earle and Tristen pack a double punch|newspaper=[[The Seattle Times]]|url=http://www.seattletimes.com/entertainment/justin-townes-earle-and-tristen-pack-a-double-punch/|access-date=May 11, 2017}}</ref> His album of the same name has been described as having a "gently flowing, urban Americana sound, with horns, organ and tangy electric guitar".<ref name="Seattle 2012" /> That year he also contributed a cover of "[[Maybe Baby (song)|Maybe Baby]]" on the 2011 tribute album ''[[Rave on Buddy Holly]]'',<ref>{{cite web|title=Various Artists: "Rave on Buddy Holly"|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/rave-on-buddy-holly-mw0002093482|access-date=June 25, 2016|website=[[AllMusic]]}}</ref> and played [[Newport Folk Festival]]<ref>{{cite web|date=August 9, 2011|title=Review: Newport Folk Festival 2011|url=http://www.glidemagazine.com/64452/review-newport-folk-festival-2011/|access-date=June 25, 2016|website=Glidemagazine.com}}</ref> and the [[Hardly Strictly Bluegrass Festival]].<ref>{{cite web|title=HSB 2011 Schedule|url=http://www.hardlystrictlybluegrass.com/2011/print-at-home/HSB11_Schedule.pdf|access-date=June 25, 2016|website=Hardlystrictlybluegrass.com}}</ref>

''Nothing's Gonna Change the Way You Feel About Me Now'' was listed at album number 37 on ''[[Rolling Stone (magazine)|Rolling Stone]]'s'' list of the top 50 albums of 2012, with the annotation as follows: "The son of country-rock renegade Steve Earle has grown into a songwriter to rival his dad."<ref>{{cite magazine|date=December 11, 2012|title=Justin Townes Earle, Nothing's Gonna Change the Way You Feel About Me Now|magazine=[[Rolling Stone]]|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/lists/50-best-albums-of-2012-20121205/justin-townes-earle-nothing-s-gonna-change-the-way-you-feel-about-me-now-19691231|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121211175226/http://www.rollingstone.com/music/lists/50-best-albums-of-2012-20121205/justin-townes-earle-nothing-s-gonna-change-the-way-you-feel-about-me-now-19691231|archive-date=December 11, 2012|access-date=May 11, 2017}}</ref>

On January 4, 2021, Steve Earle & the Dukes released the album ''[[J.T. (album)|J.T.]]'', a tribute to Justin Townes Earle.

On January 4, 2023, a tribute concert was held at the Ryman Auditorium in memory of Justin. Performers included Steve Earle & The Dukes and special guests: Shooter Jennings, Bonnie Whitmore, Amanda Shires, Willy and Cody Braun, Buddy Miller, Ben Nichols (Lucero), Dustin Welch, Elizabeth Cook, Emmylou Harris, Jason Isbell, Jessica Lea Mayfield, Joe Pug, Jon Langford, Lilly Hiatt, Scotty Melton, Stacey Earle and Mark Stuart.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Justin Townes Earle Benefit Show |url=https://www.ryman.com/event/2023-01-04-justin-townes-earle-benefit-show-at-7-30-pm |website=Ryman Auditorium}}</ref>

==Personal life and death==
Earle began using drugs at age 12 and continued for many years. In his words, "I discovered very fast that my way of doing things was going to get me in trouble, and I kept going with it, because I believed the myth for a long time, and I believed I had to destroy myself to make great art."<ref>{{Cite magazine|last=Hiatt|first=Brian|date=2020-08-24|title=Justin Townes Earle: The Rolling Stone Interview|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/justin-townes-earle-interview-1048635/|access-date=2021-01-07|magazine=Rolling Stone|language=en-US}}</ref>

He went to rehabilitation clinics nine times followed by periods of [[sobriety]]. He relapsed in September 2010 and again in 2017.<ref name="wsj24Nov2010" /><ref name="CMT" /><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.nytimes.com/aponline/2010/09/23/arts/AP-US-Music-Justin-Townes-Earle.html?ref=arts |title=Earle Postpones Tour, Enters Rehab |newspaper=[[New York Times]] |date=September 23, 2010 |access-date=September 23, 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.louisville.com/content/justin-townes-earle-review-night-jail-cant-keep-him-down-music |title=Justin Townes Earle review: A night in jail can't keep him down. |publisher=[[Louisville Magazine|Louisville.com]] |date=September 18, 2010|access-date=May 11, 2017}}</ref>

Earle moved to New York City in 2009,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/2010/09/15/129886628/justin-townes-earle-from-nashville-to-new-york-city|title=Justin Townes Earle: From Nashville To New York City|website=Npr.org|access-date=September 14, 2024}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.nashvillescene.com/music/justin-townes-earle-returns-to-tennessee-a-step-ahead-of-his-demons-once-again/article_29706a59-8ab4-5550-8d3b-7a98a418a26c.html | title=Justin Townes Earle returns to Tennessee, a step ahead of his demons once again|website=Nashvillescene.com| date=November 24, 2010 }}</ref> but returned to Nashville in 2014.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.vice.com/en/article/6ax44r/justin-townes-earle-single-mothers-interview | title=Justin Townes Earle: Living for the Tiny Revelations|website=Vice.com | date=September 5, 2014 }}</ref> He married Jenn Marie Maynard in 2013<ref>{{cite web
 | url=http://www.nashvillescene.com/nashville/with-his-latest-nashvillian-justin-townes-earle-gives-praise-to-single-mothers-and-refuses-to-be-pushed-around/Content?oid=4589517 | title=With his latest, Nashvillian Justin Townes Earle gives praise to single mothers and refuses to be pushed around | work=[[Nashville Scene]]
 | date=September 4, 2014| access-date=September 13, 2014| author=Moss, Marissa}}</ref> and he and his wife lived on the West Coast.<ref name="wsj24Nov2010"/> Their only child, a daughter named Etta, was born in June 2017.<ref>{{cite web |last=McPherson |first=David |url=http://nodepression.com/article/dad-be-justin-townes-earle-looks-back-nashville-new-record |title=Dad-to-be Justin Townes Earle looks back at Nashville in new record |publisher=No Depression |access-date=July 11, 2017 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171027232509/http://nodepression.com/article/dad-be-justin-townes-earle-looks-back-nashville-new-record |archive-date=October 27, 2017 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Earle died on August 20, 2020, in Nashville, Tennessee, at the age of 38,<ref name="NYT">{{cite web|last1=Sisario|first1=Ben|date=August 24, 2020|title=Justin Townes Earle, singer-songwriter in father's footsteps, dies at 38|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/08/24/arts/music/justin-townes-earle-dead.html|website=The New York Times}}</ref> from an accidental overdose of [[fentanyl]]-laced [[cocaine]].<ref>
{{cite magazine |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/justin-townes-earle-cause-of-death-fentanyl-1097043/ |title=Justin Townes Earle Death Ruled an Accidental Drug Overdose |last=Bernstein |first=Jonathan |magazine=Rolling Stone |date=December 1, 2020 |access-date=December 1, 2020}}</ref>
