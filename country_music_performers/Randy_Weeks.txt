'''Randy Weeks''' is an American singer and songwriter. [[Lucinda Williams]] (who covered Weeks' song "[[Can't Let Go (Randy Weeks song)|Can't Let Go]]") has said: "Randy Weeks writes amazingly well crafted, beautifully melodic songs and delivers them with his own brand of laid back vocals and surfboard cool, very hip approach."<ref name=RW-sonicbids-2017>{{cite web |url=https://www.sonicbids.com/band/randyweeks/|title=Randy Weeks|work=Sonicbids|access-date=September 25, 2017}}</ref>

== Biography ==

Weeks was born and raised in Windom, Minnesota. He first played the drums, and by age 16 he performed in a local country band. Weeks moved to [[Minneapolis]], where he switched to guitar, and played in hard rock bands. He then moved to [[Los Angeles]] to further pursue his music career.<ref name=RW-conqueroo-2017>{{cite web |url=http://www.conqueroo.com/randyweeksbio.html|title=Randy Weeks Bio|work=Conqueroo|access-date=September 25, 2017|author=Cary Baker}}</ref><ref name=RW-uncommon-2009>{{cite web |url=http://www.uncommonmusic.org/2009/interview/10-questions-randy-weeks/|title=10 Questions: Randy Weeks|work=Uncommon Music|access-date=September 25, 2017|author=Nichole Wagner|date=March 12, 2009}}</ref>

===Lonesome Strangers===
After Weeks met Jeff Rymes, they formed the Los Angeles country-rock band Lonesome Strangers.<ref name=RW-nodep-2003>{{cite web |url=http://nodepression.com/article/randy-weeks-hello-stranger|title=Randy Weeks - Hello, Stranger|work=No Depression|access-date=September 26, 2017|author=Jesse Fox Mayshark|date=August 31, 2003}}</ref> In 1985, the Strangers recorded their first album, ''Lonesome Pine'' (Wrestler). [[Pete Anderson]] included the band on the compilation album ''A Town South of Bakersfield''.<ref name=RW-orlando-1989>{{cite web |url=https://www.orlandosentinel.com/1989/07/23/from-disband-to-band-the-story-of-lonesome-strangers/|title=From Disband To Band: The Story Of Lonesome Strangers|work=Orlando Sentinel|access-date=September 26, 2017|author=Jack Hurst|date=July 23, 1989}}</ref> After that, Hightone offered them a contract and they cut the album ''The Lonesome Strangers'' and ''Land of Opportunity'' in 1997.<ref name=RW-allcountry-1997>{{cite book 
|last=Erlewine|first=Michael|date=1997|title=All Music Guide to Country: The Experts' Guide to the Best Recordings in Country Music|isbn=9780879304751|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=7Mo7xm-X1r4C|access-date=September 25, 2017}}</ref>

===Session work and songwriting===
Weeks toured with [[Dwight Yoakam]], and contributed vocals to Yoakam's albums ''[[Buenas Noches from a Lonely Room]]'' and ''Under the Covers''.<ref name=RW-cmt-2003>{{cite web |url=http://www.cmt.com/news/1476329/hightone-signs-randy-weeks/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180214202715/http://www.cmt.com/news/1476329/hightone-signs-randy-weeks/|url-status=dead|archive-date=February 14, 2018|title=HighTone Signs Randy Weeks|work=CMT|access-date=September 26, 2017|date=August 8, 2003}}</ref> He sang and played on the 1989 self-titled album by [[Chris Gaffney (musician)|Chris Gaffney]] and the Cold Hard Facts, which featured Weeks' song "I Was Just Feeling Good."

===Solo career===
Weeks' debut solo album ''Madeline'' was released by [[HighTone Records]] in 2000. It also featured [[Tony Gilkyson]] (guitar), Kip Boardman (bass), and [[Don Heffington]] (drums).<ref name=RW-nodep-2000>{{cite web |url=http://nodepression.com/album-review/randy-weeks-madeline|title=Randy Weeks - Madeline|work=No Depression|access-date=September 25, 2017|author=Buzz Mcclain|date=April 30, 2000}}</ref>

Weeks' "[[Can't Let Go (Randy Weeks song)|Can’t Let Go]]" was the sole cover song and biggest hit on [[Lucinda Williams]]’ Grammy-winning album, ''[[Car Wheels on a Gravel Road]]''.<ref name=RW-morning-2011>{{cite book |last=Atkinson|first=Brian T.|date=November 28, 2011|title=I'll Be Here in the Morning: The Songwriting Legacy of Townes Van Zandt|isbn=9781603445276|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=2WUB2VkYegkC|access-date=September 25, 2017}}</ref> The song was also performed by [[Robert Plant]] and [[Alison Krauss]] on their 2021 album ''[[Raise the Roof (album)|Raise the Roof]]''.

From 2002 until 2006, Weeks played bi-monthly Saturday gigs at the Cinema Bar in [[Culver City, California|Culver City]]. When film director [[Peter Farrelly]] saw Weeks perform there, he included a Weeks song on the [[Shallow Hal]] film soundtrack. Other films such as [[Stuck on You (film)|Stuck on You]], [[Sunshine State (film)|Sunshine State]], and [[Jack Frost (1998 film)|Jack Frost]] also feature Weeks’ songs.<ref name=RW-conqueroo-2017 />

Weeks self-released ''Sold Out at the Cinema'' in 2004,<ref name=RW-nodep-2003 /> and followed it with ''Sugarfinger'' (produced by Jamie Candiloro) in 2006.<ref name=RW-jazz-2006>{{cite web |url=https://news.allaboutjazz.com/randy-weeks-sugarfinger-to-be-released-august-22.php?width=412|title=Randy Weeks' "Sugarfinger" To Be Released August 22|work=All About Jazz|access-date=September 26, 2017|date=June 27, 2006}}</ref>

Upon relocating to [[Austin, Texas]] from [[Los Angeles]] in 2007, Weeks signed with Certifiable Records, and released the album ''Going My Way'' in 2009. Helping out were Will Sexton, [[Eliza Gilkyson]], [[Cindy Cashdollar]], [[The Georgia Satellites|Rick Richards]], and [[Mark Hallman]].<ref name=RW-conqueroo-2017 />
