'''Ted Russell Kamp''' is an American singer-songwriter. He is the bassist for [[Shooter Jennings]]' backing band. As a solo artist, Kamp has released 11 albums.

==Biography==
Born in New York, Kamp grew up in the Northeast U.S. before moving to Seattle where he began his music career. He fronted a trio, Ponticello, which released four albums. During this time, Kamp released his first solo album, ''Dedications'' (1996). Kamp moved to Los Angeles in 2001 and started a new group, Union Pacific. He performed with other bands and worked as a session musician before being hired as the bassist for Shooter Jennings' band, the .357s.<ref>{{cite web|last=Ruhlmann|first=William|title=Artist Biography |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/ted-russell-kamp-mn0000053934/biography|website=[[Allmusic]]|accessdate=May 30, 2017}}</ref>

Kamp also continued his solo career, with his album Divisadero<ref>{{cite web|title=Album Review: Ted Russell Kamp - Divisadero|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/divisadero-mw0000565354 |website=Allmusic|accessdate=May 30, 2017}}</ref><ref>
{{cite journal | date=March–April 2007| title=Album Review: Ted Russell Kamp - Divisadero | last = Berick | first = Michael | journal=[[No Depression (magazine)|No Depression]]| volume=1| issue=68 | url=http://nodepression.com/album-review/ted-russell-kamp-divisadero| issn=1088-4971| accessdate=May 30, 2017}}</ref> ranked as #34 on the [[Americana Music Association]]'s 2007 chart.

In 2011, Kamp released Get Back to the Land,<ref>{{cite web|title=Album Review: Ted Russell Kamp - Get Back to the Land|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/get-back-to-the-land-mw0002092965 |website=Allmusic|accessdate=May 30, 2017}}</ref> which made the top 25 on the Americana Radio Chart and was #1 on the Euro Americana Chart.

In January 2013, Kamp released Night Owl,<ref>{{cite magazine|title=Music: Ted Russell Kamp - Night Owl|url=http://www.popmatters.com/review/170077-ted-russell-kamp-night-owl/ |magazine=[[PopMatters]]|accessdate=May 30, 2017}}</ref> which made the US Americana Radio Chart (#34) and the Euro Americana Chart (#15).

Kamp self-released ''The Low and Lonesome Sound'' in 2015, followed a year later by the acoustic album ''Flying Solo''.<ref>{{cite magazine|title=Music: Ted Russell Kamp - The Low and Lonesome Sound|url=http://www.popmatters.com/review/ted-russell-kamp-the-low-and-lonesome-sound/ |magazine=PopMatters|accessdate=May 30, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Bassist Ted Russell Kamp, Flying Solo...|date=8 April 2016 |url=http://bassmusicianmagazine.com/2016/04/flying-solo-featuring-bassist-ted-russell-kamp/ |publisher=[[Bass Musician]]|accessdate=May 30, 2017}}</ref> He joined Ted Wulfers to record the classic song Tulsa Time to help support the Red Cross of Oklahoma. 100% of the proceeds were donated to The Oklahoma Red Cross.

Kamp is also an established producer in the music industry.
