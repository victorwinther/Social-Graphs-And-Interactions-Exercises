'''Marsha Thornton''' (born October 22, 1964, in [[Killen, Alabama]]) is a female [[country music]] singer. Between 1990 and 2003, she released three studio albums, with her first two released on [[MCA Records]]; she has also issued four singles on the [[Hot Country Songs]] charts. Her highest peaking single was "A Bottle of Wine and Patsy Cline", written by Lindy Gravelle, which reached No.&nbsp;59 in 1990.

In 1991, Thornton was shortlisted for the [[Academy of Country Music Award for New Female Artist of the Year]].<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.worldradiohistory.com/Archive-All-Music/Billboard/90s/1991/BB-1991-02-02.pdf|title=Academy Of Country Music Names Award Nominees|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|pages=41|date=February 2, 1991}}</ref>
