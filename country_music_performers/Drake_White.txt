'''William Drake White''' (born October 3, 1983<ref name="times"/>) is an American [[country music]] singer and the leader of '''Drake White & The Big Fire'''.

==Early life==
White was born in [[Hokes Bluff, Alabama]],<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.al.com/entertainment/index.ssf/2016/04/meet_alabamas_newest_rising_co.html |title=Meet Alabama's newest rising country music star |author=Haley Laurence |date= April 14, 2016|work=AL.com }}</ref> where he attended Hokes Bluff High School.  White started writing his thoughts down when he was 14, some of which would later become lyrics in his songs.  He started singing when he was asked to join the youth choir at First Baptist Church of Hokes Bluff by his parents.<ref name="times"/> He later attended [[Gadsden State Community College]] and then [[Auburn University]], graduating with a degree in building science. Later, he worked with a general contractor in [[Nashville, Tennessee]] before quitting that job in favor of a musical career.<ref name="times">{{cite web|url=http://www.gadsdentimes.com/entertainment/20120606/drake-white-from-hokes-bluff-to-nashville|title=Drake White: From Hokes Bluff to Nashville |date=June 6, 2012| first=Lisa|last=Rogers|publisher=Gadsden Times}}</ref>

==Music career==
In concert, he and his band ("Drake White & The Big Fire") opened up performances for such musicians as [[Eric Church]], [[Luke Bryan]], [[Lynyrd Skynyrd]], [[Tyler Bryant]], and Channing Wilson. White has opened for [[Little Big Town]] and the [[Zac Brown Band]].

In early 2013, he released his debut single "Simple Life" through [[MCA Nashville]].<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-615/1560148/615-spotlight-drake-whites-simple-life|title=615 Spotlight: Drake White's 'Simple Life'|author=Chuck Dauphin |magazine=Billboard|publisher=}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.musicnewsnashville.com/drake-white-releases-debut-single-the-simple-life|title=Drake White Releases Debut Single "The Simple Life" - Music News Nashville|date=26 March 2013|publisher=|access-date=11 May 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160810062510/http://www.musicnewsnashville.com/drake-white-releases-debut-single-the-simple-life/|archive-date=10 August 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> White signed to [[Big Machine Records]]' re-establishment of [[Dot Records]] in June 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.musicrow.com/2014/06/bmlgs-dot-records-signs-drake-white/|title=BMLG's Dot Records Signs Drake White|date=20 June 2014|publisher=Music Row}}</ref> The first single, "It Feels Good" released to country radio on February 24, 2015. The second single, "Livin' the Dream" released to country radio on December 7, 2015. On August 19, 2016, White released "Spark", his debut album  under Dot Records.  It charted at No. 4 on the Top Country Albums chart.<ref>{{cite magazine |url= http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/7494561/dolly-parton-back-at-no-1-on-top-country-albums-after-25-years |title=Dolly Parton Back at No. 1 on Top Country Albums After 25 Years |date=August 30, 2016 |first= Jim|last= Asker |magazine=Billboard }}</ref>  The album's third single, "Makin' Me Look Good Again", was released in 2016.  A new 5-song EP titled "Pieces" was released on May 4, 2018.

==Personal life==
In January 2019, White was diagnosed with [[arteriovenous malformation]] (AVM), a rare vascular disorder.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cnn.com/2019/08/22/entertainment/drake-white-brain-disorder-trnd/index.html|title=Country singer Drake White diagnosed with debilitating brain disorder|date=August 22, 2019|publisher=[[CNN]]}}</ref>
