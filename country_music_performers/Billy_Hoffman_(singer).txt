'''Billy Ray Hoffman''' is an American [[country music]] singer and songwriter. He released one album, ''All I Wanted Was You'', in 2000. This album charted the singles "Perfect Night" and "You're the Ticket".

==Biography==
Billy Ray Hoffman was born in [[Arkansas]] and raised in [[Poteau, Oklahoma]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://countrymusic.about.com/library/blbillyhoffmanbio.htm|title=Billy Hoffman Bio|work=[[About.com]]|accessdate=May 16, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091126093049/http://countrymusic.about.com/library/blbillyhoffmanbio.htm|archive-date=November 26, 2009|url-status=dead}}</ref> He was born with a hearing problem and is only three percent away from being [[deafness|deaf]]. He also learned to play guitar to gain dexterity after being born with underdeveloped hands.<ref>{{cite news|last=Roberts|first=M. B.|title=Who's New ... Billy Hoffman|url=http://www.countryweekly.com/vault/whos-new-billy-hoffman|accessdate=May 16, 2012|newspaper=[[Country Weekly]]|date=May 7, 2000 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150912173236/http://www.countryweekly.com/vault/whos-new-billy-hoffman|archive-date=September 12, 2015}}</ref>

In 2000, he was the first artist signed to Critter Records, which was managed by [[Joe Stampley]].<ref>{{cite news|title=COUNTRY BEAT: Billy Ray Cyrus, Kathy Mattea, Robbie Fulks ...|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1224076/country-beat-billy-ray-cyrus-kathy-mattea.jhtml|accessdate=May 16, 2012|newspaper=[[MTV]]|date=October 27, 2000|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140427013435/http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1224076/country-beat-billy-ray-cyrus-kathy-mattea.jhtml|archivedate=April 27, 2014}}</ref> Stampley produced Hoffman's debut album, which included a cover of [[Paul Davis (singer)|Paul Davis]]' "[[I Go Crazy (Paul Davis song)|I Go Crazy]]."<ref>{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p437184/biography|pure_url=yes}}|title=Billy Hoffman Biography|work=[[Allmusic]]|accessdate=May 16, 2012}}</ref> Two singles from the album charted on the ''Billboard'' [[Hot Country Songs|Hot Country Singles & Tracks]] chart.<ref name="whitburn">{{cite book|last=Whitburn|first=Joel|title=Hot Country Songs 1944 to 2008|publisher=Record Research, Inc|page=191|year=2008|isbn=978-0-89820-177-2}}</ref> The album received a mixed review from Ray Waddell of ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'', who wrote that "while lacking the vocal gymnastics heard in much of what populates current country radio, Hoffman's easygoing style mostly serves this material well."<ref>{{cite news|last=Waddell|first=Ray|title=Reviews & Previews|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=-g8EAAAAMBAJ&q=billy+hoffman&pg=PA39|accessdate=May 16, 2012|newspaper=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=July 15, 2000}}</ref> Hoffman retired from the music business after his first album and moved back to Poteau to focus on various business ventures.<ref name="ok"/>
