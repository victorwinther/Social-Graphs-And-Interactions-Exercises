'''Beverley Ann Mitchell''' (born January 22, 1981)<ref>[https://www.yahoo.com/entertainment/news/haylie-duff-7th-heaven-costar-beverley-mitchell-both-220500955-us-weekly.html?guccounter=1&guce_referrer=aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuZ29vZ2xlLmNvbS8&guce_referrer_sig=AQAAAIb30izdOK_Co5hYAiUGarOJ1Y2o92i7Tx2JjgrNJIPINT4gbIXSLGeDBuyne_YwxktkkyCxiUeWnCsDxc8Q4YvpzUOT0NloyTfMT48ySeJzM1EDqzVRg979kLv4xnOlknxdmnptldRIW7lf6Fc62uq4MdELAgqsN4SvnBuxTxQq Haylie Duff and 7th Heaven Costar Beverley Mitchell, Both Pregnant, Bond in Sweet Photo]</ref> is an American actress. She portrayed Lucy Camden on the television [[The WB]]/[[The CW]] series ''[[7th Heaven (TV series)|7th Heaven]]''.

== Early life ==
In 1999, Mitchell graduated from [[Chaminade College Preparatory School (California)|Chaminade College Preparatory School]] and then began studying film production at [[Loyola Marymount University]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://stantondaily.com/beverly-mitchell-then-now/?singlepage=1|title=Beverley Mitchell Then & Now! – Stanton Daily|website=stantondaily.com|access-date=January 26, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180127084029/http://stantondaily.com/beverly-mitchell-then-now/?singlepage=1|archive-date=January 27, 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Career==
{{BLP sources section|date=January 2023}}
Mitchell's first major film role was at age 15 in ''[[The Crow: City of Angels]]'', playing teenaged drug addict Grace. During the filming of the 1996 movie, she unsuccessfully auditioned for the role of Mary Camden in the new family drama, ''7th Heaven''. She then read for the part of sister Lucy and was cast.

In 2000, Mitchell had a small part in ''[[The Amanda Show]]'' as a customer in ''Moody's Point''. In 2003, she played [[Erica Enders]] in the [[Disney Channel]] original movie ''[[Right on Track]]'', based on the early career of the 2014 [[NHRA Mission Foods Drag Racing Series|NHRA Mello Yello Drag Racing Series]] [[Pro stock|Pro Stock]] champion and her sister [[Courtney Enders]], who was played by [[Brie Larson]]. During a hiatus from filming ''7th Heaven'' in 2005, Mitchell played [[List of Saw characters#Laura Hunter|Laura Hunter]] in the horror sequel ''[[Saw II]]''. In 2006, she recorded her debut, self-titled country album. She co-wrote eight of the songs featured on the album, including "Angel", which is about her friend who was killed in a car accident when the two were teenagers.<ref>{{cite book|title=Chicken Soup for the Preteen Soul|year=2001|publisher=Scholastic, Inc.|isbn=9781453278994|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=gnJTTy7RFNcC&q=Beverley+Mitchell}}</ref> Mitchell starred in the 2007 TV movie ''I Know What I Saw'', which aired on the [[Lifetime (TV network)|Lifetime]] network. 

In 2010, Mitchell took on the role of school counselor Katelyn O'Malley on the third season of ''[[The Secret Life of the American Teenager]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tvguide.com/news/mitchell-secret-life-1020731/|title=Exclusive: 7th Heaven Star Joins Secret Life|work=TVGuide|first=Gina|last=DiNunno|date=July 20, 2010|access-date=December 6, 2019}}</ref> In March 2016, she was cast in the [[Pop (American TV channel)|Pop]] ''[[Hollywood Darlings]]'' with fellow 1990s' child stars [[Jodie Sweetin]] and [[Christine Lakin]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/jodie-sweetin-christine-lakin-beverley-874735|title=Jodie Sweetin, Christine Lakin, Beverley Mitchell to Star in Pop Comedy (Exclusive)|first=Lesley|last=Goldberg|date=March 15, 2016|work=The Hollywood Reporter}}</ref> The series premiered April 12, 2017.

In April 2024, it was announced that Mitchell would co-host along with her former co-stars, [[Mackenzie Rosman]] and [[David Gallagher]], a 7th Heaven rewatch podcast named, Catching up with the Camdens!<ref>{{cite web|url=https://people.com/beverley-mitchell-david-gallagher-and-mackenzie-rosman-debut-7th-heaven-podcast-exclusive-8637330|title=Beverley Mitchell, David Gallagher and Mackenzie Rosman Take Fans to 7th Heaven with New Rewatch Podcast (Exclusive)|work=People.com|first=Esther|last=Kang|date=April 23, 2024|accessdate=May 29, 2024}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Mitchell married her long-time boyfriend Michael Cameron on October 1, 2008, in [[Ravello]], [[Italy]] on the [[Amalfi Coast]]. Former ''7th Heaven'' castmates [[Jessica Biel]] and [[Mackenzie Rosman]] served as bridesmaids.<ref name="wedding1">{{cite web|url=https://www.tvguide.com/news/beverley-mitchell-gets-19263/|title=Beverley Mitchell Gets Married|work=TVGuide|access-date=December 6, 2019}}</ref><ref name="wedding2">{{cite web|url= https://people.com/celebrity/beverley-mitchell-gets-married-in-italy/|title=Beverley Mitchell Gets Married in Italy|work=People|access-date=December 6, 2019|first=Ulrica|last=Wihlborg}}</ref> They have a son<ref>{{cite web |title=Beverley Mitchell Welcomes Son Hutton Michael |url=https://people.com/parents/beverley-mitchell-welcomes-son-hutton-michael/ |website=People |access-date=September 7, 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210120141955/https://people.com/parents/beverley-mitchell-welcomes-son-hutton-michael/ |archive-date=January 20, 2021 |url-status=live}}</ref> and two daughters.<ref>{{cite web|date=April 2, 2013|title=Beverley Mitchell Welcomes Daughter Kenzie Lynne|url=https://people.com/parents/beverley-mitchell-welcomes-daughter-kenzie-lynne/|work=People|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220724093712/https://people.com/parents/beverley-mitchell-welcomes-daughter-kenzie-lynne/ |archive-date=July 24, 2022 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Harmata |first1=Claudia |title=7th Heaven Star Beverley Mitchell Welcomes Third Child After Miscarriage: 'We Are Over The Moon' |url=https://people.com/parents/beverley-mitchell-welcomes-third-child-after-miscarriage/ |website=People |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220217233257/https://people.com/parents/beverley-mitchell-welcomes-third-child-after-miscarriage/ |archive-date=February 17, 2022 |url-status=live}}</ref>
