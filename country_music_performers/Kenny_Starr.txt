'''Kenneth Trebbe''' (born September 21, 1952),<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/kenny-starr-mn0001610088|title=Kenny Starr &#124; Album Discography|website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=August 10, 2021}}</ref> known professionally as '''Kenny Starr''', is an American [[country music|country]] singer.<ref name="LarkinCountry">{{cite book|title=[[Encyclopedia of Popular Music|The Guinness Who's Who of Country Music]]|editor=[[Colin Larkin (writer)|Colin Larkin]]|publisher=[[Guinness Publishing]]|date=1993|edition=First|isbn=0-85112-726-6|page=391}}</ref>

== Biography ==
Starr was born in [[Topeka, Kansas]] and grew up in [[Burlingame, Kansas]].<ref name="LarkinCountry" /> At the age of nine, he fronted his own group, the Rockin' Rebels, and in his teens he played at local clubs leading a pop group, Kenny and the Imperials.<ref name="LarkinCountry" /> Later switching to country music, a win in a local talent contest led him to perform on a bill containing [[Conway Twitty]] and [[Loretta Lynn]].<ref name="LarkinCountry" /> Lynn encouraged his efforts and, with her support, Starr secured a recording contract with [[MCA Records]].<ref name="LarkinCountry" />

He recorded for MCA between 1973 and 1978, charting 13 [[single (music)|singles]],<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/kenny-starr/chart-history/csi/|title=Kenny Starr|website=Billboard.com|access-date=August 10, 2021}}</ref> and releasing one studio album. For much of the 1970s, he was a member of Loretta Lynn's touring band, the Coal Miners.<ref name="LarkinCountry" />

Starr's biggest hit was a [[cover version|cover]] of [[David Geddes (musician)|David Geddes]]'s single "The Blind Man in the Bleachers".<ref name="LarkinCountry" /> Starr's version was a hit single in the U.S. on the country charts in 1976, reaching No.&nbsp;2 on the Country Singles chart,<ref name="auto" /> and was a minor hit on the pop charts peaking at No.&nbsp;58 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] and went to No.&nbsp;1 on the country chart in Canada. The album from which it was taken, also called ''The Blind Man in the Bleachers'', reached No.&nbsp;12 on the [[Billboard magazine|Billboard]] Country Albums chart.
