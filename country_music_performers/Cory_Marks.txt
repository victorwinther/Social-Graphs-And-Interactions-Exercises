'''Cory Gerald Joseph Marquardt''' (born October 11, 1989<ref name="Nugget">{{cite news|title=Marquardt signs record deal|url=https://www.nugget.ca/entertainment/music/marquardt-signs-record-deal |work=[[North Bay Nugget]]|publisher=[[Postmedia Network]]|date=September 26, 2018}}</ref>) is a Canadian [[country rock]] singer-songwriter better known by his stage name '''Cory Marks'''. He is signed to [[Better Noise Music]].<ref name="Invictus">{{cite news|title=Cory Marks - Biography|url=http://www.iegroup.ca/artists/cory-marks/|work=Invictus Entertainment Group|accessdate=August 14, 2020}}</ref> He has released two albums, ''[[This Man (album)|This Man]]'' in 2015 under Big Star Recordings, and most recently ''[[Who I Am (Cory Marks album)|Who I Am]]'' in 2020 under Better Noise. Since the release, the album has accumulated over 260 million global streams with his debut single "[[Outlaws & Outsiders]]" being certified Platinum in Canada, Gold in the US and reaching top 10 at US rock radio and #3 in Germany, making Marks the first to ever do so as a Canadian country act.

==Early life==
Marks was raised in [[North Bay, Ontario]], the younger brother of professional hockey player [[Matt Marquardt]], and learned how to skate at three years old.<ref>{{cite web|title= Cory Marks rise to stardom|url=https://www.mainstreet-nashville.com/opinion/cory-marks-rise-to-stardom/article_aca7ec08-f430-11ea-a269-d7e44b38b3ff.html|first=Bethany|last=Bowman|date=September 11, 2020}}</ref> He cites [[Merle Haggard]], [[Waylon Jennings]], [[Bryan Adams]], [[Ozzy Osbourne]], and [[Rush (band)|Rush]] as early musical influences.<ref>{{cite news|title=Cory Marks - Bio|url=http://www.corymarks.com/bio|work=CoryMarks.com|accessdate=August 15, 2020}}</ref> Marks played university hockey at the [[Royal Military College of Canada]] where he also pursued dreams of becoming a fighter pilot.<ref>{{cite news|title=Cory Marks hits a live wire with "Outlaws and Outsiders"|url= https://www.mynorthbaynow.com/49948/cory-marks-hits-a-live-wire-with-outlaws-and-outsiders/|first=Kortney|last=Kenney|work=My North Bay Now|date=November 20, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=INTERVIEW: CORY MARKS ‚Äì Ontario, CA; 2020|url=https://www.musicalypse.net/cory-marks-ontario-ca-2020/|work=Musicalypse|date=March 12, 2020}}</ref> He had never performed music publicly until his friends encouraged him to do so while at a bar in college.<ref>{{cite news|title=Interview~ Mixing country music with the hard-hitting stomp of rock & roll, Cory Marks|url=https://ignitemusicmag.com/2020/03/31/interview-mixing-country-music-with-the-hard-hitting-stomp-of-rock-roll-cory-marks/|first=Bryan Joe|last=Corder|work=Ignite Music Magazine|date=March 31, 2020}}</ref> He has performed using the name "Cory Marks" since the release of his debut album.<ref name="Nugget"/> Marks achieved his private pilot license in 2021.<ref>{{cite news|title=Cory Marks' single certified Gold|url=https://www.nugget.ca/news/cory-marks-single-certified-gold |work=[[North Bay Nugget]]|first=Michael|last=Lee|publisher=[[Postmedia Network]]|date=April 29, 2021}}</ref>

==Career==
===2014‚Äì2015: ''This Man''===
In July 2014, he released his first single to Canadian country radio, "Smartphone".<ref name="Smartphone"/> His second single, "21", was released in April 2015.<ref name="TwentyOne"/> His first album, ''[[This Man (album)|This Man]]'' was released on May 26, 2015 through Big Star Recordings.<ref name="ThisMan">{{cite web|title=Cory Marquardt releases his first album "This Man"|url=https://www.kissnorthbay.com/2015/05/26/cory-marquardts-releases-his-first-album-this-man/|work=Kiss 100.5 North Bay|date=May 26, 2016}}</ref><ref name="BayT">{{cite news|last=Dawson|first=Chris|title=Marquardt releases first album today|url= https://www.baytoday.ca/local-news/marquardt-releases-first-album-today-18775|work=BayToday.ca|date=May 26, 2015}}</ref> The album included his first two singles, as well as two further singles, "This Man",<ref name="TM">{{cite web|title= INTRODUCING, THIS MAN, CORY MARQUARDT|url= https://www.soundcheckentertainment.ca/introducing-this-man-cory-marquardt/|work=Sound Check Entertainment|date= July 26, 2015|accessdate=August 14, 2020}}</ref> and "Nowhere With You".<ref name="NWY">{{cite web|title=New video released by Cory Marquardt|url= https://canadianbeats.ca/2016/01/06/new-video-released-by-cory-marquardt-3/|work=Canadian Beats Media|first=Jenna|last=Melanson|date=January 6, 2016}}</ref>

=== 2016‚Äì2020: ''Who I Am'' ===
In 2016, Marks signed a deal to work with award-winning producer [[Kevin Churko]], known for work with [[Ozzy Osbourne]], [[Shania Twain]], and [[Five Finger Death Punch]].<ref>{{cite web|title=More good news for local artist Cory Marquardt|url=https://www.baytoday.ca/local-news/more-good-news-for-local-artist-cory-marquardt-305484|work=BayToday.ca|first=Chris|last=Dawson|date=May 27, 2016}}</ref> In 2018, he signed a record deal with [[Eleven Seven Label Group|Eleven Seven Music]] (later rebranded as [[Better Noise Music]]) as their flagship country artist.<ref name="Nugget"/><ref>{{cite web|title=Cory Marks Signs Record Deal with Eleven Seven Music|url=http://www.iegroup.ca/news/cory-marks-signs-record-deal-eleven-seven-music/|work=Invictus Entertainment Group|date=September 26, 2018}}</ref>

In November 2019, Marks released his first single on Better Noise, "[[Outlaws & Outsiders]]", featuring [[Ivan Moody (vocalist)|Ivan Moody]], [[Travis Tritt]], and [[Mick Mars]],<ref>{{cite web|title=North Bay's Cory Marks' new song is number one on iTunes Canada|url= https://northernontario.ctvnews.ca/north-bay-s-cory-marks-new-song-is-number-one-on-itunes-canada-1.4703034|first=Jessica|last=Gosselin|work=[[CTV News]] Northern Ontario|date=November 26, 2019}}</ref> which debuted at #1 on [[iTunes]] in Canada as well as #1 on the [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] [[Rock Digital Songs]] and [[Rock Digital Songs|Hard Rock Digital Songs]] charts and #7 on the [[Country Digital Song Sales|Country Digital Songs]] charts.<ref>{{cite web|title= Cory Marks Throws Down in Single "Outlaws & Outsiders" ft. Travis Tritt, Ivan Moody & Mick Mars|url= http://frontporchmusic.ca/music/cory-marks-outlaws-outsiders/|work=Front Porch Music|date=January 26, 2020}}</ref><ref name="Billboard"/> The song would later peak inside the top 10 on US [[Mainstream Rock (chart)|Mainstream Rock]] radio, top 5 on German rock radio, and peaked at #12 on [[Canada Rock]] radio.<ref>{{cite web|title=Big day for North Bay's Cory Marks|url=https://www.baytoday.ca/local-news/big-day-for-north-bays-cory-marks-2617951|work=BayToday.ca|date=August 7, 2020}}</ref><ref name="CanRock"/> It was also certified Gold by [[Music Canada]].<ref>{{cite web|first=Chris|last=Dawson|title=Cory Marks is golden|url=https://www.timminstoday.com/local-news/cory-marks-is-golden-3681473|work=Timmins Today|date=May 1, 2021}}</ref>

In June 2020, Marks released "[[Drive (Cory Marks song)|Drive]]",<ref name="Drive"/><ref name="Drive2"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://themusicuniverse.com/cory-marks-announces-who-i-am/|title= CORY MARKS ANNOUNCES 'WHO I AM'|work=The Music Universe|first=Buddy|last=Iahn|date=June 5, 2020}}</ref> and announced it as the second single to country radio off his second album, ''[[Who I Am (Cory Marks album)|Who I Am]]'', which was released on August 7, 2020 through Better Noise.<ref name="WhoIAm"/><ref name="CountryNote">{{cite news|title=Cory Marks, The Critically Acclaimed Next Generation of Country Rock, Releases Debut Album, WHO I AM|url=https://www.thecountrynote.com/in-the-news/cory-marks-the-critically-acclaimed-next-generation-of-country-rock-releases-debut-album-who-i-am/|date=August 7, 2020|work=The Country Note}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://themusicuniverse.com/cory-marks-who-i-am-debuts-worldwide/|title= CORY MARKS 'WHO I AM' DEBUTS WORLDWIDE|work=The Music Universe|first=Buddy|last=Iahn|date=August 11, 2020}}</ref> In October 2020, "[[Blame It on the Double]]" was released as the second single to rock radio in Germany and the United Kingdom.<ref name="BOD">{{cite web|url=https://twitter.com/corymarksmusic/status/1313249605916733440|title=Cory Marks on Twitter: "Very excited to announce that my single #BlameItOnTheDouble is now at UK & [German] rock radio!! ü•Éü•É @BetterNoise üá¨üáßüá®üá¶"|via=Twitter|accessdate=October 5, 2020}}</ref> Marks also released his first Christmas single "Jingle My Bells", a cover of a song by [[The Tractors]], that month.<ref name="JMB"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://themusicuniverse.com/cory-marks-releasing-jingle-my-bells/|title=CORY MARKS RELEASING 'JINGLE MY BELLS'|work=The Music Universe|first=Buddy|last=Iahn|date=October 8, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Cory Marks has a new Christmas tune and it's catchy!|url=https://www.baytoday.ca/local-news/cory-marks-has-a-new-christmas-tune-and-its-catchy-3142123|work=BayToday.ca|first=Chris|last=Dawson|date=December 1, 2020}}</ref> In April 2021, Marks released a new version of "Blame It on the Double" featuring Tyler Connolly of [[Theory of a Deadman]] and [[Jason Hook]].<ref>{{cite news|title=CORY MARKS PUTS THE HAMMER DOWN FOR 'BLAME IT ON THE DOUBLE' COLLAB|first=Chris|last=Parton|url=https://www.soundslikenashville.com/music/cory-marks-blame-it-on-the-double-collab/|work=Sounds Like Nashville|date=April 30, 2021|accessdate=May 4, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine|title=Cory Marks Drops Single With Jason Hook & Theory of a Deadman's Tyler Connolly|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/rock/cory-marks-blame-it-double-single-video-9565706/|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|first=Dave|last=Brooks|date=April 30, 2021}}</ref>

=== 2021‚Äìpresent: ''Sorry for Nothing'' and extended plays ===
On June 18, 2021, Marks released the extended play ''Nashville Mornings''. It included a cover of [[Blaze Foley]]'s "If I Could Only Fly", as well as a "Country Mix" of "[[Blame It on the Double]]" featuring Tyler Connolly, which was sent to Canadian country radio.<ref name="NM">{{cite web|title=CORY MARKS' NEW 'NASHVILLE MORNINGS' EP OFFERS REFRESHED APPROACH TO THE COUNTRY-ROCKER'S CATALOGUE|url=https://rocknloadmag.com/news/cory-marks-new-nashville-mornings-ep-offers-refreshed-approach-to-the-country-rockers-catalogue/|website=Rock N' Load Magazine|date=June 17, 2021|access-date=June 18, 2021|archive-date=June 18, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210618113122/https://rocknloadmag.com/news/cory-marks-new-nashville-mornings-ep-offers-refreshed-approach-to-the-country-rockers-catalogue/|url-status=dead}}</ref> He followed that up with the extended play ''Nashville Nights'' on August 20, 2021, which included a new track "In Me I Trust",<ref>{{cite web|title=Cory Marks - In Me I Trust 
|url=https://newcountrysongs.com/cory-marks/in-me-i-trust/|work=New Country Songs|accessdate=August 31, 2021}}</ref> the solo version of "[[Outlaws & Outsiders]]", and live versions of "[[Drive (Cory Marks song)|Drive]]" and "Blame It on the Double".<ref name="Nights">{{cite web|url=https://tuonelamagazine.com/review-cory-marks-ep-nashville-nights/|title=REVIEW: Cory Marks ‚Äì EP: Nashville Nights|work=Tuonela Magazine|date=August 13, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://canadianbeats.ca/2021/08/31/cory-marks-leaps-into-the-world-of-rock-heavy-with-the-release-of-his-latest-ep-nashville-nights-and-announces-his-fall-tour-dates/|title=Cory Marks leaps into the world of rock-heavy with the release of his latest EP, Nashville Nights and announces his fall tour dates|first=Nanci|last=Dagg|date=August 31, 2021|work=Canadian Beats Media}}</ref>

In November 2022, Marks released the extended play ''I Rise'', which included the singles "Burn It Up" and "Flying".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.music-news.com/news/Underground/153522/Cory-Marks-releases-new-EP-I-Rise|title=Cory Marks releases new EP 'I Rise'|date=November 11, 2022|access-date=November 18, 2022|work=Music-News.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|first=Jenna|last=Melanson|title=Cory Marks unveils new EP, I Rise (Interview)|url=https://canadianbeats.ca/2022/11/16/cory-marks-unveils-new-ep-i-rise-interview|work=Canadian Beats Media|date=November 16, 2022|access-date=November 22, 2022}}</ref> He supported [[Five Finger Death Punch]] on their headlining tour in the United States in the fall and winter of 2022 alongside [[Brantley Gilbert]].<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/pro/cory-marks-major-tour-pandemic-delayed-live-music/|title=Cory Marks Ready to Support a Major Tour, After 3-Year Pandemic Delay|date=November 4, 2022|access-date=November 18, 2022|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|first=Dave|last=Brooks|url-access=subscription}}</ref>
