'''Gerald Calvin "Jerry" Douglas''' (born May 28, 1956) is an American [[Dobro]] and [[lap steel guitar]] player and [[record producer]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.jambase.com/Articles/2424/AN-INTERVIEW-WITH-JERRY-DOUGLASBluegrass-For-A-New-Century |title=An Interview with Jerry Douglas: Bluegrass for a New Century |website=Jambase.com |date=11 May 2002 |access-date=3 October 2015 |author=Geoff Harrison |url-status=bot: unknown |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130519200702/http://www.jambase.com/Articles/2424/AN-INTERVIEW-WITH-JERRY-DOUGLASBluegrass-For-A-New-Century |archive-date=19 May 2013}}</ref> He is widely regarded as "perhaps the finest Dobro player in contemporary acoustic music, and certainly the most celebrated and prolific".<ref name="AllMusic">{{cite web |last1=Huey |first1=Steve |title=Jerry Douglas Biography |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/jerry-douglas-mn0000327130/biography |website=AllMusic.com |access-date=5 February 2023}}</ref> A fourteen-time Grammy winner, he has been called "dobro's matchless contemporary master" by The New York Times, and is among the most innovative recording artists in music,<ref>{{cite web |title=Artist Information - Jerry Douglas |url=https://www.opry.com/artists/jerry-douglas/ |website=OPRY.com |access-date=5 February 2023}}</ref> both as a solo artist and member of numerous bands, such as [[Alison Krauss and Union Station]] and [[The Earls of Leicester]]. He has been a co-director of the [[Transatlantic Sessions]] since 1998.

==Career==
[[File:Jerry Douglas (26955774667).jpg|thumb|upright=1.2|{{center|Watson Stage - MerleFest 2018}}]]
[[File:Jerry Douglas 2006.jpg|thumb|upright=1.2|Jerry Douglas playing one of his [[resonator guitar]]s]]

In addition to his fourteen solo recordings, Douglas has played on more than 1,600 albums.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.jerrydouglas.com/disc.cfm |title=Welcome to the Official Jerry Douglas Website |website=Jerrydouglas.com |access-date=2016-01-10 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160126194102/http://jerrydouglas.com/disc.cfm |archive-date=2016-01-26}}</ref><ref>[https://thestarphoenix.com/entertainment/local-arts/review-jerry-douglas-gives-bluegrass-the-cordon-bleu-treatment "REVIEW: Jerry Douglas gives bluegrass the cordon bleu treatment"]. Cam Fuller, ''Saskatoon StarPhoenix'', June 28, 2018</ref>  As a sideman, he has recorded with artists as diverse as [[Garth Brooks]], [[Ray Charles]], [[Eric Clapton]], [[Phish]], [[Dolly Parton]], [[Susan Ashton]], [[Paul Simon]], [[Mumford & Sons]], [[Keb' Mo']], [[Ricky Skaggs]], [[Elvis Costello]], [[Tommy Emmanuel]], [[James Taylor]] and [[Johnny Mathis]], as well as performing on the ''[[O Brother, Where Art Thou?]]'' soundtrack and the follow-up "Down From the Mountain" tour with [[Alison Krauss and Union Station]].<ref>{{Cite magazine |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/mountain-summer-tour-dates-come-on-down-76425/ |title='Mountain' Summer Tour Dates Come on Down |magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |date=March 18, 2002}}</ref> He has collaborated with various groups including [[The Whites]], [[New South (band)|New South]], [[The Country Gentlemen]], [[Strength in Numbers (band)|Strength in Numbers]], and [[Elvis Costello]]'s "Sugar Canes".

From 1996 to 1998, Douglas was a member of [[The GrooveGrass Boyz]].<ref name="groovegrass">{{cite web |url={{Allmusic|class=artist|id=p350665/biography|pure_url=yes}} |title=GrooveGrass biography |accessdate=2008-08-21 |last=Ankeny |first=Jason |publisher=[[Allmusic]]}}</ref>

Douglas produced a number of records, including some at [[Sugar Hill Records (bluegrass label)|Sugar Hill Records]]. He oversaw albums by [[Alison Krauss]], the [[Del McCoury Band]], [[Maura O'Connell]], [[Jesse Winchester]] and the [[Nashville Bluegrass Band]], [[The Earls of Leicester]], [[Gary Morris]], [[The Steep Canyon Rangers]]. Along with [[Aly Bain]], he serves as Music Director of the popular BBC Television series, "[[Transatlantic Sessions]]".

Since 1998, Douglas has been a member of Alison Krauss and Union Station, touring extensively and playing on a series of platinum-selling albums. When not on the road with Alison Krauss and Union Station, Douglas tours in support of his extensive body of work with his bands The Jerry Douglas Band and The Earls of Leicester, following the continued success of the latter's 2014 release ''The Earls of Leicester'' and 2015's ''Rattle and Roar''.{{cn|date=August 2024}}

Jerry Douglas appeared with Vince Gill on Eric Clapton's Crossroads Guitar Festival 2004 ("Oklahoma Borderline" and "What the Cowgirls Do").{{cn|date=August 2024}}

Douglas also made a cameo in the third "United Breaks Guitars" consumer protest video, all of which went viral.{{cn|date=August 2024}}

==Personal life==
Douglas was born in [[Warren, Ohio]],<ref name="AllMusic" /> and now lives in Nashville, Tennessee, with his wife, Jill.

==Awards and honors==
As of 2021, Douglas has been nominated for thirty-two [[Grammy Award]]s, winning fourteen.<ref name="grammy">{{cite web |url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/artists/jerry-douglas |title=Artist: Jerry Douglas |author=<!--Not stated--> |date=2021 |website=www.grammy.com |publisher=The Recording Academy |access-date=January 3, 2021}}</ref>

He has received the [[Country Music Association]]'s 'Musician of the Year' award three times, in 2002, 2005 and 2007.{{cn|date=August 2024}}

Douglas is a 10-time recipient of the [[International Bluegrass Music Association]] ("IBMA") Dobro Player of the Year Award.{{cn|date=August 2024}}

In 2004, the [[National Endowment for the Arts]] awarded Douglas a [[National Heritage Fellowship]], which is the United States' highest honor in the folk and traditional arts.<ref name="nhf">{{cite web |url=https://www.arts.gov/honors/heritage/jerry-douglas |title=Jerry Douglas: Dobro player |author=<!--Not stated--> |date=n.d. |website=www.arts.gov |publisher=National Endowment for the Arts |access-date=January 3, 2021}}</ref>

Douglas was named Artist in Residence for the [[Country Music Hall of Fame and Museum]] in 2008.{{cn|date=August 2024}}

Douglas was honored at the 36th annual [[Telluride Bluegrass Festival]] in Colorado for his twenty-fifth consecutive year playing in and at the festival.{{cn|date=August 2024}}

Douglas received the Bluegrass Star Award, presented by the Bluegrass Heritage Foundation of Dallas, Texas, on October 15, 2016. The award is bestowed upon bluegrass artists who do an exemplary job of advancing traditional bluegrass music and bringing it to new audiences while preserving its character and heritage.<ref>"Bluegrass Heritage Foundation official website". 2016. Retrieved 2016-10-18.</ref>

The [[Americana Music Association]] honored Douglas with a Lifetime Achievement Award in 2011.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://americanamusic.org/robert-plant-buddy-miller-and-justin-townes-earle-win-big-10th-annual-americana-honors-awards-show |title=Robert Plant, Buddy Miller and Justin Townes Earle Win Big at 10th Annual Americana Honors & Awards Show |publisher=Americana Music |access-date=2016-01-10 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160114214542/http://americanamusic.org/robert-plant-buddy-miller-and-justin-townes-earle-win-big-10th-annual-americana-honors-awards-show |archive-date=2016-01-14}}</ref>

Douglas received the key to the city of [[Manchester, Tennessee]], as well as to [[Coffee County, Tennessee|Coffee County]] during a performance at the 2015 Bonnaroo Music and Arts Festival.<ref>{{cite web |author=/ By Brittney McKenna |url=http://www.thebluegrasssituation.com/read/dispatches-bgs-stage-bonnaroo-2015 |title=Dispatches From The BGS Stage at Bonnaroo 2015 |publisher=The Bluegrass Situation |date=2015-06-15 |access-date=2016-01-10 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151009000012/http://www.thebluegrasssituation.com/read/dispatches-bgs-stage-bonnaroo-2015 |archive-date=2015-10-09}}</ref>
