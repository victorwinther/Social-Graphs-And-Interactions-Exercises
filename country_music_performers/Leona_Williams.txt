'''Leona Belle Helton''' (born January 7, 1943, in [[Vienna, Missouri]], United States) is an American [[country music]] singer known professionally as '''Leona Williams'''. Active since 1958, Williams has been a backing musician for [[Loretta Lynn]] and [[Merle Haggard]] and [[The Strangers (American band)|The Strangers]]. (She and Haggard were married between 1978 and 1983.) She also charted eight times on [[Hot Country Songs]], with her only Top 40 hit being a duet with Haggard titled "The Bull and the Beaver."

==Biography==
Leona Belle Helton was born January 7, 1943, in [[Vienna, Missouri]].<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web |url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p138451|pure_url=yes}} |title=Leona Williams biography |last=Loftus |first=Johnny |work=[[Allmusic]] |access-date=2 August 2010}}</ref> Active in her family's band since childhood, she had a radio program on [[KWOS]] in [[Jefferson City, Missouri]], when she was fifteen.<ref name="allmusic"/> Later on, she worked as a [[bass guitar]]ist and backing vocalist in [[Loretta Lynn]]'s road band. By 1968, Williams signed to the Hickory record label and released two singles: "Once More" and "Country Girl with Hot Pants On."<ref name="allmusic"/> In 1976, she recorded the album ''San Quentin's First Lady'' for [[MCA Records]] with [[The Strangers (American band)|The Strangers]], which was the first country album recorded by a female artist inside a prison.<ref name="allmusic"/> She also joined [[Merle Haggard]]'s road band [[The Strangers (American band)|The Strangers]] in the mid-1970s, supplanting his estranged wife, [[Bonnie Owens]]. Leona wrote two of Merle's No.&nbsp;1 hits, "[[Someday When Things Are Good]]", and "[[You Take Me For Granted]]".  She also wrote songs for [[Connie Smith]]--"Dallas", Loretta Lynn--"Get Whatcha Got And Go", and others.  Between 1978 and 1983, she was married to Haggard, and in 1978, the two charted in the country Top Ten with the song "The Bull and the Beaver."<ref name="allmusic"/> She recorded two singles for [[Elektra Records]] in 1981, and charted another duet with Haggard titled "We're Strangers Again."<ref name="whitburn">{{cite book|last=Whitburn|first=Joel|title=Hot Country Songs 1944 to 2008|publisher=Record Research, Inc|year=2008|pages=467–468|isbn=978-0-89820-177-2}}</ref> She later married singer-songwriter Dave Kirby in 1985,<ref name="whitburn"/> and remained married to him until his 2004 death. From 2005 she was with [[Ferlin Husky]], and remained with him for the last six years of his life.  Williams continues to tour with her son, Ron.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.leonawilliams.com/|title=Leona Williams|access-date=30 July 2011}}</ref>

In 2017, Williams won the Honky Tonk Female honour at the Ameripolitan Music Awards.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://theboot.com/2017-ameripolitan-music-awards-winners/|title=2017 Ameripolitan Music Awards Winners Crowned|first=Christina|last=Vinson|website=The Boot|date=16 February 2017 }}</ref>
