'''Livy Jeanne''' (born in [[Thunder Bay]], Ontario) is a Canadian [[country music]] singer.

== Career ==
Jeanne won the 13–17-year-old division of the Global Country Star Search in Edmonton in May 2010.<ref>{{cite news |last=Proulx |first=Ben |title=Jeanne jumping into spotlight |url=http://www.sherwoodparknews.com/2010/06/03/jeanne-jumping-into-spotlight |accessdate=November 29, 2013 |newspaper=Sherwood Park News |date=June 2, 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131211001505/http://www.sherwoodparknews.com/2010/06/03/jeanne-jumping-into-spotlight |archive-date=December 11, 2013 |url-status=live }}</ref> She released her debut single, "Invisible", in January 2012 about her experience being bullied in school.<ref>{{cite news |last=Di Massa |first=Michael |title=Local singer talks overcoming bullies |url=http://www.sherwoodparknews.com/2012/03/01/local-singer-talks-overcoming-bullies |accessdate=November 29, 2013 |newspaper=Sherwood Park News |date=March 1, 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131211000235/http://www.sherwoodparknews.com/2012/03/01/local-singer-talks-overcoming-bullies |archive-date=December 11, 2013 |url-status=live }}</ref> She toured schools across [[Alberta]] in 2012 on her You Don't Have to Be Invisible Tour.<ref>{{cite news |last=Tinson |first=Kayla |title=Fresh Find: Livy Jeanne talks ‘Invisible’ tour |url=http://topcountry.ca/fresh-find-livy-jeanne-talks-invisible-tour/ |accessdate=November 29, 2013 |newspaper=Top Country |date=October 24, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131203050700/http://topcountry.ca/fresh-find-livy-jeanne-talks-invisible-tour/ |archive-date=December 3, 2013 |url-status=live }}</ref> She was nominated for Artist to Watch at the Edmonton Music Awards in April 2012.<ref>{{cite news |last=Sperounes |first=Sandra |title=Artist to Watch: Livy Jeanne |url=http://blogs.edmontonjournal.com/2012/04/10/artist-to-watch-livy-jeanne/ |accessdate=November 29, 2013 |newspaper=[[Edmonton Journal]] | date=April 10, 2012}}</ref> In November 2012, she released an [[extended play]], ''Under the Radar'', via [[Black Box Music]].<ref>{{cite news |last=Patton |first=Kristi |title=Country songstress flying high with opening slot for Alan Jackson |url=http://www.pentictonwesternnews.com/entertainment/210111911.html |accessdate=November 29, 2013 |newspaper=Penticton Western News |date=June 4, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131203010859/http://www.pentictonwesternnews.com/entertainment/210111911.html |archive-date=December 3, 2013 |url-status=live }}</ref>

In July 2013, Jeanne released the single "Any Other Way".<ref>{{cite news |last=Sperounes |first=Sandra |title=New single: Livy Jeanne's Any Other Way |url=http://blogs.edmontonjournal.com/2013/07/10/new-single-livy-jeannes-any-other-way/ |accessdate=November 29, 2013 |newspaper=[[Edmonton Journal]] | date=July 10, 2013}}</ref> It peaked at number 42 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Canada Country]] chart.<ref name="charts" />

Recent songwriting highlights include securing a cut on [[The Shires (duo)|The Shires]] album ''[[My Universe (album)|My Universe]]'', which peaked at No. 3 on the UK Billboard Charts. Her song "Daddy's Little Girl" is currently the band's newest single.

Livy's two latest singles, "Wrong For You" and "Easy On You", released July 27, 2018, are a clear reflection of her personality: an unapologetic blend of charm and confidence.

On March 18, 2020, Livy released a new single, Finish This Up and continued her move into pop music from her country music origins. She then released Sharing You (single) on May 8, 2020.

== CMA Music Festival ==
Livy Jeanne has earned the right to play at Nashville's CMA Fest [[CMA Music Festival|Music Festival]] two years in a row.

In 2017, she landed herself a spot on the Music City Stage, located in the Nashville Visitor Center at Bridgestone Arena, which showcases some of the genre's rising talent.

Then returning in 2018, Jeanne played the Nashville Acoustic Corner Stage, which was sponsored by SESAC [[SESAC]].
