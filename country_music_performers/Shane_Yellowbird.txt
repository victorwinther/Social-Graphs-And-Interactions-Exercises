'''Shane Yellowbird''' (July 7, 1979{{spnd}}April 25, 2022) was a Canadian [[country music]] singer-songwriter from [[Maskwacis|Maskwacis, Alberta]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.shaneyellowbird.com/bio.cfm |title=Shane Yellowbird Official Website :: Bio |accessdate=June 25, 2010 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100131080508/http://www.shaneyellowbird.com/bio.cfm |archivedate=January 31, 2010 }}</ref> A [[Cree]], in 2007, he was named the Aboriginal Entertainer of the Year at the Aboriginal People's Choice Music Awards, [[Chevrolet|Chevy Trucks]]' Rising Star of the Year at the [[Canadian Country Music Association|Canadian Country Music Awards]] and his song "Pickup Truck" was one of the year's ten most played country songs.

==Early life==
Yellowbird was born in [[Maskwacis|Hobbema, Alberta]],<ref name="AllMusic bio">{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/shane-yellowbird-mn0001044131/biography|title=Shane Yellowbird – Biography|first=Chris|last=True|work=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=April 27, 2022|archive-date=April 27, 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220427205158/https://www.allmusic.com/artist/shane-yellowbird-mn0001044131/biography|url-status=live}}</ref> on 7 July 1979.<ref name=Banks>{{cite news|title=42-year-old Country music singer Shane Yellowbird passes away|url=https://www.wsmv.com/2022/04/27/42-year-old-country-music-singer-shane-yellowbird-passes-away/|first=Torrence|last=Banks|date=April 27, 2022|access-date=April 27, 2022|publisher=[[WSMV-TV]]|archive-date=April 27, 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220427041532/https://www.wsmv.com/2022/04/27/42-year-old-country-music-singer-shane-yellowbird-passes-away/|url-status=live}}</ref> He attended Ponoka Composite High School.<ref name=Banks/> He suffered a severe [[stuttering]] problem as a child, for which he attended a [[speech therapy|speech therapist]] who suggested he sing his sentences. This method proved successful in treating him, and led to an interest in music.<ref name="CBC obit">{{cite news|title='Trailblazer' Cree country artist Shane Yellowbird dead at 42|url=https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/edmonton/trailblazer-cree-country-artist-shane-yellowbird-dead-at-42-1.6431650|date=April 26, 2022|access-date=April 27, 2022|publisher=CBC News|archive-date=April 26, 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220426230753/https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/edmonton/trailblazer-cree-country-artist-shane-yellowbird-dead-at-42-1.6431650|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name=Mertz>{{cite news|title=Alberta country music singer-songwriter Shane Yellowbird passes away|url=https://globalnews.ca/news/8788997/alberta-country-singer-shane-yellowbird-dead-cree/|first=Emily|last=Mertz|date=April 26, 2022|access-date=April 27, 2022|publisher=Global News|archive-date=April 27, 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220427205219/https://globalnews.ca/news/8788997/alberta-country-singer-shane-yellowbird-dead-cree/|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Career==
Yellowbird was signed by O'Reilly International in 2003.<ref name=Banks/>  Three years later, he released his debut album titled ''Life Is Calling My Name''.<ref>[http://www.shaneyellowbird.com/news.cfm?JournalID=6338 Yellowbird Releases Debut Album] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070209183218/http://www.shaneyellowbird.com/news.cfm?JournalID=6338 |date=February 9, 2007 }}</ref> The album includes the singles "Beautiful Concept," "They're All About You," "Pickup Truck" and "I Remember the Music." In November 2006, Yellowbird won three awards at the Aboriginal People's Choice Music Awards ceremony, including Best New Artist, Single of the Year ("Beautiful Concept") and Best Video ("Beautiful Concept").<ref>[http://www.shaneyellowbird.com/news.cfm?JournalID=6337 Yellowbird Ropes 3 APCMA Awards] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070210011236/http://www.shaneyellowbird.com/news.cfm?JournalID=6337 |date=February 10, 2007 }}</ref>

Yellowbird opened for [[Emerson Drive]] on their cross-Canada tour,<ref>[http://www.shaneyellowbird.com/news.cfm?JournalID=10440 Shane Yellowbird to Tour with Emerson Drive] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070210011450/http://www.shaneyellowbird.com/news.cfm?JournalID=10440 |date=February 10, 2007 }}</ref> and was chosen to represent his native Canada by performing at the Fourth Annual Global Artist Party at the [[CMA Music Festival]] in June 2007.<ref>[http://www.shaneyellowbird.com/news.cfm?JournalID=14751 Yellowbird to Perform at CMA Music Festival in June] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070928100559/http://www.shaneyellowbird.com/news.cfm?JournalID=14751 |date=September 28, 2007 }}</ref> Yellowbird was named the [[Chevrolet|Chevy Trucks]] Rising Star of the Year at the 2007 [[Canadian Country Music Association|Canadian Country Music Awards]].<ref name="CBC obit"/><ref name=Amato>{{cite news|title='A truly beautiful soul': Alberta country artist Shane Yellowbird dies at age 42|url=https://edmonton.ctvnews.ca/a-truly-beautiful-soul-alberta-country-artist-shane-yellowbird-dies-at-age-42-1.5877193|first=Sean|last=Amato|date=April 26, 2022|access-date=April 27, 2022|publisher=CTV News|archive-date=April 26, 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220426220642/https://edmonton.ctvnews.ca/a-truly-beautiful-soul-alberta-country-artist-shane-yellowbird-dies-at-age-42-1.5877193|url-status=live}}</ref>

"Pickup Truck," Yellowbird's third single, also became his first Top 5 song on the Canadian Country Singles chart in the summer of 2007. The song also peaked at No.&nbsp;64 on the all-genre [[Canadian Hot 100]], while the video topped the [[CMT (Canada)|CMT]] Chevy Top 20 in July.<ref>[http://www.thatscountry.com/am2/publish/Country_News_2/003753.shtml Shane Yellowbird's "Pickup Truck" No.&nbsp;1 Video in Canada] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071212222849/http://www.thatscountry.com/am2/publish/Country_News_2/003753.shtml |date=December 12, 2007 }}</ref> It was one of the ten most played country music songs of the year in Canada.<ref>[http://www.radioandrecords.com/Formats/Charts/YearEnd/07/Canada_Country_songs.asp R&R Top Canada Country Songs] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080305023257/http://www.radioandrecords.com/Formats/Charts/YearEnd/07/Canada_Country_songs.asp |date=March 5, 2008 }}</ref> Yellowbird opened the 2007 Aboriginal People's Choice Music Awards, starring with [[Lorne Cardinal]] and [[Gabrielle Miller]] of ''[[Corner Gas]]''.<ref>[http://www.shaneyellowbird.com/news.cfm?JournalID=15516 Shane Yellowbird to Open the Aboriginal Peoples Choice Music Awards with the "Corner Gas" crew] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080115164553/http://www.shaneyellowbird.com/news.cfm?JournalID=15516 |date=January 15, 2008 }}</ref> Later that evening, he was named the Aboriginal Entertainer of the Year.<ref>[http://www.shaneyellowbird.com/news.cfm?JournalID=15629 Shane Yellowbird Grabs "Aboriginal Entertainer of the Year" and More at the 2007 APCMA's] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071206044254/http://www.shaneyellowbird.com/news.cfm?JournalID=15629 |date=December 6, 2007 }}</ref> He also won awards for Best Country CD (''Life Is Calling My Name'') and Best Music Video ("Pickup Truck").<ref>{{cite news|title=Dion, LaBelle honoured|url=https://www.thestar.com/entertainment/2007/11/05/dion_labelle_honoured.html|date=November 5, 2007|accessdate=April 27, 2022|newspaper=Toronto Star|archive-date=April 27, 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220427205235/https://www.thestar.com/entertainment/2007/11/05/dion_labelle_honoured.html|url-status=live}}</ref>  Yellowbird later won three trophies at the 2007 Canadian Aboriginal Music Awards, including Best Male Artist, Best Country Album and Best Album of the Year (''Life Is Calling My Name'').<ref>[http://www.shaneyellowbird.com/news.cfm?JournalID=15667 Shane Yellowbird Big Winner at Canadian Aboriginal Music Awards] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071206000049/http://www.shaneyellowbird.com/news.cfm?JournalID=15667 |date=December 6, 2007 }}</ref>  He was also nominated for the 2008 [[Juno Award]] for Country Recording of the Year, for ''Life Is Calling My Name''.<ref name=Germano>{{cite news|title='Trailblazer' Cree country singer Shane Yellowbird dead at age 42|url=https://www.thestar.com/entertainment/music/2022/04/26/trailblazer-cree-country-singer-shane-yellowbird-dead-at-age-42.html|first=Daniela|last=Germano|date=April 26, 2022|accessdate=April 27, 2022|newspaper=Toronto Star|agency=The Canadian Press|archive-date=April 27, 2022|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220427005755/https://www.thestar.com/entertainment/music/2022/04/26/trailblazer-cree-country-singer-shane-yellowbird-dead-at-age-42.html|url-status=live}}</ref>

Yellowbird's second album, ''It's About Time'' was released on November 17, 2009, featuring the single, "Bare Feet on the Blacktop".<ref name=Mertz/>  In the United States, the video received a nomination from the Native American Music Association.  Yellowbird won two Native American Music Awards for Best Country Recording for "Life Is Calling My Name" in 2011 and won Best Country Recording for "It's About Time" in 2012.<ref name=NAMA11>{{cite web|title=Eleventh Annual Award Winners|url=https://nativeamericanmusicawards.com/nama-11|accessdate=April 27, 2022|publisher=Native American Music Awards|archive-date=August 13, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210813182639/https://nativeamericanmusicawards.com/nama-11|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name=NAMA12>{{cite web|title=The 12th Annual Native American Music Awards|url=https://nativeamericanmusicawards.com/nama-12|accessdate=April 27, 2022|publisher=Native American Music Awards|archive-date=November 25, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211125014413/https://nativeamericanmusicawards.com/nama-12|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Personal life==
At the time of his death, Yellowbird was in a [[domestic partnership]] with fiancée Sarah Garrow.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Tributes and candlelight vigils for Cree country singer Shane Yellowbird |url=https://edmontonjournal.com/entertainment/local-arts/tributes-and-candlelight-vigils-for-cree-country-singer-shane-yellowbird |access-date=2022-05-03 |website=edmontonjournal |language=en-CA}}</ref> The couple announced their engagement hours before his death was announced. He had four children.<ref name=Mertz/> He suffered from [[epilepsy]] and had occasional [[seizure]]s.<ref name=Mertz/><ref name="CBC obit"/><ref name=Amato/>

Yellowbird died on April 25, 2022, in [[Calgary]].  He was 42 years old.<ref name=Mertz/><ref name="CBC obit"/>
