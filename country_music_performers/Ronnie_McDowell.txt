'''Ronald Dean McDowell Sr.''' (born March 25, 1950)<ref name="Whitburn"/> is an American [[country music]] artist, songwriter. McDowell charted more than thirty Top 40 hits on the ''Billboard'' country music charts. Two of his singles – "[[Older Women]]" and "[[You're Gonna Ruin My Bad Reputation]]" — reached Number One on the country charts, while eleven more reached Top Ten. He has also released more than twenty studio albums, and has been signed to [[Curb Records]] since 1986.

==U.S. Navy==
McDowell served in the [[United States Navy|US Navy]] from 1968-72. He served on board the [[USS Hancock (CV-19)|USS Hancock]] and [[USS Kitty Hawk (CV-63)|USS Kitty Hawk]].

==Career==
Following the death of [[Elvis Presley]] in 1977, McDowell, a devoted fan of Presley’s, recorded a song that became his first country and only pop hit with his self-penned tribute song "The King Is Gone," which he recorded on the independent Scorpion [[record label]]. The record took off immediately, gaining [[airplay (radio)|airplay]] on [[Country music|country]] and [[pop (music)|pop]] [[radio station]]s across the [[United States]] and around the world.<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/ronnie-mcdowell-mn0000336842|title=Ronnie McDowell biography|last=Huey|first=Steve|work=[[Allmusic]]|access-date=November 20, 2012}}</ref>

It peaked at number thirteen on [[Billboard Hot 100|Billboard's Hot 100 singles]], and became a [[Music recording sales certification|gold record]]. In January 1978, McDowell performed the song on the [[NBC]] special, ''Nashville Remembers Elvis on His Birthday'', in which he appeared alongside a number of Presley's contemporaries. To date, "The King Is Gone" has sold more than five million copies.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ronniemcdowell.com/press-kit|title=Ronnie McDowell - Press Kit|website=Ronniemcdowell.com|access-date=April 14, 2020}}</ref>

McDowell was commissioned to cover a number of Presley's songs for the soundtrack to 1979 made-for-TV Presley biography film ''[[Elvis (1979 film)|Elvis]]'',<ref name="LarkinCountry"/> during which [[Kurt Russell]], portraying Presley, lip-synched to McDowell's vocals. He actually recorded 36 songs, but not all of them were used in the film.<ref name="AZ">{{cite book|last=Worth|first=Fred|title=Elvis: His Life from A to Z|year=1992|publisher=Outlet|isbn=978-0-517-06634-8|pages=308–309}}</ref> McDowell also sang the Presley vocals for the 1981 TV movie ''[[Elvis and the Beauty Queen]]'' and for the 1988 TV miniseries ''[[Elvis and Me]]''.{{citation needed|date=July 2017}}

All of the Presley's vocals for the 1990 TV series, ''[[Elvis (1990 TV series)|Elvis]]'', were performed by McDowell.<ref name="LarkinCountry"/> McDowell also contributed to the 1997 Showtime special, ''[[Elvis Meets Nixon]]''. He scored a second hit for the Scorpion label entitled "I Love You, I Love You, I Love You" before being signed by [[Sony Music Entertainment|CBS Records]] [[Epic Records|Epic]] in 1979.<ref name="LarkinCountry">{{cite book|title=[[Encyclopedia of Popular Music|The Guinness Who's Who of Country Music]]|editor=[[Colin Larkin (writer)|Colin Larkin]]|publisher=[[Guinness Publishing]]|date=1993|edition=First|isbn=0-85112-726-6|pages=249/250}}</ref>

McDowell charted a string of hit [[single (music)|single]]s and albums for Epic between 1979 and 1986.  Every single release, except one, became a [[Top 40|Top 10]] hit, including "Older Women" and "You're Gonna Ruin My Bad Reputation".{{citation needed|date=July 2017}} Other hits during his Epic years included "Watching Girls Go By", "Personally", "You Made A Wanted Man Of Me", "Wandering Eyes", "[[All Tied Up (song)|All Tied Up]]", and "[[In a New York Minute (song)|In a New York Minute]]".{{citation needed|date=July 2017}}

Moving to [[Curb Records]] in 1986, McDowell scored a Top 10 hit with "[[It's Only Make Believe]]", a [[duet (music)|duet]] with [[Conway Twitty]] on what had been Twitty's breakthrough [[rock and roll]] hit in 1958.<ref name="LarkinCountry"/>  Initially a member of McDowell's back-up band would substitute for Twitty during live performances. Recently, however, McDowell has performed the song live with Twitty's pre-recorded voice, followed by a solo from a member of the back-up band.{{citation needed|date=July 2017}}
[[File:Ronnie_McDowell_MMP_Music_Award_and_Hall_of_Fame_Ceremony.jpg|thumb|Ronnie McDowell at MMP Music Award and Hall of Fame Ceremony]]

In 1988, he teamed with [[Jerry Lee Lewis]] for a duet that McDowell wrote, entitled "You're Never Too Old To Rock N' Roll". He recorded yet another Top 10 hit with his [[cover version]] of the pop standard "[[Unchained Melody]]," which also became a No.&nbsp;1 country [[music video]]. He started appearing in larger venues and touring before headlining his own shows.{{citation needed|date=July 2017}}

In 2002, McDowell recorded two albums for [[Curb Records]], one consisting of beach music with [[Rock & Roll Hall of Fame]]r [[Bill Pinkney|Bill Pinkney's Original Drifters]], ''Ronnie McDowell with Bill Pinkney's Original Drifters''. The second project, a country album, entitled ''Ronnie McDowell Country'', a collection of six new McDowell penned songs and a few country standards.

In January 2017, McDowell collaborated with [[Richard Lynch (musician)|Richard Lynch]] on a military tribute duet "Love Tattoo".<ref>{{cite web|last1=Jamm|first1=Rick|title=Richard Lynch and Ronnie McDowell: "Love Tattoo" – a military tribute duet|url=http://jamsphere.com/newreleases/richard-lynch-and-ronnie-mcdowell-love-tattoo-a-military-tribute-duet|website=JamSphere.com|access-date=29 March 2017}}</ref>

On January 17, 2018, he unveiled "The Magic Moment", his original painting of Presley getting his first guitar at the Tupelo Hardware Store in Tupelo, thus kicking off the pilot for his forthcoming TV show, entitled ''Ronnie McDowell Painting America''.

On January 22, 2019, McDowell was inducted into the Mississippi Music Project Hall of Fame in Biloxi, Mississippi and was awarded the MMP Music Award for his lifelong commitment to the music industry, by Commander Joseph W. Clark.<ref>{{Cite web |title=News sept 2020 |url=https://www.mmpglobalentertainment.com/newssept2020.html |access-date=2023-10-12 |website=www.mmpglobalentertainment.com}}</ref>

In 2023, a collaboration with [[Dolly Parton]] and the [[Jordanaires]], “I Dreamed About Elvis” (credited to “Dolly Parton with Ronnie McDowell and special guests The Jordanaires”), was released on Parton’s 2023 album Rockstar. It is unknown when McDowell’s contributions were recorded, but the Jordanaires’ vocals would have to have been recorded at some point prior to 2013, when group leader/first tenor Gordon Stoker died and when the group disbanded.

==Personal life==
Ronnie McDowell resides in [[Hendersonville, Tennessee]]. He has five children. His son, Tyler Dean McDowell, was also signed to [[Curb Records]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=392&dat=20020410&id=PCMIAAAAIBAJ&sjid=ez8DAAAAIBAJ&pg=2930,2586890|title=Country music star Ronnie McDowell to play Colonnade|last=Zelk|first=Chris|date=2002-04-10|work=Fort Oglethorpe Press|access-date=2009-07-12}}</ref> Another son, Ronnie Jr. and a nephew, Chris, recorded in a band called Six Shooter on Curb in 1991.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=AT&p_theme=at&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=0EB7D44D07B9569A&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM|title=Night Beat|last=Phillips|first=Emo|date=1992-07-10|work=[[Atlanta Journal-Constitution]]|access-date=2009-08-10}}</ref>
