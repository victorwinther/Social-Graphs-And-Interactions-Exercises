'''The War and Treaty''' are an American husband and wife duo consisting of singer-songwriters Michael Trotter Jr. and [[Tanya Trotter]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/the-war-and-treaty-mn0003749672/biography|title=The War and Treaty Biography|website=[[AllMusic]]|first=Marcy|last=Donelson|accessdate=October 18, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite magazine|last=Bernstein|first=Jonathan|date=2020-09-18|title=The War and Treaty's Journey Toward Hope|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/war-treaty-hearts-town-interview-1059146/|access-date=2021-03-03|magazine=Rolling Stone|language=en-US}}</ref> Their debut studio album ''[[Healing Tide]]'' (2018), received critical acclaim upon its release. Soon afterwards, they released their sophomore album ''Hearts Town'' (2020), which featured a collaboration with American singer [[Emmylou Harris]], and spawned the [[Adult Alternative Songs|Triple A]] top 40 hit single "Five More Minutes".<ref>{{Cite web |date=2022-10-26 |title=Music is a matter of life, death, redemption for The War and Treaty's lead singers, Tanya and Michael Trotter |url=https://www.sandiegouniontribune.com/entertainment/music/story/2022-10-26/music-a-matter-of-life-and-death-for-the-war-and-treatys-tanya-trotter-and-michael-trotter-jr |access-date=2023-11-11 |website=San Diego Union-Tribune |language=en-US}}</ref> In 2022, they signed to [[UMG Nashville]], and released their major label debut  ''[[Lover's Game]]'' (2023). Their collaboration with [[Zach Bryan]] on the song "[[Zach Bryan (album)|Hey Driver]]" reached the top 20 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. 

In 2023, The War and Treaty became the first black duo to be nominated for the [[Country Music Association Award for Vocal Duo of the Year|Country Music Association Award for Duo of the Year]];<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-10-17 |title=CMA Awards 2023: Nominees, Host and How to Watch |url=https://wwd.com/pop-culture/culture-news/cma-awards-2023-how-to-watch-1235880149/ |access-date=2023-11-10 |website=WWD}}</ref> along with being the first black duo to be nominated for the [[Academy of Country Music Awards|Academy of Country Music Award]] for Duo of the Year.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-04-13 |title=Nominations Announced for the 58th Academy of Country Music Awards |url=https://www.acmcountry.com/news/nominations-announced-for-the-58th-academy-of-country-music-awards |access-date=2023-11-10 |website=www.acmcountry.com |language=en}}</ref> They have received two nominations for the [[66th Annual Grammy Awards]], including [[Grammy Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]]. An upcoming biopic based on the group’s life is set to be released.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Willman |first=Chris |date=2024-01-16 |title=The War and Treaty: How the Best New Artist Grammy Nominees Came to Be ‘Friends’ With the Emmys, Too, and Win Over Superfan Zach Bryan |url=https://variety.com/2024/music/news/war-and-treaty-grammys-best-new-artist-nominee-emmys-in-memoriam-1235874511/ |access-date=2024-01-16 |website=Variety |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=D'Alessandro |first=Anthony |date=2022-06-06 |title=Gilbert Films, Get Lifted Film Co & Harvey Mason Media Producing ‘The War And Treaty’; Numa Perrier Directing |url=https://deadline.com/2022/06/the-war-and-treaty-numa-perrier-directing-1235039133/ |access-date=2024-01-16 |website=Deadline}}</ref>

==Career==
In 2016, Michael Trotter Jr. and Tanya Blount released the duet album ''Love Affair'' as Trotter & Blount.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://blackandmarriedwithkids.com/2013/06/power-couple-michael-tanya-trotter-blount/|title=Power Couple: Michael & Tanya Trotter (Blount)|work=Black and Married With Kids|first=Christine|last=St. Vil|date=13 June 2013|accessdate=1 April 2018}}</ref> In 2017, The War and Treaty released the EP ''Down to the River'', described as a mix of 'blues, gospel, soul, bluegrass, country' and heralded by Estey as 'a splendidly made immediate classic about conflict and redemption'.<ref name="Soul Bounce EP">{{cite web|url=http://www.soulbounce.com/2017/07/the-war-and-treaty-down-to-the-river-video/|title=The War & Treaty "Down To The River" A Sight To Behold|work=Soul Bounce|date= July 17, 2017|accessdate=1 April 2018}}</ref><ref name="Paste">{{cite web|url=https://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2017/07/the-war-and-treaty-down-to-the-rivermp4.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190403231339/https://www.pastemagazine.com/articles/2017/07/the-war-and-treaty-down-to-the-rivermp4.html |archive-date=April 3, 2019 |title=The War & Treaty "Down To The River" A Sight To Behold|work=[[Paste (magazine)|Paste]]|first=Chris |last=Estey|date= July 17, 2017|accessdate=1 April 2018}}</ref> Yet the two can sensually help heal the wounds from those confessional blasts with a sweet ballad like "Till The Mornin’" — they truly live up to the dynamic tension of their moniker.'<ref name="Paste"/>

In 2018, ''[[Rolling Stone (magazine)|Rolling Stone]]'' reported The War and Treaty's debut album ''[[Healing Tide]]'' is due for release on August 10, 2018, describing the lead single as a 'joyfully relentless title track, reminiscent of classic [[Ike and Tina Turner]] rock-infused soul'.<ref name="RS"/>

On September 25, 2020, The War and Treaty released their sophomore studio album, ''Hearts Town'', on [[Rounder Records]]. On April 18, 2021 they joined [[Dierks Bentley]] for a performance of [[U2]]'s "[[Pride (In the Name of Love)]]" for the 56th [[Academy of Country Music Awards]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.cmt.com/news/1832840/see-all-the-performers-and-presenters-for-the-2021-academy-of-country-music-awards/ | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210419005838/http://www.cmt.com/news/1832840/see-all-the-performers-and-presenters-for-the-2021-academy-of-country-music-awards/ | url-status=dead | archive-date=April 19, 2021 | title=See All the Performers and Presenters for the 2021 Academy of Country Music Awards }}</ref>

The group signed a major label deal with [[Universal Music Group Nashville]] in May 2022.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.udiscovermusic.com/news/the-war-and-treaty-sign-umg-nashville/ | title=Husband and Wife Americana Duo the War and Treaty Sign to UMG Nashville | date=23 May 2022 }}</ref> They performed a cover of "[[It's Only Rock 'n Roll (But I Like It)]]" alongside [[Brothers Osborne]] at the [[56th Annual Country Music Association Awards]] on November 9, 2022 and surprise-released their second EP ''Blank Page'' on the same day.<ref>{{cite magazine | url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/cma-awards-brothers-osborne-war-and-treaty-1234627204/ | title=The War & Treaty and Brothers Osborne Cover a Rolling Stones Classic at the CMA Awards | magazine=[[Rolling Stone]] | date=10 November 2022 }}</ref> Their major label debut album ''[[Lover's Game]]'', was released on March 10, 2023. 

The duo collaborated with American country star [[Zach Bryan]] on the track "Hey Driver" from his [[Zach Bryan (album)|self-titled]] fourth studio album. The song debuted at number 14 on the [[Billboard Hot 100]] marking the duo's first appearance on the chart.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Zellner |first=Xander |date=2023-09-06 |title=Hot 100 First-Timers: Sierra Ferrell & The War And Treaty Debut With Zach Bryan Features |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/chart-beat/hot-100-first-timers-sierra-ferrell-the-war-and-treaty-debut-zach-bryan-features-1235407419/ |access-date=2024-01-14 |website=Billboard |language=en-US}}</ref>
