'''Williams Riley''' was an American [[country music]] band composed of Steve Williams ([[lead vocals]]), Derek George ([[guitar]], [[background vocals]]), Charlie Hutto (guitar, background vocals), Joe Rogers ([[steel guitar]], [[fiddle]], [[harmonica]]), Dave Guidry ([[bass guitar]]), Travis Thibodaux ([[Keyboard instrument|keyboards]], background vocals) and Nick Buda ([[drums]]).<ref name="musicrow">{{cite web |last=King |first=Christie |title=On the Music Row Cover |url=http://www.musicrow.com/2009/07/on-the-music-row-cover-williams-riley/ |work=Music Row |date=2009-07-13 |accessdate=2009-09-28}}</ref> Derek George had formerly played guitar for [[Bryan White]], and before that, he was a member of the band [[Pearl River (band)|Pearl River]]. Williams Riley self-released its debut album in 2009 and has charted on the U.S. country charts with "Country Livin'." This album includes collaborations with White, as well as [[Slash (musician)|Slash]] and [[Edwin McCain]].<ref>{{cite web |last=Horne |first=Adam |title=Williams Riley Band Help Slash Go Country |url=http://www.theboot.com/2009/01/13/williams-riley-band-help-slash-go-country/ |work=The Boot |date=2009-01-13 |accessdate=2009-09-28}}</ref> The band's self-titled debut album received a favorable review from Matt Bjorke of Roughstock, who called it a "well-crafted album of contemporary country music."<ref>{{cite web |last=Bjorke |first=Matt |title=''Williams Riley'' review |url=http://www.roughstock.com/reviews/williams-riley-band-williams-riley-band |work=Roughstock |date=2009-06-23 |accessdate=2009-09-28}}</ref>

On June 21, 2010, the band's label, Golden Music announced it was shutting its Nashville division. George produced [[Randy Houser]]'s 2013 album ''[[How Country Feels]]''.
