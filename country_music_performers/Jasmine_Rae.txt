'''Jasmine Rae''' is an Australian singer and songwriter who has released five studio albums via [[ABC Music]] Australia. Rae has been nominated for four [[ARIA Music Awards]] and has won two [[CMC Music Awards]],<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.jasminerae.com.au/news.cfm |title=Jasmine Rae – Listen Here: Official Website |accessdate=21 June 2011 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110701122234/http://www.jasminerae.com.au/news.cfm |archivedate=1 July 2011  }}</ref> and received the 'Global Artist of the Year' award by the US [[Country Music Association]] in 2013. She is also an eleven-time Golden Guitar nominee.

==Biography==
===2008–2010: Telstra Road to Tamworth and ''Look It Up'' ===
In 2008, Rae auditioned for and won the Telstra Road to Tamworth.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.beinvolved.com.au/spo-arts-a-community/country-music/road-to-tamworth-previous-winners |title=Telstra Road to Tamworth - Previous Winners |access-date=6 February 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100712060217/http://www.beinvolved.com.au/spo-arts-a-community/country-music/road-to-tamworth-previous-winners |archive-date=12 July 2010 |url-status=dead  }}</ref> Her prize was a recording contract with [[ABC Music]] and a performance at the 2008 CMA music festival in [[Nashville]].

Rae recorded and release her debut studio album ''[[Look It Up (album)|Look It Up]]'' in September 2008. The title track, "[[Look It Up (song)|Look It Up]]" was later covered by American country artist [[Ashton Shepherd]], which become a Top 20 hit on the U.S. ''Billboard'' Hot Country Songs chart.

In 2009, Rae toured with [[Brooks & Dunn]].

===2011–2012: ''Listen Here'' ===
In March 2011, Rae released her second studio album ''[[Listen Here (Jasmine Rae album)|Listen Here]]''. The album debuted at No. 1 on the ARIA Australian Artists Country Chart and No. 35 on the ARIA Albums Chart. Two singles "Hunky Country Boys" and "I'll Try Anything" topped the Australian Country airplay charts.{{citation needed|date=April 2022}} Those songs plus "I Faked It" also went to No. 1 on the CMC Music Video chart. The album was nominated for the [[ARIA Award for Best Country Album]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.destinationnsw.com.au/news-and-media/media-releases/2011-aria-awards-nominations-announced|title=2011 ARIA Awards nominations announced {{!}} Destination NSW|website=www.destinationnsw.com.au|language=en-AU|access-date=20 August 2018}}</ref> Jasmine was also a Golden Guitar nominee for Female Artist of the Year at the Australian Country Music Awards the following January.

In 2012 Australian Music fans voted Jasmine Rae, CMC OZ Artist of the Year.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.countrymusicchannel.com.au/pages/main-menu/news/latest-news/the-cmc-awards-winners |title=Country Music News - Latest local and international country music news &#124; Country Music Channel |access-date=10 September 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120324161514/http://www.countrymusicchannel.com.au/pages/main-menu/news/latest-news/the-cmc-awards-winners |archive-date=24 March 2012 |url-status=dead}}</ref> She remains the only female solo artist to ever win the award. In November 2012, Rae released a Christmas EP titled ''[[Santa's Little Helper (EP)|Santa's Little Helper]]''.

In 2013 she received a CMA Award (US) for Global Artist of the Year<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://thewest.com.au/entertainment/music/country-starlet-is-boss-of-her-domain-ng-ya-358206|title=Jasmine Rae|date=2 September 2013|work=The West Australian|access-date=20 August 2018}}</ref>

===2013–2014: ''If I Want To'' ===
In August 2013, Rae released her third studio album ''[[If I Want To]]''; the title track shot straight to the top of the CMC Top 30 Countdown chart and held strong for four weeks. The track also topped the Australian Country Radio charts. ''[[If I Want To]]'' was nominated for ARIA Award for Best Country Album.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://musicfeeds.com.au/news/aria-awards-2013-nominees-revealed-fine-arts-award-winners-announced/#/slide/1|title=ARIA Awards 2013 Nominees Revealed, Fine Arts Award Winners Announced – Music Feeds|date=1 October 2013|work=Music Feeds|access-date=20 August 2018|language=en-US}}</ref> and four Golden Guitar Awards at the 2014 CMA Awards; for Album of the Year, Top Selling Album of the Year, Single of the Year and Female Artist of the Year.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.northernstar.com.au/news/lee-kernaghan-leads-2014-golden-guitar-nominations/2084025/|title=Lee Kernaghan leads the 2014 Golden Guitar nominations|last=Cronin|first=Seanna|work=Northern Star|access-date=20 August 2018|language=en}}</ref>

In March 2014, Jasmine Rae won her second CMC Music Award for 'Female Oz Artist of the Year.'<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.countrymusicchannel.com.au/morgan-evans-wins-big-at-the-cmc-awards|title=Morgan Evans wins big at the CMC Awards {{!}} Country Music Channel on Foxtel|date=1 March 2014|work=Country Music Channel|access-date=20 August 2018|language=en-US}}</ref> Rae featured on [[Adam Brand (musician)|Adam Brand]]'s song "Quit This Time"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2prEUwvmQ70 |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211220/2prEUwvmQ70 |archive-date=2021-12-20 |url-status=live|title=Jasmine Rae & Adam Brand – Quit This Time (Music Video)|last=Jasmine Rae|date=30 September 2014|publisher=|via=YouTube}}{{cbignore}}</ref> from Brand's album ''[[My Side of the Street]]'' which was released in August 2014.

===2015–2019: ''Heartbeat'' ===
Rae released her fourth album, titled ''[[Heartbeat (Jasmine Rae album)|Heartbeat]]'' through ABC Music / Universal Music Australia on 15 May 2015. The single "Heartbeat" became her ninth number-one Australian country single<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.jasminerae.com/#!heartbeartno1radio/csca|title=JASMINE RAE – OFFICIAL WEBSITE – Singer/Songwriter, Actor, Entertainer|website=jasminerae.com}}</ref> and "When I Found You": – a song she originally wrote for her best friends wedding has been viewed more than 10 million times on YouTube.<ref>{{cite web|author=Jasmine Rae|title=Jasmine Rae – When I Found You (Official Music Video)|website=[[YouTube]]|date=1 November 2015|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L-T-aSHM0fc |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211220/L-T-aSHM0fc |archive-date=2021-12-20 |url-status=live|access-date=20 August 2018}}{{cbignore}}</ref> "Everybody Wants to Take My Money" was a finalist in the 2016 International Songwriting Competition.

''Heartbeat'' was nominated for two Golden Guitar awards in 2016 and Jasmine was again nominated for CMC 'Female Oz Artist of the Year' in 2016<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.countrymusicchannel.com.au/2016-cmc-music-awards-finalists-announced|title=2016 CMC Music Awards finalists announced {{!}} Country Music Channel on Foxtel|date=3 March 2016|work=Country Music Channel|access-date=20 August 2018|language=en-US}}</ref> and 2017.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.workingbull.com.au/news/cmc-music-awards-2017-finalists/|title=CMC Music Awards 2017: Finalists • Working Bull Pty Ltd|date=1 March 2017|work=Working Bull Pty Ltd|access-date=20 August 2018|language=en-US}}</ref> Like ''[[If I Want To]]'', the album was produced by Luke Wooten.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://themusic.com.au/music/streams/2015/05/12/jasmine-rae-heartbeat/|title=EXCLUSIVE: Jasmine Rae – Heartbeat|website=theMusic|language=en-GB|access-date=20 August 2018}}</ref>

In 2018, Jasmine toured with US country singer [[Granger Smith]]<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.northerndailyleader.com.au/story/5626945/why-2018-has-been-a-roller-coaster-ride-for-jasmine-rae/ |title=Jasmine Rae reflects on Granger Smith tour success and When I Found You court battle|newspaper=The Northern Daily Leader |date=4 September 2018}}</ref> and began writing for a new album.

In 2019, Jasmine released the singles "Right Now" and "Party on the Couch"<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://tonedeaf.thebrag.com/country/jasmine-rae-and-friends-enjoy-a-party-on-the-couch/|title=Jasmine Rae and friends enjoy a 'Party On The Couch' - Tone Country|accessdate=16 May 2023}}</ref>—her first official music video in over two and a half years.

Jasmine has also been announced as an artist on the 2020 CMC Rocks QLD line-up.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://themusic.com.au/news/cmc-rocks-qld-2020-line-up/rZO1oaCjoqU/ | title=Miranda Lambert, Kip Moore & Morgan Evans Lead CMC Rocks QLD 2020 Line-up }}</ref>

===2020–present: ''Lion Side'' ===
In 2020 Jasmine released the new singles "Green Light" and "Don't Do It for the Haters" plus the album ''Lion Side''. The album was nominated for the ARIA Award for Best Country Album<ref>{{Cite web|title=ARIA Awards 2020 Nominees|url=https://www.aria.com.au/awards/nominees|access-date=2021-02-02|website=www.aria.com.au|language=en}}</ref> and the album peaked at #11 on the ARIA Albums chart, #2 on the AIR Independent Albums chart and #1 on the ARIA Australian Country Albums chart. Jasmine was also nominated for 'Female Artist of the Year' and 'Contemporary Country Album of the Year' at the 2021 Golden Guitar Awards.<ref>{{Cite web|title=2021 Golden Guitar Nominees Revealed|url=https://www.kixcountry.com.au/country-confidential/47957-2021-golden-guitar-nominees-revealed-updating|access-date=2021-02-02|website=www.kixcountry.com.au|date=19 November 2020|language=en-gb}}</ref>

==Awards==
===ARIA Music Awards===
The [[ARIA Music Awards]] are a set of annual ceremonies presented by [[Australian Recording Industry Association]] (ARIA), which recognise excellence, innovation, and achievement across all genres of the [[music of Australia]]. They commenced in 1987. Rae has been nominated for four [[ARIA Music Awards]], all in the category [[ARIA Award for Best Country Album|Best Country Album]].<ref name="ARIA 2020 Wins">{{cite web | url = https://www.aria.com.au/awards/news/and-the-2020-aria-award-goes-to | title = And the 2020 ARIA Awards Go To… | publisher = Australian Recording Industry Association (ARIA) | access-date = 3 December 2020 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20201126005048/https://www.aria.com.au/awards/news/and-the-2020-aria-award-goes-to | archive-date = 26 November 2020 | url-status = live }}</ref>
