'''Chase Spencer Yaklin''', better known by his stage name '''Chase Bryant'''<ref name=BrianMansfield2015>{{cite web|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/music/2015/01/18/chase-bryant-on-the-verge/21081143/|title=On the Verge: Chase Bryant gets 'Back' to the country|work=USA Today|accessdate=15 February 2019|author=Brian Mansfield|date=18 January 2015}}</ref> (born October 9, 1992) is an American singer and songwriter. Bryant is a family name shared by his grandfather, Jimmy Bryant, who performed with [[Roy Orbison]] and [[Waylon Jennings]].

In early 2015 Bryant's debut single, "[[Take It On Back]]", became a top 10 single on the [[Country Airplay]] chart, which is published weekly by ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''. Bryant co-wrote and co-produced his debut EP, featuring the second single "[[Little Bit of You]]", which came out on September 23, 2014, on [[Broken Bow Records|Red Bow]] Records.

== Musical career ==
Bryant is the grandson of Jimmy Bryant, who performed with [[Roy Orbison]], and the nephew of Jeff and Junior Bryant, co-founders of the group [[Ricochet (band)|Ricochet]].<ref name="boot">{{cite web|url=http://theboot.com/chase-bryant-new-single-tour/|title=Chase Bryant Chasing His Dreams With Single, Upcoming Tour - The Boot|work=The Boot}}</ref><ref name="lc">{{cite web|url=http://www.lcsun-news.com/las_cruces-opinion/ci_25593095/llcmf-roy-orbisons-grandson-chase-bryant-returns-music |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140819125857/http://www.lcsun-news.com/las_cruces-opinion/ci_25593095/llcmf-roy-orbisons-grandson-chase-bryant-returns-music |archivedate=August 19, 2014 |title=LCCMF: Chase Bryant returns to music festival|work=Las Cruces Sun-News}}</ref>

In August 2013, Bryant signed with Red Bow Records, a division of [[Broken Bow Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.alicetx.com/news/article_a95d83f9-a32b-56a4-90c9-5e27eb8b19d7.html|title=Orange Grove's Chase Bryant explodes onto country music scene|work=Alice Echo-News Journal|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150120001002/http://www.alicetx.com/news/article_a95d83f9-a32b-56a4-90c9-5e27eb8b19d7.html|archivedate=2015-01-20}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.musicrow.com/2013/08/new-signing-for-red-bow-records/|title=New Signing For Red Bow Records|work=MusicRow - Nashville's Music Industry Publication - News, Songs From Music City|date=19 August 2013 }}</ref> A year later, he released his debut single "[[Take It On Back]]", which has charted in the top 10 of ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''{{'}}s [[Country Airplay]] chart. The EP album's second single, "[[Little Bit of You]]" released to country radio on March 30, 2015. "Room to Breathe", was released to country radio on July 4, 2016.

Bryant began touring with [[Brantley Gilbert]] in early 2015. After that Bryant was one of two opening acts, along with [[Billy Currington]], on [[Tim McGraw]]'s 2015 Shotgun Rider Tour.<ref>{{cite web|author= |url=http://tasteofcountry.com/tim-mcgraw-2015-shotgun-rider-tour/ |title=Tim McGraw Announces 2015 Shotgun Rider Tour Dates |website=Tasteofcountry.com |date= |accessdate=2016-04-10}}</ref> After leaving Broken Bow, Bryant self-released the album ''Upbringing'' in 2021.<ref name="upbringing"/>

==Musical stylings==
Bryant is [[left-handed]] but plays a right-handed guitar upside-down.<ref>{{cite web|author= |url=http://tasteofcountry.com/chase-bryant-country-music-upside-down/ |title=The Guitar Freak: Chase Bryant Turning Country Upside Down |website=Tasteofcountry.com |date= |accessdate=2016-04-10}}</ref>
