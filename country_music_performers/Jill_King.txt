'''Jill Christine King''' (born April 2, 1973, in [[Arab, Alabama]]<ref name="allmusic"/>) is an American [[country music]] artist. A graduate of [[Vanderbilt University]],<ref name="allmusic"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.countrystarsonline.com/FanFairFeatures/JillKing.htm |title=Jill King |accessdate=January 7, 2008 |work=Country Stars Online |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20061020153353/http://www.countrystarsonline.com/FanFairFeatures/JillKing.htm |archivedate=October 20, 2006 }}</ref> she spent several years in [[Nashville, Tennessee]], before being discovered at [[Tootsie's Orchid Lounge]], a popular venue for singer-songwriters in Nashville.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.countrystandardtime.com/d/article.asp?xid=541&t=Jill_King_another_singer_from_Tootsies_makes_good |title=Jill King, another singer from Tootsie's, makes good |accessdate=January 7, 2008 |last=Teverbaugh |first=Rick |work=Country Standard Time}}</ref>

In 2003, she released her debut album, ''Jillbilly'', on the independent Blue Diamond label. The album's first and third singles both entered ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' Hot Country Singles & Tracks (now [[Hot Country Songs]]) charts. Several of her singles have also charted on the independent ''Music Row'' music charts as well.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://magazine.angrycountry.com/article.php?story=20050119160844666 |title=Jill King jumps seven spots on Music Row charts and jumps back into the studio |accessdate=January 7, 2008 |date=January 1, 2005|work=Angry Country |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303175530/http://magazine.angrycountry.com/article.php?story=20050119160844666 |archive-date=March 3, 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Her 2008 single "[[Somebody New (Billy Ray Cyrus song)|Somebody New]]" is a cover of a song previously recorded by [[Billy Ray Cyrus]].

King founded her own label, Foundher Records, in 2010. She released her third album, ''Rain on Fire'', that same year.
