'''David Brent Nail''' (born May 18, 1979) is an American [[country music]] artist and the frontman of ''David Nail & The Well Ravens''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.davidnail.com/about/|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20160708073156/http://www.davidnail.com/about/|archive-date = July 8, 2016|title = David Nail - - About}}</ref> In 2002, he debuted the single "Memphis" from an unreleased album for [[Mercury Records]] Nashville. Five years later, he signed with [[Universal Music Group Nashville|MCA Nashville]], for which he has released four studio albums: ''[[I'm About to Come Alive]]'', ''[[The Sound of a Million Dreams]]'', ''[[I'm a Fire (album)|I'm a Fire]]'', and ''[[Fighter (David Nail album)|Fighter]]'' plus two [[extended plays]], ''1979'' and ''Uncovered''. The albums have produced seven chart entries on [[Hot Country Songs]] and [[Country Airplay]], including two that have reached No.&nbsp;1: "[[Let It Rain (David Nail song)|Let It Rain]]" (featuring [[Sarah Buxton]]) and "[[Whatever She's Got]]", plus the top 10 hit "[[Red Light (David Nail song)|Red Light]]".

==Career==
David Nail's first recording contract was with [[Mercury Records]] Nashville, a division of [[Universal Music Group Nashville]].<ref>[http://www.musicdish.com/mag/index.php3?id=6564 Event Review: WCTO Cat Country 96 Stage At MusicFest]</ref> While there, he recorded a self-titled debut album, which was produced by [[Keith Stegall]] and John Kelton.<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p529054/biography|pure_url=yes}}|title=David Nail biography|last=Leggett|first=Steve|work=[[Allmusic]]|access-date=July 27, 2010}}</ref> The lead-off single, "Memphis", reached number 52 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' Hot Country Singles & Tracks (now [[Hot Country Songs]]) charts. Deborah Evans Price gave the song a mostly-positive review, calling it a "solid performance" but saying that she considered its storyline implausible.<ref>{{cite magazine|last=Price|first=Deborah Evans|date=June 15, 2002|title=Singles: — David Nail: "Memphis"|magazine=Billboard|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=DBEEAAAAMBAJ&q=%22david+nail%22+%22Memphis%22&pg=PA24}}</ref> Advance copies of the album were sent to radio stations, but it was not commercially released.<ref name="cst">{{cite web |url=http://www.countrystandardtime.com/news/newsitem.asp?xid=544 |title=David Nail signs new record deal |access-date=July 27, 2010 |date=April 20, 2007 |work=[[Country Standard Time]]}}</ref> He also sang backing vocals on the track "But I Can't Let You Go" on then-labelmate [[Meredith Edwards (singer)|Meredith Edwards]]' 2001 album ''[[Reach (Meredith Edwards album)|Reach]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/reach-mw0000586766|title=''Reach''|last=Cohoon|first=Rick|work=Allmusic|access-date=May 7, 2017}}</ref>

===''I'm About to Come Alive'' and ''The Sound of a Million Dreams'' (2007–2012)===

Nail signed to [[MCA Records]] Nashville, also a division of Universal Music Group Nashville, in April 2007.<ref name="cst"/> His debut album for the label, ''[[I'm About to Come Alive]]'', was produced by [[Frank Liddell]] and Mike Wrucke, the same producers who work with [[Miranda Lambert]]. The album includes a duet with Lambert, co-writes with [[Kenny Chesney]] and [[Rascal Flatts]]' lead singer [[Gary LeVox]], and five songs that Nail co-wrote.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.countrystandardtime.com/news/newsitem.asp?xid=2889|title=David Nail fans get "Red Light"|date=May 12, 2009|publisher=[[Country Standard Time]]|access-date=July 27, 2010}}</ref>

The first single was "I'm About to Come Alive", originally recorded by [[Train (band)|Train]] on their 2003 album ''[[My Private Nation]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.the9513.com/david-nail-im-about-to-come-alive/ |title=David Nail – "I'm About to Come Alive" |access-date=July 27, 2010 |last=Malec |first=Jim |date=April 22, 2008 |publisher=The 9513 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090630095605/http://www.the9513.com/david-nail-im-about-to-come-alive |archive-date=June 30, 2009}}</ref> Nail's version peaked at number 47 on the country chart. The second single, "[[Red Light (David Nail song)|Red Light]]," entered Top 40 in May 2009 and peaked at number 7. "[[Turning Home]]", which Chesney co-wrote with Scooter Carusoe, was the album's third single,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blogs.tennessean.com/tunein/2010/01/24/david-nails-success-starts-at-red-light/|title=David Nail's success starts at 'Red Light'|last=Watts|first=Cindy|date=January 24, 2010|publisher=The Tennessean|access-date=July 27, 2010}}</ref> reaching a peak of number 20.
[[File:Davidnail.jpg|thumb|upright=0.9 |Nail being interviewed in 2010]]
In February 2011, Nail released his fifth single, "[[Let It Rain (David Nail song)|Let It Rain]]", which featured [[Sarah Buxton]] on guest vocals. It served as the lead-off single to his second studio album, ''[[The Sound of a Million Dreams]]'', which was released on November 15, 2011.<ref>{{cite news|last=Betts|first=Stephen L.|title=David Nail Fans Will Soon Hear 'The Sound of a Million Dreams'|url=http://www.theboot.com/2011/09/28/david-nail-sound-of-a-million-dreams-new-album/|access-date=September 29, 2011|newspaper=The Boot|date=September 28, 2011}}</ref> In January 2012, "Let It Rain" became Nail's first No. 1 hit. The title track was released as the album's second and final single, and reached a peak of number 38 on the Hot Country Songs chart.

Nail released a three-track digital EP titled ''1979'' on July 17, 2012. The EP includes a cover of [[Adele]]'s "[[Someone like You (Adele song)|Someone like You]]".<ref>{{cite news|title=David Nail Releases 1979 on July 17, 2012|url=http://www.davidnail.com/news_d.aspx?nid=7689|access-date=June 29, 2012|date=June 29, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130515092003/http://www.davidnail.com/news_d.aspx?nid=7689|archive-date=May 15, 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref>

===''I'm a Fire'' and ''Fighter'' (2013–2017)===
A new single, "[[Whatever She's Got]]", was released to country radio on May 28, 2013. It served as the lead-off single to his third studio album, ''[[I'm a Fire (album)|I'm a Fire]]'', which was released on March 4, 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gotcountryonline.com/2014/01/06/david-nail-releases-track-listing-for-new-album-im-a-fire-out-march-4/ |title=David Nail Releases Track Listing for New Album "I'm a Fire" Out March 4 &#124; Got Country |access-date=January 6, 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140107005655/http://www.gotcountryonline.com/2014/01/06/david-nail-releases-track-listing-for-new-album-im-a-fire-out-march-4/ |archive-date=January 7, 2014 }}</ref> It reached number one on the [[Country Airplay]] chart in February 2014. The second single from the album was announced to be "[[Kiss You Tonight]]", co-written by [[David Cook (singer)|David Cook]], an ''[[American Idol]]'' winner.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.roughstock.com/blog/david-nail-releases-kiss-you-tonight-as-second-single-from-upcoming-i-m-a-fire-album |title=David Nail Releases "Kiss You Tonight" as Second Single from Upcoming "I'm a Fire" Album &#124; New Country Music, Listen to Songs & Video &#124; Roughstock.com |access-date=February 28, 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140226174237/http://www.roughstock.com/blog/david-nail-releases-kiss-you-tonight-as-second-single-from-upcoming-i-m-a-fire-album |archive-date=February 26, 2014 }}</ref>

On July 20, 2015, David Nail released a new single entitled "[[Night's on Fire]]." The song peaked at No. 14 on Country Airplay in July 2016, and served as the lead-off single to his fourth studio album ''[[Fighter (David Nail album)|Fighter]]''.

In May 2016 he released an EP of covers titled "Uncovered." It included "[[In the Ghetto]]", "[[Can't Feel My Face]]", "[[In the Air Tonight]]", and "[[Send My Love (To Your New Lover)]]".

In September 2016, the album's second single, "[[Good at Tonight]]" featuring [[Brothers Osborne]], was released to country radio. It failed to reach the Top 50 of the country charts and it was announced in early 2017 that David Nail had parted ways with MCA Nashville after 10 years with the label.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://theboot.com/david-nail-leaves-mca-nashville/ |title=David Nail Splits With MCA Nashville |website=Theboot.com |date=February 15, 2017 |access-date=April 18, 2020}}</ref>

===''David Nail and The Well Ravens'' (2018–present)===
In July 2018, Nail announced a new project as the frontman of the band ''David Nail & The Well Ravens''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/country/8465329/david-nail-heavy-well-ravens |title=David Nail & the Well Ravens Premiere 'Heavy' Video, Talk Evolving Idea of Success |website=Billboard.com |date=July 1, 2018|access-date=April 18, 2020}}</ref> The band's first album titled ''Only This And Nothing More'', was independently released on September 14, 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.davidnail.com/davidnailandthewellravens/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171203224952/http://www.davidnail.com/davidnailandthewellravens/|archive-date=December 3, 2017|title=David Nail - - David Nail and the Well Ravens}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Nail was born and grew up in [[Kennett, Missouri]], where his father was a high [[school band]] [[conducting|director]]. He married longtime girlfriend Catherine Werne on June 6, 2009, in [[Franklin, Tennessee]]. On December 12, 2015, Nail and Werne welcomed twins, son Lawson Brent Nail and daughter Lillian Catherine Nail.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://celebritybabies.people.com/2015/12/12/david-nail-welcomes-twins-son-lawson-daughter-lillian/ |title=David Nail Welcomes Twins Lawson Brent and Lillian Catherine – Moms & Babies – Celebrity Babies and Kids - Moms & Babies - People.com |website=Celebritybabies.people.com |access-date=August 27, 2016}}</ref>

They welcomed a baby girl, Ellie Britton, in 2020.

Nail participated in the 2010 [[Taco Bell]] All-Star Legends and Celebrity Softball Game, hitting a home run past the right-field fence in [[Angel Stadium]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blog.gactv.com/blog/2010/07/14/david-nail-leads-country's-good-sports/ |title=Country Décor, American Travel, Southern Food Recipes, Rustic Weddings &#124; GAC |website=Blog.gactv.com |access-date=August 27, 2016}}</ref>

He attended [[Arkansas State University]] where he was a member of [[Pi Kappa Alpha]].<ref>[http://www.countryrokmusic.com/artist/David_Nail/24424/ ]{{dead link|date=August 2016}}</ref>

He is an avid fan of the [[MLB]]'s [[St. Louis Cardinals]] and sang "[[God Bless America]]" during Game 7 of the Cardinals' [[2011 World Series]] game versus the [[Texas Rangers (baseball)|Texas Rangers]].

He sang the [[national anthem]] at the [[Mizzou Tigers]] 2012 homecoming football game versus the [[University of Kentucky]].
