'''Hunter Easton Hayes''' (born September 9, 1991)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.musicnewsnashville.com/hunter-hayes-celebrates-his-20th-birthday-at-house-of-blues|title=Hunter Hayes Celebrates His 20th Birthday at House of Blues|author=Dan Harr|work=Music News Nashville|date=September 14, 2011|access-date=September 14, 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111110064648/http://www.musicnewsnashville.com/hunter-hayes-celebrates-his-20th-birthday-at-house-of-blues|archive-date=November 10, 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref> is an American multi-genre singer, songwriter, record producer, and multi-instrumentalist. He is proficient at more than 30 instruments.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-615/6077406/hunter-hayes-leads-country-youth-revolution-storyline-video-interview|title=Hunter Hayes Leads Country's Youth Revolution, Without a Pickup Truck or Beer in Sight|author=Deborah Evans Price|magazine=Billboard|date=May 6, 2014|access-date=May 6, 2014}}</ref>

Hayes released his [[Hunter Hayes (album)|self-titled debut album]] in 2011.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://tasteofcountry.com/hunter-hayes-debut-album/ |title=Hunter Hayes Sets Release Date for Debut Album |work=Taste of Country |date=August 24, 2011 |access-date=November 7, 2011 |first=Amanda |last=Hensel}}</ref> It reached number seven on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] and number one on the [[Top Country Albums]], and sold over 1.1 million copies. Its most successful single, "[[Wanted (Hunter Hayes song)|Wanted]]", sold over 3.5 million copies and made Hayes the youngest male act to ever top the [[Hot Country Songs|''Billboard'' Hot Country Songs]].

Hayes' commercial success and his talent both as a songwriter and instrumentalist prompted ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' to call him the leader of "Country Music's Youth Revolution" in 2014.<ref>{{cite magazine |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-615/6077406/hunter-hayes-leads-country-youth-revolution-storyline-video-interview |title=Hunter Hayes Leads Country's Youth Revolution, Without a Pickup Truck or Beer in Sight |magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |date=May 6, 2014}}</ref> He has been nominated for five [[Grammy Award]]s including [[Grammy Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]], and won the 2012 [[CMA Award]] for [[Country Music Association Award for New Artist of the Year|New Artist of the Year]] and three [[Broadcast Music, Inc.|BMI Awards]].

==Life and career==
Hayes was born on September 9, 1991, in [[Breaux Bridge, Louisiana]]; he is an only child.<ref>[http://www.hunterhayes.com/bio Hunter's Story] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120209104810/http://www.hunterhayes.com/bio |date=February 9, 2012 }}. HunterHayes.com. Retrieved on February 21, 2012.</ref> His parents both have [[Cajuns|Cajun]] ancestry, along with [[English people|English]], [[Scotch-Irish Americans|Scots-Irish]], and [[Germans|German]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0371048/bio?ref_=nm_ov_bio_sm#trivia|title=Hunter Hayes : Biography|publisher=IMDb.com|access-date=May 10, 2014}}</ref> He grew up [[Catholic Church|Catholic]], singing songs in both English and French.<ref>{{cite journal |url=http://www.people.com/people/archive/article/0,,20123577,00.html |title=Squeeze Play |journal=People |first=Jeremy |last=Helligar |date=October 27, 1997 |volume=48 |issue=17 |page=113 |access-date=May 2, 2013 |archive-date=April 21, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140421054547/http://www.people.com/people/archive/article/0,,20123577,00.html |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Wilkinson |first=Hannah |title=You Are Good Enough: Hunter Hayes and Jen Ledger Find Their Value In Christ |url=https://www.jesuscalling.com/podcast/good-enough-hunter-hayes-jen-ledger-find-value-christ/ |access-date=2022-08-14 |website=Jesus Calling |date=August 16, 2018 |language=en-US}}</ref>

Hayes started performing when he was 2. At 4, he began making appearances locally and on national television,<ref>{{cite news|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=iz0dAAAAIBAJ&pg=5100,573829&dq=hunter-hayes+accordion&hl=en|title=Kids keep Cajun traditions thriving|last=Rose|first=Christopher|date=April 4, 1997|work=The Tuscaloosa News|access-date=June 30, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://tasteofcountry.com/hunter-hayes-music-interview-2011/|title=Hunter Hayes Was 'Born' to Make Country Music|last=Conaway|first=Alanna|work=Taste of Country|date=June 13, 2011 |access-date=June 30, 2011}}</ref> including ''[[Maury (TV series)|Maury]]'', ''[[Rosie O'Donnell Show]]'', and the [[Nickelodeon]] game show ''[[Figure It Out]]'', where he performed "[[Jambalaya (On the Bayou)]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mPpjNgdT2dk/ |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211219/mPpjNgdT2dk |archive-date=2021-12-19 |url-status=live|title=Hunter Hayes - "Jambalaya" on Figure it Out|website=[[YouTube]]|date=October 16, 2011 |access-date=August 1, 2012}}{{cbignore}}</ref> [[American Songwriter]] writes that "Hayes received his first guitar from actor [[Robert Duvall]] at age 6"<ref name="American Songwriter">{{cite magazine|url=http://www.americansongwriter.com/2012/05/hunter-hayes-is-martin-guitars-latest-brand-ambassador/ |title=Hunter Hayes Is Martin Guitars' Latest Brand Ambassador |magazine=American Songwriter |author=Evan Schlansky |date=May 21, 2012 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130128022554/http://www.americansongwriter.com/2012/05/hunter-hayes-is-martin-guitars-latest-brand-ambassador/ |archive-date=January 28, 2013 |access-date=June 6, 2012 |df=mdy-all }}</ref> At age 7, he was invited to perform for President [[Bill Clinton]] for a [[White House]] lawn party. At 13, he appeared on [[America's Most Talented Kids]], a show hosted by [[Dave Coulier]]; he performed the hit [[Hank Williams]] song, "[[Hey Good Lookin' (song)|Hey Good Lookin']]". At age 9, Hunter released his debut independent studio album ''"Through My Eyes"'' on October 8, 2000, under Suagartown Records and Louisiana Red Hot Records.<ref>{{cite web|title=Through My Eyes - Hunter Hayes {{!}} Songs, Reviews, Credits {{!}} AllMusic|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/through-my-eyes-mw0000053846|website=AllMusic|access-date=29 December 2017}}</ref> He released several more independent albums: ''Make a Wish'' in 2001, ''Holidays with Hunter'' in 2003, ''Honoring our French Heritage'' in 2006, and ''Songs About Nothing'' in 2008.

In 2008, he moved from Breaux Bridge to [[Nashville, Tennessee]], and signed with [[Universal Music Publishing Group]] as a songwriter. In 2010, Hayes co-wrote "Play" for the [[Rascal Flatts]] album ''[[Nothing Like This (album)|Nothing Like This]]''. In September, he was signed to [[Atlantic Records]] Nashville and began working on what would become his major-label debut.

Hayes appeared as the opening act of 10 dates of country star [[Taylor Swift]]'s [[Speak Now World Tour]] throughout the summer of 2011 and concluded his leg of the tour in St. Louis, Missouri.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.hunterhayes.com/news/hunter-hayes-will-open-taylor-swift-speak-now-north-american-tour-2011 |title=Hunter Hayes will open for Taylor Swift SPEAK NOW North American Tour 2011 |work=HunterHayes.com |date=March 4, 2011 |access-date=May 2, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120506142749/http://www.hunterhayes.com/news/hunter-hayes-will-open-taylor-swift-speak-now-north-american-tour-2011 |archive-date=May 6, 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref> In April 2011, he went on his own radio tour where he debuted songs that would appear on his debut album.<ref name="radiotour">{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mpNAs08ZcVc  |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211219/mpNAs08ZcVc |archive-date=2021-12-19 |url-status=live|title=Hunter Hayes – Radio Tour (Week 1) |access-date=October 30, 2011 |author=Hunter Hayes |website=[[YouTube]] |date=April 2011}}{{cbignore}}</ref> His debut headlining tour was the Most Wanted Tour; the first leg of the tour began on October 8, 2011, in [[Beaumont, Texas]], and ended on December 4, 2011, in [[San Bernardino, California]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://tasteofcountry.com/hunter-hayes-2011-tour-dates/ |title=Hunter Hayes Sets Fall 2011 Tour Dates |work=Taste of Country |date=September 13, 2011 |access-date=November 7, 2011 |first=Alanna |last=Conaway}}</ref>

Hayes and singer [[Victoria Justice]] contributed a duet to the soundtrack of 2011's ''[[Footloose (2011 film)|Footloose]]'' remake, recording a cover of [[Mike Reno]]'s and [[Ann Wilson]]'s 1984 hit "[[Almost Paradise (song)|Almost Paradise]]" from the original film. From January to February 2012, Hayes was the opening act on the [[Rascal Flatts]] "Thaw Out" tour.<ref>[http://www.rascalflatts.com/story/news/rascal-flatts-announce-ithaw-out-2012i-tour Rascal Flatts Announce Thaw Out 2012 Tour]. RascalFlatts.com (October 17, 2011). Retrieved on February 21, 2012.</ref>
[[File:Hunter Hayes.jpg|thumb|Hayes signing autographs in [[Frederick, Maryland]] in August 2012]]

"Where We Left Off" is an original song, written by Hayes, that can be heard on the [[Act of Valor: The Album|soundtrack]] for the 2012 war film ''[[Act of Valor]]''.<ref>[http://www.sltrib.com/sltrib/entertainment/53539733-81/act-valor-original-songs.html.csp 'Act of Valor' soundtrack features original songs from Lady Antebellum, more]. During the 2012 [[O Music Awards]], [[The Flaming Lips]] broke a [[Guinness World Record]] (previously held by [[Jay-Z]]) for most concerts played in multiple cities in a 24-hr. period. Hayes was the opening act for them at the Hattiesburg stop.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.cmt.com/news/country-music/1688734/hunter-hayes-performs-on-o-music-awards.jhtml |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121105061428/http://www.cmt.com/news/country-music/1688734/hunter-hayes-performs-on-o-music-awards.jhtml |url-status=dead |archive-date=November 5, 2012 |title=Hunter Hayes Performs on O Music Awards |work=[[Country Music Television]] |date=June 28, 2012 |access-date=May 2, 2013}}</ref>

In May 2012, [[Martin Guitars]] named Hayes as C.F. Martin & Co.'s newest brand ambassador. [[American Songwriter]] quoted Hayes on the subject: "It is an absolute honor to be named an official Martin Ambassador and to be recognized by the Martin family."<ref name="American Songwriter"/>

On September 7, 2012, he was inducted at the [[Mahalia Jackson Theater]] in [[New Orleans|New Orleans, Louisiana]], into the [[Louisiana Music Hall of Fame]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://finance.yahoo.com/news/hunter-hayes-inducted-louisiana-music-194054387.html |title=Hunter Hayes Inducted Into Louisiana Music Hall of Fame |work=[[Yahoo! ===2011–2014: ''Hunter Hayes'' and ''Storyline''===

Hayes's debut single, "[[Storm Warning (song)|Storm Warning]]", released to radio on May 16, 2011. His [[Hunter Hayes (album)|self-titled]] major-label debut album was released on October 11, 2011.<ref>[http://www.hunterhayes.com/news/hunter-hayes-release-self-titled-debut-album-atlantic-records-october-11 HUNTER HAYES TO RELEASE SELF-TITLED DEBUT ALBUM ON ATLANTIC RECORDS OCTOBER 11] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120220175525/http://www.hunterhayes.com/news/hunter-hayes-release-self-titled-debut-album-atlantic-records-october-11 |date=February 20, 2012 }}. HunterHayes.com (August 24, 2011). Retrieved on 2012-02-21.</ref> He co-wrote every song, played every instrument, and along with Dann Huff, produced the album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.countrystandardtime.com/news/newsitem.asp?xid=5319|title=Hunter Hayes becomes Next Big Thing|date=April 22, 2011|work=Country Standard Time|access-date=June 30, 2011}}</ref> "[[Wanted (Hunter Hayes song)|Wanted]]", the second single, was released to radio on March 5, 2012.

On July 24, 2012, he appeared on the CBS's ''The Talk'', where he was then given an RIAA Gold Certification plaque for "Wanted".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.hunterhayes.com/news/hunters-interview-and-performance-talk |title=Hunter's Interview And Performance On The Talk |work=HunterHayes.com |date=July 25, 2012 |access-date=May 2, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121015165352/http://www.hunterhayes.com/news/hunters-interview-and-performance-talk |archive-date=October 15, 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Just shy of two months since "Wanted" went gold, the song was certified Platinum by RIAA on August 23, 2012.

"Wanted" became his first No. 1 single,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://tasteofcountry.com/hunter-hayes-wanted-no-1-single/ |title=Hunter Hayes Gets What He 'Wanted' With First No. 1 Single |work=Taste of Country |date=September 18, 2012 |first=Alanna |last=Conaway |access-date=May 2, 2013}}</ref> making him the youngest solo male act to top Hot Country Songs, breaking the record set in 1973 by [[Johnny Rodriguez]].<ref>{{cite magazine |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-615/475001/hunter-hayes-makes-history-little-big-town-shines-on-country-charts |title=Hunter Hayes Makes History, Little Big Town Shines on Country Charts |magazine=Billboard |date=September 20, 2012 |first=Wade |last=Jessen |access-date=May 2, 2013}}</ref> Twenty weeks after first appearing in the top spot and under a new chart format, "Wanted" returned to the top spot.<ref>{{cite magazine |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/1539423/ask-billboard-whats-so-unusual-about-hunter-hayes-new-no-1/ |title=Ask Billboard: What's so unusual about Hunter Hayes's New No. 1 |magazine=Billboard |first=Gary |last=Trust |date=February 18, 2013 |access-date=May 2, 2013}}</ref> "[[Somebody's Heartbreak]]", the album's third single, went to No. 1 on the [[Country Airplay]] chart.<ref>{{cite magazine|title=Hunter Hayes Dupe 1 - Chart History (Country Airplay)|url= https://www.billboard.com/artist/hunter-hayes-dupe-1/chart-history/csa/ |magazine=Billboard|accessdate=February 11, 2023}}</ref>

Starting September 14, 2012, Hayes began appearing as the opening act on 90+ dates for [[Carrie Underwood]]'s [[Blown Away Tour]].

He was also nominated for three Grammys including [[Grammy Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]] at the [[2013 Grammy Awards]]; he is the youngest male country artist to be nominated in all three categories for which he was nominated.<ref>{{cite magazine |url=http://www.billboardhcom/articles/columns/the-615/1538011/hunter-hayes-my-grammy-ritual |title=Hunter Hayes: My Grammy Ritual |magazine=Billboard |date=February 6, 2013 |first=Matt |last=Diehl |access-date=May 2, 2013 }}{{Dead link|date=January 2020 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=About Hunter Hayes|url=http://www.cmt.com/artists/hunter-hayes/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121220034549/http://www.cmt.com/artists/hunter-hayes/|url-status=dead|archive-date=December 20, 2012|access-date=March 16, 2013}}</ref> The album's fourth single, "[[I Want Crazy]]", which is from the deluxe ''Encore'' edition, was released to country radio on April 7, 2013. It peaked at number two on the Country Airplay chart in August 2013. Following it was "[[Everybody's Got Somebody but Me]]", a re-recording of a song from the original press of the album. The re-recorded version features guest vocals from [[Jason Mraz]].

Hunter was one of five country singers who won a CMT Artist of the Year award in 2013. This award is awarded every year to a group of country musicians who have had an outstanding year.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cmt.com/news/country-music/1718091/hunter-hayes-interview-cmt-artists-of-the-year.jhtml |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131130075801/http://www.cmt.com/news/country-music/1718091/hunter-hayes-interview-cmt-artists-of-the-year.jhtml |url-status=dead |archive-date=November 30, 2013 |title=News : Hunter Hayes Interview: CMT Artists of the Year |publisher=CMT |date=November 28, 2013 |access-date=May 9, 2014}}</ref>

Hayes performed "[[Invisible (Hunter Hayes song)|Invisible]]", the lead single from his second album, at the [[56th Annual Grammy Awards]].<ref>{{cite web|author=Craig Shelburne |url=http://www.cmt.com/news/country-music/1721157/hunter-hayes-debuts-invisible-on-grammys.jhtml |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140129074928/http://www.cmt.com/news/country-music/1721157/hunter-hayes-debuts-invisible-on-grammys.jhtml |url-status=dead |archive-date=January 29, 2014 |title=News : Hunter Hayes Debuts "Invisible" on Grammys |publisher=CMT |date=January 26, 2014 |access-date=May 9, 2014}}</ref> ''[[Storyline (Hunter Hayes album)|Storyline]]'' was released on May 6, 2014.<ref name="news.radio.com">{{cite web |url=http://news.radio.com/2014/03/11/hunter-hayes-new-album-storyline-to-drop-may-6/ |title=Hunter Hayes' New Album 'Storyline' to Drop May 6 « Radio.com |publisher=News.radio.com |date=March 11, 2014 |access-date=May 9, 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140321031056/http://news.radio.com/2014/03/11/hunter-hayes-new-album-storyline-to-drop-may-6/ |archive-date=March 21, 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref> In an effort to promote his album and to raise awareness to end child hunger, Hunter broke the Guinness World Record for the most concerts played in multiple cities in 24 hours, previously held by [[The Flaming Lips]].<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-615/6084729/hunter-hayes-breaks-the-flaming-lips-guinness-world-record-while-raising-awareness-most-concerts-played-in-multiple-cities-in-24-hours-conagra-Foods |title=Hunter Hayes Breaks Guinness World Record For Most Concerts Played in multiple cities in 24 hours |magazine=Billboard |date=May 10, 2014 |access-date=August 9, 2014}}</ref> The album's second single, "[[Tattoo (Hunter Hayes song)|Tattoo]]" released to country radio on June 16, 2014.

On July 20, 2014, Hayes's self-titled debut album was certified double platinum by the RIAA.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&ar=Hunter+Hayes&ti=Hunter+Hayes#search_section|title=Gold & Platinum - RIAA|website=[[Recording Industry Association of America]]}}</ref> Following that was "[[Light Me Up (Hunter Hayes song)|Light Me Up]]", another single from the deluxe version of his debut album, in 2015.

===2015–2018: ''The 21 Project'' and canceled album===
On May 21, 2015, Hayes released "[[21 (Hunter Hayes song)|21]]", which was the title track for both his EP and third studio album, [[The 21 Project]]. He also appeared on the track "I Can Play Guitar" on [[Brett Kissel]]'s album ''[[Pick Me Up (album)|Pick Me Up]]''.

On November 2, 2016, Hayes performed with four holograms of himself on Jimmy Kimmel Live's after the CMA show.

In March 2017, Hayes performed as part of the [[C2C: Country to Country]] festival in the UK where he played alongside [[Dan + Shay]] and [[Darius Rucker]] supporting [[Reba McEntire]] and would return in 2019 with [[Carly Pearce]] and [[Dustin Lynch]] in support of [[Lady A]]. Following "21," Hayes released several songs including “[[Yesterday's Song|Yesterday’s Song]]” which were slated to be part of a studio album set to be released in February 2018. Hayes then canceled the project citing “shifted priorities.”<ref>{{cite magazine |url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/country/8527354/hunter-hayes-wild-blue-interview|title=Hunter Hayes on His New Album, 'Wild Blue': 'What This Record Did for Me Was Something Pretty Profound'|first= Melinda |last=Newman |date= August 14, 2019 |magazine=Billboard }}</ref>

Hayes also appeared as a guest star in the Nickelodeon show ''Bella and the Bulldogs''.{{Citation needed|date=July 2019}}

===Academy of Country Music Awards===
The [[Academy of Country Music Awards]], also known as the ACM Awards, were first held in 1966, honoring the industry's accomplishments during the previous year. It was the first country music awards program held by a major organization. The academy's signature "hat" trophy was created in 1968. The awards were first televised in 1972 on [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]].

===Grammy Awards===
A [[Grammy Award]] (originally called '''Gramophone Award'''), or '''Grammy''', is an accolade by the [[National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences]] (NARAS) of the United States to recognize outstanding achievement in the [[music industry]]. The annual presentation ceremony features performances by prominent artists, and the presentation of those awards that have a more popular interest.
