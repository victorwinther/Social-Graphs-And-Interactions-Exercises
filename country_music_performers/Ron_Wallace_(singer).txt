'''Ron Wallace''' (born in Pompano Beach, Florida<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ronwallaceofficial.com|title=Ron Wallace Homepage|date=January 22, 2019|website=ronwallaceofficial.com/bio/}}</ref>) and raised in Kansas City, Missouri, is an American [[country music]] singer-songwriter. Wallace was signed to [[Sony]]/[[Columbia Records]] and charted one single on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs|Hot Country Singles & Tracks]] chart. He released an EP, ''It Must Be Love'', independently in late March 2015.

==Career==
Wallace moved to [[Nashville, Tennessee|Nashville]] in 1987, where he found work as a demo singer.<ref name="billboard">{{cite news|last=Flippo|first=Chet|title=Demo Singer Ron Wallace Steps Out|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=1g0EAAAAMBAJ&q=%22Ron%20wallace%22%20%22listening%20now%22&pg=PA30|accessdate=February 9, 2014|newspaper=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=September 9, 1995}}</ref> He sang background vocals on [[Faith Hill]]'s 1993 album ''[[Take Me as I Am (Faith Hill album)|Take Me as I Am]]''<ref>{{cite web|title=''Take Me as I Am'' – Faith Hill {{!}} Credits|url={{AllMusic|class=album|id=mw0000104050/credits|pure_url=yes}}|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|accessdate=February 9, 2014}}</ref> and [[Ty Herndon]]'s 1995 debut ''[[What Mattered Most]]''.<ref>{{cite AV media notes |title=What Mattered Most |others=Ty Herndon |year=1995 |publisher=Epic Records |id=66397}}</ref> In 1994, Wallace signed to [[Columbia Records]].<ref name="billboard" /> His debut single, "I'm Listening Now", was released in August 1995.<ref>{{cite web|title=I'm Listening Now – Ron Wallace|url={{AllMusic|class=album|id=mw0000645935|pure_url=yes}}|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|accessdate=February 9, 2014}}</ref> Deborah Evans Price of ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' gave the song a favorable review, writing that "Wallace has a strong voice and displays a rather moving interpretive ability."<ref>{{cite news|last=Price|first=Deborah Evans|title=Single 19, 1995}}</ref> It peaked at number 65 on the ''Billboard'' [[Hot Country Songs|Hot Country Singles & Tracks]] chart.<ref name="whitburn">{{cite book|title=Hot Country Songs 1944 to
