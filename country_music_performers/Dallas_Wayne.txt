{{short description|American singer-songwriter}}

'''Dallas Wayne''' (born 1956)<ref name="nodepression1">{{cite web|url=https://www.nodepression.com/dallas-wayne-thats-how-i-got-to-springfield/ |title=Dallas Wayne - That's How I Got to Springfield |publisher=No Depression |date= November 2000|accessdate=2021-12-30}}</ref> is a singer, songwriter, voice-over artist and on-air radio personality for [[SiriusXM Satellite Radio]].<ref name="siriusxm1">{{cite web|url=https://www.siriusxm.com/channels/willies-roadhouse|title=Willie's Roadhouse: Willie Nelson's Classic Country}}</ref> A native of [[Springfield, Missouri]], he grew up in [[Branson, Missouri]] and [[Cape Girardeau, Missouri]].<ref name="nodepression1"/> Dallas began performing professionally while in high school.

He moved to [[Nashville, Tennessee|Nashville]], in 1975<ref name="nodepression1"/> where he further developed his vocal style singing [[demo (music)|demos]] for many of the top publishing houses in the music industry.  His roommate in Nashville was [[Dennis Morgan (songwriter)]].<ref name="nodepression1"/>

While touring Europe in the early 90s, Dallas forged a deal with Texicalli Records in [[Finland]] and was signed to record an album. He went on to record six albums. Later, he moved to [[Scandinavia]], where he became a staff writer for [[Warner/Chappell Music]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.discogs.com/artist/1329009-Dallas-Wayne|title=Dallas Wayne|website=[[Discogs]] }}</ref>

After four years living and touring in Europe, Dallas returned to the U.S. and signed a record deal with [[HighTone Records]]. In addition to recording two albums of his own on the HighTone label, Dallas was a part of the honky-tonk supergroup the TwangBangers.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/the-twangbangers-mn0000919650/biography|title = The Twangbangers Biography, Songs, & Albums| website=[[AllMusic]] }}</ref>

Dallas moved to Austin in 2003; two years later, he released the CD ''I'm Your Biggest Fan'' on the [[Koch Records]] Nashville label. In 2009, Dallas released the album ''I'll Take the Fifth'' on Smith Entertainment records. Dallas also performed with the legendary country band Heybale from 2011 to 2020 every Sunday night at The Continental Club in Austin.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://heybalemusic.com/ |title=Hey, it's Heybale! |publisher=Heybalemusic.com |date= |accessdate=2021-12-30}}</ref>

In 2016 and 2018, respectively, Dallas recorded the albums ''Songs the Jukebox Taught Me'' and ''Songs the Jukebox Taught Me Volume 2'' which were released on the Heart of Texas Records' label. With these albums, Dallas pays tribute to his classic [[country music]] heroes and teachers in a fresh collection of standards featuring some well-known special guests, including Willie Nelson and Jeannie Seely.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hillbillyhits.com/shop/Dallas-Wayne-c36034825|title=Dallas Wayne &#124; Heart of Texas Records Online Shopping &#124; Heart of Texas Country Music}}</ref>

In addition, Jeannie Seely and Willie Nelson recorded a song that Dallas wrote called "Not a Dry Eye in the House", which was released as a radio single on Curb Records in 2021.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.curb.com/2021/08/jeannie-seely-announces-release-of-not-a-dry-eye-in-the-house-to-radio/|title = Jeannie Seely Announces Release of "Not a Dry Eye in the House" to Radio}}</ref>

Dallas' most recent album is "Coldwater, Tennessee" - an all-original project released in April 2022 by BFD/Audium Nashville and distributed exclusively through The Orchard.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Dallas-Wayne-Releases-New-Single-I-Hit-The-Road-And-The-Road-Hit-Back-20220225 | title=Dallas Wayne Releases New Single 'I Hit the Road (And the Road Hit Back)' }}</ref>

Dallas currently resides in Bristol, TN,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://dianediekman.com/dianes-country-music-newsletter-29-july-2020/|title = Diane Diekman » Blog Archive » Diane's Country Music Newsletter — 29 July 2020}}</ref> where he pursues his radio career, songwriting and touring.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dallaswayne.com/default.htm |title=Dallas Wayne Home |publisher=Dallaswayne.com |date= |accessdate=2021-12-30}}</ref>

==Radio shows==
Dallas Wayne can be heard on Sirius XM Satellite Radio, Monday through Friday from noon to 6 p.m. [[Eastern Time Zone|Eastern]] at Willie's Roadhouse (channel 61), Tuesday through Friday in Outlaw Country (channel 62) from 10 a.m. to noon Eastern, and on Sunday from 10 a.m. to noon Eastern at Willie's Roadhouse.<ref name="siriusxm1"/>

==Awards==
The [[Academy of Western Artists]] presented Dallas with a Will Rogers Award for Classic Country Major Market DJ of the Year in 2006 for his work at KHYI 95.3 FM in [[Dallas]], Texas. He received a second Will Rogers Award in 2009 for DJ of the Year, this time in recognition of his work at Sirius XM Satellite Radio.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://awaawards.org/?page_id=2074|title = Past Winners – Media – Academy of Western Artists}}</ref>

The Ameripolitan Music Awards named Dallas the DJ of the year in 2014. The same year, Dallas received the Ameripolitan Honky Tonk Group of the year award as part of the band Heybale! <ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ameripolitan.com/copy-of-about|title=Ameripolitan Music Awards}}</ref>

Dallas was inducted into the [[Texas Country Music Hall of Fame]]'s Disc Jockey Hall of Fame in 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tcmhof.com/disc-jockey|title = Disc Jockey - Texas Country Music Hall of Fame}}</ref>

In 2018, Dallas was awarded the CMA of Texas Choice Award (formerly CMA of Texas Hall of Fame).

Dallas and Heybale! were named the Academy of Western Artists’ Pure Country Group of the Year in 2020.

Nashville's Reunion of Professional Entertainers (R.O.P.E.) named Dallas the Country Disc Jockey of the Year in 2023.
