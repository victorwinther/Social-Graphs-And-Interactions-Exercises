'''Kimberly Richey''' (born December 1, 1956) is an American singer and songwriter.

==Early life==
Richey was born in [[Zanesville, Ohio]], on December 1, 1956.

==Career==
Richey entered music in the 1990s, and secured her first recording contract with Mercury Nashville at the age of 37.<ref name="NPR KR on MS">{{cite news |last1=Staff writer |title=Kim Richey On Mountain Stage |url=https://www.npr.org/event/music/17760482/kim-richey-on-mountain-stage |access-date=January 19, 2017 |publisher=NPR |date=April 17, 2013}}</ref><ref name="NPR 2007"/> She spent the next few years promoting her albums and touring with [[Wynonna Judd]] and others.

===Compositions===
Her songs have been recorded by [[Trisha Yearwood]] ("[[Believe Me Baby (I Lied)]]"), [[Radney Foster]] ("[[Nobody Wins (Radney Foster song)|Nobody Wins]]"), and [[Brooks & Dunn]] ("[[Every River]]").<ref name="NPR KR on MS"/><ref name="NPR 2007b">{{cite news |last1=Dye |first1=David |title=Kim Richey: Sweetly Alluring, Folk-Friendly Country |url=https://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=12045853 |access-date=February 2, 2017 |publisher=NPR |date=July 17, 2007}}</ref>

===Recordings===
Her May 1995 self-titled debut album was produced by [[Richard Bennett (guitarist)|Richard Bennett]]. It contained the singles "Just My Luck" and "Those Words We Said."<ref name=KR-nodep-1995>{{cite news |title=Kim Richey - Self-Titled |url=http://nodepression.com/album-review/kim-richey-self-titled |access-date=April 3, 2017 |work=No Depression |date=August 31, 1995}}</ref>

Her follow-up album, ''[[Bitter Sweet (Kim Richey album)|Bitter Sweet]]'', was produced by [[Angelo Petraglia|Angelo]] and released in 1997. It contained the single "I Know".<ref name=KR-stdtime-1997>{{cite news |last1=Remz |first1=Jeffery |title=Kim Richey hopes life is more sweet than bitter |url=http://www.countrystandardtime.com/d/article.asp?xid=714 |access-date=April 3, 2017 |work=Country Standard Time |date=May 1, 1997}}</ref>

''Glimmer'' was released in 1999. Produced by [[Hugh Padgham]] ([[XTC]]), the album also features guitarist [[Dominic Miller]] ([[Sting (musician)|Sting]]).<ref name="NPR 2007b" /><ref name=KR-washpost-1999>{{cite news |last1=Harrington |first1=Richard |title=Kim Richey: 'Glimmer' Of Hope |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/archive/lifestyle/1999/07/28/kim-richey-glimmer-of-hope/cf9c9420-930b-4662-bc13-61bc99266017/ |access-date=April 3, 2017 |newspaper=Washington Post |date=July 28, 1999}}</ref>

''Rise'' was released in 2002 and was produced by [[Bill Bottrell]].<ref name="NPR 2002">{{cite news |last1=Naylor |first1=Brian |title=Kim Richey |url=https://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=1151585 |access-date=February 2, 2017 |publisher=NPR |date=October 13, 2002}}</ref><ref name=KR-washpost-2002>{{cite news |last1=Himes |first1=Geoffrey |title=Kim Richey: Rise: Lost Highway |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/archive/lifestyle/2002/11/08/kim-richey-rise-lost-highway/efc51612-820b-495f-8c86-ec3e4698564c/ |access-date=April 3, 2017 |newspaper=The Washington Post |date=November 8, 2002}}</ref>

Her 2007 album ''Chinese Boxes'' was recorded in London and produced by [[Giles Martin]].<ref name="NPR 2007">{{cite news |last1=Staff writer |title=At Home in Nashville with Kim Richey |url=https://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=16499430 |access-date=January 19, 2017 |publisher=NPR |date=November 22, 2007}}</ref><ref name=KR-nodep-2007>{{cite news |title=Kim Richey - Chinese Boxes |url=http://nodepression.com/album-review/kim-richey-chinese-boxes |access-date=April 3, 2017 |work=No Depression |date=May 31, 2007}}</ref>

''Wreck Your Wheels'' was released in 2010. It was produced by [[Neilson Hubbard]] in his studio.<ref name=KR-boot-2010>{{cite news |last1=Betts |first1=Stephen |title=Kim Richey Crafts a Beautiful 'Wreck' |url=http://theboot.com/kim-richey-interview-wreck-your-wheels-album/ |access-date=April 3, 2017 |work=The Boot |date=September 15, 2015}}</ref>

Released in 2013, ''Thorn in My Heart'' was again produced by Neilson Hubbard and features guest vocals from Trisha Yearwood.<ref name=KR-popmatters-2013>{{cite news |last1=Horowitz |first1=Steve |title=Kim Richey: Thorn in My Heart |url=http://www.popmatters.com/review/169976-kim-richey-thorn-in-my-heart/ |access-date=April 3, 2017 |work=PopMatters |date=April 25, 2013}}</ref> A limited edition version of the album was released in 2014 as ''Thorn in My Heart: The Work Tapes'' with only Richey on guitar and vocals.<ref name=KR-yeprock-2014>{{cite web |url=http://www.yeproc.com/kim-richeys-thorn-heart-work-tapes/ |title=Kim Richey's Thorn In My Heart: The Work Tapes |work=Yep Rock Records |access-date=April 4, 2017 |author=uncredited}}</ref>

''Edgeland'' was released March 30, 2018, and produced by Brad Jones. ''Edgeland'' includes three different tracking bands of Nashville’s roots players. Like ''Thorn in My Heart'', ''Edgeland'' was released on Yep Roc Records.<ref name=KR-yeprock-2019>{{cite web |url=https://yeproc.11spot.com/music/artists/kim-richey-62/kim-richey-edgeland-cd-lp-pre-order/ |title=Kim Richey's Edgeland |work=Yep Rock Records |access-date=July 1, 2019 |author=uncredited}}</ref>
