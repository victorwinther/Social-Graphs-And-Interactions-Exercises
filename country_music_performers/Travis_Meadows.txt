'''Travis Meadows''' (born 1965) is an American [[country music]] singer and songwriter. He has released four albums and written a number of songs for other country artists.

==Early life==
Meadows was born in 1965 in Mississippi, growing up in [[Jackson, Mississippi|Jackson]].<ref name="rs">{{cite web | url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/features/travis-meadows-songs-interview-20141008 | title=Nashville's Most Badass Songwriter Travis Meadows Shares Tragic Life Stories | publisher=Rolling Stone Country | date=October 8, 2015 | accessdate=August 20, 2015 | archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150907042531/http://www.rollingstone.com/music/features/travis-meadows-songs-interview-20141008 | url-status=live | archivedate=September 7, 2015}}</ref><ref name="ng"/> He began using drugs at an early age,<ref name="rs"/> and at 14 was diagnosed with bone cancer, which led to the loss of most of his right leg.<ref name="rs"/>

==Career==
At the age of 16, he began playing in local bands, starting his musical career as a drummer before beginning to play the harmonica and guitar by the time he was 21.<ref name="ng"/> During his early 20s, he performed with blues musicians such as [[Sam Myers]], and moved to [[Gatlinburg, Tennessee|Gatlinburg]], where he began writing songs.<ref name="ng"/> In his mid-20s, Meadows became a Christian missionary and later a preacher, traveling around the United States and overseas, as well as writing and performing Christian music.<ref name="ng"/> By his late 30s, however, he had become disenchanted with the church, and moved to Nashville to pursue a songwriting career.<ref name="ng"/> He signed a contract with [[Universal Music Publishing]], releasing his debut album ''My life 101'' I'm 2007, but at the same time his life-long drinking and drug habits led to a multi-year descent into alcoholism and abuse.<ref name="rs"/> After four trips to rehab, he succeeded in regaining sobriety in 2010.<ref name="rs"/>

In 2011, Meadows released a studio album, titled ''Killin' Uncle Buzzy'', written in 2010 while he was recovering from his alcohol addiction during his fourth period in rehab.<ref name="rs"/> According to Meadows, a counselor had suggested keeping a journal, which led to him writing songs instead.<ref name="rs"/> In 2013, after his songwriting contract with Universal expired, he began writing independently before signing with independent record label [[Kobalt Music]] and releasing a third album, ''Old Ghosts & Unfinished Business''.<ref name="ng">{{cite web | url=http://nashvillegab.com/2013/07/exclusive-interview-travis-meadows.html | title=Exclusive: Songwriter Travis Meadows on writing Dierks Bentley's "Riser," his tragic past and finding redemption | publisher=Nashville Gab | date=July 16, 2013 | accessdate=August 20, 2015}}</ref>

In 2016, Meadows was featured in an episode of the television show ''American Elements''.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Travis Meadows : American Elements|url = http://www.carbontv.com/videos/0_6bxjfvyj|publisher = CarbonTV|access-date = February 10, 2016}}</ref>

In 2017, Meadows released his fourth album, ''First Cigarette''. The album was produced by Jeremy Spillman and assisted by Jay Joyce, and is the first album by Meadows to receive label support.<ref>{{cite magazine |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/country/features/why-travis-meadows-is-nashvilles-favorite-underdog-w508798 |title=Why Songwriter Travis Meadows Is Nashville's Favorite Underdog |first=Dan |last=Hyman |date=October 13, 2017|magazine=Rolling Stone }}</ref> It is also his first album to appear on Billboard charts, reaching No. 21 on Heatseekers Albums and No. 48 on Independent Albums. It sold 1,100 copies in its debut week,<ref>{{cite news |url=http://roughstock.com/news/2017/10/42474-top-10-country-albums-sales-chart-october-24-2017 |title=TOP 10 Country Albums Sales Chart: October 24, 2017 |first= Matt |last= Bjorke  |date= October 24, 2017 |work=Roughstock }}</ref> reaching No. 17 on Americana/Folk Album Sales and No. 34 on Country Album Sales.<ref>{{cite magazine |url=http://www.billboard.com/biz/charts/2017-11-04/country-album-sales |title=Country Album Sales|date=November 4, 2017 |magazine=Billboard }}</ref>

In April 2021, [[Earache Records]] reissued ''Killin' Uncle Buzzy'' for the record's 10th Anniversary, including a limited edition clear vinyl. This was the first time the album had been available on vinyl and released by a label.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Travis Meadows - Killin' Uncle Buzzy|url=https://www.discogs.com/Travis-Meadows-Killin-Uncle-Buzzy/release/16943934|access-date=April 27, 2021|website=[[Discogs]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=March 12, 2021|title=10th Anniversary Reissue: Killin' Uncle Buzzy - Pre-order with Earache Records!|url=https://travismeadows.com/blogs/latest-news/posts/10th-anniversary-reissue-killin-uncle-buzzy-pre-order-with-earache-records|access-date=April 27, 2021|website=Travis Meadows}}</ref>
