'''Jerry Leon Wallace''' (December 15, 1928<ref name="LarkinCountry">{{cite book|title=[[Encyclopedia of Popular Music|The Guinness Who's Who of Country Music]]|editor=[[Colin Larkin (writer)|Colin Larkin]]|publisher=[[Guinness Publishing]]|date=1993|edition=First|isbn=0-85112-726-6|page=433}}</ref> – May 5, 2008)<ref name="Obit"/> was an American [[country music|country]] and [[pop music|pop]] singer. Between 1958 and 1964, Wallace charted nine hits on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]], including the No. 8 "[[Primrose Lane]]" that was later used as the theme song for the television series ''[[The Smith Family (TV series)|The Smith Family]]''. He made his debut on the country music charts in 1965, entering it thirty-five times between then and 1980.<ref name="LarkinCountry"/> In that timespan, Wallace charted within the country Top Ten four times. His only number one song was "[[If You Leave Me Tonight I'll Cry]]," a song which gained popularity after it was used in an episode of the 1970s TV series ''[[Night Gallery]]''.<ref name="LarkinCountry"/>

==Biography==
Wallace was born in [[Guilford, Missouri]], United States.<ref name="LarkinCountry"/>

Wallace performed for the eighth [[Cavalcade of Jazz]] concert held at [[Wrigley Field (Los Angeles)|Wrigley Field]] in Los Angeles which was produced by [[Leon Hefflin, Sr.]] on June 1, 1952.  Also featured that day were [[Roy Brown (blues musician)|Roy Brown]] and His Mighty Men, [[Anna Mae Winburn]] and Her Sweethearts, [[Toni Harper]], [[Louis Jordan]], [[Jimmy Witherspoon]] and [[Josephine Baker]].<ref>“Largest Jazz Cavalcade in History To Feature Nation’s Top Entertainers” article, ''[[The California Eagle]]'', May 29, 1952.</ref>

His better-known songs include "How the Time Flies" (1958), "Primrose Lane" (1959, written by [[Wayne Shanklin]] and [[Red Callender|George Callender]]), "Shutters and Boards" (1963, written by American film actor [[Audie Murphy]] and Canadian song producer [[Scott Turner (songwriter)|Scott Turner]]), "[[In the Misty Moonlight]]" (1964),<ref name="LarkinCountry"/> and {{nihongo|"Otoko no Sekai"|男の世界|extra=lit. "The World of the Man", English title: "The Lovers of the World"}} (1970), his biggest selling [[single (music)|single]] that was released in Japan only. "Primrose Lane" was his biggest hit, reaching No. 8 pop and No. 12 R&B in the US, selling over one million copies and awarded a [[music recording sales certification|gold disc]].<ref name="The Book of Golden Discs">{{cite book

}}</ref> After his song "[[If You Leave Me Tonight I'll Cry]]" was featured in the 1972 ''[[Night Gallery]]'' episode "The Tune in Dan's Cafe," the song became a No. 1 hit on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' magazine [[Hot Country Singles & Tracks|Hot Country Singles]] chart that August, spending two weeks at the top of the [[record chart|chart]], crossing to No.&nbsp;38 pop.<ref name="whitburn">{{cite book|last=Whitburn|first=Joel|title=Hot Country Songs 1944 to 2008|publisher=Record Research, Inc|year=2008|page=446|isbn=0-89820-177-2}}</ref> "How The Time Flies" was Wallace's first big hit, reaching No. 11 pop and No. 11 R&B.<ref name="LarkinCountry"/>

In 1972, he gained nomination for the [[Country Music Association]] Award as Male Vocalist of the Year, and his song "To Get To You" gained nomination for Single of the Year.

Wallace died on May 5, 2008, in [[Corona, California]], after suffering [[congestive heart failure]].<ref name="Obit">{{cite web|url=http://www.cmt.com/news/news-in-brief/1587133/singer-jerry-wallace-dies-in-california.jhtml |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080512161834/http://www.cmt.com/news/news-in-brief/1587133/singer-jerry-wallace-dies-in-california.jhtml |url-status=dead |archive-date=May 12, 2008 |title=Singer Jerry Wallace Dies in California |publisher=CMT |access-date=2015-08-18}}</ref> A United States Navy veteran of World War II, Wallace was buried at [[Riverside National Cemetery]] in [[Riverside, California]].
