'''Troy Olsen''' (born July 12, 1973) is an American [[country music]] singer-songwriter signed to [[Capitol Records Nashville|EMI Nashville]], He co-wrote [[Blake Shelton]]'s 2009 hit single "[[I'll Just Hold On]]" and [[Tim McGraw]]'s "Ghost Town Train," a cut from the album ''[[Southern Voice (album)|Southern Voice]]''.He also has cuts by many artists including Kid Rock, Michael English, Allison Moorer, Chris Young, Patty Loveless, and Aaron Watson. He has also written several songs for major motion pictures.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bmi.com/news/entry/547922|title=Troy Olsen, More Than a 'Summer Thing'|date=April 22, 2010|work=[[Broadcast Music Incorporated]]|accessdate=May 11, 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url= http://www.kvoa.com/news/musician-troy-olsen-makes-tucson-proud/|title=Musician Troy Olsen makes Tucson proud|last=Taylor|first=Rebecca|date=April 22, 2010|work=KVOA|accessdate=May 11, 2010}}</ref>

Olsen released his debut single, "Summer Thing," in April 2010, followed by a digital self-titled [[extended play]] on May 11.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blog.cmt.com/2010-05-11/troy-olsen-arrives-early-on-ghost-town-train/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100514013951/http://blog.cmt.com/2010-05-11/troy-olsen-arrives-early-on-ghost-town-train/|url-status=dead|archive-date=May 14, 2010|title=Troy Olsen arrives early on ''Ghost Town Train''|last=Bonaguro|first=Alison|date=May 11, 2010|work=[[Country Music Television|CMT]]|accessdate=May 11, 2010}}</ref> "Summer Thing" debuted at No.&nbsp;57 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs]] charts dated for the week ending May 15, 2010. Olsen wrote the song with [[Ben Hayslip]] and [[Jimmy Yeary]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.charlestoncitypaper.com/charleston/troy-olsen-brings-country-to-the-jammer/Content?oid=2189574|title=Troy Olsen brings country to the 'Jammer|last=Lesemann|first=T.
