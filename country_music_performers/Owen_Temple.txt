'''Owen Temple''' (born September 4, 1976 in [[Kerrville, Texas|Kerrville]], Texas) is an American [[Folk music|folk]] and [[country music]] songwriter and musician based in [[Austin, Texas|Austin]], Texas.

== Career ==
Work with producer and [[pedal steel guitar]] musician [[Lloyd Maines]] led to 1997's ''[[General Store (album)|General Store]]'' and 1999's ''[[Passing Through (Owen Temple album)|Passing Through]]''. The year 2002 saw the release of the [[Phil Madeira]]-produced ''[[Right Here and Now]].''

In 2007 and 2011, Temple was a [[Kerrville Folk Festival]] New Folk Finalist, and he was the winner of the 2007 [[B. W. Stevenson]] Songwriting Competition.

''[[Two Thousand Miles]]'', a fourth studio project, was produced by Maines and was released on January 22, 2008 on El Paisano Records. A review in [[Allmusic]] noted "great lyrics full of insight and plainspoken poetry."<ref>[{{AllMusic|class=album|id=r1268729|pure_url=yes}} ''Two Thousand Miles'' Allmusic, Poet, J.]</ref>

A fifth studio album, ''[[Dollars and Dimes]]'', was released June 9, 2009.  It is a concept album focused on different regions of North America during hard times.<ref>[http://www.suntimes.com/lifestyles/hoekstra/1652006,TRA-News-Detours05.article] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090711140502/http://www.suntimes.com/lifestyles/hoekstra/1652006,TRA-News-Detours05.article |date=2009-07-11 }} Hoekstra, Dave (July 5, 2009). "Playing off North America's regions, travel album delivers concept richly". ''[[Chicago Sun-Times]]''.</ref> In July 2009, the album was the No.&nbsp;1 record on the Euro Americana Chart.<ref>[http://www.euroamericanachart.nl/0709.html] for July 2009. Retrieved July 5, 2009.</ref>  In August 2009, the album was No.&nbsp;5 on the Freeform American Roots chart.<ref>[http://www.tcmnradio.com/far/far120.htm] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131126065540/http://www.tcmnradio.com/far/far120.htm |date=2013-11-26 }} for August 2009.  Retrieved August 10, 2009.</ref>

A sixth studio album, ''[[Mountain Home (album)|Mountain Home]]'', was released April 26, 2011, and the songs on the album focus on small Texas towns and the eccentrics inhabiting them.<ref>[http://www.austinchronicle.com/music/2011-05-13/owen-temple-mountain-home/] Caliguiri, Jim (May 13, 2011). "Texas Platters: Owen Temple - Mountain Home" ''[[Austin Chronicle]]''.</ref>

''[[Stories They Tell]]'', a seventh studio album, was released on September 24, 2013.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://jennifinlaypromotions.com/owentemple.htm |title=Owen Temple - Stories They Tell |access-date=2013-09-03 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131214193357/http://jennifinlaypromotions.com/owentemple.htm |archive-date=2013-12-14 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

An eighth studio album, ''[[Rings on a Tree]]'', was released September 29, 2023, and its songs, many co-written with Walt Wilkins, [[Hal Ketchum]], and others, comprise a concept album focused on intergenerational interactions. <ref>[http://www.rootshighway.it/outsiders/2023/temple.htm] Ricaldone, Remo (December 19, 2023). "Reviews: Owen Temple - Rings on a Tree" ''RootsHighway''.</ref><ref>[https://www.americanaboogie.com/americana-music-releases-for-sept-29th/] Frater, Bill (September 28, 2023). "Americana Music Releases for Sept. 29th" ''Americana Boogie''.</ref>

A book on songwriting, co-authored with Gordy Quist, titled ''Songfarmer: Writing More and Better Songs'' was published on November 1, 2015.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://songfarmer.com/post/140510862321/songfarmer-writing-more-and-better-songs|title=Songfarmer: Writing More and Better Songs published by El Paisano Press |access-date=December 28, 2022}}</ref>

Dreamer: A Tribute to Kent Finlay, released in early 2016 on Austin-based Eight 30 Records, features Temple's version of Finlay's "Mines of Terlingua."<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/dreamer-a-tribute-to-kent-finlay-mw0002916063 |title=Various Artists: Dreamer: A Tribute to Kent Finlay |work=AllMusic }}</ref>
