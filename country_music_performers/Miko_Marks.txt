'''Miko Marks''' is an American Americana singer based in Oakland, California. She was born in [[Flint, Michigan]]. She attended from 1990 to 1994 [[Grambling State University]] in Louisiana where she got her degree in Political Science.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.mikomarks.com/about | title=About }}</ref>  She later moved to San Francisco. Marks was named ''Best New Country Artist'' at the 2006 [[New Music Weekly]] Awards.<ref name="Henderson">{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=jtMDAAAAMBAJ&q=%22Miko+Marks%22&pg=PA88|title=The Storyteller Miko Marks|last=Henderson|first=Shirley|date=June 2008|work=[[Ebony (magazine)|Ebony]]|page=88|access-date=June 5, 2010}}</ref>

==Career==
Marks released her debut single, "Freeway Bound," in 2005. It was followed by her debut album, also titled ''Freeway Bound'', which was released on September 13, 2005. Three further singles ("Kickin' Back," "Mama," and "Don't Come Cryin' to Me") were released from the album in 2006. "Mama" featured Marks' first music video, which included a guest appearance by [[Erykah Badu]] assuming the title role.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mikomarks.com/about.html |title=Official Miko Marks Website - 2007 Country Album of the Year |access-date=April 7, 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100613005353/http://www.mikomarks.com/about.html |archive-date=June 13, 2010 }}</ref> Her second studio album, ''It Feels Good'', followed in 2007 after the release of lead-off single "Locked and Loaded." Both were produced by [[Ron Cornelius]] and released under the Mirrome Records imprint.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mirrome.com/ |title=Home |website=mirrome.com}}</ref>

In his review of ''It Feels Good'', Stewart Mason of [[allmusic]] awarded the album 3 and 1/2 stars, drawing comparisons to the [[Dixie Chicks]] and [[Gretchen Wilson]] while commenting that the album "clicks on every level, making it a solidly enjoyable neo-traditionalist country listen."<ref>{{AllMusic|class=album|id=r1112542|pure_url=yes}}</ref>

In 2021, Marks released “Our Country'',''” her first new album in over a decade.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Rodman|first=Sarah|date=2021-02-17|title=Black, Female and Carving Out Their Own Path in Country Music|language=en-US|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2021/02/17/arts/music/black-women-country-music.html|access-date=2021-02-19|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> The album's lead single and music video are "We Are Here".<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.cmt.com/news/1832519/premiere-miko-marks-video-we-are-here/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210409181322/http://www.cmt.com/news/1832519/premiere-miko-marks-video-we-are-here/|url-status=dead|archive-date=April 9, 2021|title = PREMIERE: Miko Marks Highlights the Struggles of Her Hometown in "We Are Here" Video}}</ref>

In October 2022, Marks performed on the [[Grand Ole Opry]] for the first time. According to [[CMT (American TV channel)|CMT]], her performance received two standing ovations.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.cmt.com/news/5zcnk5/dream-realized-miko-marks-receives-two-standing-ovations-at-grand-ole-opry-debut | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221019072054/https://www.cmt.com/news/5zcnk5/dream-realized-miko-marks-receives-two-standing-ovations-at-grand-ole-opry-debut | url-status=dead | archive-date=October 19, 2022 | title=Dream Realized: Miko Marks Receives Two Standing Ovations at Grand Ole Opry Debut }}</ref>

In October of 2023, Marks collaborated with the Fisk Jubilee Singers for an updated version of her song "Jubilee" <ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.tennessean.com/story/entertainment/music/2023/10/13/miko-marks-releases-jubilee-a-rootsy-collaboration-with-the-fisk-jubilee-singers/71166044007/ | title=Miko Marks releases 'Jubilee,' a rootsy collaboration with the Fisk Jubilee Singers | website=[[The Tennessean]] }}</ref>

== Family ==
She married young and had a son, Justin. Her husband works as local firefighter.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.mikomarks.com/about | title=About }}</ref>
