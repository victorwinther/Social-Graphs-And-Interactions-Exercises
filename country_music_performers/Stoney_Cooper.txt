'''Dale Troy Cooper''' (October 16, 1918 – March 22, 1977),<ref name="LarkinCountry">{{cite book|title=[[Encyclopedia of Popular Music|The Guinness Who's Who of Country Music]]|editor=[[Colin Larkin (writer)|Colin Larkin]]|publisher=[[Guinness Publishing]]|date=1993|edition=First|isbn=0-85112-726-6|pages=94/5}}</ref> known professionally as '''Stoney Cooper''', was an American [[country music|country]] star and member of the [[Grand Ole Opry]]. He played the [[fiddle]] and the guitar.

==Biography==
Cooper was the son of Kenny and Stella (Raines) Cooper of [[Harman, West Virginia]], United States.<ref name="LarkinCountry"/>  His family was among the first settlers of Randolph and Pendleton counties, and these roots in the Appalachian mountains had an impact upon his music.

While in high school, Cooper was a member of the Leary Family Singers.<ref name="LarkinCountry"/> In 1939, he married [[Wilma Lee]] Leary.<ref name="LarkinCountry"/> They became Wilma Lee and Stoney Cooper, one of the biggest country music acts of the 1940s through the 1960s.  They had one daughter, Carol Lee Cooper.<ref name="LarkinCountry"/>

In 1947, they were cast members of ''[[WWVA Jamboree]]'' in [[Wheeling, West Virginia]] until 1957.<ref name="LarkinCountry"/> Their band was called The Clinch Mountain Clan.  In 1948, Cooper signed a recording contract with Columbia with the help of [[Fred Rose (musician)|Fred Rose]] that lasted to 1955. In 1954, Cooper joined the [[Grand Ole Opry]].<ref name="LarkinCountry"/>

He also recorded for [[Rich-R-Tone Records|Rich-R-Tone]], [[Hickory Records]] and [[Decca Records|Decca]].

==Death==
In 1973, Stoney's health began to fail; he suffered a series of heart attacks and spent long periods in the hospital.<ref name="LarkinCountry"/> Finally, he suffered a heart attack on February 4, 1977, from which he died in the intensive care unit of a Nashville hospital on March 22, 1977.<ref name="LarkinCountry"/>
