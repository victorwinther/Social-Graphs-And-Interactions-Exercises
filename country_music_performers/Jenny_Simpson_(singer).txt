'''Jenny Simpson''' (born in 1973 in [[Nashville, Tennessee]]<ref name="whitburn">{{cite book|last=Whitburn|first=Joel|title=Hot Country Songs 1944 to 2008|publisher=Record Research, Inc|page=382|year=2008|isbn=978-0-89820-177-2}}</ref>) is an American [[country music]] singer. She was signed to [[Universal Music Group Nashville|Mercury Nashville]] and released her self-titled debut album in 1998.<ref>{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p350451/biography|pure_url=yes}}|title=Jenny Simpson Biography|work=[[Allmusic]]|accessdate=May 14, 2012}}</ref>

Simpson's only single, "Ticket Out of Kansas", peaked at number 54 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs|Hot Country Singles & Tracks]] chart.<ref name="whitburn" /> It received a favorable review from Deborah Evans Price of ''Billboard'', who said that "Simpson's nuanced vocal performance makes listeners feel as if they are boarding the bus with her."<ref>{{cite news|last=Price|first=Deborah Evans|title=Reviews & Previews|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=FQoEAAAAMBAJ&q=%22jenny+simpson%22+%22ticket+out+of+kansas%22&pg=PA24|accessdate=May 14, 2012|newspaper=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=October 10, 1998}}</ref>
