'''Rick Monroe''' is an American country singer based in [[Nashville]], Tennessee. 

==Life and career==

Rick Monroe was born in [[Clearwater, Florida]]. He grew up in [[California]], [[Connecticut]], [[Kansas]], [[North Carolina]], and [[England]].<ref name="wbtw">{{cite web|url=http://wbtw.com/2017/05/18/country-artist-rick-monroe-visits-grand-strand/|title=Country artist Rick Monroe visits Grand Strand|publisher=WBTW|date=May 18, 2017}}</ref> He started playing the drums at age nine but then switched to singing.<ref name="nash" />

Monroe started working the [[coffeehouse]] circuits, but then formed his own band and developed a touring schedule of over 100 dates a year.<ref name="music" /> In 1996, Monroe established his own record label, Divorce Records, under which he would later release all his four albums.<ref name="music">{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/rick-monroe-mn0000364911/biography|title=Rick Monroe: Biography|publisher=AllMusic}}</ref> In 1999, when he was based in [[Los Angeles]], Monroe recorded his album ''Shame'' at CAM studios in [[Bulverde, Texas]].<ref>{{cite magazine|title=Texas: San Antonio|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=nw4EAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA40|date=March 20, 1999|magazine=Billboard|page=40}}</ref>

Calling him a "musician's musician", ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' commented that his "warm, supple" voice brought "an edgy energy" to the song.<ref>{{cite magazine|title=Singles|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=6xQEAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA53|date=June 4, 2005|magazine=Billboard|page=53}}</ref> In 2007, Monroe moved to [[Nashville]].<ref name="nash" /> His "Crazy Not To" single, released in the summer of 2012, reached top-40 radio and chart status.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.media-match.com/uk/music/forum/general/rick-monroe-to-support-emcee-the-2013-jaegermeister-country-tour-with-headliner-aaron-lewis-605463|title=Rick Monroe To Support & Emcee The 2013 Jägermeister Country Tour With Headliner Aaron Lewis|publisher=Music Match|date=April 23, 2013}}</ref>

''It’s A Love Thing,'' his EP from 2014, included the song "Fires Out", which debuted at #52 of [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'s Country Indicator,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/files/pdfs/country_update_0316.pdf|title=Country Update|publisher=Billboard|date=March 16, 2015}}</ref> and was a Top 40 Music Row hit,<ref name="tour">{{cite web|url=https://www.axs.com/news/rick-monroe-i-m-just-trying-to-have-a-good-time-89176|title=Rick Monroe: 'I'm just trying to have a good time'|publisher=AXS|date=August 31, 2015}}</ref> and "Great Minds Drink Alike," which debuted at #58 and has remained his most popular song on [[YouTube]] with a video featuring fan footage.<ref name="nash">{{cite web|url=http://sportsandentertainmentnashville.com/se-is-listening-to-rick-monroe/|title=S&E is listening to Rick Monroe|publisher=Sports & Entertainment Nashville|date=September 10, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/files/pdfs/country_update_0622.pdf|title=Country Update|publisher=Billboard|date=June 22, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BzQ25sQJDc8|title=Rick Monroe - "Great Minds Drink Alike"|publisher=YouTube|date=September 14, 2015}}</ref>

In 2017, Monroe toured the country promoting his new work.<ref name="wbtw" /> He released his six-track EP ''Gypsy Soul'' on May 12, 2017.<ref name="wect">{{cite web|url=http://www.wect.com/story/35155450/rick-monroe|title=First at Four: Country music artist Rick Monroe embraces his 'Gypsy Soul'|publisher=WECT|date=April 15, 2017}}</ref> The first single was "This Side of You", which made the [[Music Row]] Country Breakout Chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.musicrow.com/wp-content/CBOcharts/CBOChartMASTER.pdf|title=Country Breakout Chart|publisher=Music Row|date=June 8, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://tasteofcountry.com/rick-monroe-this-side-of-you/|title=Hear Rick Monroe's Sultry 'This Side of You' [Exclusive Premiere]|publisher=Taste of Country|date=March 6, 2017}}</ref> The song went to the charts during the second week of its release. Monroe was one of the few independent artists given a spot on a sanctioned [[Country Music Association|CMA]] stage during the 2017 [[CMA Music Festival]].<ref name="life" />

Monroe has performed in 17 countries, and every U.S. state except for Oregon.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mrgrecordings.com/MRG/Rick_Monroe.html|title=About Rick Monroe|publisher=MRG Recordings}}</ref> He played with artists such as [[Lee Brice]], [[Aaron Lewis (musician)|Aaron Lewis]], [[Eric Church]], [[Charlie Daniels]], [[Dwight Yoakam]], [[Montgomery Gentry]], [[Patty Loveless]], [[Trick Pony]], [[Emerson Drive]], [[Pat Green]], [[Randy Houser]], and [[Josh Thompson (singer)|Josh Thompson]]. In 2016 alone, he traveled over 100,000 miles and played over 120 shows.<ref name="life">{{cite web|url=https://lifeisloaded.com/blogs/news/loaded-with-talent-nashville-based-country-music-artist-rick-monroe|title=Loaded with talent: Nashville-based country music artist, Rick Monroe is taking off as one of the country's most successful independent artists|publisher=Life Is Loaded|date=May 22, 2017}}</ref> He was the Brand Ambassador at seven consecutive [[Jägermeister]] Country Tours.<ref name="tour" />

Monroe writes most of his own songs.<ref name="nash" /> He has been described as playing "fast and loose [[country music]] about cars and beers, tinged with a big [[rock and roll|rock 'n' roll]] sound"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.press-citizen.com/story/entertainment/go-iowa-city/2017/01/11/shawshank-redemption-and-more-iowa-city-entertainment-best-bets-jan-12-18/96193988/|title='Shawshank Redemption' and more Iowa City entertainment best bets: Jan. 12-18|publisher=Iowa City Press Citizen|date=January 11, 2017}}</ref> and singing with a "memorable [[blues]]-infused vocal."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gratefulweb.com/articles/rick-monroes-gypsy-soul-streets-may-12|title=Rick Monroe's 'Gypsy Soul' Streets May 12|publisher=Grateful Web}}</ref>

==Private life==

He is active in humanitarian causes such as Safe Haven Family Shelter, the Frank Foundation, [[St. Jude Children's Research Hospital]], and the [[Children's Miracle Network]].<ref name="nash" />

==Discography==

Rick Monroe released five studio albums, one live album, two EPs, and several singles. He has also composed for other singers.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/rick-monroe-mn0000364911/discography|title=Rick Monroe: Discography|publisher=AllMusic}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/artist/rick-monroe/id4221738|title=Rick Monroe|publisher=iTunes}}</ref>
