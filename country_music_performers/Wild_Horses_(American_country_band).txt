'''Wild Horses''' is an American [[country music]] band composed of Lon Holland ([[accordion]], [[percussion instrument|percussion]]), Steve Kellough ([[bass guitar]]), Michael Blake Mahler ([[lead guitar]], vocals), Ralph McCauley ([[drum kit|drums]]), Angela Rae ([[lead vocals]]), and Chris Sigmon (steel, fiddle, & [[Dobro]]).<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.wildhorsesband.com/aboutus.htm |title=Wild Horses: About Us |accessdate=2008-03-28 |work=WildHorses.com |archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20080706111228/http://www.wildhorsesband.com/aboutus.htm <!-- Bot retrieved archive --> |archivedate = 2008-07-06}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.rlc.cc.il.us/pressroom/newsstory.php?id=507 |title=Country and blues take center stage at BBQ showdown |accessdate=2008-05-06 |date=2005-06-03 |work=Rend Lake College}}</ref> Mahler and Rae are husband and wife, and guitarist Joe Lee Koenig was formerly a member.<ref name="whitburn">{{cite book|last=Whitburn|first=Joel|title=Hot Country Songs 1944 to 2008|publisher=Record Research, Inc.|year=2008|pages=462|isbn=978-0-89820-177-2}}</ref>

Founded in 1989 in the state of [[Texas]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.primesourceentertainment.com/bands/featured/wild_horses.html |title=Featured band: Wild Horses |accessdate=2008-03-03 |work=Prime Source Entertainment |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20061123172826/http://www.primesourceentertainment.com/bands/featured/wild_horses.html |archivedate=November 23, 2006 }}</ref> the band signed to [[Epic Records]] in 2002, releasing their debut single "I Will Survive" that year. The song went on to peak at No.&nbsp;46 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' Hot Country Singles & Tracks (now [[Hot Country Songs]]) charts,<ref name="whitburn"/> and was included on a compilation album entitled ''Dancin' with Thunder'', which was issued in association with [[Professional Bull Riders]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://countrymusic.about.com/library/bldancinwiththunderrev.htm |title=CD Review: Dancin' With Thunder - Various Artists |accessdate=2008-03-28 |last=Huenke |first= Trisha |work=[[About.com]]}}</ref> An album, ''Frontier Free for All'', was released in 2003 on Persimmon Records.
