'''Kenneth Mobley Brice Jr.''' (born June 10, 1979), known professionally as '''Lee Brice''', is an American [[country music]] singer and songwriter, signed to [[Curb Records]]. Brice has released five albums with the label: ''[[Love Like Crazy]]'', ''[[Hard 2 Love (Lee Brice album)|Hard to Love]]'', ''[[I Don't Dance (album)|I Don't Dance]]'', ''[[Lee Brice (album)|Lee Brice]]''. and ''Hey World''. He has also released eighteen singles, of which eight have reached number one on ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Country Airplay]]: "[[A Woman Like You (Lee Brice song)|A Woman Like You]]", "[[Hard to Love (Lee Brice song)|Hard to Love]]", "[[I Drive Your Truck]]", "[[I Don't Dance (Lee Brice song)|I Don't Dance]]", "[[Rumor (Lee Brice song)|Rumor]]", "[[I Hope You're Happy Now (Carly Pearce and Lee Brice song)|I Hope You're Happy Now]]", "[[One of Them Girls]]" and "[[Memory I Don't Mess With]]". He has also charted within the top 10 with "[[Love Like Crazy (song)|Love Like Crazy]]", "[[Parking Lot Party]]", "[[Drinking Class]]", and "[[That Don't Sound Like You]]". "Love Like Crazy" was the top country song of 2010 according to [[Billboard Year-End|''Billboard'' Year-End]], and broke a 62-year-old record for the longest run on the country chart.

Besides his own material, he has also co-written singles for artists like [[Garth Brooks]], [[Adam Gregory]], the [[Eli Young Band]], and [[Tim McGraw]]. One of his compositions, Brooks's "[[More Than a Memory]]", was the first song ever to debut at number one on the country chart, while the Eli Young Band's "[[Crazy Girl]]" was the ''Billboard'' Year-End top country song of 2011.

==Biography==
Kenneth Mobley Brice, Jr. was born on June 10, 1979, in [[Sumter, South Carolina]], to Kenneth, Sr. and Carleen Brice<ref name="weekly"/> His younger brother, Lewis, was a contestant on Season 1 of [[Country Music Television]]'s ''[[Can You Duet]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cmt.com/shows/series/can_you_duet/season_one_contestants/lewis_brice.jhtml|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080606040615/http://www.cmt.com/shows/series/can_you_duet/season_one_contestants/lewis_brice.jhtml|url-status=dead|archive-date=June 6, 2008|title=CMT : Can You Duet : Lewis Brice|publisher=Cmt.com|access-date=17 October 2014}}</ref> As a child, he played [[piano]] and guitar, sang in church, and wrote his own songs.<ref name="allmusic"/> He entered and won three different talent contests in high school.

He attended [[Clemson University]] on a [[American football|football]] scholarship [https://clemsontigers.com/former-clemson-football-player-lee-brice-wins-song-of-the-year/]. He played special teams there as the long snapper, but after an arm injury, he decided to focus on a country music career.

===Musical career===
[[File:Lee Brice.png|right|250px|thumb|Brice at Billy Bob's Texas for Willie Nelson's 4th of July Picnic in 2011]]
By 2007, Brice began working as a songwriter, with cuts by [[Jason Aldean]], [[Keith Gattis]], and [[Cowboy Crush]] among others.<ref name="allmusic"/> Along with [[Billy Montana]] and [[Kyle Jacobs (songwriter)|Kyle Jacobs]], he co-wrote [[Garth Brooks]]'s 2007 single "[[More Than a Memory]]",<ref name="allmusic"/> the first single in the history of the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs]] chart to debut at number one.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.gactv.com/gac/nw_headlines/article/0,,GAC_26063_5690035,00.html |title=Garth Brooks Back on Top |access-date=5 August 2010 |date=5 September 2007 |work=[[Great American Country]] |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110709193012/http://www.gactv.com/gac/nw_headlines/article/0,,GAC_26063_5690035,00.html |archive-date=9 July 2011 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Also in 2007, he signed to [[Curb Records]], releasing his debut single "She Ain't Right", which peaked at number 29 on the country chart.<ref name="whitburn">{{cite book|last=Whitburn|first=Joel|title=Hot Country Songs 1944 to 2008|publisher=Record Research, Inc.|year=2008|page=59|isbn=978-0-89820-177-2}}</ref> It was followed by "Happy Endings" and "Upper Middle Class White Trash" at numbers 32 and 44.<ref name="whitburn"/> All three songs were to have been included on an album entitled ''Picture of Me'',<ref name="allmusic"/> which was never released. He continued to write songs for others, including Canadian singer [[Adam Gregory]]'s singles "[[Crazy Days (song)|Crazy Days]]" and "[[What It Takes (Adam Gregory song)|What It Takes]]". He appeared on [[Cledus T. Judd]]'s 2007 album ''[[Boogity, Boogity – A Tribute to the Comedic Genius of Ray Stevens]]'', singing duet vocals on a rendition of the [[Albert E. Brumley]] gospel song "Turn Your Radio On".

===2009–2010: ''Love Like Crazy''===
In August 2009, he charted with his fourth single, "[[Love Like Crazy (song)|Love Like Crazy]]",<ref name="boot">{{cite web|url=http://www.theboot.com/2009/09/21/lee-brice-says-its-ok-to-let-his-songs-go/|title=Lee Brice Says It's OK to Let His Songs Go|last=Hackett|first=Vernell|date=21 September 2009|work=The Boot|access-date=1 March 2010}}</ref> a song written by [[Tim James (country music songwriter)|Tim James]] and [[Doug Johnson (record producer)|Doug Johnson]]. It was the first release from his [[Love Like Crazy|debut album of the same name]], on which he co-produced all but one track with Johnson.<ref name="LLC liner">{{cite AV media notes |title=Love Like Crazy |others=Lee Brice |year=2010 |type=CD booklet |publisher=Curb Records |id=78977}}</ref> "Love Like Crazy" reached top 10 on the Hot Country Songs chart in July 2010 during its forty-sixth week on the chart, setting a record for the slowest climb into the top 10.<ref name="trust">{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/957468/chart-beat-thursday-katy-perry-lee-brice-judy-collins|title=Chart Beat Thursday: Katy Perry, Lee Brice, Judy Collins|last=Trust|first=Gary|date=8 July 2010|magazine=Billboard|access-date=9 July 2010}}</ref> In September 2010, the song charted for a fifty-sixth week, making it the longest-charting song in the chart's history; it broke a record set by [[Eddy Arnold]], whose 1948 single "[[Bouquet of Roses (song)|Bouquet of Roses]]" spent fifty-four weeks on the chart.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/956584/lee-brice-ties-country-songs-longevity-record|title=Lee Brice Breaks Country Songs Longevity Record|last=Trust|first=Gary|date=7 September 2010|magazine=Billboard|access-date=7 September 2010}}</ref> "Love Like Crazy" ultimately peaked at number 3. The album's second single was "[[Beautiful Every Time]]" at number 30. Also in 2010, Brice co-wrote labelmate [[Tim McGraw]]'s single "[[Still (Tim McGraw song)|Still]]".<ref name="boot"/>

===2011–2019: ''Hard 2 Love'', ''I Don't Dance'' and ''Lee Brice''===
[[File:Lee Brice (49627463527).jpg|thumb|Brice performs in 2020]]
Brice released his sixth single, "[[A Woman Like You (Lee Brice song)|A Woman Like You]]", in late 2011. It was the first release from a second album for Curb, ''[[Hard 2 Love (Lee Brice album)|Hard 2 Love]]'', which was released on April 24, 2012.<ref>Conaway, Alanna. [http://tasteofcountry.com/lee-brice-hard-2-love-album-release-date/ Lee Brice’s Sophomore Album ‘Hard 2 Love’ in Stores April 24]. TasteofCountry.com. March 6, 2012. Retrieved March 6, 2012.</ref> In April 2012, it became his first number one on the [[Country Airplay]] chart. "[[Hard to Love (Lee Brice song)|Hard to Love]]" became his second number one in late 2012. The album's third single, "[[I Drive Your Truck]]", was released on December 3, 2012, and reached number one on the Country Airplay chart in 2013. The album's fourth single, "[[Parking Lot Party]]", was released to country radio on May 13, 2013, and peaked at number 6 on the Country Airplay chart in November 2013. Brice also co-produced the single "Love Is War" for the duo [[American Young]], a duo whose membership includes "A Woman Like You" co-writer Jon Stone.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/american-young-mw0002703260|title=''American Young''|work=Allmusic|access-date=8 October 2015}}</ref>

In early 2014, Brice released the single "[[I Don't Dance (Lee Brice song)|I Don't Dance]]". It was the lead single to his third studio album [[I Don't Dance (album)|of the same name]], which was released on September 9, 2014. Brice produced the album by himself.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.musicrow.com/2014/06/lee-brice-unveils-new-album-coming-sept-9/|title=Lee Brice Unveils New Album Coming Sept. 9|work=MusicRow - Nashville's Music Industry Publication - News, Songs From Music City|date=10 June 2014 |access-date=17 October 2014}}</ref> "I Don't Dance" became Brice's fourth number one hit in August 2014. "[[Drinking Class]]" is the album's second single. It peaked at number two on the Country Airplay chart in May 2015. The album's third single, "[[That Don't Sound Like You]]" released to country radio on May 11, 2015. During this time, he also toured on a very busy schedule, doing over 100 live shows every year between 2011 and 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://concerty.com/artist/48a6d065-1fda-4280-ae39-df1394d419cd|title=''Lee Brice Biography''|work=Concerty.com}}</ref>

On November 3, 2017, Brice released the self-titled album, Lee Brice. It is his fourth album and it was released via Curb Records. The album's lead single is "Boy" and features 14 other songs.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nashcountrydaily.com/2017/05/19/lee-brice-announces-release-date-for-new-self-titled-album-drops-new-boy-video-watch/|title=Lee Brice Announces Release Date for New Self-Titled Album & Drops New "Boy" Video [Watch]|date=19 May 2017}}</ref>

In October 2019, [[Carly Pearce]] released a duet with Brice titled "I Hope You're Happy Now."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nashcountrydaily.com/2019/09/27/carly-pearce-and-lee-brice-team-up-for-powers-new-single-i-hope-youre-happy-now-listen/|title=Carly Pearce and Lee Brice Team Up for Powerful New Single, "I Hope You're Happy Now" [Listen]|author=Jim Casey|date=September 27, 2019|publisher=Nash Country Daily|access-date=September 27, 2019}}</ref>

===2020–present: ''Hey World''===
In April 2020, Brice released the single, "[[One of Them Girls]]", as the lead-off single to his fifth studio album, ''[[Hey World]]'', due for release November 20, 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://tasteofcountry.com/lee-brice-hey-world-album/|title=Lee Brice Announces 'Hey World' Album, Releases Video for Somber Title Track|last=Trapp|first=Philip|date=September 12, 2020|access-date=October 14, 2020|work=Taste of Country}}</ref> "One of Them Girls" spent three weeks at number one on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Country Airplay]] chart in October 2020, becoming Brice's longest-lasting number one to date. In early 2023, Brice embarked on his headlining "Beer Drinking Opportunity Tour" in Canada, alongside [[Tenille Arts]] and [[Josh Ross]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hopestandard.com/local-news/country-star-lee-brice-bringing-beer-drinking-opportunity-tour-to-abbotsford/|title=Country star Lee Brice bringing Beer Drinking Opportunity Tour to Abbotsford|first=Ben|last=Lypka|date=January 17, 2023|work=Hope Standard|publisher=[[Black Press]]|access-date=January 23, 2023}}</ref>

==Personal life==
In April 2013, Brice married his longtime girlfriend Sara Nanette Reeveley. The couple have three children: sons Takoda Brice (born in 2008) and Ryker Mobley Brice (born in 2013), and daughter Trulee Nanette Brice (born in 2017). Brice's cousin and often music inspiration, Michael Cericola, serves as the children's godfather.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cmt.com/news/1722546/lee-brice-raves-about-his-superhuman-wife/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402111610/http://www.cmt.com/news/1722546/lee-brice-raves-about-his-superhuman-wife/|url-status=dead|archive-date=April 2, 2015|title=Lee Brice Raves About His Superhuman Wife|last=Bonaguro|first=Alison|date=19 February 2014|work=[[Country Music Television]]|access-date=16 March 2015}}</ref> Reeveley is featured on the track "See About a Girl" on ''Hard 2 Love''.<ref name="H2L liner">{{cite AV media notes |title=Hard 2 Love |others=Lee Brice |year=2012 |type=CD booklet |publisher=Curb Records |id=79316}}</ref>

On November 8, 2020, Brice announced that he tested positive for [[COVID-19]] during the [[COVID-19 pandemic in the United States|ongoing pandemic]], but was [[asymptomatic]]. As a result, he was unable to perform with [[Carly Pearce]] on their single, "[[I Hope You're Happy Now (Carly Pearce and Lee Brice song)|I Hope You're Happy Now]]", at the [[54th Annual Country Music Association Awards|54th CMA Awards]],<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/country/9480471/lee-brice-coronavirus-covid-19-positive-cma-awards|title=Lee Brice Tests Positive for COVID-19 Ahead of CMA Awards|last=Iasimone|first=Ashley|date=November 8, 2020|access-date=November 9, 2020|magazine=Billboard}}</ref> and [[Charles Kelley]] of [[Lady A]] performed with Pearce instead.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/awards/9482659/carly-pearce-charles-kelley-2020-cma-awards|title=Carly Pearce & Charles Kelley Sing 'I Hope You're Happy Now' at 2020 CMA Awards|last=Lynch|first=Joe|date=November 11, 2020|access-date=November 13, 2020|magazine=Billboard}}</ref>
