'''The Cadillac Three''', originally known as '''The Cadillac Black''', is an American [[rock music|rock]] band consisting of [[Jaren Johnston]] (lead vocals, guitar), Kelby Ray (lap steel guitar, bass guitar, vocals), and Neil Mason (drums, vocals). All three members were originally in the band [[American Bang]]. The group has released three albums through [[Big Machine Records]], and has charted three singles on the [[Hot Country Songs]] and [[Country Airplay]] charts. In addition to their own work, Johnston has written singles for [[Keith Urban]], [[Tim McGraw]], [[Steven Tyler]], [[Dallas Smith (singer)|Dallas Smith]], and [[Jake Owen]].

==History==
Before its foundation, all three members were in the group [[American Bang]], which also featured Ben Brown as a fourth member; Mason and Brown had been in the band [[Llama (band)|Llama]] before that.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://revelrymusicgroup.com/clients/neil-mason-the-cadillac-three|title=Neil Mason|work=Revelry Music Group|access-date=10 December 2013}}</ref> Between 2011 and 2013, Johnston co-wrote "[[You Gonna Fly]]" for [[Keith Urban]], "[[Southern Girl]]" for [[Tim McGraw]], and "[[Days of Gold (song)|Days of Gold]]" for [[Jake Owen]], the last of which was also co-written by Mason.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.roughstock.com/blog/the-cadillac-three-ready-the-release-of-epic-new-single-the-south-big-machine-records-label-debut|title=The Cadillac Three Ready the Release of "Epic" New Single "The South," Big Machine Records Label Debut|last=Conaway|first=Alanna|date=10 October 2013|work=Roughstock|access-date=10 December 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131213232709/http://www.roughstock.com/blog/the-cadillac-three-ready-the-release-of-epic-new-single-the-south-big-machine-records-label-debut|archive-date=2013-12-13|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bigmachinelabelgroup.com/artist/The_Cadillac_Three|title=The Cadillac Three|work=Big Machine Records|access-date=10 December 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131202145010/http://www.bigmachinelabelgroup.com/artist/The_Cadillac_Three|archive-date=2013-12-02|url-status=dead}}</ref>

The group released their debut self-titled album in 2012 (then titled ''The Cadillac Black'').  In February 2013, the group signed to [[Big Machine Records]] and reissued their album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.musicrow.com/2013/02/big-machine-label-group-signs-the-cadillac-three/|title=Big Machine Label Group Signs The Cadillac Three|last=Nicholson|first=Jessica|date=19 February 2013|work=[[MusicRow]]|access-date=10 December 2013}}</ref> The album includes their own rendition of "Days of Gold".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/the-cadillac-three-mw0002544467|title=The Cadillac Three|work=Allmusic|access-date=12 April 2014}}</ref>

In late 2013, the trio released its debut single, "The South." It features guest vocals from [[Florida Georgia Line]], [[Dierks Bentley]], and [[Eli Young Band]] lead singer Mike Eli. Billy Dukes of [[Taste of Country]] praised the song for its [[Southern rock]] influences.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tasteofcountry.com/the-cadillac-three-the-south/|title=The Cadillac Three (Feat. Florida Georgia Line, Dierks Bentley, Mike Eli), 'The South' – ToC Critic's Pick [Listen]|last=Dukes|first=Billy|date=13 November 2013|work=Taste of Country|access-date=10 December 2013}}</ref> The song debuted at number 58 on the [[Country Airplay]] charts dated for the week ending December 21, 2013.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www1.billboard.biz/bbbiz/photos/pdf/2013/country_update_1209.pdf|title=Billboard Country Update|date=December 9, 2013|magazine=Billboard|access-date=10 December 2013|archive-date=12 December 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131212185511/http://www1.billboard.biz/bbbiz/photos/pdf/2013/country_update_1209.pdf|url-status=dead}}</ref> On January 15, 2014, the group played their song on an episode of [[Nashville (2012 TV series)|Nashville]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blogs.tennessean.com/tunein/2014/01/15/abcs-nashville-returns-with-special-guests-kelly-clarkson-the-cadillac-three/|title=ABC's 'Nashville' returns with special guests Kelly Clarkson, The Cadillac Three|date=January 14, 2014|work=The Tennessean|access-date=23 January 2014}}</ref>

In 2015 the band played a show in America on Friday, flew from America to the UK to play Download Festival on Saturday and ended up hitting the stage using fellow American band Black Stone Cherry's equipment as TC3's had not made it to the UK with them. Within hours of their set at Download they were heading back to America for a different festival appearance the following day. On April 23, 2015, the band played on Mane Stage in Long Beach, California.<ref>[http://search.proquest.com/docview/1675908130]{{dead link|date=August 2018}}</ref>

The band's second album, ''Bury Me in My Boots'', was released on August 5, 2016. It debuted at No. 5 on the ''Billboard'' Top Country Albums chart, selling 11,000 copies in the first week.<ref>{{cite magazine |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/7476046/country-music-charts-jon-pardi-boots-blake-shelton|title=Jon Pardi's 'Boots' Kicks Up First Country Airplay No. 1|first=Jim |last=Asker |date=August 16, 2016 |magazine=Billboard}}</ref>

The band went on their first headlining US tour with Monster Energy Outbreak Presents from January 19 to March 17.

They released their third album, ''Legacy'', on August 25, 2017.<ref name="RS">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/country/news/the-cadillac-threes-new-album-legacy-see-the-tracklist-w486857|title=The Cadillac Three's New Album 'Legacy': See the Tracklist|magazine=Rolling Stone|date=June 9, 2017|access-date=August 25, 2017}}</ref>

They released their fourth album ''Country Fuzz'' on February 7, 2020.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://sixshootercountry.com/2020/02/10/the-cadillac-three-country-fuzz-album-review/ | title=The Cadillac Three "Country Fuzz" – Album Review | date=10 February 2020 }}</ref>

On October 23, 2020, the band released their fifth album ''Tabasco & Sweet Tea'' via Big Machine Records.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-10-23|title=The Cadillac Three Expand Their Palate With 'Tabasco & Sweet Tea' [Interview]|url=https://musicrow.com/2020/10/the-cadillac-three-expand-their-palate-with-tabasco-sweet-tea-interview/|access-date=2020-10-26|language=en}}</ref>

On April 26, 2021, it was announced that the band would have their own radio show on [[Planet Rock (radio station)]] on every Saturday night throughout May 2021 called Garage Radio, which is in celebration of their 10th Anniversary as a band.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Colothan|first=Scott|date=April 26, 2021|title=The Cadillac Three to host new show Garage Radio on Planet Rock|url=https://planetradio.co.uk/planet-rock/news/rock-news/the-cadillac-three-garage-radio/|access-date=April 27, 2021|website=[[Planet Rock (radio station)|Planet Rock]]}}</ref>

On October 27th, 2023, they released their sixth album 'The Years Go Fast' on the 'Big Machine' record label.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://shop.bigmachinelabelgroup.com/products/the-cadillac-three-the-years-go-fast-digital-album | title=The Cadillac Three - the Years Go Fast Digital Album }}</ref>
