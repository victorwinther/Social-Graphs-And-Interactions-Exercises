'''Cale Dodds''' is an American country music artist and songwriter who is currently signed to Spirit Music Group. <ref>{{cite web |title=Cale Dodds Signs With Spirit Music Nashville/Fluid Music Revolution |url=https://musicrow.com/2022/06/cale-dodds-signs-with-spirit-music-nashville-fluid-music-revolution/ |website=Music Row}}</ref>

== Musical career ==
Cale Dodds has been writing songs since the age of 12.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.digitaljournal.com/entertainment/music/cale-dodds-shares-details-about-new-single-take-you-back/article/518386|title=Cale Dodds shares details about new single 'Take You Back' (Includes interview)|date=2018-03-26|access-date=2018-06-13}}</ref> At the age of 22, after attending [[Columbus State University]], Dodds made the move from [[Columbus, Georgia|Columbus, GA]] to [[Nashville, Tennessee|Nashville, TN]] to pursue his career in music.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.thegeorgeanne.com/arts_and_entertainment/article_6ccd32cc-3d2b-11e4-84f7-4b02fd7810b8.html|title=Chasing The Dream with Cale Dodds|last=Lewis|first=Alia|work=The George-Anne|access-date=2018-06-13|language=en}}</ref> In 2014, Cale released his first EP titled “Wild and Wreckless”<ref>{{Citation|title=Wild and Reckless - EP by Cale Dodds on iTunes|date=2014-05-07|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/wild-and-reckless-ep/873177736|language=en-US|accessdate=2018-06-13}}</ref>—a five-song project recorded at [[Ocean Way Recording|Ocean Way Studio]] with Kyle Jacobs and Matt McClure.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://theboot.com/cale-dodds-free-download-wild-and-reckless-ep/|title=Cale Dodds Offering Free Download of 'Wild and Reckless' EP|website=The Boot|language=en|access-date=2018-06-13}}</ref> In 2015, Dodds received a cut from [[A Thousand Horses]] for his song [[(This Ain't No) Drunk Dial|“Drunk Dial,”]] which reached number 23 on Billboards's [[Country Airplay]] Chart; and in 2016, [[Warner/Chappell Music]] signed Dodds to a worldwide publishing agreement.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://musicrow.com/2016/03/warnerchappell-music-signs-cale-dodds/|title=Warner/Chappell Music Signs Cale Dodds|date=2016-03-14|work=MusicRow - Nashville's Music Industry Publication - News, Songs From Music City|access-date=2018-06-13|language=en-US}}</ref> In March 2016, Dodds independently released his second project, "People Watching - EP", produced by Corey Crowder and Zach Abend. Since the release, his songs "People Watching" and "Lying" have been streamed over 5 million times, and “Acting Our Age,” was selected for the EA Sports [[Madden NFL 17|Madden ‘17]] soundtrack which also featured artists such as [[Blake Shelton]] and [[Brantley Gilbert]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.30asongwritersfestival.com/artist/cale-dodds|title=Cale Dodds  {{!}} 30asongwritersfestival.com|website=www.30asongwritersfestival.com|language=en|access-date=2018-06-13|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170116053425/http://www.30asongwritersfestival.com/artist/cale-dodds|archive-date=2017-01-16|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Cale has also been tour support for artists like [[Sam Hunt]], [[Brett Eldredge]], and [[Billy Currington]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://tasteofcountry.com/cale-dodds-music-teaser/|title=Cale Dodds Releases New EP Teaser Trailer|website=Taste of Country|language=en|access-date=2018-06-13}}</ref>

In January 2017, Cale Dodds joined [[Warner Music Group|Warner Music Nashville]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://musicrow.com/2017/01/warner-music-nashville-signs-cale-dodds/|title=Warner Music Nashville Signs Cale Dodds|date=2017-01-30|work=MusicRow - Nashville's Music Industry Publication - News, Songs From Music City|access-date=2018-06-13|language=en-US}}</ref> Later that year, in September, Dodds released his first major label "double"—a two-sided single—featuring "All Over" and "Lying."<ref>{{Citation|title=All Over - Single by Cale Dodds|date=2017-09-29|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/all-over-single/1288122190|language=en-US|accessdate=2018-06-13}}</ref> The second double followed in November with "Out Of My System" and "People Watching,"<ref>{{Citation|title=Outta My System by Cale Dodds|date=2017-11-17|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/outta-my-system/1307288187?i=1307288188|language=en-US|accessdate=2018-06-13}}</ref> and the third in January 2018 with Cale's radio single "Take You Back" and "Like We Do."<ref>{{Citation|title=Take You Back by Cale Dodds|date=2018-01-12|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/take-you-back/1326408083?i=1326408510|language=en-US|accessdate=2018-06-13}}</ref>

"Take You Back" arrived to country music radio in March,<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.soundslikenashville.com/news/cale-dodds-take-back-radio/|title=Cale Dodds Will Live His Dream Of Sending a Song to Radio|date=2018-01-19|work=Sounds Like Nashville|access-date=2018-06-13|language=en-US}}</ref> but prior to hitting the airwaves, [[Rolling Stone]] had already named Cale Dodds one of their November 2017 "New Country Artists You Need To Know."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/country/lists/10-new-country-artists-you-need-to-know-november-2017-w511601/cale-dodds-w511605|title=10 New Country Artists You Need to Know: November 2017|website=Rolling Stone|access-date=2018-06-13}}</ref> [[CMT (U.S. TV channel)|CMT]] Listen Up revealed him as one of their "18 for 2018" class members—a year-long campaign that spotlights "country music's most promising newcomers."<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.cmt.com/news/1789438/cmt-listen-up-reveals-18-for-2018/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171214232519/http://www.cmt.com/news/1789438/cmt-listen-up-reveals-18-for-2018/|url-status=dead|archive-date=December 14, 2017|title=CMT Listen Up Reveals "18 for 2018"|work=CMT News|access-date=2018-06-13}}</ref> Billboard and Rolling Stone both named Dodd's 2018 [[CMA Music Festival|CMA Fest]] performance as one of the best things they saw from the four-day music festival.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/country/8460165/cma-fest-2018-day-2-recap-carrie-underwood-blake-shelton|title=The 17 Best Moments From CMA Fest 2018 Day 2|work=Billboard|access-date=2018-06-13}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/country/lists/cma-music-festival-2018-30-best-things-we-saw-w521321/best-spider-man-cale-dodds-w521349|title=CMA Music Festival 2018: 30 Best Things We Saw|website=Rolling Stone|access-date=2018-06-13}}</ref>

In early October 2018, Cale Dodds shared new music with a studio and acoustic version of his song, "What We Gonna Do About It;"<ref>{{Citation|title=What We Gonna Do About It by Cale Dodds|date=2018-10-05|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/what-we-gonna-do-about-it/1437459328?i=1437459498|language=en-US|access-date=2018-10-26}}</ref> and followed with another two versions of "Where I Get It From" which was released in November of that same year.<ref>{{Citation|title=Where I Get It From by Cale Dodds|date=2018-11-02|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/where-i-get-it-from/1440082227?i=1440082232|language=en-US|access-date=2018-11-16}}</ref>

Most recently, in March 2019, Dodds released his current single "I Like Where This Is Going"<ref>{{Citation|title=I Like Where This Is Going - Single by Cale Dodds|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/i-like-where-this-is-going-single/1455473002|language=en-us|access-date=2019-03-20}}</ref>—a song written with hit writers [[Nicolle Galyon]] and [[Busbee]] about "anticipation" and seeing "the uncertainty of what’s going to happen, while having faith that it’s going to unfold in the right way."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://countrymusictattletale.com/2019/03/17/cale-dodds-tells-fans-i-like-where-this-is-going-in-brand-new-single/|title=Cale Dodds Tells Fans "I Like Where This Is Going" In Brand New Single|date=2019-03-17|website=Country Music Tattle Tale - Your country music news source|language=en-US|access-date=2019-03-20}}</ref>
