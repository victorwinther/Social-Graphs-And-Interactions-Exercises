'''John David Carver''' (born November 24, 1940)<ref name="LarkinCountry">{{cite book|title=[[Encyclopedia of Popular Music|The Guinness Who's Who of Country Music]]|editor=[[Colin Larkin (writer)|Colin Larkin]]|publisher=[[Guinness Publishing]]|date=1993|edition=First|isbn=0-85112-726-6|page=71/2}}</ref> is an American [[country music]] artist. Between 1968 and 1977, he charted 15 Top 40 hits on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' country chart. His highest-charting single was a cover of [[Tony Orlando]]'s "[[Tie a Yellow Ribbon Round the Ole Oak Tree]]", a cover that reached No.&nbsp;1 for him in 1974. He also had cover success with his version of the [[Starland Vocal Band]]'s "[[Afternoon Delight (song)|Afternoon Delight."]] Carver lives today in rural [[Wilson County, Tennessee]].

==Biography==
===Early life===
Carver was born and grew up in a rural area near [[Jackson, Mississippi]], United States,<ref name="LarkinCountry"/> and sang in a local country gospel quartet with his family.<ref name="LarkinCountry"/> He went on to form his own band, the Capital Cowboys, which were sponsored by an ice-cream company. Carver embarked on his first national tour in 1959, playing at clubs and fairs, and moved to [[Los Angeles]] in 1965, where he made regular appearances on local television and led the house band at the [[Palomino Club (North Hollywood)|Palomino Club]],<ref name="LarkinCountry"/> with such performers as [[Buck Owens]], [[Johnny Cash]], [[Patsy Cline]], [[Linda Ronstadt]], [[The Flying Burrito Brothers]], [[Hoyt Axton]], [[Willie Nelson]], [[Merle Haggard]], and [[Jerry Lee Lewis]].

===Rise to fame===
Carver was discovered by [[Del Shannon]], who got Carver a deal with Imperial Records.<ref name="LarkinCountry"/> His debut single for Imperial, was "Think About Her All The Time" b / w "One Way Or The Other" (Imperial 66173) in mid-1966. Both sides were written, produced and arranged by Shannon, who was a longtime fan of country music.  Carver's composition "New Lips" was recorded by [[Roy Drusky]] in 1967.<ref name="LarkinCountry"/> His self-titled debut album was released later that year, and contained the minor hit "Your Lily White Hands"; he had a few more modest successes with [[country pop]] offerings like 1968's "I Still Didn't Have the Sense to Go" and 1969's "That's Your Hang Up."<ref name="LarkinCountry"/>

===Later career===
In 1972, he moved to [[ABC Records|ABC]] and had a major country hit with a version of [[Tony Orlando and Dawn|Tony Orlando and Dawn's]] [[Tie a Yellow Ribbon Round the Ole Oak Tree|"Tie A Yellow Ribbon 'Round the Ole Oak Tree."]]<ref name="LarkinCountry"/> A string of [[Top 40]] country hits followed over the next five years, including the Top Tens "You Really Haven't Changed" (1973), "Don't Tell (That Sweet Old Lady of Mine)" (1974), and another country cover of a pop hit, the [[Starland Vocal Band|Starland Vocal Band's]] [[Afternoon Delight (song)|"Afternoon Delight"]] (1976). Carver's last Top 40 hit was 1977's "[[Living Next Door to Alice]]",<ref name="LarkinCountry"/> and his final charting single was a 1981 cover of [[ABBA|ABBA's]] "[[SOS (ABBA song)|S.O.S.]]"<ref name="LarkinCountry"/> He subsequently became a regular performer in [[Branson, Missouri]].

==Awards==
Johnny Carver has received positive critical response, and has been given several awards:
* The Arkansas Traveler Award
* The [[Country Music Association|Country Music Association (CMA)]] "Broader Acceptance & Progress of Country Music" Award
* The State of Mississippi Executive Department Merit Award
* [[Broadcast Music Incorporated|The Broadcast Music]] Citation of Achievement Award
* Four [[American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers|American Society of Composers, Authors, and Publishers (ASCAP)]] Merit Awards
* Mississippi's First Number One Country Artist
* Mississippi's Annual "Johnny Carver Day"—August 10
