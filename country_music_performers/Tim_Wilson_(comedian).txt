'''Timothy Collins Wilson''' (August 5, 1961 – February 26, 2014) was an American comedian and [[country music]] artist, whose act combined stand-up comedy and original songs.<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p138928|pure_url=yes}}|title=Tim Wilson biography|access-date=June 12, 2008|last=Ankeny|first=Jason|work=[[Allmusic]]}}</ref>

He released more than a dozen comedy albums, including several for [[Capitol Records]] Nashville, and made frequent appearances with ''Wilhite and Wall'', ''[[John Boy and Billy]]'', ''[[Big D and Bubba]]'' and ''[[Bob and Tom Show]]''. Wilson also appeared on numerous television programs, including ''[[The Tonight Show with Jay Leno]]'' and ''American Revolution Country Comedy'' on [[Country Music Television|CMT]]. In 2011, Wilson appeared on CMT's ''[[Ron White]]'s Comedy Salute to the Troops''.

In 2012, Wilson appeared on the [[Showtime (TV network)|Showtime]] comedy special ''[[Billy Gardell]]'s: Road Dogs'' with Gardell hosting along with comedians Ben Creed and Kenny Rogerson.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://recoverycomedy.com/promo-ken-rogerson.html |title=Clean and Sober Comedy! |website=Recoverycomedy.com |access-date=April 13, 2020}}</ref>

==Life and career==
Timothy Collins Wilson was born August 5, 1961, in [[Columbus, Georgia]], and attended [[Presbyterian College]] in [[Clinton, South Carolina]], as an English major.<ref name="whitburn1">{{cite book|last=Whitburn|first=Joel|title=Hot Country Songs 1944 to 2008|publisher=Record Research, Inc.|year=2008|page=472|isbn=978-0-89820-177-2}}</ref> His parents were school teachers. He was a self-described [[Libertarianism|libertarian]], and sometimes included his political standpoints in his comedy routines.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theadvocates.org/celebrities/tim-wilson.html|title=Tim Wilson – Libertarian|access-date=June 12, 2008 |work=TheAdvocates.org|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080423054811/http://www.theadvocates.org/celebrities/tim-wilson.html|archive-date=April 23, 2008}}</ref> He co-wrote several parodies for the 1980s comedy duo [[Pinkard & Bowden]], as well as comedian [[Jeff Foxworthy]]'s 1996 single "[[Redneck 12 Days of Christmas]]."<ref name="allmusic"/> He wrote and recorded the novelty songs "Garth Brooks Has Ruined My Life" and "The Ballad of John Rocker."<ref name="whitburn">{{cite book|last=Whitburn|first=Joel|title=Hot Country Songs 1944 to 2012|publisher=Record Research, Inc.|year=2013|page=377|isbn=978-0-89820-203-8}}</ref>

Wilson began his recording career in [[Atlanta]], on the Southern Tracks label with music publisher [[Bill Lowery (record producer)|Bill Lowery]]. Members of the [[Atlanta Rhythm Section]] played on many of Wilson's early recordings, with ARS keyboardist Dean Daughtry producing.<ref name="allmusic"/> Wilson later recorded extensively in [[Muscle Shoals, Alabama]], with the [[Muscle Shoals Rhythm Section]]. He co-produced ''Allnight Allstars'' with Muscle Shoals engineer Steve Melton. The project appeared on [[Capitol Nashville]] and includes [[Levon Helm]], [[Gregg Allman]], [[Jimmy Hall]], [[Bobby Whitlock]], and members of both the Atlanta Rhythm Section and the Muscle Shoals Rhythm Section.<ref>{{cite web|title=All Night All Stars|url=http://www.probiography.com/bios/allnightallstars.htm|access-date=February 27, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120309184616/http://www.probiography.com/bios/allnightallstars.htm|archive-date=March 9, 2012}}</ref>

In 2009, Wilson and Roger Keiss wrote a detective book entitled ''Happy New Year – ted'', about serial killer [[Ted Bundy]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.glasgowdailytimes.com/entertainment/local_story_218111209.html?start:int=15|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130124092451/http://www.glasgowdailytimes.com/entertainment/local_story_218111209.html?start:int=15|url-status=dead|archive-date=January 24, 2013|title=Comedian Tim Wilson to perform at Plaza Saturday|last=Kinslow|first=Gina|date=August 6, 2009|work=Glasgow Daily Times|access-date=September 22, 2009}}</ref> He can be heard discussing his research and his book in great detail while visiting the ''Off The Air Podcast'', hosted by [[Chick McGee]] of the ''[[Bob and Tom Show]]''.

Wilson was posthumously inducted into the Georgia Music Hall of Fame in September 2015.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Associated Press|title=Georgia Music Hall of Fame inducts Gregg Almman, 8 others|url=http://savannahnow.com/news/2015-09-27/georgia-music-hall-fame-inducts-gregg-allman-8-others#|website=savannahnow.com|access-date=October 21, 2015}}</ref>

==Death==
Wilson died of a heart attack on February 26, 2014. Early reports stated that he died in [[Nashville, Tennessee]]; however, his longtime friend and manager Chris Dipetta clarified that he had been traveling, but had made it to his hometown of [[Columbus, Georgia]], before he died.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Fernandez|first1=Maria Elena|title=Comedic country singer Tim Wilson dies at 52|url=http://www.today.com/entertainment/comedic-country-singer-tim-wilson-dies-52-2D12183366|access-date=August 26, 2014|agency=[[Today (U.S. TV program)|''Today'']]|publisher=[[NBC News]]|date=February 27, 2014}}</ref> More details from the referenced story state: "Wilson, 52, drove to Columbus from a gig in Michigan to visit his brother en route to a weekend show in Birmingham, Ala. when he started feeling ill, said Dipetta, who worked with Wilson for 30 years. Dipetta continued, "I talked to him yesterday afternoon and he said he felt bad, was short of breath, and I said, "get to the hospital now!" Wilson's brother rushed him to a nearby hospital and Wilson suffered a massive heart attack and died at 9:15&nbsp;p.m. Wilson left behind his wife, and two children.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mlive.com/entertainment/bay-city/index.ssf/2014/02/bob_tom_show_comedian_tim_wils.html |title=Bob & Tom Show comedian Tim Wilson dies 4 days after giving last performance in downtown Bay City &#124; MLive.com |website=www.mlive.com |access-date=13 January 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140227204838/http://www.mlive.com/entertainment/bay-city/index.ssf/2014/02/bob_tom_show_comedian_tim_wils.html |archive-date=27 February 2014 |url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>Stallings, Amy (February 27, 2014).[http://www.whas11.com/news/Report-Comedian-Tim-Wilson-dies-after-suffering-heart-attack-247479521.html Report: Comedian Tim Wilson dies after suffering heart attack] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140303063925/http://www.whas11.com/news/Report-Comedian-Tim-Wilson-dies-after-suffering-heart-attack-247479521.html |date=March 3, 2014 }} whas11.com; retrieved February 27, 2014.</ref>

==Tribute show==
On May 9, 2014, The Bob and Tom show sponsored a tribute comedy show to honor Wilson and help provide an education for his son.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bobandtom.com/common/page.php?pt=Join+us+for+the+Tim+Wilson+Benefit+Show&id=1414&is_corp=0|title=Join us for the Tim Wilson Benefit Show|date=April 21, 2014|publisher=bobandtom.com|access-date=May 10, 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140512214445/http://www.bobandtom.com/common/page.php?pt=Join+us+for+the+Tim+Wilson+Benefit+Show&id=1414&is_corp=0|archive-date=May 12, 2014}}</ref> This three-and-a-half-hour show was presented at the [[Paramount Theatre (Anderson, Indiana)]]. The announced lineup included ''the Bob and Tom show'' personalities ([[Bob Kevoian]], [[Tom Griswold]], [[Kristi Lee]], and [[Chick McGee]]), as well as [[John Means (comedian)|Dr. Gonzo]], Heywood Banks, [[Drew Hastings]], and [[Donny Baker]]. Surprise guests were introduced during the show and included, [[Duke Tumatoe]], [[Indianapolis Colts]] punter [[Pat McAfee]], Ricky Rydell and Scotty Bratcher. Additional music talent was provided by [[Steve Allee]], keyboard and PJ Yinger, trumpet. Additionally, Bob Kevoian did an emotional solo performance of a Tim Wilson tribute song on ukulele. Wilson's last appearance on The Bob and Tom Show was on December 10, 2013.
