'''Melonie Cannon''' is an American singer, blending [[Country music|country]] and [[Bluegrass music|bluegrass]] on her solo albums and session work. [[Willie Nelson]] said, "Melonie Cannon is one of the best singers I know."<ref name=MC-willie-2010>{{cite web |url=http://willienelson.com/story/and-the-wheels-turn-melonie-cannon/|title=And the Wheels Turn – Melonie Cannon|work=WillieNelson.com|access-date=August 20, 2017|author=Willie Nelson|date=July 20, 2010}}</ref>

==Biography==
===Early years===
Cannon was born in Jackson, Tennessee, but her family moved to Nashville when she was three years old. She is the daughter of country music songwriter and producer [[Buddy Cannon]],<ref name=MC-billboard-2004>{{cite magazine |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/66543/melonie-cannon|title=Melonie Cannon|magazine=Billboard|access-date=August 19, 2017|date=August 18, 2004}}</ref> and the sister of songwriter Marla Cannon-Goodman.<ref name=MC-musicrow-2016>{{cite news|last1=Nicholson|first1=Jessica|title=Carnival Music Adds Marla Cannon-Goodman To Roster|url=https://musicrow.com/2016/02/carnival-music-adds-marla-cannon-goodman-to-roster/|access-date=August 20, 2017|work=Music Row|date=February 23, 2016}}</ref>

At age 14, she began singing background vocals for artists such as [[John Michael Montgomery]], [[George Jones]], and [[Kenny Chesney]]. At age 16, she recorded a duet of "Cry Cry Darlin'" with [[Sammy Kershaw]].<ref name=MC-enjoy-2004>{{cite news|last1=Stone|first1=Steven|title=Melonie Cannon|url=http://www.enjoythemusic.com/magazine/music/0406/folk/cannon.htm|access-date=August 20, 2017|work=Enjoy the Music}}</ref>

After a stint in the Army, she returned to Nashville to sing demos and help her father with his publishing and production companies.<ref name=MC-nodep-2004>{{cite news|last1=Baxter|first1=David|title=Melonie Cannon - Self-Titled|url=http://nodepression.com/album-review/melonie-cannon-self-titled
|access-date=August 19, 2017|work=No Depression|date=August 31, 2004}}</ref><ref name=MC-standard-2004>{{cite web |url=http://www.countrystandardtime.com/d/article.asp?xid=324|title=Melonie Cannon fires away|work=Country Standard Time|access-date=August 20, 2017
|author=Dan MacIntosh|date=October 1, 2004}}</ref> Ronnie Bowman heard her sing at Nashville's [[Station Inn]], and helped her assemble a group of musicians to record with.<ref name=MC-online-2004>{{cite web |url=http://www.countrystarsonline.com/CSO/reviewarchives/MelonieCannon_JM.htm|title=Melonie Cannon|work=Country Stars Online|access-date=August 19, 2017|author=Jim Moulton}}</ref>

===Solo recordings===
Her self-titled debut album, released in 2004 on  the Skaggs Family record label, was produced by Bowman, and featured a blend of bluegrass and country music.<ref name=MC-billboard-2004 /> Guest artists included [[Stuart Duncan]], [[Dan Tyminski]], [[Barry Bales]], [[Rob McCoury]], [[Rob Ickes]], Randy Kohrs, and [[Jerry Douglas]].<ref name=MC-nodep-2004 />

In 2008, Cannon released ''And the Wheels Turn'' on [[Rural Rhythm Records|Rural Rhythm]]. Cannon and [[Willie Nelson]] sang a duet on "Back To Earth."<ref name=MC-bgtoday1-2008>{{cite news|last1=Lawless|first1=John|title=Melonie Cannon – And The Wheels Turn (part 1)|url=https://bluegrasstoday.com/melonie-cannon-and-the-wheels-turn-part-1/|access-date=August 20, 2017|work=Bluegrass Today|date=September 24, 2008}}</ref> [[Dan Tyminski]], [[Ronnie Bowman]], and [[Sonya Isaacs]] provide vocals. The album was co-produced by Buddy Cannon and Bowman. [[Adam Steffey]], Randy Kohrs, Tim Stafford, Wyatt Rice, [[Aubrey Haynie]], Jody King, [[Barry Bales]], and Deanie Richardson provide instrumental support.<ref name=MC-hyper-2008>{{cite web |url=http://www.hyperbolium.com/2008/11/17/melonie-cannon-and-the-wheels-turn/
|title=Melonie Cannon: And the Wheels Turn|work=Hyperbolium|access-date=August 20, 2017|date=November 17, 2008}}</ref>

===Personal life===
Cannon has been publicly forthcoming about her successful treatment of an addiction to prescription medications. She sought and received treatment via rehab and the support of friends and family.<ref name=MC-hope-2009>{{cite web |url=https://hoperiverentertainment.com/2009/11/04/melonie-cannon-opens-up-about-addiction/|title=Melonie Cannon Opens Up about Addiction|work=Hope River Entertainment|access-date=August 20, 2017|date=November 4, 2009}}</ref>
