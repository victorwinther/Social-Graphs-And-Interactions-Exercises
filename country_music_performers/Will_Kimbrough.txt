'''William Adams Kimbrough''' (born May 1, 1964) is an American singer-songwriter, multi-instrumentalist and producer based in [[Nashville, Tennessee|Nashville]], [[Tennessee]], United States.

==Biography==
Kimbrough was born in [[Mobile, Alabama]], and started his musical career as a founding member of Will & the Bushmen,<ref name="Gordon">{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p219242|pure_url=yes}}|title=Biography: Will Kimbrough|last=Gordon|first=Rev. Keith A.|publisher=[[Allmusic]]|access-date=May 24, 2010}}</ref> a college band in the 1980s that recorded a handful of albums and singles and made it to [[MTV]]. He then went on to form the [[Bis-Quits]] with long-time friend Tommy Meyer.<ref name="Gordon" /> The Bis-quits produced an [[eponymous]] album which was released on [[John Prine]]’s [[Oh Boy Records]] label.

Kimbrough is also a producer and has produced albums for [[Adrienne Young]], [[Rodney Crowell]],<ref name="Gordon" /> [[Todd Snider]],<ref name="Gordon" /> [[Kate Campbell]], [[Steve Poltz]], [[Kim Richey]], [[Garrison Starr]], [[Matthew Ryan (musician)|Matthew Ryan]], and [[Josh Rouse]].

His songs have been recorded by [[Jimmy Buffett]], [[Little Feat]], [[Jack Ingram]], [[Todd Snider]] and more. Kimbrough has also collaborated with many artists including [[Rosanne Cash]], [[Guy Clark]], [[Rodney Crowell]], [[Steve Earle]], [[Gomez (band)|Gomez]], [[Emmylou Harris]], [[The Jayhawks]], [[Mark Knopfler]], [[Buddy Miller]], [[John Prine]], [[Toumani Diabaté]], [[Kim Richey]], [[Josh Rouse]], [[Matthew Ryan (musician)|Matthew Ryan]], [[Billy Joe Shaver]], [[Todd Snider]], [[Michael C. Parris]], [[Mavis Staples]], [[Garrison Starr]], [[Adrienne Young]], and others.<ref name="Will Kimbrough">{{cite web|last=Farley|first=Mike|title=Will Kimbrough Bio|url=http://www.willkimbrough.com/bio/|website=Willkimbrough.com|access-date=October 7, 2012}}</ref>

Kimbrough was recognized in 2004 as the 'Instrumentalist of the Year' by the [[Americana Music Association]] – an award that had previously been awarded three years in a row to [[Jerry Douglas]].<ref name="Will Kimbrough" /> His songs demonstrate a literate facility that he pairs with guitar playing so fluid that his fans created a t-shirt suggesting he is an alien.<ref name=Phillips>{{cite news|last=Phillips|first=Casey|title=Will Kimbrough is an Alien|url=http://ehis.ebscohost.com/eds/detail?sid=7489a0c3-c5c8-4b4b-94f4-057a7590949b%40sessionmgr12&vid=4&hid=4&bdata=JnNpdGU9ZWRzLWxpdmUmc2NvcGU9c2l0ZQ%3d%3d#db=nfh&AN=2W61475723427|access-date=October 7, 2012|newspaper=Chattanooga Times/Free Press (TN)|date=October 8, 2009}}</ref>

Kimbrough teamed up with [[Tommy Womack]], John Deaderick, Paul Griffith and Dave Jacques in 2005 to create the five piece band, Daddy.  They recorded a live album in Frankfort, Kentucky, ''Daddy at the Women's Club'', and another album, ''For a Second Time''.

In 2007, Kimbrough released an EP – his solo studio recording that revealed a hint of what would develop on his next album release.<ref name="Will Kimbrough" />

Kimbrough has released several solo albums to date, and has collaborated on many more, as well as serving as a [[session musician]] and [[sideman]]. Kimbrough was one of the guest artists at [[Jimmy Buffett]]'s Live from the Gulf Coast Concert on July 11, 2010.

In March 2012, it was announced that Kimbrough had joined [[Trigger Hippy]] in place of guitarist [[Audley Freed]].

In frequent visits to visit his family, Kimbrough joined a number of other Southern Alabama singer-songwriters beginning in 2013 to form Willie Sugarcapps.

A song he co-wrote with John Hahn and recorded by [[Shemekia Copeland]], "Too Far to Be Gone", was nominated for a [[Blues Music Award]] in 2023.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rockandbluesmuse.com/2023/01/10/2023-blues-music-award-nominees-announced/|title=2023 Blues Music Award Nominees Announced|first=Martine|last=Ehrenclou|website=Rockandbluesmuse.com|date=10 January 2023|access-date=19 April 2023}}</ref>

==Influences==
Born and raised on the Alabama Gulf Coast, Kimbrough ingested eclectic FM radio sounds and the music of nearby [[New Orleans]] as a young man. His sound has been influenced by traditions of [[folk music|folk]], [[blues]], [[gospel music|gospel]], [[country music|country]], [[punk rock]] and [[jazz]].

Kimbrough described "When Your Lovin' Comes Around" from his 2014 release, ''Sideshow Love'', as "a little bit of a tribute to [[J. J. Cale]]," who he said was a significant influence on his music.<ref>From an interview on [http://americanamusicshow.com/episode178 episode 178 of the Americana Music Show], released February 10, 2014</ref>

==Social commentary==
The songs on his album ''Americanitis'', such as "Warring Ways" and "Everyone's in Love", have been described by Kimbrough as anti-war and anti-greed themed.<ref name=Phillips />

His album ''Wings'' explores themes surrounding the conflicts between family and career, love and work, parents and children. The music is based in [[folk rock]], with touches of guitar, cello, saxophone, trumpet, banjo, and [[Hammond organ]].<ref name="Will Kimbrough" />
