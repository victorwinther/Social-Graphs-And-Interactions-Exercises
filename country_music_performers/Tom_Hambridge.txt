'''Thomas Jay Hambridge'''<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.ascap.com/ace/search.cfm?requesttimeout=300&mode=results&searchstr=1528370&search_in=c&search_type=exact&search_det=t,s,w,p,b,v&results_pp=10&start=1 |title=American Society of Composers |work=ascap.com |year=2011 |accessdate=September 17, 2011}}</ref> (born December 20, 1960)<ref>{{cite web | url=http://zildjian.com/Artists/H/Tom-Hambridge |title=Artist Website – Bio |work=zildjian.com |year=2011 |accessdate=September 17, 2011}}</ref> is an American rock, [[Country music|country]], and [[blues]], producer, songwriter, musician and vocalist. Hambridge has received two [[Grammy Awards]],<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.grammy.com/news/and-the-grammy-went-to-buddy-guy  |title=Grammy Website – Living Proof |publisher=National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences |year=2011 |accessdate=September 17, 2011}}</ref> an ASCAP award,<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.ascap.com/eventsawards/awards/ascapcma/2007/winners.html |title=ASCAP Awards 2007 |work=ascap.com |year=2011 |accessdate=September 17, 2011}}</ref> seven Grammy nominations,<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.nashvillemusicguide.net/october-2010-producers-spotlight-tom-hambridge/ |title=Nashville Music Guide −2010 Producers Spotlight, Tom Hambridge |work=nashvillemusicguide.net |year=2011 |accessdate=September 17, 2011}}</ref> seven Boston Music Awards, and has been inducted into the Buffalo Hall of Fame.<ref name="buffalo">{{cite web | url=http://www.buffalomusic.org/2001_thambridge.html  |title=Buffalo Hall of Fame Inductees – 2001 |work=buffalomusic.org |year=2011 |accessdate=September 17, 2011}}</ref> In December 2015, Hambridge was given the key to his hometown of [[Buffalo, New York]] with Mayor [[Byron Brown]] declaring December 28 "Tom Hambridge Day."<ref>{{cite web | url= http://buffalonews.com/2015/12/28/music-talent-tom-hambridge-has-his-own-day-in-buffalo/ |title= Tom Hambridge Day|work=buffalonews.com |year=2015 |accessdate=January 25, 2017}}</ref> Hambridge's songs have been recorded by several notable artists and have been featured in movie productions, commercials and television programs. He has been referred to as "The White [[Willie Dixon]]" by Rock and Roll Hall of Fame Inductee [[Buddy Guy]]<ref>{{cite web | url= http://zildjian.com/Artists/H/Tom-Hambridge|title= White Willie Dixon |work=zildjian.com |year=2011 |accessdate=September 17, 2011}}</ref> and [[Susan Tedeschi]]'s "Secret Weapon".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.moderndrummer.com/updatefull/200001290/Tom%20Hambridge|title= Susan Tedeschi's Secret Weapon |website=Moderndrummer.com |year=2011 |accessdate=September 17, 2011}}</ref>

==Biography==
Born and raised in [[Buffalo, New York]], Hambridge began learning the drums at the age of 5. He played his first paying gig, a bar mitzvah, in third grade. Throughout his school years, Hambridge played in garage bands, his high school orchestra and jazz band. After graduating from high school in 1979, he received a scholarship at Boston's Berklee College of Music.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.berklee.edu/bt/131/alum_profile.html |title=Alum Profile: Tom Hambridge 83-Renaissance Man in a City of Specialists &#124; Berklee College of Music|first= Mark|last=Small|work=berklee.edu |year=2011 |accessdate=September 17, 2011}}</ref>
[[File:Tom Hambridge Drums.jpg|thumb|left |alt= Tom Playing Drums.| Tom Hambridge]]
 
He received his degree in Professional Music in 1983. After graduation, he worked for three years as drummer and lead singer for the blues guitarist [[Roy Buchanan]]. While working with Buchanan, Hambridge contributed as a musician on Buchanan's release, ''Live: Amazing Grace''.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/live-amazing-grace-r1626349/credits |title=Roy Buchanan – Live Amazing Grace |first= Roy|last=Buchanan |publisher=AllMusic |year=2011 |accessdate=September 17, 2011}}</ref> In the meantime, he formed the band "T.H. and the Wreckage". In 1988, the band released ''Born to Rock,''<ref name="blackrose">{{cite web | url=http://www.blackroserecords.net/store.html|title= Black Rose Records |work=blackroserecords.net |year=2011 |accessdate=September 17, 2011}}</ref> one of several independent, self-produced albums Hambridge has completed. ''Born To Rock'' was the first of the many [[Boston Music Awards]] he has received. In the meantime, he assisted promoters assemble backup bands for artists such as [[Bo Diddley]], [[Percy Sledge]], [[Chuck Berry]], [[Gary Puckett]], and [[Sha Na Na]].<ref name="buffalo"/>

Hambridge released his own album ''Still Running'' in 1996.<ref name="stillrunning">{{cite web | url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/still-running-r289975|title= Still Running, Hambridge |last= Tom Hambridge |publisher=AllMusic |year=2011 |accessdate=September 17, 2011}}</ref>

In 1997, he produced [[Susan Tedeschi]]'s ''[[Just Won't Burn]]''. Hambridge wrote Tedeschi's Top 10 hits "Rock Me Right" and "It Hurt So Bad".<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.eminashville.com/bios/thambridge.html |title=EMI Nashville – Tom Hambridge |work=eminashville.com |year=2011 |accessdate=September 17, 2011}}</ref> In the meantime, he also released his own album ''Balderdash'' in 2000.<ref name="balderdash">{{cite web | url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/balderdash-r503030|title= Balderdash, Hambridge |last= Tom Hambridge |publisher=AllMusic |year=2011 |accessdate=September 17, 2011}}</ref> Hambridge received a subsequent 2004 Grammy nomination ([[Grammy Award for Best Contemporary Blues Album]]) for his contributions on [[Johnny Winter]]'s release ''I'm A Bluesman''  ("Cheatin' Blues" and "Lone Wolf").<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.artistdirect.com/nad/store/artist/album/0,,2890478,00.html |title= Artist Direct – Johnny Winter -I'm A Bluesman |work=artistdirect.com |year=2011 |accessdate=September 17, 2011}}</ref> Shortly thereafter, Hambridge released his album ''Bang N' Roll'' (2004) and the album ''Live'' (2007).<ref name="bangnroll">{{cite web | url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/bang-n-roll-r705780|title= Bang N' Roll, Hambridge |publisher=AllMusic |year=2011 |accessdate=September 17, 2011}}</ref>

In 2008, he received Grammy nominations ([[Grammy Award for Best Traditional Blues Album]]) for his work on [[Buddy Guy]]'s ''[[Skin Deep (Buddy Guy album)|Skin Deep]]''. ''Skin Deep'' debuted at number 68 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]] which was the highest position of any of Guy's previous albums, and was number 1 on the Billboard Blues Album Chart. Hambridge released his own album ''Boogieman'' (2009).<ref name="boogieman">{{cite web | url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/boogieman-r1553953|title= Boogieman, Hambridge |publisher=AllMusic |year=2011 |accessdate=September 17, 2011}}</ref> In 2011, he won the [[Grammy Award for Best Contemporary Blues Album]] for co-writing and producing [[Buddy Guy]]'s album ''[[Living Proof (Buddy Guy album)|Living Proof]]''.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.usatoday.com/life/music/news/2010-10-31-buddy-guy_N.htm |title= USA Today – Buddy Guy, Living Proof |first=Steve |last=Jones |work=Artistdirect.com |year=2011 |accessdate=September 17, 2011}}</ref>

He was credited as the producer, songwriter and percussionist for [[George Thorogood and the Destroyers]] 2011 release ''[[2120 South Michigan Ave.]]'', which included the song "Going Back" that reached number 1 on Classic Rock Radio. Also in 2011, he wrote and produced [[B. B. King]] and [[Buddy Guy]]'s duet "Stay Around A Little Longer."<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.grammy.com/news/and-the-grammy-went-to-buddy-guy |title= Grammy – Buddy Guy, Stay Around A Little Longer |publisher=National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences |year=2011 |accessdate=September 17, 2011}}</ref>

Other recognized 2011 successes include writing/co-writing all songs on [[Quinn Sullivan (musician)|Quinn Sullivan]]'s release ''Cyclone''<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/cyclone-r2141383&sa=U&ei=eoJvTqq4AvSKsALZtLjCCQ&ved=0CBgQFjABOAo&usg=AFQjCNEm_JWDfRJwNJvB369YVbpAFE9pQw |title=Quinn Sullivan, Cyclone |publisher=AllMusic |year=2011 |accessdate=September 17, 2011}}</ref> (2011, number 7 on the Billboard Blues chart, July 30, 2011).<ref name="cyclone">{{cite magazine | url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/2011-07-30/blues-albums |title=Quinn Sullivan, Cyclone |magazine=Billboard |year=2011 |accessdate=September 17, 2011}}</ref> Later in 2013, Hambridge would do the same on [[Quinn Sullivan (musician)|Quinn Sullivan]]'s release ''Getting There'',<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/getting-there-mw0002550681 |title=Getting There |publisher=AllMusic |accessdate=January 25, 2017}}</ref> which charted at 38 on the Billboard Blues Chart.<ref>{{cite magazine |url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/277262/quinn-sullivan/chart|title=Billboard Blues Charts – Getting There |magazine=Billboard |accessdate=January 25, 2017}}</ref>

In 2015, Hambridge earned his second [[Grammy Award for Best Blues Album]] for his contributions as a musician, producer, composer and mixer on [[Buddy Guy]]'s [[Born to Play Guitar]].

In 2016, Hambridge produced [[Mike Zito]]'s album, ''Make Blues Not War'',<ref>{{cite web | url= http://www.allmusic.com/album/make-blues-not-war-mw0002985066  |title= Mike Zito – Make Blues Not War |publisher=AllMusic |year=2016 |accessdate=January 25, 2017}}</ref> which debuted at number 1 on the [[Billboard Chart]] on December 10, 2016<ref>{{cite magazine | url= http://www.billboard.com/artist/311241/mike-zito/chart?sort=position&f=308  |title= Billboard Music Charts – Mike Zito|magazine=Billboard |year=2016 |accessdate=January 25, 2017}}</ref> and earned his seventh [[Grammy Award]] Nomination<ref>{{cite magazine | url= http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7597556/grammys-nominees-complete-list-2017  |title= Grammy Nomination – Kenny Neal's Bloodline|magazine=Billboard |year=2016 |accessdate=January 25, 2017}}</ref> for his production work on [[Bloodline (Kenny Neal album)|Bloodline]].

King]] and others in a performance for the White House's "Red, White and Blues" Black History Month celebration concert.  During the performance, President [[Barack Obama]] provided a brief history of the Blues and accompanied the group during the song "[[Sweet Home Chicago]]".  On October 14, 2015, Hambridge returned to the [[White House]] with [[Buddy Guy]], [[Marty Sammon]], [[Trombone Shorty]], [[Carol Burnett]], [[Queen Latifah]], [[MC Lyte]], [[Keb Mo]], [[Smokey Robinson]] and others for "A Celebration of American Creativity: In Performance at the White House", commemorating the 50th anniversary of the [[National Endowment for the Humanities]].
