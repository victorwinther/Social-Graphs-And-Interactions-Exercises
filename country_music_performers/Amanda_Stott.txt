'''Amanda Stott''' (born May 6, 1982) is a Canadian singer and [[songwriter]] from [[Brandon, Manitoba]], [[Canada]].

==Early life and career==
Stott was born in [[Brandon, Manitoba]], [[Canada]] to Cyril and Tiena Stott.<ref>{{cite web |title=Winnifred Fanny (nee Hockley) Stott - Obituary |url=https://passages.winnipegfreepress.com/passage-details/id-281741/STOTT_Winnifred |website=passages.winnipegfreepress.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Obituary for Stanley Durnin Stott |url=https://memorieschapel.com/book-of-memories/1441585/stott-stanley/obituary.php |website=memorieschapel.com}}</ref> She grew up on her family farm in rural [[Manitoba|Manitoba, Canada]]. She has an older brother named Conrad.<ref>{{cite web |title=happy birthday to my big brother, conrad!!! hope you have a fabulous day! love you! #tbt |url=https://www.facebook.com/amandastottmusic/photos/a.241765749206530/499923283390774/ |website=facebook.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=ne of my favorite photos of my brother and i. #throwbackthursday |url=https://www.instagram.com/p/QF0Wb5EXku/?fbclid=IwAR2YwPg0kQBAR8PyOcG1Vx-fMdf00vco8R-ZC8iG2n75nOr-pgMpBC3lu78 |website=instagram.com}}</ref> Her father was a saxophonist and pianist. She started singing in the church choir at Brandon Calvary Temple when she was just three years old. She first gained prominence as a country singer at the Dauphin Country Fest in 1994. In 1999, she signed with [[Warner Music Canada]] and her first [[Amanda Stott (album)|self-titled album]] was released in 2000. This album prompted the single "Black Is Black" which quickly became a hit on country radio and television.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://jam.canoe.ca/Music/Artists/S/Stott_Amanda/2000/03/31/750067.html|archive-url=https://archive.today/20120711120324/http://jam.canoe.ca/Music/Artists/S/Stott_Amanda/2000/03/31/750067.html|url-status=usurped|archive-date=July 11, 2012|accessdate=January 6, 2012|title=Flying Stott|author=John Kendle|publisher=jam!}}</ref> She was also nominated for a [[Juno Award]] for Best New Solo Artist.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://jam.canoe.ca/Music/Artists/S/Stott_Amanda/2001/01/25/750063.html|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130628044527/http://jam.canoe.ca/Music/Artists/S/Stott_Amanda/2001/01/25/750063.html|url-status=usurped|archive-date=June 28, 2013|accessdate=May 25, 2013|title=Country queens lead list of local nominees |author=Pat St. Germain|publisher=jam!}}</ref>

After a few years away from the spotlight, Stott returned with a more adult pop sound and her first single, "Paper Rain", reached No. 1 on the [[Canadian Singles Chart]]. On March 8, 2005, her second album, ''Chasing the Sky'', was released. It included "Paper Rain" and follow up singles "Homeless Heart" and "She'll Get Over It".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://jam.canoe.ca/Music/Artists/S/Stott_Amanda/AlbumReviews/2005/03/10/956576.html|archive-url=https://archive.today/20120710011830/http://jam.canoe.ca/Music/Artists/S/Stott_Amanda/AlbumReviews/2005/03/10/956576.html|url-status=usurped|archive-date=July 10, 2012|accessdate=January 6, 2012|title=Chasing the Sky|work=Winnipeg Sun|author=Darryl Sterdan}}</ref>

On April 13, 2005, Stott performed at the [[Yukon Arts Centre]] in [[Whitehorse]], [[Yukon]].<ref>{{cite web |title=The New Amanda Stott Brings Her New Voice |url=https://whatsupyukon.com/arts-entertainment/yukon-music/the-new-amanda-stott-brings-her-new-voice/ |website=whatsupyukon.com|date=April 8, 2005 }}</ref>

Stott performed on July 1, 2005 as part of the [[Canada Day]] celebrations, taking place on [[Parliament Hill]] in Ottawa. In the summer of 2005 she toured Canada with fellow female Canadian singers [[Keshia ChantÃ©]], Christine Evans and [[Cassie Steele]]. In December 2005 she was a part of the Holiday Train cross-Canada tour along with [[Wayne Rostad]] and [[the Moffatts]].{{citation needed|date=October 2018}}

She was a featured singer in the travelling [[Cirque du Soleil]] arena show called [[Delirium (Cirque du Soleil)|Delirium]], touring North America.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.grelin.com/stott.htm|accessdate=January 6, 2012|title=Amanda Stott|publisher=Grelin Entertainment}}</ref>

In 2012, she released a single called "Now's Our Time" which features her husband, Matt Young.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://open.spotify.com/album/6jowf8zXcurL8JOWNfdd19|title=Amanda Stott - Now's Our Time (feat. Matt Young)|work=[[Spotify]]|accessdate=August 24, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=NOW'S OUR TIME Contest Announcement!!! |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PbmT7A9CbKY |website=youtube.com}}</ref>

After nine years of hiatus on music, On January 9, 2023, Stott announced she was traveling to [[Israel]] for co-hosting the ''Back to the Bible Canada'' organization in [[Abbotsford, British Columbia|Abbotsford, BC]] for their event known as "The Israel Experience 2023".<ref>{{cite web |title=The Israel Experience 2023 - Back to the Bible Canada |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=b3FbmdC4whk |website=youtube.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=I am so thrilled to be a part of this! |url=https://www.facebook.com/amandastottmusic/posts/pfbid02EyiSFWHsn2M5Ld1wuwZwJbKjSSjokqpaUt4QkUhNuKFYWRaheEPWaAbrZPRWzc4Pl?locale=ms_MY |website=facebook.com}}</ref>
