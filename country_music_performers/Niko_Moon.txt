'''Niko Moon''' (born '''Nicholas Cowan'''; September 20, 1982) is an American [[country pop]] singer and songwriter formerly signed to [[RCA Records Nashville|RCA Nashville]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-07-19|title=Niko Moon Signs To Sony's RCA Nashville Imprint|url=https://musicrow.com/2019/07/niko-moon-signs-to-sonys-rca-nashville-imprint/|access-date=2021-02-03|website=MusicRow.com|language=en-US}}</ref> He has written songs for [[Dierks Bentley]], [[Zac Brown Band]], [[Rascal Flatts]], and [[Morgan Wallen]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Niko Moon {{!}} Credits|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/niko-moon-mn0003359916/credits|access-date=2021-02-03|website=AllMusic|language=en}}</ref> He was also a member of the group [[Sir Rosevelt]] with [[Zac Brown]] and Ben Simonetti.<ref>{{Cite magazine|title=Zac Brown Reveals Diplo & Skrillex-Influenced Dance-Pop Project Sir Rosevelt: Interview|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7526468/zac-brown-sir-rosevelt-interview-dance-pop-edm-diplo-skrillex|access-date=2021-02-03|magazine=Billboard|language=en}}</ref>

== Background ==

Nicholas Cowan is originally from Tyler, Texas but relocated to Douglasville, Georgia, when he was 10. His father, a truck driver and his mother, a waitress, introduced Niko to the works of artists like [[John Prine]] and [[Patty Griffin]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://musicrow.com/2019/08/on-the-row-niko-moon-talks-good-time-and-making-music-with-a-positive-impact/|title=On The Row: Niko Moon Talks "Good Time" And Making Music With A Positive Impact|date=August 26, 2019}}</ref> He started out as a drummer after seeing his father play. His father was also a musician and gave up being a touring drummer when Niko was born.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.iheart.com/content/2019-08-06-is-niko-moon-reimagining-country-music-with-a-new-sound/ |title=Is Niko Moon Reimagining Country Music With A New Sound? |first= Lauryn |last=Snapp|date=August 6, 2019|work=iHeart Radio }}</ref>

== Career ==
Niko Moon first found success as a co-writer on songs for the Zac Brown Band, including “Heavy Is the Head”, “Homegrown”, “Beautiful Drug", "Keep Me in Mind", and "Loving You Easy” as well as [[Keep Me in Mind (Zac Brown Band song)|Keep Me in Mind]] and Island Song under the credits as Nic Cowan.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/niko-moon-mn0003359916|title=Niko Moon &#124; Credits|website=AllMusic}}</ref> He also co-produced the Zac Brown Band albums [[Jekyll + Hyde|Jekyll+Hyde]] and [[Welcome Home (Zac Brown Band album)|Welcome Home]], and co-wrote the song “Back To Life” for [[Rascal Flatts]] along with Cary Barlowe, Shay Mooney, and Fred Wilhelm.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://tasteofcountry.com/rascal-flatts-back-to-life-good-morning-america/|title=Rascal Flatts Take Over Times Square for 'GMA' Performance|first=Jacklyn|last=Krol|website=Taste of Country}}</ref> He appeared on [[Colt Ford]]’s album ''[[Every Chance I Get (album)|Every Chance I Get]]'' on the track "Waste Some Time" with [[Nappy Roots]].

In 2010, under the name Nic Cowan, Niko Moon self-released his debut EP titled "Cheap Wine," which featured seven tracks. The following year saw the release of his first full-length album, "Hard Headed," also under the Nic Cowan moniker. According to Moon, the album's title track, "Hard Headed," delves into themes of resisting control from a romantic partner. Distributed by Southern Ground Records, the album includes 13 tracks, among them notable songs such as "Reno," "New Sh*t," and "Cut it Loose (feat. Zac Brown)."<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://first-avenue.com/performer/nic-cowan/ | title=Nic Cowan | date=2012}}</ref>

In 2016, Moon formed the group Sir Rosevelt with Zac Brown and Ben Simonetti, another co-writer with the Zac Brown Band. The group's track “The Bravest” was used during the 2018 FIFA World Cup.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.nashcountrydaily.com/2017/11/17/zac-browns-new-trio-sir-rosevelt-releases-new-song-the-bravest-listen/ |title=Zac Brown's New Trio, Sir Rosevelt, Releases New Song, "The Bravest" [Listen] |first= Jim |last=Casey | date= November 17, 2017 |work=Nash Country Daily}}</ref>
Moon and Simonetti co-wrote a song on [[Michael Franti]]’s album Stay Human Vol. II.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://relix.com/articles/detail/track-by-track-michael-franti-stay-human-vol-ii/|title=Track By Track: Michael Franti 'Stay Human Vol. II'|date=February 19, 2019|website=Relix Media}}</ref>

In 2019, Niko Moon signed a recording contract with [[Sony Music Nashville]] imprint [[RCA Records Nashville|RCA Nashville]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.musicconnection.com/singer-songwriter-niko-moon-signs-rca-nashville/|title=Singer/Songwriter Niko Moon signs to RCA Nashville|date=July 19, 2019|website=[[Music Connection Magazine]]}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Niko is married to singer-songwriter Anna Moon. They have a daughter, Lily Ann Moon.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.thelist.com/430702/the-truth-about-niko-moons-marriage/ | title=The Truth About Niko Moon's Marriage | date=7 June 2021 }}</ref>
