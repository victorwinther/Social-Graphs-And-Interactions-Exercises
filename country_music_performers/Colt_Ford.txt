'''Jason Farris Brown''' (born August 27, 1969) known professionally as '''Colt Ford''', is an American [[country rap]] musician, songwriter, entrepreneur, and former [[professional golfer]]. He is also the co-founder of record label [[Average Joes Entertainment]]. Ford has released seven albums through Average Joes and has charted multiple singles on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs]] and [[Country Airplay]] charts. In addition, he co-wrote "[[Dirt Road Anthem]]" for [[Jason Aldean]], "[[Country Must Be Country Wide]]" for [[Brantley Gilbert]], and "[[Mind on You (George Birge song)|Mind on You]]" for [[George Birge]].

==Biography==
Jason Farris Brown was born and raised in [[Athens, Georgia]].<ref>{{cite web|author=David Jeffries |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/colt-ford-mn0001028746/biography |title=Colt Ford &#124; Biography & History |publisher=[[AllMusic]] |access-date=2016-03-04}}</ref> He was a [[Professional golfer|professional golf]]er,<ref name="cmt"/> playing on the Nationwide Tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cbssports.com/golf/story/11789089|title=Up & Down: Mickelsons appreciate thoughts; sour note by singer|last=Elling|first=Steve|work=[[CBS]] Sports|access-date=2009-07-03}}</ref> Later, he turned his interests to music, taking influence from [[country music]] and [[hip hop music|hip hop]].<ref name="cmt">{{cite web |title=Colt Ford biography |url=http://www.cmt.com/artists/az/ford__colt/bio.jhtml |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080620194200/http://www.cmt.com/artists/az/ford__colt/bio.jhtml |url-status=dead |archive-date=June 20, 2008 |work=[[Country Music Television|CMT]] |access-date=1 July 2009}}</ref> Assuming the [[stage name]] Colt Ford, he released his debut album, ''[[Ride Through the Country]]'', on December 2, 2008, through [[Average Joes Entertainment]], which he cofounded.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.goerie.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/20100605/ENTERTAINMENT0302/306059983/-1/entertainment03|title=Ford takes a different country road|last=Talbott|first=Chris|date=4 June 2010|work=GoErie.com|access-date=26 July 2011|archive-date=22 September 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120922073207/http://www.goerie.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/20100605/ENTERTAINMENT0302/306059983/-1/entertainment03|url-status=dead}}</ref> This album included the singles "No Trash in My Trailer"<ref name="sun">{{cite web |title=Ford brings upbeat style, 'No Trash in My Trailer' crooner to perform country/hip-hop mix |url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=MB&s_site=myrtlebeachonline&p_multi=MB&p_theme=realcities&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=122DE17536DB9FF8&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM |date=2008-08-29 |work=Sun News |access-date=1 July 2009}}</ref> (a cover of a Mike Dekle/[[Byron Hill]] song) and "Ride Through the Country" (a duet with [[John Michael Montgomery]]), the latter of which did not chart until the week of October 10, 2009, when it debuted at number 57 on [[Hot Country]]

He also appeared on a rap [[remix]] of [[Montgomery Gentry]]'s late-2008 Number One single "[[Roll with Me]]".<ref name="boot">{{cite web |title=New Faces: Colt Ford |url=http://www.theboot.com/2009/02/02/new-faces-colt-ford/ |date=2009-02-02 |work=The Boot |access-date=1 July 2009}}</ref> Ford's debut album also has guest appearances from country singer [[Jamey Johnson]], as well as [[Bone Crusher (rapper)|Bone Crusher]] and [[Jermaine Dupri]], Adrian Young of [[No Doubt (band)|No Doubt]], and Jeremy Popoff of [[Lit (band)|Lit]].<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web |title=Colt Ford biography |url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p1065034|pure_url=yes}} |author=David Jeffries |work=[[Allmusic]] |access-date=1 July 2009}}</ref> The album did not enter the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' albums charts until 2009.

Ford co-wrote and originally recorded the song "[[Dirt Road Anthem]]" for his debut album ''Ride Through the Country''; it was later released by [[Brantley Gilbert]], who co-wrote the song, on his 2010 album ''[[Halfway to Heaven (album)|Halfway to Heaven]]'',<ref>{{cite web|last=Waterman |first=Doug |url=http://www.americansongwriter.com/2011/09/the-story-behind-the-song-dirt-road-anthem/ |title=The Story Behind The Song: "Dirt Road Anthem" « American Songwriter |website=Americansongwriter.com |date=2011-09-21 |access-date=2016-03-04}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=CMT.com : Brantley Gilbert : Album |url=http://www.cmt.com/artists/az/gilbert__brantley/2506478/album.jhtml |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100326125555/http://www.cmt.com/artists/az/gilbert__brantley/2506478/album.jhtml |url-status=dead |archive-date=March 26, 2010 |work=CMT |access-date=2011-02-05}}</ref> and again by [[Jason Aldean]] on his album ''[[My Kinda Party]]'', also from 2010.<ref>{{cite web |title=My Kinda Party >> Music >> Jason Aldean |url=http://www.jasonaldean.com/music/all/my_kinda_party-6 |work=Jason Aldean |access-date=2011-02-05}}</ref>

Ford wrote the theme song "Buck 'em" for the [[Professional Bull Riders]] association. He also appears as a guest vocalist on the track "Tailgatin'" on [[Cledus T. Judd]]'s 2009 album ''[[Polyrically Uncorrect]]'', a song which Ford wrote with Johnson and Popoff.<ref>{{cite web |title=Music |url=http://www.cledustjudd.net/music.html |work=Cledus T. Judd |access-date=1 July 2009 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090623072013/http://www.cledustjudd.net/music.html |archive-date=23 June 2009 }}</ref> Ford's second studio album, ''[[Chicken & Biscuits]]'', was released in April 2010, following the release of its title track.

Ford released a third studio album on May 3, 2011, called ''Every Chance I Get''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allaccess.com/country/future-releases|title=Future Releases for Country Radio Stations|publisher=All Access|access-date=February 11, 2011}}</ref> The album's lead-off single, "Country Thang", debuted at number 55 for the country chart week ending February 19, 2011. "She Likes to Ride in Trucks", featuring [[Craig Morgan (singer)|Craig Morgan]], served as the album's second single.

Ford's fourth album, ''[[Declaration of Independence (album)|Declaration of Independence]]'', became his first number 1 album on Top Country Albums. Its first charted single is "Back", a duet with [[Jake Owen]]. "Back" is Ford's highest charting single to date, making Top 40 on [[Hot Country Songs]]. In 2012, Colt Ford headlined the Declaration Of Independence Tour with supporting acts [[The Lacs]], [[Lenny Cooper]], and [[JB and the Moonshine Band]]. In the fall of 2012 Ford announced he would be touring with [[JJ Lawhorn]] on the Answer To No One Tour.

Colt Ford's fifth studio album, ''[[Thanks for Listening (Colt Ford album)|Thanks for Listening]]'', was released on July 1, 2014.

"4 Lane Gone" is the first single from his sixth studio album, ''[[Love Hope Faith]]''. Ford released a music video for the single on September 11, 2016<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vOgszDDOAx8 |title=YouTube |publisher=[[YouTube]] |access-date=2020-04-05}}{{cbignore}}{{Dead Youtube links|date=February 2022}}</ref> ''Love Hope Faith'' was released on May 5, 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/love-hope-faith/id1207486899|title=Love Hope Faith by Colt Ford on iTunes|date=5 May 2017 |publisher=[[Apple Inc.]]|access-date=May 5, 2017}}</ref>

Colt's next album ''[[We the People, Volume 1]]'' was released in September 2019.

On April 5, 2024, Ford suffered a [[heart attack]] after a concert in [[Arizona]], and was placed in [[intensive care]] soon after.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://people.com/colt-ford-suffered-heart-attack-arizona-performance-icu-8627453 | title=Colt Ford in ICU After Suffering Heart Attack Following Arizona Performance }}</ref>
