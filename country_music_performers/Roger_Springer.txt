'''Roger Springer''' (born June 15, 1962, in [[Caddo, Oklahoma]]) is an American [[country music]] artist. Springer's only single as a solo artist, "The Right One Left," was released in 1992 on [[MCA Nashville]] and peaked at No.&nbsp;69 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs|Hot Country Singles & Tracks]] chart.

In 1998, Springer formed the country music trio '''Springer!''' with Shara Johnson and Joe Manuel.<ref name="business">[http://www.allbusiness.com/retail-trade/miscellaneous-retail-retail-stores-not/4627561-1.html Popular Uprisings: Billboard's Weekly Coverage Of Hot Prospects For The Heatseekers Chart]</ref> Their first single, "Don't Try to Find Me," peaked at No.&nbsp;64 on the Hot Country Singles & Tracks chart.<ref>[{{AllMusic|class=album|id=r424068/charts-awards|pure_url=yes}} allmusic ((( The Roger Springer Band > Charts & Awards > Billboard Singles )))]</ref> A second single, "Ain't Nothing but a Cloud," was released in 1999 but failed to chart. Renamed '''The Roger Springer Band''', [[Giant Records (Warner Bros. subsidiary label)|Giant]] released the trio's eponymous debut album in July 1999 shortly before they disbanded.

As a songwriter, Springer has co-written single releases by [[Mark Chesnutt]] ("I Might Even Quit Lovin' You," "[[It's a Little Too Late (Mark Chesnutt song)|It's a Little Too Late]]," "Let It Rain," "Thank God for Believers"), [[Sammy Kershaw]] ("Matches")<ref name="business" /><ref>[{{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p317853/songs/songs-composed-by|pure_url=yes}} allmusic ((( Roger Springer > Songs > Songs Composed By )))]</ref> and [[Love and Theft (band)|Love and Theft]] ("[[Dancing in Circles]]").
