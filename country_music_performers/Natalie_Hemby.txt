'''Natalie Nicole Hemby Wrucke''' (born March 24, 1977) is an American [[country music]] songwriter and singer. She has written songs for [[Lee Ann Womack]], [[Eli Young Band]], [[Toby Keith]], [[Miranda Lambert]], [[Sunny Sweeney]], [[Little Big Town]], [[Jon Pardi]], [[Kacey Musgraves]], [[Kelly Clarkson]], [[Lauren Daigle]], and [[Lady Gaga]]. In 2019, she joined the quartet [[The Highwomen]] alongside [[Brandi Carlile]], [[Amanda Shires]] and [[Maren Morris]].

==Early life==
Hemby was born in [[Bloomington, Illinois]].<ref name="New Names">{{Cite news|url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/47735838/new-names/ |title=New Names |newspaper=[[The Pantagraph]] |page=38 |date=March 30, 1977 |access-date=March 31, 2020 |via=Newspapers.com}}</ref>
She is the daughter of Nashville studio guitarist Tom Hemby and Deanna Hemby.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/songwriter-spotlight-natalie-hemby-84446/ |title=Songwriter Spotlight: Natalie Hemby  |last=Hight |first=Jewly |date=August 20, 2014 |magazine=Rolling Stone |access-date=September 15, 2019}}</ref>

== Career ==
=== Songwriting ===
Hemby has accumulated eight No. 1 ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' singles during her career. Hemby's cuts include "[[White Liar]]" and "[[Only Prettier]]" by [[Miranda Lambert]],<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.ccmmagazine.com/article/songwriting-tips-with-natalie-hemby/ |title=Songwriting Tips with Natalie Hemby |last=Hemby |first=Natalie |date=June 8, 2011 |website=CCM Magazine |access-date=May 9, 2013}}</ref> "[[Pontoon (song)|Pontoon]]" and "[[Tornado (song)|Tornado]]" by [[Little Big Town]],<ref name="emi" /> "[[Drinks After Work (song)|Drinks After Work]]" by [[Toby Keith]] and "[[Automatic (Miranda Lambert song)|Automatic]]" by [[Miranda Lambert]]. She is currently a writer at Universal Music Group Nashville (UMPG), and has formerly been affiliated with [[EMI Publishing]] and [[Carnival Music]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.musicrow.com/2011/10/natalie-hemby-finds-new-publishing-home/ |title=Natalie Hemby Finds New Publishing Home |last=Skates |first=Sarah |date=October 31, 2011 |website=Music Row |access-date=May 9, 2013}}</ref>

===Solo albums===
On January 13, 2017, Hemby released her first studio album, ''[[Puxico (Natalie Hemby album)|Puxico]]'', named after the [[Puxico, Missouri|Missouri town]] where her grandfather lived, via the label GetWrucke Productions which she runs with her husband, music producer Mike Wrucke.<ref>{{Cite magazine |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/country/news/miranda-lambert-songwriter-natalie-hemby-readies-new-album-w452536 |title=Miranda Lambert Songwriter Natalie Hemby Readies New Album |last=Moss |first=Marissa R. |date=November 28, 2016 |magazine=Rolling Stone |access-date=January 5, 2017}}</ref>

In February 2021, Hemby signed with [[Fantasy Records]]. Produced by [[Mike Wrucke]], her first album on Fantasy, ''Pins and Needles'', was released on October 8, 2021.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.popmatters.com/natalie-hemby-2021-basement-east |title=Natalie Hemby Celebrates 'Pins and Needles' Drop At Nashville's Basement East |last=Quinn |first=Rick |date=October 18, 2021 |website=PopMatters.com |access-date=January 11, 2022}}</ref>

===The Highwomen===
Hemby was revealed as the final member of [[The Highwomen]], a country music group that already featured [[Brandi Carlile]], [[Maren Morris]] and [[Amanda Shires]], on April 1, 2019, when the group performed live for the first time at the [[Bridgestone Arena]] as part of an 87th birthday tribute concert for [[Loretta Lynn]].<ref>{{Cite magazine |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/highwomen-brandi-carlile-loretta-lynn-birthday-concert-816499/ |title=See the Highwomen's Live Debut at Loretta Lynn Birthday Show |first=Marissa R. |last=Moss |magazine=[[Rolling Stone]] |date=April 2, 2019 |access-date=March 31, 2020}}</ref> "Redesigning Women" was released on July 19, 2019, as the first single from their self-titled debut album set for release on September 6, 2019.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://tasteofcountry.com/the-highwomen-redesigning-women-video/ |title=The Highwomen Light the Ultimate Bonfire in 'Redesigning Women' Video |first=Cillea |last=Houghton |website=[[Taste of Country]] |date=July 19, 2019 |access-date=March 31, 2020}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Hemby is married to record producer Mike Wrucke.<ref name="emi">{{Cite web |url=http://www.emimusicpub.com/global-offices/us-nashville/songwriters/natalie-hemby.php |title=Natalie Hemby |year=2015 |website=EMI Music Publishing |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150219032254/http://www.emimusicpub.com/global-offices/us-nashville/songwriters/natalie-hemby.php |archive-date=February 19, 2015 |access-date=February 9, 2017}}</ref>
