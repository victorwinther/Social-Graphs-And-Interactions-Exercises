'''Nate Smith''' (born September 19, 1985) is an American [[country music]] singer signed to [[Sony Music Nashville]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-11-05|title=Sony Music Nashville Signs Nate Smith|url=https://musicrow.com/2021/11/sony-music-nashville-signs-nate-smith/|access-date=2022-01-24|website=MusicRow.com|language=en-US}}</ref> After his song "Wildfire" went viral on [[TikTok]] in 2020, he signed a publishing deal with [[Sony Music Publishing|Sony/ATV]]. He gained prominence on social media before landing his major label record deal. He was previously signed to [[Word Records]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-12-21|title=Sony Music Nashville C&I Country Roundup - C&I Magazine|url=https://www.cowboysindians.com/2021/12/sony-music-nashville-ci-country-roundup/|access-date=2022-01-24|website=Cowboys and Indians Magazine|language=en-US}}</ref>

== Early life and career ==
Born and raised in Paradise, California, Smith was introduced to performing music by his church youth leader and cites [[Garth Brooks]], [[Tom Petty]], and [[Fleetwood Mac]] as his early influences. After moving to Nashville at age 23, he signed a record deal with Word Records and a publishing deal with [[Centricity Music]]. During this time, he opened for artists including [[Brett Eldredge]], [[Eli Young Band]], and [[X Ambassadors]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-10-16|title=Sony/ATV Nashville Signs Nate Smith To Worldwide Deal|url=https://musicrow.com/2020/10/sony-atv-nashville-signs-nate-smith-to-worldwide-deal/|access-date=2022-01-24|website=MusicRow.com|language=en-US}}</ref> Nate will be performing at "Wild West Festival" in Hays Kansas in summer of 2024.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2024-06-15|title=Wild West Festival|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20240615233044/https://wildwestfestival.com/|access-date=2024-06-24|website=Wild West Festival|language=en-us}}</ref>

After returning to Paradise, California, in 2018, Smith's home burned down in the [[Camp Fire (2018)|Butte County Camp Fire]].<ref>{{Cite news|last=Barber|first=Molly|title=One Of These Days, Our Interview with Nate Smith|work=Mountain Valley Living|url=https://www.mountainvalleyliving.com/2019/01/one-of-these-days-our-interview-with-nathaniel-smith/}}</ref> The tragedy inspired him to write his breakout song "One of These Days". News of the release was reported by local press and several national outlets including [[NPR]], which encouraged Smith to return to Nashville and continue pursuing music.<ref>{{Cite news|title=A Song Of Tribute To The Lost Town Of Paradise|language=en|work=NPR.org|url=https://www.npr.org/2018/12/13/676226073/a-song-of-tribute-to-the-lost-town-of-paradise|access-date=2022-01-24}}</ref>

After relocating, Smith released his next single, "Wildfire". A clip of the performer singing his track went viral on the social media platform TikTok, gaining over 3 million views.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Natesmithcountry on TikTok|url=https://www.tiktok.com/@natesmithmusic/video/7007486587781123333?lang=en&is_copy_url=1&is_from_webapp=v1|access-date=2022-01-24|website=TikTok|language=en}}</ref> A few months later, in November 2021, Smith signed a record deal with Sony Music Nashville and dropped his major label debut, "Raised Up", which he co-wrote with hit songwriter Jonathan Smith. He soon followed it with "I Don't Wanna Go to Heaven".<ref>{{Cite web|last=Nicholson|first=Jessica|date=2021-11-19|title=First Country: Chris Stapleton, Adele, Cole Swindell, Lainey Wilson, Gabby Barrett|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/first-country-chris-stapleton-adele-cole-swindell-lainey-wilson-1234999428/|access-date=2022-01-24|website=Billboard|language=en-US}}</ref> The track helped land him on 2022 Artists to Watch lists by ''Country Now'', ''Country Lifestyle Network'' and ''PopWrapped''.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Liptak|first=Carena|date=2021-12-28|title=Country Next: 15 Artists to Watch in 2022|url=https://countrynow.com/country-next-15-artists-to-watch-in-2022/|access-date=2022-01-24|website=Country Now|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Rian|first=Lindsi|date=2022-01-10|title=Music Monday: One's to Watch in 2022|url=https://cowboylifestylenetwork.com/music-monday-ones-to-watch-in-2022/|access-date=2022-01-25|website=Cowboy Lifestyle Network|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Perkins|first=Trevor|title=31 Country Newcomers To Watch In 2022|url=https://popwrapped.com/31-country-newcomers-to-watch-in-2022/}}</ref>

After Arista Nashville closed in March 2023, Smith was reassigned to [[RCA Records]]' Nashville division.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://variety.com/2023/music/news/sony-dissolves-arista-nashville-1235563003/ | title=Sony Music Dissolves Arista Nashville Label | publisher=Variety | date=March 23, 2023 | accessdate=March 27, 2023 | author=Jem Aswad}}</ref> The song "Bulletproof" was released on February 2, 2024 as the lead single from Smith's EP ''Through the Smoke''.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://backstagecountry.com/2024/02/09/nate-smith-delivers-new-single-announces-through-the-smoke-ep/ | title=Nate Smith Delivers New Single, Announces ''Through The Smoke'' EP | publisher=Backstage Country | date=February 9, 2024 | accessdate=May 15, 2024| author=Lauren Hoffman}}</ref> The song was subsequently re-recorded featuring [[Avril Lavigne]]. This version was released on May 16, 2024.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://newcountry1039fm.iheart.com/content/2024-05-15-nate-smith-texts-avril-lavigne-with-a-crazy-idea-see-the-conversation/ | title=Nate Smith Texts Avril Lavigne With A 'Crazy Idea' — See The Conversation | publisher=[[WZDA|New Country 103.9]] | date=May 15, 2024 | accessdate=May 15, 2024| author=Kelly Fisher}}</ref>
