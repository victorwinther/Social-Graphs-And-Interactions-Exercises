'''Anna Dasha Novotny''' (born February 27, 2000) is an American country and pop singer-songwriter. She has released one EP, ''$hiny Things'' (2021), and two studio albums: ''Dirty Blonde'' (2023) and ''What Happens Now?'' (2024). Her song "[[Austin (Dasha song)|Austin]]" peaked at No. 18 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] in 2024.

==Biography==
===Early life and education===
Dasha was born and raised in [[San Luis Obispo, California]],<ref name=herhit/> and is of [[Czechs|Czech]] descent. When she was 5 years old, she started participating in [[musical theater]] productions. She danced ballet and hip hop. She won national poetry competitions in school and then learned to play the guitar and piano at age 8. Her dad was her first [[talent manager]] and booked gigs for her in coffee shops and wineries at age 10. Her brother, who is a guitarist for [[Beauty School Dropout (band)|Beauty School Dropout]], helped produce her music. She released her first song when she was 13 years old after her father gave her a small recording budget.<ref name=herhit>{{cite news | url=https://www.today.com/popculture/music/who-is-dasha-austin-rcna146713 |last=Wood |first=Becca |title=Dasha performs her hit 'Austin' at the CMT Music Awards — her first time visiting the Texas city | work=[[Today (American TV program)|Today]] | date=April 8, 2024}}</ref><ref name=lover>{{Cite magazine | url=https://www.billboard.com/music/country/dasha-austin-song-tiktok-dance-chartbreaker-may-2024-interview-1235675010/ | title=Chartbreaker: Dasha Left a Lover in the Dust — and Lassoed a Country-Pop Breakout With 'Austin' | first=Jessica | last=Nicholson | magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | date=May 7, 2024}}</ref>

She attended [[San Luis Obispo High School]], where she performed in ''[[Guys and Dolls]]'' (2016), ''[[Footloose (musical)|Footloose]]'' (2017), ''[[Annie (musical)|Annie]]'' (2018), and ''[[Bye Bye Birdie]]'' (2018) and was also a member of the Improv Team.<ref>{{Cite news | url=https://sites.google.com/slcusd.org/drama/players/alumni/anovotny | title=Dasha Novotny | work=[[San Luis Obispo High School]]}}</ref> She then attended [[Belmont University]] until the onset of the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], when she [[dropped out]] to pursue her music career.<ref>{{cite magazine |url=https://www.billboard.com/business/record-labels/austin-dasha-signs-warner-records-1235640308/ |last=Nicholson |first=Jessica |title='Austin' Hitmaker Dasha Signs to Warner Records  | magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | date=March 25, 2024}}</ref>

===Career===
Dasha released the single "Don't Mean a Thing" through Famouz Records in April 2020.<ref name=lover/> Later that year, she signed with Quadio Records, a subsidiary of [[Disruptor Records]].<ref name=lover/> "Don't Mean a Thing" and the follow up singles "None of My Business", "$hiny Things", and "21st Birthday" appeared on the 2021 [[extended play]] ''$hiny Things'' released through Quadio Records.<ref name=lover/>

In 2021 and 2022, Dasha released singles for her debut album ''Dirty Blonde'' (2023).<ref name=lover/>

In November 2023, Dasha released the single "[[Austin (Dasha song)|Austin]]", a foray into [[country music]] which marked her first entry on the ''[[Billboard charts]]'' and peaked at number 19 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] in June 2024. The song was also successful worldwide, peaking within the top ten of the charts in Belgium (Flanders), Ireland, the Netherlands, Norway, and the United Kingdom.<ref>{{cite magazine |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/chart-beat/billboard-charts-first-timers-dasha-yard-act-ole-60-1235632307/ |last=Zellner |first=Xander |title=10 First-Timers on Billboard's Charts This Week: Dasha, Yard Act, Ole 60, Charlotte Cardin & More  | magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] | date=March 13, 2024}}</ref> That month, she signed with [[Warner Records]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://musicrow.com/2024/03/dasha-signs-with-warner-records/ 
 | last=Hollabaugh |first=Lorie |title=Dasha Signs With Warner Records | work=[[MusicRow]] |date=March 25, 2024}}</ref> Her second full-length album, ''What Happens Now?'' was released in February 2024. In April 2024, she performed "Austin" live at the 2024 CMT Awards in Austin, Texas.<ref name=herhit/>
In August 2024 she performed “Austin” live at Allsång på Skansen in Sweden. In September 2024, she will make her first award show performance at the [[2024 Canadian Country Music Awards]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ca.billboard.com/music/awards/kd-lang-reclines-reunion|title=k.d. lang and The Reclines To Reunite After 35 Years At The 2024 CCMA Awards|first=Kerry|last=Doole|work=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] Canada|date=September 4, 2024|access-date=September 11, 2024}}</ref>
