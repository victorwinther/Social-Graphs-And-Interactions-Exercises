'''Jameson Rodgers''' (born October 17, 1987) is an American [[country music]] singer and songwriter signed to [[Columbia Nashville]]/River House, whose debut single "Some Girls" charted on the Country Airplay chart.

==Early life==
Jameson Rodgers grew up in [[Batesville, Mississippi]], United States.  Rodgers loved baseball and music and played baseball at Northwest MS Community College before finishing college at [[University of Southern Mississippi]].  Rodgers started to write songs and perform, eventually developing a local following along the way.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://megacountry.livenation.com/news/13936-new-artist-spotlight-jameson-rodgers-006|title=New Artist Spotlight: Jameson Rodgers - News|website=Megacountry.livenation.com|accessdate=13 March 2019}}</ref> He later moved to Nashville in 2010 with his friend and never left.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.raisedrowdy.com/2018/03/07/jamesoninterview/|title=Jameson Rodger's gritty, truthful voice and well crafted lyrics have his star shooting straight for the sky (Interview)|date=7 March 2018|website=Raisedrowdy.com|accessdate=13 March 2019}}</ref>

==Career==
In 2014, Rodgers received The [[ASCAP]] Foundation Leon Brettler Award and landed a publishing deal with Combustion Music. In 2016, he released his first EP and two years later, a self-titled EP in 2018, which featured the hit "[[Some Girls (Jameson Rodgers song)|Some Girl]]s". The song was featured on [[SiriusXM]]'s The Highway and has over 80 million streams.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://theshotgunseat.com/interview-jameson-rodgers-translates-strong-work-ethic-success-music-city/|title=Interview: Jameson Rodgers Translates A Strong Work Ethic Into Success in Music City|date=19 August 2018|website=Theshotgunseat.com|accessdate=13 March 2019}}</ref> Rodgers has co-penned Platinum-selling hits for [[Florida Georgia Line]] (Top 10 single "[[Talk You Out of It]]") and [[Chris Lane]] (No. 1 hit "[[I Don't Know About You]]"), along with "Camouflage Hat" on [[Jason Aldean]]'s 2019 album ''[[9 (Jason Aldean album)|9]]'' and the title track of Luke Bryan's 2020 album ''[[Born Here Live Here Die Here]]''. Jameson won the 2020 MusicRow Discovery Artist of the Year award. Rodgers spent 2019 supporting Luke Combs on his Beer Never Broke My Heart Tour, playing more than 60 arenas across the US and Canada.

[[Pandora Radio]] named Jameson Rodgers one of 2018 Country Artists to Watch.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://newyork.cbslocal.com/2019/03/01/jameson-rodgers-acm-awards-luke-combs/|title=Jameson Rodgers On ACM Award Nomination: 'It Would Be A Dream Come True'|date=1 March 2019|website=Newyork.cbslocal.com|accessdate=13 March 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://b985.fm/b98-5-new-country-spotlight-jameson-rodgers/|title=B98.5 New Country Spotlight: Jameson Rodgers|first1=Quinn|last1=Alex|website=B985.fm|date=10 September 2018 |accessdate=13 March 2019}}</ref>

In 2019, Rodgers signed a recording deal with River House Artists/[[Columbia Nashville]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://musicrow.com/2019/01/jameson-rodgers-signs-with-river-house-columbia-nashville-as-they-announce-long-term-joint-venture/|title=Jameson Rodgers Signs With River House/Columbia Nashville As They Announce Long-Term Joint Venture|date=10 January 2019|website=Musicrow.com|accessdate=13 March 2019}}</ref>

In 2020, he achieved his first charting song on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' charts; his debut hit "Some Girls" reached number one on the [[Country Airplay]] chart and number 29 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]].
