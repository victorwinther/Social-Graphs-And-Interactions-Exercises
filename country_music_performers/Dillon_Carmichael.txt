'''Dillon Carmichael''' (born November 8, 1993) is an American [[country music]] singer. He signed to Riser House Entertainment in 2017 <ref>{{Cite web|date=2017-09-28|title=Dillon Carmichael Signs Publishing And Artist Deal With Riser House, Preps Debut With Dave Cobb|url=https://musicrow.com/2017/09/dillon-carmichael-signs-publishing-and-artist-deal-with-riser-house-preps-debut-with-dave-cobb/|access-date=2021-10-25|website=MusicRow.com|language=en-US}}</ref> where he released his debut single, "Dancing Away With My Heart".  In 2021 Carmichael released the Hot Beer EP, which included the title single.<ref name="musicrow.com">{{Cite web|date=2021-03-22|title=Dillon Carmichael To Release 'Hot Beer' EP, Out May 14|url=https://musicrow.com/2021/03/dillon-carmichael-to-release-hot-beer-ep-out-may-14/|access-date=2021-10-25|website=MusicRow.com|language=en-US}}</ref> Later in 2021, Carmichael delivered his second album, ''Son of A'', consisting of fourteen tracks with songs written by [[Hardy (singer)|Michael Hardy]] and [[Ashley Gorley]]. As well as songs written with [[Jessi Alexander]], and [[David Lee Murphy]].<ref name="musicrow.com"/>

==Biography==
Carmichael grew up in [[Burgin, Kentucky]]. He came from a musical family that included several other musicians, including two of his uncles: singers [[John Michael Montgomery]] and Eddie Montgomery, the latter a member of [[Montgomery Gentry]].<ref name="allmusic"/>

Carmichael began playing guitar at age twelve, and joined his friends at talent shows in his area. At age 17, he and his family were contacted by a representative of The Song Factory publishing company, who encouraged Carmichael to move to [[Nashville, Tennessee]] after he had graduated from high school. In 2018, he was recommended to record producer [[Dave Cobb]], who produced Carmichael's debut album ''Hell on an Angel'' in 2018.<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web | url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/dillon-carmichael-mn0003756530/biography | title=Dillon Carmichael biography | publisher=AllMusic | access-date=March 9, 2020}}</ref> On August 21, 2018, Carmichael made his Grand Ole Opry debut.  He had spent a year and half as a security guard at the Opry from 2015-2016."<ref>{{cite magazine | url=https://www.billboard.com/music/country/dillon-carmichael-opry-debut-8482429/ | title=Dillon Carmichael Chats About His Grand Ole Opry Debut In Heartfelt Video| magazine=Billboard | date=October 30, 2018 | author=Gary Graff}}</ref> In addition to playing at the Grand Ole Opry, Carmichael has played multiple festivals and toured with [[Brooks & Dunn]]<ref>{{cite magazine | url= https://www.kixbrooksradio.com/2022/05/27/dillon-carmichael-hitting-the-road-with-brothers-osborne-brooks-dunn-kip-moore-cody-johnson/ | title=Dillon Carmichael Hitting the Road with Brothers Osborne, Brooks & Dunn, Kip Moore, & Cody Johnson| magazine=KixBrooksRadio | date=May 27, 2022| author=jwills}}</ref> and [[Lynyrd Skynyrd]]<ref>{{cite magazine | url= https://glidemagazine.com/217686/lynyrd-skynyrd-brings-last-of-the-survivors-farewell-tour-to-biloxi-show-review-photos/ | title=Lynyrd Skynyrd Brings Last Of The Survivors Farewell Tour To Biloxi | magazine=Glide Magazine | date=December 9, 2018| author=Leslie Michelle Derrough}}</ref>

''[[Rolling Stone]]'' Country described the album as "Featuring his signature booming baritone vocals and a sonic blend that mixes the hard-nosed Outlaw country with the melodic edge of Southern rock."<ref>{{cite magazine | url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/dillon-carmichael-preps-debut-album-hell-on-an-angel-shares-title-track-630348/ | title=Dillon Carmichael Preps Debut Album 'Hell on an Angel,' Shares Title Track | magazine=Rolling Stone | date=April 26, 2018 | access-date=March 9, 2020 | author=Chris Parton}}</ref> In 2020, Dillon charted on Country Airplay with the single release "I Do for You".<ref name="billboard"/>
