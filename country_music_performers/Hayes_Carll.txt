'''Joshua Hayes Carll''' (born January 9, 1976),<ref>Family Tree Legends [http://www.familytreelegends.com/records/20902?c=search&first=Joshua&last=Carll Joshua Hayes Carll]</ref> known professionally as '''Hayes Carll''', is a singer-songwriter. A native of [[The Woodlands, Texas|The Woodlands]],<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.chron.com/neighborhood/article/Woodlands-man-signed-by-Lost-Highway-Records-9579768.php|title=Woodlands man signed by Lost Highway Records|first=Tiffany|last=Williams|date=April 24, 2006|newspaper=[[Houston Chronicle]]}}</ref> Texas, his style of roots-oriented songwriting has been noted for its plainspoken poetry and sarcastic humor.

==Career==
After releasing his debut album, ''Flowers & Liquor,'' in 2002, Carll was voted Best New Act by the ''Houston Press''.<ref>{{cite news|title=Hot Tunes, Summer in the City Houston Press Music Awards Showcase 2002|url=http://www.houstonpress.com/content/printVersion/226601/|newspaper=Houston Press|date=July 18, 2002}}</ref> Since then he's been compared to other Texas songwriters, including [[Townes Van Zandt]], who he said "ruined me and saved me at the same time."<ref>{{cite book|last=Atkinson|first=Brian T.|title=I'll Be Here in the Morning: The Songwriting Legacy of Townes Van Zandt|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=2WUB2VkYegkC&pg=PR16|year=2011|publisher=Texas A&M University Press|isbn=9781603445276|page=xvi}}</ref> He released his second album, ''Little Rock'', in 2004. Produced by R.S. Field, ''Little Rock'' was the first self-released album to reach No. 1 on the Americana Chart. Carll signed with Lost Highway records in 2006 and released his next album, ''Trouble in Mind'', in 2008. It was ranked No. 60 of the year by ''[[The Village Voice]]''.<ref name="Pazz + Jop 2008 Albums">{{cite web |work=[[The Village Voice]] |date=January 2, 2009 |title=Pazz + Jop Albums 2008 |url=http://www.villagevoice.com/pazznjop/Albums/2008/ |access-date=January 21, 2009 |archive-date=October 16, 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121016185209/http://www.villagevoice.com/pazznjop/albums/2008/ |url-status=dead }}</ref> "She Left Me For Jesus," which appeared on ''Trouble in Mind'', was the [[Americana Music Association#Song of the Year|Americana Music Association Song of the Year 2008]].<ref name="Americana Music Honors & Awards">{{cite web |work=[[Americana Music Association]] |date=June 15, 2009|title=Americana Music Honors & Awards |url=https://americanamusic.org/awards/2008 |access-date=August 22, 2023 }}</ref>  Four songs by Carll appeared in the 2010 film ''[[Country Strong]]'', three of which also appeared on the film's second soundtrack album, ''[[Country Strong: More Music from the Motion Picture]]''.

''KMAG YOYO & Other American Stories''  was released in 2011. to record ''Lovers and Leavers'' and then released the album in April 2016 on his own Highway 87 Records to great critical acclaim.

Carll was nominated for a [[Grammy Award]] in 2016, [[Best Country Song]], for "Chances Are".<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title = Songs We Love: Hayes Carll, 'The Love That We Need'|url = https://www.npr.org/2016/01/19/463184888/songs-we-love-hayes-carll-the-love-that-we-need|website = NPR.org|access-date = February 22, 2016|first = Jewly|last = Hight}}</ref> In 2019, he released his sixth album, ''What It Is'', on Dualtone Records. He re-recorded many of his songs, including duets with [[Ray Wylie Hubbard]] and his wife, [[Allison Moorer]], for 2020's ''Alone Together Sessions''. His latest album, ''You Get It All'', was released on October 29, 2021 on Dualtone Records.
