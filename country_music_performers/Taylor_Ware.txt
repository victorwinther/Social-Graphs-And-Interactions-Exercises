'''Taylor Marie Ware''' (born September 17, 1994) is an American singer and [[yodel]]er from [[Franklin, Tennessee|Franklin]], [[Tennessee]].

Before Ware knew how to yodel, she performed at a county fair at age four.<ref name="AP 2006">{{cite news |title=Girl hopes to yodel her way to $1 million |url=https://www.spokesman.com/stories/2006/aug/16/girl-hopes-to-yodel-her-way-to-1-million/ |access-date=30 June 2021 |work=[[The Spokesman-Review]] |agency=Associated Press |date=August 16, 2006}}</ref><ref name="Stroud 2008"/> Her talent was singing and playing a [[fiddle]].<ref name="AP 2006"/> When she was six she decided to sing to seniors, so she started an Adopt-a-Grandparent program. She taught herself to yodel from an audiotape and instruction book when she was seven years old,<ref name="AP 2006"/> after going to a music convention with country singer Naomi Hills.<ref name="Stroud 2008">{{cite news |last1=Stroud |first1=Rob |title=Young yodeler's career began at fair |url=https://jg-tc.com/news/young-yodelers-career-began-at-fair/article_1c2c3aac-8744-50e1-aede-2f830c182a51.html |access-date=30 June 2021 |work=Journal Gazette & Times Courier |date=June 26, 2008}}</ref>

In 2003, at age 9, Ware won $10,000 in the [[Yahoo!]] Yodel Challenge and the Sonic Search for a Star.<ref name="Yancey 2006">{{cite news |last1=Yancey |first1=Jessy |title=Hometown rallies around 11-year-old yodeler |url=http://www.williamsonherald.com/news/hometown-rallies-around-11-year-old-yodeler/article_f6c6f6d4-929f-5adf-a450-5504472269f1.html |access-date=30 June 2021 |work=Williamson Herald |date=August 2, 2006 |quote=Updated Apr 7, 2014}}</ref> She was later featured in a Yahoo commercial.<ref>{{cite web |title=TAYLOR WARE - YAHOO YODEL Commercial |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZSTtG1qYams  |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211219/ZSTtG1qYams |archive-date=2021-12-19 |url-status=live|website=YouTube | date=3 September 2007 |access-date=30 June 2021}}{{cbignore}}</ref> She also participated in a challenge to beat a [[Guinness world record]]. Ware performed on the ''[[Grand Ole Opry]]'' with [[Riders in the Sky (band)|Riders in the Sky]] when she was nine years old.<ref name="Yancey 2006"/>

When Ware was nine, she was given a dog as a gift by [[Wayne Brady]] after she appeared on his now defunct television show, ''[[The Wayne Brady Show]]''. She named the dog Brady after him.

Ware honed her yodeling skills while taking lessons from country music star [[Margo Smith]]<ref name="Stroud 2008" /> who is known worldwide as the Tennessee Yodeler.

At age eleven, she competed on [[NBC]]'s ''[[America's Got Talent (season 1)|America's Got Talent]]'' and advanced to the final competition (which aired on August 16, 2006).<ref name="AP 2006"/> She was one of the top three finalists<ref name="LancasterOnline 2006">{{cite news |last1=Espenshade |first1=Linda |title=Young talent makes big impression on behalf of yodeling |url=https://lancasteronline.com/features/young-talent-makes-big-impression-on-behalf-of-yodeling/article_a9dbce46-a379-5019-b765-80d7e1b8f4f0.html |access-date=30 June 2021 |work=LancasterOnline |date=September 18, 2006 |quote=Updated Sep 11, 2013}}</ref> but she lost to 11-year-old [[Bianca Ryan]], the million-dollar winner and two runners up, The Millers and All That. Ware was an opening act at the [[Liberty Bowl]] for singer [[LeAnn Rimes]] in [[Memphis, Tennessee]] on December 29, 2006, performing the [[national anthem]].<ref name="Tatko-Peterson 2009"/>

Ware released her self-titled debut album in 2004. In 2007, she released her second album, titled ''America's Yodeling Sweetheart.''<ref name="Tatko-Peterson 2009">{{cite news |last1=Tatko-Peterson |first1=Ann |title='America's Got Talent' finalists: Who's found success? |url=https://www.mercurynews.com/2009/09/11/americas-got-talent-finalists-whos-found-success/ |access-date=1 July 2021 |work=The Mercury News |date=September 11, 2009}}</ref>

She appeared in an episode of ''[[Laguna Beach: The Real Orange County|Laguna Beach]]'' as herself.<ref name="Tatko-Peterson 2009"/><ref>[http://www.tv.com/taylor-ware/person/452052/summary.html?q=Taylor%20Ware&tag=search_results;more;0 Taylor Ware - TV.com<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> She also has appeared in commercials for Beech Bend Park in Bowling Green, Kentucky. Ware's younger brother, Harrison, is also a singer and yodeler. He has performed onstage at the [[Tennessee State Fair]].
