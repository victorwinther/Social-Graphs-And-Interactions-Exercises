'''James Darrell Scott''', known as '''Darrell Scott''' (born August 6, 1959),<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/darrell-scott-mn0000566087 |title=Darrell Scott &#124; Music Biography, Credits and Discography |website=[[AllMusic]] |date=August 6, 1959 |access-date=July 16, 2014}}</ref> is an American singer-songwriter and multi-instrumentalist.  The son of musician Wayne Scott, he moved as a child to East Gary, Indiana (known today as [[Lake Station, Indiana|Lake Station]], Indiana). He was playing professionally by his teens in Southern California. Later, Darrell moved to Toronto then Boston. He attended [[Tufts University]], where he studied poetry and literature. He has lived in Nashville, Tennessee, since about 1995.  He has written several mainstream [[country music|country]] hits, and he has also established himself as one of [[Nashville, Tennessee|Nashville]]'s premier session instrumentalists.  

==Musical career==
[[File:BandOfJoy.jpg|thumb|250px|left|Scott (second-right) with [[Band of Joy]] at [[Birmingham Symphony Hall]], October 27, 2010]]
Scott has collaborated with [[Steve Earle]], [[Sam Bush]], [[Emmylou Harris]], [[John Cowan]], [[Verlon Thompson]], [[Guy Clark]], [[Tim O'Brien (musician)|Tim O'Brien]], [[Kate Rusby]], [[Jimmie Dale Gilmore]], [[Mary Gauthier]], [[Dan Tyminski]], and many others.  His music has attracted a growing fanbase, and he tours regularly with his own band.  His album, ''Crooked Road'', was released May 25, 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/a-crooked-road-mw0001985519 |title=A Crooked Road – Darrell Scott &#124; Songs, Reviews, Credits, Awards |website=AllMusic |date=May 25, 2010 |access-date=July 16, 2014}}</ref>
In early 2005, Scott's ''Theatre of the Unheard'' won in The 4th Annual Independent Music Awards for Album of the Year.<ref>[http://www.independentmusicawards.com/ima_new/jukeboxes2005.asp] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090702234906/http://www.independentmusicawards.com/ima_new/jukeboxes2005.asp|date=July 2, 2009}}</ref>

He won the 2007 [[Americana Music Honors & Awards|''Song of the Year'' award]] from the [[Americana Music Association]] for his song "Hank William's Ghost"<ref>[http://americanamusic.org/index.htm?id=18347&sid=18331] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100613002019/http://americanamusic.org/index.htm?id=18347&sid=18331|date=June 13, 2010}}</ref> which appears on his album ''The Invisible Man'' released in 2006.

In 2010, he was announced as part of the [[Band of Joy]], alongside [[Robert Plant]], credited as performing vocals, mandolin, guitar, accordion, pedal, lap steel and banjo.

In 2010, [[Brad Paisley]]'s cover of the song "You'll Never Leave Harlan Alive" was the closing song played on the TV drama [[Justified (TV series)|Justified]] during the final scene of the final episode of the first season. It was used again in the final episode of the second season.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tv.com/shows/justified/forums/youll-never-leave-harlan-alive-95927-3095548/ |title=Justified: "You'll Never Leave Harlan Alive" |website=TV.com |access-date=July 16, 2014}}</ref> The fourth season's final episode used a version by [[Dave Alvin]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.aceweekly.com/2013/04/justified-season-4-finale-episode-13-ghosts/ |title=Justified Season 4 Finale, Episode 13 Ghosts |date=April 3, 2013 |publisher=Ace Weekly |access-date=July 16, 2014}}</ref> The fifth season's final episode used a version by the [[Ruby Friedman]] Orchestra.<ref>{{cite web|last=Sepinwall |first=Alan |url=http://www.hitfix.com/whats-alan-watching/season-finale-review-justified-restitution-daddys-home |title=Season finale review: Justified – Restitution: Daddys home? |website=Hitfix.com |date=April 8, 2014 |access-date=July 16, 2014}}</ref> The final episode of the series featured the original composition by Darrell Scott himself.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Bianculli|first1=David|title='Justified' Ends With An Unpredictable, Poetic And Memorable Finale|url=https://www.npr.org/2015/04/15/399842184/justified-ends-with-an-unpredictable-poetic-and-memorable-finale|website=Npr.org|access-date=April 16, 2018|date=April 15, 2015}}</ref>

In January 2011, his album ''A Crooked Road'' won the award for the Country Album category from The 10th Annual Independent Music Awards.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.independentmusicawards.com/ima/artist/darrell-scott |title=Darrell Scott |website=Independentmusicawards.com |access-date=July 16, 2014}}</ref>
