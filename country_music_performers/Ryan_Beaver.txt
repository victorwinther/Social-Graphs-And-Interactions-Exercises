'''Ryan Embry Beaver''' (born March 17, 1984)<ref name="Public Records">{{cite web |title=U.S. Public Records Index |url=https://familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:V8P9-14C |author=Texas Birth Index |year=2002 |publisher=Family Search |accessdate=May 18, 2016}}</ref> is an American country music singer-songwriter from [[Emory, Texas]].  Born on March 17, 1984, and growing up in East Texas,<ref name="americansongwriter">[http://www.americansongwriter.com/2012/11/daily-discovery-ryan-beaver-how-about-you/ Daily Discovery: Ryan Beaver, "How About You" | American Songwriter<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> Beaver moved South and began his musical career while he was attending [[Texas State University]] in [[San Marcos, Texas]]. Beaver currently lives in [[Nashville, Tennessee]].

== Career ==
Wrote the #1 song "[[Pretty Little Poison]]" performed by [[Warren Zeiders]].  It was the first number one for both of them.

Beaver's first album, ''Under the Neons'', was released on February 27, 2008.<ref name="itunes.apple">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/artist/ryan-beaver/id275409231 |title=iTunes – Music – Ryan Beaver |publisher=Itunes.apple.com |date= |accessdate=2012-08-19}}</ref> The album spawned 3 singles for Texas radio: "I Shoulda Kissed You", "Under the Neons", and "Streets of Austin".

Beaver's second album, ''Constant'', was released February 8, 2011.<ref name="itunes.apple" />  David Grissom produced the album, with co-write credits for himself, [[Wade Bowen]], Brian Keane, Ben Danaher, and Paul Eason.  "For A While", "Hate", and "Nobody Wants to be Alone" were the singles sent to radio respectively.  "Hate" became Beaver's highest charting single to date (#2 for 3 weeks) on the Texas Music Chart<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.texasmusicchart.com |title=Texas Music for the Country |publisher=Texasmusicchart.com |date= |accessdate=2012-08-19}}</ref> and earned a "Favorite Song of 2011" nod from galleywinter.com.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://galleywinter.com/favorites-of-2011 |title=Favorites of 2011 &#124; Galleywinter |publisher=Galleywinter.com |date=2011-12-15 |accessdate=2012-08-19 }}{{dead link|date=April 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

Country Weekly described his lyrics as "thoughtful, introspective, and extremely well crafted" in their December 10, 2012, issue.<ref>[http://www.countryweekly.com/music/edge-ryan-beaver On the Edge: Ryan Beaver – Country Weekly<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>

Beaver's first music video for "How About You" was featured on CMT Pure, CMT.com,<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20140114202743/http://www.cmt.com/artists/ryan-beaver/ Ryan Beaver | New Music And Songs | CMT<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> Yallwire.com,<ref>[http://www.yallwire.com/player/ryanbeaverhowaboutyou.html Yallwire Optimal Quality Detector<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> NoDepression.com among others.

== Songwriting ==

A BMI writer, Beaver co-wrote "Along the Way" and "More Than Willing" with [[Rob Baird]] (''I Swear It's The Truth'' released on Carnival Records). He also co-wrote "Day by Day", "Make Or Break Me" and "Leavin' Stephenville" with [[Kyle Park]], the latter two appearing on Park's ''Make Or Break Me'' album released in 2011.  As of 2017, Beaver is signed to the Nashville-based publishing company, SMACKSongs.<ref>[https://musicrow.com/2017/10/smacksongs-expands-publishing-roster/ SMACKSongs Extends Publishing Roster<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>
