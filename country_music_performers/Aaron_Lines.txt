'''Anthony Aaron Lines''' (born November 17, 1977) is a Canadian [[country music|country]] musician. He has recorded for [[RCA Records Nashville|RCA Nashville]], [[BNA Records|BNA]] and On Ramp Records, and has charted three singles on the [[Hot Country Songs]] charts in the United States, in addition to landing one number one hit on the [[Canada Country]] chart.

==Musical history==

===''Love Changes Everything''===
Aaron Lines' debut album, ''Love Changes Everything'', was released in Canada in 2001 by [[independent record label]] Combustion Music. The first two singles, "Love Changes Everything" and "I Can Read Your Heart," both found success on Canadian country radio. Lines was nominated for Best New Country Artist/Group at the 2002 [[Juno Award]]s, and Best New Artist at the 2002 [[Canadian Country Music Association]] (CCMA) Awards.

===''Living Out Loud''===
As Lines' career was taking off in Canada, he set his sights on an American record deal. He performed a showcase for [[RCA Records Nashville|RCA Nashville]] in May 2001. The next day, the label phoned to offer Lines a record deal.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20040301030706/http://www.cmt.com/artists/az/lines_aaron/bio.jhtml CMT.com : Aaron Lines : Biography<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> He immediately began work on his debut album, ''[[Living Out Loud (album)|Living Out Loud]]'', released on January 7, 2003. The album debuted at No.&nbsp;9 on [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'s [[Top Country Albums]] chart, while the first single, "[[You Can't Hide Beautiful]]," reached No.&nbsp;4 on Billboard's [[Hot Country Songs]] chart. The album was a success in Canada as well, and at the 2003 CCMA Awards, Lines received six nominations, including the [[Kraft Foods|Kraft Cheez Whiz]] Fans' Choice Award. When the awards were handed out in September, Lines was named Male Artist of the Year, and also picked up the Rising Star Award.

In 2003, Lines was an opening act for [[Martina McBride]]'s Greatest Hits tour.

===''Waitin' on the Wonderful''===
Aaron Lines' second album, ''[[Waitin' on the Wonderful]]'', was released on September 6, 2005. The title track stalled at No.&nbsp;36 on US Country and the album was not released in the US.  Meanwhile, in Canada, the first three singles from the album all reached the top 10 on the Canadian Country Singles chart. In fact, Lines was the most played country artist on Canadian radio in 2005.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.aaronlines.com/fastfacts.html |title=:: Aaron Lines Official Website ::<!-- Bot generated title --> |access-date=June 13, 2007 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070929033814/http://www.aaronlines.com/fastfacts.html |archive-date=September 29, 2007 |url-status=dead }}</ref> For two years, Lines had the most played song on Canadian country radio – "Waitin' on the Wonderful" in 2005, and "Lights of My Hometown" in 2006.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.aaronlines.com/biography.html |title=:: Aaron Lines Official Website ::<!-- Bot generated title --> |access-date=June 13, 2007 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070929033820/http://www.aaronlines.com/biography.html |archive-date=September 29, 2007 |url-status=dead }}</ref> At the 2006 CCMA Awards, Lines was again nominated for the Kraft Cheez Whiz Fans' Choice Award.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ccma.org/Media/2006August02-02.pdf |title=2006 CCMA Awards Nominees |website=Canadian Country Music Association |accessdate=June 13, 2007 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070928080950/http://www.ccma.org/Media/2006August02-02.pdf |archivedate=September 28, 2007 }}</ref>

===''Moments That Matter''===
Lines moved back from [[Nashville, Tennessee|Nashville]] to Canada in 2006 to work on his third album, ''[[Moments That Matter]]''. The album was released on June 12, 2007, and distributed by [[Fontana Distribution]]. The first single, "[[Cheaper to Keep Her (song)|Cheaper to Keep Her]]," became Lines' first No.&nbsp;1, reaching the top spot in 11 short weeks. The accompanying music video features cameos from [[Rhett Warrener]], [[Darren McCarty]], [[Jamie McLennan]], [[Richie Regehr]], and Lines' good friend [[Paul Brandt]]. ''Moments That Matter'' was nominated for the 2008 [[Juno Award]] for Country Recording of the Year.

===''Sunday Afternoon''===
Aaron Lines' fourth studio album, ''Sunday Afternoon'', was released on March 16, 2010, by On Ramp Records.<ref>{{cite news|title=Aaron Lines Releases "Sunday Afternoon" |url=http://www.oreillyinternational.com/emailblasts/aaronlines_sundayafternoon.htm |accessdate=September 12, 2012 |newspaper=O'Reilly International |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304000743/http://www.oreillyinternational.com/emailblasts/aaronlines_sundayafternoon.htm |archivedate=March 4, 2016 }}</ref> The first single, "Sand" was released in April 2009. The second single "I Haven't Even Heard You Cry" was released on January 6, 2010.
