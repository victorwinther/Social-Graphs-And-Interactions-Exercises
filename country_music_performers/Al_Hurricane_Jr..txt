'''Al Hurricane Jr.''' (born '''Alberto Nelson Sanchez Jr.''' on October 30, 1959<ref name="NMTech">{{cite web|url=http://www.nmtpas.org/node/215 |title=4th of July Celebration – NMT Performing Arts Series |publisher=[[New Mexico Tech]] |accessdate=2014-05-10 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141006145017/http://www.nmtpas.org/node/215 |archivedate=2014-10-06 }}</ref>) is an American singer-songwriter. He is known as "El Godson" of [[New Mexico music]], a nickname which was inherited from his father [[Al Hurricane]], who is considered "The Godfather" of New Mexico music.<ref name="AlibiTributeGodfather">{{cite web |url=http://alibi.com/music/46412/Paying-Tribute-to-the-Godfather-of-New-Mexico-Musi.html|title=Paying Tribute to the Godfather of New Mexico Music – Alibi|date=February 27, 2014 |accessdate=2014-05-10}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.news-bulletin.com/files/archives/36509-12-24-03.html |archive-url=https://archive.today/20140511072059/http://www.news-bulletin.com/files/archives/36509-12-24-03.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=2014-05-11 |title=Godfather of New Mexico at mid-century of career |accessdate=2014-05-10 }}</ref> He has long been his father's protégé, he has created numerous solo albums, and has contributed songs such as "Flor De Las Flores" to New Mexico's unique style of Spanish music.<ref>{{cite AV media |people=Al Hurricane |year=2011 |title=Flor De Las Flores (Live) |trans-title=|medium=Audio recording |url= |accessdate= |time= |location=Live at the Kimo, Vol 1 (album) |publisher= |id= |isbn= |oclc= |quote=I'd like to keep things going here, with a little song, that made my son very popular, "Flor de las Flores" }}</ref>

==Biography==
"Al Hurricane Jr." was born Alberto Nelson Sanchez Jr. to Alberto Nelson Sanchez and Nettie M. Fleming on October 30, 1959, in Albuquerque, New Mexico. He began performing music at the age of five, and performed the song [[Love Potion No. 9 (song)|Love Potion #9]] during his first performance at the [[Albuquerque Civic Auditorium]].<ref name="NMTech"/>
