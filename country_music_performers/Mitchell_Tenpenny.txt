'''James Mitchell Tenpenny III''' (born August 17, 1989) is an American [[country pop]] singer and songwriter from [[Nashville, Tennessee]]. He has released three studio albums and five [[extended play]]s through Riser House Records, with the first being ''[[Telling All My Secrets]]'' released in December 2018. He charted in 2018 with the single "[[Drunk Me]]", released on [[Columbia Records]] Nashville. He also co wrote "[[If the Boot Fits]]" with [[Granger Smith]] and the song made it to the top 10 on country airplay.

==Early life==
Tenpenny was born and raised in [[Nashville, Tennessee]].<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.cmt.com/news/1784017/mitchell-tenpenny-takes-his-turn-center-stage/ |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20170718032217/http://www.cmt.com/news/1784017/mitchell-tenpenny-takes-his-turn-center-stage/ |url-status= dead |archive-date= July 18, 2017 |title=Mitchell Tenpenny Takes His Turn Center Stage | first=Samantha |last=Stephens |date=July 17, 2017 |work=CMT}}</ref>  His grandmother was music publishing executive [[Donna Hilley]].  According to Tenpenny, when he was 11, his grandmother introduced to him songwriters [[Bobby Braddock]] and [[Curly Putman]] and he became interested in songwriting.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.foxnews.com/opinion/2017/05/12/mothers-day-remembering-my-incredible-country-music-grandmother-donna-hilley.html |title=Mother's Day: Remembering my incredible country music grandmother Donna Hilley |first=Mitchell |last=Tenpenny|date= May 12, 2017 |work=Fox News }}</ref>  He grew up listening to country, rock, and R&B, and cited [[Vince Gill]], [[John Mayer]], and [[Michael Jackson]] among his musical influences.<ref name="rollingstone"/>  He attended [[Lipscomb Academy]] in Nashville where he played football. He then enrolled at [[Middle Tennessee State University]] where he originally intended to play college football, but became more interested being in a band.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.mitchell10penny.com/nashvilleborn-and-raised/ |title=Nashville Born and Raised |work=Mitchell10penny.com |date=5 January 2018 }}</ref> He graduated with a music business degree.<ref name="musicrow">{{cite web |url=https://musicrow.com/2014/12/musicrowpics-mitchell-tenpenny/ |title=MusicRowPics: Mitchell Tenpenny |work=Music Row |date= December 18, 2014}}</ref>

==Music career==

Tenpenny released his debut album, titled ''Black Crow'', in 2015. A track, "Canes Creek", featured the bluegrass band [[The SteelDrivers]].<ref name="rollingstone">{{cite magazine |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/premieres/hear-mitchell-tenpenny-jam-with-the-steeldrivers-on-canes-creek-20150428 |title=Hear Mitchell Tenpenny Jam With the SteelDrivers on 'Cane's Creek' |first=Joseph |last=Hudak |date=April 28, 2015 |magazine=Rolling Stone}}</ref>  His debut single was the title track of the album, "Black Crow".<ref name="nashcountry">{{cite web |url=http://www.nashcountrydaily.com/2015/05/04/whos-new-mitchell-tenpenny/ |title=Who's New: Mitchel Tenpenny |first= Tammy |last=Ragusa |date = May 4, 2015|work=Nash Country Daily }}</ref> In his early songwriting career, he wrote a number of songs with artists such as [[Nick Fradiani]];<ref>{{cite magazine |url= http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/pop/7461788/nick-fradiani-hurricane-american-idol-interview |title=Nick Fradiani Talks New Album 'Hurricane' & Writing His Own Music |date=August 5, 2016 |first= Fred|last= Bronson |magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] }}</ref> an early success is the Top 10 song he co-wrote "[[If the Boot Fits]]", recorded by [[Granger Smith]].<ref>{{cite magazine |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/7318592/makin-tracks-granger-smith-if-the-boot-fits-cinderella-country-update |title=Makin' Tracks: Granger Smith's 'If The Boot Fits' Embraces The Cinderella Story |date=March 31, 2016 |first= Tom |last=Roland |magazine=Billboard}}</ref>  He signed a publishing deal with Sony ATV Music Publishing Nashville,<ref name="musicrow"/> and was involved in the documentary film ''The Voyage'' in 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wakeboardingmag.com/blog/features/2014/05/14/the-voyage#page-5 |title=The Voyage |first= Brandon |last=Judd |date=May 14, 2014|work=WakeBoarding }}</ref> He was signed to a newly formed Riser Records in 2016.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.tennessean.com/story/money/real-estate/2016/09/09/music-rows-killen-building-sold-15m-cash/89987450/ |title=Music Row's Killen Building sold for $1.5M cash |first=Getahn |last=Ward|work=Tennessean.com |date= September 9, 2016 }}</ref>

In July 2017, Tenpenny released an EP, ''Linden Ave'', named after the street where his grandmother Donna Hilley used to live.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thecountrynote.com/in-the-news/mitchell-tenpenny-set-to-release-linden-ave-july-14/ |title=Mitchell Tenpenny's "Truck I Drove In High School" Takes Us Down Memory Lane |date=July 23, 2017 |first=Christina|last= Foster |work=The Country Note}}</ref> The EP charted at No. 10 on Billboard's [[Independent Albums]] chart, No. 6 in [[Heatseekers Albums]], and No. 12 in Country Album Sales, with 2,100 copies sold in its first week.<ref>{{cite magazine |url=http://www.billboard.com/biz/search/charts?f[0]=ts_chart_artistname%3Atenpenny&f[1]=ss_bb_type%3Achart_item&type=2&artist=tenpenny |title=Mitchell Tenpenny |magazine=Billboard }}</ref><ref name="roughstock july 2017">{{cite news |url=http://roughstock.com/news/2017/07/42078-top-10-country-albums-chart-july-24-2017 |title=Top 10 Country Albums Chart: July 24, 2017 |first= Matt |last= Bjorke  |date= July 24, 2017 |work=Roughstock }}</ref>

In January 2018, Tenpenny entered into a joint venture with Riser House/Columbia Nashville and released a self-titled EP on February 23, featuring a new single "[[Drunk Me]]".<ref name="hoffman"/>

He released his second album and full-length major label debut album titled ''[[Telling All My Secrets]]'' on December 14, 2018.<ref name="carr">{{cite web |url=http://theboot.com/mitchell-tenpenny-telling-all-my-secrets/ |title=Hear the Title Track of Mitchell Tenpenny's Debut Album, 'Telling All My Secret'|first=Courtney |last=Carr |date=October 10, 2018 |work=The Boot }}</ref>  It debuted at No. 5 on ''Billboard''{{'}}s Top Country Albums chart, and No. 53 on [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]],<ref>{{cite magazine |url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/country-albums/2018-12-29 |title=Top Country Albums |magazine=Billboard |date=December 29, 2018 }}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine |url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/billboard-200/2018-12-29 |title=Billboard 200 |magazine=Billboard |date=December 29, 2018 }}</ref> after accruing 20,000 album equivalent units in its first week.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://sonymusicnashville.com/mitchell-tenpenny-achieves-countrys-biggest-new-artist-debut-of-2018/ |title=Mitchell Tenpenny achieves country's biggest new artist debut of 2018 |date=December 24, 2018 |work=Sony Music}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Tenpenny has been in a long-term relationship with fellow country singer [[Meghan Patrick]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.soundslikenashville.com/music/mitchell-tenpenny-meghan-patrick-neon-christmas-video/|first=Chris|last=Parton|title=Mitchell Tenpenny and Girlfriend Meghan Patrick Have a 'Neon Christmas' |work=Sounds Like Nashville|date=December 8, 2020}}</ref> On November 24, 2021, the two got engaged in the bar where they first met.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://tasteofcountry.com/mitchell-tenpenny-meghan-patrick-engaged/|title= Mitchell Tenpenny and Meghan Patrick Are Engaged|first=Courtney|last=Carr|date=November 26, 2021|work=Taste of Country}}</ref> Tenpenny and Patrick married on October 23, 2022.<ref name="marriage-1">{{cite news |title=Country Stars Mitchell Tenpenny and Meghan Patrick Marry in Rustic Farm Wedding: See the Photos |url=https://people.com/country/mitchell-tenpenny-marries-meghan-patrick-wedding-photos/ |access-date=24 October 2022 |publisher=People |date=24 October 2022}}</ref>
