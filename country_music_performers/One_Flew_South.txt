'''One Flew South''' was an American [[country music]] group composed of Eddie Bush, Chris Roberts, and Royal Reed, all three of whom sing [[lead vocals]] and play [[acoustic guitar]]. The group's first recording was a song for the soundtrack to the 2006 [[Disney]] animated film ''[[The Fox and the Hound 2]]''. In 2007, One Flew South signed to [[Decca Records]], releasing their debut album ''[[Last of the Good Guys]]'' in May 2008. The album featured collaborations with [[Marcus Hummon]] and [[JD Souther]], producing the single "My Kind of Beautiful" which charted on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs]] charts.

==Biography==
[[Image:One Flew South with J.D. Souther - Photo by Anthony Pepitone.jpg|Eddie Bush, Royal Reed, JD Souther, Chris Roberts|thumb|275px|right]]
After a solo career, Royal Reed, a native of [[El Paso, Texas]], met Chris Roberts, a native of [[New Orleans, Louisiana|New Orleans]] in the late 1990s while both were starring in a production of the [[Broadway musical]] ''[[The Civil War (musical)|The Civil War]]'', alongside singer [[Larry Gatlin]] in [[New York City]].<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web |url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p881968|pure_url=yes}} |title=One Flew South biography |accessdate=2008-06-09 |last=Leggett |first=Steve |work=[[Allmusic]]}}</ref> Gatlin, who had once fronted a trio which also included his two brothers (Larry Gatlin & The Gatlin Brothers) mentored Reed and Roberts suggesting they add a third member to complete their vocal-centric group.<ref name="one"/> Initially, they could not find a suitable third member for the trio. Eventually, they befriended [[Marcus Hummon]] with whom they began to write songs.<ref name="one"/> Through Hummon, the two discovered [[South Carolina]] native Eddie Bush who was added to the group in 2005.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.americanchronicle.com/articles/61059 |title=New Artist Spotlight: One Flew South |accessdate=2008-06-09 |last=Doerschuk |first=Robert L. |date=2008-05-07 |work=American Chronicle}}</ref> The three members discovered that they worked well together as a vocal trio, and they chose the name One Flew South.<ref name="that"/> All three sing [[lead vocals]] on their songs.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.charleston.net/news/2008/jun/05/lowcountry_native_musician_just_flew_sou43338/?wap |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120727233126/http://www.charleston.net/news/2008/jun/05/lowcountry_native_musician_just_flew_sou43338/?wap |url-status=dead |archive-date=2012-07-27 |title=Lowcountry native musician just 'Flew South' with band |accessdate=2008-06-09 |last=Grant |first=Devin |date=2008-06-05 |work=Charleston Post and Courier }}</ref>

One Flew South's first recording was the song "Friends for Life" which Hummon co-wrote; the trio recorded the song on the soundtrack to the 2006 film ''[[The Fox and the Hound 2]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://countrymusic.about.com/od/cdreviewsmz/fr/FoxAndHound2.htm |title=Various Artists - The Fox and the Hound 2 (Soundtrack) |accessdate=2008-06-09 |last= Coleman |first=Kathy |work=[[About.com]]}}</ref> After the song was released, the trio began recording demo tapes while seeking a record deal. With Hummon's help they were signed to a recording contract with [[Decca Records]] in 2007, as well as a publishing contract with Sony/ATV Publishing.<ref name="one">{{cite web |url=http://www.oneflewsouth.com/index.html?var1=http://www.oneflewsouth.com/about.html?var1=http://www.oneflewsouth.com/about_frame.html |title=About One Flew South |accessdate=2008-06-09 |work=OneFlewSouth.com }}{{Dead link|date=November 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> Their debut album, ''Last of the Good Guys'' was released on May 27, 2008, debuting at No.&nbsp;67 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Top Country Albums]] charts;<ref name="that">{{cite web |url=http://www.thatscountry.com/am2/publish/Country_News_2/004364.shtml |title=Newcomers One Flew South to release debut album |accessdate=2008-06-09 |date=2007-05-28 |work=That's Country }}{{dead link|date=March 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> Most of the album's songs were co-written by the group's three members along with Hummon and rock songwriter [[JD Souther]].<ref name="review">{{cite web |url={{AllMusic|class=album|id=r1314301|pure_url=yes}} |title=Last of the Good Guys: Overview |accessdate=2008-06-09 |last=Erlewine |first=Stephen Thomas |authorlink=Stephen Thomas Erlewine |work=Allmusic}}</ref> Hummon also produced the album in addition to playing guitar, piano, and mandolin on it. The album's lead-off single, "My Kind of Beautiful" was co-written by [[Andy Griggs]], who previously recorded it on his 2005 album ''[[This I Gotta See]]''. It went for radio adds on June 2<ref>{{cite web |url=http://top40-charts.com/news.php?nid=40519 |title=Newcomers One Flew South's Debut Single 'My Kind Of Beautiful' Gets Early Radio Response |accessdate=2008-06-09 |date=2008-05-23 |work=Top40-Charts.com}}</ref> and debuted at No.&nbsp;57 on the ''Billboard'' [[Hot Country Songs]] charts for the chart week of June 21, 2008 where it stayed for a week. For the week of August 16, 2008, the song re-entered the Hot Country Songs charts at No.&nbsp;56.
