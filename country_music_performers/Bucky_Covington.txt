'''William Joel''' "'''Bucky'''" '''Covington III''' (born November 8, 1977) is an American [[country music]] singer. He placed eighth on the [[American Idol (season 5)|5th season]] of the [[Fox Broadcasting Company|Fox Network's]] talent competition series ''[[American Idol]]''. In December 2006, he signed a recording contract with [[Lyric Street Records]]. His self-titled [[Bucky Covington (album)|debut album]], produced by Dale Oliver and Mark Miller of the band [[Sawyer Brown]], was released on April 17, 2007. The album debuted at number one on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Top Country Albums]] chart, and produced three hit singles on the [[Hot Country Songs]] charts: "[[A Different World (song)|A Different World]]" at number six, "[[It's Good to Be Us]]" at number eleven, and "[[I'll Walk]]" at number ten. Three more singles: "[[I Want My Life Back]]", "[[Gotta Be Somebody]]", and "[[A Father's Love (The Only Way He Knew How)]]", were released for an unreleased second album, titled ''I'm Alright'', and later included on his 2012 album, ''[[Good Guys (album)|Good Guys]].''

==Biography==
===Personal life===
William Joel “Bucky” Covington was born in [[Rockingham, North Carolina]], to Gene Covington and Deborah Gates on November 8, 1977 - along with his identical [[twin]] brother, Robert David "Rocky" Covington.
Bucky is the nickname derived from his grandfather "Buck".<ref>[http://myidol.idolonfox.com/blogs/cheeselady/2006/03/11/Bucky_and_Rocky MyIDOL – American Idol<!-- Bot generated title -->] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070927065803/http://myidol.idolonfox.com/blogs/cheeselady/2006/03/11/Bucky_and_Rocky |date=2007-09-27 }}</ref> He graduated in the class of 1996 from [[Scotland High School]] in [[Laurinburg, North Carolina]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Bucky Covington |url=https://northcarolinamusichalloffame.org/vtour/inductee-item/bucky-covington/#toggle-id-1 |website=North Carolina Music Hall Of Fame Virtual Tour |access-date=15 June 2020}}</ref> He worked at Covington's Body Shop in [[Rockingham, North Carolina]].<ref>[http://www.laurinburgexchange.com/pages/full_story/push?article-Local+idol-+Bucky+Covington-+returns%20&id=20576272&instance=top_stories]{{dead link|date=January 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

At the age of 18, Covington taught himself how to play the guitar and began performing at clubs. He is also a [[bassist]], [[drummer]], and [[songwriter]]. Over the years, Covington has expanded his talents, both [[country music|country]] and [[rock music|rock]].  After some time, he elected to perform both original and cover material in a cross-genre vein. Rocky is also a musician and is the former lead singer of the North Carolina band ''Swamp Cat''.

In 1998, when the Covington twins were 20 years old, they were in a minor automobile mishap.  Bucky allegedly pretended to be Rocky. They were arrested for confusing the authorities. The plaintiff failed to identify which twin was driving, since the twins were identical.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1528396/20060411/index.jhtml|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20060420092904/http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1528396/20060411/index.jhtml|url-status=dead|archive-date=April 20, 2006|title=Report: 'Idol' Finalist Bucky Covington Duped Police To Help Twin|work=MTV News}}</ref>

After ''Idol'', Bucky bought a house in [[Franklin, Tennessee]], near Nashville, which he shared with Rocky and his wife Terra. Rocky also joined Bucky's band as a drummer. On February 13, 2007, he and his wife Crystal [[legal separation|separated]] after more than seven years of marriage.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.people.com/people/package/americanidol2007/article/0,,20007868_20011779,00.html|title=Bucky Covington & Wife Split - American Idol, Bucky Covington |website=People.com}}</ref> Covington became engaged to Katherine Cook in 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tasteofcountry.com/bucky-covington-theft-accusation/|title=Bucky Covington Accused of Theft|date=25 July 2011|work=Taste of Country}}</ref>

On October 10, 2014, Covington's fiancee, Katherine Cook, gave birth to their daughter, Kennedy Taylor Covington.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://celebritybabies.people.com/2014/10/10/bucky-covington-welcomes-daughter-kennedy-taylor/|title=Bucky Covington Welcomes Daughter Kennedy Taylor – Moms & Babies – Celebrity Babies and Kids - Moms & Babies |website=People.com}}</ref>

===''American Idol''===
In the 2005-06 season, the Covington twins auditioned for ''American Idol'' in [[Greensboro, North Carolina]], individually. Of the two, Bucky advanced to the final twelve.  On March 22, Bucky remained in the bottom three, along with [[Lisa Tucker (singer)|Lisa Tucker]] and [[Kevin Covais]]. On April 12, when Covington was in the bottom three with [[Ace Young]] and [[Elliott Yamin]], the votes went to the latter two.  In the Top 8 results show of season six of American Idol, he appeared in one of the front rows.

===Debut album: ''Bucky Covington''===
In November and December 2006, Bucky Covington performed on the GAC Country Music Christmas tour, making him the first Idol from the 5th season to be part of a major non-American Idol concert tour.<ref>[http://www.countrymusicchristmastour.com/ GAC Country Christmas<!-- Bot generated title -->] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070110170400/http://www.countrymusicchristmastour.com/ |date=2007-01-10 }}</ref>

Covington negotiated with [[Disney Music Group|Buena Vista Music Group]]'s country label, [[Lyric Street Records]]. His debut single, "[[A Different World (song)|A Different World]]", was released to country radio on January 16, 2007, coinciding with the premiere of [[American Idol (season 6)]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.syracuse.com/idolthoughts/2007/01/the_numbers_are_in_and_theyre_1.html|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130816055752/http://blog.syracuse.com/idolthoughts/2007/01/the_numbers_are_in_and_theyre_1.html|url-status=dead|title=The Numbers are in and They're Not Pretty|first=Donna|last=Reynolds|date=January 4, 2007|website=Syracuse.com|archive-date=August 16, 2013|access-date=March 25, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.cmt.com/artists/news/1549185/20070103/rascal_flatts.jhtml|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070107230504/http://www.cmt.com/artists/news/1549185/20070103/rascal_flatts.jhtml|url-status=dead|archive-date=January 7, 2007|title=CMT}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.gactv.com/gac/nw_headlines/article/0,,GAC_26063_5256649,00.html |title=GAC |access-date=2007-01-07 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070105234103/http://www.gactv.com/gac/nw_headlines/article/0,,GAC_26063_5256649,00.html |archive-date=2007-01-05 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Its initial debut on radio was on Sirius Satellite Radio's ''New Country'' channel in late December 2006.

His debut album, ''[[Bucky Covington (album)|Bucky Covington]]'', was released on April 17, 2007, to positive reviews. It debuted on the Billboard 200 at number four selling 61,000 copies. It also debuted at number one on the Top Country Albums chart, making the album the best opening week for a debut album by a male on the chart since [[Billy Ray Cyrus]]' 1992 debut with ''[[Some Gave All]]''.<ref>Katie Hasty, [http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/1052570/lavignes-new-thing-debuts-atop-billboard-200 "Lavigne's New 'Thing' Debuts Atop Billboard 200"], Billboard.com, April 25, 2007.</ref> Covington's debut surpassed the previous record held by [[Jason Michael Carroll]]'s ''[[Waitin' in the Country]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.jasonmichaelcarroll.com/site.php?em2253=116321_-1__0_~0_-1_2_2007_0_0&content=news|title=jmc|access-date=2007-07-21|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070927190040/http://www.jasonmichaelcarroll.com/site.php?em2253=116321_-1__0_~0_-1_2_2007_0_0&content=news|archive-date=2007-09-27|url-status=dead}}</ref> He performed selections from his album during a nationwide tour in 2007, and the track "Empty Handed" appeared in NASCAR 08 as part of the in-game soundtrack. Overall, ''Bucky Covington'' accounted for three singles. "A Different World" peaked at number six, followed by "It's Good to Be Us" at number eleven and "I'll Walk" at number ten.

===Unreleased second album: ''I'm Alright'' and ''Good Guys''===
In January 2009, Lyric Street noted that Covington has been in the studio recording his second album, ''I'm Alright''. The lead-off single, "[[I Want My Life Back]]" was released to radio in April 2009 and it peaked at number 32. In early October 2009, Covington released a cover version of [[Nickelback]]'s hit single "[[Gotta Be Somebody]]" which peaked at number 51 in December 2009 after spending only three weeks on the chart. The album's third single, "[[A Father's Love (The Only Way He Knew How)]]", was released in March 2010, and peaked at number 23.

The album was expected for release on April 27, 2010. However, in April 2010, it was announced [[Lyric Street Records]] would be closing, but Covington would be transferred to another label owned by Disney Music Group.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cmt.com/news/news-in-brief/1637143/covington-fowler-dickerson-follow-rascal-flatts-to-disney-music-group.jhtml|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100514093818/http://www.cmt.com/news/news-in-brief/1637143/covington-fowler-dickerson-follow-rascal-flatts-to-disney-music-group.jhtml|url-status=dead|archive-date=May 14, 2010|title=Covington, Fowler, Dickerson Follow Rascal Flatts to Disney Music Group|work=CMT News}}</ref> However, Covington remained unsigned until October 2011 when it was announced that he had signed as the "premier artist" for [[eOne Music|E1 Music]]'s Entertainment One Nashville label.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nashville.com/music/2011/10/bucky-covington-signs-new-record-deal/ |title=Bucky Covington Signs New Record Deal |access-date=15 January 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303224444/http://www.nashville.com/music/2011/10/bucky-covington-signs-new-record-deal/ |archive-date=3 March 2016 }}</ref> Covington released his second studio album, ''[[Good Guys (album)|Good Guys]]'', on September 11, 2012. Two singles—"I Wanna Be That Feeling" and "Drinking Side of Country"—were released by E1 Music in promotion of the album.
