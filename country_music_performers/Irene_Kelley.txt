'''Irene Kelley''' is an American [[Country music|country]] and [[Bluegrass music|bluegrass]] musician. As a songwriter, she has written for [[Ricky Skaggs]], [[Alan Jackson]], [[Loretta Lynn]], and [[the Osborne Brothers]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Lawless|first1=John|title=Carolina Wind- new single from Irene Kelley|url=http://bluegrasstoday.com/carolina-wind-new-single-from-irene-kelley/|accessdate=2 July 2016|publisher=Bluegrass Today|date=16 March 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Recker|first1=Scott|title=Pennsylvania Coal|url=http://www.popmatters.com/review/178322-irene-kelley-pennsylvania-coal/|accessdate=5 July 2016|publisher=PopMatters|date=12 February 2014}}</ref>

==Early life==
Kelley is originally from Pennsylvania and in high school sang in a Led Zeppelin cover band.<ref name="ColumbusLedZepplin">{{cite news|last1=Clementi|first1=Tyler|title=How Led Zeppelin led to Bluegrass for Irene Kelley|url=http://www.columbusunderground.com/how-led-zeppelin-led-to-bluegrass-for-irene-kelley-dd1|accessdate=4 July 2016|publisher=Columbus Underground|date=8 February 2015}}</ref> She was asked to leave the group after suggesting the band cover songs from Dolly Parton.<ref name="ColumbusLedZepplin"/>

==Career==
Kelley moved to Nashville, Tennessee in the early 1980s. This was after visiting on her honeymoon in 1983 and discovering the first gathering of the [[International Bluegrass Music Association]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Fox|first1=Randy|title=Irene Kelley's roots run deep through Pennsylvania Coal|url=http://www.nashvillescene.com/nashville/irene-kelleys-roots-run-deep-through-pennsylvania-coal/Content?oid=4059487|accessdate=2 July 2016|publisher=Nashville Scene|date=13 February 2014|ref=NashScene}}</ref> Kelley co-wrote the [[Ricky Skaggs]] and [[The Whites|Sharon White]] song "[[Love Can't Ever Get Better Than This]]" with Nancy Montgomery. The song peaked at number ten on the ''Billboard'' [[Hot Country Songs|Hot Country Singles]] chart in 1987 and was awarded Duet of the Year at the [[Country Music Association Awards]] that same year.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Cooper|first1=Peter|title=Ricky Skaggs and Sharon White find harmony|url=http://www.tennessean.com/story/entertainment/music/peter-cooper/2014/10/03/ricky-skaggs-sharon-white-find-harmony/16586533/|accessdate=2 July 2016|publisher=The Tennessean|date=3 October 2014}}</ref> Kelley signed to [[MCA Records]] in the late 1980s.<ref name="whitburn"/>
