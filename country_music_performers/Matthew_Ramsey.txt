'''Matthew Thomas Ramsey''' (born October 21, 1977) is an American [[country music]] singer and songwriter who is the lead vocalist and rhythm guitarist of his band [[Old Dominion (band)|Old Dominion]],<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.cmt.com/artists/old-dominion/| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141014083139/http://www.cmt.com/artists/old-dominion/| url-status=dead| archive-date=October 14, 2014| title= Old Dominion | publisher= CMT | date= 2015 | accessdate= October 8, 2015 }}</ref> with several hit songs on country radio to his credit.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.countryaircheck.com/pdf_publication/Issue_446%20-%20May%204,%202015.pdf| title= Country Aircheck Weekly May 4, 2015 | publisher= Country Aircheck | date= May 4, 2015 | accessdate= October 26, 2015 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.countryaircheck.com/pdf_publication/Issue_468%20-%20October%205,%202015.pdf| title= Country Aircheck Weekly October 5, 2015 | publisher= Country Aircheck | date= October 5, 2015 | accessdate= October 26, 2015 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/6282598/old-dominion/chart?f=1226| title= Billboard Chart History: Old Dominion | publisher= Billboard | date= October 27, 2015 | accessdate= October 27, 2015 }}</ref>

==Early life==
Ramsey grew up in [[Buchanan, Virginia]], where he attended [[James River High School (Buchanan, Virginia)|James River High School]]. He learned to play the drums at a young age before picking up the guitar at age 14.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.roanoke.com/arts_and_entertainment/music/old-dominion-country-band-has-roanoke-valley-roots/article_d98905b4-ea90-11e3-9679-0017a43b2370.html| title= Old Dominion country band has Roanoke valley roots | publisher= The Roanoke Times | date= June 3, 2014 | accessdate= October 27, 2015 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url= http://olddominionband.com/about | title= About Old Dominion | publisher= Old Dominion | date= 2015 | accessdate= October 27, 2015 | archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20151023040427/http://olddominionband.com/about | archive-date= October 23, 2015 | url-status= dead }}</ref> He received a degree in Illustration from [[Virginia Commonwealth University]].

==Career==
Ramsey moved to Nashville in 2000 to pursue a career in songwriting, where he met [[Trevor Rosen]] and the two became members of the group [[Old Dominion (band)|Old Dominion]].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/brandy-clark-and-old-dominion-laugh-about-haters-and-hot-rollers-20140623?page=2| title= Brandy Clark and Old Dominion Laugh About Hater and Hot Rollers | publisher= Rolling Stone | date=June 23, 2014 | accessdate=October 26, 2015 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://radio.com/2015/04/24/old-dominion-interview-new-music-to-know/| title= New Music to Know: Old Dominion is the Band Writing Today's Country Hits | publisher= radio.com | date=April 24, 2015 | accessdate=October 26, 2015 }}</ref> Ramsey has written several hit songs including [[The Band Perry]]'s "[[Chainsaw (The Band Perry song)|Chainsaw]]",<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.musicrow.com/2014/06/musicrowpics-the-band-perry-receives-challenge-coins-for-chainsaw/| title= The Band Perry Receives Challenge Coins for 'Chainsaw' | publisher= Music Row Magazine | date=June 5, 2014 | accessdate=October 8, 2015 }}</ref> [[Craig Morgan]]'s "[[Wake Up Lovin' You]]",<ref>{{cite web | url= http://theboot.com/craig-morgan-wake-up-lovin-you-lyrics/| title= Story Behind the Song: Craig Morgan, 'Wake Up Lovin' You' | publisher= the Boot | date=February 18, 2015 | accessdate=October 7, 2015 }}</ref> [[Dierks Bentley]]'s "[[Say You Do (Dierks Bentley song)|Say You Do]]",<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.cmt.com/news/1755530/dierks-bentley-celebrates-no-1-success-of-say-you-do-with-songs-writers/| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150620051243/http://www.cmt.com/news/1755530/dierks-bentley-celebrates-no-1-success-of-say-you-do-with-songs-writers/| url-status=dead| archive-date=June 20, 2015| title= Dierks Bentley Celebrates No. 1 Success With Song's Writers | publisher= CMT | date=June 17, 2015 | accessdate=October 7, 2015 }}</ref> [[Kenny Chesney]]'s "[[Save It for a Rainy Day (Kenny Chesney song)|Save It for a Rainy Day]]",<ref>{{cite web | url=http://tasteofcountry.com/kenny-chesney-save-it-for-a-rainy-day/| title= Kenny Chesney, 'Save It For a Rainy Day' | publisher= Taste of Country | date= June 16, 2015 | accessdate= October 27, 2015 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://theboot.com/kenny-chesney-save-it-for-a-rainy-day-no-1/| title= Kenny Chesney's 'Save It For a Rainy Day' Becomes 4th Straight No. 1 Song | publisher= the Boot | date= September 24, 2015 | accessdate= October 27, 2015 }}</ref> as well as songs by [[Sam Hunt]] and [[Luke Bryan]]. The band released its debut studio album, ''[[Meat and Candy]]'', which included the number one singles "[[Break Up with Him]]" and "[[Song for Another Time]]", and top five single "[[Snapback (song)|Snapback]]", on November 6, 2015.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/country/6721849/old-dominion-rides-countrys-new-wave/| title= Old Dominion Takes an Unusual Path to Country's Top 10 | publisher= Billboard | date=October 8, 2015 | accessdate=October 8, 2015 }}</ref> The band has released three more albums, ''[[Happy Endings (Old Dominion album)|Happy Endings]]'', their [[Old Dominion (album)|self-titled album]], and ''[[Time, Tequila & Therapy]]'' in 2017, 2019, and 2021, respectively.
