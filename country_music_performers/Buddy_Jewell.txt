'''Buddy Jewell Jr. ''' (born April 2, 1961) is an American [[country music]] singer who was the first winner on the [[USA Network]] talent show ''[[Nashville Star]]''. Signed to [[Columbia Records]] in 2003, Jewell made his debut on the American country music scene with the release of his [[Buddy Jewell (album)|self-titled album]], which produced the singles "[[Help Pour Out the Rain (Lacey's Song)|Help Pour Out the Rain]]" and "[[Sweet Southern Comfort]]". Another album, ''[[Times Like These (Buddy Jewell album)|Times Like These]]'', followed in 2005.

== Biography ==
Buddy Jewell was born in [[Lepanto, Arkansas|Lepanto]], Arkansas, on April 2, 1961.<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p560551/biography|pure_url=yes}}|title=Buddy Jewell biography|last=Jeffries|first=David|work=[[Allmusic]]|accessdate=June 5, 2009}}</ref> He began playing guitar after buying one from a schoolmate during childhood, and saved the money that he earned bagging groceries to buy guitar lesson books. Jewell also listened to the music that his father, also named Buddy, played for him, and was taught by his uncle Clyde how to play "[[What a Friend We Have in Jesus]]".<ref name="cmt">{{cite web|url=http://www.cmt.com/artists/az/jewell_buddy/bio.jhtml|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20040423011156/http://www.cmt.com/artists/az/jewell_buddy/bio.jhtml|url-status=dead|archive-date=April 23, 2004|title=Buddy Jewell biography|work=[[Country Music Television|CMT]]|accessdate=June 5, 2009}}</ref> By age 15, Jewell had also taught himself how to play [[Johnny Cash]]'s "I Still Miss Someone."<ref name="cmt"/> After graduating from [[Osceola High School (Arkansas)|Osceola High School]], he attended [[Arkansas State University]] where he was a member of [[Pi Kappa Alpha]].{{Citation needed|date=November 2018}} Jewell majored in television and radio in college, although he left in his junior year to marry, despite the marriage only lasting two-and-a-half years.<ref name="cmt"/>

Jewell later moved to [[Camden, Arkansas]], at age 21 in pursuit of a musical career. There, he discovered a band called White Oak, which was seeking a new lead singer.<ref name="allmusic"/> This band was sponsored by a booking agency whose roster also included [[Canyon (country music band)|Canyon]] and a band founded by a then-unknown [[Trace Adkins]].<ref name="cmt"/> After touring with White Oak for four years, he moved to [[Dallas]], Texas, where he took a role in a gunfighting show at [[Six Flags over Texas]]. He later entered a singing competition that was sponsored by the band [[Alabama (American band)|Alabama]], whose music was also an inspiration to him.<ref name="cmt"/> He won the competition's top prize, which was an opening slot for the band.<ref name="allmusic"/>

After winning the competition, he competed on ''[[Star Search]]'' where he won Male Vocalist on several episodes.<ref name="cmt"/> He later decided to move to [[Nashville, Tennessee]], in 1993, and found work two years later as a demo singer. As a demo singer, he recorded more than 5,000 demos. Among the songs that Jewell recorded demos for were "[[Write This Down (song)|Write This Down]]" for [[George Strait]], "[[A Little Past Little Rock]]" for [[Lee Ann Womack]], "[[The One (Gary Allan song)|The One]]" for [[Gary Allan]] and "[[You're Beginning to Get to Me]]" for [[Clay Walker]].<ref name="cmt"/> Jewell also self-released albums entitled ''One in a Row'' and ''Far Enough Away'' in 2001 and 2002 respectively.<ref name="cmt"/> In 1999, he appeared on comedian [[Bill Engvall]]'s album ''[[Dorkfish]]'', providing vocals to the track "I'm a Cowboy" and appearing in its music video.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.newspapers.com/image/77278911/ | title=Public picks a gem for first 'Nashville Star' | work=[[Herald and Review]] | date=June 6, 2003 | accessdate=September 25, 2024 | author=Tamela Partridge | pages=4}}</ref>

==''Nashville Star'' and major-label music career==
In 2003, Jewell competed in the first season of the television singing competition ''[[Nashville Star]]''. He became the show's first winner that season, and was soon signed to a recording contract with [[Columbia Records]] Nashville.<ref name="allmusic"/>

===2003–2004: ''Buddy Jewell''===
On May 5, 2003, two days after his win, Jewell's debut single "[[Help Pour Out the Rain (Lacey's Song)|Help Pour Out the Rain]]" was shipped to radio. It became the highest-debuting single by a new country artist since the singles charts were first tabulated via [[Nielsen SoundScan]] in 1990.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.accessmylibrary.com/coms2/summary_0286-23277057_ITM|title=Nashville Star Winner Buddy Jewell Breaks Record With Debut Single; 'Help Pour Out the Rain (Lacey's Song)' Highest Debut Single By Solo Artist.|date=May 15, 2003|work=PR Newswire|accessdate=August 8, 2009}}</ref> This song reached number three on the country charts and 29 on the pop charts. It was the first single from his [[Buddy Jewell (album)|self-titled]] debut album, which was produced by former [[RCA Records]] artist [[Clint Black]] and was recorded in ten days.<ref name="allmusic"/><ref name="polish">{{cite web |url=http://www.cmt.com/news/country-music/1473512/buddy-jewell-polishes-his-star.jhtml |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110606174037/http://www.cmt.com/news/country-music/1473512/buddy-jewell-polishes-his-star.jhtml |url-status=dead |archive-date=June 6, 2011 |title=Buddy Jewell Polishes His Star |accessdate=August 3, 2008 |work=[[Country Music Television|CMT]] }}</ref> ''Buddy Jewell'' sold 500,000 copies and earned a gold certification from the [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA), in addition to producing a second number three country hit in "[[Sweet Southern Comfort]]", which also reached number 40 on the pop charts. This song was followed by the number 38 country single "One Step at a Time".<ref name="whitburn">{{cite book|title=Hot Country Songs 1944 to 2008|last=Whitburn|first=Joel|date=August 2008|publisher=Record Research, Inc.|isbn=978-0-89820-177-2|pages=210}}<!--|accessdate=October 6, 2009--></ref>

===2004–2005: ''Times Like These''===
Jewell's second album for Columbia, ''[[Times Like These (Buddy Jewell album)|Times Like These]]'', was released in 2005. This album did not perform as well at radio, with its first single ("If She Were Any Other Woman") reaching number 27 on the charts, and the second single ("So Gone") failing to chart entirely.<ref name="whitburn"/> By the end of the year, Jewell was dropped from Columbia's roster.

===2005–2011: ''Country Enough''===
He did not release another single until "This Ain't Mexico" in 2008, a self-released single. This was included on an album entitled ''Country Enough'', which was released on Diamond Dust Records in 2008. In May 2011 Jewell released a new album "I Surrender All".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.amazon.com/I-Surrender-All-Buddy-Jewell/dp/B0050N9HCO/ref=sr_1_3?keywords=Buddy+jewell&qid=1551985115&s=gateway&sr=8-3|title=I Surrender All|via=Amazon}}</ref> He released two songs from this album  "Jesus, Elvis, And Me" which was a country dance hit and "Somebody Who Would Die For You" which went up to No. 1 on the Christian Charts.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.unicornone.com/buddyjewell.html|title=TITLE: Buddy Jewell|website=www.unicornone.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://earbits.com/track/no-grease-no-gravy|title=Earbits -|website=earbits.com}}</ref>

===2015: ''My Father's Country''===
In 2015, Jewell signed with [[Lamon Records]] in Nashville and recorded an album produced with [[Grammy]] nominated artist-producer [[Dave Moody (musician)|Dave Moody]]. The album ''My Father's Country'' featured a collection of classic country songs, including [[Abilene (song)|Abilene]], [[Behind Closed Doors (Charlie Rich song)|Behind Closed Doors]], [[Galveston (song)|Galveston]] and other songs Jewell grew up listening to his father sing. Buddy Jewell was inducted into the Arkansas Entertainers Hall of Fame on Tuesday, September 29, 2015, at the Noah's Event Center in Little Rock, AR.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://katv.com/news/local/arkansas-entertainers-hall-of-fame-inducts-six-new-notable-arkansas-personalities|title=Arkansas Entertainers Hall of Fame inducts six new notable Arkansas personalities|website=katv.com|date=September 30, 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151103002027/https://katv.com/news/local/arkansas-entertainers-hall-of-fame-inducts-six-new-notable-arkansas-personalities|archive-date=November 3, 2015}}</ref>

===2017–2019===

In 2017 Jewell recorded an EP album "Reloaded". His single from the album is "I'm There" and it went to number one on the Power Source Christian Country charts.{{citation needed|date=May 2021}}
