'''David Allen Loggins''' (November 10, 1947 – July 10, 2024) was an American singer, songwriter, and musician. He is best known for his 1974 hit single "[[Please Come to Boston]]" as well as his 1984 duet with [[Anne Murray]], "[[Nobody Loves Me Like You Do]]".

==Musical career==
Loggins wrote his 1974 song composition "[[Please Come to Boston]]", which was a No. 5 on the pop chart (No. 1 on the Easy Listening chart) in the U.S.<ref>{{Cite magazine |last=Lynch |first=Jessica |date=2024-07-12 |title=Dave Loggins, 'Please Come to Boston' Singer and Masters Theme Composer, Dies at 76 |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/dave-loggins-obituary-1235729601/ |access-date=2024-07-13 |magazine=Billboard}}</ref> 
He also wrote the song "[[Pieces of April (song)|Pieces of April]]" for the band [[Three Dog Night]], which was a top 20 success in 1973.{{cn|date=July 2024}} He had written songs for [[Jimmy Buffett]] (Buffett's "[[Come Monday]]" was bumped from the charts by "Please Come to Boston" in late July 1974), [[Tanya Tucker]], [[Restless Heart]], [[Wynonna Judd]], [[Reba McEntire]], [[Gary Morris]], [[Billy Ray Cyrus]], [[Alabama (American band)|Alabama]], [[Toby Keith]], [[Don Williams]], and [[Crystal Gayle]]. Loggins wrote the number one hits "Morning Desire" by [[Kenny Rogers]] and "You Make Me Want To Make You Mine" by [[Juice Newton]].{{Citation needed|date=July 2024}}

During 1984, he recorded "[[Nobody Loves Me Like You Do]]," a duet with [[Anne Murray]], which scored number one on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs|Hot Country Singles]] chart. Loggins and Murray were named Vocal Duo of the Year at the [[Country Music Association Awards|CMA Awards]] during 1985.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://theboot.com/anne-murray-two-cma-awards |title=Country Music Memories Anne Murray Makes History at the CMAs |date=October 8, 2020 |website=The Boot |access-date=August 28, 2021}}</ref> One of Loggins' most famous musical compositions is "Augusta," which he wrote while visiting the [[Augusta National Golf Club]] in 1981. The next year, CBS began using the song at the [[Masters Tournament|Masters Golf Tournament]] as the theme song for its coverage each year.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.wideopencountry.com/masters-theme-song/ |title=Country Songwriter Dave Loggins Wrote the Masters Theme |date=October 2020 |website=Wide Open Country |access-date=August 28, 2021 |archive-date=August 28, 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210828173331/https://www.wideopencountry.com/masters-theme-song/ |url-status=live }}</ref> In 1982, [[David Lasley]] released a cover version of Loggins’ “[[If I Had My Wish Tonight]]”, originally released by Loggins in 1979.<ref>{{Cite magazine |title=David Lasley |url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/david-lasley/ |access-date=2022-06-10 |magazine=Billboard |language=en-US |archive-date=June 10, 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220610234917/https://www.billboard.com/artist/david-lasley/ |url-status=live }}</ref> 

In 1995, Loggins was inducted to the [[Nashville Songwriters Hall of Fame]].<ref name="nashvillesongwritersfoundation">{{cite web |url=http://nashvillesongwritersfoundation.com/Site/inductee?entry_id=2461 |title=Dave Loggins − Nashville Songwriters Hall of Fame |access-date=August 28, 2021 |publisher=[[Nashville Songwriters Hall of Fame]]}}</ref>

==Personal life and death==

David Allen Loggins was born on November 10, 1947 in [[Mountain City, Tennessee]]. Before becoming a musician, Loggins was employed as a [[technical drawing|draftsman]] and as an insurance salesman.<ref name="nashvillesongwritersfoundation" /> 

His second cousin, [[Kenny Loggins]], is also a singer-songwriter.<ref>{{cite web |title= iTunes biography: Dave Loggins |url= https://itunes.apple.com/us/artist/dave-loggins/id198984 |publisher= iTunes |access-date= June 25, 2013 |archive-date= November 11, 2013 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20131111115055/https://itunes.apple.com/us/artist/dave-loggins/id198984 |url-status= live }}</ref>

Loggins died in [[Nashville, Tennessee|Nashville]] on July 10, 2024, at the age of 76.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.tennessean.com/obituaries/pokr0878306 |title=David Allen Loggins |publisher=[[The Tennessean]] |date=July 11, 2024 |accessdate=July 11, 2024}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Haring |first1=Bruce |title=Dave Loggins Dies: Grammy-Nominated Songwriter For 'Please Come To Boston' Was 76 |url=https://deadline.com/2024/07/dave-loggins-dead-songwriter-please-come-to-boston-obituary-1236008223/ |website=Deadline.com |publisher=[[Deadline Hollywood]] |access-date=14 July 2024 |date=12 July 2024}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Willman |first1=Chris |title=Dave Loggins, Singer Known for '70s Smash 'Please Come to Boston,' and Composer of Golf's Masters Theme, Dies at 76 |url=https://variety.com/2024/music/obituaries-people-news/dave-loggins-dead-please-come-to-biston-singer-songwriter-1236068565/ |website=variety.com |publisher=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]] |date=12 July 2024}}</ref>
