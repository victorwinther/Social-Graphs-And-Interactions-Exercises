'''Shelly Fairchild''' (born August 23, 1977) is an American music recording artist. Signed to [[Columbia Records]] in 2004, she released her first album, ''[[Ride (Shelly Fairchild album)|Ride]]'', in early 2005. It included the single "You Don't Lie Here Anymore", a No.&nbsp;35 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' Hot Country Singles & Tracks (now [[Hot Country Songs]]) charts.

==Biography==
Fairchild was born in [[Clinton, Mississippi]], to a musical family.<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web |url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p682231|pure_url=yes}} |title=Shelly Fairchild biography |accessdate=June 11, 2008 |last=Leggett |first=Steve |work=[[Allmusic]]}}</ref> She began performing at an early age in church and later in her high school's show choir. Fairchild studied communications, theater and music at [[Mississippi College]] and subsequently starred in local stage shows including ''[[Grease (musical)|Grease]]'' and ''Always Patsy Cline''. She also traveled with the national touring company for ''Beehive: The 60's Musical''.<ref name="allmusic"/>

==Personal life==
On July 29, 2017, Fairchild married the music executive Deborah DeLoach<ref>{{cite web|url= http://www.vevasound.com |title=VEVA Sound |accessdate= January 19, 2017}}</ref> in [[Vail, Colorado]]. Their elopement was covered in ''[[Brides (magazine)|Brides]]'' which included venue and catering by Collective Vail, Fairchild's dress by [[Free People]], DeLoach's dress by Thread & Needle, from BHLDN, officiant Susie Kincade and photography by Mackenzie Neville.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.brides.com/story/country-musician-boho-elopement-in-colorado|title=A Country Musician's Boho Elopement in Colorado |author=Jaimie Mackey |work=Brides |accessdate=November 6, 2017}}</ref> Their wedding was voted by ''Brides'' as the Top 50 Favorite Real Wedding Photos of 2017.{{citation needed|date=May 2023}} The couple resides in Nashville, Tennessee.{{citation needed|date=May 2023}}

==Career==
In 2004, Fairchild signed to [[Columbia Records]].<ref name="allmusic"/> Her first album, ''[[Ride (Shelly Fairchild album)|Ride]]'', was issued in early 2005. Its lead-off single, "You Don't Lie Here Anymore", co-written with Clay Mills, peaked at No.&nbsp;35 on the U.S. ''Billboard'' [[Hot Country Songs]] charts, although subsequent singles failed to chart. She toured in support of the album including an opening spot on [[Rascal Flatts]]' sold-out ''Here's to You'' tour which also featured [[Blake Shelton]], but was dropped from Columbia by the end of the year.

In 2008, Fairchild signed a record deal with [[Stroudavarious Records]] which was founded by producer [[James Stroud]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.roughstock.com/audio/shelly-fairchild-its-all-gonna-work-out|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090106212430/http://www.roughstock.com/audio/shelly-fairchild-its-all-gonna-work-out|url-status=dead|archive-date=January 6, 2009|title=Shelly Fairchild ‚Äî "It's All Gonna Work Out"|work=Roughstock|accessdate=January 5, 2009}}</ref> Her first release was the song "It's All Gonna Work Out", which she released for a charity album called ''We Are Enterprise''.

On April 11, 2011, Fairchild's second album,, ''Ruby's Money'', was released. Free from the confines of a major label, she set out to take control of her music and released "Ruby‚Äôs Money" on her own label, Revelation Nation Records. She took her unstoppable voice to another level, creating an album that reflected a "Memphis kind of feel" with horns. She co-wrote a song called "Love Everybody", which was an anchor for this record.<ref name="Shelly Fairchild ‚Äî Sip Magazine">{{cite web|url=https://thesipmag.com/shelly-fairchild/|title=Shelly Fairchild |author=Lauchlin Fields |date=November 2, 2015|work=The 'Sip |accessdate=October 5, 2015}}</ref>

In 2014, Fairchild was one of three background singers for [[Martina McBride]]'s "Everlasting Tour". Touring with McBride, Fairchild explained, was a huge highlight of her career. "If someone thinks I‚Äôm good enough to come and sing backgrounds for them, I‚Äôm like, ‚ÄòYeah‚Äô ‚Äî especially if it's music I love. Martina is somebody I‚Äôve looked up to as a singer my whole life."<ref name="Shelly Fairchild ‚Äî Sip Magazine"/>

In 2016, Fairchild did a [[PledgeMusic]] campaign and surpassed her goal by 41% in just two days to record her latest album Buffalo.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pledgemusic.com/projects/shellyfairchild|title=Shelly Fairchild PledgeMusic|work=PledgeMusic|accessdate=May 15, 2016}}</ref> 
Her third studio album, ''Buffalo'', was released December 2016. This record entered the Top 100 Country albums on [[iTunes]] in the first week.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/shellyfairchild/photos/buffalo-broke-through-top-100-on-the-top-country-albums-chart-on-itunes-i-cant-b/10154624416711147/ |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/iarchive/facebook/32934371146/10154624416711147 |archive-date=2022-02-26 |url-access=limited|title=Shelly Fairchild|work=iTunes Chart|accessdate=December 2, 2016}}{{cbignore}}</ref> Fairchild went on to release her first single, "Mississippi Turnpike" which she debuted in her first music video in ten years. It premiered exclusively on Taste Of Country<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tasteofcountry.com/shelly-fairchild-mississippi-turnpike-video/|title=Shelly Fairchild|work=Taste of Country|accessdate=June 17, 2017}}</ref> and went on to play repeatedly on [[CMT (U.S. TV channel)|CMT]] making this her first independent play on the major network since her first album ''Ride''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/BVkVTjllI9f/|title=Shelly Fairchild|work=Instragram|accessdate=June 20, 2017}}</ref>

In July 2017, ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' magazine listed Shelly has the Top 10 New Country Artists You Need To Know.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country-lists/10-new-country-artists-you-need-to-know-july-2017-201932/shelly-fairchild-205203/|title=Shelly Fairchild|magazine=Rolling Stone|date=July 11, 2017|accessdate=July 11, 2017}}</ref> Shelly is asked to sing regularly on the ''[[Grand Ole Opry]]'' and has her own designated artist page on the ''Opry'' website.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.opry.com/artist/shelly-fairchild/shelly-fairchild-205203/|title=Shelly Fairchild |work=Grand Ole Opry|accessdate=January 11, 2017}}{{dead link|date=May 2023}}</ref>

Shelly is also an active writer and artist for TV and Film. In January 2017 Shelly signed with Nashville-based Licensing Firm Resin8. Working with various songwriters and producers ‚Äì Shelly is consistently writing and recording for new film and TV opportunities.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://resin8music.com/artists/|title=Shelly Fairchild Resin8|work=Resin8|accessdate=January 31, 2017}}</ref>

In July 2020, Fairchild was awarded the LGBTQ Rising Star Award at [[Ty Herndon]] "Love and Acceptance" show streamed on [[Country Music Television]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.goodhousekeeping.com/life/entertainment/a33001049/how-to-watch-stream-concert-for-love-and-acceptance-cmt/ |title=How to Watch and Stream the CMT Benefit 'Concert for Love and Acceptance' |author=Selena Barrientos |date=June 30, 2020 |work=Good Housekeeping |accessdate=June 30, 2020}}</ref>

In August 2020, Fairchild was featured in Brandon Stansell's documentary ''Three Chords and A Lie'' which dos not sugarcoat the truth about coming out in many parts of America ‚îÄ depicting some truly heart-wrenching moments in his story. It streams on [[OutTV (Canadian TV channel)]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.grammy.com/grammys/news/brandon-stansell-talks-emotional-documentary-three-chords-and-lie/ |title=Brandon Stansell Talks Emotional Documentary, 'Three Chords And A Lie'|author=Jason Scott |publisher=GRAMMYs |date=August 26, 2020|accessdate=May 24, 2023}}</ref>

In June 2021, Fairchild sang at the [[Ryman Auditorium]] participating as a featured artist in the Nashville and Austin LGBT Chamber of Commerce Pride Event. Streaming on all of its social channels, Tina Cannon, executive director of Austin LGBT Chamber of Commerce, said in a statement, "Working together with Nashville, we are combining the talent of two of the greatest musical communities in the world to produce an amazing live show that directly supports LGBTQ+ artists."<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.austin360.com/story/entertainment/music/2021/06/01/lgbtq-pride-month-austin-nashville-livestream-pinkstar-patrice-pike-pleasure-venom-alanna-royale/5290914001/ |title=Austin and Nashville artists team up for Pride livestream featuring Patrice Pike, p1nkstar |author=Deborah Sengupta Stith |website=Austin 360 |date=June 1, 2021 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20210622082107/https://www.austin360.com/story/entertainment/music/2021/06/01/lgbtq-pride-month-austin-nashville-livestream-pinkstar-patrice-pike-pleasure-venom-alanna-royale/5290914001/ |archivedate=June 22, 2021 |accessdate=May 24, 2023 }}</ref>

In July 2021, Fairchild began touring with [[Lennon Stella]] as one of her background singers for her festival dates including Wonderstruck, Bonnaroo, Roots N Blues and Moon Crush.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/CRu_wAGBCj3/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230929082404/https://www.instagram.com/p/CRu_wAGBCj3/|url-status=dead|archive-date=September 29, 2023|title=Lennon Stella Tour Dates |website=Instagram|accessdate=July 24, 2021}}</ref>
