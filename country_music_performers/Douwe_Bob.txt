'''Douwe Bob Posthuma''' (born 12 December 1992), professionally known by only his first and middle names '''Douwe Bob''' ({{IPA|nl|ˈdʌuə ˈbɔp}}), is a Dutch singer-songwriter. He won the Dutch talent show ''[[De beste singer-songwriter van Nederland]]''. He specializes in [[folk music|folk]] and [[country music]] and has released four albums. He [[Netherlands in the Eurovision Song Contest 2016|represented the Netherlands]] in the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2016]].<ref>{{Cite web|title = AVROTROS confirms: Douwe Bob Heading to Stockholm! – ESCBubble| date=22 September 2015 |url = http://escbubble.com/2015/09/avrotros-confirms-douwe-bob-to-stockholm/|access-date = 22 September 2015}}</ref>

==Early life==
Douwe Bob Posthuma was born on 12 December 1992 in [[Amsterdam]] in the [[Netherlands]]. He was born into a [[Frisians|Frisian]] family.<ref>"[http://www.lc.nl/cultuur/Wat-er-Fries-is-aan-Douwe-Bob-21337737.html Wat er Fries is aan Douwe Bob]" (in Dutch), ''[[Leeuwarder Courant]]'', 2016. Retrieved 10 May 2016.</ref> His father, Simon Posthuma, is known for being a member of [[The Fool (design collective)|The Fool]] design collective.

Douwe Bob started playing piano at the age of 6, initially concentrating on classical music and [[jazz]]. He took up the guitar at age 14. As a singer he was inspired by country, folk and pop music from the 1950s to the mid-1970s.

==Career==
[[File:Douwe-bob-1427460070.jpg|thumb|upright|Douwe Bob in December 2015.]]

===2012–2013: ''Born in a Storm''===
{{Main|Born In a Storm}}
In 2012, he competed in the Dutch TV talent show ''[[De beste singer-songwriter van Nederland]]'' (The Best Singer-Songwriter of the Netherlands). He played the songs "Standing Here Helpless", "Icarus", and in the finale, "[[Multicoloured Angels]]". In the finale, he was joined by Dutch singer [[Tim Knol]] and the latter's band. He won the finals<ref name=brabant>{{cite web|url=http://www.omroepbrabant.nl/?news/2269881483/%E2%80%98Het+kwartje+viel+op+Paaspop%E2%80%99,+Douwe+Bob+(22)+voelde+zich+in+Schijndel+voor+het+eerst+op+z%E2%80%99n+gemak.aspx|title='Het kwartje viel op Paaspop', Douwe Bob (22) voelde zich in Schijndel voor het eerst op z'n gemak|work=Omroep Brabant}}</ref><ref name=nu>{{cite web|url=http://www.nu.nl/cd-recensies/3414262/douwe-bob---born-in-a-storm.html|title=Douwe Bob – Born In A Storm|work=NU|date=4 May 2013 }}</ref><ref name=stentor>{{cite web|url=http://www.destentor.nl/algemeen/show/douwe-bob-posthuma-winnaar-beste-singer-songwriter-1.906395|title=Douwe Bob Posthuma winnaar Beste Singer-Songwriter|work=Centrale Redactie (web)|access-date=6 April 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150923213944/http://www.destentor.nl/algemeen/show/douwe-bob-posthuma-winnaar-beste-singer-songwriter-1.906395|archive-date=23 September 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> and "Multicoloured Angels" reached number 17 in the weekly music chart [[Dutch Top 40]]. His debut album, ''[[Born in a Storm]]'', was released on 3 May 2013. The bulk of the songs were written during a holiday in Morocco with {{Interlanguage link multi|Matthijs van Duijvenbode|nl}}. In June 2013, Douwe Bob played at the [[Pinkpop Festival]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.volkskrant.nl/dossier-pinkpop/pinkpop-2013-douwe-bob-is-gelukkig-geen-singer-songwriter-meer~a3459539/|title=Pinkpop 2013: Douwe Bob is gelukkig geen singer-songwriter meer|author=Door: Just Fontein|work=De Volkskrant|date=15 June 2013 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://go.3fm.nl/festivals-pinkpop/nieuws-detail/4288/Douwe-Bob-op-Pinkpop|title=Douwe Bob op Pinkpop|access-date=1 July 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304071608/http://go.3fm.nl/festivals-pinkpop/nieuws-detail/4288/Douwe-Bob-op-Pinkpop|archive-date=4 March 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref> At the end of 2013, he appeared in the documentary ''Whatever Forever: Douwe Bob'' to talk about his relationship with his father Simon Posthuma, during [[International Documentary Film Festival Amsterdam|IDFA]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.idfa.nl/industry/tags/project.aspx?id=3c51edc0-84c1-47bb-afc6-702b11bc07b9&tab=dfs|title=Whatever Forever: Douwe Bob – IDFA|work=idfa.nl|access-date=7 April 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151004111638/https://www.idfa.nl/industry/tags/project.aspx?id=3c51edc0-84c1-47bb-afc6-702b11bc07b9&tab=dfs|archive-date=4 October 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref>

===2014–2015: ''Pass It On''===
{{Main|Pass It On (Douwe Bob album)}}
In 2014, Douwe Bob became one of the Ambassadors of Freedom for the {{Interlanguage link multi|Bevrijdingsfestival|nl}}.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bevrijdingsfestivals.nl/ambassadeurs/douwe_bob|title=Bevrijdingsfestivals – Douwe Bob|work=bevrijdingsfestivals.nl|access-date=7 April 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150411162303/http://www.bevrijdingsfestivals.nl/ambassadeurs/douwe_bob|archive-date=11 April 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref> In January 2015, he released the single "[[Hold Me (Anouk & Douwe Bob song)|Hold Me]]", produced in collaboration with [[Anouk (singer)|Anouk]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://wiwibloggs.com/2015/01/17/netherlands-anouk-douwe-bob-duet-hold-me/73335/|title=The Netherlands: Anouk releases new duet 'Hold me'|author=Mike (Michael)|work=Eurovision 2015 Predictions, Polls, Odds, Rankings – wiwibloggs|date=17 January 2015 }}</ref> In February 2015, Douwe Bob announced his new album ''[[Pass It On (Douwe Bob album)|Pass It On]]''. The album was released on 15 May 2015 by record company [[Universal Music Group]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.universalmusic.nl/nieuws/douwe-bob-kondigt-album-pass-it-on-aan|title=Douwe Bob Kondigt Album 'Pass It On' Aan « Nieuws « Universal Music – Your Music Ondemand|work=universalmusic.nl}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.doornroosje.nl/event/douwe-bob2/|title=Douwe Bob + Max Meser|work=Doornroosje}}</ref>

===2016–2017: Eurovision Song Contest and ''Fool Bar''===
{{Main|Fool Bar}}
On 6 May 2016, he released his third studio album ''[[Fool Bar]]'' by record company Universal Music Group. The album peaked at number four on the [[MegaCharts|Dutch Albums Chart]]. Douwe Bob represented his country in the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2016]] in Stockholm,<ref>{{cite web|last1=Escudero|first1=Victor M.|title=The Netherlands: Douwe Bob To Represent The Netherlands|url=http://www.eurovision.tv/page/news?id=the_netherlands_douwe_bob_to_stockholm|website=eurovision.tv|access-date=22 September 2015}}</ref> where he performed the song "[[Slow Down (Douwe Bob song)|Slow Down]]" in the semi-finals and finals on 10 and 14 May 2016. He placed eleventh in the final, racking up a total of 153 points. He was the Dutch spokesperson for the [[Eurovision Song Contest 2017]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://eurovoix.com/2017/04/21/netherlands-douwe-bob-announced-spokesperson/|title=Netherlands: Douwe Bob Announced Spokespersons|date=21 April 2017|website=Eurovoix|last1=Granger|first1=Anthony|access-date=21 April 2017}}</ref>

===2018–present: ''The Shape I'm In'' and ''Singel 39''===
On 21 April 2017, Douwe Bob was announced as the fourth coach of the seventh season of [[The Voice Kids (Dutch TV series)|The Voice Kids]],<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.show.nl/social-media/twitter-social-media/2017/douwe-bob-vierde-coach-voice-kids/|title=Douwe Bob vierde coach in The Voice Kids|date=21 April 2017|work=Show.nl|access-date=1 May 2017|language=nl}}</ref> which started on 23 February 2018. In the following season, he did not return and was replaced by [[Anouk (singer)|Anouk]]. On 9 November 2018, Douwe Bob released his fourth studio album ''The Shape I'm In'', which he described as being a break-up record. In 2019, he composed the title song for the Dutch feature film ''[[Singel 39]]''.<ref name="gouden_film_eb">{{cite web|url=https://www.entertainmentbusiness.nl/film/singel-39-bekroond-met-gouden-film/|title=Singel 39 bekroond met Gouden Film|date=17 May 2019|publisher=Entertainment Business|access-date=7 June 2019|archive-date=7 June 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190607190838/https://www.entertainmentbusiness.nl/film/singel-39-bekroond-met-gouden-film/|language=nl|url-status=live}}</ref> He also played a small role in the film.<ref name="gouden_film_eb"/> The film won the [[Golden Film]] award after having sold 100,000 tickets.<ref name="gouden_film_eb"/>

Douwe Bob appeared in the 2023 season of ''[[The Masked Singer (Dutch TV series)|The Masked Singer]]''.

==Personal life==
On 10 March 2016, in an interview with [[OutTV (Europe)|OutTV]], Douwe Bob revealed that he identifies as [[bisexuality|bisexual]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.telegraaf.nl/prive/25366861/__Douwe_Bob_komt_uit_de_kast__.html|title=Douwe Bob komt uit de kast|language=nl|publisher=Telegraaf|date=10 March 2016|access-date=11 March 2016|archive-date=17 March 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160317063045/http://www.telegraaf.nl/prive/25366861/__Douwe_Bob_komt_uit_de_kast__.html|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Posthuma has three children with three different women: a daughter with Loes van Delft; a second daughter with an unknown woman; and a son with former girlfriend Anouk.<ref>[https://www.ad.nl/show/douwe-bob-kreeg-in-december-niet-een-maar-twee-kinderen-derde-op-komst~a07a0e4f/ Douwe Bob expecting third child (DUTCH)]</ref>
