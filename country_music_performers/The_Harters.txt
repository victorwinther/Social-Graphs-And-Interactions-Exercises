'''The Harters''' were an American country music trio based in [[Arizona]]. It was composed of lead singer Leslie Harter and her brothers, [[J. Michael Harter|Michael]] and Scott. The trio released its debut single, "Jenny," in early 2010 and charted with it on ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs]].

==Biography==
Brothers Michael and Scott Harter, and their sister Leslie, were involved in various other musical endeavors before forming the band together. Michael had moved to [[Nashville]] to become a country songwriter/performer, recording one album for [[Broken Bow Records]] in 2002 under the name [[J. Michael Harter]].<ref name="mcn"/> Scott went to school in San Diego where he played in [[alternative rock]] bands.<ref name="weekly"/> Leslie remained in Arizona where she decided at age 18 that she was going to pursue a career in music. In 2005, the Harters recorded one single, "Drunk Chick," under the name SEVEN, with [[David Malloy]] as producer.<ref name="mcn">{{cite web |url=http://www.musiccitynews.com/clients4.php |title=MCN Country Music - SEVEN |access-date=16 March 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20061220044448/http://musiccitynews.com/clients4.php |archive-date=20 December 2006 |work=Music City News}}</ref>

==Critical reception==
Karlie Justus of Engine 145 gave "If I Run" a "thumbs up", comparing Leslie's voice to that of [[Trisha Yearwood]] and praising the lyrics.<ref name="archive">{{cite web |url=http://www.engine145.com/the-harters-if-i-run/ |title=The Harters - "If I Run" - Engine 145 |website= |access-date=3 January 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141218105007/http://www.engine145.com/the-harters-if-i-run/ |archive-date=18 December 2014 |url-status=dead}}</ref> Giving favorable singles to both singles, Bobby Peacock of Roughstock praised the "clever turns of phrase" and melody of "Jenny",<ref name="archive2">{{cite web |url=http://www.roughstock.com/blog/the-harters-jenny- |title=The Harters - "Jenny" &#124; Roughstock.com |website= |access-date=3 January 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100323192937/http://www.roughstock.com/blog/the-harters-jenny- |archive-date=23 March 2010 |url-status=dead}}</ref> and the "uncluttered" production of "If I Run". He compared the latter song to [[The Band Perry]].<ref name="archive3">{{cite web |url=http://www.roughstock.com/blog/the-harters-if-i-run- |title=The Harters - "If I Run" &#124; New Country Music, Listen to Songs & Video &#124; Roughstock.com |website= |access-date=3 January 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101029050017/http://www.roughstock.com/blog/the-harters-if-i-run- |archive-date=29 October 2010 |url-status=dead}}</ref>
