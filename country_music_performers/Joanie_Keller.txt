'''Joanie Keller''' (born in [[Wayne, Nebraska]]<ref name="whitburn">{{cite book|last=Whitburn|first=Joel|title=Hot Country Songs 1944 to 2008|publisher=Record Research, Inc|page=220|year=2008|isbn=978-0-89820-177-2}}</ref>) is an American [[country music]] singer. Keller began performing in her father's band when she was a small child.<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|last=Proefrock|first=Stacia|title=Joanie Keller – Music Biography|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=mn0000138444|pure_url=yes}}|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|accessdate=March 11, 2013}}</ref> After graduating high school, she played local clubs in [[Colorado]] before moving to [[Nashville, Tennessee|Nashville]] to pursue a career as a country singer.<ref name="allmusic" />

Keller's debut album, ''Sparks Are Gonna Fly'', was released by [[Broken Bow Records]] in April 2000.<ref name="allmusic" /> Its first single, "Three Little Teardrops", peaked at number 66 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs|Hot Country Singles & Tracks]] chart.<ref name="whitburn" /> The album received a mixed review from Joel Bernstein of ''[[Country Standard Time]]'', who wrote that "it's too smooth and polished to grab the hard-core honkytonkers, but it's often too country for much of today's younger audience."<ref>{{cite web|last=Bernstein|first=Joel|title=Joanie Keller – Sparks Are Gonna Fly|url=http://www.countrystandardtime.com/d/cdreview.asp?xid=1411|work=[[Country Standard Time]]|accessdate=March 11, 2013}}</ref> Carrie Attebury of [[about.com]] compared Keller favorably to [[Linda Davis]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Attebury|first=Carrie|title=CD Review: ''Sparks Are Gonna Fly'' – Joanie Keller|url=http://countrymusic.about.com/library/blsparksrev.htm|work=[[about.com]]|accessdate=March 11, 2013|archive-date=February 18, 2003|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20030218042519/http://countrymusic.about.com/library/blsparksrev.htm|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Since parting ways with Broken Bow, Keller has continued to perform in Nashville with the band 45 RPM.<ref name="norfolk">{{cite news|last=Pitzer|first=Carrie|title=Nebraska Music Hall of Fame to induct pioneers, legends|url=http://www.norfolkdailynews.com/entertainment/nebraska-music-hall-of-fame-to-induct-pioneers-legends/article_af990354-a049-5678-89dd-541c42983b3b.html|accessdate=March 11, 2013|newspaper=[[Norfolk Daily News]]|date=July 30, 2009}}</ref> She was inducted into the Nebraska Music Hall of Fame in 2009.<ref name="norfolk" />
