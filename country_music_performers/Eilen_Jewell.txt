'''Eilen Jewell''' (born April 6, 1979) is an American singer-songwriter from [[Boise, Idaho]]. She has released 13 albums.

==Early life and education==
Jewell grew up in Boise, Idaho and attended St. John’s College in [[Santa Fe, New Mexico|Santa Fe]], New Mexico.<ref name="Boise Weekly">{{cite web |url=http://www.boiseweekly.com/boise/wide-open-spaces/Content?oid=933544 |title=Wide Open Spaces |author=Amy Atkins |date=August 29, 2007 |publisher=Boise Weekly |accessdate=August 22, 2011 |archive-date=September 27, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130927062258/http://www.boiseweekly.com/boise/wide-open-spaces/Content?oid=933544 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://jacksonville.com/entertainment/music/2009-05-08/story/music_notes_eilen_jewell_darryl_wise_and_more |title=Music Notes: Eilen Jewell, Darryl Wise and more |publisher=Jacksonville.com |date=May 8, 2009 |accessdate=May 16, 2015}}</ref> After living in Boston for several years, she returned to Boise in 2015. Her 2015 album, Sundown Over Ghost Town, was largely inspired by her return to Boise.<ref>from an interview on [http://americanamusicshow.com/episode247 Americana Music Show #247], published May 19, 2015.</ref>

==Career==
Jewell began her musical career [[busking]] on the streets of Santa Fe while attending college.<ref name="Boise Weekly"/> She then moved to Los Angeles and performed on the streets of [[Venice, Los Angeles|Venice Beach]].<ref name="Boise Weekly"/> Jewell moved to [[Massachusetts]] where she performed at local music clubs.<ref name="Boise Weekly"/>

In December 2005, Jewell recorded a "live demo" album called ''Nowhere in Time'' and later recorded the album ''Boundary County'' with Jason Beek on percussion, Daniel Kellar on violin, Jerry Glenn Miller on guitars and Johnny Sciascia on upright bass.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.acousticmusic.com/fame/p04027.htm |title=FAME Review: Eilen Jewell – Boundary Country |publisher=Acousticmusic.com |date= |accessdate=May 16, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{AllMusic|class=album|id=r948521|tab=review|label=Boundary County|first=Ronnie D|last=Lankford}}</ref><ref>NPR, September 3, 2007, Eilen Jewell: Songs from 'Sinners and Strangers', David Dye, Retrieved July 2011</ref>

[[File:EilenJewellBand.jpg|thumb|right|Jewell and her band in Buffalo, New York, 2011.]]

Her album ''Letters from Sinners & Strangers'', was recorded at the Signature Sounds studio in [[Pomfret, Connecticut]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rtrfm.com.au/stories/type/reviews/category/pop/802 |title=Pops in the Pines « RTRFM / The Sound Alternative |publisher=Rtrfm.com.au |date= |accessdate=May 16, 2015}}</ref>

In early 2009 Jewell released the album ''Sea of Tears''<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.boston.com/ae/music/cd_reviews/articles/2009/04/27/eilen_jewell_sea_of_tears/ |title=Eilen Jewell, 'Sea of Tears' |date=April 27, 2009 |first=Stuart |last=Munro |work=[[The Boston Globe]]}}</ref> which was called a "startling departure"  by a Sydney ''Morning Herald'' music critic.<ref>The Sydney Morning Herald (Australia), May 9–10, 2009.{{Verify source|what is the article title? page number? url?|date=July 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url= https://www.dispatch.com/entertainmentlife/20180709/concert-review--eilen-jewell-singer-songwriter-displays-history-of-traditional-american-music|title= Concert review: Eilen Jewell: Singer-songwriter displays history of Traditional American Music|work= Dispatch|date= July 9, 2018}}</ref>

In 2010, Jewell released the album ''Butcher Holler'', an album of [[Loretta Lynn]] covers and in 2011, her fourth album of original music, ''Queen of the Minor Key''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://content.usatoday.com/communities/popcandy/post/2011/06/new-podcast-joseph-arthur-arctic-monkeys-and-more-new-tunes/1 |title=New podcast! Joseph Arthur, Arctic Monkeys and more new tunes |publisher=Content.usatoday.com |date=June 2, 2011|accessdate=May 16, 2015}}</ref>

Jewell and her band toured the U.S., Canada, Europe,<ref>{{cite web|last=Östnäs |first=Magnus |title=Folkscenens nya stjärna i Uppsala |date=April 29, 2010 |url=http://www.unt.se/kultur-noje/folkscenens-nya-stjarna-i-uppsala-924068.aspx|publisher=Uppsala Nya Tidning |accessdate=December 22, 2016 }}</ref> the UK and Australia beginning in 2005 including performances at South by Southwest and NPR's [[World Cafe (radio program)|World Cafe]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Jewell |first=Eilen |url=https://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=14158918 |title=Eilen Jewell: Songs from 'Sinners and Strangers' : World Cafe |publisher=Npr.org |date= |accessdate=May 16, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url= https://www.ksut.org/post/what-night-eilen-jewell-and-cannondolls-rocked-strater-theatre|title= What a Night! Eilen Jewell and the Cannondolls Rocked the Strater Theatre. |date= February 17, 2018|work= [[KSUT]]}}</ref>
