'''Randall Lamar Howard''' (May 9, 1950 – June 9, 2015) was an American [[Country music|country]] singer, songwriter, guitarist, and producer.<ref name="Inc.1973b">{{cite book|author=Nielsen Business Media, Inc.|title=Billboard|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=AgkEAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA44|date=October 6, 1973|publisher=Nielsen Business Media, Inc.|pages=44–|issn=0006-2510}}</ref> His music involved [[outlaw country]], [[traditional country]], old [[southern gospel]], [[blues]] and [[Christian country]].

== Early life ==
Howard was born May 9, 1950, in [[Macon, Georgia]].<ref name="Whitburn1989">{{cite book|first=Joel|last=Whitburn|title=Joel Whitburn's top country singles, 1944–1988: compiled from Billboard's country charts, 1944–1988|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=kMhHAAAAMAAJ|date=May 1989|publisher=Record Research|isbn=978-0-89820-070-6|page=144}}</ref>

== Career ==
Howard worked as a disk jockey and program director at WDEN radio in Macon, Georgia.<ref name="Inc.1973a">"Utopian in Georgia. {{cite book|author=Nielsen Business Media, Inc.|title=Billboard|url=https://archive.org/details/bub_gb_FgkEAAAAMBAJ|date=13 January 1973|publisher=Nielsen Business Media, Inc.|pages=[https://archive.org/details/bub_gb_FgkEAAAAMBAJ/page/n31 32]–|issn=0006-2510}}</ref>  He also appeared on television.  He formed the company Utopian Enterprises in 1973 and produced a number of records.<ref name="Inc.1973">"Macon forms Country Effort". {{cite book|author=Nielsen Business Media, Inc.|title=Billboard|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=OgkEAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA17|date=28 April 1973|publisher=Nielsen Business Media, Inc.|pages=17–|issn=0006-2510}}</ref><ref name="Inc.1974">"Macon Music Utopia Gets Life Insurance". {{cite book|author=Nielsen Business Media, Inc.|title=Billboard|url=https://archive.org/details/bub_gb_gQkEAAAAMBAJ|date=May 25, 1974|publisher=Nielsen Business Media, Inc.|pages=[https://archive.org/details/bub_gb_gQkEAAAAMBAJ/page/n44 45]–|issn=0006-2510}}</ref>

As a recording artist, Howard released seven albums and CDs, all of which have charted in both national and international trade magazines. His album ''All American Redneck'' was listed in BillBoard's Top Album Picks in 1983.<ref name="Top Albums1983">"Billboard's Top Album Picks". {{cite book|author=Nielsen Business Media, Inc.|title=Billboard|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=oSQEAAAAMBAJ&pg=PT60|date=March 26, 1983|publisher=Nielsen Business Media, Inc.|pages=60–|issn=0006-2510}}</ref>

As a writer, Howard's songs were recorded by [[The Geezinslaw Brothers]], [[Robin Lee (singer)|Robin Lee]] and [[Hank Williams III]].

[[Hank Williams III]]'s CD ''[[Straight to Hell (album)|Straight to Hell]]'' features Randy Howard's song entitled "My Drinkin' Problem".<ref>[https://www.chicagotribune.com/1996/09/15/the-grandson-also-rises/ "The Grandson Also Rises: Hank Williams Iii Tries His Hand At The Family Business"]. ''Chicago Tribune'', September 15, 1996|By Jack Hurst</ref> 

Howard appeared on [[Nashville Now]], [[Entertainment Tonight]], [[Good Morning America]] and [[USA Today]].

On June 9, 2015, Howard was shot and killed at his home in Lynchburg, Tennessee during an altercation with bounty hunter Jackie Shell.<ref>[https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2015/jun/16/randy-howard-country-singer-death-bounty-hunter-laws "Country singer Randy Howard's shooting death sparks scrutiny of bounty hunter laws"].  Max Blau June 16, 2015</ref><ref>[http://tasteofcountry.com/randy-howard-dies-shootout-bounty-hunter/ "Outlaw Country Singer Randy Howard Dies in Shootout With Bounty Hunter"]. ''Taste of Country'', By Sterling Whitaker June 11, 2015</ref>
