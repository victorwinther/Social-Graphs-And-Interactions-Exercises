It's the 14th but I'm still overwhelmed|last=Hale|first=Lucy|access-date=June 9, 2013|date=June 9, 2013|publisher=Twitter.com}}</ref>) is an American actress and singer.<ref>{{cite news |first=Ray|last=Richmond |url=https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/review/bionic-woman-158986 |title=Bionic Woman |newspaper=[[The Hollywood Reporter]] |agency=[[Associated Press]] |date=September 23, 2007 |access-date=June 30, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |first=Brian|last=Lowry |url=https://variety.com/2008/tv/reviews/privileged-1200470864/ |title=Review: 'Privileged' |journal=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]] |date=September 8, 2008 |access-date=June 30, 2015}}</ref> She has received various accolades, including seven [[Teen Choice Awards]] (the most for any actress in a single series), a [[Gracie Awards|Gracie Award]], a [[People's Choice Award]], and two [[Young Hollywood Awards]].

After early recognition for her roles as Becca Sommers in the series ''[[Bionic Woman (2007 TV series)|Bionic Woman]]'' (2007) and Rose Baker in the series ''[[Privileged (TV series)|Privileged]]'' (2008–2009), Hale received her [[breakthrough role]] as [[Aria Montgomery]] in the [[Freeform (TV channel)|Freeform]] series ''[[Pretty Little Liars]]'' (2010–2017), for which she received global stardom and critical acclaim. She starred as Stella Abbott in ''[[Life Sentence (TV series)|Life Sentence]]'' (2018), [[Katy Keene|the titular character]] in ''[[Katy Keene (TV series)|Katy Keene]]'' (2020) and DC Lake Edmunds in ''[[Ragdoll (TV series)|Ragdoll]]'' (2021). She has appeared in films such as ''[[The Sisterhood of the Traveling Pants 2]]'' (2008), ''[[A Cinderella Story: Once Upon a Song]]'' and ''[[Scream 4]]'' (both 2011), and led the films ''[[Truth or Dare (2018 film)|Truth or Dare]]'' and ''[[Dude (film)|Dude]]'' (both 2018), ''[[Fantasy Island (film)|Fantasy Island]]'' (2020), ''[[The Hating Game]]'' (2021), and ''[[Which Brings Me to You]]'' (2024).

In addition to acting, Hale has also ventured into music. In 2003, she first came to prominence as one of the five winners of the reality show ''[[American Juniors]]'', a children's spin-off of [[Fox Broadcasting Company|Fox]]'s ''[[American Idol]]'' and began as a solo artist with signing [[Hollywood Records]] in 2012, on her debut studio album, ''[[Road Between]]'' (2014). She is also credited with hosting ''[[Dick Clark's New Year's Rockin' Eve]]'' from 2016 to 2021.

==Career==

=== 2003–2009: Early roles ===
In 2003, Hale first appeared on television as a contestant in the [[Fox Broadcasting Company|Fox]] reality show ''[[American Juniors]]''. On July 30, 2003, Hale finished in fourth place<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.realitytvworld.com/news/lucy-hale-becomes-4th-member-of-final-american-juniors-group-1514.php|title=Lucy Hale becomes 4th member of final "American Juniors" group|work=Reality TV World|access-date=July 18, 2018}}</ref> and part of a [[American Juniors (group)|group of the same name]] formed with the top 5 finishers (Chauncey Matthews, Danielle White and Tori & Taylor Thompson) but the group disbanded in 2005, after their [[American Juniors (album)|self-titled studio album]]'s lackluster sales.<ref>[{{AllMusic|class=album|id=r667573|pure_url=yes}} American Juniors]. Allmusic. Retrieved November 11, 2008.</ref>

Hale moved to Los Angeles at age 15 in the hopes of getting a record deal.<ref>{{cite web|last=Minsker|first=Maria|title=Pretty Little Liars Secrets: A Q&A with Lucy Hale|url=http://www.collegemagazine.com/editorial/2683/Pretty-Little-Liars-Preview-A-QA-with-Lucy-Hale|work=College Magazine|access-date=January 7, 2014|date=May 30, 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140516042536/http://www.collegemagazine.com/editorial/2683/Pretty-Little-Liars-Preview-A-QA-with-Lucy-Hale|archive-date=May 16, 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Sciarretto|first=Amy|title='Pretty Little Liars' Star Lucy Hale Shares Details About Upcoming Country Album [VIDEO]|url=http://popcrush.com/pretty-little-liars-star-lucy-hale-country-album-video/|work=PopCrush|access-date=January 8, 2014|date=October 28, 2013}}</ref> Shortly after, she started auditioning and received a minor role on ''[[Drake & Josh]]''<ref>{{cite web|last=Chen|first=Eva|title=Girl Talk: Lucy Hale|url=http://www.teenvogue.com/entertainment/cover-stars/2010-12/lucy-hale-teen-vogue-february-2011|work=Teen Vogue |access-date=January 8, 2014|date=December 2012}}</ref> and other guest roles on shows such as ''[[Ned's Declassified School Survival Guide]]'', ''[[The O.C.]]'', and ''[[How I Met Your Mother]]''. She appeared in two episodes of the [[Disney Channel]] show ''[[Wizards of Waverly Place]]''.

In 2007, Hale appeared in [[NBC]]'s short-lived series ''[[Bionic Woman (2007 TV series)|Bionic Woman]]''. Hale plays Becca Sommers, the younger sister of the title character [[Jaime Sommers (Bionic Woman)|Jaime Sommers]] (played by [[Michelle Ryan]]). The character was originally portrayed by [[Mae Whitman]] in the unaired pilot episode, after which Whitman departed the series.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.tvguide.com/news/bionic-sis-gets-11566/|title=Bionic Sis Gets New Face/Ears, More Casting News|website=[[TV Guide]]|date=July 17, 2007|access-date=July 28, 2018}}</ref>

In August 2008, Hale made her big screen debut in ''[[The Sisterhood of the Traveling Pants 2]]'' portraying Effie Kaligaris, the younger sister of [[Lena Kaligaris]] (played by [[Alexis Bledel]]).<ref name="BuddyTV" /> Hale then appeared in [[The CW]] series ''[[Privileged (TV series)|Privileged]]'' as Rose Baker, with co-stars [[Ashley Newbrough]] and [[JoAnna Garcia]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Bianco|first=Robert |title='Privileged': The kids are all white, and kind of shallow, too |url=http://usatoday30.usatoday.com/life/television/reviews/2008-09-08-privileged_N.htm|work=[[USA Today]]|access-date=November 11, 2008|date=September 9, 2008}}</ref> She starred in the [[Lifetime (TV network)|Lifetime]] television film ''[[Sorority Wars]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=TV: Sorority Wars, the Definitive Lucy Hale Interview|url=http://www.goshtv.net/?p=192|work=Gosh!TV |access-date=January 8, 2014|date=October 17, 2009|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303234553/http://www.goshtv.net/?p=192|archive-date=March 3, 2016}}</ref>

=== 2010–2012: ''Pretty Little Liars'' and music ===
[[File:Lucyhalepeopleschoice.jpg|thumb|268x268px|Hale at the [[38th People's Choice Awards]] in 2012]]
In October 2009, Hale was cast as [[Aria Montgomery]] in ''[[Pretty Little Liars]]'', based on the [[Pretty Little Liars (book series)|book series of the same name]] by [[Sara Shepard]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/hr/content_display/news/e3i7d72e67f57c98169ba0d1ff67dbb4492|archive-url=https://archive.today/20100127172430/http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/hr/content_display/news/e3i7d72e67f57c98169ba0d1ff67dbb4492|url-status=dead|archive-date=2010-01-27|title=ABC Family nears 'Little Liars' pickup|work=[[The Hollywood Reporter]]|access-date=July 25, 2018|date=October 19, 2009}}</ref> The television series lasted seven seasons from 2010 to 2017, with Hale's performance being praised by critics. She also received a number of awards and nominations, including seven [[Teen Choice Awards]], a [[Gracie Allen Award]], a [[People's Choice Awards|People's Choice Award]] and a [[Young Hollywood Awards|Young Hollywood Award]].

In January 2010, Hale appeared as the singer Phoebe Nichols and her identical body double Vanessa Patton in the ''[[CSI: Miami]]'' episode "Show Stopper".<ref>{{cite web|title=''CSI: Miami'' Episode: "Show Stopper"|url=https://www.tvguide.com/tvshows/csi-miami-2010/episode-12-season-8/show-stopper/100101l|work=[[TV Guide]]|access-date=May 9, 2018|date=January 11, 2010|archive-date=March 31, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140331221303/http://www.tvguide.com/tvshows/csi-miami-2010/episode-12-season-8/show-stopper/100101l/|url-status=dead}}</ref> In 2011, Hale had a cameo role as Sherrie in the [[slasher film|slasher]] film ''[[Scream 4]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Shenae Grimes, Lucy Hale Join 'Scream 4' Cast|url=http://www.celebuzz.com/shenae-grimes-lucy-hale-join-s243891/|work=Celebuzz|access-date=July 4, 2012|date=August 27, 2010}}</ref> Later, she was cast as the aspiring singer Katie Gibbs in ''[[A Cinderella Story: Once Upon a Song]]'', the third installment of [[A Cinderella Story (film series)|''A Cinderella Story'' series]] and was released on direct-to-video in September 2011.{{citation needed|date=November 2019}} She recorded songs for [[A Cinderella Story: Once Upon a Song#Soundtrack|the film's soundtrack]].<ref>{{cite web|last=Huver|first=Scott|date=January 2, 2011|title=Lucy Hale Is Addicted to Social Networking|url=http://www.wetpaint.com/pretty-little-liars/articles/lucy-hale-is-addicted-to-social-networking|access-date=June 15, 2012|work=Wetpaint|publisher=The Cambio Network}}</ref>

Throughout her career, Hale made appearances of music videos with artists: [[Foy Vance]]'s "[[The Wild Swan (album)|She Burns]]", Chase Jordan's "Lose Control (Take a Sip)" and [[Jackson Harris]]' "Come Back Down to Earth".

Hale hosted an episode of [[MTV]]'s ''[[Punk'd]]'' aired on April 26, 2012, where she successfully pranked with ''Pretty Litte Liars'' co-star [[Ian Harding]], [[Vanessa Hudgens]] and [[Josh Hutcherson]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Ian Harding Gets Pranked by Lucy Hale on Punk'd: "I'm So Glad I Didn't Cry!" (VIDEO)|url=http://www.wetpaint.com/pretty-little-liars/articles/ian-harding-gets-pranked-by-lucy-hale-on-punkd-im-so-glad-i-didnt-cry-video|work=[[Wetpaint]]|publisher=The Cambio Network|access-date=April 29, 2012|first=Maria Mercedes|last=Lara|date=April 27, 2012}}</ref> The same year, Hale played the voice of Periwinkle, a twin sister of [[Tinker Bell]] (voiced by [[Mae Whitman]]) in the animated [[Disney]] film ''[[Secret of the Wings]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.shineon-media.com/2012/10/15/debby-ryan-and-lucy-hale-discover-the-secret-of-the-wings-video/|title=Debby Ryan and Lucy Hale Discover the "Secret of the Wings" (Video)|work=Shine on Media|date=October 15, 2012|access-date=August 3, 2018}}</ref> In June 2012, Hale announced that she had signed a record deal with [[Hollywood Records]] (co-partnership with [[Disney Music Group|DMG Nashville]]).<ref>{{cite web|last=Bentley|first=Jean|date=June 12, 2012|title='Pretty Little Liars' star Lucy Hale lands record deal with Hollywood Records|url=http://blog.zap2it.com/pop2it/2012/06/pretty-little-liars-star-lucy-hale-lands-record-deal-with-hollywood-records.html|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120615203307/http://blog.zap2it.com/pop2it/2012/06/pretty-little-liars-star-lucy-hale-lands-record-deal-with-hollywood-records.html|archive-date=June 15, 2012|access-date=June 13, 2012|work=[[Zap2it]]}}</ref>

In January 2024, Hale announced that she was two years [[Sobriety|sober]] from alcohol.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://people.com/health/lucy-hale-celebrates-1-year-of-sobriety/|title=Lucy Hale Celebrates 1 Year of Sobriety: 'You Are Not Alone and You Are Loved'|work= People |first= Marisa|last=Sullivan|date=February 15, 2023|accessdate=March 5, 2023}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last= Giang-Paunon |first= Stephanie |date=2024-01-03 |title=Actress Lucy Hale celebrates big milestone in sobriety after 'self-destructive' battle with alcohol |url=https://www.foxnews.com/entertainment/actress-lucy-hale-celebrates-milestone-sobriety-self-destructive-battle-alcohol |access-date=2024-01-11 |website=Fox News |language=en-US}}</ref>  She started drinking at 14 and worked on quitting alcohol on several occasions.  In an episode of ''The Diary of a CEO'' [[podcast]], Hale said: "I just held on to that belief that the real Lucy came out when she was drinking. It also quieted my mind… my brain just doesn't shut off and it's exhausting. I was a textbook [[Binge drinking|binge drinker]], blackout, wouldn't remember what I did or what I said, which is scary."<ref>{{Cite magazine |date=2023-02-24 |title= Lucy Hale Opens Up About Her Decision to Get Sober After Being A "Textbook Binge Drinker" |url= https://www.vanityfair.com/style/2023/02/lucy-hale-alcholism-sobriety-decision-textbook-binge-drinker-diary-ceo-podcast |access-date= 2024-01-11 |magazine=Vanity Fair |language=en-US}}</ref> She struggled with [[anxiety]] and [[depression]].<ref>[https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/general-news/lucy-hale-life-feels-good-sober-1236000551/ Lucy Hale Says “Life Feels So Good Now” After Getting Sober: “I’m Choosing Me”]</ref>
