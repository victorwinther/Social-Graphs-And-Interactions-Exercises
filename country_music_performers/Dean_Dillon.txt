'''Dean Dillon''' (born '''Larry Dean Flynn'''; March 26, 1955) is an American [[country music|country]] musician and songwriter. Between 1982 and 1993, he recorded six studio albums on various labels, and charted several singles on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' country charts. Since 1993, Dillon has continued to write hit songs for other artists, most notably [[George Strait]].

In 2002, Dillon was inducted into the [[Nashville Songwriters Hall of Fame]].<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|last=Sterling |first=Todd |title=Dean Dillon |work=[[AllMusic]] |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/dean-dillon-mn0000226643 |access-date=March 5, 2013}}</ref> In 2020, he was inducted into the [[Country Music Hall of Fame and Museum|Country Music Hall of Fame]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.tennessean.com/story/entertainment/music/2020/08/12/hank-williams-jr-marty-stuart-dean-dillon-country-music-hall-fame/3352839001/|title=Hank Williams Jr., Marty Stuart, Dean Dillon to enter the Country Music Hall of Fame|first=Matthew Leimkuehler and Dave|last=Paulson|newspaper=[[The Tennessean]]|access-date=August 6, 2021}}</ref>

==Early life ==
Dean Dillon was born Larry Dean Flynn<ref name="cmhof"/> on March 26, 1955, in [[Lake City, Tennessee]], where he was raised. He began playing the guitar at the age of seven, and when he was 15 he made his first public appearance as a singer and performer in the [[Knoxville]] [[variety show]] ''Jim Clayton Startime''.<ref name="allmusic"/> After completing [[Oak Ridge High School (Oak Ridge, Tennessee)|Oak Ridge High School]] in 1973 he hitchhiked to [[Nashville]] with hopes of starting a music career.<ref name="dillon">{{cite web|title=Biography |work=Dean Dillon |url=http://www.deandillon.com/bio/ |access-date=March 5, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.knoxnews.com/story/entertainment/music/2020/08/13/east-tennessee-native-dean-dillon-joins-country-music-hall-fame/3358799001/ |title=East Tennessee native Dean Dillon joins the Country Music Hall of Fame|work=Knoxville News Sentinel|date=August 13, 2020|first=Allie|last=Clouse}}</ref> Dillon first recorded on the Plantation label as Dean Rutherford, and then as Dean Dalton. Upon moving to [[RCA Records]], he was persuaded to change his recording name; record executive Jerry Bradley randomly picked the name Dillon from a telephone book.<ref name="cmhof"/>

==Career==
===Recording artist===
As a recording artist between 1979 and 1983, Dillon charted eight times, including one top 30 hit, "I'm into the Bottle (To Get You out of My Mind)".  He was featured on several duet albums with songwriting partner [[Gary Stewart (singer)|Gary Stewart]].<ref name="cmhof"/> Early songwriting success earned Dillon a recording deal with [[Capitol Records]] for  whom he released two studio albums. In 1991, now at [[Atlantic Records]], Dillon released his most successful and most recent studio album, ''Out of Your Ever Lovin' Mind''.<ref name="allmusic"/>

===Songwriter===
As a songwriter, early successes includes [[David Allan Coe]]'s 1981 hit "[[Tennessee Whiskey (song)|Tennessee Whiskey]]". Dillon has written many singles for [[George Strait]], including "[[Unwound (song)|Unwound]]", "[[Down and Out (George Strait song)|Down and Out]]", "[[Marina del Rey (song)|Marina del Rey]]", "[[The Chair (song)|The Chair]]", "[[Nobody in His Right Mind Would've Left Her]]", "[[It Ain't Cool to Be Crazy About You]]", "[[Ocean Front Property (song)|Ocean Front Property]]", "[[Famous Last Words of a Fool]]", "[[I've Come to Expect It from You]]", "[[If I Know Me]]", "[[Easy Come, Easy Go (George Strait song)|Easy Come, Easy Go]]", "[[Lead On (song)|Lead On]]", "[[The Best Day (George Strait song)|The Best Day]]", "[[She Let Herself Go]]", "[[Living for the Night]]", "[[Here for a Good Time (song)|Here for a Good Time]]", "[[Drinkin' Man]]", "[[I Believe (George Strait song)|I Believe]]" and "[[Every Little Honky Tonk Bar]]". Strait and his son Bubba co-wrote the latter five with Dillon.<ref name="allmusic"/>

===Record label===
In 2018 he met [[Sundance Head]] at a [[Houston Rodeo]] and fell in love with his voice. "The first time I heard 'Dance' I thought, 'where has THIS guy been hiding? I've been in this business all my life, and with the exception of a couple of people, I've never heard anybody sing the way this man does. He is extremely special," says Dillon.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://sundancehead.com/about-sundance/ |title=About Sundance |publisher=Sundancehead.com |access-date=April 10, 2020}}</ref> With the help of business associates from [[Texas]], Dillon created his own record label WildCatter Records, signing [[Sundance Head]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/8479246/dean-dillon-wildcatter-records-sundance-head |title=Dean Dillon Launches Wildcatter Records, Signs 'Voice' Winner Sundance Head |website=Billboard.com |access-date=April 10, 2020}}</ref> On January 25, 2019, their first album together, ''Stained Glass and Neon'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.countryschatter.com/2019/01/new-music-from-sundance-head-stained-glass-and-neon/ |title=New music from Sundance Head – "Stained Glass and Neon" – |website=Countryschatter.com |date=January 11, 2019 |access-date=April 10, 2020}}</ref> was released.

==Personal life==
Dillon's daughter, [[Jessie Jo Dillon]], is also a songwriter.  He also has another daughter, named Song, who is also a singer songwriter.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.musicconnection.com/songwriter-profile-jessie-jo-dillon/ | title=Songwriter Profile: Jessie Jo Dillon | date=October 26, 2020 }}</ref>

==Songs written==
Dillon has worked with a younger generation of country stars including [[Toby Keith]] and [[Kenny Chesney]]. In 2002, he was inducted into the Nashville Songwriters Hall of Fame along with [[Bob Dylan]] and [[Shel Silverstein]]. Dillon co-wrote two songs on Toby Keith's 2005 album ''[[Honkytonk University]]'', and five on his 2006 album ''[[White Trash with Money]]'', including the single "[[A Little Too Late (Toby Keith song)|A Little Too Late]]".
