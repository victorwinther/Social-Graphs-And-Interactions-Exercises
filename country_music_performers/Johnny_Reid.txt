'''John Kirkland Reid''' (born August 21, 1973) is a Scottish-Canadian [[Country music|country]] music artist. Reid moved to Canada in July 1989 when he was 16 from [[Scotland]] [[United Kingdom]]. His father, a diesel mechanic, wanted to give Reid and his brother opportunities he did not think they would have in Scotland. He graduated from [[Turner Fenton Secondary School]] in [[Brampton]], [[Ontario]], in 1992. Reid went on to attend [[Bishop's University]] in [[Lennoxville]], [[Quebec]], where he met his wife (and was a kicker on the varsity football team).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://dailygleaner.canadaeast.com/liveit/article/631981|title=Canadian country star Johnny Reid being courted by labels around the world}}</ref>

Reid moved to [[Nashville, Tennessee]] to make country music; he and his family reside in Nashville.<ref name="The Sunday Post">{{cite news|url=http://www.sundaypost.com/tv-showbiz/music-theatre/johnny-reid-the-biggest-scots-star-you-ve-never-heard-of-1.103022|title=Johnny Reid – the biggest Scots star you've never heard of|date=2013-06-15|work=[[The Sunday Post]]}}</ref> He was nominated for the 2008 [[Juno Award]] for Country Recording of the Year for ''Kicking Stones''. Reid was named Male Artist of the Year at both the 2008 and 2009 [[Canadian Country Music Association|CCMA]] Awards. He also performed at the Canada Day celebrations on Parliament Hill on July 1, 2010, which the [[Elizabeth II|Queen]] and [[Prince Philip]] attended.

==Music career==

===''Another Day, Another Dime'' and ''Johnny Reid''===
Reid's debut album, ''Another Day, Another Dime'', was released in 1997 on JCD Records. No singles were ever released from the album. However, it was his second album, which was self-titled and released in 2000, where he saw minimal chart success. Three singles from this album were released and all three of which charted. The lead-off and debut single, "Given Up on Me", was a minor hit on the ''[[RPM (magazine)|RPM]]'' Country Tracks chart. It reached a peak of No.&nbsp;45. A second single, "Runnin' Wild" charted at No.&nbsp;50. The album's third and final single, "She Don't Wanna Hear", just missed the Top 40 of the country chart, peaking at No.&nbsp;41.

===[[Born to Roll (album)|''Born to Roll'']]===
Reid's first single in four years, "[[You Still Own Me]]", was released in 2004. It was the lead-off single to his third studio album, ''[[Born to Roll (album)|Born to Roll]]''. The charted in the 20 of the Canadian country charts. ''Born to Roll'' was released on March 15, 2005, via [[Open Road Recordings]], and peaked at No.&nbsp;5 on the Canadian Top Country Albums chart. A second single, "Sixty to Zero", charted to No.&nbsp;19, however it was the album's third single to get Reid into the Top 10 of the charts. "Missing an Angel" eventually reached No.&nbsp;6 on the charts, giving him his first Top 10 single of his career. A fourth single, "Time Flies", also managed to chart in the Top 10, peaking at No.&nbsp;9. A fifth and final single, "Gypsy in My Soul", only made No.&nbsp;12. ''Born to Roll'' became his first certified album, being certified Gold by the [[Canadian Recording Industry Association]] (CRIA).

In 2007, Canadian music group [[Emerson Drive]] covered "You Still Own Me". They took the song to No.&nbsp;22 on the U.S. ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs]] chart.

===''[[Kicking Stones]]'' and [[Dance with Me (Johnny Reid album)|''Dance with Me'']]===
In 2007, a new single entitled "Love Sweet Love", would be released and act as the lead-off single to ''[[Kicking Stones]]'', which was released on April 10, 2007. The single peaked at No.&nbsp;11 on the Canadian country charts and was followed by a string of Top 20 singles. The second single, the album's title track, reached No.&nbsp;13, and "[[Darlin' (Poacher song)|Darlin']]", the album's third single, became Reid's first Top 5 single. Two additional singles, "Thank You" and "Out of the Blue", were released, peaking at No.&nbsp;13 and No.&nbsp;7 on the charts, respectively.

Reid's fifth studio album, ''[[Dance with Me (Johnny Reid album)|Dance with Me]]'', was released in March 2009, and debuted at No.&nbsp;1 on the Canadian Top Country Albums chart and No.&nbsp;3 on the [[Canadian Albums Chart]].<ref>"SOCAN@Country Music Awards", ''Words and Music'', Winter 2010</ref> "[[A Woman Like You (Johnny Reid song)|A Woman Like You]]" was released as the first single from the album and reached No.&nbsp;4 on the country charts, peaking No.&nbsp;4 in 2009. The title track was then released and reached No.&nbsp;10, followed by "Old Flame", which has so far peaked in the Top 10.

A Christmas album, entitled ''[[Christmas (Johnny Reid album)|Christmas]]'', was released November 10, 2009. Reid's first live DVD, ''Live at the Jubilee'' was released on February 9, 2010.

===[[A Place Called Love (Johnny Reid album)|''A Place Called Love'']]===
In 2010, Johnny Reid signed with [[EMI]] for the release of his next studio album, ''[[A Place Called Love (Johnny Reid album)|A Place Called Love]]'', released August 31, 2010. The first single, "[[Today I'm Gonna Try and Change the World]]" was released on June 7. Reid's second live DVD, ''Heart & Soul'' was released on March 29, 2011, in Canada.

===[[Fire It Up (Johnny Reid album)|''Fire It Up'']]===
2012 marked the release of Johnny Reid's second album for EMI, ''[[Fire It Up (Johnny Reid album)|Fire It Up]]''. The [[Fire It Up (Johnny Reid song)|title track]] was released on January 10 to Canadian radio and [[iTunes]]. The album features Canadian guest artists [[Carolyn Dawn Johnson]] and [[Serena Ryder]]. The album was also released as a deluxe version with four bonus songs and a DVD featuring the making of the album.

Reid released his second Christmas album, ''[[A Christmas Gift to You]]'', on October 22, 2013.<ref>{{cite news|title=Johnny Reid brings Christmas tour to Edmonton|url=https://edmontonjournal.com/life/Johnny+Reid+brings+Christmas+tour+Edmonton/8997588/story.html|access-date=October 22, 2013|newspaper=[[Edmonton Journal]]|date=October 4, 2013}}</ref>

===''[[What Love Is All About]]''===
Reid's first studio album for [[Universal Music Group|Universal Music Canada]], ''[[What Love Is All About]]'', was released on November 13, 2015. The album features a duet with [[Kardinal Offishall]]. Reid's first single off the album was "A Picture of You". It was followed by "Honey Honey".

=== ''Revival'' ===
Reid released ''Revival'' with [[Universal Music Group|Universal Music Canada]] on November 3, 2017. The album featured 13 original songs and marked Reid's most soul-inspired album to date. In 2018, Revival was nominated for two JUNO Awards in the categories of Album of the Year and Adult Contemporary Album of the Year. On November 9, 2018, Reid released ''Revival Live'', his first live album, which was recorded during the 2018 Revival Live Canadian tour.

== Maggie (the Musical) ==
In 2022, Reid announced that his new musical, co-written with Canadian playwright Matt Murray, would premiere at Theatre Aquarius in [[Hamilton, Ontario]] in 2023.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Maggie The Musical to Premiere in Hamilton, ON in 2023 |url=https://www.johnnyreid.com/news/maggie-the-musical-to-premiere-in-hamilton-on |access-date=2022-11-25 |website=www.johnnyreid.com |date=15 July 2022 |language=en}}</ref> The show is also scheduled to headline [[Charlottetown Festival|The Charlottetown Festival]] in summer 2023<ref>{{Cite news |last=Gallant |first=Isabelle |date=December 1, 2021 |title=New musical by Johnny Reid to headline Charlottetown Festival in 2023 |work=CBC News |url=https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/prince-edward-island/pei-charlottetown-festival-lineup-2023-1.6670868}}</ref> and will have a limited run at The [[Savoy Theatre, Glace Bay|Savoy Theatre]] in Glace Bay, Nova Scotia, in fall 2023. Set in [[Lanarkshire]], [[Scotland]], ''Maggie'' is based on a true story and inspired by Reid's grandmother. It features music co-written by Johnny Reid, Matt Murray and Canadian musical theatre composer Bob Foster and is produced by Michael Rubinoff, the originating producer of [[Come from Away|''Come From Away'']]. Originally titled ''My Bonnie Lass'', the show was developed at [[Sheridan College]]’s prestigious Canadian Music Theatre Project in 2018,<ref>{{Cite web |author=BWW News Desk |title=Sheridan's Canadian Music Theatre Project Launches Four New Musicals |url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/toronto/article/Sheridans-Canadian-Music-Theatre-Project-Launches-Four-New-Musicals-20171011 |access-date=2022-11-29 |website=BroadwayWorld.com |language=en}}</ref> receiving a studio theatre production at the college in 2019.
