'''Andy Gibson''' (born September 15, 1981) is an American [[country music]] singer.  He co-wrote "[[Don't You Wanna Stay]]", a duet between [[Jason Aldean]] and [[Kelly Clarkson]] which reached No. 1 on the country music charts in 2011. Later in the year, he signed to R&J Records and released his debut single, "Wanna Make You Love Me".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://randjrecords.com/index.cfm?id=19|title=Andy Gibson|work=R&J Records|accessdate=October 4, 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120424225454/http://www.randjrecords.com/index.cfm?id=19|archive-date=April 24, 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref> Billy Dukes of ''Taste of Country'' gave the song four stars out of five, saying that it "begin[s] gathering attention after frequent listens."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tasteofcountry.com/andy-gibson-wanna-make-you-love-me/|title=Andy Gibson — "Wanna Make You Love Me"|last=Dukes|first=Billy|date=September 30, 2011|work=Taste of Country|accessdate=October 4, 2011}}</ref> The song has been made into a music video directed by David McClister, which has aired on [[Country Music Television|CMT]].<ref name="cmt">{{cite web|url=http://www.cmt.com/videos/andy-gibson/692555/wanna-make-you-love-me.jhtml|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111121014604/http://www.cmt.com/videos/andy-gibson/692555/wanna-make-you-love-me.jhtml|url-status=dead|archive-date=November 21, 2011|title="Wanna Make You Love Me" video|work=CMT|accessdate=October 4, 2011}}</ref> Gibson moved to [[Curb Records]] in April 2012 following the closure of R&J,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.allaccess.com/net-news/archive/story/105086/andy-gibson-moves-to-curb-records|title=Andy Gibson Moves To Curb Records|date=April 20, 2012|work=All Access|accessdate=April 21, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.roughstock.com/blog/r-j-records-to-close-andy-gibson-to-curb |title=R&J Records To Close; Andy Gibson To Curb |last=Bjorke |first=Matt |date=April 23, 2012 |work=Roughstock |accessdate=April 23, 2012 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120425225405/http://www.roughstock.com/blog/r-j-records-to-close-andy-gibson-to-curb |archivedate=April 25, 2012  }}</ref> although some R&J staff continued promotion of "Wanna Make You Love Me".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.musicrow.com/2012/04/rj-records-closing/|title=R&J Records Closing|last=Freeman|first=Jon|date=April 23, 2012|work=Music Row|accessdate=April 23, 2012}}</ref> Gibson's first Curb single, "Summer Back", entered the charts in late 2012.
