'''Michael Lane Turner''' (born February 3, 1967, in [[Monahans, Texas]]) is an American singer-songwriter. Signed to [[Warner Bros. Records]] since 2004, he released two singles for the label, including "Always Wanting More (Breathless)", which reached No.&nbsp;56 on the [[Hot Country Songs]] charts in 2004. Lane signed with Original Signal Recordings in 2009.

== Early life ==
Turner was born in Monahans, Texas, and attended school in [[Levelland, Texas|Levelland]], which he described as "a place so flat you can watch your dog run away for two days".<ref>{{cite news|last=Johnson|first=Natalie|title=Lane Turner could be the next country star|work=[[Lubbock Avalanche-Journal]]|date=September 2, 2008|url=http://www.lubbockonline.com/stories/090208/liv_326496802.shtml|accessdate=October 1, 2008}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Meet "The Next GAC Star" Finalist Lane Turner, Page 1|publisher=[[Great American Country]]|url=http://www.gactv.com/gac/nw_feature/article/0,3038,GAC_26499_5917657_01,00.html|accessdate=October 1, 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120211172244/http://www.gactv.com/gac/nw_feature/article/0,3038,GAC_26499_5917657_01,00.html|archive-date=February 11, 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref> He received his first guitar as a gift from his parents when he was eleven years old and his father taught him to play the songs he heard on the radio.<ref name=Bio>{{cite web|title=Lane Turner|publisher=[[New Country Star]]|url=http://www.newcountrystar.com/LaneTurner.htm|accessdate=October 1, 2008}}</ref> He attended [[Texas Tech University]] in [[Lubbock, Texas|Lubbock]], where he was an [[All-America]]n [[decathlon|decathlete]]. He also played in a band called Diamondback during his time in college.<ref name=Bio /><ref>{{cite web|title=Meet "The Next GAC Star" Finalist Lane Turner, Page 2|publisher=[[Great American Country]]|page=2|url=http://www.gactv.com/gac/nw_feature/article/0,3038,GAC_26499_5917657_02,00.html|accessdate=October 1, 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080916222058/http://www.gactv.com/gac/nw_feature/article/0,3038,GAC_26499_5917657_02,00.html|archive-date=September 16, 2008|url-status=dead}}</ref>

== Singing career ==
Turner signed to Warner Bros. Records in 2004. He released two singles for the label—"Always Wanting More (Breathless)" and "Let You Go"—but no album. He also co-wrote [[Blaine Larsen]]'s single "I Don't Know What She Said".

In 2008, Turner was a contestant on ''The Next GAC Star'', which aired on the network [[Great American Country]]. He completed the competition as runner up to winning band One Night Rodeo.<ref name=Bio /><ref>{{cite news|last=Keel|first=Beverly|date=September 1, 2008|title=2 from Tennessee compete in Bravo model search|work=The Tennessean|url=http://www.tennessean.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/20080915/COLUMNIST0501/809150347/1005/ENTERTAINMENT|accessdate=October 1, 2008}} {{Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=The Votes Are In: Who's The Next GAC Star?|publisher=[[Great American Country]]|url=http://www.gactv.com/gac/nw_headlines/article/0,3034,GAC_26063_5937578_,00.html|accessdate=October 1, 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080926154459/http://www.gactv.com/gac/nw_headlines/article/0,3034,GAC_26063_5937578_,00.html|archive-date=September 26, 2008|url-status=dead}}</ref>

As of early 2011, Lane has replaced Dustin Evans as lead vocalist for the country music band Western Underground, originally fronted by [[Chris LeDoux]].<ref name="westernunderground.com"/>

Lane has continued in the Western Underground tradition as the base player for Ned Ledoux’s band.  [[Ned LeDoux]].
