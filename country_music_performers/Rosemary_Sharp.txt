'''Rosemary Sharp''' is an American [[country music]] singer. Between 1987 and 1988, Sharp released four singles on Canyon Creek Records, including "If You're Going to Tell Me Lies" which peaked at 67<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/rosemary-sharp|title=Rosemary Sharp If You're Gonna Tell Me Lies Chart History|magazine=Billboard|access-date=February 9, 2020}}</ref> and "Real Good Heartache" which peaked at 76.<ref>{{Cite book|last=Whitburn|first=Joel|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=AZszAQAAIAAJ&q=rosemary+sharp+real+good+heartache|title=Top Country Singles, 1944 to 2001: Chart Data Compiled from Billboard's Country Singles Charts, 1944–2001|date=2002|publisher=Record Research|isbn=978-0-89820-151-2|pages=497|language=en}}</ref> Three of her songs reached the Top 20 on the ''[[RPM (magazine)|RPM]]'' Country Tracks chart in Canada.<ref>[http://www.collectionscanada.gc.ca/rpm/028020-110.01-e.php?PHPSESSID=or6t8bec3u9j94qn4p1q18enu5&q1=rosemary+sharp&q2=Country+Singles&interval=24 ''RPM'' Country Singles]</ref> Her single "Didn't You Go and Leave Me" is included in the collection of the [[National Museum of American History]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Didn't You Go and Leave Me |url=https://americanhistory.si.edu/collections/search/object/nmah_1271155 |access-date=12 May 2022 |work=[[National Museum of American History]] |publisher=[[Smithsonian]]}}</ref>
