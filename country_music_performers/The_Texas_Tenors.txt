'''The Texas Tenors''' are a three-time [[Emmy Award]]-winning [[classical crossover]], [[trio (music)|trio]] vocal group formed in 2009 by [[country music|country]] singer JC Fisher, pop singer Marcus Collins and [[opera]] singer John Hagen. They were a top four finalist in the [[America's Got Talent (season 4)|fourth season]] of ''[[America's Got Talent]]'', making them the highest ranking vocal group in the show's history.<ref name="thetexastenors">{{cite web|url=http://www.thetexastenors.com/bios/the-texas-tenors|title=The Texas Tenors, 12 Irish Tenors or 3 Redneck Tenors &#124; The Texas Tenors|publisher=thetexastenors.com|access-date=18 January 2014}}</ref>

In 2013, The trio filmed their first [[PBS]] special with [[Phoenix Symphony|The Phoenix Symphony]], performing songs from their second album ''You Should Dream''.  Along with being one of only two acts from ''[[America's Got Talent]]'' (The Texas Tenors and [[Jackie Evancho]]) to star in their own television special for [[PBS]], The Texas Tenors achieved another milestone in 2014 when they were honored with five [[Rocky Mountain Emmy Awards|Emmy Awards]] nominations and three wins for the self-produced special. The group also supports many charitable organizations.<ref>thetexastenors.com</ref>

The group has performed more than 2000 concerts in over 20 countries including [[Great Britain]] and [[China]]. In August 2017, The Texas Tenors premiered their second [[PBS]] special and on September 8, 2017, released their third studio album of the same name, ''Rise'', which debuted at #1 on the [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard Classical Albums Chart]], #1 [[Billboard (magazine)|Heatseeker New Artist Chart]] and #22 [[Billboard (magazine)|Top Current Albums]] resulting in the group's highest charting positions and most successful sales week ever.<ref name="tttref">{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/biz/search/charts?f[0]=ts_chart_artistname%3Athe%20texas%20tenors%20&f[1]=ss_bb_type%3Achart_item&type=2&artist=the%20texas%20tenors%20|title=The Texas Tenors Billboard Chart History; The Texas Tenors|publisher=billboard.com/biz|access-date=October 2, 2017}}</ref>

In 2018, the trio released ''A Collection of Broadway & American Classics'' exclusively at their live concerts and through their website at the request of their fan club.  Without distribution or a national campaign, the album debuted at #1 on the [[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard Classical Albums Chart]] and spent 10 weeks in the top 10.<ref name="ClassicalBillboard">{{cite web|url= https://www.billboard.com/charts/classical-albums/2018-08-18|title=Billboard Chart; Classical Albums|publisher=billboard.com|access-date=August 8, 2018}}</ref>

==Career==

===''America's Got Talent'' (2009)===
The trio auditioned for [[America's Got Talent (season 4)|''America's Got Talent'' season four]], performing a crossover version of "[[Mountain Music (song)|Mountain Music]]". They advanced week after week, finishing in fourth place.

===After ''America's Got Talent''===
The group's self-titled debut album, ''The Texas Tenors'', was released in 2009. The album contained all four songs that were performed on ''America's Got Talent'', some [[arias]] such as "[[Nessun dorma]]" and "[[La donna è mobile]]" as well as other songs. In 2011, the group remastered the album and included some new material. The album has sold 225,000 units to date.<ref name="thetexastenors2">{{cite web|url=http://thetexastenors.com/albums/country-roots-classical-sound-remastered-special-edition/|title=Country Roots: Classical Sound (Remastered Special Edition) &#124; The Texas Tenors|publisher=thetexastenors.com|access-date=18 January 2014}}</ref>

Their second studio album, ''You Should Dream'', was released on December 10, 2013. Produced by [[Academy Award]] and [[Grammy Award|Grammy]] nominated producer [[Nigel Wright (record producer)|Nigel Wright]], ''You Should Dream'' features "[[God Bless the USA]]", "[[Unchained Melody]]", [[The Rolling Stones]]' "[[Wild Horses (The Rolling Stones song)|Wild Horses]]", two original songs and a 65 piece orchestra.<ref name="thetexastenors4">{{cite web|url=http://thetexastenors.com/eshop/?slug=index&cPath=23|title=eShop &#124; The Texas Tenors|publisher=thetexastenors.com|access-date=18 January 2014}}</ref>

The Texas Tenors are one of only two acts from [[America's Got Talent]] to star in their own television special for [[PBS]], the group achieved a milestone in 2014 when they were honored with five [[Rocky Mountain Emmy Awards|Emmy Awards]] nominations and three wins.

In 2017, The Texas Tenors' third studio album ''Rise'' debuted at #1 on the [[Billboard (magazine)|Classical Albums]] Chart and #5 on the [[Billboard (magazine)|Top Country Albums]] Chart resulting in the most successful week of sales for the group and their highest charting positions to date.<ref name="tttref"/>

===''America's Got Talent: The Champions'' (2019)===
In 2019, The Texas Tenors competed on ''[[America's Got Talent: The Champions]]''. Their performance consisted of singing "[[Unchained Melody]]" as a trio and in the end, was acclaimed by the judges and the audience.
