'''Amy Dalley''' is an American [[country music]] artist. Signed to [[Curb Records]] in 2003, she left the label in 2008. Dalley has released seven singles, of which five have entered the U.S. ''Billboard'' [[Hot Country Songs]] charts despite never releasing an album. Her highest-peaking single is "[[Men Don't Change]]", which reached a peak of number 23 in 2004. In addition to her own music, Dalley co-wrote [[Reba McEntire]]'s single "My Sister". Her album, ''It's Time'', was issued independently via digital retailers and as well as a physical copy in 2009.

==Biography==
Dalley's first musical performance was at a talent show at her high school. She continued to perform around her hometown of [[Kingsport, Tennessee]].<ref name="curb">{{Cite web |url=http://www.curb.com/artists/artistbio_T1.cfm?ID=107 |title=Curb.com : Amy Dalley biography |access-date=June 19, 2007 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070927032235/http://www.curb.com/artists/artistbio_T1.cfm?ID=107 |archive-date=September 27, 2007 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref name="cmt">[https://web.archive.org/web/20040824103652/http://www.cmt.com/artists/az/dalley_amy/bio.jhtml CMT.com : Amy Dalley : Biography]</ref> After briefly attending [[East Tennessee State University]], she went on to perform at the [[Dollywood]] amusement park.<ref name="cmt"/> In 1994, she moved to [[Nashville, Tennessee]] and formed a band called the Gypsy Hillbillies, which toured nationally.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.womenofcountry.com/5questionswithamy.shtml |title=5 Questions with Amy Dalley |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120207092931/http://www.womenofcountry.com/5questionswithamy.shtml |archive-date=February 7, 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref> She was later signed to a publishing deal at Hamstein Music.<ref name="cmt"/> One of her songs, "Dream Too Small", was featured in an episode of ''[[Dawson's Creek]]''.<ref name="curb"/>

Dalley's first single, "Love's Got an Attitude (It Is What It Is)", was released in 2003, peaking at No.&nbsp;27 on the country charts. It was followed by "I Think You're Beautiful" at No.&nbsp;43 and her highest-charting single, "Men Don't Change" at No.&nbsp;23. Her fourth single, the No.&nbsp;29 "I Would Cry", charted in 2005, and her album was delayed again. Later that same year, [[Reba McEntire]] charted the single "[[My Sister (Reba McEntire song)|My Sister]]", which Dalley co-wrote with [[Roxie Dean]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.reba.com/music/discography/roomtobreathe/ |title=Reba McEntire \\ Music |access-date=June 19, 2007 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070605235930/http://www.reba.com/music/discography/roomtobreathe/ |archive-date=June 5, 2007 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

In 2006, Dalley released "Good Kind of Crazy", which spent one week at No.&nbsp;60 on the charts, and "Let's Try Goodbye", which did not chart either. She left Curb in 2008. She finally released her album ''It's Time'' through her label Mad Jack Records, on January 19, 2009, through online musical vendors.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://americannoise.com/amy-dalley-interview/|title= Amy Dalley Tries Goodbye: Her Life After Curb|last=Malec|first=Jim|date=January 16, 2009|publisher=American Noise|accessdate=January 16, 2009}}</ref> The album was produced by Dalley and Jack Sizemore and included re-recordings of her singles "I Think You're Beautiful", "Let's Try Goodbye", and "Men Don't Change".<ref>{{cite AV media notes|title=It's Time|year=2009|type=Compact disc|publisher=Mad Jack Records}}</ref>

Dalley released her second album ''Coming Out of the Pain'' on her label Mad Jack Records in 2011. It was re-released in 2012 on Rock Ridge Records.
