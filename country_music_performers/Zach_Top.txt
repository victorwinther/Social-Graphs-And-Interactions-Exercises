'''Zach Top''' (born September 26, 1997) is an American [[country music]] singer-songwriter from [[Sunnyside, Washington]] signed to Leo33.<ref name="Music Row"/>

==Background==
Zach Top was born on September 26, 1997,<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |title=Zach Top |url=https://www.opry.com/artists/zach-top |access-date=July 17, 2024 |website=[[Grand Ole Opry]]}}</ref> and grew up on a ranch in [[Sunnyside, Washington]]. He got his musical start performing as part of a [[Bluegrass music|bluegrass]] band with his siblings called Top String.<ref name="Zach Top Bio">{{cite web|url=https://zachtop.com/bio|title=Bio — Zach Top Official|website=Zach Top|access-date=June 7, 2024}}</ref> After performing as part of various bluegrass groups throughout his teens and early twenties, Top relocated to [[Nashville, Tennessee|Nashville]] in 2021, where he first signed a publishing deal with Major Bob Music.<ref name="Zach Top Bio"/> He began recording as a solo artist in 2019, releasing a string of bluegrass singles that culminated in the release of his self-titled debut [[Extended play|EP]] with RBR Entertainment on May 13, 2022.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://rbrentertainment.com/zack-top|title=RBR Entertainment — Zach Top|website=RBR Entertainment|access-date=June 7, 2024}}</ref> 

He pivoted to recording [[country music]] and in September 2023, it was announced that Top was signed as the flagship artist for the newly-formed independent record label Leo33.<ref name="Music Row">{{cite web|url=https://musicrow.com/2023/09/leo33-signs-flagship-artist-zach-top/|title=Leo33 Signs Flagship Artist Zach Top|author=LB Cantrell|date=September 6, 2023|website=Music Row|access-date=June 7, 2024}}</ref> His debut single to country radio, "[[Sounds Like the Radio]]", was released on January 8, 2024, and was the most-added song at the format with 55 stations adding the song upon impact.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://issuu.com/countryaircheck/docs/issue_891_-_janaury_8_2024|title=Country Aircheck Weekly January 7, 2024, Issue 891|date=January 8, 2024|website=Country Aircheck|access-date=June 7, 2024}}</ref> It has since charted in the top 30 of the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Country Airplay]] chart. Top co-wrote "Sounds Like the Radio" with [[Carson Chamberlain]] and Wyatt McCubbin, inspired by his love of 1990s-era country music and artists such as [[George Strait]], [[Keith Whitley]], and [[Randy Travis]].<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/country/zach-top-sounds-like-the-radio-makin-tracks-1235611345/|title=Zach Top on Revisiting the '90s – 'The Peak of Country Music' — With Throwback 'Sounds Like The Radio'|author=Tom Roland|date=February 21, 2024|magazine=Billboard|access-date=June 7, 2024}}</ref> It served as the lead single to Top's debut studio album, ''[[Cold Beer & Country Music]]'', which was released on April 5, 2024.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://holler.country/reviews/zach-top-cold-beer-and-country-music/|title=Zach Top – Cold Beer & Country Music Review and Tracklist|author=Soda Canter|date=April 4, 2024|website=Holler|access-date=June 7, 2024}}</ref>
