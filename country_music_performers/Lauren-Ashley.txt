'''Lauren-Ashley''' Redmond (born November 15, 1991), more commonly known as Lauren-Ashley, is a [[country singer]]/songwriter based in [[Nashville, TN]]. Lauren-Ashley was raised in [[Griffin, Georgia]], where she attended [[Flint River Academy]] for high school and began singing. She attended [[Georgia Southern University]] for one year (2010–2011), where she won GSU Idol and shortly after began recording her first [[Extended play|EP]] with producer Trey Roth of Black Cat Studio.<ref name=Brown>{{cite web|last=Brown|first=Jan|title=Interview With Lauren-Ashley|url=http://laurenashleymusic.webstarts.com/about.html|publisher=WebStarts|accessdate=October 25, 2011}}</ref>

Lauren-Ashley currently resides in Nashville, TN and has been performing often in the Southeastern United States and recording. Notable performances include opening for [[Ronnie Milsap]], opening for [[Luke Bryan]], and performing at the [[Country Music Hall of Fame]] in Nashville.<ref name=Brown/> She was awarded Georgia Country Female Artist of the Year in November 2011 by voters.<ref name=Georgia-country.com />

==Biography==
Lauren-Ashley Redmond was born November 15, 1991, in Griffin, Georgia.<ref name=Brown/> Ever since her early years in Griffin, music was a part of Lauren-Ashley's life. Family members of Lauren-Ashley recall her singing since she could talk. As a child, she performed in church and school talent shows.<ref name=Marshall>{{cite web|last=Marshall|first=Sheila|title=Griffin Country Singer Gains Statewide Fan Base|url=http://www.laurenashleymusic.com/news/griffin-country-singer-gains-statewide-fan-base/|publisher=Stone Soup|accessdate=October 25, 2011}}</ref> However, Lauren-Ashley's involvement in music became more serious in high school. At Flint River Academy, she participated in [[cheerleading]] and [[softball]].<ref name=Biography>{{cite web|title=Biography|url=http://www.laurenashleymusic.com/biography/|publisher=LaurenAshleyMusic|accessdate=October 31, 2011}}</ref> Her musical activities in high school were Literary Team and All-Select Chorus.<ref name=Brown/> Her senior year of high school, she won second place in a state talent competition.<ref name=Marshall /> 
After graduating from Flint River Academy, Lauren-Ashley attended [[Georgia Southern University]] for the 2010–2011 academic year. In October 2010, Lauren-Ashley represented her sorority, [[Alpha Delta Pi]], in the GSU Idol Competition, which is an [[American Idol]]-based singing competition for Georgia Southern University students.<ref name=Homecoming>{{cite web|title=Homecoming|url=http://www.georgiasouthern.edu/traditions/homecoming.php|publisher=Georgia Southern University|accessdate=November 1, 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111115183328/http://www.georgiasouthern.edu/traditions/homecoming.php|archive-date=November 15, 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref> After three rounds of performances, Lauren-Ashley was named the winner of the GSU competition.<ref name=Biography />
Soon after her GSU Idol win, Lauren-Ashley began working with producer Trey Roth of Black Cat Studio, where an [[Extended play|EP]] was being made.<ref name=Biography /> Since the release of the EP, Lauren-Ashley has been promoting her EP and performing those songs as well as covers throughout the Southeastern USA.

==Artistry==

===Musical style and influences===
Lauren-Ashley's musical influences include [[Miranda Lambert]], [[Stevie Nicks]], [[Jason Aldean]], and [[Eric Church]].<ref name=Brown/> Also influenced by [[Carrie Underwood]], her work is characterized by country vocals and music with a rock edge.<ref name=Biography /> As far as song-writing, all of Lauren-Ashley's EP tracks are originals.<ref name=Marshall /> In the same manner of artists such as [[Johnny Cash]] and [[Kris Kristofferson]], Lauren-Ashley writes songs about whatever is happening in life at the moment.<ref name=Wheaton>{{cite news|last=Wheaton|first=Bobby|title=Lauren-Ashley|newspaper=Kitchen Drawer Magazine|date=July 1, 2011}}</ref>

===Stage performances and public image===
In Lauren-Ashley's career thus far, she has received mostly positive reception. Audiences seem to enjoy the amount of energy in her performances.<ref name=Wheaton /> Bobby Wheaton believes what sets her apart is her song-writing content choices and her instincts, but does not know whether she will "reach the country radio rotation."<ref name=Wheaton /> Based on her CD sales and nomination for Georgia Country Female Artist of the Year, Shelia Marshall calls her a local and state-wide favorite.<ref name=Marshall /> Georgia-Country.com gave her EP a positive review, saying about the product that she "displays a great range of vocal talent and creates product that is fun and easy to listen to. While she is definitely influenced by the likes of Carrie [Underwood] and Miranda [Lambert], she also sets herself apart."<ref name=Georgia-Country>{{cite web|title=Lauren Ashley "Lauren Ashley"|url=http://www.georgia-country.com/reviews.php|publisher=Georgia-Country.com|accessdate=November 3, 2011}}</ref>

==Awards==
Lauren-Ashley's career was kickstarted by her win of Georgia Southern University's GSU Idol competition in 2010. Since then, she has also won a national songwriting competition, Wake Up Nashville—and Dream Big.<ref name=Brown /> Her song, "Cold November" placed first, and her song "Drive" placed in the top ten.<ref name=Biography /> Most recently, Lauren-Ashley won 2011 Georgia Country Female Artist of the Year.<ref name=Georgia-country.com>{{cite web|title=The 2011 Georgia Country Awards|url=http://www.georgia-country.com/awards.php|publisher=Georgia-country.com|accessdate=November 29, 2011}}</ref>

==Performances==

===Style===
Lauren-Ashley performs both her original tracks as well as covers, including [[Heidi Newfield]]'s "[[Johnny and June]]," as well as [[Carrie Underwood]]'s "[[Last Name (song)|Last Name]]".<ref name=Wheaton /> The atmosphere is high-energy from the rock edge brought on to the country songs by her vocals as well as the band.<ref name=Wheaton />
