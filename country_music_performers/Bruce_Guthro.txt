'''Bruce Guthro''' {{post-nominals|country=CAN|size=100%|ONS}} (August 31, 1961 – September 5, 2023) was a Canadian singer-songwriter from [[Cape Breton Island]], Nova Scotia. Guthro recorded as a solo artist, and was lead vocalist for the Scottish [[Celtic rock]] band [[Runrig]] from 1998,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.prnewswire.co.uk/cgi/news/release?id=56326|title=RUNRIG NAME NEW SINGER: BRUCE GUTHRO JOINS TOP SCOTTISH BAND|date=July 17, 1998|work=prnewswire.co.uk}}</ref> until the group retired in 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.heraldscotland.com/arts_ents/16584918.the-last-dance-thousands-say-farewell-to-runrig/|title=The Last Dance: thousands say farewell to Runrig|website=HeraldScotland|date=August 20, 2018 |language=en|access-date=October 10, 2019}}</ref> Guthro received several ECMAs ([[East Coast Music Awards]]),<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.ecma.ca/association.asp?section%3D14 |title=East Coast Music Association - Event History |access-date=February 26, 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090221200715/http://ecma.ca/association.asp?section=14 |archive-date=February 21, 2009 }}</ref> and hosted and conceptualized the Canadian TV show ''Songwriters Circle'', on which guests included [[Jim Cuddy]], [[Colin James]], and [[Alan Doyle]] (of the Canadian band [[Great Big Sea]]).<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://books.google.com/books?id=EhEEAAAAMBAJ&q=1998+canadian+radio+music+award+bruce+guthro&pg=PA62 |title = Billboard|date = September 15, 2001}}</ref>

Guthro was also the father of musicians [[Dylan Guthro]] and Jodi Guthro.<ref name="auto">{{cite web |title=Dylan Guthro gets the stone rolling |url=http://thechronicleherald.ca/artslife/60368-dylan-guthro-gets-stone-rolling |website=thechronicleherald.ca |access-date=January 13, 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120212122458/http://thechronicleherald.ca/artslife/60368-dylan-guthro-gets-stone-rolling |archive-date=February 12, 2012 |url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.metronews.ca/news/halifax/2015/06/03/songwriter-circles-a-jam-for-guthro-popular-nova-scotia-musician-says-event-offers-unique-window-into-artists-head.html |title=Songwriter circles 'a jam' for Nova Scotia musician Bruce Guthro &#124; Metro News |website=www.metronews.ca |access-date=January 13, 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303175957/http://www.metronews.ca/news/halifax/2015/06/03/songwriter-circles-a-jam-for-guthro-popular-nova-scotia-musician-says-event-offers-unique-window-into-artists-head.html |archive-date=March 3, 2016 |url-status=dead}}</ref> He co-produced Dylan's award-winning 2012 debut album ''All That's True'' with [[Dave Gunning]] and co-wrote five of the album's songs.<ref name="auto"/><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://thehalifaxmusicphile.blogspot.com/2012/11/2012-music-nova-scotia-music-awards.html|title=The Halifax Musicphile: 2012 Music Nova Scotia Awards – List of Winners and Nominnees|website=Thehalifaxmusicphile.blogspot.com|date=November 13, 2012}}</ref>

Guthro died from cancer on September 5, 2023, five days after his 62nd birthday.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/nova-scotia/bruce-guthro-songwriter-obituary-1.6957898 |title= Cape Breton singer-songwriter Bruce Guthro dies at 62 |website=cbc.ca|date=September 6, 2023}}</ref> On October 4, 2023, he was posthumously appointed a member of the [[Order of Nova Scotia]] for contributions to the music industry, Canadian culture and philanthropy.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://novascotia.ca/news/release/?id=20231004003|title=2023 Order of Nova Scotia Recipients Announced|author=Lieutenant Governor of Nova Scotia|website=novascotia.ca|date=May 11, 2018 |language=en|access-date=2023-11-02}}</ref>
