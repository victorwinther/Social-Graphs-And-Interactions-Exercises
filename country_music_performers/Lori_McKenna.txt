'''Lorraine McKenna''' ({{née}} '''Giroux''';<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.bostonglobe.com/magazine/2012/03/11/lori-mckenna-writing-her-heart-out/zxXbmVKZmSzEK2foxB9hrO/story.html|title=Stoughton's Lori McKenna is Writing her Heart Out|last=Helman|first=Scott|date=March 11, 2012|website=Boston Globe}}</ref> born December 22, 1968) is an American [[Folk music|folk]], [[Americana (music)|Americana]], and [[country music]] singer-songwriter. In [[58th Annual Grammy Awards|2016]], she was nominated for the [[Grammy Award for Song of the Year]] and won [[Grammy Award for Best Country Song|Best Country Song]] for co-writing the hit single "[[Girl Crush]]" performed by [[Little Big Town]].<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=http://winchester.wickedlocal.com/entertainment/20160216/stoughtons-lori-mckenna-wins-grammy-award/1|title=Stoughton's Lori McKenna Wins Grammy Award|last=Barbuto|first=Dana|date=February 16, 2016|website=Wicked Local - Winchester Star}}</ref> In 2017, she again won [[Grammy Award for Best Country Song|Best Country Song]] at the [[59th Annual Grammy Awards]] for writing "[[Humble and Kind]]" performed by [[Tim McGraw]].<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|url=http://people.com/country/lori-mckenna-tim-mcgraw-humble-and-kind/|title=Watch 'Humble & Kind' Songwriter Lori McKenna Sing the CMA-Winning Tim McGraw Hit She Wrote|last=Finan|first=Eileen|date=November 2, 2016|website=People.com}}</ref> 
McKenna along with [[Lady Gaga]], [[Natalie Hemby]] and [[Hillary Lindsey]] wrote the second single off the soundtrack to the 2018 film ''[[A Star Is Born (2018 film)|A Star Is Born]]'' called "[[Always Remember Us This Way]].” McKenna performed backing vocals along with Lindsey and Hemby, and the song received a nomination for [[Grammy Award for Song of the Year|Song of the Year]] at the [[62nd Annual Grammy Awards]].

==Early and personal life==
McKenna was born and raised in [[Stoughton, Massachusetts]], where she still lives today.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.cbsnews.com/news/lori-mckenna-a-yankee-at-home-in-country-musics-capital/|title=Lori McKenna, a Yankee at Home in Country Music's Capital|author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.-->|date=February 12, 2017|website=CBS News}}</ref> Her mother died when she was seven years old, a theme often touched on in her music. She met her husband, Gene McKenna, in third grade<ref name=":0" /> and married him at age 19. She has five children and has been married for more than 30 years.<ref name=":1" />

McKenna first started writing lullabies to her children. Her brother, who first introduced her to the guitar, encouraged her to attend an open-mic night at the Old Vienna Kaffeehause in [[Westborough, Massachusetts]] in 1996.<ref name=":0" /> The organizer heard her play and encouraged her to come back, becoming her informal manager and booking shows for her around Boston.<ref name=":0" />

==Career==
McKenna was managed by Gabriel Unger from 2000 to 2004. During this time, she released her first four albums under [[Signature Sounds Recordings|Signature Sounds]] and developed a folk music fan base in the Northeast.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://nodepression.com/article/lori-mckenna-dreams-everyday-housewife|title=Lori McKenna: Dreams of an Everyday Housewife|last=Gleason|first=Holly|date=August 31, 2007|website=No Depression: The Journal of Roots Music|access-date=August 20, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180820234944/http://nodepression.com/article/lori-mckenna-dreams-everyday-housewife|archive-date=August 20, 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref> She won awards at the [[Boston Music Awards]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.bostonmusicawards07.com/|title=Boston Music Awards 07|website=www.bostonmusicawards07.com|access-date=2018-08-20}}</ref> and performed at the [[Sundance Film Festival]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.gettyimages.be/gebeurtenis/sundance-film-festival-ascap-music-cafe-with-anna-nalick-lori-mckenna-los-pinguos-suzanne-vega-and-ricki-lee-jones-107657413#olivia-arciero-during-2005-sundance-film-festival-ascap-music-cafe-picture-id115856768|title=2005 Sundance Film Festival - ASCAP Music Cafe with Anna Nalick, Lori McKenna, Los Pinguos, Suzanne Vega and Ricki Lee Jones|work=Getty Images|access-date=2018-08-20|language=nl}}</ref>

In 2004, singer-songwriter [[Mary Gauthier]] introduced McKenna's album ''[[Bittertown]]'' to her Nashville friends. Upon hearing it, [[Faith Hill]] stalled her completed 2005 record ''[[Fireflies (Faith Hill album)|Fireflies]]'' to replace tracks with covers of McKenna's songs.<ref name="allmusic" /><ref name=":0" /> Hill and husband [[Tim McGraw]] became McKenna's champions and asked her to tour with them in 2006.<ref name=":2">{{Cite news|url=http://www.latimes.com/entertainment/music/la-et-ms-songwriter-lori-mckenna-on-new-album-and-hits-for-others-20161020-snap-story.html|title=Lori McKenna Stays Humble about Success, Nominations|last=Rodman|first=Sarah|date=September 19, 2016|newspaper=LA Times}}</ref> Hill took McKenna to perform with her on the ''[[The Oprah Winfrey Show|Oprah Winfrey Show]]''.<ref name=":5">{{Cite web|url=http://country1025.com/2016/02/get-to-know-the-girl-behind-girl-crush-lori-mckenna-humbleandkind/|title=Get to Know: The Hometown Girl behind "Girl Crush," Lori McKenna|last=Kruse|first=Carolyn|date=February 6, 2016|website=Country 102.5|access-date=August 20, 2018|archive-date=August 21, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180821093751/https://country1025.com/2016/02/get-to-know-the-girl-behind-girl-crush-lori-mckenna-humbleandkind/|url-status=dead}}</ref> McGraw helped bring McKenna to [[Warner Bros. Nashville]] in 2007 and produced her album ''Unglamorous.''<ref name=":2" /> Following the lackluster sales, she parted ways with Warner Bros. She then signed with [[Universal Music Group|Universal Music Group Publishing]] in 2009 and independently released her next album ''Lorraine'' in 2011.<ref name=":2" />

Since then, McKenna has become "one of the industry’s most in-demand songwriters."<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://pac.adelphi.edu/au_event/three-women-and-the-truth-mary-gauthier-lori-mckenna-and-gretchen-peters/|title=Three Women and the Truth|author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.-->|website=Adelphi University Performing Arts Center|date=12 June 2023 }}</ref> She has written songs for artists including [[Sara Evans]], [[Reba McEntire]], [[Tim McGraw]], [[Carrie Underwood]], and [[Keith Urban]].<ref name=":5" /> She wrote 10 songs that made it to the Billboard Hot Country list, including [[Hunter Hayes]]' "[[I Want Crazy]]", Faith Hill's "[[Stealing Kisses]]", Tim McGraw's "[[Humble and Kind]]"; and [[Little Big Town]]'s "Your Side of the Bed", "Sober", and "Girl Crush."<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/chart-beat/7332956/lori-mckenna-country-chart-hits-solo-songwriters|title=Lori McKenna Brings the Solo Songwriter Back to No. 1 on Hot Country Songs|last=Trust|first=Gary|date=April 12, 2016|magazine=Billboard}}</ref>

McKenna writes solo and co-writes with songwriters [[Hillary Lindsey|Hillary Lindsay]] and [[Liz Rose]]. The group wrote the song "[[Girl Crush]]", and it became a hit for the band [[Little Big Town]].<ref name=":2" /> The song received Grammy nominations for both [[Grammy Award for Song of the Year|Song of the Year]] and [[Grammy Award for Best Country Song|Best Country Song]], winning the latter in 2016.<ref name=":3" /> The song was nominated for Song of the Year Song at the [[Academy of Country Music Awards]],<ref name="2016ACMawards" /> and won Song of the Year at the [[Country Music Association Awards]].<ref name=":6" />

McKenna won another Grammy the next year for Best Country Song for "Humble and Kind" by Tim McGraw.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tennessean.com/story/entertainment/music/2017/02/12/grammys-lori-mckenna-wins-best-country-song/97829604/|title=Grammys: Lori McKenna Wins Best Country Song|last=Thanki|first=Juli|date=February 12, 2017|website=Tennessean}}</ref> McKenna wrote the song as "lullaby, guidebook, and tribute" to her five children.<ref name=":2" /> The song was named Song of the Year at the 2016 [[Country Music Association Awards|CMA Awards]]<ref name=":2" /> and won favorite country song at the [[American Music Awards]] the same year.<ref name=":4" /> In 2017, "Humble and Kind" was nominated for Song of the Year and Single Record of the Year at the [[Academy of Country Music Awards|ACM Awards]]. McKenna was named Songwriter of the Year at the ACM's that same year.<ref name="2017acmresults" />

McKenna has received critical acclaim for her album ''The Bird and the Rifle''. The album was produced by [[Dave Cobb]] and nominated for [[Grammy Award for Best Americana Album|Best Americana Album]] at the [[59th Annual Grammy Awards|59th annual Grammy Awards]], and the single "Wreck You" was nominated for [[Grammy Award for Best American Roots Song|Best American Roots Song]] and [[Grammy Award for Best American Roots Performance|Best American Roots Performance]].<ref name="2017GrammyResults" /> McKenna was nominated for the 2017 [[American Music Award for Artist of the Year|Americana Music Awards Artist of the Year]].<ref name="2017americananoms" /> Her 10th studio album, ''The Tree'', was produced by [[Dave Cobb]] and released in summer 2018 through Thirty Tiger Records.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/2018/07/12/627245736/first-listen-lori-mckenna-the-tree|title=Lori McKenna's 'The Tree' Documents What Happens Between Life|last=McKenna|first=Britney|date=July 12, 2018|website=NPR}}</ref>

In 2015, McKenna was signed to a management and publishing deal with Creative Nation, a Nashville-based company owned and operated by songwriter Luke Laird and his wife Beth.<ref name=":2" /> On September 25, 2018, McKenna signed a publishing deal with Creative Nation for a three-year deal.

In 2020, McKenna released her eleventh album, ''[[The Balladeer (album)|The Balladeer]]'', through CN Records and [[Thirty Tigers]]. Music critic [[Robert Christgau]] hailed it as "the most consistently top-notch album of her late-blooming career".<ref name="CG">{{cite web|last=Christgau|first=Robert|author-link=Robert Christgau|date=August 12, 2020|url=https://robertchristgau.substack.com/p/consumer-guide-august-2020|title=Consumer Guide: August, 2020|work=And It Don't Stop|publisher=[[Substack]]|access-date=August 12, 2020|url-access=subscription}}</ref>

In 2020, McKenna co-wrote "[[A Beautiful Noise]]" with 7 other female writers - [[Alicia Keys]], [[Brandi Carlile]], [[Brandy Clark]], [[Hillary Lindsey]], [[Ruby Amanfu]], [[Hailey Whitters]] and [[Linda Perry]] - and the song was performed by [[Alicia Keys]] and [[Brandi Carlile]] with the purpose of inspiring American voters to vote in the [[2020 United States presidential election|2020 Presidential Election.]]<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-10-30|title=Watch Alicia Keys and Brandi Carlile premiere new song 'A Beautiful Noise' {{!}} NME|url=https://www.nme.com/en_asia/news/music/watch-alicia-keys-and-brandi-carlile-premiere-new-song-a-beautiful-noise-2805132|access-date=2020-12-27|website=NME {{!}} Music, Film, TV, Gaming & Pop Culture News|language=en}}</ref>

In 2021, singer-songwriter [[Taylor Swift]] released her second re-recorded album ''[[Red (Taylor's Version)]]'', including a song called "[[I Bet You Think About Me]]" featuring [[Chris Stapleton]]. The song was written by Swift and McKenna in 2011.
