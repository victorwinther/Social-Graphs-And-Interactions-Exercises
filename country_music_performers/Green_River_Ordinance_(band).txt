'''Green River Ordinance''' is an American rock band from [[Fort Worth, Texas]], United States. Their name refers to [[Green River Ordinance]]s, laws which prohibit door-to-door sales unless the house's owner gives permission to do so.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://skopemag.com/2008/11/19/green-river-ordinance-debut-set-for-february-2009 |title=GREEN RIVER ORDINANCE DEBUT SET FOR FEBRUARY 2009 &#124; Skope Entertainment Inc |website=skopemag.com |access-date=2 February 2022 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120714011054/http://skopemag.com/2008/11/19/green-river-ordinance-debut-set-for-february-2009 |archive-date=14 July 2012 |url-status=dead}}</ref>

==History==
===Early years===
Bassist Geoff Ice and guitarist Jamey Ice are brothers.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.popculturemadness.com/interview/Green-River-Ordinance.html |accessdate=March 14, 2009 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090412024825/http://www.popculturemadness.com/interview/Green-River-Ordinance.html |archivedate=April 12, 2009 |title=Interview with Green River Ordinance }}</ref> Early in their career (circa 2003), the band regularly did opening gigs with [[Flickerstick]] in Texas,<ref>[http://www.fwweekly.com/content.asp?article=3283 ] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20061017153635/http://www.fwweekly.com/content.asp?article=3283 |date=October 17, 2006 }}</ref> and by 2005 had toured with [[Collective Soul]] and played with [[Eisley]] and [[Mutemath]], among others.<ref>{{cite web|author=Andrew Leahey |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/green-river-ordinance-mn0001044525/biography |title=Green River Ordinance &#124; Biography & History |publisher=[[AllMusic]] |date= |accessdate=October 22, 2015}}</ref> The band's initial releases, a 2005 full-length and a 2007 EP,<ref>{{cite web|author= |url=http://www.popmatters.com/pm/review/green-river-ordinance-the-beauty-of-letting-go-way-back-home |title=Green River Ordinance: The Beauty of Letting Go/Way Back Home |publisher=PopMatters |date= |accessdate=October 22, 2015}}</ref> were both released on the small independent label [[For Mona]]. Their debut release for [[Virgin Records]], ''Out of My Hands'', was released in February 2009, and garnered comparisons to 1990s mainstream rock acts such as [[Sister Hazel]],<ref>[http://www.dfw.com/131/story/100832.html ]{{dead link|date=October 2015}}</ref> [[Third Eye Blind]] and [[Matchbox 20]],<ref>{{cite web|last=Freedman |first=Pete |url=http://www.dallasobserver.com/2009-02-26/music/green-river-ordinance/ |title=Green River Ordinance |publisher=Dallas Observer |date=February 26, 2009 |accessdate=October 22, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.idahostatesman.com/music/story/681012.html |title=Boise, Meridian, Nampa, Caldwell news by Idaho Statesman |publisher=Idahostatesman.com |date= |accessdate=October 22, 2015}}</ref> as well as contemporaries like [[Augustana (band)|Augustana]] and [[The Fray]].<ref>{{cite web|author=Andrew Leahey |url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/out-of-my-hands-mw0000809555 |title=Out of My Hands - Green River Ordinance &#124; Songs, Reviews, Credits |publisher=AllMusic |date=February 24, 2009 |accessdate=October 22, 2015}}</ref> ''Out of My Hands'' peaked at No. 10 on the [[Billboard magazine|Billboard]] Heatseekers chart. The single "Come On" reached No. 17 on the [[Billboard magazine|Billboard]] [[Adult Top 40]] late in 2009; the single "On Your Own" reached No. 37 on the same chart in 2010.<ref>{{cite web|author=Andrew Leahey |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/green-river-ordinance-mn0001044525/awards |title=Green River Ordinance &#124; Awards |publisher=AllMusic |date= |accessdate=October 22, 2015}}</ref>

===2009 and 2010===
[[File:Green River Ordinance at Music Farm 1.jpg|thumb|260px|Green River Ordinance performing in 2009]]
In 2009 and 2010, GRO saw two Top 40 radio singles, songs on over 20 television shows ("[[So You Think You Can Dance (U.S. TV series)|So You Think You Can Dance]]", "[[The Hills (TV series)|The Hills]]", "[[The Young and the Restless]]"), two music videos on MTV and VH1, as well as tours with several nationally touring artists, such as [[Goo Goo Dolls]], [[Collective Soul]], [[Switchfoot]], [[Train (band)|Train]], [[Lifehouse (band)|Lifehouse]], and [[American Idol]] winners [[David Cook (singer)|David Cook]] & [[Kris Allen]].<ref name="Billboard.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/303112/green+river+ordinance/chart?f=343 |title=Green River Ordinance - Chart history |publisher=Billboard |date= |accessdate=October 22, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/music/artist/green_river_ordinance/artist.jhtml |accessdate=October 26, 2010 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100929135642/http://www.mtv.com/music/artist/green_river_ordinance/artist.jhtml |title=Green River Ordinance|website=[[MTV]] |archivedate=September 29, 2010 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author= |url=http://www.vh1.com/video/green-river-ordinance/457048/come-on.jhtml |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100212132629/http://www.vh1.com/video/green-river-ordinance/457048/come-on.jhtml |url-status=dead |archive-date=February 12, 2010 |title="Come On" by Green River Ordinance &#124; Music Video |publisher=VH1.com |date= |accessdate=October 22, 2015}}</ref> Billboard Magazine even stated, "Green River Ordinance has established itself as a pop-rock act to keep an eye on in 2010."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/review/1069785/green-river-ordinance-come-on |title=Green River Ordinance, "Come On" |publisher=Billboard |date=February 5, 2010 |accessdate=October 22, 2015}}</ref>  GRO's original song "Rise Up" was featured on the [[AT&T Team USA Soundtrack (2010)]] alongside [[Mariah Carey]], [[3 Doors Down]], Train, and [[Rascal Flatts]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://top40.about.com/b/2010/02/16/atts-team-usa-soundtrack-supports-the-2010-usa-winter-olympics-team.htm |title=Mariah Carey Biography - Profile of Mariah Carey |publisher=Top40.about.com |accessdate=October 22, 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130512193207/http://top40.about.com/b/2010/02/16/atts-team-usa-soundtrack-supports-the-2010-usa-winter-olympics-team.htm |archive-date=May 12, 2013 |url-status=dead |df=mdy-all }}</ref>  Their Capitol/Virgin Records debut album ''Out of My Hands'' and various singles were featured on Zune, Walmart.com, AmazonMP3, iTunes, Rhapsody, and Napster as Staff Picks, Song of the Day, What’s Hot, Track of the Week, and other promotions.

GRO left EMI on August 6, 2010 and 14 days later proceeded to go on tour with Goo Goo Dolls and Switchfoot. Also, they were featured on every episode of MTV's show "If You Really Knew Me" and also had their video "On Your Own" featured after shows during credits on MTV, all of this while being independent of any label ties.  On September 6, 2010, GRO released "The Morning Passengers - Acoustic Sessions EP" to iTunes only, and it reached No 3 on the Billboard Heatseekers Chart, as well as the No. 39 on ''Billboard'' Independent Albums.<ref name="Billboard.com"/>

===2011===
After leaving EMI, GRO turned to [[Kickstarter]] to raise over $40,000 from their fans in order to produce a new album.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pegasusnews.com/news/2010/oct/18/green-river-ordinance-41000-new-album-kickstarter |accessdate=April 2, 2012 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20101020114817/http://www.pegasusnews.com/news/2010/oct/18/green-river-ordinance-41000-new-album-kickstarter/ |archivedate=October 20, 2010 |title=Green River Ordinance raises $41,000 for new album, to be recorded in 2011}}</ref> While recording the new album, amidst touring, they released two additional EPs: ''Songs We Like From Before We Were Born'' and ''Waterhope''.

===2012–2014===
The band released their third studio album, ''Under Fire'', on February 28, 2012.  They began a national tour, "Under Fire - The North American Tour," on March 29, 2012 in support of album, with [[Graham Colton]] as their opener.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://hmmagazine.com/blog/news/green-river-ordinance-confirms-headlining-tour/ |title=Green River Ordinance confirms headlining tour |date=March 1, 2012 |author= Doug Van Pelt |work=HM Magazine }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.antimusic.com/news/12/March/19Green_River_Ordinance_and_Graham_Colton_Tour.shtml |title=Green River Ordinance and Graham Colton |date=March 19, 2012 |work=Anti Music }}</ref> The album was fully funded by their fans, through the launch of a Kickstarter campaign over a year earlier. The album features the hit Texas Country song, "Dancing Shoes,"<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hmmagazine.com/2012/02/green-river-ordinance-rocks-country-audiences/ |accessdate=July 12, 2012 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120211213551/http://www.hmmagazine.com/2012/02/green-river-ordinance-rocks-country-audiences/ |archivedate=February 11, 2012 |title =GREEN RIVER ORDINANCE ROCKS COUNTRY MUSIC WITH "DANCING SHOES" }}</ref> as well as "Heart of Me."<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.alternativeaddiction.com/musicnews/article/2430/The-Journey-of-Green-River-Ordinance |title=New Music, Music News, Interviews, & More |publisher=Alternative Addiction |date= |accessdate=October 22, 2015 |archive-date=September 30, 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150930180147/http://www.alternativeaddiction.com/musicnews/article/2430/The-Journey-of-Green-River-Ordinance |url-status=dead }}</ref>

===2015–present===
On September 23, 2015, the band announced that their fourth full-length studio album, ''[[Fifteen (Green River Ordinance album)|Fifteen]]'', would be released on January 22, 2016, through Residence Music.<ref name="wsj9-23-2015">{{cite web|last=Danton|first=Eric R.|url=https://blogs.wsj.com/speakeasy/2015/09/23/listen-to-green-river-ordinances-maybe-its-time-gravity-exclusive-song/|title=Listen to Green River Ordinance's 'Maybe It's Time (Gravity)' (Exclusive Song)|work=[[The Wall Street Journal]]|publisher=[[News Corp (2013–present)|News Corp]]|date=September 23, 2015|accessdate=September 29, 2015}}</ref>

==Charity work==
In April 2010, Green River Ordinance launched their own charity site, in order to help make a difference for those less fortunate. Through this site, Green River Ordinance offers ten songs for purchase and information on five charities each member of the band picked. The consumer can select which of the songs they would like to purchase and select which band member's charity they would like their money to go toward. 100% of the proceeds go to the charity of the consumer's choice.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thehopegros.com |accessdate=October 26, 2010 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100823214348/http://www.thehopegros.com/ |archivedate=August 23, 2010 |title=Green River Ordinance }}</ref>

On October 8, 2010, [[International Justice Mission]] released the compilation album ''Freedom'' exclusively through [[Family Christian Stores]] as a joint effort to raise funds and awareness to end global slavery.  Green River Ordinance is featured with their song "Don't Give Up."  They are alongside the biggest names in contemporary Christian music and are one of the only secular artists on the compilation due to their zealous passion on the topic of slavery.  13,000 physical units were sold the first week in stores.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ijm.org/articles/spotlight |title=Stories &#124; International Justice Mission |publisher=Ijm.org |date= |accessdate=October 22, 2015}}</ref>

On October 15, 2010, Green River Ordinance was direct support for [[Bon Jovi]] in Gulf Shores, AL as part of the [[BP]]-sponsored concert series "Concerts for the Coast," done in hopes of attracting tourists to the region in the off-season during the oil spill recovery.   There were 37,000+ people in attendance.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.concertsforthecoast.com/green-river-ordinance-and-little-big-town-will-support-bon-jovi-and-brad-paisley/143/ |title=Yahoo!検索 - ダツもう痛くない安心サロンは.xyz |publisher=Concertsforthecoast.com |accessdate=October 22, 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101020032735/http://www.concertsforthecoast.com/green-river-ordinance-and-little-big-town-will-support-bon-jovi-and-brad-paisley/143/ |archive-date=October 20, 2010 |url-status=dead |df=mdy-all }}</ref>
