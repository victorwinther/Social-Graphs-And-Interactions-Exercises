'''Charles David Houston''' (December 9, 1935 – November 30, 1993)<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://secondhandsongs.com/artist/7891|title=David Houston &#124; SecondHandSongs|website=Secondhandsongs.com|access-date=August 7, 2021}}</ref> was an American [[country music]] singer. His peak in popularity came between the mid-1960s and the early 1970s.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1993/12/01/obituaries/david-houston-singer-57.html|title=David Houston; Singer, 57|date=December 1, 1993|access-date=August 7, 2021|website=[[The New York Times]]}}</ref>

His biggest success came in 1966, when his recording of "[[Almost Persuaded (song)|Almost Persuaded]]", topped the ''[[Billboard magazine|Billboard]]'s'' Hot Country Singles chart for nine weeks, and garnered Houston a pair of [[Grammy Award]]s.

==Biography==
[[File:David Houston home in Minden, LA IMG 0842.JPG|200px|right|thumb|David Houston's childhood home in [[Minden, Louisiana|Minden]], Louisiana]]
Houston was born in [[Bossier City, Louisiana|Bossier City]] in northwestern [[Louisiana]], United States.<ref name="LarkinCountry">{{cite book|title=[[Encyclopedia of Popular Music|The Guinness Who's Who of Country Music]]|editor=[[Colin Larkin (writer)|Colin Larkin]]|publisher=[[Guinness Publishing]]|date=1993|edition=First|isbn=0-85112-726-6|pages=193/4}}</ref> He was a descendant of [[Sam Houston]], the first president of the [[Republic of Texas]] and [[Confederate States of America|Confederate]] General [[Robert E. Lee]].<ref name="LarkinCountry"/> His godfather was 1920s pop singer [[Gene Austin]].<ref name="LarkinCountry"/> Like Austin, Houston lived briefly as a youth in a house at the intersection of Marshall and Goodwill streets in [[Minden, Louisiana|Minden]], the seat of [[Webster Parish, Louisiana|Webster Parish]] in northwestern Louisiana. Another musician from Minden, Tommy Tomlinson, collaborated with Houston in the single "Mountain of Love".

Houston was one of the earliest artists with [[National Recording Corporation]] in [[Atlanta]], [[Georgia (U.S. state)|Georgia]]. In 1963, he rose to national stardom with "Mountain of Love"; the song, which was different from the [[Mountain of Love|tune]] made famous by composer Harold Dorman, [[Johnny Rivers]], and [[Charley Pride]], rose to number two on ''[[Billboard magazine|Billboard]]'s'' Hot Country Singles chart, as did "Livin' in a House Full of Love" (1965).<ref name="LarkinCountry"/>

In 1966, Houston recorded "[[Almost Persuaded (song)|Almost Persuaded]]". This song, which is unrelated to the [[Philip Bliss|Philip Paul Bliss]] hymn of the same title, is the tale of a married man managing to resist a temptress he meets in a tavern. Houston's recording of it quickly reached number one that August, eventually spending nine weeks atop ''Billboard's'' Hot Country Singles chart.<ref name="LarkinCountry"/> For 46 years, no song did as well until [[Taylor Swift]] matched its nine-week record on December 15, 2012, with "[[We Are Never Ever Getting Back Together]]". (Swift's song went on to surpass the nine-week run of "Almost Persuaded", spending a 10th week at No. 1 in early 2013.)

Houston was awarded two [[Grammy Award]]s for [[Grammy Award for Best Country & Western Recording|Best Country & Western Recording]] and [[Grammy Award for Best Male Country Vocal Performance|Best Country & Western Performance, Male]] in 1967 for "Almost Persuaded" which began a string of top five Houston singles through 1973, including six more number ones: "[[With One Exception]]" and "[[You Mean the World to Me (David Houston song)|You Mean the World to Me]]" (1967); "[[Have a Little Faith (David Houston song)|Have a Little Faith]]" and "[[Already It's Heaven]]" (1968); "[[Baby, Baby (I Know You're a Lady)]]" (1970); and 1967's "[[My Elusive Dreams]]" duet with [[Tammy Wynette]].<ref name="LarkinCountry"/>

In later years, Houston sang duets with [[Barbara Mandrell]] on several of her early hits, most notably 1970's "After Closing Time" and 1972's "I Love You, I Love You".<ref name="LarkinCountry"/>

Houston's last Top 10 country hit came in 1974 with "Can't You Feel It", though he continued making records until 1989.<ref name="LarkinCountry"/>

Houston died of a brain [[aneurysm]] on November 30, 1993, in Bossier City, one week before his 58th birthday.<ref name="auto"/> He had been residing in the [[New Orleans]] suburb of [[Kenner, Louisiana|Kenner]].
