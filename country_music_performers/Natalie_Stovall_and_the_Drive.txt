'''Natalie Stovall and the Drive''' was an American [[country music]] group composed of Natalie Stovall (lead vocals, fiddle), Miguel Cancino (guitar), Zach Morse (bass), Joel Dormer (guitar, vocals), and James Bavendam (drums).

Their debut single, "Baby Come On with It", was released in February 2014 after receiving airplay on [[Sirius XM Radio]].<ref name="billboard">{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-615/5869760/615-spotlight-natalie-stovall-and-the-drive-bring-it-with-baby-come|title=615 Spotlight: Natalie Stovall and The Drive Bring It With 'Baby Come On With It'|last=Dauphin|first=Chuck|date=15 January 2014|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=1 April 2014}}</ref>

Stovall was raised in [[Columbia, Tennessee]]. She had performed on the [[Grand Ole Opry]] and ''[[The Oprah Winfrey Show]]''. She met Bavendam in college, recruited Cancino through a [[Craigslist]] ad, then met Morse and Dormer through mutual friends.

Their debut album, released on Hitshop/[[Warner Bros. Records]], will be produced by [[Paul Worley]]<ref name="billboard"/> and Clarke Schleicher.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.musicrow.com/2014/01/musicrowpics-natalie-stovall-and-the-drive/|title=MusicRowPics: Natalie Stovall and The Drive|last=Nicholson|first=Jessica|date=15 January 2014|work=[[MusicRow]]|access-date=1 April 2014}}</ref> In advance of its release, the band released a self-titled [[extended play]] that includes the single and five additional songs.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://theboot.com/natalie-stovall-and-the-drive-baby-come-on-with-it/|title=Natalie Stovall and the Drive Release Debut Single|last=Allers|first=Hannahlee|date=10 January 2014|work=The Boot|access-date=1 April 2014}}</ref> In 2014 the group released their single, "Mason Jar".<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.countryweekly.com/videos/natalie-stovall-drive-discuss-new-single-mason-jar |title=Natalie Stovall & the Drive Discuss New Single "Mason Jar" - Country Weekly |access-date=2015-01-22 |archive-date=2015-01-23 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150123010452/http://www.countryweekly.com/videos/natalie-stovall-drive-discuss-new-single-mason-jar |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Leading up to the 2014 CMA Awards, the group was featured in the "On The Road To The  CMA Awards" digital series with JCPenney. Their first national television commercial was aired during the show, with behind the scenes footage available on the JCPenney YouTube<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.musicrow.com/2014/10/natalie-stovall-hosts-web-series-leading-up-to-cma-awards/ |title=Natalie Stovall Hosts Web Series Leading Up To CMA Awards |website=Musicrow.com |date=2014-10-31 |access-date=2020-04-29}}</ref>

==''The Voice''==
Natalie Stovall took part in [[The Voice (U.S. season 13)|season 13]] of the American ''[[The Voice (U.S. TV series)|The Voice]]''. In the Blind Audition broadcast on [[NBC]] on October 9, 2017, she performed [[Adele]]'s "[[If It Hadn't Been For Love]]" and joined Team [[Blake Shelton]]. In the battles, she faced off against Adam Cunningham, singing [[Little Big Town]]'s song, "[[Boondocks (song)|Boondocks]]". Stovall lost her battle, and was sent home. However, Shelton brought her back into the competition for the playoffs, giving her one more chance as his comeback artist. She was eliminated again after her performance of "[[Callin' Baton Rouge]]" during the playoffs.

In May 2020, Stovall joined the group [[Runaway June]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://tasteofcountry.com/natalie-stovall-joins-runaway-june/|title=Natalie Stovall Joins Runaway June to Replace Hannah Mulholland|author=Angela Stefano|date=May 15, 2020|publisher=Taste of Country|access-date=May 15, 2020}}</ref> During the [[COVID-19 pandemic in the United States|COVID-19 pandemic]], she presented livestream performances of the [[Grand Ole Opry]] alongside [[Bobby Bones]].
