'''Lenny Cooper''' (born 1988 in [[Jacksonville, North Carolina]]<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|last=Leggett|first=Steve|title=Lenny Cooper {{!}} Biography|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=mn0002519137/biography|pure_url=yes}}|work=[[AllMusic]]|accessdate=September 4, 2014}}</ref>) is an American [[country rap]] singer-songwriter.<ref>[http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-615/6229298/lenny-cooper-the-grind-interview "Lenny Cooper Explains How His Father & Brother Turned Him Into a Country Rapper"] ''Billboard''. Retrieved February 4, 2015.</ref> Cooper is signed to [[Colt Ford]]'s record label, [[Average Joes Entertainment]], and released his debut album, ''Diesel Fuel'', in 2012.<ref name="allmusic" /> Cooper's second album, ''Mud Dynasty'', was released on May 7, 2013.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Carter|first1=Liv|title=UCN Interview – Lenny Cooper: "The new album feels totally like me."|url=http://www.urbancountrynews.com/ucn-interview-lenny-cooper-the-new-album-feels-totally-like-me/|accessdate=September 4, 2014|work=Urban Country News|date=April 26, 2013}}</ref> It sold 3,000 copies in its first week of release, debuting at number 34 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Top Country Albums]] chart and number 198 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Bjorke|first1=Matt|title=Country Album Chart News For May 15, 2013: Lady Antebellum Debuts At #1; Pistol Annies Top 5 with New Albums; Country Music Dominates|url=http://roughstock.com/news/country-album-chart-news-for-may-15-2013-lady-antebellum-debuts-at-1-pistol-annies-top-5-with-new-albums-country-music-dominates/|accessdate=September 4, 2014|work=Roughstock|date=May 15, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140905051232/http://roughstock.com/news/country-album-chart-news-for-may-15-2013-lady-antebellum-debuts-at-1-pistol-annies-top-5-with-new-albums-country-music-dominates/|archive-date=September 5, 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Cooper released his third album, ''The Grind'', on August 26, 2014.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Frazer|first1=Bear|title=Lenny Cooper: Staying On The Grind|url=https://arena.com/article/lenny-cooper-staying-on-the-grind|accessdate=September 4, 2014|work=Arena|date=August 20, 2014}}</ref> The album features collaborations with Ford, [[Bucky Covington]] and [[Bubba Sparxxx]].<ref>{{cite news|last1=Thompson|first1=Gayle|title=Lenny Cooper Releasing 'The Grind'|url=http://theboot.com/lenny-cooper-the-grind/|accessdate=September 4, 2014|work=The Boot|date=August 9, 2014}}</ref> It sold 2,400 copies in its first week of release, debuting at number 17 on the ''Billboard'' Top Country Albums chart and number 142 on the ''Billboard'' 200.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Bjorke|first1=Matt|title=Country Album Sales Report – September 3, 2014|url=http://roughstock.com/news/country-album-sales-report-09-03-2014/|accessdate=September 4, 2014|work=Roughstock|date=September 3, 2014|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140904072435/http://roughstock.com/news/country-album-sales-report-09-03-2014/|archivedate=September 4, 2014}}</ref>
