'''Steven Lee Olsen''' (born November 25, 1985) is a Canadian [[country music]] artist and songwriter. He is best known for his singles "[[Raised by a Good Time]]" and "[[Outta Yours]]", as well as for co-writing the [[Grammy]]-nominated single "[[Blue Ain't Your Color]]" by [[Keith Urban]].

Olsen has also written songs for singers such as [[Craig Morgan (singer)|Craig Morgan]], [[Garth Brooks]], [[Dallas Smith]], [[Rascal Flatts]], [[The Judds]], and [[Kevin Borg]].<ref>{{cite web | title=Steven Lee Olsen |url=http://www.reverbnation.com/stevenleeolsen | work=Reverb Nation | accessdate=June 10, 2009 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web| title=The Core Entertainment, Universal Music Canada Launch Joint Venture, Sign Steven Lee Olsen |url=https://musicrow.com/2021/07/the-core-entertainment-universal-music-canada-launch-joint-venture-sign-steven-lee-olsen/ |work=MusicRow |first=Lorie|last=Hollabaugh|date=July 21, 2021}}</ref>

==Career==
He released his debut single "Now" in 2009, which was included on his 2009 debut album ''Introducing Steven Lee Olsen'', released on RGK Records, . He followed his debut single with the release of another single "Make Hay While the Sun Shines". Olsen toured in the 2009 [[CMT (Canada)|CMT]] on Tour Live show, along with [[Jason Blaine]], [[Tara Oram]] and [[The Higgins]].<ref>{{cite web |title=CMT Tour Headed to Sarnia |url=http://www.theobserver.ca/ArticleDisplay.aspx?e=1591223 | work=The Observer | accessdate=June 10, 2009 }}</ref>

In April 2014, Olsen signed with [[Sony Music Nashville|Columbia Nashville]].<ref>{{cite web| title=Columbia Nashville Signs Steven Lee Olsen |url=http://www.musicrow.com/2014/04/columbia-nashville-signs-steven-lee-olsen/ |work=MusicRow |date=April 24, 2014 |accessdate=April 24, 2014}}</ref> Later that year, he released his major-label debut single, "[[Raised by a Good Time]]", which was certified Gold by [[Music Canada]] in August 2015. He released the album ''Timing Is Everything'' on his own label SLO Circus Records in 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://musicrow.com/2018/11/steven-lee-olsen-releases-debut-album-timing-is-everything/|title=Steven Lee Olsen Releases Debut Album 'Timing Is Everything'|date=November 9, 2018|first=LB|last=Cantrell|work=Music Row Magazine}}</ref>

In 2021, Olsen signed a joint record deal with [[Universal Music Canada]] and The Core Entertainment,<ref>{{cite web| title=The Core Entertainment and Universal Music Canada Launch Joint Venture; Steven Lee Olsen First Signing|url=https://variety.com/2021/music/news/core-universal-music-canada-steven-lee-olsen-1235024309/|first=Jem|last=Aswad|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]] |date=July 21, 2021}}</ref> and released the extended play ''[[Relationship Goals (EP)|Relationship Goals]]'',<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.umusic.ca/press-releases/grammy-nominated-singer-songwriter-steven-lee-olsen-announces-ep-relationship-goals-out-july-30/|title=GRAMMY-NOMINATED SINGER-SONGWRITER STEVEN LEE OLSEN ANNOUNCES EP, RELATIONSHIP GOALS, OUT JULY 30|date=July 22, 2021|work=[[Universal Music Canada]]}}</ref> which included the 2021 [[Canadian Football League|CFL]] Song of the Year, "What You're Made Of".<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.cfl.ca/2021/08/06/steven-lee-olsen-sings-way-cfl-tsn/|title= STEVEN LEE OLSEN SINGS HIS WAY INTO THE CFL ON TSN|access-date=August 10, 2021|work=The Snap|publisher=[[Canadian Football League]]}}</ref> In early 2023, he released the single "[[Outta Yours]]" and joined the Canadian leg of [[Old Dominion (band)|Old Dominion]]'s "No Bad Vibes Tour" as an opening act.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.insideottawavalley.com/opinion/contributors/outta-yours-canadian-steven-lee-olsen-releases-new-single/article_eee11be1-48c9-5eaf-8d24-6692310283d5.html|title='Outta Yours': Canadian Steven Lee Olsen releases new single|first=Scott|last=Campbell|work=InsideOttawaValley.com|date=March 10, 2023|access-date=July 11, 2023|publisher=[[Metroland Media Group]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://519magazine.com/shawn-austin-returns-to-budweiser-gardens-tonight-with-old-dominion/|title=Shawn Austin Returns to Budweiser Gardens Tonight with Old Dominion|first=Whitney|last=South|work=519 Magazine|date=January 30, 2023|access-date=February 6, 2023}}</ref> "Outta Yours" would chart on the [[Canadian Hot 100]], marking a return to the chart for Olsen after nine years.<ref name="Can Hot"/>

In July 2023, Olsen made his debut performance at the [[Grand Ole Opry]] in [[Nashville, Tennessee]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com/entertainment/movies/from-scarborough-to-the-grand-ole-opry-steven-lee-olsen-spins-a-yarn-of-crushed/article_afbe7f95-b2cf-56be-bee4-24c55094e0c6.html|title=From Scarborough to the Grand Ole Opry: Steven Lee Olsen spins a yarn of crushed dreams, miracles, and a guardian angel|first=Nick|last=Krewen|date=July 22, 2023|work=[[Toronto Star]]|access-date=September 2, 2023}}</ref> In August 2023, he released the single "Out Here" concurrently with the announcement of his new distribution deal with [[independent label]] Starseed Records.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://canadianbeats.ca/2023/08/27/steven-lee-olsen-shares-new-single-out-here/|title=Steven Lee Olsen shares new single, "Out Here"|date=August 27, 2023|first=Nanci|last=Dagg|work=Canadian Beats Media|access-date=August 27, 2023}}</ref> He followed that up with "Bigger Than This Town" in April 2024, which is slated to be included on an album of the same name.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://canadianbeats.ca/2024/04/15/steven-lee-olsen-shares-bigger-than-this-town/|title=STEVEN LEE OLSEN SHARES “BIGGER THAN THIS TOWN”|date=April 15, 2024|work=Canadian Beats Media|access-date=April 22, 2024}}</ref>
