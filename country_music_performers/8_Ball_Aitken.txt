'''8 Ball Aitken''' (born 9 June 1981) is an [[Australians|Australian]] singer, songwriter, and guitarist. 8 Ball plays blues, swamp-blues, swamp-rock, alternative country, and Americana music. He is notable for his fusion of [[Blues|Australian blues]] and [[country music]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.8ballaitken.com |title=8 Ball Music |publisher=8 Ball Aitken |access-date=29 April 2010| archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20100414061022/http://www.8ballaitken.com/| archive-date= 14 April 2010 | url-status= live}}</ref>

Aitken is the founder of the Mt Coot-tha Songwriters Festival.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.grassrootsmusicfestival.com.au |title=Grass Roots: Welcome |publisher=Grassrootsmusicfestival.com.au |access-date=29 April 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170801150623/http://www.grassrootsmusicfestival.com.au/ |archive-date=1 August 2017 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==History==
At the 2008 Q Song Awards, Aitken won Blues and Roots Song of the Year for "Yellow Moon". At the same awards in 2009, "Outback Booty Call" won Country Song of the Year.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.fasterlouder.com.au/news/local/14458/Q-Song-2008-award-winners-announced.htm |title=Q Song 2008 award winners announced on |publisher=Fasterlouder.com.au |date=2008-08-14 |access-date=2010-04-29 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120307114406/http://www.fasterlouder.com.au/news/local/14458/Q-Song-2008-award-winners-announced.htm |archive-date=7 March 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Aitken's song "Cowboy Movie" was nominated for the Australian Blues & Roots Song of the Year at the [[APRA Music Awards of 2009]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.apra-amcos.com.au/news/allnews/2009APRAMusicAwardsNomineesAnnounced.aspx |title=2009 APRA Music Awards: Nominees Announced |date=27 May 2009|access-date=29 April 2010|url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090531125825/http://www.apra-amcos.com.au/news/allnews/2009APRAMusicAwardsNomineesAnnounced.aspx |archive-date=31 May 2009}}</ref>

Aitken third studio album ''Rebel with a Cause'' was produced by Garth Porter.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.8ballaitken.com/Rebel-With-A-Cause-scp3000.html |title=8 Ball Music: Rebel with a Cause |access-date=2 November 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111105061145/http://www.8ballaitken.com/Rebel-With-A-Cause-scp3000.html |archive-date=5 November 2011 |url-status=dead }}</ref> The album produced two top 10 country hits, "Cyclone Country" & "Hands on Top of the Wheel".<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.countrymusicradio.com.au/index.cfm?page_id=1025&news_display_year_3229=2009&display_news_id_3229=1041 |title=Archive |publisher=Country Music Radio |access-date=29 April 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091017041620/http://www.countrymusicradio.com.au/index.cfm?page_id=1025&news_display_year_3229=2009&display_news_id_3229=1041 |archive-date=17 October 2009 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

In 2013, Aitken had a one song in the Australian Country Music Radio Charts with song "She's Going to Mexico, I'm Going to Jail".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.8ballaitken.com/|title=8 Ball Aitken: Bio|website=8 Ball Aitken Homepage|access-date=6 January 2015}}</ref>
