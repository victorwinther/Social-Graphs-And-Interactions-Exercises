'''Waylon Malloy Payne''' (born April 5, 1972) is an American [[country music|country]] [[singing|singer]], [[songwriter]], [[musician]] and [[actor]]. He is the son of the country singer [[Sammi Smith]].

==Early life==
Payne was born in [[Nashville, Tennessee]], the son of [[guitarist]] [[Jody Payne]] and [[Grammy Award]]-winning country singer [[Sammi Smith]]. His father became a longtime picker for [[Willie Nelson]]; his mother toured with [[Waylon Jennings]]. Payne is named for Jennings, who is his [[godparent|godfather]]. Due to the divorce of his parents and their heavy [[concert|touring]] schedules, when he was about four months old Payne's mother placed him with her brother and sister-in-law, Bob and Yvonne, in [[Vidor, Texas]]. Although he spent summers out on the road with his mother, he lived with his uncle and aunt, who were strict [[Christians]], until he was about age 18.

After high school, Payne enrolled in a [[seminary]] to become a [[Minister (Christianity)|minister]]. Payne had also acquired a taste for [[beer]], [[Cannabis (drug)|marijuana]], and [[popular music]], which made his downfall complete in the eyes of his aunt and uncle. "I haven't seen them since," he has said. "I was branded a sinner and basically disowned."  <ref name=fwweekly>{{cite web |title=Pilgrim and a Preacher and a Problem when He's Stoned |first=Jeff |last=Prince |work=[[Fort Worth Weekly]] |url=http://www.fwweekly.com/content.asp?article=4508 |date=2007-01-03 |access-date=2007-08-05}}</ref>

==Music==
Payne's [[music industry]] [[career]] began to take shape in the [[nightclub|clubs]] of [[Los Angeles, California|Los Angeles]]. He was part of the popular ''Eastbound and Down'' country night at the King King Club in [[Hollywood, Los Angeles, California|Hollywood]], which featured musicians playing pure, uncompromising [[Traditional music|roots music]] and appealed to such artists as [[Lucinda Williams]] and [[Dwight Yoakam]]. Prohibited was the playing of anything but traditional classics by [[Hank Williams]], [[George Jones]] and other performers of true country.

While in L.A., Payne wrote and recorded some songs with the help of his group of musical friends, which included [[record producer|producer]] [[Keith Gattis]]. With the record completed, but no deal to market it, Payne was playing [[New York City|New York]] with Willie Nelson and [[Pat Green]] when Green suggested he play the album for his [[record label|label]], [[The Universal Motown/Universal Republic Group|Republic/Universal]]. Payne was signed with the label and his d√©but album, "The Drifter," was released June 22, 2004.

Payne is signed with [[Carnival Music]] and since returning to Nashville has had two cuts with [[Miranda Lambert]] and has been back writing with [[Lee Ann Womack]] which resulted in multiple writing credits on her new album. Womack has previously received a Grammy nomination for Payne's "Solitary Thinkin"in 2010 for Best Female Country Vocal Performance.

Since 2010 Waylon has received album credits for [[Cory Morrow|Cory Marrow 's]] "Brand New Me," [[Charlie Robison|Charlie Robison 's]] "Live at Billy Bob's Texas" and "Deep in the Heart: Big Songs for Little Texans Everywhere," [[Lee Ann Womack|Lee Ann Womack 's]] "Hollywood" and "The Lonely, the Lonesome & the Gone," [[Ashley Monroe|Ashley Monroe 's]] "Sparrow," [[Wade Bowen|Wade Bowen 's]] "Solid Ground," and [[Aaron Lewis|Aaron Lewis's]] "State I'm In."

==Acting==
Payne was featured in the role of fellow country music artist [[Jerry Lee Lewis]] in the hit [[20th Century Fox]] [[film|movie]] ''[[Walk the Line]]'' ([[2005 in film|2005]]), which stars [[Joaquin Phoenix]] as [[Johnny Cash]] and [[Reese Witherspoon]] as [[June Carter Cash|June Carter]], and did all his own singing. Payne's feature on the soundtrack album allowed him to share the Grammy Award won. Payne then starred as [[Hank Garland]] in an [[independent film|independent]] [[feature film|feature]] about the legendary guitarist's life titled ''[[Crazy (2007 film)|Crazy]]'' ([[2007 in film|2007]]).

Waylon Payne has also made guest appearances on [[television program|television]], including the series ''[[CSI: Crime Scene Investigation]]''.

Waylon Payne also stars in [[Monte Hellman]]'s thriller ''[[Road to Nowhere (film)|Road to Nowhere]]''.

Waylon Payne was also featured in the role as Tony Nash in the 2014 film ''[[The Identical]]''.
