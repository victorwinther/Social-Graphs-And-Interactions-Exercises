'''Brett Ryan Eldredge''' (born March 23, 1986) is an American [[country music]] singer, songwriter and record producer. Eldredge has had five No.&nbsp;1 singles on the ''Billboard'' Country Airplay chart, three of which came from his debut album, ''[[Bring You Back]]'': "[[Don't Ya]]", "[[Beat of the Music]]", and "[[Mean to Me (Brett Eldredge song)|Mean to Me]]".

==Early life==
Eldredge was born on March 23, 1986, in [[Paris, Illinois]], to Robin Beth ([[née]] Vonderlage) and Christopher "Chris" Eldredge. He has an older brother, Brice.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.parisbeacon.com/news/article_ebcb9a00-6722-11e1-a214-001871e3ce6c.html?TNNoMobile |title=One-on-One with Brett Eldredge - The Paris Beacon-News: News |publisher=Parisbeacon.com |date=2012-03-05 |access-date=2015-04-23 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20141119220324/http://www.parisbeacon.com/news/article_ebcb9a00-6722-11e1-a214-001871e3ce6c.html?TNNoMobile |archive-date=November 19, 2014 |url-status=dead |df=mdy-all }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://familytreemaker.genealogy.com/users/d/i/n/Donald-L-Dingman/ODT5-0002.html |title=Donald-L-Dingman - User Trees - Genealogy.com |publisher=Familytreemaker.genealogy.com |access-date=2015-04-23}}</ref> He studied at [[Elmhurst College]] in [[Elmhurst, Illinois]]. His cousin, Terry Eldredge, is a former member of [[the Grascals]].<ref name="allmusic"/>

== Career ==
Eldredge co-wrote the track "I Think I've Had Enough" for [[Gary Allan]], who recorded it for his eighth studio album, ''[[Get Off on the Pain]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.warnermusicnashville.com/artist/brett-eldredge|title=Brett Eldredge biography|work=Warner Music Nashville|access-date=October 5, 2010}}</ref> By late 2010, he had signed to [[Atlantic Records]] and released his debut single, "[[Raymond (song)|Raymond]]" on September 27, 2010. It debuted at number 52 on the US ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs]] chart dated for the week ending October 16, 2010<ref>{{cite magazine |url=http://www.billboard.com/#/column/chartbeat/chart-highlights-dance-club-play-alternative-1004118866.story?tag=hpfeed |title=Chart Highlights: Dance/Club Play & Alternative Songs & More |date=October 4, 2010 |magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=October 5, 2010}}</ref> and reached a peak of number 23. "[[It Ain't Gotta Be Love]]" was released as his second single and reached number 46. Eldredge performed on the [[Grand Ole Opry]] on October 29, 2010.<ref name="cst">{{cite web|url=http://www.countrystandardtime.com/news/newsitem.asp?xid=4665|title=Brett Eldredge makes Opry debut Friday|date=October 28, 2010|work=[[Country Standard Time]]|access-date=October 30, 2010}}</ref>

A third single, "[[Don't Ya]]", was released to country radio in October 2012. It became his first single to reach the top 20 of the Hot Country Songs chart and his first to appear on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100. His debut studio album, ''[[Bring You Back]]'', was released on August 6, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blog.gactv.com/blog/2013/05/15/brett-eldredge-to-release-debut-album-august-6/|title=Brett Eldredge to Release Debut Album August 6|date=May 15, 2013 |work=[[Great American Country|GAC]]|access-date=May 31, 2013}}</ref> "Don't Ya" reached number one on the [[Country Airplay]] chart the same month. The album's second single, "[[Beat of the Music]]", also reached number one on the same chart. The album's third single, "[[Mean to Me (Brett Eldredge song)|Mean to Me]]", was released to country radio on July 14, 2014. It reached number one on the Country Airplay chart in March 2015. On August 17, 2023, Eldredge released the ten year edition of the album featuring a bonus track called "Adios Old Friend".<ref>{{cite web|url= https://musicrow.com/2023/08/brett-eldredge-to-release-10th-anniversary-edition-of-bring-you-back|title=Brett Eldredge to Release 10th Anniversary of 'Bring You Back'|author=Lorie Hollabaugh|publisher=Music Row|date=August 17, 2023|access-date=August 17, 2023}}</ref>

Eldredge's sixth single overall, "[[Lose My Mind (Brett Eldredge song)|Lose My Mind]]", was released in 2015. It is the lead single to his second studio album, ''[[Illinois (Brett Eldredge album)|Illinois]]''. It reached number one on the Country Airplay chart in October 2015. The album's second single, "[[Drunk on Your Love]]" released to country radio on November 9, 2015. It reached at number two on the Country Airplay chart in April 2016. The album's third single, "[[Wanna Be That Song]]" released to country radio on May 23, 2016. It reached at number one on the Country Airplay in January 2017. He was one of the opening acts on [[Taylor Swift]]'s [[Red Tour]]. On Memorial Day 2015, he sang "Take Me Out to The Ballgame" during the seventh Inning Stretch at Wrigley Field while the Cubs played host to the Washington Nationals.

In 2016, Eldredge was selected as one of 30 artists to perform on "''Forever Country''", a mash-up track of [[Take Me Home, Country Roads]]; [[On the Road Again (Willie Nelson song)|On the Road Again]]; and [[I Will Always Love You]] which celebrates 50 years of the [[CMA Awards]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://abcnews.go.com/Entertainment/scenes-cmas-historic-music-video-featuring-30-country/story?id=42129062|title=30 Country Music Stars Join Forces for Historic CMA Music Video|date=2016-09-22|website=ABC News|access-date=2016-10-26}}</ref>

Eldredge released his first Christmas album, ''[[Glow (Brett Eldredge album)|Glow]]'', in October 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iheart.com/news/brett-eldredge-names-his-christmas-album-15097408/|title=Brett Eldredge Names His Christmas Album 'Glow'|work=iheart.com|access-date=October 27, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161019041217/http://www.iheart.com/news/brett-eldredge-names-his-christmas-album-15097408/|archive-date=October 19, 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In March 2017, he released "[[Somethin' I'm Good At]]", the lead single to his [[Brett Eldredge (album)|self titled fourth studio album]].<ref name="RS">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/country/news/hear-brett-eldredges-new-song-something-im-good-at-w468707|title=Hear Brett Eldredge's New Song 'Something I'm Good At' - Rolling Stone|magazine=[[Rolling Stone]]|last=Moss|first=Melissa R.|date=February 24, 2017|access-date=March 17, 2017}}</ref> The album's second single, [[The Long Way (Brett Eldredge song)|"The Long Way"]] released to country radio on August 21, 2017, and peaked at #3. The album's third single was "Love Someone".<ref name="country airplay">{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/brett-eldredge/chart-history/csa|title=Brett Eldredge Chart History (Country Airplay)|magazine=Billboard|access-date=November 30, 2021}}</ref>

On April 17, 2020, Eldredge released the single "Gabrielle", the lead single off his fifth studio album ''[[Sunday Drive]]'', released on July 10, 2020.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/country/9416264/brett-eldredge-sunday-drive|title = Brett Eldredge Shifts into a Higher Gear on 'Sunday Drive'| magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] }}</ref>

On April 22, 2022, Eldredge released the single "Songs About You", the lead single from the [[Songs About You (album)|album of the same name]]; the album was released on June 17, 2022.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://country.iheart.com/alternate/amp/2022-04-22-brett-eldredge-reveals-album-release-date-as-he-debuts-latest-song|title=Brett Eldredge reveals album release date as he debuts latest song|work=iHeartCountry|access-date=April 22, 2022}}</ref>

On September 17, 2024, Eldredge announced his eighth and third Christmas album ''Merry Christmas (Welcome to the Family)''. It is scheduled to be released on September 27, 2024. It will be his first album released independently after parting ways with [[Warner Records|Warner Nashville]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/music/music-news/brett-eldredge-launches-warm-cozy-records-talks-third-holiday-album-1235777190|title=Brett Eldredge Reveals New Holiday Album, Launches Warm and Cozy Records|author=Jessica Nicholson|magazine=Billboard|date=September 17, 2024|access-date=September 17, 2024}}</ref>
