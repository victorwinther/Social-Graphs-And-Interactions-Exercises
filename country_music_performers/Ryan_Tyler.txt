'''Ryan McPherson Rygmyr'''<ref>{{cite web | url=https://repertoire.bmi.com/Search/Search?Main_Search_Text=last%20thing%20she%20said&Main_Search=Title&Sub_Search=Please%20Select&Search_Type=all&View_Count=0&Page_Number=0 | title=Search results for 'The Last Thing She Said' | publisher=Broadcast Music Incorporated | accessdate=March 26, 2022}}</ref> (born October 6, 1973), known professionally as '''Ryan Tyler''', is an American [[country music]] artist. Signed to the [[Arista Nashville]] label, charted two singles: "Run, Run, Run" in 2003 and "The Last Thing She Said".

==Biography==
Ryan Lindsay McPherson was born October 6, 1973, in [[Atlanta, Georgia]], and raised in [[Duluth, Georgia]].<ref name="whitburn"/> She is an alumna of [[Arizona State University]]. After graduating from there, McPherson moved to [[Seattle, Washington]] with her husband, Tyler Rygmyr. She then moved again to [[Nashville, Tennessee]], in 2001 to pursue a country music career. She chose her husband's first name as her stage name, because she considered it easier to pronounce than her married name.<ref name="arizona"/> Two years after moving to Nashville, she was signed by [[Arista Nashville]] in 2003.<ref name="arizona"/> The label released two singles: "Run, Run, Run" and "The Last Thing She Said", which both charted on ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs]] between 2003 and 2004.<ref name="whitburn"/> "Run, Run, Run" was co-written by Hunter Davis, [[Little Texas (band)|Little Texas]] guitarist Porter Howell, and Canadian country singer [[Kortney Wilson|Kortney Kayle]], while Tyler wrote "The Last Thing She Said" with Nashville songwriter Shaye Smith and record producer [[Mickey Jack Cones]].<ref name="whitburn"/> In addition to these, Tyler co-wrote [[Kerry Harvick]]'s 2004 single "Cowgirls" with [[Hillary Lindsey]] and [[Angelo Petraglia]].<ref>Whitburn, p. 160</ref> Deborah Evans Price of ''Billboard'' reviewed "The Last Thing She Said" favorably, calling it a "poignant story song" while also praising Tyler's vocal and the production of [[Scott Hendricks]].<ref>{{cite magazine | url=https://books.google.com/books?id=OhAEAAAAMBAJ&dq=%22ryan+tyler%22+%22last+thing+she+said%22&pg=PA35 | title=Reviews: Country | author=Deborah Evans Price | magazine=Billboard | pages=35 | date=July 3, 2004}}</ref>

Tyler's album for Arista Nashville was never completed due to her contract expiring,<ref name="arizona"/> and she was released from Arista's roster in 2004.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.allbusiness.com/transportation-communications/communications-radio/4361191-1.html |title=ON THE ROW: RLG Trims Artist Rosters |accessdate=2008-01-07 |last=Stark |first=Phyllis |date=2004-08-13 |work=Airplay Monitors}}</ref> After exiting Arista Nashville in 2004, Tyler performed a series of concerts in Arizona that also included [[Clay Walker]], [[Tracy Lawrence]], and [[Dusty Drake]].<ref name="arizona">{{cite news | url=https://www.newspapers.com/image/221270367/ | title=A gal who won't take 'no' | work=Arizona Daily Star | date=September 9, 2004 | accessdate=March 26, 2022 | pages=47}}</ref>
