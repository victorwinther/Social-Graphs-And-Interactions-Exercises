'''Sarah Johns''' (born 1979) is an American [[country music]] artist. Signed to [[BNA Records]] in 2006, she released her debut album ''Big Love in a Small Town'', which accounted for two charting [[single (music)|singles]] on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs]] charts, including the No.&nbsp;39 "The One in the Middle."

==Musical career==
Sarah Johns was born in Pollard, Kentucky. She began singing regularly while attending the [[University of Kentucky]].<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/1049268/sarah-johns|title=Sarah Johns|magazine=Billboard|accessdate=13 January 2010}}</ref>

In early 2006, Sarah Johns signed to Show Dog Nashville (now [[Show Dog-Universal Music]]), a [[record label]] owned by [[Toby Keith]]. Although she toured with Keith on his Big Throwdown 2 tour, she did not release anything on Show Dog, and left the label that April.<ref>{{cite magazine|last=Stark|first=Phyllis|date=2006-04-01|title=Hats Off!|magazine=Billboard|pages=24}}</ref> A year later, Johns signed with [[BNA Records]].<ref name="cw">{{cite journal | author = Chris Neal | date =2007-08-13 | title = Who's New | journal = [[Country Weekly]] | volume = 14 | issue = 17 | pages = 72 | issn = 1074-3235}}</ref> She released her debut single "The One in the Middle" on April 30, 2007, and made Top 40 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs]] charts with it.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://countrymusic.about.com/od/countrysingles2007/a/043007_singles.htm |title=Country Music Single Releases - Week of April 30, 2007 |accessdate=2007-07-20 |last=Webb |first=Jennifer |work=about.com}}</ref> On June 2, 2007, Johns made her debut on the [[Grand Ole Opry]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.countrystandardtime.com/news/newsitem.asp?xid=653&t=Sarah_Johns_makes_Opry_debut |title=Sarah Johns makes Opry debut |accessdate=2007-07-20 |work=[[Country Standard Time]]}}</ref> Johns' debut album, ''Big Love in a Small Town'', was released on August 28, 2007, and peaked at No.&nbsp;198 on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]]. [[Allmusic]] critic Thom Jurek rated the album three stars out of five, comparing Johns' song choices and attitudes to those of [[Tammy Wynette]] and [[Gretchen Wilson]] and saying, "This record isn't all the way there, but it's well on the way and might blow up the charts anyway. ''Big Love in a Small Town'' is an auspicious debut by a young talent."<ref>{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=album|id=r1183602|pure_url=yes}}|title=''Big Love in a Small Town'' review|last=Jurek|first=Thom|work=Allmusic|accessdate=2009-10-01}}</ref> Its second single, "He Hates Me", peaked at No.&nbsp;47 on the country charts.<ref name="whitburn"/>
