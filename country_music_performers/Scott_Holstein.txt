'''Scott Holstein''' (born May 5, 1974) is an American country and bluegrass singer-songwriter.

Holstein was born in [[Boone County, West Virginia]] and lives in [[Nashville, Tennessee]].<ref name=ross>{{cite web|last=Ross|first=Joe|title=Scott Holstein|url=http://www.rootsmusicreport.com/index.php?page=reviews&search=single&id=275|publisher=Roots Music Report|accessdate=August 19, 2012}}</ref><ref name=thompson>{{cite web|last=Thompson|first=Richard|title=Scott Holstein – Cold Coal Town|date=November 16, 2011 |url=http://bluegrasstoday.com/33896/scott-holstein-cold-coal-town/|publisher=Bluegrass Today|accessdate=August 19, 2012}}</ref> His [[Bluegrass music|bluegrass]] and [[country music]] is influenced by [[Ralph Stanley]], [[Larry Sparks]], and [[Keith Whitley]]. He writes his own music, and he is a [[baritone]] singer. He released his first full-length album, ''Cold Coal Town,'' in 2011.<ref>{{cite news|last=Richards |first=Chris |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/click-track/post/what-you-should-be-listening-to-david-heartbreak-scott-holstein-motion-sickness-of-time-travel-ricardo-villalobos-wolves-in-the-throne-room/2011/09/29/gIQABNet7K_blog.html |title=What you should be listening to: David Heartbreak, Scott Holstein, Motion Sickness of Time Travel, Ricardo Villalobos, Wolves in the Throne Room – Click Track |newspaper=The Washington Post |date=September 29, 2011 |accessdate=August 19, 2012}}</ref> Contributing artists include Don Rigsby of the [[Lonesome River Band]], [[Grammy Award]] winner Randy Kohrs, Clay Hess of [[Ricky Skaggs & Kentucky Thunder]], Aaron Ramsey of [[Mountain Heart]], Jay Weaver, Tim Crouch and Scott Vestal from the [[Sam Bush|Sam Bush Band]].<ref name=ross/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://bluegrassmusic.com/content/2011/reviews/scott-holstein-cold-coal-town |title=Scott Holstein – Cold Coal Town « Bluegrass Unlimited |publisher=Bluegrassmusic.com |date=September 1, 2011 |accessdate=August 19, 2012}}</ref>
