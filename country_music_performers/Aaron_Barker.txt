'''Aaron Barker''' (born March 5, 1953, in [[San Antonio|San Antonio, Texas]]<ref name="whitburn">{{cite book|last=Whitburn|first=Joel|title=Hot Country Songs 1944 to 2008|publisher=Record Research, Inc|page=43|year=2008|isbn=978-0-89820-177-2}}</ref>) is an American singer-songwriter.

Barker has written No. 1 songs for [[George Strait]] ("[[Baby Blue (George Strait song)|Baby Blue]]", "[[Love Without End, Amen]]", "[[Easy Come, Easy Go (George Strait song)|Easy Come, Easy Go]]") and [[Lonestar]] ("[[What About Now (Lonestar song)|What About Now]]").<ref name="musicvf">{{cite web|title=Aaron Barker – Top Songs as Writer|url=http://www.musicvf.com/songs.php?page=artist&artist=Aaron+Barker&tab=songaswriterchartstab|work=Music VF|accessdate=February 14, 2014}}</ref> His songs have also been recorded by [[Doug Supernaw]], [[Clay Walker]], [[Neal McCoy]], [[Aaron Tippin]] and others.<ref name="musicvf" /><ref>{{cite web|title=Aaron Barker {{!}} Credits|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=mn0000489280/credits|pure_url=yes}}|work=[[AllMusic]]|accessdate=February 14, 2014}}</ref> Barker turned "Love Without End, Amen" into a book in 2002.<ref>{{cite news|title=Strait's Hit Becomes a Book|url=http://www.cmt.com/news/news-in-brief/1452016/straits-hit-becomes-a-book.jhtml|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140115062641/http://www.cmt.com/news/news-in-brief/1452016/straits-hit-becomes-a-book.jhtml|url-status=dead|archive-date=January 15, 2014|accessdate=February 14, 2014|newspaper=[[Country Music Television]]|date=January 29, 2002}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Holden|first=Larry|title=Story Behind The Song ...'Love Without End, Amen'|url=http://www.countryweekly.com/vault/story-behind-song-love-without-end-amen|accessdate=February 14, 2014|newspaper=[[Country Weekly]]|date=February 13, 2002}}</ref> He was inducted into the Texas Heritage Songwriters' Hall of Fame in 2007.<ref>{{cite web|title=Aaron Barker {{!}} TxHSA|url=http://www.texasheritagesongwriters.com/inductee/aaron-barker/|work=Texas Heritage Songwriters' Association|accessdate=February 14, 2014}}</ref>

Barker's debut album, ''The Taste of Freedom'', was released by [[Atlantic Records]] in August 1992.<ref>{{cite news|last=Abbott|first=Jim|title=Here's what's new in the record racks|url=https://www.orlandosentinel.com/1992/08/07/heres-whats-new-in-the-record-racks-19/|access-date=February 14, 2014|newspaper=[[Orlando Sentinel]]|date=August 7, 1992}}</ref> The title track peaked at number 73 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs|Hot Country Singles & Tracks]] chart.<ref name="whitburn" />

Barker also writes and performs commercials for [[Blue Bell Creameries|Blue Bell Ice Cream]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Advocate Editorial Board opinion: Songwriters' art supports groups in need|url=http://www.victoriaadvocate.com/news/2013/nov/12/vp_concert_editorial_111313_224912/|accessdate=February 14, 2014|newspaper=[[The Victoria Advocate]]|date=November 12, 2013}}</ref>

On August 8, 2016, it was announced at a private press conference that Barker would be inducted into the [[Nashville Songwriters Hall of Fame]] at a ceremony held on October 9, 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://heartsbluffmusic.com/2016/08/aaron-barker-to-be-inducted-into-nashville-songwriters-hall-of-fame/|title=Aaron Barker To Be Inducted into Nashville Songwriters Hall of Fame {{!}} Hearts Bluff Music|website=heartsbluffmusic.com|access-date=September 2, 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160921021722/https://heartsbluffmusic.com/2016/08/aaron-barker-to-be-inducted-into-nashville-songwriters-hall-of-fame/|archive-date=September 21, 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>
