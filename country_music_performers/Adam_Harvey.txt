'''Adam Harvey''' (born 21 December 1974) is an Australian [[country music]] singer. Harvey has sold over half a million records, has been nominated five times for an [[ARIA Music Awards|ARIA Music Award]] and has won nine [[Country Music Awards of Australia|golden guitars at the Country Music Awards of Australia]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://cruisincountry.com.au/2019/performers/adam-harvey/|title=Adam Harvey|publisher=Cruis' Country|access-date=4 August 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180804110221/http://cruisincountry.com.au/2019/performers/adam-harvey/|archive-date=4 August 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Biography==
Harvey got his start musically learning country classics on the guitar as a small boy. His first gig came at the age of ten, and by his school years he was performing rock covers at a club when he was discovered and went off on tour as support for [[Tania Kernaghan]].<ref>{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p698129|pure_url=yes}}|title=Adam harvey|publisher=[[AllMusic]]|last=True|first=Chris|access-date=29 January 2010}}</ref> In 1998 Harvey won his first [[Country Music Awards of Australia|Country Music (CMAA) Award]] for 'Vocal Collaboration of the Year' with Tanya Self for "Drive Away".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.country.com.au/index.cfm?page_id=1134 |title=Country Music Awards of Australia 1990–1999 |publisher=[[Country Music Association of Australia|CMAA]] |access-date=29 January 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091017180321/http://www.country.com.au/index.cfm?page_id=1134 |archive-date=17 October 2009 }}</ref> In 2001 he won another CMAA Award for 'Vocal Collaboration of the Year' this time with [[Beccy Cole]], Darren Coggan and [[Felicity Urquhart|Felicity]], for "Do I Ever Cross Your Mind".<ref name="CMAA">{{cite web|url=http://www.country.com.au/index.cfm?page_id=1135 |title=Country Music Awards of Australia 2000–2008 |publisher=[[Country Music Association of Australia|CMAA]] |access-date=29 January 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091017175108/http://www.country.com.au/index.cfm?page_id=1135 |archive-date=17 October 2009 }}</ref>

In 2002, Harvey won two CMAA Awards; 'Album of the Year' and 'Male Vocalist of the Year' for ''Workin' Overtime''.<ref name="CMAA"/> ''Workin' Overtime'' was nominated for [[ARIA Award for Best Country Album]] at the [[ARIA Music Awards of 2002|2002 ARIA Awards]].<ref name="ARIA">{{cite web|url=http://www.ariaawards.com.au/history-by-artist.php?letter=A&artist=Adam%20Harvey |title=ARIA Award History – Adam Harvey |publisher=[[Australian Recording Industry Association]] |access-date=29 January 2010 }}{{dead link|date=October 2016 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

In 2004, Harvey won a second CMAA Award for 'Male Vocalist of the Year' that year.<ref name="CMAA"/> In 2005 he received his third [[ARIA Music Awards of 2005|ARIA award]] nomination for Best Country Album ''Can't Settle for Less''.<ref name="ARIA"/> In 2008, he won Album of the Year ''I'm Doin' Alright''.<ref name="CMAA"/>

In 2009, he sang the [[Australian national anthem]] before the Australia vs New Zealand [[rugby league]] test match.{{citation needed|date=December 2021}}

Harvey has been nominated for a CMAA Award in 2010 for Collaboration of the Year with [[John Williamson (singer)|John Williamson]] for "King of the Road".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.country.com.au/index.cfm?page_id=1226 |title=Finalists – 38th CMAA Country Music Awards of Australia |publisher=[[Country Music Association of Australia|CMAA]] |access-date=27 January 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100301214250/http://www.country.com.au/index.cfm?page_id=1226 |archive-date=1 March 2010 |url-status=dead |df=dmy }}</ref>
