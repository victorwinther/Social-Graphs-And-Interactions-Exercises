'''Jace Everett Beasley''' (born May 27, 1972) is an American singer-songwriter and musician. Signed to [[Epic Records]] in 2005, he released his debut single "That's the Kind of Love I'm In" in 2005, which peaked at No. 52 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs]] charts, and was the first single from his self-titled debut album. He also co-wrote [[Josh Turner]]'s Number One single "[[Your Man (Josh Turner song)|Your Man]]".  His song "[[Bad Things (Jace Everett song)|Bad Things]]" is the theme for the [[Home Box Office|HBO]] series ''[[True Blood]]'' and peaked No. 2 in Norway in 2009. In the same year, he released his third album, ''Red Revelations'', under the Weston Boys label that peaked No. 12 in Norway.

== Biography ==
Jace Everett was born in [[Evansville, Indiana]] in 1972. His father's jobs kept them on the move through [[Indianapolis]] and then [[St. Louis, MO]] before moving to [[Fort Worth, TX]] at the age of six. He started playing music in his church and in school concerts. He later moved to [[Nashville, TN]] to attend [[Belmont University]].<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web |url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p725883|pure_url=yes}} |title=Jace Everett biography |accessdate=July 17, 2008 |last=Monger |first=James Christopher |work=[[Allmusic]]}}</ref>

In 2005, Everett was signed to [[Epic Records]]. He released his first single, "That's The Kind of Love I'm In", in June 2005. This song charted at No. 52 on the country charts, and was followed in early 2006 by the release of his self-titled debut album.<ref name="allmusic"/><ref name="bmi"/> Also in 2006, [[Josh Turner]] charted at Number One on the country charts with the song "[[Your Man (Josh Turner song)|Your Man]]", which Everett co-wrote, and for which Everett received a [[Broadcast Music Incorporated]] award.<ref name="bmi">{{cite web |url=http://www.bmi.com/news/entry/334763 |title=Charts Stand By 'Your Man' |accessdate=June 4, 2010 |date=April 12, 2006 |work=[[Broadcast Music Incorporated]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.cmt.com/news/country-music/1526605/ascap-bmi-honor-josh-turner-and-writers-of-your-man.jhtml |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081202021356/http://www.cmt.com/news/country-music/1526605/ascap-bmi-honor-josh-turner-and-writers-of-your-man.jhtml |url-status=dead |archive-date=December 2, 2008 |title=ASCAP, BMI Honor Josh Turner and Writers of "Your Man" |accessdate=June 4, 2010 |last=Morris |first=Edward |date=March 20, 2006 |work=[[Country Music Television|CMT]]}}</ref> The album's other singles — "Bad Things," "Nowhere in the Neighborhood" and "Everything I Want" — did not chart in the United States, although "Bad Things" reached No. 2 in Norway, No. 30 in Sweden and No. 49 in UK in 2009.<ref name="VG"/><ref name="Sweden"/><ref name="OCC"/>

"Bad Things" is used as the theme song for the [[Home Box Office|HBO]] TV series ''[[True Blood]]''.<ref>{{cite magazine |url=http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0,,20222109,00.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080901234206/http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0,,20222109,00.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=September 1, 2008 |title=True Blood – TV Review|accessdate=October 22, 2008|date= September 7, 2008|last=Tucker |first=Ken |magazine=[[Entertainment Weekly]]}}</ref> Charlaine Harris pays homage to the song by including "Bad Things" playing at Merlotte's in the 2010 Sookie Stackhouse mystery ''[[Dead in the Family]]''. It won a 2009 [[Broadcast Music Incorporated]] award in the cable television category and was nominated for a 2009 [[Scream Award]] for "Best Scream Song of the Year."<ref>[http://www.bmi.com/news/entry/538576 "BMI Film & Television Awards Tout Composers of Year’s Top Film, Television, & Cable Music"] Retrieved on September 2, 2009.</ref><ref>[http://www.spike.com/event/scream2009/page/vote/category/33121 "Best Scream Song of the Year, 2009"] Spike.com Retrieved on September 2, 2009.</ref> His album, entitled ''Red Revelations'', was released in June 2009.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://www.trueblood-online.com/jace-everett/jace-everett-talks-about-his-new-cd-red-revelations| title=Jace Everett talks about his new CD "Red Revelations"| accessdate=July 17, 2009| work=True Blood Online| archive-date=September 3, 2009| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090903135917/http://www.trueblood-online.com/jace-everett/jace-everett-talks-about-his-new-cd-red-revelations| url-status=dead}}</ref> His duet Evil (Is Going On), a cover of the song written by Willie Dixon, with [[CC Adcock]] is part of the [[True Blood (season 2)|True Blood season 2]] soundtrack.<ref>[http://www.dreadcentral.com/news/37631/true-blood-listen-season-2-soundtrack-live-stream-watch-full-season-3-trailer True Blood: Listen to the Season 2 Soundtrack Live Stream; Watch the Full Season 3 Trailer]</ref> A new album, "Mr. Good Times" was released in 2011. His latest album, "Terra Rosa" was released digitally on July 23, 2013.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.jaceeverett.com/let-the-games-begin/ |title="JaceEverett.com: Let The Games Begin" |access-date=October 27, 2013 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20131028004524/http://www.jaceeverett.com/let-the-games-begin/ |archive-date=October 28, 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref>
