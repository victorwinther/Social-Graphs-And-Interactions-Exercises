'''Bradley Walker''' is an American [[Bluegrass music|bluegrass]] and [[country music]] singer and songwriter.

==Early life==
Bradley Walker is a native of [[Athens, Alabama]]. He was born with [[muscular dystrophy]], and has been in a wheelchair all his life. He was a student at [[Limestone County School District|East Limestone High School]] where he played percussion in the school band.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://archive.decaturdaily.com/decaturdaily/news/060721/walker.shtml |title=Riding on a 'Highway of Dreams' |first= Holly|last= Hollman |work=The Decatur Daily |date=July 21, 2006 }}</ref>

Walker started singing when he was two or three years old, and began performing in public when he was four.<ref name="waff">{{cite news |url=http://www.waff.com/story/13305350/overcoming-the-odds-country-singer-lives-out-his-dreams |title=Overcoming the Odds: Country singer lives out his dreams |first=Kimberly|last= Essex |work=WAFF48 }}</ref>  When he was ten, he was invited on stage to perform with [[The Oak Ridge Boys]], and when he was eleven, he sang with The Oak Ridge Boys on [[The Nashville Network]]'s [[Nashville Now]] as well as on the [[The Jerry Lewis MDA Labor Day Telethon|Jerry Lewis MDA Telethon]].<ref name="profile">{{cite web |url=http://americanprofile.com/articles/singer-bradley-walker/ |title=Singer Thrives Despite Disease |first= Michael|last= McCall |date=January 20, 2008 |work=American Profile }}</ref>

He works at the [[Browns Ferry Nuclear Power Plant]] as a Material Inventory Coordinator while pursuing a music career.<ref name="waff"/>

==Music career==
In 1998,  Walker formed a band, The Trinity Mountain Boys, and began to perform at bluegrass festivals.<ref name="profile" /> In 2001, he joined the Georgia-based group Lost Horizon.<ref>{{cite magazine |url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/297056/bradley-walker/biography |title=Bradley Walker > Biography |magazine=Billboard |access-date=2016-11-13 |archive-date=2016-11-29 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161129220949/http://www.billboard.com/artist/297056/bradley-walker/biography |url-status=dead }}</ref>  He was signed to [[Rounder Records]], and released his debut album, ''Highway of Dreams'', in 2006. The album was produced by [[Carl Jackson]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=6955339 |title='Highway of Dreams' for Singer Bradley Walker|date=January 23, 2007 |work=NPR}}</ref>  For his performance on the album, he won the Male Vocalist of the Year Award from the [[International Bluegrass Music Association]].<ref name="rolling stone"/>

===2016:  ''Call Me Old-Fashioned''===
Walker's second album, ''Call Me Old-Fashioned'', was produced by [[Rory Feek]] and recorded at the [[Joey + Rory]] studio out on their farm.  Walker had known Joey and Rory Feek since 2007, and he was asked by Rory Feek to sing the hymn "[[Leave It There]]" at the funeral of [[Joey Feek]] in accordance with her wishes.<ref name="rolling stone">{{cite magazine |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/country/features/bradley-walker-on-joey-feeks-influence-behind-new-album-w445844 |title=Bradley Walker on Joey Feek's Influence Behind New Album |magazine= Rolling Stone |first=Deborah |last=Evans Price|date=October 20, 2016 }}</ref> [[Bill Gaither (gospel singer)|Bill Gaither]] of the [[Gaither Music Group]], who was also at the funeral service, heard Walker's performance and signed Walker to his label.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.gospelherald.com/articles/67436/20161021/christian-artist-born-with-muscular-dystrophy-discusses-faith-influence-of-rory-and-joey-feek-album-call-me-old-fashioned.htm |title=Christian Artist Born with Muscular Dystrophy Discusses Faith, Influence of Rory and Joey Feek, Album 'Call Me Old-Fashioned' |first=Leah Marianne |last=Klett|date= October 21, 2016|work=Gospel Herald }}</ref> The album includes a posthumous duet with Joey Feek, "In The Time That You Gave Me",  using vocals she recorded before her death.<ref name="rolling stone"/> The album was released on September 23, 2016,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/call-me-old-fashioned-mw0002974140 |title=Call Me Old-Fashioned |first=Mark |last=Deming |work=AllMusic }}</ref> and debuted at No. 9 on the Top Country Albums chart.<ref>{{cite magazine |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/country/7550131/bradley-walker-call-me-old-fashioned-album-joey-rory-show|title=Bradley Walker Reflects on His Surprise Top 10 Country Album & Singing at Joey Feek's Funeral |date=October 25, 2016 |first= Chuck |last=Dauphin |magazine=Billboard }}</ref>

===2017–19: ''Blessed: Hymns & Songs of Faith'' ===
On October 6, 2017, Walker released ''Blessed: Hymns & Songs of Faith''. The album, produced by Ben Isaacs, features collaborations with [[Vince Gill]], [[Alison Krauss]], [[Rhonda Vincent]], [[Jimmy Fortune]], [[The Oak Ridge Boys]], [[The Isaacs]] and [[Ricky Skaggs]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Blessed-Hymns-and-Songs-of-Faith-by-Bradley-Walker-ft-Alison-Krauss-More-Out-Now-20171006 |title='Blessed: Hymns and Songs of Faith' by Bradley Walker ft, Alison Krauss & More Out Now|date=October 6, 2017 |work = Music World.com }}</ref>  The album received a [[GMA Dove Award]] for the Bluegrass/Country/Roots Album of the Year in 2018.<ref name="whnt" />

He also performed a song "Leave It There" in the Homecoming CD/DVD release ''Give the World A Smile'' in 2017.<ref>{{cite web |url= https://www.gastongazette.com/entertainment/20170406/bill-and-gloria-gaither-share-2-new-homecoming-albums|title=Bill and Gloria Gaither share 2 new 'Homecoming' albums |work=Gaston Gazette |first=Wade |last=Allen |date= April 6, 2017}}</ref>

Walker appeared at the [[Crossroads Guitar Festival 2019]] alongside Vince Gill.<ref>[https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-news/eric-clapton-2019-crossroads-festival-live-album-1065415/ Eric Clapton’s 2019 Crossroads Fest Set for Live LP/Concert Film Release ] ''[[Rolling Stone]]'', September 23, 2020. Retrieved 11 October 2021</ref>

===2020&ndash;present: Brothers of the Heart===

In 2020, Walker collaborated with [[Jimmy Fortune]], Ben Isaacs, and Mike Rogers to release an album ''Brotherly Love'' and a concert DVD/TV special filmed at Rory Feek's barn/studio near Columbia, TN.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.soundslikenashville.com/news/jimmy-fortune-isaacs-rogers-walker-elizabeth-premiere/ |title=Jimmy Fortune, Ben Isaacs, Mike Rogers and Bradley Walker Deliver New Take on Statlers' Classic 'Elizabeth' |first= Deborah |last= Evans Price|date= September 18, 2020|work=Sounds Like Nashville }}</ref> The album was released digitally on May 29, 2020, and the DVD and CD released on September 4, 2020.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.thechristianbeat.org/index.php/new-music/8383-jimmy-fortune-bradley-walker-mike-rogers-ben-isaacs-release-gaither-music-group-cd-dvd-tv-special |title=Jimmy Fortune, Bradley Walker, Mike Rogers & Ben Isaacs Release Gaither Music Group CD, DVD & TV Special |date= September 3, 2020 |first= Herb |last=Longs|work=The Christian Beat }}</ref> The concert film aired on the [[Circle (TV network)|Circle]] network on October 20, 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thechristianbeat.org/index.php/news/8725-gaither-music-group-s-brotherly-love-premieres-on-circle-network |title=Gaither Music Group's 'Brotherly Love' Premieres On Circle Network |date= October 20, 2020 |first= Herb |last=Longs |work=The Christian Beat}}</ref> The collaboration was successful and they decided to continue with another project, and while they were recording their second album they named their group Brothers of the Heart.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.ccmmagazine.com/ccm-cafe/a-conversation-with-brothers-of-the-heart-fortune-walker-rogers-isaacs/ |title=A Conversation with Brothers of the Heart (Fortune, Walker, Rogers, Isaacs) |first= Brandon |last=Woolum |date= February 10, 2023 |work=CCM Magazine}}</ref>

In February 2023, Brothers of the Heart released the second album, ''Listen To The Music''.  The album reached No. 5 on the Top Christian Albums chart, and No. 32 on Top Album Sales.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://todayschristianent.com/music-news-brothers-of-the-heart-to-perform-on-the-opry-feb-7-listen-to-the-music-hits-1-on-billboard-christian-album-chart/ |title=
Music News: Brothers of the Heart to perform on The Opry Feb. 7; 'Listen To The Music' hits #1 on Billboard Christian album chart |work=Today Christian Entertainment }}</ref>
