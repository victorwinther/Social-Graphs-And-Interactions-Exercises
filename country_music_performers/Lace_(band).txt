'''Lace''' was a [[Canadians|Canadian]] [[country music]] group who formed in 1998 with the backing of music producer [[David Foster]].<ref>{{cite news| last =Strauss| first =Neil| title =The pop life: Seeking models who can sing | page =E3| newspaper =[[The New York Times]]| date =1998-12-24| url =https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9E0CE5DD113CF937A15751C1A96E958260| accessdate =2008-03-31 }}</ref><ref>{{cite news| last =Mclaughlin| first =John P| title =She's plucky in Lace: Beverley Mahood and mates trying for breakthrough album| page =B4| newspaper =[[The Province]]| date =2001-07-20}}</ref> Active between 1998 and 2001, the band charted four singles on national country music charts, in addition to releasing a self-titled studio album on [[143 Records]] (in association with [[Warner Bros. Records]] Nashville).

== Biography ==
Foster's plan to put together an all-female country trio began to take shape after seeing a music video of [[Beverley Mahood]] on [[CMT (Canada)|CMT Canada]] while he was visiting his mother in [[Victoria, British Columbia]]; he requested to meet with Mahood, and soon after signed her to a recording contract on his label, [[143 Records]].<ref>{{cite news| last =Mclaughlin| first =John P| title =Singin' Dixie: They're like the Chicks, only two-thirds Canadian. Lace is the latest country girl band| page =B1| newspaper =[[The Province]]| date =1999-11-16}}</ref> Mahood later suggested her friend Giselle, a fellow singer-songwriter in [[Ontario]], prior to David Foster introducing them to the group's third member, [[Austin, Texas]] native Corbi Dyann. The trio received a [[Juno Award]] nomination in 2000 for [[Juno Awards of 2000#Best Country Group or Duo|Best Country Group or Duo]].<ref name="Canoe1">{{cite magazine| last =Ross| first =Mike| title =Three cheers! Lace's Beverley Mahood loves trio harmonies| magazine =[[Jam!]]| date =2000-07-22| url =http://jam.canoe.ca/Music/Artists/L/Lace/2000/07/22/746782.html| archive-url =https://archive.today/20130115060151/http://jam.canoe.ca/Music/Artists/L/Lace/2000/07/22/746782.html| url-status =usurped| archive-date =January 15, 2013| accessdate =2008-03-31 }}</ref> They also received nominations for Group of the Year and Video of the Year ("I Want a Man") at the 2000 [[Canadian Country Music Association|Canadian Country Music Awards]].<ref>{{cite news| last =Fuller| first =Cam| title =Touring a treat for Lace| page =D1| publisher =[[Saskatoon StarPhoenix]]| date =2000-08-11}}</ref>

Their 1999 self-titled album includes record producing credits of Foster, [[Chris Farren (country musician)|Chris Farren]], [[Humberto Gatica]], and J. Richard Hutt. Songwriting credits on the album include [[Linda Thompson (actress)|Linda Thompson]], [[Deana Carter]], [[Sarah McLachlan]] and [[John Scott Sherrill]].<ref>{{cite news| last =North| first =Peter| title =Time for Canada to try on some country Lace| page =C3| newspaper =[[Edmonton Journal]]| date =1999-11-25}}</ref> The group's best-known song was "I Want a Man".<ref>{{cite magazine| last =Van Wyk| first =Anika| title =Mahood gets 'Moody' on new album| magazine =[[Jam!]]| date =2004-03-10| url =http://jam.canoe.ca/Music/Artists/M/Mahood_Beverley/2004/03/10/747339.html| archive-url =https://archive.today/20120711201531/http://jam.canoe.ca/Music/Artists/M/Mahood_Beverley/2004/03/10/747339.html| url-status =usurped| archive-date =July 11, 2012| accessdate =2008-03-31 }}</ref>

In 2000, Corbi Dyann left the group and Canadian Stacey Lee replaced her.<ref name="Canoe1" /><ref>{{cite news| last =Sweetland| first =Phil| title =Mahood merges beauty, talent| page =G3| newspaper =[[Calgary Herald]]| date =2000-04-01}}</ref>

The trio often was seen as attempting to capitalize on the success of girl groups [[Dixie Chicks]] and [[SHeDAISY]].<ref>{{cite news| last =Flynn| first =Andrew| title =Lace takes on country girl groups with Foster's help| page =1| publisher =[[Canadian Press]]| date =1999-11-27}}</ref>
