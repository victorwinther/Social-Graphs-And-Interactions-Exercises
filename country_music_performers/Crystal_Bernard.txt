==Early life==
Born in [[Garland, Texas]] to [[televangelist]] Jerry Wayne Bernard and teacher Gaylon Fussell,<ref name="leszczak"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://jerrybarnard.com|title=Jerry Bernard|access-date=May 22, 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171007063139/http://jerrybarnard.com/|archive-date=October 7, 2017|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name="People">{{cite web|url=https://people.com/archive/crystal-a-go-go-vol-42-no-21|title=Crystal-a-Go-Go|author=Michael A. Lipton|date=November 22, 1993|access-date=May 21, 2020}}</ref><ref name=Legacy.com>{{cite web|title=Mrs. Gaylon Bernard|website=[[Legacy.com]] |url=https://www.legacy.com/funeral-homes/obituaries/name/gaylon-bernard-obituary|date=March 2, 2018|access-date=May 21, 2020}}{{Dead link|date=October 2022 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> Bernard became an entertainer at a young age, singing [[gospel music|gospel songs]] with her older sister, [[Robyn Bernard|Robyn]] (1959–2024), also an actress. One recording of the two that has survived from those years is a song called "The Monkey Song" on ''Feudin' Fussin' and Frettin' '', recorded when Crystal was eight years old.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://wfmu.org/playlists/shows/15387|title=WFMU: Mark Allen: Playlist from June 16, 2005|website=wfmu.org|access-date=September 30, 2017}}</ref> The recording is from a 1972 [[Thomas Road Baptist Church]] service led by [[Jerry Falwell]]. In addition to Robyn, she also has two younger sisters: Scarlett and Angelique Bernard.{{Citation needed |date=June 2024}} 

Bernard studied acting at [[Alley Theatre]] while growing up in [[Houston]], Texas. She attended [[Spring High School]] and continued her education at [[Baylor University]], <!--notable? where she briefly dated Richard Wayne Kennedy, --> studying acting and international relations.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20080113020421/http://movies.nytimes.com/person/5758/Crystal-Bernard Bernard's Biography] from [[Allmovie]] via ''The New York Times'' website</ref>

==Career==

===Television===
[[File:Crystal Bernard at the 1991 Emmy Awards cropped and airbrushed.jpg|thumb|Bernard at the [[18th Daytime Emmy Awards|1991 Emmy Awards]]]]
Bernard got her start in prime time television on the sitcom ''[[Happy Days]]'', playing K.C. Cunningham during season 10 (1982–83). Then, after various guest appearances on other series, she joined the cast of the resurrected, [[Broadcast syndication|syndicated version]] of the sitcom ''[[It's a Living]]'',<ref>{{cite news|url=http://tv.nytimes.com/show/156763/It-s-a-Living/overview|work=The New York Times|title=It's a Living}}</ref> playing waitress Amy Tompkins. In 1989, she starred in a television pilot, ''Chameleons'', created by [[Glen A. Larson]]. She played Shelley, a self-proclaimed crazed heiress who discovers that her eccentric millionaire uncle Jason Carr has been murdered. She sets out on a quest to find the truth behind his murder.

She played strong-willed airport lunch counter operator and cellist Helen Chappel Hackett on the sitcom ''[[Wings (1990 TV series)|Wings]]'' for the series entire eight season duration (1990–97). In 1999, she starred in the television movie ''To Love, Honor and Betray'' opposite [[David Cubitt]] and [[James Brolin]].

===Film===
Early in her film career, she appeared in ''[[Young Doctors in Love]]'' (1982), along with her ''Happy Days'' co-star [[Ted McGinley]]. She starred in Deborah Brock's [[Roger Corman]]-produced horror film ''[[Slumber Party Massacre 2]]'' (1987).
Later, her work included a role in the film ''[[Jackpot (2001 film)|Jackpot]]'' (2001) and starring roles in ''Welcome to Paradise'' (2007) and ''[[Grave Misconduct]]'' (2008). She also starred in two [[Hallmark Channel]] movies, ''[[Single Santa Seeks Mrs. Claus]]'' (2004) and its sequel, ''[[Meet the Santas]]'' (2005).

===Theatre===
Bernard has appeared in these stage productions since the end of ''Wings'':
* 1999: a Los Angeles production of ''[[Crimes of the Heart]]''
* 2001: a 1999 [[Marquis Theatre]] revival of ''[[Annie Get Your Gun (musical)|Annie Get Your Gun]]''<ref>[http://www.ibdb.com/productionreplacements.asp?ID=6287 Replacements and additions to the 1999 ''Annie Get Your Gun'' revival] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081204061111/http://www.ibdb.com/productionreplacements.asp?ID=6287|date=December 4, 2008}} from the [[Internet Broadway Database]]</ref> wherein she was the last in a series of actresses to play [[Annie Oakley]]
* 2005: West Coast premiere of ''Barbra's Wedding'', starring opposite [[Daniel Stern (actor)|Daniel Stern]] (who also wrote the play) at the Falcon Theatre in [[Burbank, California]]<ref>{{cite web| url =http://www.falcontheatre.com/Pages/05-06_Barbra%27sWedding.php| title =Barbra's Wedding| website =Falcontheatre.com| date =September 14, 2005| access-date =June 22, 2018| archive-date =December 13, 2019| archive-url =https://web.archive.org/web/20191213033456/http://www.falcontheatre.com/Pages/05-06_Barbra%27sWedding.php| url-status =dead}}</ref>

===Music===
In addition to acting, Bernard has a music career as a singer-songwriter. Albums include ''The Girl Next Door'' (1996) and ''Don't Touch Me There'' (1999). The latter includes a gospel track she recorded with her father and one sung with [[country music|country]] artist [[Billy Dean]].  The single "Don't Touch Me There" peaked at No. 25 on the ''Billboard'' Adult Contemporary chart.

She co-wrote the song "If I Were Your Girl" with [[Rhett Lawrence]], which appeared on [[Paula Abdul]]'s album ''[[Head over Heels (Paula Abdul album)|Head over Heels]]'' (1995).  She also co-wrote the song "Come To Me" on the debut self titled album of Angie & Debbie Winans (1993).

Bernard sang a duet with [[Peter Cetera]] called "(I Wanna Take) Forever Tonight", released on Cetera's album ''[[One Clear Voice]]'' (1995).  The single peaked at No. 22 on the ''Billboard'' Adult Contemporary chart and No. 86 on the Hot 100.

She sang a duet with [[Jim Messina (musician)|Jim Messina]] called "Watching the River Run".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0gLoewpn060|title=Crystal Bernard & Jim Messina Watching The River Run|last=Artiscindesign|date=March 10, 2014|access-date=September 30, 2017|via=YouTube}}</ref>

She appeared in the video for "[[Birdhouse in Your Soul]]" by [[They Might Be Giants]].
