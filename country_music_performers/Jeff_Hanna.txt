'''Jeffrey R. Hanna''' (born July 11, 1947) is an American singer-songwriter and performance musician, best known for his association with the [[Nitty Gritty Dirt Band]]. His professional music career has spanned seven decades.

==Early life==
Hanna was born in Detroit, Michigan.<ref name="discogs">{{cite web |url=https://www.discogs.com/artist/330658-Jeff-Hanna |title=Jeff Hanna Discography |work=discogs.com |publisher=[[Discogs]] |accessdate=2016-04-12 }}</ref> In 1962, he moved with his family to Long Beach, California. As a high school student there, he and some friends started a [[jug band]] that ultimately evolved into the Nitty Gritty Dirt Band.<ref name="encyclopedia">{{cite encyclopedia |editor-last=Stambler |editor-first=Irwin |editor2-last=Landon |editor2-first=Grelun |encyclopedia=Country Music: The Encyclopedia |pages=135–137 |title=Dirt Band, Nitty Gritty Dirt Band |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=d18K8QWcEo8C&q=jeff+hanna+biography&pg=PA135 |date=2000-07-14 |publisher=Macmillan |isbn=9780312264871 |accessdate=2016-04-12 }}</ref>

==Musical career==
He was one of the founders and is the longest-serving member of the Nitty Gritty Dirt Band, where he has been a singer, songwriter, lead guitarist, drummer and [[Washboard (musical instrument)|washboard player]]. Through the years, he has been a major force in keeping the band together and maintaining its blend of folk, country and rock music.<ref name="discogs"/><ref name="encyclopedia"/>

Hanna has over 380 recording credits, primarily as a composer, but also as a vocalist, guitarist ([[acoustic guitar|acoustic]], [[electric guitar|electric]], [[steel guitar|steel]], [[slide guitar|slide]], [[Twelve-string guitar|twelve-string]], and [[baritone guitar|baritone]]), arranger, and producer.<ref name="credits">{{cite web |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/jeff-hanna-mn0000189629/credits |title=Jeff Hanna - Credits |work=allmusic.com |publisher=[[AllMusic]] |accessdate=2016-04-12 }}</ref>

In addition to the Nitty Gritty Dirt Band, his credits include work with artists such as [[Linda Ronstadt]], [[Suzy Bogguss]], [[The Texas Tenors]], [[Patty Loveless]], [[Rascal Flatts]], [[Matraca Berg]], [[Hannah Montana]], [[Emmylou Harris]], [[The Chieftains]], [[Johnny Cash]], [[June Carter Cash]], [[Earl Scruggs]], [[Michael Martin Murphey]], [[Dickey Betts]], and [[Steve Martin]].<ref name="credits"/>

In 2006, his composition "[[Bless the Broken Road]]", co-written with [[Marcus Hummon]] and Bobby Boyd in 1994, won a [[Grammy Award for Best Country Song]].<ref name="awards">{{cite web |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/jeff-hanna-mn0000189629/awards |title=Jeff Hanna - Awards |work=allmusic.com |publisher=[[AllMusic]] |accessdate=2016-04-12 }}</ref> It has been recorded by the Nitty Gritty Dirt Band, Marcus Hummon, and, in the Grammy year, [[Rascal Flatts]].<ref name="theboot">{{cite web |url=http://theboot.com/jeff-hanna-matraca-berg-country-love-stories/ |title=Jeff Hanna + Matraca Berg — Country's Greatest Love Stories |work=theboot.com |accessdate=2016-04-12 }}</ref>

==Personal life==
Hanna's children are visual artist Christopher Hanna, and Jaime Hanna. Jamie was formerly a touring member of [[the Mavericks]] before founding the duo [[Hanna-McEuen]]. He joined Nitty Gritty Dirt Band in 2018.<ref name="ArtistDirect">{{cite web |url=http://www.artistdirect.com/artist/bio/hanna-mceuen/3273816 |title=Hanna-McEuen Biography |authorlink=[[Artistdirect]] |work=artistdirect.com |publisher=ARTISTdirect |accessdate=2016-04-12 }}</ref>

He has been married to [[Matraca Berg]] since December 5, 1993. The couple met while touring with [[Clint Black]] in the late 1980s. They live in Nashville, Tennessee.<ref name="theboot"/>
