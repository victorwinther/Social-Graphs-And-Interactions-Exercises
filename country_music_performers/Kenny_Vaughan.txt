'''Kenneth Vaughan''' is an American guitarist. He is best known as a long-time member of [[Marty Stuart]]’s supporting band, The Fabulous Superlatives.

==Career==
===Early life===
Vaughan was born in [[Oklahoma]], but raised in [[Denver, Colorado]]. His guitar instructor was  [[Bill Frisell]]. In the late 1960s, Vaughan played in a number of rock bands in the nearby [[Littleton, Colorado]], area (where he lived), including a progressive rock group called Amos. Soon after, Vaughan joined a local progressive jazz band, and then began playing country music in local bars.<ref name=KV-roots-2017>{{cite web|url=http://musiccityroots.com/artist/kenny-vaughan/ |title=Kenny Vaughan |work=Music City Roots |access-date=27 April 2017 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150118024820/http://musiccityroots.com/artist/kenny-vaughan/ |archive-date=18 January 2015 }}</ref>

Vaughan was a member of the  Colorado punk band Jonny III in the late 1970s and early 80s. This band started Vaughan's partnership with his long-time songwriting partner Jeffrey Leroy Smith, better known as Leroy X.<ref name=KV-punk-2017
>{{cite web | url=http://scarletdukes.com/colpunk/jonny3.shtml| title=Jonny III | work=Colorado New Wave/Punk Rock| access-date= 27 April 2017| author=Icepick Phil}}</ref> He moved to [[Nashville, Tennessee|Nashville]] in the 1980s, where he became known as a country music guitarist.<ref name=KV-bmi-2011>{{cite news|last1=Mallernee Barnes|first1=Ellen|title=Hillbilly Rock: With 'V,' Kenny Vaughan Takes the Mic|url=https://www.bmi.com/news/entry/hillbilly_rock_with_v_kenny_vaughan_takes_the_mic|access-date=9 April 2017|work=BMI News|date=1 December 2011}}</ref>

Along with Greg Garing, Vaughan was in part responsible for the revitalization of Nashville's historic [[Broadway (Nashville, Tennessee)|Lower Broadway]] district. They drew crowds of listeners while playing in the back room of [[Tootsie's Orchid Lounge|Tootsie's]].<ref name=KV-ky.com-2012>{{cite news|last1=Tunis|first1=Walter|title=Walter Tunis: Kenny Vaughan makes Willie's the place to be|url=http://www.kentucky.com/entertainment/music-news-reviews/article44391414.html|access-date=28 April 2017|work=Kentucky.com|date=29 November 2012}}</ref>

Vaughan was briefly a member of [[McBride & the Ride]] in 1994,<ref name="new">{{cite journal |last=Mansfield |first=Brian |author-link=Brian Mansfield |date=March 1994 |title=McBride & the Ride: Now They Are Six |journal=New Country |volume=1 |issue=1 |pages=11}}</ref> and later, of [[Trent Summar & the New Row Mob]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/trent-summar-mn0000017417/biography|title = Trent Summar Biography, Songs, & Albums|website = [[AllMusic]]}}</ref>

===Touring and recording===
Vaughan has been a member of [[Marty Stuart]]’s Fabulous Superlatives since its inception in 2002.<ref name=fan-website>{{cite web|title=Marty Stuart Fan Page: The Band|url=http://www.martystuart.com/band-crew.htm|access-date=17 December 2017|publisher=Sherry}}</ref><ref name=HS-nodep-2005>{{cite web|url=http://nodepression.com/article/marty-stuart-party-may-come-end-road-goes-forever|title=Marty Stuart - The party may come to an end, but the road goes on forever|work=No Depression|access-date=October 13, 2017|author=Jon Weisberger|date=August 31, 2005|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170930164100/http://nodepression.com/article/marty-stuart-party-may-come-end-road-goes-forever|archive-date=September 30, 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> Previously, he toured with Lucinda Williams as she promoted ''[[Car Wheels on a Gravel Road]]''. He also has supported [[Allison Moorer]] and [[Kim Richey]].<ref name=KV-lexington-2012>{{cite news|last1=Tunis|first1=Walter|title=Country guitarist Kenny Vaughan steps out on his own|url=http://www.kentucky.com/entertainment/music-news-reviews/article44365353.html|access-date=27 April 2017|work=Lexington Herald Reader|date=21 June 2012}}</ref>

Vaughan's only solo album (so far) is ''V'', made with members of The Fabulous Superlatives and [[Marty Stuart]] himself. The album, released in 2011, is a mix of country, rockabilly, and blues songs written by Vaughan, and includes three instrumental tracks.<ref name=KV-premier-2011>{{cite news|last1=Ellis|first1=Andy|title=CD Review: Kenny Vaughan - "V"
|url=https://www.premierguitar.com/articles/CD_Review_Kenny_Vaughan_V|access-date=27 April 2017|work=Premier Guitar|date=18 October 2011}}</ref><ref name=KV-nodep-2011>{{cite news|last1=Lines|first1=Mando|title=Kenny Vaughan: V|url=http://nodepression.com/article/kenny-vaughan-v|access-date=28 April 2017|work=No Depression|date=13 September 2011}}</ref>

In addition to his session work and touring with the Fabulous Superlatives, Vaughan performs as part of his trio whenever he returns to Nashville. The Kenny Vaughan Trio also includes Jeffrey Clemens ([[G Love and Special Sauce]]) on drums and Dave Roe ([[Johnny Cash]]) on bass.<ref name=KV-jfp-2012>{{cite news|last1=Morrisey|first1=Larry|title=Not Just a Country Picker|url=http://www.jacksonfreepress.com/news/2012/oct/24/not-just-country-picker/|access-date=28 April 2017|work=Jackson Free Press|date=24 October 2012}}</ref>

==Awards==
In 2002, Vaughan was inducted into the Colorado Country Music Hall of Fame.<ref name=KV-ccmbhof-2017>{{cite web | url=http://www.ccmhof.com/colomain/inductees.htm| title=CCMHOF Inductees| work=Colorado Country Music Hall of Fame| access-date= 27 April 2017}}</ref>

In 2006, Vaughan received the Americana Music Association's Instrumentalist of the Year award, and the Lifetime Achievement Award for Instrumentalist.<ref name=KV-star-2006>{{cite news|last1=staff writer|title=Neil Young, James McMurtry win Americana music awards|url=http://www.thestar.com.my/story/?file=%2F2006%2F9%2F23%2Fapworld%2F20060923114320&sec=apworld|access-date=27 April 2017|work=The Star Online|date=23 September 2006}}</ref>

==Music Equipment==
Vaughan primarily plays chambered-body guitars by Floyd Cassista with [[Lindy Fralin]] pickups. His amplifiers have been a [[Fender Princeton]] Reverb and a [[Valco]] 1x12 combo.<ref name=KV-gplayer-2006>{{cite news|last1=Fox|first1=Darrin|title=Marty Stuart: One of Country Music's New Traditionalists|url=http://www.guitarplayer.com/artists/1013/marty-stuart-one-of-country-musics-new-traditionalists/16293|access-date=27 April 2017|work=Guitar Player|date=8 August 2006}}</ref>

Working with input from Vaughan, Marty Stuart, and Paul Martin, RS Guitarworks produced the Superlative series of guitars and basses.<ref name=KV-rsguitar-201u>{{cite web | url=http://www.rsguitarworks.net/cms2/portfolio/superlative-series/| title=Superlative Series Guitars & Basses | work=RD Guitarworks| access-date= 28 April 2017}}</ref>
