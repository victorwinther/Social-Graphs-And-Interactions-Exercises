'''Jaida Dreyer''' is an American-Canadian [[country music]] singer and songwriter. She was signed as the flagship artist on [[Streamsound Records]], a label founded in 2012 by [[Byron Gallimore]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.jaidadreyer.com/about |accessdate=August 30, 2012 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120629161927/http://www.jaidadreyer.com/about |title=About {{!}} Jaida Dreyer |archivedate=June 29, 2012 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.musicrow.com/2012/02/gallimore-wilkes-launch-streamsound-records/ |title=Gallimore, Wilkes Launch Streamsound Records : MusicRow – Nashville's Music Industry Publication – News, Songs From Music City |publisher=Musicrow.com |date=February 21, 2012 |accessdate=September 8, 2013}}</ref>

==Biography==
Dreyer debuted in 2012 with the singles "Guy's Girl" and "Confessions", both of which charted on [[Hot Country Songs]]. She also wrote "[[Home Alone Tonight]]" was recorded by country music artist [[Luke Bryan]] as a duet with [[Karen Fairchild]] of [[Little Big Town]] for his fifth studio album, ''[[Kill the Lights (Luke Bryan album)|Kill the Lights]]'' (2015). The song was performed on the [[American Music Awards]] and followed with a multi-week number 1 single.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.engine145.com/introducing-jaida-dreyer-my-story-is-a-blessing-in-a-blender/ |title=Introducing Jaida Dreyer: "My Story is a Blessing in a Blender" |publisher=Engine 145 |date=April 12, 2010 |accessdate=September 8, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141218104634/http://www.engine145.com/introducing-jaida-dreyer-my-story-is-a-blessing-in-a-blender/ |archive-date=December 18, 2014 |url-status=dead |df=mdy-all }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.songlyrics.com/carolyn-dawn-johnson/at-the-time-lyrics/ |title=Carolyn Dawn Johnson - At The Time Lyrics |publisher=Songlyrics.com |date= |accessdate=September 8, 2013}}</ref>

In early 2013, Deyer released her third single, "Half Broke Horses". Dreyer's debut album, ''I Am Jaida Dreyer'', was released on February 26, 2013.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.roughstock.com/blog/jaida-dreyer-i-am-jaida-dreyer-tracklist-and-cover-art |title=Jaida Dreyer "I Am Jaida Dreyer" Tracklist And Cover Art &#124; New Country Music, Listen to Songs & Video |publisher=Roughstock.com |accessdate=September 8, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131203031634/http://www.roughstock.com/blog/jaida-dreyer-i-am-jaida-dreyer-tracklist-and-cover-art |archive-date=December 3, 2013 |url-status=dead |df=mdy-all }}</ref> Dreyer wrote nine songs for the [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]]'s television musical drama, ''[[Nashville (2012 TV series)|Nashville]]''. "Dreams" was performed by [[Hayden Panettiere]]'s character Julliette Barnes<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.musicrow.com/2013/11/primetime-nashville-hanky-panky-woman|title=Primetime 'Nashville': "Hanky Panky Woman"|date=November 21, 2013|publisher=|accessdate=November 23, 2018}}</ref> and "This Town" performed by [[Clare Bowen]]'s character Scarlett O'Connor.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://abc.go.com/music-lounge/blogs/nashville/this-town|title=Watch Music Lounge TV Show - ABC.com|website=ABC|accessdate=November 23, 2018}}</ref>

She competed as a contestant on the [[USA Network]]'s ''Real Country'' show in 2018, and was crowned the winner of its inaugural season.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://tasteofcountry.com/real-country-winner-jaida-dreyer/|title=Jaida Dreyer Named Season 1 Winner of USA's 'Real Country'|author=Billy Dukes|date=December 12, 2018|publisher=Taste of Country|accessdate=April 20, 2019}}</ref>
