'''Caitlin Cary''' (born October 28, 1968) is an [[alternative country]] musician and visual artist from [[Seville, Ohio]].

==Early life==

Caitlin Cary is the youngest of seven siblings (all older brothers). Her entire family was involved in music to some degree, with her parents' love for singing and her father's interest in building instruments.<ref name=yeproc>{{cite web|last=Berick|first=Michael|title=Caitlin Cary|url=http://www.yeproc.com/artists/caitlin-cary|work=Yep Roc Records|accessdate=20 December 2013}}</ref> She had begun to play the violin at age five, but put it aside as a teenager.<ref name=popmattersbio>{{cite web|last=Dechert|first=Renee|title=Caitlin Cary|url=http://www.popmatters.com/feature/cary-caitlin-020514/|work=Pop Matters|accessdate=20 December 2013}}</ref> In addition to the violin, she also played her father's harpsichords, where she wrote some of her own songs.<ref name=yeproc />

Cary went to college at the [[College of Wooster]] in [[Ohio]].<ref name=yeproc /> She began working on a degree in English. During her college time, she picked up playing the violin again, and she formed a small 'jokey' band called Garden Weasels. After graduating from the College of Wooster, she enrolled in the graduate program in creative <!-- creative what? --> at [[North Carolina State]].<ref name=popmattersbio />

==Career==
===Whiskeytown===
In 1993, musician [[Ryan Adams]] contacted Cary and asked her if she would play violin in a band that he was starting. Cary agreed, and they formed [[Whiskeytown]].<ref name=popmattersbio />

===Solo career===
In 2000, Cary released her first solo EP ''Waltzie'', produced by [[Chris Stamey]].<ref name=CC-nodep-2000>{{cite news|last1=Blackstock|first1=Peter|title=Caitlin Cary – Waltzie (EP)|url=http://nodepression.com/album-review/caitlin-cary-waltzie-ep|accessdate=9 April 2017|work=No Depression|date=31 August 2000}}</ref>

Cary's debut album ''While You Weren't Looking'' was released in 2002 and featured Whiskeytown's [[Mike Daly]] (guitar), who co-wrote and played on most of the songs.<ref name=CC-billboard-2002>{{cite magazine|last1=uncredited|title=Whiskeytown's Caitlin Cary Records 'When You Weren't Looking'|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/76323/whiskeytowns-caitlin-cary-records-when-you-werent-looking|accessdate=10 April 2017|magazine=Billboard|date=28 March 2002}}</ref> Personnel also included Mike Santoro (bass), Skillet Gilmore (drums), and Jen Gunderman (keyboards). [[Thad Cockrell]], Tonya Lamm (Hazeldine), and Lynn Blakey provided harmonies.<ref name=popmattersbio />

2003's ''I'm Staying Out'' featured guest appearances from [[Mary Chapin Carpenter]], [[Mitch Easter]], [[Don Dixon (musician)|Don Dixon]], Greg Humphreys,  [[Audley Freed]], and [[Jane Scarpantoni]].<ref name=CC-nodep-2003>{{cite news|title=Album Review: Caitlin Cary – I'm Staying Out|url=http://nodepression.com/album-review/caitlin-cary-im-staying-out|accessdate=10 April 2017|work=No Depression |date=30 April 2003}}</ref>

===Other projects===
In 2005, Cary released an album of duets, ''Begonias'', with [[Thad Cockrell]] with songs composed by the duo.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2005/06/23/AR2005062300567.html|title=Caitlin Cary & Thad Cockrell "Begonias" Yep Roc|last=Himes|first=Geoffrey|date=June 24, 2005|newspaper=[[The Washington Post]]|accessdate=2008-11-14}}</ref>

In 2013, Cary co-founded the [http://ncmusiclovearmy.org/ North Carolina Music Love Army] with [[Jon Lindsay (musician)|Jon Lindsay]]. The collective of North Carolina–based musicians created the ''We Are Not For Sale: Songs of Protest'' LP to oppose the regressive actions of the [[North Carolina General Assembly]]. The album was released worldwide via [[Redeye Distribution|Redeye]] on November 26, 2013.<ref name=CC-blueindian-2014>{{cite news|last1=Pritchard|first1=Sean|title=NC Music Love Army – "Dear Mr. McCrory" featuring Jon Lindsay, Caitlin Cary, and BJ Barham|url=http://www.theblueindian.com/features/nc-army-dear-mr-mccrory/|accessdate=9 April 2017|work=The Blue Indian|date=3 September 2014}}</ref>

In 2010, Caitlin performed with Matt Douglas (lead singer and songster for The Proclivities) in Raleigh's annual Love Hangover show, in which male/female duos sing love song covers.<ref name=CC-slowcoustic-2011>{{cite news|last1=Altobelli|first1=Nicholas|title=Guest Post: Nicholas Altobelli interviews Caitlin Cary|url=http://slowcoustic.com/2011/12/22/guest-post-nicholas-altobelli-interviews-caitlin-cary/|accessdate=10 April 2017|work=Slowcoustic|date=22 December 2011}}</ref> They then formed the group Small Ponds, who released an EP on Last Chance Records in September 2010.<ref name=CC-indyweek-2010>{{cite news|last1=Haver Currin|first1=Grayson|title=The Small Pond's Caitlin Cary & Matt Douglas Are The Small Ponds|url=https://indyweek.com/music/features/small-pond-s-caitlin-cary-matt-douglas-small-ponds/|accessdate=24 November 2018|work=Indy Week|date=15 September 2010}}</ref>

===Visual arts===
Cary is an accomplished visual artist, creating fabric collages she calls "Needle Print." Examples of her work are prominently featured on her website.<ref name=CC-artspace-2017>{{cite web | url=http://artspacenc.org/portfolio-items/caitlin-cary/| title=Artist Profile: Caitlin Cary| work=ArtSpace NC| accessdate=10 April 2017| author=uncredited }}</ref>

==Personal life==
Cary is married to drummer/artist Skillet Gilmore, and they live in South [[Raleigh, North Carolina]].<ref name=CC-waltermag-2017>{{cite news|last1=Davis|first1=Tracy|title=Pictures of music: Skillet Gilmore and Caitlin Cary|url=http://www.waltermagazine.com/art_and_culture/pictures-of-music-skillet-gilmore-and-caitlin-cary/|accessdate=10 April 2017|work=Walter Magazine}}</ref>
