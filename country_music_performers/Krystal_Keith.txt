'''Krystal Ladawn Covel Sandubrae''', known professionally as '''Krystal Keith''' (born September 30, 1985) is an American [[country music]] singer. 

==Early life==
Keith was born on September 30, 1985, to fellow country singer [[Toby Keith]]<ref>{{cite web|last=Geller |first=Wendy |url=http://music.yahoo.com/blogs/our-country/yes-she-toby-daughter-krystal-keith-stands-her-180435013.html |title=Yes, She's Toby's Daughter! But Krystal Keith Stands Out on Her Own Merit &#124; Our Country ‚Äì Yahoo Music |publisher=Music.yahoo.com |date=April 30, 2013 |access-date=December 10, 2013}}</ref> and Tricia Covel (n√©e Lucus). She has one younger brother, Stelen Keith Covel,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tasteofcountry.com/toby-keith-son-stelen-football/ |title=Toby Keith's Son, Stelen Keith Covel, Makes Youth All-American Bowl |publisher=Tasteofcountry.com |date=November 7, 2012 |access-date=December 10, 2013}}</ref> and one maternal half-sister, *[https://judgenews.net/shelley-covel-rowland/ Shelley Covel Rowland], who was adopted by Toby upon his marriage to their mother.

==Career==
Keith's first chart entry was in 2004, when she sang duet vocals with her father on a [[Cover (music)|cover]] of [[Inez and Charlie Foxx]]'s [[Mockingbird (Inez & Charlie Foxx song)|"Mockingbird"]] which appeared on his 2004 album ''[[Greatest Hits 2 (Toby Keith album)|Greatest Hits 2]]''. The two also performed the song together at the 2004 [[Country Music Association]] awards.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://tasteofcountry.com/krystal-keith-interview-toby-new-songs/ |title=Krystal Keith Interview: Toby's Daughter Talks New Songs, Shania and Life as a Oklahoma Redneck |publisher=Tasteofcountry.com |date=April 19, 2013 |access-date=December 10, 2013}}</ref> After this song was released, Keith attended college at her father's insistence. She graduated from the [[University of Oklahoma]].<ref name="gac">{{cite web|last=Addison |first=Daryl |url=http://blog.gactv.com/blog/2013/04/19/gac-album-review-krystal-keiths-self-titled-ep/ |title=GAC Album Review: Krystal Keith's Self-Titled EP &#124; GAC News & Notes |publisher=Blog.gactv.com |date=April 19, 2013 |access-date=December 10, 2013}}</ref>

In mid-2013, Keith signed to [[Show Dog-Universal Music]]. She released a four-song [[extended play]], which includes her debut single "Daddy Dance with Me". She wrote the song as a surprise to her father and performed it for the first time on the day of her wedding.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theboot.com/2013/04/29/krystal-keith-father-toby-keith/ |title=Krystal Keith's Childhood Not Affected by Father's Fame |publisher=Theboot.com |date=April 29, 2013 |access-date=December 10, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Wyland |first=Sarah |url=http://blog.gactv.com/blog/2013/03/21/krystal-keith-to-release-self-titled-ep-april-16/ |title=Krystal Keith To Release Self-Titled EP April 16 &#124; GAC News & Notes |publisher=Blog.gactv.com |date=March 21, 2013 |access-date=December 10, 2013}}</ref> The song is included on her debut album ''Whiskey & Lace'', produced by her father and [[Mark Wright (record producer)|Mark Wright]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cmt.com/artists/krystal-keith/biography |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130806054414/http://www.cmt.com/artists/krystal-keith/biography/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=August 6, 2013 |title=Krystal Keith Bio |publisher=CMT |access-date=December 10, 2013}}</ref> The EP received a positive review from Daryl Addison of [[Great American Country]], who called her a "confident young singer with a strong voice".<ref name="gac"/> The second single from ''Whiskey & Lace'', "Get Your Redneck On", was released to country radio on September 30, 2013.
