'''Tyler Dickerson''' (born 1993 in [[Hattiesburg, Mississippi]]) is an American [[country music]] artist signed to [[Lyric Street Records]]. He released his debut [[single (music)|single]], "Tell Your Sister I'm Single," to radio in early 2010.

==Biography==
Tyler Dickerson was born in [[Hattiesburg, Mississippi]] to Steven and Crystal Dickerson. He began performing at an early age, and by eleven he had started performing at venues in [[Florida]]. In 2005, he won Florida Male Vocalist and Male Entertainer of the Year awards for his age group.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=888&dat=20051109&id=TrsNAAAAIBAJ&sjid=hnIDAAAAIBAJ&pg=4016,4609483|title=A real crowd-pleaser: Eleven-year-old Tyler Dickerson is on his way to becoming a star in the country music industry|last=Miller|first=Michelle|date=9 November 2005|work=Pasco Times|pages=3|access-date=3 March 2010}}</ref> He had also become a regular performer at [[Tootsie's Orchid Lounge]] in [[Nashville, Tennessee]].<ref name="cst">{{cite web|url=http://www.countrystandardtime.com/news/newsitem2.asp?xid=3792|title=Tyler Dickerson releases debut single|date=14 February 2010|work=[[Country Standard Time]]|access-date=3 March 2010}}</ref>

Dickerson signed to a recording contract with [[Lyric Street Records]] in late 2009 after entering a talent search associated with [[John Rich]] of [[Big & Rich]],<ref name="cst"/> who also mentored him.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://communities.canada.com/REGINALEADERPOST/blogs/ploughboymansion/archive/2010/03/01/tyler-dickerson-s-career-is-on-the-move.aspx|title=Tyler Dickerson's career is on the move|last=Dedekker|first=Jeff|date=1 March 2010|work=The Leader-Post|access-date=3 March 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://archive.today/20120714091748/http://communities.canada.com/REGINALEADERPOST/blogs/ploughboymansion/archive/2010/03/01/tyler-dickerson-s-career-is-on-the-move.aspx|archive-date=14 July 2012}}</ref> He released his first [[single (music)|single]], "Tell Your Sister I'm Single," in February 2010.<ref name="cst"/> The song is written by Amanda Williams, Trevor Rosen, and Isaac Rich (brother of John Rich).  Dickerson also signed on as an opening act for [[Brooks & Dunn]]'s Last Rodeo Tour.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theboot.com/2010/02/26/tyler-dickerson-tour/|title=Tyler Dickerson to Join Brooks & Dunn's Last Rodeo Tour|last=Conaway|first=Alanna|date=26 February 2010|work=The Boot|access-date=3 March 2010}}</ref> Bobby Peacock of Roughstock gave the song a positive review, saying that Dickerson "clearly plays the premise for laughs."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.roughstock.com/blog/tyler-dickerson-tell-your-sister-i-m-single-|title=Tyler Dickerson — "Tell Your Sister I'm Single"|last=Peacock|first=Bobby|date=29 January 2010|work=Roughstock|access-date=3 March 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100202160351/http://www.roughstock.com/blog/tyler-dickerson-tell-your-sister-i-m-single-|archive-date=2 February 2010}}</ref> "Tell Your Sister I'm Single" debuted at number 51 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs]] charts dated for the week ending March 13, 2010.

Dickerson now sings in the Louisiana-based band Rockin Rouge.

Dickerson auditioned for ''[[The Voice (U.S. season 9)|The Voice]]'' on September 28, 2015. He performed "[[Hard to Handle (song)|Hard to Handle]]," and was chosen by [[Blake Shelton]] (the only one who turned his chair) to be on his team. He was eliminated from the show in the top 48, after losing to Zach Seabaugh on a performance of [[Travis Tritt]]'s "[[I'm Gonna Be Somebody]]."
