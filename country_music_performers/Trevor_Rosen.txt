'''Trevor Joseph Rosen''' (born February 18, 1975) is an American [[country music]] songwriter and musician. He is also a member of the American country music band [[Old Dominion (band)|Old Dominion]], in which he plays guitar and keyboards.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://olddominionband.com/about/| title= About Old Dominion | publisher=Old Dominion | date=2015 | accessdate=October 7, 2015 }}</ref>

==Early life==
Trevor Rosen grew up in [[Woodhaven, Michigan]],<ref>{{cite web|author= |url=http://tasteofcountry.com/old-dominion-new-ep-writers-regret/ |title=Old Dominion Talk Swagger, Their New EP and Writers' Regret |website=Tasteofcountry.com |date= 3 September 2014|accessdate=2016-06-16}}</ref> a southern suburb of Detroit. He attended high school at Gabriel Richard Catholic High School. He learned piano at a young age before picking up the guitar at age 16 and forming the Detroit area rock trio Unfair Superpowers.

==Career==
Rosen moved to Nashville in the fall of 2003 to pursue a career in songwriting. He met [[Matthew Ramsey (songwriter)|Matthew Ramsey]] soon after arriving and the two began collaborating, eventually becoming founding members of the hit supergroup, [[Old Dominion (band)|Old Dominion]]. Rosen signedwith SMACKtown Music and Wrensong/Reynsong Publishing in 2013.<ref>{{cite web | url= http://www.musicrow.com/2013/11/rosen-re-signs-publishing-deal/| title=Rosen Re-Signs Publishing Deal | publisher=Music Row | date=November 4, 2013 | accessdate=October 5, 2015 }}</ref> He is currently signed to a publishing deal with Twelve6 Entertainment.[https://musicrow.com/2019/08/trevor-rosen-signs-with-twelve6-entertainment/].

The Nashville Association of Independent Publishers named Rosen and the other members of [[Old Dominion (band)|Old Dominion]] the 2016 Songwriter/Artist of the Year, the same year they were honored as the Music Row Best New Artist and the Academy of Country Music's Best New Duo/Group of the Year. He co-wrote 8 of the 11 songs on their debut album [[Meat and Candy]], including the Number One hits:  Break Up With Him, [[Snapback (song)|Snapback]] and [[Song For Another Time]]. In the year following, Rosen co-wrote 9 of the 12 songs for Old Dominion's second studio album, ''[[Happy Endings (Old Dominion album)|Happy Endings]]'', including the Number One hits, "[[No Such Thing as a Broken Heart]]" and "[[Written in the Sand (song)|Written in the Sand]].".<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.smacksongs.com/trevor-rosen-1| title= Trevor Rosen | publisher= SMACKSongs | date=January 26, 2017 | accessdate=January 27, 2017 }}</ref> In 2019, Rosen and Matt Ramsey were once again named the Songwriter/Artists of the Year. Rosen wrote 10 of the 11 songs on Old Dominion's latest self-titled album, [[Old Dominion (album)]], two of which went on to become Number One hit songs, [[Make It Sweet]] and [[One Man Band (Old Dominion song)]]. These along with Michael Ray's number one hit song, [[One That Got Away|The One That Got Away]], which Rosen also co-wrote, earned him the CMA Triple Play Award for having 3 number ones within a 12-month period [https://musicrow.com/2019/01/cma-reveals-triple-play-awards-recipients/].

Rosen's songwriting accolades expand well beyond [[Old Dominion (band)|Old Dominion]]’s success, as he has written multiple number one songs including [[Dierks Bentley]]’s"[[Say You Do (Dierks Bentley song)|Say You Do]]",<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.cmt.com/news/1755530/dierks-bentley-celebrates-no-1-success-of-say-you-do-with-songs-writers/| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150620051243/http://www.cmt.com/news/1755530/dierks-bentley-celebrates-no-1-success-of-say-you-do-with-songs-writers/| url-status=dead| archive-date=June 20, 2015| title= Dierks Bentley Celebrates No. 1 Success With Song's Writers | publisher= CMT | date=June 17, 2015 | accessdate=October 7, 2015 }}</ref> [[The Band Perry]]'s [[Better Dig Two]],<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/1532233/the-band-perry-dig-up-another-no-1-on-country-songs-chart/| title=The Band Perry Dig Up Another No. 1 on Country Songs Chart | magazine=Billboard | date=January 24, 2013 | accessdate=October 7, 2015 }}</ref> [[Blake Shelton]]’s [[Sangria (song)|Sangria]]<ref>{{cite web | url=http://news.iheart.com/articles/entertainment-news-104651/blake-sheltons-sangria-reaches-top-of-13737490/| title= Blake Shelton's 'Sangria' Reaches Top of Charts | publisher= iHeart Radio | date=July 6, 2015 | accessdate= October 7, 2015 }}</ref> and the longest charting number one song in Country Aircheck history, [[William Michael Morgan]]’s [[I Met a Girl (William Michael Morgan song)|I Met A Girl]]. He has also written songs recorded by [[Jake Owen]], [[Scotty McCreery]], [[Keith Urban]], [[Craig Morgan]], [[Dustin Lynch]], [[Randy Houser]], and [[Randy Rogers Band]] as well as songs performed on the ABC show ''[[Nashville (2012 TV series)|Nashville]]''.
