'''Logan Mize''' is a [[country music]] artist/songwriter from [[Clearwater, Kansas]] signed to Big Yellow Dog Music.

==Music career==
In April 2010, several years after moving to Nashville, Mize signed a publishing/record deal with Big Yellow Dog Music and a booking deal with [[William Morris Endeavor]] followed in August 2010.<ref>[http://www.roughstock.com/reviews/album-review-logan-mize-nobody-in-nashville Album Review: Logan Mize - Nobody In Nashville | Country Music Reviews, Taylor Swift to Lady Antebellum | Roughstock.com<!-- Bot generated title -->] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120320155555/http://www.roughstock.com/reviews/album-review-logan-mize-nobody-in-nashville |date=2012-03-20 }}</ref> Mize has received air play as a "Highway Find" on satellite radio channel [[The Highway (Sirius XM)]]. Logan has also experienced success as a songwriter, notably when country music singer [[Bucky Covington]] recorded his song “Mexicoma” on his album ''[[Good Guys (album)|Good Guys]]'', which charted to No. 30 on [[Billboard Country Albums]] and No.43 on [[Independent Albums|Billboard Independent Albums]].<ref>[http://www.theboot.com/2012/09/12/bucky-covington-interview-good-guys-album/ Bucky Covington Hopes 'Good Guys' Finish First - The Boot<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> Logan's TV features include the “Fabric of Life” Cotton commercial alongside [[Hayden Panettiere]] and in the [[The CW|CW]] series, “[[Hart of Dixie]]", which featured both Logan and his band.<ref>[http://www.dailyjournal.net/view/local_story/Q-A-Meet-the-artist-LOGAN-Mize_1386209415/ Q&A Meet the Artist Logan Mize<!-- Bot generated title -->] {{webarchive|url=https://archive.today/20140228175922/http://www.dailyjournal.net/view/local_story/Q-A-Meet-the-artist-LOGAN-Mize_1386209415/ |date=2014-02-28 }}</ref>

In 2012, Mize's second album, ''Nobody in Nashville'' (released on Big Yellow Dog Music), charted to No. 49 on [[Billboard Country Albums]] and No. 15 on [[Billboard Heatseekers|Billboard Heatseekers Albums]].<ref>[http://www.billboard.biz/bbbiz/others/taylor-swift-scores-sixth-no-1-country-song-1006548752.story#0X3w8BTPrsgkTtHT.99 Taylor Swift Scores Sixth No. 1 Country Song With 'Ours' | Billboard<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> Mize has opened for multiple headlining acts including [[Lady Antebellum]], [[The Band Perry]], [[Leann Rimes]], [[Eric Church]], [[Dierks Bentley]], [[Charlie Daniels Band]], [[Blake Shelton]] (on the Blake Shelton Country Cruise), [[Stoney LaRue]], [[Hank Williams]] and a special tribute with [[Merle Haggard]]. In the fall of 2013, Logan accompanied [[Leann Rimes]] on her UK tour in [[Glasgow]], [[London]], [[Birmingham]], and [[Manchester]].<ref>[http://www.ukcountrymusic.net/2013/10/logan-mize-used-single-review/ Logan Mize Used Up Single Review<!-- Bot generated title -->] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150518091157/http://www.ukcountrymusic.net/2013/10/logan-mize-used-single-review/ |date=2015-05-18 }}</ref>

On July 28, 2017, Mize released another album, ''Come Back Road''. A single from the album "Ain't Always Pretty" was first released in 2016  and received over 20 million streams on [[Spotify]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/country/premieres/logan-mizes-come-back-road-hear-new-song-w494123 |title=Hear Logan Mize's Driving New Song 'Come Back Road' |first= Jon|last= Freeman|date=July 26, 2017 |work=Rolling Stone}}</ref> He released his 2021 concept album ''Welcome to Prairieville'' with Blake Chaffin and his wife Jill Martin.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-10-03|title=Logan Mize Welcomes You To Prairieville with His New Album|url=https://www.newcountry963.com/2021/10/03/logan-mize-welcomes-you-to-prairieville-with-his-new-album/|access-date=2021-10-06|website=New Country 96.3|language=en-US}}</ref>

Mize has over 350 million catalog streams across all platforms. His single, Better Off Gone, is RIAA Certified Gold and has over 100 million streams.

== Family Relations ==
His great uncle was Billy Mize, largely known for crafting and evolving the legendary Bakersfield sound, which included musical pioneers Merle Haggard and Buck Owens.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Logan Mize |url=http://www.bigyellowdogmusic.com/logan-mize |access-date=2024-05-25 |website=BIG YELLOW DOG |language=en-US}}</ref>
