'''Ladies of the Canyon''' is a Canadian band from [[Montreal]], [[Quebec]] composed of Tara Martin, Maia Davies, Jasmine Bleile and Anna Ruddick. Their sound encompasses a melding of influences, such as roots, rock, country, and Americana.  The group has released two albums.

==History==
Founded in 2005, Ladies of the Canyon began their career playing small venues in Montreal’s popular Mile-End neighbourhood. At the time their music consisted mainly of "countrified" 1970s Californian soft-rock sound;<ref>[http://exclaim.ca/music/article/day_three_jeff_tweedy_jenny_lewis_july_talk_ladies_of_canyon-fort_york_toronto_on_july_6 "Day Three: Jeff Tweedy, Jenny Lewis, July Talk, Ladies of the Canyon Fort York, Toronto ON, July 6"]. ''Exclaim!'', By Stuart Henderson Jul 07, 2014</ref> over time their musical style expanded to include classic folk and contemporary rock.<ref>[http://www.indyish.com/review-david-martel-cd-release-cabaret-jpr/ "Review: David Martel CD Release @ Cabaret JPR"]. ''Indyish'', James Finnerty, June 10th, 2008</ref>

The group released their first studio album, ''[[Haunted Woman]]'', in 2010 through Kindling/[[Warner Music Canada]].<ref>[http://exclaim.ca/music/article/ladies_of_canyon-haunted_woman "Ladies of the Canyon Haunted Woman"]. ''Exclaim!'', By Jason Schneider Jun 28, 2010</ref><ref>{{usurped|1=[https://web.archive.org/web/20171030003607/http://www.chartattack.com/news/2010/06/01/ladies-of-the-canyon-bring-country-to-montreal/ "Ladies Of The Canyon Bring Country To Montreal"]}}. ''Chart Attack'' - Jun 1, 2010</ref> In the three years following this release, Ladies of the Canyon appeared on stage with  [[Broken Social Scene]] and [[The Dears]], were nominated for a [[Canadian Country Music Association]] award. The band worked with producer Mark Howard to record their second album, ''Diamond Heart''.<ref>[http://thechronicleherald.ca/artslife/1161949-halifax-pop-explosion-where-canyon-country-explodes "Halifax Pop Explosion: Where Canyon country explodes"]. ''Chronicle Herald'', By Stephen Cooke October 21, 2013</ref>  In 2012, during the recording of this album, Sargeant was replaced by drummer Tara Martin.<ref>[http://www.femmemetalwebzine.net/2013/09/25/ladies-of-the-canyon-diamond-heart-2013/ "Ladies of the Canyon – “Diamond Heart” (2013)"]. ''Femme Metal'', September 25, 2013</ref>  ''Diamond Heart'' was released in September, 2013.

In 2014 the Ladies of the Canyon toured with the [[Barenaked Ladies]].<ref>[https://www.thesnipenews.com/music/interviews/ladies-of-the-canyon/ "Ladies of the Canyon"]. ''The Snipe News'', January 21, 2014</ref>

==Solo careers==
Maia Davies has launched a solo career by releasing her debut [[French language]] album, ''Héritage'', using her [[Mononymous person|mononym]] Maia (now known as Maia Lily, stylized as Maïa Lily).<ref>[http://journalmetro.com/culture/160805/lheritage-personnel-de-maia/ "L'héritage personnel de Maïa"] . Montreal ''Métro'':  23 September 2012 Camille Laurin-Desjardins</ref> Jasmine Bleile released her first solo album under the name Satellītes on November 17, 2018.<ref>[https://satellitesofmontreal.com Satellītes website]</ref> Senja Sargeant released her selftitled [[Extended play|EP]], produced by [[Shane Alexander (musician)|Shane Alexander]], in 2022 and celebrated the event with a release party in the venue Bitterzoet in [[Amsterdam]] where Shane played along in her band.<ref>[https://www.discogs.com/release/25226569-Senja-Sargeant-Senja-Sargeant Discogs - Senja Sargeant – Senja Sargeant]</ref><ref>[https://www.setlist.fm/setlist/senja-sargeant/2022/bitterzoet-amsterdam-netherlands-23b054c3.html Setlist.fm - Senja Sargeant in Bitterzoet, Amsterdam]</ref>
