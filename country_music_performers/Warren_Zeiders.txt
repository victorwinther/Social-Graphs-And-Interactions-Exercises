'''Warren Zeiders''' is an American [[country music]] singer and songwriter. He has released two studio albums under [[Warner Records]], [[Pretty Little Poison (album)|''Pretty Little Poison'']] (2023) and ''Relapse'' (2024). He has achieved one number one hit at [[country radio]], being the [[Pretty Little Poison|title track from his debut album]].

==Career==
Zeiders studied business at [[Frostburg State University]], where he also recorded and posted covers on TikTok.<ref name="NTenn">{{cite web |last1=Leimkuehler |first1=Matthew |title=Rising country singer Warren Zeiders makes his Nashville debut |url=https://www.tennessean.com/story/life/2022/04/25/warren-zeiders-viral-country-singer-tiktok-headlines-his-first-nashville-show/7399050001/ |website=Nashville Tennessean}}</ref> He began co-writing and singing original songs, including "[[Ride the Lightning (717 Tapes)|Ride the Lightning]]", which gained over 500 million views on TikTok.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://musicrow.com/2021/10/warren-zeiders-leverages-tiktok-stardom-releases-debut-ep-717-tapes/|title=Warren Zeiders Leverages TikTok Stardom, Releases Debut EP, '717 Tapes'|work=Music Row|last=Farthing|first=Lydia|date=October 22, 2021|access-date=November 7, 2021}}</ref>

=== 2021–2022: ''717 Tapes'' ===
In 2021, Zeiders signed with [[Warner Records]]<ref name="NTenn" /> and re-released "Ride the Lightning", which debuted at number 30 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs]] chart.<ref name="country">{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/country-songs/2021-07-10|title=Hot Country Songs: July 10, 2021|magazine=Billboard|access-date=November 7, 2021|url-access=subscription}}</ref> The song was certified platinum in both the United States<ref>{{cite web |title=Warren Zeiders - Ride the Lightning |url=https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&ar=Warren+Zeiders&ti=Ride+the+Lightning+-+717+Tapes&format=Single&type=#search_section |website=RIAA}}</ref> and Canada.<ref name="MCan">{{cite web |date=16 March 2023 |title=Gold Platinum |url=https://musiccanada.com/gold-platinum/?_gp_search=pretty%20little%20poison |website=Music Canada}}</ref> In 2021 and 2022, he released two EP's titled ''717 Tapes'' and ''717 Tapes, Vol II'', which were eventually combined into his first album release, ''717 Tapes: The Album''.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Reel |first=Chris |date=2022-10-04 |title=Warren Zeiders Releases Debut Album, '717 Tapes: The Album' |url=https://www.raisedrowdy.com/2022/10/04/warren-zeiders-releases-debut-album-717-tapes-the-album/ |access-date=2024-08-28 |language=en-CA}}</ref>

=== 2023–present: ''Pretty Little Poison'' and ''Relapse'' ===
"[[Pretty Little Poison]]" was released as Zeiders' country radio debut single on July 10, 2023, and became his first number one hit in March 2024.<ref name="zeiders">{{cite magazine |date=February 23, 2024 |title=Warren Zeiders Notches His First Country Airplay No. 1 With 'Pretty Little Poison': 'Lord Willing, It Ain't My Last' |last=Asker |first=Jim |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/chart-beat/warren-zeiders-pretty-little-poison-number-1-country-airplay-chart-1235613871/ |magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=March 12, 2024 }}</ref> His [[Pretty Little Poison (album)|debut album of the same name]] was released on August 10, 2023.

On November 17, 2023, Zeiders released "[[Sin So Sweet]]" as a single that would later be included on the deluxe edition of ''Pretty Little Poison''.<ref>{{Cite web |last=https://plus.google.com/110686239645171179005 |title=Warren Zeiders Earns First Top 20 Country Radio Single + Shares New Track, "Sin So Sweet" |url=https://shorefire.com/releases/entry/warren-zeiders-earns-first-top-20-country-radio-single-shares-new-track-sin-so-sweet |access-date=2024-08-28 |website=Shore Fire Media |language=en}}</ref> "Heartbreaker", the second single from the deluxe edition of ''Pretty Little Poison'', was released on February 2, 2024.<ref>{{Cite web |last=https://plus.google.com/110686239645171179005 |title=Warren Zeiders Kicks Off 2024 With “Heartbreaker” |url=https://shorefire.com/releases/entry/warren-zeiders-kicks-off-2024-with-heartbreaker |access-date=2024-08-28 |website=Shore Fire Media |language=en}}</ref> It peaked at 41 on the US Country Songs Billboard chart. ''Pretty Little Poison (Deluxe Edition)'' was released two weeks later on February 16. 2024.<ref>{{Citation |title=Pretty Little Poison (Deluxe) by Warren Zeiders on Apple Music |date=2023-08-10 |url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/pretty-little-poison-deluxe/1730012497 |access-date=2024-08-28 |language=en-US}}</ref>

On May 17, 2024, Zeiders released "Betrayal", the lead single from his upcoming second studio album.<ref>{{Cite web |last=White |first=Logan |date=2024-05-17 |title=Warren Zeiders seeks revenge on new single "Betrayal" |url=https://substreammagazine.com/2024/05/warren-zeiders-seeks-revenge-on-new-single-betrayal/ |access-date=2024-08-28 |website=Substream Magazine |language=en-US}}</ref> An a cappella version and an instrumental version were included with the release. On June 7, 2024, Zeiders released "Relapse", along with an a cappella and instrumental version, as the second single from his upcoming second studio album.{{citation needed|date=August 2024}} On July 12, 2024, Zeiders announced his second studio album, ''Relapse'', releasing on August 23, 2024.<ref>{{Cite web |last=https://plus.google.com/110686239645171179005 |title=Warren Zeiders Announces New Project Relapse, Out August 23 Via Warner Records |url=https://shorefire.com/releases/entry/warren-zeiders-new-project-relapse-out-august-23-via-warner-records |access-date=2024-08-28 |website=Shore Fire Media |language=en}}</ref> Zeiders was invited to record a song for [[Twisters: The Album]], and his song "The Cards I've Been Dealt" was released on July 19, 2024.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Chua |first=Jeremy |title=‘Twisters’ soundtrack has landed: Hear new songs from your favorite country stars |url=https://southernillinoisnow.com/2024/07/19/twisters-soundtrack-has-landed-hear-new-songs-from-your-favorite-country-stars/ |access-date=2024-08-28 |website=South Central Illinois' News, Sports and Weather Station |language=en}}</ref> Two more singles were released from ''Relapse'', including "Addictions," and "Intoxicated."<ref>{{Cite web |last=Hubbard |first=Stephen |title=Warren Zeiders readies for ‘Relapse’ with "Addictions" |url=https://southernillinoisnow.com/2024/07/12/warren-zeiders-readies-for-relapse-with-addictions/ |access-date=2024-08-28 |website=South Central Illinois' News, Sports and Weather Station |language=en}}</ref> ''Relapse'' was officially released on August 23, 2024.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Daykin |first=James |date=2024-08-23 |title=Warren Zeiders' new album 'Relapse' Showcases Heartfelt Storytelling |url=https://entertainment-focus.com/2024/08/23/warren-zeiders-new-album-relapse-showcases-heartfelt-storytelling/ |access-date=2024-08-28 |website=Entertainment Focus |language=en-US}}</ref>
