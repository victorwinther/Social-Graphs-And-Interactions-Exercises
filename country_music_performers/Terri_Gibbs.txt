'''Teresa Fay Gibbs''' (born June 15, 1954)<ref name="LarkinCountry">{{cite book|title=[[Encyclopedia of Popular Music|The Guinness Who's Who of Country Music]]|editor=[[Colin Larkin (writer)|Colin Larkin]]|publisher=[[Guinness Publishing]]|date=1993|edition=First|isbn=0-85112-726-6|page=158}}</ref> is an American [[country music]] artist. Between 1980 and 2017, she recorded eleven [[studio album]]s, including four for [[MCA Records]] and one for [[Warner Bros. Records]]. She also charted 13 [[single (music)|singles]] on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' country singles charts in that timespan, including her debut single, "[[Somebody's Knockin']]", which reached No.&nbsp;8 on the country charts, No.&nbsp;13 on the pop charts and No.&nbsp;3 on the Adult Contemporary charts. She also entered the country top 20 with "Rich Man", "Mis'ry River", "Ashes to Ashes" and "Anybody Else's Heart but Mine." Gibbs has been blind since infancy.

==Biography==
Gibbs was born in [[Miami]], [[Florida]], United States, but raised in the [[Augusta, Georgia]], suburb of [[Grovetown, Georgia|Grovetown]].<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p10647/biography|pure_url=yes}}|title=Terri Gibbs biography|last=Huey|first=Steve|work=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=2009-08-09}}</ref> Although initially having eyesight, she had been born premature and subsequently lost her eyesight in an incubator accident.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://onlineathens.com/stories/032302/ent_0323020007.shtml |title=Terri Gibbs takes the stage in Athens this weekend &#124; Online Athens |access-date=2016-03-23 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160404030818/http://onlineathens.com/stories/032302/ent_0323020007.shtml |archive-date=2016-04-04 |url-status=dead }}</ref> In her own words, "I was quite small (2 lbs., 11 ounces) and, so, they had to put me in an incubator. At that point in time, they did not know to cover the eyes and my retinas were damaged." At six months of age, Gibbs was diagnosed with retrolental fibroplasia and declared blind.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ameridisability.com/post/singer-terri-gibbs-always-had-a-clear-vision-for-music-despite-blindness|title=Singer Terri Gibbs Always Had a Clear Vision for Music Despite Blindness|website=Ameridisability.com|date=5 January 2018 |access-date=7 August 2021}}</ref> She learned to play [[piano]] at age three.<ref name="allmusic"/> As a child, she sang in the church [[choir]] and at talent contests, and she opened for [[Bill Anderson (singer)|Bill Anderson]] at the age of 17.<ref name="miller">{{cite book|last=Miller|first=Zell|title=They Heard Georgia Singing|publisher=Mercer University Press|year=1996|isbn=0-86554-504-9|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=7kv8EdRRkiQC&q=%22terri+gibbs&pg=PA116|pages=117–118|access-date=2009-08-09}}</ref> Her parents wanted her to be treated no differently from sighted people, so she was sent to public school and graduated from [[Butler High School (Augusta, Georgia)|Butler High School]] in Augusta in 1972.<ref>Don Rhodes. "Groveton Singer Determined to Win a Place in Music."  Augusta (GA) Chronicle, December 15, 1974, p. F5.</ref> She performed in and around the Augusta area and eventually, she met [[Chet Atkins]], who advised her to move to [[Nashville, Tennessee]], to pursue a country music career, which she did at age eighteen.<ref name="allmusic"/> After failing to find a record deal, she moved to Miami and joined a band called Sound Dimension.<ref name="allmusic"/> She continued to perform locally, later forming a band called the Terri Gibbs Trio, which performed at a [[Steak and Ale]] in [[Augusta, Georgia]].<ref name="miller"/> Gibbs then sent a [[demo (music)|demo]] tape to [[record producer]] Ed Penney of [[MCA Records]], signing to the label in 1980.<ref name="allmusic"/>  Penney was a former Boston disc jockey and a long-time songwriter. He liked her voice on her demo, but he felt she needed stronger material. He co-wrote "Somebody's Knockin'" for her and also produced the song.<ref>Nancy Bishop. "Terri Gibbs: At Last Fame Is Knockin' at Her Door."  Dallas Morning News,  November 23, 1981, p. C8.</ref>

==Musical career==
Gibbs' first single release was "[[Somebody's Knockin']]", which was also the title of her 1981 debut album.  This song was a crossover hit upon its 1980 release, reaching No.&nbsp;8 on the U.S. country charts, No.&nbsp;13 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]] and No.&nbsp;3 on the Adult Contemporary charts.<ref name="charts">{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p10647/charts-awards/billboard-singles|pure_url=yes}}|title=Chart history for Terri Gibbs|work=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=2009-08-09}}</ref>  It was followed by "Rich Man", a No.&nbsp;19 country hit which also entered the pop charts.<ref name="charts"/>  Her debut album won her the [[Academy of Country Music]]'s Top New Female Vocalist award.<ref name="allmusic"/>  She was also the first winner of the [[Country Music Association]]'s Horizon Award (which is awarded to emerging artists),<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cmt.com/microsites/cma/archives/1981.jhtml|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081021041954/http://www.cmt.com/microsites/cma/archives/1981.jhtml|url-status=dead|archive-date=October 21, 2008|title=CMA archives|work=[[Country Music Television|CMT]]|access-date=2009-08-09}}</ref> and was nominated for a [[Grammy Award for Best Country Song]] and [[Best Female Country Vocal Performance]] for "Somebody's Knockin'."<ref name="miller"/> Gibbs performed on the [[Grand Ole Opry]] as well.<ref name="miller"/>

MCA released her second album, ''I'm a Lady'', later in 1981.  This album was less successful,<ref name="allmusic"/> although it produced a No.&nbsp;12 hit in "Mis'ry River,"<ref name="charts"/> its second and final single. 1982's ''Some Days It Rains All Night Long'' produced a Top 20 hit in "Ashes to Ashes" and a No.&nbsp;45 in its title track.<ref name="whitburn">{{cite book|last=Whitburn|first=Joel|title=Hot Country Songs 1944 to 2008|publisher=Record Research, Inc|page=157|year=2008|isbn=978-0-89820-177-2}}</ref>  Also in 1981 and 1982, Gibbs toured with [[George Jones]] and sang duets with him onstage.<ref name="allmusic"/>  Her final MCA album, ''Over Easy'', also produced a minor hit in "Anybody Else's Heart but Mine", and after releasing a compilation called ''The Best of Terri Gibbs'', she left the label.<ref name="kingsbury">{{cite book|last=Kingsbury|first=Paul|title=The Encyclopedia of Country Music|publisher=Sourcebooks, Inc.|year=2004|isbn=0-19-517608-1|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=v4GQDYx_RnkC&q=%22terri+gibbs%22&pg=PA199|pages=198–199|access-date=2009-08-09}}</ref>

Gibbs switched to [[Warner Bros. Records]] for her fifth album, 1985's ''Old Friends''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://pqasb.pqarchiver.com/chicagotribune/access/25065555.html?dids=25065555:25065555&FMT=ABS&FMTS=ABS:FT&type=current&date=Apr+14%2C+1985&author=Jack+Hurst&pub=Chicago+Tribune+%28pre-1997+Fulltext%29&desc=OLD+FRIENDS+TERRI+GIBBS+%28WARNER+BROS.%29&pqatl=google|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121021214842/http://pqasb.pqarchiver.com/chicagotribune/access/25065555.html?dids=25065555:25065555&FMT=ABS&FMTS=ABS:FT&type=current&date=Apr+14,+1985&author=Jack+Hurst&pub=Chicago+Tribune+(pre-1997+Fulltext)&desc=OLD+FRIENDS+TERRI+GIBBS+(WARNER+BROS.)&pqatl=google|url-status=dead|archive-date=October 21, 2012|title=Terri Gibbs - ''Old Friends''|last=Hurst|first=Jack|date=1985-04-14|work=[[The Chicago Tribune]]|access-date=2009-08-09}}</ref>  All three of this album's singles — "A Few Good Men" (which featured [[Kathy Mattea]] on [[backing vocalist|backing vocals]]<ref name="whitburn"/>) "Rockin' in a Brand New Cradle" and "Someone Must Be Missing You Tonight" — missed the country Top 40.<ref name="whitburn"/>

Gibbs later shifted her focus to [[Contemporary Christian music]], recording ''Turn Around'' and ''Comfort the People'' in 1987 and 1988, respectively, on Canaan Records.<ref name="kingsbury"/>  The former gave Gibbs her second Grammy nomination.<ref name="kingsbury"/> ''What a Great Day'', her third Christian album, followed in 1990 on the Morning Gate label.<ref name="kingsbury"/>  In 2002, Terri released the album, ''No Doubt About It,'' followed by the 2010 release of ''Your Grace Still Amazes Me.'' In 2014, ''The Best Of Terri Gibbs,'' featuring 10 of her best loved Country hits.  In June 2017, Terri released her sixth Christian album, ''Sum It All Up.''

== Personal life ==
Gibbs married [[Grovetown, Georgia]], city [[councilman]] David Daughtry on April 28, 1988, and remained married to him until his death in February 2008.<ref name="kingsbury" /><ref name="biography">{{cite web|url=https://www.terrigibbs.us/Biography.html|title=Biography|work=Terri Gibbs|access-date=2009-08-09|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20090204180540/http://www.terrigibbs.us/Biography.html|archivedate=2009-02-04}}</ref> They had one son, David Wayne Daughtry II, born October 20, 1989.<ref name="biography" />

==Awards and honors==
Gibbs has won several industry awards. She won [[Academy of Country Music]]'s Best New Female Vocalist and the [[Country Music Association]]'s inaugural Horizon Award (for artists rising to new levels of prominence).<ref>{{cite web|last=Gibbs|first=Terri|title=Awards|url=http://www.cmt.com/artists/az/gibbs_terri/bio.jhtml|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20040723181202/http://www.cmt.com/artists/az/gibbs_terri/bio.jhtml|url-status=dead|archive-date=July 23, 2004|publisher=CMT|access-date=23 November 2012}}</ref>  Gibbs performed during the Artists Music Guild's 2012 AMG Heritage Awards on November 10, 2012, and upon the completion of her performance she was presented with a Lifetime Achievement Award for her years of dedication to her craft without having any prior knowledge that she would be receiving such honors.<ref>{{cite web|last=Gibbs|first=Terri|title=Gibbs given a Lifetime Achievement Award|url=http://artistsmusicguild.com/content/2012-amg-award-recipients?preview=true&preview_id=611&preview_nonce=460175e19a|publisher=Artists Music Guild|access-date=23 November 2012}}{{Dead link|date=June 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=no }}</ref>
