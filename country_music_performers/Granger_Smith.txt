My soul mate, best friend, my happiness. He has released eleven studio albums, one live album, and two EPs. "[[Backroad Song]]" became his [[One-hit wonder|lone number-one single]] in 2016 and he had a second top ten hit with "[[If the Boot Fits]]" in 2017.

==Early life==
Smith was born in [[Dallas, Texas]]. At 14, he became interested in music and decided to pursue it as a hobby, teaching himself guitar.<ref name=Lubbock>{{cite news|last=Johnson|first=Natalie|title=Granger Smith returns from Iraq, Kuwait, and White House|url=http://lubbockonline.com/stories/082808/fea_464018158.shtml|newspaper=Lubbock-Avalanche Journal|access-date=March 29, 2014}}</ref> After graduating from [[Lake Highlands High School]] in 1998, Smith attended [[Texas A&M University]] in [[College Station, Texas]], and was a member of the [[Texas A&M University Corps of Cadets|Corps of Cadets]]. After his second year, he moved from Texas to [[Nashville, Tennessee|Nashville]] after signing a songwriting contract with EMI Music Publishing. Later, in 2005, he returned to Texas and re-enrolled at Texas A&M to complete his degree. He continued to play live and record music, including "We Bleed Maroon", an homage to his alma mater and fellow Aggies.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.grangersmith.com/news?n_id=217 |title=LAKE HIGHLAND WELCOMES HOME GRANGER SMITH}}</ref>

==Career==
Granger Smith was signed to a contract at the age of nineteen in Nashville. He performed three times at the [[White House]], and in 2008 he traveled to perform for soldiers in [[Iraq]] and [[Kuwait]].<ref name="Lubbock"/> His 2013 album ''[[Dirt Road Driveway]]'' peaked at number 15 on US country charts and number 11 on US indie charts.

On August 12, 2015, Smith announced he had signed with [[Broken Bow Records]] imprint Wheelhouse Records. Smith's EP ''[[4x4 (Granger Smith album)|4x4]]'' was co-produced by Smith and [[Frank Rogers (record producer)|Frank Rogers]]. The album debuted on the Top Country Albums chart at number 6, and reached number 51 on ''Billboard'' 200. The lead single from the album was "Backroad Song", which sold over 32,000 downloads in its first week of release.

Some of Smith's recordings made use of a stage name named Earl Dibbles Jr.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.countrycountdownusa.com/2017/02/11/who-is-earl-dibbles-jr/|title=Who Is Earl Dibbles Jr.?|date=February 11, 2017|website=Country Countdown USA}}</ref>

In December 2018, Smith released a single from his soundtrack album called ''They Were There, A Hero's Documentary'', which came out on November 30, 2018.<ref name="Itunes">{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/they-were-there-heros-documentary-original-motion-picture/1440136437|title=Granger Smith New Documentary called 'They Were There, A Hero's Documentary (Original Motion Picture Soundtrack)'|publisher=[[iTunes Store]]|date=November 30, 2018|access-date=November 30, 2018}}</ref> "They Were There" and its music video came out on the same day, December 7, 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wG69eX4Rxwo|title=Granger Smith New Single Music Released from 'They Were There, A Hero's Documentary (Original Motion Picture Soundtrack)'|via=YouTube|date=December 7, 2018|access-date=December 7, 2018}}</ref>

On July 19, 2019, Smith performed for an audience of 86,000 as the opening act for the [[The Garth Brooks Stadium Tour|Garth Brooks Stadium Tour]] at the Albertsons Stadium in Boise.

On January 1, 2022, Smith took over ''After MidNite'', [[Premiere Networks]]' long-running overnight [[country radio]] show. The show had previously been hosted by [[Blair Garner]], then Cody Alan. Later that year, he joined Canadian country music group [[High Valley]] on their single "[[Country Music, Girls and Trucks]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://tasteofcountry.com/high-valley-granger-smith-country-music-girls-and-trucks-music-video/|title=High Valley and Granger Smith Throw Old-Fashioned Country Field Party in 'Country Music, Girls and Trucks' Video [Watch]|first=Grace|last=Lenehan Vaughn|work=Taste of Country|date=June 2, 2022|access-date=August 23, 2022}}</ref>

In November 2022, Smith released his eleventh studio album ''Moonrise''. The album was featured in the Pure Flix film of the same name, which Smith starred in, released on December 15, 2022.

On April 11, 2023, Smith announced that he would be leaving country music touring in order to focus on ministry at his church in Austin, Texas. His "Like A River Tour" served as his farewell tour. The tour concluded on August 26, 2023.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Granger Smith Is Leaving Country Music to Focus on Ministry: 'A New Direction for My Life' (Exclusive) |url=https://people.com/country/granger-smith-announces-hes-leaving-country-music-for-ministry-exclusive/ |access-date=2023-04-11 |website=Peoplemag |language=en}}</ref>

==Personal life==
He married Amber Emily Bartlett on February 11, 2010. They met on the set of the music video for Smith's song "Don't Listen to the Radio", and she has been featured in several of his music videos since. The couple has one daughter and three sons.<ref name="river">{{cite web|title=Granger Smith's Real-Life Love Story Plays Out in 'Happens Like That' Music Video|url=http://tasteofcountry.com/granger-smith-wife-happens-like-that-video/|access-date=February 22, 2018|work=Taste of Country|date=August 7, 2017 }}</ref>

On June 6, 2019, Smith announced that the couple's youngest son, River Kelly Smith, had died following a drowning accident at their home.<ref>{{cite news|date=June 6, 2019|title=Country singer Granger Smith announces death of 3-year-old son|work=USA Today|url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/music/2019/06/06/granger-smith-son-dead-tragic-accident/1370451001/|access-date=}}</ref> River had previously appeared in the video for "Happens Like That".<ref name="river"/>

On March 11, 2021, Smith and his wife announced they were having their fourth child, a boy, in August.<ref>{{Cite web|date=March 11, 2021|title=Granger Smith and Wife Amber Expecting Baby Boy 21 Months After the Death of Son River|url=https://people.com/parents/granger-smith-wife-amber-pregnant-expecting-baby-boy-after-death-of-son-river/|access-date=|website=People|language=en}}</ref> Their son, Maverick Beckham Smith, was born August 20, 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|date=August 20, 2021|title=Granger Smith and Wife Amber Welcome Son Maverick Beckham: 'So Many Emotions'|url=https://people.com/parents/granger-smith-wife-amber-welcome-son-maverick-beckham/|access-date=|website=People|language=en}}</ref>

Smith is a [[Christians|Christian]].
