'''Chuck Wagon and the Wheels''' was an American [[country music]] group with a [[professional wrestling]] theme. It was composed of members:  Carl "Cal" Pyle (background vocals), and Sid Sequin (bass guitar, vocals), pseudonyms of brothers [[Gordon Kennedy (musician)|Gordon Kennedy]], Bryan Kennedy, and Shelby Kennedy.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.copyrightencyclopedia.com/chris-wood-suite-of-songs-i-fell-for-you-bill-crosby-tunes/|title=Chris Wood--Suite of songs, I fell for you, Bill Crosby tunes|website=Copyrightencyclopedia.com|accessdate=2020-03-25}}</ref> The group signed to [[Lyric Street Records]] in 2000, releasing their album ''Off the Top Rope'' that year.<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|url={{Allmusic|class=artist|id=p547461/biography|pure_url=yes}}|title=Chuck Wagon & the Wheels biography|last=Phares|first=Heather|website=[[AllMusic]]|accessdate=2009-02-26}}</ref> It included the single "Beauty's in the Eye of the Beerholder," which peaked at 75 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' country singles charts. Bryan Kennedy purchased the rights to the name Chuck Wagon and the Wheels from the founding member of the original group, Chuck W. Maultsby, in 1998.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://tucson.com/entertainment/music/wheels-reunion-benefit-concert-this-saturday/article_4f689c0f-bae2-5060-a016-c65962c948c2.html | title=Wheels reunion, benefit concert this Saturday | work=Tucson.com | agency=May 19, 2006 | accessdate=May 30, 2021}}</ref>
