==Career==
In 2012 Rachele Lynae signed with Momentum Label Group of [[Nashville, Tennessee|Nashville]], Tennessee. At the end of her senior year at Belmont University, Rachele Lynae recorded a five-song [[extended play|EP]]. After sharing the EP with now-manager Jimmy Murphy, Lynae was introduced to [[Jamie O'Neal]], Murphy's daughter.<ref name=bikman/>

Working with Jamie O'Neal, [[Stephanie Bentley]], and producer Jimmy Murphy, Rachele co-wrote and recorded "Party 'Til the Cows Come Home", a country party anthem.<ref>{{cite AV media

In 2012, Lynae and [[Jamie O'Neal]] released a duet of "[[Have Yourself a Merry Little Christmas]]" as a holiday single and video, which was subsequently featured in [[People (magazine)|People Magazine]]'s Country Special Issue's Holiday Playlist.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gotcountryonline.com/2013/11/16/rising-country-star-rachele-lynae-featured-in-the-new-issue-of-people-country/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140301221842/http://www.gotcountryonline.com/2013/11/16/rising-country-star-rachele-lynae-featured-in-the-new-issue-of-people-country/|archive-date=March 1, 2014|website=gotcountryonline.com|date=November 16, 2013|title=Rising Country Star Rachele Lynae Featured In The New Issue Of PEOPLE Country}}</ref> She was also listed in Nashville Lifestyles' Top 20 Musicians to Watch.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.nashvillelifestyles.com/bestof/top-musicians-to-watch |title=Top 20 Musicians to Watch – Nashville Lifestyles |access-date=2014-02-25 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140303084645/http://www.nashvillelifestyles.com/bestof/top-musicians-to-watch |archive-date=2014-03-03 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Lynae's 2013 single, "Fishin' For Something" was very well received at country radio, and topped the Renegade Radio/Nashville.com Chart in August.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.nashville.com/music/2013/08/rachele-lynae-tops-the-renegade-radionashville-com-chart/ |title=Rachele Lynae Tops the Renegade Radio/Nashville.com Chart « Nashville.com |accessdate=2014-02-25 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131011225012/http://www.nashville.com/music/2013/08/rachele-lynae-tops-the-renegade-radionashville-com-chart/ |archivedate=2013-10-11 }}</ref> Earlier that same year, Lynae was invited to become an honorary "Friends and Family" member of the [[Country Music Hall of Fame and Museum]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.gotcountryonline.com/2013/02/19/newcomer-rachele-lynae-becomes-honorary-friends-and-family-member-of-the-country-music-hall-of-fame-and-museum/ |title=Newcomer Rachele Lynae Becomes Honorary Friends and Family Member Of The Country Music Hall of Fame and Museum|date=February 19, 2013 |access-date=2014-02-25 |archive-date=2014-03-02 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140302042331/http://www.gotcountryonline.com/2013/02/19/newcomer-rachele-lynae-becomes-honorary-friends-and-family-member-of-the-country-music-hall-of-fame-and-museum/ |url-status=dead |website = gotcountryonline.com}}</ref> "Touch The Stars," released on February 18, 2014, was Lynae's third single release<ref name="allaccess.com"/> and received airplay at [[Sirius XM Radio]]'s country channel, [[The Highway (Sirius XM)|The Highway]].

Lynae's self-titled debut album, which was released on April 22, 2014, was produced by Jamie O'Neal and included her first three singles: "Party 'Til the Cows Come Home," "Fishin' for Something," and "Touch the Stars."

Lynae has opened for such legendary artists as [[Wynonna Judd]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.ticketweb.com/t3/sale/SaleEventDetail?dispatch=loadSelectionData&eventId=3599494|title=Ticket Purchase Page for Sierra View Music Fest|website=ticketweb.com|access-date=2014-02-25|archive-date=2016-03-04|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304032252/http://www.ticketweb.com/t3/sale/SaleEventDetail?dispatch=loadSelectionData&eventId=3599494|url-status=dead}}</ref> and [[Leon Russell]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.gtrnews.com/greater-tulsa-reporter/11842/country-newcomer-opens-for-leon-russell |title=GTR Newspapers &#124; Find Local Tulsa, Bixby, Broken Arrow, Jenks, Union, and Owasso News, Sports, and Entertainment:Country Newcomer Opens for Leon Russell |access-date=2014-02-25 |archive-date=2015-04-18 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150418031423/http://www.gtrnews.com/greater-tulsa-reporter/11842/country-newcomer-opens-for-leon-russell |url-status=dead }}</ref> She has played at famed Nashville's local bar [[Tootsie's Orchid Lounge]] and made five appearances at Nashville's 2012 [[Country Music Association Awards|Country Music Awards]] events, including one acoustic performance and several autograph signings.<ref name=bio/>
