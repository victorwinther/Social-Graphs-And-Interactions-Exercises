'''The Ranch''' was an Australian-American [[country music]] trio, which formed in 1997 by [[Keith Urban]] (guitar, keyboards and vocals), Jerry Flowers (bass guitar and background vocals) and Peter Clarke (drums and vocals).<ref name="NLA 1">{{Citation | author1 = The Ranch | author2=Urban, Keith | authorlink2 = Keith Urban | author3 = Rust, Vernon | title = The Ranch | publication-date = 1997 | publisher = Capitol: Manufactured and distributed by WEA. [[National Library of Australia]] | location = [[Nashville, Tennessee|Nashville, Tenn]] | url = http://trove.nla.gov.au/work/8867792 | access-date = 17 August 2014 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=SAEC&p_theme=saec&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=0EAFE7FAD5D99D77&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM |title="Funktry", Chesney & Willie |access-date=2008-09-05 |last=Alexander |first=Wiley |date=1997-10-10 |work=San Antonio Express-News}}</ref><ref name="whitburn">{{cite book|last=Whitburn|first=Joel|title=Hot Country Songs 1944 to 2008|publisher=Record Research, Inc|date=2008|page=338|isbn=978-0-89820-177-2}}</ref> Most of the group's material was co-written by Urban and Vernon Rust.<ref name="NLA 1"/><ref name="Ruhlmann"/>

They issued a [[The Ranch (album)|self-titled album]] on [[Capitol Records]] in that year, and two related singles, "Walkin' the Country" and "Just Some Love", which appeared on the country charts before the group disbanded in 1998.<ref name="Ruhlmann">{{cite web | url = http://www.allmusic.com/album/the-ranch-mw0000325776 | title = ''The Ranch'' – Keith Urban | publisher = [[AllMusic]] | author = Ruhlmann, William | access-date=17 August 2014 }}</ref> Besides its two singles, ''The Ranch'' included "[[Some Days You Gotta Dance]]", which was issued in 2001 as a single by the [[Dixie Chicks]], and also had Urban on guitar.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20040405190312/http://www.cmt.com/artists/az/urban_keith/bio.jhtml Keith Urban Biography]</ref> After disbanding The Ranch, Urban resumed his solo career.<ref name="Ruhlmann"/> Due to his solo success, The Ranch's album was re-issued in February 2004 on Capitol/[[EMI Records|EMI]] as ''Keith Urban in The Ranch'' with two bonus tracks: "Billy" and "[[Stuck in the Middle with You]]", the latter is a cover version of a single by the 1970s band [[Stealers Wheel]].<ref name="Ruhlmann 1">{{cite web | url = http://www.allmusic.com/album/release/in-the-ranch-mr0001212200 | title = ''In the Ranch'' – Keith Urban | author = Ruhlmann, William | publisher = [[AllMusic]] | access-date = 17 August 2014 }}</ref> It also featured two music videos, "Walkin' the Country" and "Clutterbilly".<ref name="Ruhlmann 1"/>

After The Ranch disbanded, Urban began a solo career. Many of his solo efforts have included Flowers.
