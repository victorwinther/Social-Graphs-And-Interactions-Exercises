'''Brett Kissel''' (born May 27, 1990) is a Canadian [[country music|country]] singer. He has achieved four number-one hits on the Canadian country chart with "[[Airwaves (Brett Kissel song)|Airwaves]]", "[[Drink About Me]]", "[[A Few Good Stories]]", and "[[Make a Life, Not a Living]]". Kissel has released the albums, ''[[Started with a Song]]'' (2013), ''[[Pick Me Up (album)|Pick Me Up]]'' (2015), ''[[We Were That Song]]'' (2017), ''[[Now or Never (Brett Kissel album)|Now or Never]]'' (2020), ''[[What Is Life? (album)|What Is Life?]]'' (2021), and ''[[The Compass Project]]'' (2023).

==Early life==
Kissel was born in St. Paul, Alberta,<ref name="edmonton">{{cite news|last=Murray|first=Tom|title=Country singer Brett Kissel heads to Nashville|url=https://edmontonjournal.com/entertainment/music/Country+singer+Brett+Kissel+heads+Nashville/8527146/story.html|accessdate=August 2, 2013|newspaper=[[Edmonton Journal]]|date=June 14, 2013|archive-date=September 16, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130916051411/http://www.edmontonjournal.com/entertainment/music/Country+singer+Brett+Kissel+heads+Nashville/8527146/story.html|url-status=dead}}</ref> the son of Brenda and Gordon Kissel. He has one older brother, Jamison. He grew up on a cattle ranch in northeastern Alberta's Flat Lake region, where his family operates the Wengzynowski North View cattle ranch.

==Career==
===2000–2011: Early years===
Kissel recorded his first album, ''Keepin' It Country'', when he was 12 years old.<ref name="vermilion">{{cite news|title=Allied Arts Presents Brett Kissel|url=http://vermilionvoicetest.wordpress.com/2011/11/21/allied-arts-presents-brett-kissel/|accessdate=August 2, 2013|newspaper=Vermilion Voice Test|date=November 21, 2011}}</ref>

Kissel released three more albums, ''By Request'' in 2004, ''Tried and True – A Canadian Tribute'' in 2006 and ''My Roots Run Deep'' in 2008.<ref name="vermilion" /><ref name="edmonton" /> In 2006, Kissel was nominated for the Chevy Trucks Rising Star Award at the [[Canadian Country Music Association]] Awards.<ref>{{cite news|last=Feniak|first=Jenny|title=Road Hammers will open CCMAs|url=http://jam.canoe.ca/Music/Artists/R/Road_Hammers/2006/09/09/pf-1817912.html|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130802134137/http://jam.canoe.ca/Music/Artists/R/Road_Hammers/2006/09/09/pf-1817912.html|url-status=usurped|archive-date=August 2, 2013|accessdate=August 2, 2013|newspaper=[[Edmonton Sun]]|date=September 9, 2006}}</ref> Kissel was 16 years old at the time, making him the youngest CCMA nominee in the history of the awards show.<ref name="edmonton" />

On May 10, 2013, Kissel signed a record deal with [[Warner Music Canada]].<ref name="bonnyville">{{cite news|last=Mendler|first=Andrew|title=Brett Kissel signs record deal with Warner|url=http://www.bonnyvillenouvelle.ca/article/20130521/BNV0307/305219969/brett-kissel-signs-record-deal-with-warner|accessdate=August 2, 2013|newspaper=Bonnyville Nouvelle|date=May 21, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131004213901/http://www.bonnyvillenouvelle.ca/article/20130521/BNV0307/305219969/brett-kissel-signs-record-deal-with-warner|archive-date=October 4, 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> His debut single, "[[Started with a Song (song)|Started with a Song]]", was released on June 17.<ref name="bonnyville" /> It became the most added song at Canadian country radio in its first week, surpassing a Canadian country radio record set by [[Taylor Swift]]'s "[[We Are Never Ever Getting Back Together]]".<ref name="stpaul">{{cite news|last=Huser|first=Janice|title=Kissel's 'Started With A Song' breaks radio record|url=http://www.spjournal.com/article/20130709/STP0801/307099966/0/stp|accessdate=August 2, 2013|newspaper=St. Paul Journal|date=July 9, 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130724064600/http://www.spjournal.com/article/20130709/STP0801/307099966/0/STP|archive-date=July 24, 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> It debuted at number 87 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Canadian Hot 100]] for the week of July 27, 2013.<ref>{{cite web|title=Canada Singles Top 100 (July 27, 2013) – Music Charts|url=http://acharts.us/canada_singles_top_100/2013/30|work=acharts.us|accessdate=August 2, 2013}}</ref> The music video for the song premiered on [[CMT (Canada)|CMT Canada]] on June 27.<ref name="stpaul" /> Kissel's first album for Warner, also titled ''[[Started with a Song]]'', was released on October 1, 2013.<ref name="bonnyville" />

On February 14, 2014, Kissel released his third single from his ''Started with a Song'' album titled "3-2-1". On March 29, 2014, Kissel won his first [[Juno Award]] for [[Juno Award for Breakthrough Artist of the Year|Breakthrough Artist of the Year]], and also performed on the 2014 Juno Awards broadcast on [[CTV Television Network|CTV]] alongside [[OneRepublic]], [[Tegan and Sara]], [[Bachman–Turner Overdrive]] and [[The Sheepdogs]].<ref>Sperounes, Sandra. [https://edmontonjournal.com/Alberta+Brett+Kissel+wins+Juno+Breakthrough+Artist+Year/9677807/story.html "Alberta's Brett Kissel wins Juno for Breakthrough Artist of the Year"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181215122137/http://www.edmontonjournal.com/Alberta+Brett+Kissel+wins+Juno+Breakthrough+Artist+Year/9677807/story.html |date=December 15, 2018 }}. ''Edmontonjournal.com''.</ref>

In September 2014, Kissel led all country singers with eight nominations<ref>[https://torontosun.com/2014/07/16/brett-kissel-leads-2014-ccma-awards-nominations/wcm/077f527f-a339-45f6-a01a-1616a247a9e7 "Brett Kissel leads 2014 CCMA Awards nominations"] ''Toronto Sun'', July 16, 2014.</ref> for the Canadian Country Music Awards held in Edmonton, Alberta.

===2015–2018: ''Pick Me Up'' and ''We Were That Song''===

In September 2017 Kissel won Male Artist of the Year, Interactive Artist of the Year, Country Music Program or Special of the Year and Video of the Year for I Didn't Fall in Love With Your Hair at the 2017 [[Canadian Country Music Association|Canadian Country Music Awards]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ccma.org/cgi/page.cgi/_article.html/News/Complete_List_of_2017_CCMA_Award_Winners|title=Complete List of 2017 CCMA Award Winners – Canadian Country Music Association|website=[[Canadian Country Music Association]]|language=en|access-date=January 4, 2018|archive-date=November 24, 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181124211554/https://www.ccma.org/cgi/page.cgi/_article.html/News/Complete_List_of_2017_CCMA_Award_Winners|url-status=dead}}</ref> He also released a new album [[We Were That Song]] on December 8, 2017.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://tourbusentertainment.com/brett-kissel-song-album-review/|title=Brett Kissel – We Were That Song – Album Review – Tour Bus Entertainment|date=December 7, 2017|work=Tour Bus Entertainment|access-date=January 4, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref> In 2018 he went on a tour across Canada to support the album.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thechronicleherald.ca/living/the-best-nova-scotia-concerts-of-2018-270134/|title=The best Nova Scotia concerts of 2018|first=Stephen|last=Cooke|newspaper=[[The Chronicle Herald]]|date=December 20, 2018|access-date=March 16, 2020}}</ref>

===2019–2021: ''Now or Never'', ''What Is Life?''===

which was released on January 1, 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://edmonton.ctvnews.ca/now-or-never-brett-kissel-celebrates-new-album-1.4750585|title='Now or Never': Brett Kissel celebrates new album|last=Williams|first=Rob|publisher=[[CTV News]]|date=January 2, 2020|access-date=March 16, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite States in New Album, 'Now or Never' (Exclusive)| first=Gayle|last=Thompson|publisher=[[CBS Interactive|Popculture.com]]|date=February 10, 2020|access-date=March 16, 2020}}</ref> "Drink About Me" would become Kissel's second number one on the Canada Country chart, but wouldn't find a place on the American Country Billboard chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://canadianbeats.ca/2020/01/21/brett-kissels-new-single-drink-about-me-earned-a-top-spot-on-the-charts-last-week/|title=Brett Kissel's new single "Drink About Me" earned a top spot on the charts last week|first=Nanci|last=Dagg|date=January 21, 2020|work=Canadian Beats Media|accessdate=November 14, 2020}}</ref> "[[She Drives Me Crazy (Brett Kissel song)|She Drives Me Crazy]]" was released as the second single off the album in February 2020.<ref name="SheDrivesMeCrazy">{{cite web |url=https://canadianbeats.ca/2020/02/01/brett-kissel-releases-new-single-and-video-she-drives-me-crazy/|title=Brett Kissel releases new single and video, "She Drives Me Crazy"|date=February 1, 2020|first=Dutch|last=Bickell|work=Canadian Beats Media}}</ref>

In September 2020, Kissel released this third single off ''Now or Never'', "[[A Few Good Stories]]"<ref>{{cite web |url=https://globalnews.ca/video/7336087/tms-couch-ella-country-singer-brett-kissel-performs-a-few-good-stories|title=TMS Couch-ella: Country singer Brett Kissel performs 'A Few Good Stories'|date=September 15, 2020|work=[[Global News]]}}</ref> and picked up 4 [[CCMA Awards]], winning Male Artist of the Year, Fan's Choice, Creative Director, and Album of the Year for ''Now it Never''.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.durhamregion.com/whatson-story/10210841-brett-kissel-wins-big-at-ccma-awards/|title=Brett Kissel wins big at CCMA Awards|date=September 28, 2020|work=DurhamRegion.com}}</ref> Kissel then signed his first American record deal with Verge Records, a Nashville-based partner of [[ONErpm]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://musicrow.com/2020/09/brett-kissel-inks-deal-with-verge-records/|title=Brett Kissel Inks Deal With Verge Records|date=September 30, 2020|first=Hollabaugh|last=Lorie|work=MusicRow Magazine}}</ref> "A Few Good Stories" became Kissel's third Number One on Canada Country,<ref name="Canada Country"/> "[[She Drives Me Crazy (Brett Kissel song)|She Drives Me Crazy]]" became Kissel's second Platinum-certified single,<ref name="MC"/> and "[[Drink About Me]]" was nominated for Single of the Year at the [[2021 Juno Awards]].<ref>{{cite web|first=Holly|last=Gordon|url=https://www.cbc.ca/music/junos/news/the-weeknd-jp-saxe-jessie-reyez-and-justin-bieber-lead-2021-juno-award-nominations-1.5940551|title=The Weeknd, JP Saxe, Jessie Reyez and Justin Bieber lead 2021 Juno Award nominations|work=[[CBC Music]]|date=March 9, 2021}}</ref>

In March 2021, Kissel released the single "[[Make a Life, Not a Living]]", and announced his fifth major-label album ''[[What Is Life? (album)|What Is Life?]]'', which was released on April 9, 2021.<ref name="CN">{{cite web|url=https://cochranenow.com/articles/brett-kissel-says-new-record-is-heart-driven-not-hit-driven|title=Brett Kissel says new record is heart driven not hit driven|work=CochraneNow|first=Lauren|last=Meister|date=March 4, 2021}}</ref> "Make a Life, Not a Living" debuted by setting a record as the most-added song at Canadian country radio in a single week ever.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://canadianbeats.ca/2021/03/22/brett-kissel-releases-video-for-make-a-life-not-a-living/|title=Brett Kissel releases video for "Make a Life Not a Living"|date=March 22, 2021|first=Nanci|last=Dagg|work=Canadian Beats Media}}</ref> It later became his fourth career Number One at Canadian country radio,<ref name="Canada Country"/> and his first song to chart in the top 50 of the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Canadian Hot 100]].<ref name="Can Hot 100"/> Kissel won the "Fans' Choice" award at the [[2021 Canadian Country Music Awards]] in November 2021.<ref name="toCCMA"/>

==Personal life==
Brett Kissel married Cecilia Friesen in July 2011 in Edmonton, Alberta. They lived in Nashville, Tennessee for several years.  In 2016, Cecilia gave birth to their first daughter.<ref>{{Cite web|title = ET Canada {{!}} Blog – Country Singer Brett Kissel Welcomes New Baby Girl, Mila|url = http://www.etcanada.com/blogs/etc_176236/country-singer-brett-kissel-welcomes-new-baby-girl-mila/music|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20160120195208/http://www.etcanada.com/blogs/etc_176236/country-singer-brett-kissel-welcomes-new-baby-girl-mila/music|url-status = dead|archive-date = January 20, 2016|website = etcanada|access-date = February 10, 2016}}</ref> Over the next six years, she gave birth to their second daughter, and two sons.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hellomagazine.com/celebrities/20220515140267/brett-kissel-juno-awards-opens-up-about-family/|title=Brett Kissel opens up about family and what he learned during the coronavirus pandemic|date=May 15, 2022|work=[[Hello Magazine]]|access-date=May 28, 2023|first=Zachary|last=Harper}}</ref> Kissel and his family now reside on his family farm in northern Alberta.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://musicandtournews.com/exclusive-interview-brett-kissel-discusses-south-album-project/|title=EXCLUSIVE INTERVIEW: BRETT KISSEL DISCUSSES 'SOUTH ALBUM' PROJECT|date=February 7, 2023|work=Music and Tour News|access-date=February 9, 2023}}</ref>
