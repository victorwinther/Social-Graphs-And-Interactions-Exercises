'''Christopher F. Gaffney''' (October 3, 1950 – April 17, 2008)<ref name="Dead"/> was an American singer and songwriter from the [[Southwestern United States|Southwest]]. His career, both as a solo musician and as a member of several bands, was as eclectic as his musical tastes. Although he never achieved widespread fame, Gaffney, who died at the age of 57 from [[liver cancer]],<ref>{{cite journal
  | last = Owens
  | first = Kevin
  | title = Passing Notes: Chris Gaffney
  | journal = [[Guitar Player]]
  | volume = 42

  | date = August 2008
  }}</ref> left his mark on country, rock, soul, and other forms of American music. In its obituary, the ''[[Los Angeles Times]]'' described Gaffney as "a peer of [Dave] [[Dave Alvin|Alvin]], [[Los Lobos]], [[X (U.S. band)|X]] and the [[Red Hot Chili Peppers]] in chronicling the life of [[Southern California]]."<ref name=LA>Mike Boehm, ''Los Angeles Times'', [http://www.latimes.com/entertainment/news/arts/la-me-gaffney18apr18,1,93437.story April 18, 2008].</ref>

==Career==
A self-described "army brat," '''Christopher F. Gaffney''' was born in [[Vienna]], Austria,<ref name="Dead"/> he then moved to [[Livorno, Italy]] and New York City as a young child. But Gaffney grew up primarily in southern California and [[Arizona]].<ref name=bio>{{Cite web |url=http://www.haciendabrothers.com/meet_the_band/chris_gaffney.html |title=Gaffney's bio at haciendabrothers.com |access-date=May 21, 2008 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080522072701/http://www.haciendabrothers.com/meet_the_band/chris_gaffney.html |archive-date=May 22, 2008 |url-status=dead }}</ref> In addition to music, Gaffney loved sports, especially boxing, and earned an LA [[Golden Gloves]] championship in 1967 and even trained with boxing hall-of-famer Jackie McCoy.<ref name=NPR>{{Cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=90510533|title=Remembering The Hacienda Bros. Chris Gaffney|website=Npr.org|accessdate=November 11, 2020}}</ref>

As a child, he learned to play the [[accordion]] and listened to [[Norteño (music)|norteno]], [[country music|country]],
and [[rock & roll]].<ref name=allmusic>[{{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p1618|pure_url=yes}} Gaffney's bio at Allmusic.com]</ref> As a teenager, Gaffney played in various house band and eventually released his first solo album, ''Road to Indio'', in 1986. Produced by friend Wyman Reese, his debut album demonstrated his "genre-bending"<ref name=austin>{{Cite web|url=http://www.austin360.com/music/content/music/stories/2008/04/0424xlgaffney.html|title=Obituary |website=Austin360.com|accessdate=November 11, 2020}}</ref> tastes by showcasing forays into [[honky tonk]], soul, and [[Bakersfield sound|Bakersfield country]].<ref name=allmusic/>

His next album was as Chris Gaffney & the Cold Hard Facts. Released in 1990, this album revealed Latino influences and dealt with issues of poverty and working-class life. Two years later, Gaffney released ''Mi Vida Loca'' which has been described as a "cross between [[Merle Haggard]] and [[The Blasters]]."<ref name=loca>[{{AllMusic|class=album|id=r7921|pure_url=yes}} Allmusic's review of ''Mi Vida Loca'']</ref> His next solo album, ''[[Loser's Paradise]]'', (1995) was produced by Gaffney's friend [[Dave Alvin]]<ref name=official>{{Cite web |url=http://www.haciendabrothers.com/home/ |title=Hacienda Brothers' official website |access-date=May 21, 2008 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080522072656/http://www.haciendabrothers.com/home/ |archive-date=May 22, 2008 |url-status=dead }}</ref> 
and featured contributions from [[Lucinda Williams]] and [[Jim Lauderdale]].

In 2002, Gaffney formed the band [[Hacienda Brothers]] with guitarist and songwriter [[Dave Gonzalez (guitarist)|Dave Gonzalez]], founding member of [[The Paladins]]. They recorded their third studio album shortly before Gaffney's death in early 2008. He died from [[liver cancer]] in [[Newport, California]].<ref name="Dead">{{Cite web|url=http://thedeadrockstarsclub.com/2008.html?_ult_url_params=sec=srp&slk=web&cpos=&pos=9&concept=standard&query=judy+ann+santos+everlasting+glow&cbid=|title=The Dead Rock Stars Club 2008 January to June|website=Thedeadrockstarsclub.com|accessdate=November 11, 2020}}</ref>

In addition to his solo and band work, Gaffney has also toured as a member of [[Dave Alvin]] & the Guilty Men<ref name=official/> and contributed to albums by [[Lucinda Williams]], The Iguanas, [[Tom Russell]], [[Christy McWilson]], The Lonesome Strangers, and Billy Bacon.

In 2009, a tribute album to Gaffney was released, ''A Man of Somebody's Dreams: A Tribute to the Songs of Chris Gaffney'', which included songs played by fellow Hacienda Brothers as well as others such as [[Dave Alvin]] and [[Los Lobos]].<ref>{{cite news

In 2011, Dave Alvin included a song about Gaffney, "Run Conejo Run", in his album ''Eleven Eleven''.
