'''Larry Campbell''' (born February 21, 1955) is an American singer and multi-instrumentalist who plays many stringed instruments (including guitar, [[mandolin]], [[pedal steel guitar]], [[slide guitar]], and violin) in genres including country, folk, blues, and rock. Campbell is best known for his time as part of [[Bob Dylan]]'s [[Never Ending Tour]] band from 1997 to 2004, his association with [[Levon Helm]] of [[The Band]], and the musical director of the Midnight Rambles.

Larry Campbell also has extensive experience as a studio musician. Over his 40-plus year career he has recorded with such artists as Levon Helm, [[Judy Collins]], [[Lucy Kaplansky]], [[Richard Shindell]], [[Linda Thompson (singer)|Linda Thompson]], [[Sheryl Crow]], [[Chris Castle]], [[Paul Simon]], [[B. B. King]], [[Willie Nelson]], [[Eric Andersen]], [[Buddy and Julie Miller]], [[Kinky Friedman]], [[Little Feat]], [[Hot Tuna]], [[Cyndi Lauper]], [[k.d. lang]], [[Anastasia Barzee]], [[Rosanne Cash]] and [[Ayọ]], among others.

== Biography ==
During the 1970s and 1980s, Campbell performed regularly on New York City's burgeoning [[country music]] scene, at well-known venues such as [[Greenwich Village]]'s legendary [[Lone Star Cafe]], City Limits, The Rodeo Bar, and O'Lunney's, near the [[United Nations]]. He contributed his talents to several musicals.

Beginning in the late 1970s, Campbell was also a member of The Woodstock Mountains Revue, a unique folk group that featured Artie & [[Happy Traum]], [[Pat Alger]], [[Jim Rooney (music)|Jim Rooney]], [[Bill Keith (musician)|Bill Keith]], [[John Herald]], [[Eric Andersen]] and [[John Sebastian]].<ref>{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p28322/biography|pure_url=yes}}|title=Biography: Woodstock Mountain Revue|last=Ruhlmann|first=William|publisher=Allmusic|accessdate=May 21, 2010}}</ref>

In 1982, Campbell performed in the orchestra for ''Alaska – The Musical'', playing [[fiddle]], acoustic and electric guitar, [[pedal steel]] and [[banjo]]. Campbell also performed in the orchestra for ''[[Big River (musical)|Big River]]'' in 1985, and ''Rhythm Ranch'' in 1989. In addition, he played pedal steel guitar, banjo, fiddle and guitar for the entire run of ''[[The Will Rogers Follies]]'', which opened on Broadway on May 1, 1991.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Larry Campbell – Broadway Cast & Staff {{!}} IBDB |url=https://www.ibdb.com/broadway-cast-staff/larry-campbell-389223 |access-date=2024-01-01 |website=www.ibdb.com}}</ref>

Campbell was a member of [[Bob Dylan]]'s "Never Ending Tour" band from March 31, 1997, until November 21, 2004.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Padgett |first=Ray |title=Larry Campbell Goes Deep on His Eight Years with Bob Dylan |url=https://www.flaggingdown.com/p/larry-campbell-goes-deep-on-his-eight |access-date=2024-01-01 |website=www.flaggingdown.com |language=en}}</ref> Through his association with Dylan's bass player [[Tony Garnier (musician)|Tony Garnier]], Campbell joined the band, replacing John Jackson as a guitarist, and expanded the role to multi-instrumentalist, playing instruments such as cittern, violin/fiddle, pedal steel guitar, lap steel guitar, mandolin, banjo, and slide guitar.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2020-05-22 |title=The Beginner's Guide to Bob Dylan's Never Ending Tour |url=https://pitchfork.com/thepitch/the-beginners-guide-to-bob-dylans-never-ending-tour/ |access-date=2024-01-01 |website=Pitchfork |language=en-US}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite web |date=2012-04-20 |title=Levon Helm Remembered: Larry Campbell Talks (2007) |url=https://jambands.com/features/2012/04/20/levon-helm-remembered-larry-campbell-talks-2007/ |access-date=2024-01-01 |website=Jambands |language=en-US}}</ref>

Since Campbell's departure from Dylan's band, he has continued to make guest appearances with various artists and live acts including [[Peter Wolf]], [[Elvis Costello]], [[Emmylou Harris]], [[Rosanne Cash]], [[Furthur (band)|Furthur]], and [[Phil and Friends]]. He has also produced albums for many artists, including [[Jorma Kaukonen]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.redhouserecords.com/store/aint-no-hurry|title=Ain't in No Hurry|date=May 28, 2015|publisher=}}</ref>

Campbell toured regularly with [[Levon Helm]], in addition to producing Helm's two Grammy-winning albums, ''[[Dirt Farmer]]'' and ''[[Electric Dirt]],'' and acting as the musical director for Helm's Midnight Ramble concerts.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/268942/levon-helm-going-electric-this-june|title=Levon Helm Going "Electric" This June|date=April 14, 2009|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|accessdate=November 24, 2010}}</ref><ref name=":0" />

On September 18, 2008, at the 7th Annual [[Americana Music Association]] Honors and Awards Show, Campbell was presented with The Award for Lifetime Achievement for an Instrumentalist.<ref>{{cite web |title=Awards – 2008 |url=https://americanamusic.org/awards/2008 |accessdate=March 28, 2020 |website=AmericanaMusic |publisher=The Americana Music Association}}</ref>

Campbell played banjo, fiddle, and pedal steel on [[The Black Crowes]] 2009 album ''[[Before the Frost...Until the Freeze]]''. He also appears on the [[Outlaw Country]] band ''[[Whitey Morgan and the 78's (album)|Whitey Morgan and the 78's]]'' recorded at Levon Helm's studio in December 2009 and January 2010, and ''Last Bird Home'' by [[Chris Castle]], also recorded at Levon's studio in 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.goerie.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/20110915/ENTERTAINMENT0301/309149867 |title=Americana songwriter, Womacks play Edinboro, Erie gigs|publisher=GoErie.com|date=September 15, 2011|accessdate=July 25, 2015}}</ref>

Campbell played guitar, pedal steel, banjo, slide, mandolin, and fiddle on [[Dan Brenner]]'s 2011 album ''Little Dark Angel'' produced by ten-time Grammy winner [[Jay Newland]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Jay Newland Discography|url=http://www.jdmanagement.com/jaynewland/newlanddiscography.pdf|publisher=Jdmanagement.com|accessdate=July 25, 2015|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131021210825/http://www.jdmanagement.com/jaynewland/newlanddiscography.pdf|archivedate=October 21, 2013}}</ref>

In 2012, Campbell won a Grammy award for the 2011 release ''Ramble at the Ryman''.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Larry Campbell {{!}} Artist {{!}} GRAMMY.com |url=https://www.grammy.com/artists/larry-campbell/8677 |access-date=2024-01-01 |website=www.grammy.com}}</ref>

Campbell and his wife, Teresa Williams, released a self-titled album on June 23, 2015, on [[Red House Records]].<ref>{{Cite news |last=Dougherty |first=Steve |date=2015-06-17 |title=Backups Larry Campbell and Teresa Williams Step Forward |language=en-US |work=Wall Street Journal |url=http://www.wsj.com/articles/backups-larry-campbell-and-teresa-williams-step-forward-1434562763 |access-date=2024-01-01 |issn=0099-9660}}</ref>

In 2019, Campbell and his wife filled in for [[Little Feat]] guitarist [[Paul Barrere]] during the band's 50th Anniversary Tour. The duo played with the band for most of the tour, from October 7–24, with [[Scott Sharrard]] taking over for the tour's last two dates on October 26 and 27, 2019.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Kahn |first1=Andy |title=Larry Campbell, Teresa Williams & Scott Sharrard To Fill-In For Little Feat's Paul Barrere |url=https://www.jambase.com/article/little-feat-paul-barrere-larry-campbell-teresa-williams-scott-sharrard |website=JamBase |accessdate=March 28, 2020}}</ref> The duo's stint with Little Feat came shortly before Barrere's death on October 26, 2019.<ref>{{cite web |title=Paul Barrere: July 3, 1948 – October 26, 2019 |url=https://www.littlefeat.net/news.html?n_id=3859 |website=Little Feat |accessdate=March 28, 2020}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Campbell is married to singer Teresa Williams.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.jambands.com/features/2007/04/22/larry-campbell-rock-n-rolls-right-hand-man|title=Larry Campbell: Rock N Rolls Right Hand Man|date=April 22, 2007|publisher=[[Relix]] Media Group|accessdate=November 24, 2010}}</ref><ref name=":1">{{Cite web |last=Blickenstaff |first=Jacob |title=The enduring musical romance of Larry Campbell and Teresa Williams |url=https://www.motherjones.com/media/2015/12/larry-campbell-teresa-williams-interview/ |access-date=2024-01-01 |website=Mother Jones |language=en-US}}</ref> Williams and Campbell met in New York City while playing country music in the 1980s.<ref name=":1" /> They live in Woodstock, New York.<ref name=":2">{{Cite magazine |last=Browne |first=David |date=2020-05-18 |title=Larry Campbell and Teresa Williams Celebrate His COVID-19 Recovery in Song |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/larry-campbell-teresa-williams-covid-19-let-us-get-together-right-down-here-1001337/ |access-date=2024-01-01 |magazine=Rolling Stone |language=en-US}}</ref>

On March 24, 2020, Williams, announced that Campbell had been diagnosed with [[COVID-19]] amidst the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Barry |first1=John |title=Coronavirus: Musician Larry Campbell tests positive; played with Bob Dylan, Levon Helm |url=https://www.poughkeepsiejournal.com/story/news/local/2020/03/25/coronavirus-larry-campbell-played-bob-dylan-tests-positive/5077126002/ |website=Poughkeepsie Journal |publisher=USA Today |accessdate=March 28, 2020}}</ref> After a two-week illness, the couple released a performance of Rev. Gary Davis's "Let Us Get Together Right Down Here".<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-features/larry-campbell-coronavirus-interview-976353/|title=Veteran Rock Guitarist Larry Campbell on Battling Coronavirus: 'This Thing Has Been a Beast'|first1=David|last1=Browne|magazine=[[Rolling Stone]]|date=April 2, 2020}}</ref><ref name=":2" />
