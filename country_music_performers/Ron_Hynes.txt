'''Ron Hynes''' (December 7, 1950 – November 19, 2015) was a [[Folk music|folk]] singer-songwriter from [[Newfoundland and Labrador]]. He was especially known for his composition "[[Sonny's Dream]]",<ref name="Moore2017">{{cite book|author=Christopher Moore|title=The Big Book of Canada (Updated Edition): Exploring the Provinces and Territories|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=zmS0DgAAQBAJ&pg=PA23|date=April 4, 2017|publisher=Tundra Books|isbn=978-1-101-91894-4|pages=23–}}</ref> which has been recorded worldwide by many artists and was named the 41st greatest Canadian song of all time on the 2005 [[CBC Radio One]] series ''[[50 Tracks: The Canadian Version]]''.<ref>{{cite news|title="50 Tracks" list of essential Canadian music|url=http://www.cbc.ca/50tracks/essentialcanadianmusic.html|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20050412002329/http://www.cbc.ca/50tracks/essentialcanadianmusic.html|archivedate=April 12, 2005|accessdate=April 23, 2013|newspaper=Canadian Broadcasting Corporation|date=2005}}</ref>

==Biography==
Ron Hynes was born in [[St. John's, Newfoundland and Labrador]] in December 1950, and raised in [[Ferryland]]. He was a founding member of [[The Wonderful Grand Band]], one of Newfoundland's most popular performing groups, and has released seven solo albums. His debut album, ''Discovery'', released in 1972, was the first album composed of totally original content by a Newfoundland artist. Hynes is a seven-time [[East Coast Music Awards]] winner, and past [[Juno Award|Juno]] and [[Canadian Country Music Awards]] nominee.<ref>{{cite news|title=Ron Hynes to undergo surgery|url=https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/newfoundland-labrador/ron-hynes-to-undergo-surgery-1.1209399|access-date=April 23, 2013|newspaper=Canadian Broadcasting Corporation|date=July 18, 2012}}</ref> He was named Artist of the Year ('92) and was presented with the prestigious Arts Achievement Award (2004) by the Newfoundland/Labrador Arts Council. In 2002, Hynes received an honorary Doctor of Letters from [[Memorial University]] in St. John's in recognition of his original songwriting and his contribution to the cultural heritage of Newfoundland. In 2006, Hynes was honored as the recipient of the St. John's Folk Arts Council's Lifetime Achievement Award.

Widely regarded as one of Canada's premier singer-songwriters with a career spanning over 30 years, Hynes' songs have become part of the fabric of Newfoundland culture. His work is also known outside the province; Hynes' songs have been covered worldwide by over 100 artists, including [[Emmylou Harris]], [[Valdy]] and [[Christy Moore]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Ron Hynes on CBC Music|url=http://music.cbc.ca/#/artists/Ron-Hynes|accessdate=April 23, 2013|newspaper=[[Canadian Broadcasting Corporation]]}}</ref>

Most recently, Hynes was the winner of Male Solo Recording of the Year at the 2007 [[East Coast Music Awards]], and picked up three awards at the 2006 [[MusicNL]] awards show in November 2006. The awards included Entertainer of the Year, Songwriter of the Year and Folk/Roots Artist of the Year.

Hynes narrated two audiobooks for Rattling Books, ''Hard Light: 32 Little Stories'' by [[Michael Crummey]]<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.overdrive.com/media/133106/hard-light | title=Hard Light: 32 Little Stories by Michael Crummey | publisher=Rattling Books / [[Rakuten OverDrive]] | accessdate=August 3, 2019}}</ref> and ''Death on the Ice'' by [[Cassie Brown]].<ref>{{cite book | url=https://sellfy.com/p/qR6q/ | title=Death on the Ice (Cassie Brown, Harold Horwood) The Great Newfoundland Sealing Disaster of 1914 | publisher=Rattling Books audiobooks, Alca Productions | author=Cassie Brown, [[Harold Horwood]] | year=2011 | isbn=978-0-9782065-7-4 | orig-year=1972}}</ref>

He also had occasional acting roles, including in the films ''[[The Adventure of Faustus Bidgood]]'', ''[[Secret Nation]]'', ''Anchor Zone'', ''[[Violet (2000 film)|Violet]]'' and ''The Bingo Robbers'', and the television series ''[[Dooley Gardens]]'' and ''[[Emily of New Moon (TV series)|Emily of New Moon]]''.

==Illness and death==
In July 2012, it was announced that Ron Hynes was diagnosed with throat cancer.<ref>{{cite news|title=Musician Ron Hynes diagnosed with cancer|url=https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/newfoundland-labrador/musician-ron-hynes-diagnosed-with-cancer-1.1209396|access-date=April 23, 2013|newspaper=Canadian Broadcasting Corporation|date=July 28, 2012}}</ref> On August 11, 2012, Hynes performed to a sold-out crowd of more than 3,000 at the Mile One Centre in St. John's. This was his last performance before undergoing cancer treatment.  The concert included a reunion of Hynes' old band, [[The Wonderful Grand Band]].<ref>{{cite news|title='Magical' Ron Hynes performs before cancer treatment|url=https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/newfoundland-labrador/magical-ron-hynes-performs-before-cancer-treatment-1.1238692|access-date=April 23, 2013|newspaper=[[CBC News]]|date=August 12, 2012}}</ref> In the fall of 2013, he was in remission and back on tour, including participation in a [[Vinyl Cafe]] tour broadcast on [[CBC Radio]]. Hynes died at a hospital in St. John's on November 19, 2015, at the age of 64.<ref>{{cite news|author=Michael MacDonald |url=https://www.theglobeandmail.com/arts/music/newfoundland-and-labrador-folk-legend-ron-hynes-dead-at-64/article27381444/ |title=Newfoundland and Labrador folk legend Ron Hynes dead at 64 |newspaper=[[The Globe and Mail]] |date= November 19, 2015|accessdate=November 20, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author= |url=http://www.thetelegram.com/News/Local/2015-11-19/article-4349461/Reports-songwriter-Ron-Hynes-dead/1 |title=Songwriter Ron Hynes dead: reports – Local |publisher=The Telegram |date= |accessdate=November 20, 2015}}</ref>

After Hynes' death, actor and writer [[Joel Thomas Hynes]],<ref>[http://www.durhamregion.com/whatson-story/5155826-singer-ron-hynes-brings-his-folk-classic-to-greenbank-hall/ "Singer Ron Hynes brings his folk classic to Greenbank hall"]</ref> his nephew, announced on Facebook<ref>{{cite web|author=The Canadian Press |url=http://www.ctvnews.ca/entertainment/family-friends-hope-ron-hynes-death-shines-light-on-drug-addiction-1.2674108 |title=Family, friends hope Ron Hynes' death shines light on drug addiction |publisher=[[CTV News]] |date= November 25, 2015|accessdate=November 25, 2015}}</ref> that Hynes suffered from drug addictions that led to his demise. "He remained a hardcore addict right to his final days. And it killed him.", said Joel Thomas Hynes.

==Stage and screen career==
Hynes had starred on screen and stage as an actor/writer in numerous roles dating back to the 1970s, including ''The Bard of Prescott Street'', "Wonderful Grand Band", ''[[CODCO|The Best of CODCO]]'', ''[[Hank Williams: The Show He Never Gave]]'', ''The Island Opry Show'', ''The Lost Island Opry'', ''[[Secret Nation]]'', ''Anchor Zone'' and ''[[Dooley Gardens]]''.

==Tributes==
An independent tribute album, entitled ''11:11 – Newfoundland Women Sing Songs by Ron and Connie Hynes'', was released in 1997, with various female artists performing songs written by Ron Hynes and his ex-wife Connie.

A documentary on Hynes, entitled ''Man of a Thousand Songs'' and directed by [[William D. MacGillivray]], debuted at the [[2010 Toronto International Film Festival]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Jankovic|first=Jovana|title=Review: Man of a Thousand Songs – RWMFF 2010|url=http://thetfs.ca/2010/11/09/review-man-of-a-thousand-songs-tiff-2010/|accessdate=April 23, 2013|newspaper=Toronto Film Scene|date=November 9, 2010}}</ref>

The 2023 tribute album ''Sonny Don't Go Away'' featured versions of Hynes's songs performed by artists such as [[Tim Baker (musician)|Tim Baker]], [[Alan Doyle]], [[The Once]], [[Fortunate Ones]], [[Amelia Curran (musician)|Amelia Curran]], [[The Ennis Sisters]], [[Rum Ragged]], [[Matthew Byrne (musician)|Matthew Byrne]], [[The Dardanelles (band)|The Dardanelles]] and [[Kellie Loder]].<ref>Calum Slingerland, [https://www.google.com/search?client=firefox-b-d&q=tim+baker+leaving+on+the+evening+tide "Tim Baker, Alan Doyle, the Once Cover Ron Hynes for New Tribute Album"]. ''[[Exclaim!]]'', August 29, 2023.</ref>
