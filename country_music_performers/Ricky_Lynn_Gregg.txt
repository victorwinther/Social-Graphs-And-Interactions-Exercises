'''Ricky Lynn Gregg''' (born August 22, 1959) is a [[country music]] artist of Native American descent.<ref name="oldies">{{cite web |url=http://www.oldies.com/artist-biography/Ricky-Lynn-Gregg.html |title=Ricky Lynn Gregg biography |accessdate=August 23, 2007 |work=Oldies.com}}</ref> Active between the years of 1992 and 2001, he has recorded three studio albums: two on [[Capitol Records Nashville|Capitol]]/[[Liberty Records]] (1992's ''Ricky Lynn Gregg'' and 1994's ''Get a Little Closer'') and one on Rowe Music Group (2001's ''Careful What You Wish For''). His first two albums produced three hit singles on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' country music charts, including the No.&nbsp;36-peaking "If I Had a Cheatin' Heart".

==Musical career==
Ricky Lynn Gregg was raised in [[Longview, Texas|Longview]], Texas, and began singing in the church at a very early age. His earliest influences were gospel and country. As a teenager in school Gregg was influenced by rock & roll and formed the "Ricky Lynn Gregg Project" playing in local venues around his hometown. In 1978 Gregg moved to [[Ft Worth]], Texas and began performing as guitarist and singer for a band known as "Savvy" with their debut album "Made In Texas" being released in 1982.<ref name="eagleaudiorecording">{{cite web |url=http://www.eagleaudiorecording.com/savvyalbum.html |title=Savvy's New Album Release |accessdate=April 5, 2015 |date=April 4, 1982 |work=eagleaudiorecording.com}}</ref> Gregg was also a member of [[Head East]] between 1984 and 1987. By 1992, Gregg was performing as a solo singer; the same year, he signed to [[Liberty Records]] and released his self titled debut album.<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web |url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p39168|pure_url=yes}} |title=allmusic ((( Ricky Lynn Gregg > Biography ))) |accessdate=August 23, 2007 |last=Seida |first=Linda |work=[[Allmusic]]}}</ref> The album produced a No.&nbsp;34 single in "If I Had a Cheatin' Heart", a cover of a [[Mel Street]] song.<ref name="allmusic"/><ref name="oldies"/> Following it were "Can You Feel It" and "Three Nickels and a Dime". In 1993, ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' ranked him at No.&nbsp;4 on their list of Top New Country Artists of the Year.<ref name="allmusic"/>

A second album, titled ''Get a Little Closer'', was released on Liberty in 1994, with its title track being the only single. One year later, Gregg's manager, [[Jimmy Bowen]], retired due to thyroid cancer. In 1997, Gregg found another manager, named Eddie Rhines, who helped the singer rebuild his fan base.<ref name="allmusic"/> By 2001, his third album, titled ''Careful What You Wish For'', was released on the then-newly established independent label Rowe Music Group (RMG).<ref name="allmusic"/>

Gregg has also begun a charity called Trail of Hope, which provides clothing for underprivileged [[Cherokee]], [[Choctaw]] and [[Sioux]] Indians.<ref name="allmusic"/>
