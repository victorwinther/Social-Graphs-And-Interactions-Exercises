'''Elvie Shane''' (born May 22, 1988) is an American [[country music]] singer. He is signed to [[BBR Music Group]] and in 2020 he released his debut single "[[My Boy (Elvie Shane song)|My Boy]]", which has charted on [[Country Airplay]].

Shane was born in [[Caneyville, Kentucky]], where he grew up singing in his church and listening to country music that his father played. After attending [[Western Kentucky University]], he dropped out of college to focus on music. He began performing publicly in 2012, which led to him competing on ''[[American Idol]]'' in 2016. Although he was eliminated in the first round, he released multiple songs online including "County Roads" and "My Boy". In 2020, he was signed to [[BBR Music Group|Wheelhouse]], which released "My Boy" to country radio.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/elvie-shane-mn0003938176/biography | title=Elvie Shane biography | publisher=AllMusic | accessdate=February 13, 2021}}</ref>

Shane wrote the song with Russell Sutton, Lee Starr, and Nick Columbia.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://tasteofcountry.com/elvie-shane-my-boy/ | title=Elvie Shane's 'My Boy' Tells a Loving Stepfather's Story [Listen] | work=Taste of Country | date=20 October 2020 | accessdate=February 13, 2021}}</ref> In December 2020, he released a music video which was directed by [[Peter Zavadil]].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.soundslikenashville.com/music/elvie-shane-honors-stepdads-my-boy-video/ | title=Elvie Shane Honors Stepdads in Moving 'My Boy' Video | website=Sounds Like Nashville | date=18 December 2020 | accessdate=February 13, 2021}}</ref> "My Boy" also made the [[Country Airplay]] charts, reaching the top 10 of the chart in September 2021.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/charts/country-airplay/2021-09-18|title=Country Airplay Songs: September 18, 2021|magazine=Billboard|access-date=September 21, 2021|url-access=subscription}}</ref>
