'''Andrew Warriner Combs'''<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.ascap.com/repertory#/ace/search/workID/906071628 |title=HIDE AND SEEK |website=ASCAP |publisher=American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers |access-date=March 13, 2023}}</ref> is an American singer, songwriter, and guitarist from [[Dallas, Texas]], who currently resides in [[Nashville, Tennessee]].

==Career==
Andrew Combs released his debut [[Extended play|EP]], ''Tennessee Time'', in May 2010, which has been compared to [[Mickey Newbury]] and [[Guy Clark]].<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.americansongwriter.com/2010/07/andrew-combs-tennessee-time/ | title = Andrew Combs: Tennessee Time | magazine = [[American Songwriter]] | first = David | last = Inman | date = July 27, 2010 | publisher = ForASong Media, LLC | access-date = June 1, 2015}}</ref> In April 2012, Combs released a vinyl 7-inch called "Big Bad Love".<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.nashvillescene.com/nashvillecream/archives/2012/04/10/pre-order-andrew-combs-big-bad-love-7-inch-hear-big-bad-love-and-take-it-from-me-now-fresh-tracks/ | first = D. Patrick | last = Rodgers | title = Pre-Order Andrew Combs' 'Big Bad Love' 7-Inch | date = April 10, 2012 | website = Nashville Scene | via = Nashvillescene.com | access-date = June 1, 2015}}</ref>

In July 2012, Combs signed to the Nashville-based music publishing arm of New York City record label, [[Razor & Tie]].<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.razorandtiemusicpublishing.com/artist/andrew-combs | archive-url = https://archive.today/20130201013506/http://www.razorandtiemusicpublishing.com/artist/andrew-combs | archive-date = February 1, 2013 | title = Razor & Tie artist page: Andrew Combs | publisher = Razorandtiemuscipublishing.com | access-date = June 1, 2015 | url-status = dead}}</ref>

In October 2012, Combs released ''Worried Man'', which ''American Songwriter'' gave a 4-star review, writing: "As singer/songwriter first albums go, it'll be tough to beat this as one of the years finest, from a newcomer who is hopefully just tapping into his talent."<ref>{{cite magazine | url = http://www.americansongwriter.com/2012/10/andrew-combs-worried-man/ | title = Andrew Combs: Worried Man | first = Hal | last = Horowitz | magazine = [[American Songwriter]] | date = October 30, 2012 | publisher = ForASong Media, LLC | access-date = June 1, 2015}}</ref>

In 2013, Combs supported [[Shovels & Rope]] and [[Caitlin Rose]] on national tours<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.songkick.com/artists/3428966-andrew-combs/gigography | title = Andrew Combs: Gigography | website = Songkick | via = Songkick.com | access-date = June 1, 2015}}</ref> and performed at the Newport Folk Festival<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.newportfolkfest.net/lineup/saturday/joe-fletcher | title = Nashville to Newport 2013 | publisher = NewporfFolkfest.net | access-date = June 1, 2015 | url-status = dead | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20130902023409/http://www.newportfolkfest.net/lineup/saturday/joe-fletcher | archive-date = September 2, 2013 }}</ref>

In 2015, Combs released ''All These Dreams'', his second studio album.

In 2016, he performed on the main stage at the [[C2C: Country to Country|Country to Country festival]] in the UK alongside [[Chris Stapleton]], [[Kacey Musgraves]] and [[Eric Church]].

In 2022, Combs performed on the first compilation released by 3Sirens, an East Nashville based indie label founded by [[The Grahams]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2022-06-24 |title=From Their Secret Studio in Nashville, The Grahams Cultivate a Community |url=https://thebluegrasssituation.com/read/from-their-secret-studio-in-nashville-the-grahams-cultivate-a-community/ |access-date=2023-12-11 |website=The Bluegrass Situation |language=en-US}}</ref> Appearing alongside [[Dylan LeBlanc]], [[The Grahams]] and [[Caitlin Rose]], Combs recorded a cover of Radiohead's 'High and Dry' for the special release.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2022-09-27 |title=Album Review: Andrew Combs - Sundays - |url=https://music.mxdwn.com/2022/09/27/reviews/album-review-andrew-combs-sundays/ |access-date=2023-12-11 |website=mxdwn Music |language=en-US}}</ref>
