'''Vernon Oxford''' (June 8, 1941 – August 18, 2023)<ref name=amg/> was an American [[country music]] singer and guitarist.

==Biography==
He was born in [[Rogers, Arkansas]], United States.<ref name=amg/> Oxford was raised in [[Wichita, Kansas]], United States, where his father played old-time [[fiddle]].<ref name=amg/> He began playing professionally in [[Utah]] in 1960, mostly playing in the Kansas area in the early 1960s, then relocated to [[Nashville]] in 1964.<ref name=amg>{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/vernon-oxford-mn0000270132/biography|title=Vernon Oxford &#124; Biography & History|website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=August 9, 2021}}</ref> In 1965, he met [[Harlan Howard]], who got him signed to [[RCA Victor]] and helped him find material to record.<ref name=amg/> He then released seven singles and one LP, ''Woman Let Me Sing You a Song''; none of them charted, and he was soon dropped from RCA.<ref name=amg/>

His career saw a resurgence in Britain, where he was first rediscovered in the middle of the 1970s.<ref name=amg/> A best-of was issued there in 1974, and RCA signed him again to tour there.<ref name=amg/> His hit singles "Shadows of My Mind", "Redneck (The Redneck National Anthem)", and "A Good Old Fashioned Saturday Night Honky Tonk Barroom Brawl" (U.S. Country No.&nbsp;55, 1977) reinvigorated his career in America; in Britain, he was well known for "I've Got to Get Peter Off Your Mind" and "Field of Flowers".<ref name=amg/> He also recorded a comedy duet with [[Jim Ed Brown]], "Mowing The Lawn".<ref name="LarkinCountry">{{cite book|title=[[Encyclopedia of Popular Music|The Guinness Who's Who of Country Music]]|editor=[[Colin Larkin (writer)|Colin Larkin]]|publisher=[[Guinness Publishing]]|date=1993|edition=First|isbn=0-85112-726-6|page=305}}</ref> After a break of several years, he began a career as a [[gospel music|gospel]] singer in 1981.<ref name=amg/>

==Recording career==
Mostly as an independent artist, Oxford continued to record new material. Oxford's classic recordings have been reissued by numerous labels, including a comprehensive box set from [[Bear Family Records]], and some compilations from Westside UK and the Morello label, which is a division of UK-based [[Cherry Red Records]].

==Acting career==
As an actor, Oxford appeared in several films including ''[[The Thing Called Love]]'' starring [[River Phoenix]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.25thframe.co.uk/movies/index.php|title=The Thing Called Love|website=25thframe.co.uk|access-date=August 9, 2021}}</ref> and [[Coal Miner's Daughter (film)|''Coal Miner's Daughter'']], a 1980 film about American singer [[Loretta Lynn]], who was portrayed by actress [[Sissy Spacek]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://en.notrecinema.com/communaute/stars/stars.php3?staridx=212812|title=Welcome on notreCinema|website=En.notrecinema.com|access-date=August 9, 2021}}</ref>
