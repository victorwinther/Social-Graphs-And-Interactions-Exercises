'''Kira Isabella Wilkie''' (born September 18, 1993) is a Canadian [[country music]] artist. Isabella was signed to [[Sony Music]] in 2009.<ref>{{cite web|title=Kira Isabella Biography|url=http://www.cmt.ca/artist/kira-isabella/|work=[[CMT (Canada)|CMT]]|accessdate=November 2, 2011}}</ref> Her first single, "Love Me Like That", was released in 2011 and charted on the [[Canadian Hot 100]]. Isabella released her second single in 2011 entitled "A Real Good Radio". Her third single, "[[A Little More Work]]", was released in 2012. Her debut studio album, ''[[Love Me Like That (album)|Love Me Like That]]'', was released in October 2012, and produced two further singles. Kira Isabella announced dates for a 2013 Canadian tour with [[Terri Clark]] in October 2012 and [[Carrie Underwood]] in December 2012.<ref name="kiraisabella.ca">{{cite web|url=http://www.kiraisabella.ca/ |title=The Official Kira Isabella Site |publisher=Kiraisabella.ca |date= |accessdate=2014-05-20}}</ref> In 2013, Isabella signed with U.S. record label HitShop Records.<ref>{{cite web|title=HitShop Records Signs Canadian Country Singer Kira Isabella|url=http://www.allaccess.com/net-news/archive/story/124514/hitshop-records-signs-canadian-country-singer-kira|work=All Access Music Group|accessdate=November 20, 2013}}</ref> The lead single from her second album, "[[Quarterback (song)|Quarterback]]", was released in spring 2014.

==Early life==

Kira Isabella began singing and playing the guitar at 7 years old, under the tutelage of her father and her singing teacher Trina Langthorne. Her ethnic background is [[Colombians|Colombian]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://instagram.com/p/BdzqeoCFN0E/ |title=Instagram post by Jim • Jan 11, 2018 at 11:19am UTC, Kira Isabella commenting on her heritage |website=Instagram |language=en |access-date=2018-01-21}}</ref> She was inspired by fellow Canadian country singer [[Shania Twain]] and her then recent album, ''[[Up! (album)|Up!]]'', to enter the country music industry. Her songwriting began development through writing poetry, beginning in the fourth grade.<ref name="torontowide.blogspot.ca">{{cite web|url=http://torontowide.blogspot.ca/2012/08/in-conversation-kira-isabella.html |title=Torontowide: Kira Isabella: In Conversation |publisher=Torontowide.blogspot.ca |date= |accessdate=2014-05-20}}</ref> At the age of 14, Kira Isabella won the Rising Star Award in the Female Open Category of a Blyth, Ontario, arts festival. The consequential media exposure that led well-known music producers to Kira Isabella would help launch her career as a country singer.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cmt.ca/artist/kira-isabella/ |title=Kira Isabella &#124; Biography, Videos, YouTube Videos, Music Downloads, Social Network Updates, And More |publisher=CMT.ca |date=2012-10-02 |accessdate=2014-05-20}}</ref>

==Influences==

Female country singers such as [[Faith Hill]], [[Sheryl Crow]], [[Shania Twain]] and [[Taylor Swift]] have been cited by Kira Isabella as strong musical influences.<ref name="torontowide.blogspot.ca"/>

==Tours and festivals==

To date, Kira Isabella has performed as a guest singer on two tours. From October to December 2012, she has worked with [[Carrie Underwood]] on her [[Blown Away Tour]] and in 2013, with [[Terri Clark]].<ref name="kiraisabella.ca"/> She will have performed in the [[Boots and Hearts Music Festival]] line-up for two years consecutively by August 2013. Her first headlining show was June 30, 2013 in Calabogie, Ontario at the Calabogie Country Music Fest.<ref name="kiraisabella.ca"/> She also performed at the free biweekly Peterborough Musicfest on July 31, 2013.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.ptbomusicfest.ca/index.php?option=com_jtagcalendar&eventId=11 |title=Peterborough Musicfest |publisher=Ptbomusicfest.ca |date=2013-07-31 |accessdate=2014-05-20}}</ref>
