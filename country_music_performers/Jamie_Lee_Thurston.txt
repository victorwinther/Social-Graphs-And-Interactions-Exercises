'''Jamie Lee Thurston''' is an American [[country music]] singer. He was raised in [[Waterbury, Vermont]] and performed with his father starting at age 15. After moving to Los Angeles, California, he moved again to [[Nashville, Tennessee]] in 1999.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.countryweekly.com/vault/jamie-lee-thurston-straight-shooter|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304052945/http://www.countryweekly.com/vault/jamie-lee-thurston-straight-shooter|archivedate=March 4, 2016|title=Jamie Lee Thurston: Straight Shooter|date=March 3, 2003|work=[[Country Weekly]]|accessdate=February 21, 2013}}</ref>

In 2003, he released the album ''I Just Wanna Do My Thing'' via View 2. The album included the single "It Can All Be Gone", which peaked at number 59 on the [[Hot Country Songs]] charts. He later signed to [[Warner Bros. Records]] Nashville, then to Country Thunder.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.countryaircheck.com/email_publication/CAT_02_20_07/|title=Country Aircheck|date=February 20, 2007|accessdate=February 21, 2013}}</ref>

Thurston wrote [[Rodney Atkins]]' 2009 single "[[15 Minutes (Rodney Atkins song)|15 Minutes]]", and cuts by [[Trace Adkins]] and [[Montgomery Gentry]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.jamieleethurston.com/bio.php |title=Bio |work=Jamie Lee Thurston |accessdate=February 21, 2013 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130115211604/http://www.jamieleethurston.com/bio.php |archivedate=January 15, 2013 }}</ref> In 2013, Thurston appeared on [[Game Show Network]]'s ''[[Family Trade]]'' to compose a jingle for G. Stone Motors in exchange for a new truck.<ref name="Family Trade">{{cite episode|series=Family Trade|network=[[Game Show Network]]|date=April 16, 2013|season=1|number=8}}</ref> The jingle, "We Trade for Anything" was also used as the show's opening credits theme music.<ref name="Family Trade"/>

Thurston's life is the basis of a screenplay co-written by Tim Rhys, the founder of ''[[MovieMaker]]'' magazine.
