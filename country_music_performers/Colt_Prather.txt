'''Colt Prather''' (born December 10, 1971, in [[El Paso, Texas]]<ref name="whitburn">{{cite book|last=Whitburn|first=Joel|title=Hot Country Songs 1944 to 2008|publisher=Record Research, Inc|page=328|year=2008|isbn=978-0-89820-177-2}}</ref>) is an American [[country music]] singer-songwriter. Prather was discovered playing with the house band at a [[Nashville, Tennessee|Nashville]] restaurant and signed to [[Epic Records]].<ref>{{cite news|title=BMI Visits Colt Prather and Friends|url=http://www.bmi.com/news/entry/233759|accessdate=July 11, 2011|newspaper=[[Broadcast Music Incorporated]]|date=June 24, 2003}}</ref> His third single for the label, "I Won't Go On and On," peaked at number 48 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs|Hot Country Singles & Tracks]] chart in early 2004.<ref name="whitburn" /> The song received a favorable review from Deborah Evans Price of ''Billboard'', who said that "Prather is the owner of a voice that should easily separate him from the rest of the pack" and "success in the country format is surely forthcoming."<ref>{{cite news|last=Price|first=Deborah Evans|title=''Billboard'' Picks|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=0A8EAAAAMBAJ&q=%22colt%20prather%22&pg=PA56|accessdate=July 11, 2011|newspaper=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=March 13, 2004}}</ref> By June 2004, Prather had exited the label.<ref>{{cite news|last=Mansfield|first=Brian|title=A breath of fresh country air|url=https://www.usatoday.com/life/music/news/2004-06-16-nashvillle-main_x.htm|accessdate=July 11, 2011|newspaper=[[USA Today]]|date=June 16, 2004}}</ref>
