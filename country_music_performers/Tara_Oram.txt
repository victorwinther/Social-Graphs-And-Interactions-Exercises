'''Tara Oram''' (born April 28, 1984) is a Canadian [[country music]] recording artist and TV personality.  She was a top six finalist on the [[Canadian Idol (Season 5)|fifth season]] of ''[[Canadian Idol]]'' in 2007. In 2009, Oram received the "Rising Star" [[Canadian Country Music Award]] and was nominated for a [[Juno Award]], for Country Album of the Year, for her debut album ''Chasing the Sun''.

==Early life==

Oram was born in [[Hare Bay, Newfoundland and Labrador|Hare Bay]], [[Newfoundland and Labrador|Newfoundland]] but moved frequently during her early years.  Oram first moved to the [[Milton, Ontario|Malton]] area of [[Mississauga, Ontario]] when she was 10 years old, and later to [[Brampton]], where she attended [[Bramalea Secondary School]].

== Career ==

At age 16 Oram was signed to indie label Wellcraft Music Group and released her first country music single called, "More Than I Dreamed" which was her first top 50 hit.  She later toured with a local agency called Performerz, which allowed her to sing in theatres, malls and festivals throughout Ontario.  When 19 Oram joined her first real band, Big Catch.  The band played the bar/club circuit and corporate events around Ontario for the better part of four years, performing a mix of country, top 40, rock, R'n B and retro. In 2007, Oram auditioned for the hit show [[Canadian Idol]], earning a 6th-place finish on the hit reality show.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://jam.canoe.ca/Television/TV_Shows/C/Canadian_Idol/2007/08/14/4418454-sun.html |archive-url= https://archive.today/20120710004054/http://jam.canoe.ca/Television/TV_Shows/C/Canadian_Idol/2007/08/14/4418454-sun.html |url-status= usurped |archive-date= July 10, 2012 |title=Oram booted from 'Canadian Idol' |author= Lindsey Ward |work= Canoe |date=August 14, 2007 |accessdate=May 23, 2011}}</ref> The next year, Oram signed her first major record deal with Open Road/Universal Music Canada and also appeared on reality TV series called, "The Tara Diaries" on [[Country Music Television|CMT]].<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.cmt.ca/Shows/thetaradiaries |title=Shows : The Tara Diaries |work= CMT |year=2011 |accessdate=May 23, 2011}}</ref> CMT filmed a Christmas special she hosted, "The Night Before Christmas".<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.openroadrecordings.com/2010/10/newfoundland-native-tara-oram-holds-benefit-for-communities-affected-by/ |title=Newfoundland Native Tara Oram Holds Benefit For Communities Affected by Hurricane Igor  |work=Open Road Recordings  |accessdate=May 23, 2011}}</ref> In October 2008, Oram's first show as a signed artist was opening for her Idol, [[Marty Stuart]] in Halifax, Nova Scotia.

In 2009, CMT and [[YTV (TV channel)|YTV]] Oram appeared a celebrity judge on the TV series, "Karaoke Star Junior".<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.cmt.ca/news/NewsItem.aspx?news_id=7974 |title= Tara Oram Loves Canada's Karaoke Kids |work=cmt.ca |date=January 31, 2009 |accessdate=May 23, 2011}}</ref>

On July 10, 2010, Oram was the opening act for [[Taylor Swift]] and performed at the second annual [[Cavendish Beach Music Festival]] in [[Cavendish, Prince Edward Island|Cavendish]], Prince Edward Island, singing to a crowd of more than 35,000 people.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.theguardian.pe.ca/Living/Commuting/2010-07-07/article-1522252/Fans-line-up-for-world-class-talent-in-Cavendish/1 |title=Fans line up for world-class talent in Cavendish - Commuting - the Guardian |access-date=August 1, 2010 |archive-date=March 7, 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120307171448/http://www.theguardian.pe.ca/Living/Commuting/2010-07-07/article-1522252/Fans-line-up-for-world-class-talent-in-Cavendish/1 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Oram became a judge on [[YTV (TV channel)|YTV's]] Canadian reality TV show, [[The Next Star]] since Season 5, which premiered July 18, 2012.

===Awards and nominations===
Oram received nominations for the 2009 East Coast Music Awards in the categories Female Solo Recording of the Year and Video of the Year, and won in the Country Recording of the Year award.<ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.cbc.ca/news/entertainment/hey-rosetta-triumphant-at-east-coast-music-awards-1.779468 |title=Hey Rosetta! triumphant at East Coast Music Awards |work= CBC|date= March 1, 2009 |access-date=May 23, 2011}}</ref>   
She also received a CRMA (Canadian Radio Music Award) nomination as Best New Group/Solo Artist. 
On February 2, 2009 Oram was nominated for a Juno Award in the Country Recording of the Year category. 
Oram won the 2009 CCMA Rising Star award.  She was also nominated for Rising Star, Female Artist of the Year and Video of the Year.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.channelcanada.com/Article3247.html |title= CMT Congratulates The 2009 Canadian Country Music Awards {{as written|Nomenees}} |work=channelcanada.com |date=July 29, 2009 |accessdate=May 23, 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100104235833/https://www.channelcanada.com/Article3247.html|archive-date=January 4, 2010}}</ref>

In 2015, Tara Oram received a Star on Brampton's Walk of Fame.{{Citation needed|date=August 2017}}
