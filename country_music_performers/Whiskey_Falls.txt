'''Whiskey Falls''' was an American country music group composed of [[Seven Volpone|Seven Williams]] (lead vocals, guitar), [[Wally Brandt]] (guitar, vocals) [[Damon Johnson]] (guitar, vocals), and [[Buck Johnson (musician)|Buck Johnson]] (lead vocals, [[mandolin]], guitar, keyboards). Seven Williams and Wally Brandt were former members of [[Seven and the Sun]], while Damon Johnson was once the frontman of the [[Southern rock]] band [[Brother Cane]] and a guitarist for [[Alice Cooper]]. Whiskey Falls released one [[Whiskey Falls (album)|self titled album]] for [[Midas Records Nashville]] in 2007 before disbanding.

==Biography==
The band's lead singer is [[Seven Volpone|Seven Williams]] (born Keith Volpone). Whiskey Falls' other three members, Wally Brandt, Buck Johnson, and Damon Johnson (the latter two are no relation), all sing lead and harmony vocals and play guitar, and Buck Johnson plays keyboard as well.<ref name="gac">{{cite web |url=http://www.gactv.com/gac/ar_artists_a-z/article/0,,GAC_26071_5667674,00.html |title= Artists A to Z: Whiskey Falls: Biography |accessdate=2007-09-23 |work=[[Great American Country|GAC.com]]}}</ref> In 2007, Whiskey Falls signed to a recording contract with [[Midas Records Nashville]] label. Their self-titled debut album produced two Top 40 singles on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs]] charts in "Last Train Running" (#32)<ref>{{cite web|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=hQ4EAAAAMBAJ&q=we3kings+billboard+last+train+running&pg=PA85 |title=Billboard - Google Books |date=2007-09-22 |accessdate=2014-08-01}}</ref> and "Falling Into You" (#40).<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://archive.org/details/bub_gb_RxMEAAAAMBAJ |page=[https://archive.org/details/bub_gb_RxMEAAAAMBAJ/page/n46 47] |quote=we3kings billboard falling into you. |title=Billboard - Internet Archive |publisher=Internet Archive |date=2008-03-01 |accessdate=2014-08-01}}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://archive.org/details/bub_gb_RxMEAAAAMBAJ |page=[https://archive.org/details/bub_gb_RxMEAAAAMBAJ/page/n35 36] |quote=whiskey falls billboard falling into you. |title=Billboard - Internet Archive |publisher=Internet Archive |date=2008-03-01 |accessdate=2014-08-01}}</ref>

In the short time together as a band, Whiskey Falls was named 2008's “Breakout Band of the Year” by Nashville's Music Row, has been featured in People Magazine, USA Weekend, Billboard Magazine and Country Weekly.  They have also made appearances on national television including performances on The Early Show, The Jimmy Kimmel Show, Extra!, GAC,<ref name="toughestcowboy.com">{{cite web |url=http://www.toughestcowboy.com/newsarticle.aspx?n=39 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120814122527/http://www.toughestcowboy.com/newsarticle.aspx?n=39 |archivedate=2012-08-14 |title=Whiskey Falls joins the 2009 Toughest Cowboy Tour}}</ref> and at several NASCAR pre-race events,<ref>{{cite web|author= |url=http://archive.paddocktalk.com/story-68457.html |title=Whiskey Falls Headlines Race Week Line-up at JR Motorsports|publisher=Archive.paddocktalk.com |date= |accessdate=2014-08-01}}</ref> as well as being featured in television commercials for AAMCO Transmissions.<ref>{{cite web|author= |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5gwoF1JT_SY&list=PLB8CA03DE5B41F88C  |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211221/5gwoF1JT_SY |archive-date=2021-12-21 |url-status=live|title=AAMCO - "I've Got a Guy" - Feat. Whiskey Falls |publisher=YouTube |date=2011-11-01 |accessdate=2014-08-01}}{{cbignore}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://digitalrodeo.com/users/whiskeyfallsfanpage |title=WhiskeyFallsFanPage |publisher=digitalrodeo.com |date= |accessdate=2014-08-01}}</ref>

The group and their song "Load Up the Bases (The Baseball Song)" are featured in promos for [[Atlanta Braves]] telecasts on [[FSN South]] and [[SportSouth]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vBBgwOBxUKg  |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211221/vBBgwOBxUKg |archive-date=2021-12-21 |url-status=live|title=TBS - Atlanta Braves (MLB) Feat. Whiskey Falls "Load Up the Bases" |publisher=YouTube |date=2011-11-01 |accessdate=2014-08-01}}{{cbignore}}</ref> as well as for [[Colorado Rockies]] telecasts on [[FSN Rocky Mountain]]. This song also charted at No.&nbsp;55 on the country music charts from unsolicited airplay during the 2007 World Series. It was re-released again in 2008 and re-entered at No.&nbsp;55 on the country charts from unsolicited airplay during the 2008 World Series.

In the summer of 2009, Damon Johnson returned to the [[Alice Cooper]] Band and was featured in Cooper's 2010 live concert DVD & CD, "Theatre of Death - Live At Hammersmith". Johnson worked his fifth tour with Alice Cooper in 2011, and departed the band in September of that year to become a full member of [[Thin Lizzy]]. Cooper is also credited with assisting greatly in Johnson's beloved golf game, as the two played hundreds of rounds in their years of touring the world together.

In December 2009, Damon returned for a brief stint with Whiskey Falls to join the Toughest Cowboy Tour. The band also penned the theme song for Mark Burnett's show of the same title on [[Spike (TV channel)]].<ref name="toughestcowboy.com"/>{{Full citation needed|date=August 2013}}

in December 2011, the band got together to record the Christmas single, "Rudolph Guide My Way" for online downloading.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/rudolph-guide-my-way-single/id487601793 |title=iTunes - Music - Rudolph Guide My Way - Single by Whiskey Falls |publisher=Itunes.apple.com |date=2011-12-06 |accessdate=2014-08-01}}</ref>

Seven Williams and Wally Brandt have since dedicated their time to their music production company, We3Kings Music writing and producing music for television and film.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/431303/whiskey+falls/chart |title=Whiskey Falls - Chart history |magazine=Billboard |accessdate=2014-08-01}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gactv.com/gac/ar_artists_a-z/article/0,,gac_26071_5667674,00.html |title=Artists : Whiskey Falls : Whiskey Falls Biography : Great American Country |publisher=Gactv.com |date=2014-06-27 |accessdate=2014-08-01}}</ref> They have since written the theme songs to NBC's The John Walsh Show, Fox's [[Tru calling|Tru Calling]] and [[The Simple Life]] and ABC's The Two-Timer,<ref name="ascap.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.ascap.com/playback/2004/june/new-members.aspx |title=New Members |publisher=Ascap.com |date=2004-06-01 |accessdate=2014-08-01}}</ref> NBC's [[Minute to win it|Minute to Win It]], [[Tabatha's Salon Takeover|Tabatha's Salon Makeover]] to [[Sarah Palin's Alaska]], [[Dance Moms]] and Ryan & Tatum: The O’Neals.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.sesac.com/News/Article.aspx/2012/Film%20and%20TV%20Composers/We%203%20Kings |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130630175923/http://www.sesac.com/News/Article.aspx/2012/Film%20and%20TV%20Composers/We%203%20Kings |archivedate=2013-06-30 |title=We 3 Kings}}</ref> They have also written cues and promos for several TV shows including NBC's [[Las Vegas (TV series)|Las Vegas]], [[Starting Over (TV series)|Starting Over]], [[Access Hollywood]] and [[Entertainment Tonight]]. Their major motion picture credits include [[America's Sweethearts]], [[Summer Catch]] and Columbia Pictures feature The Quest.<ref name="ascap.com"/> and most recently, 20th Century Fox [[Diary of a Wimpy Kid: Dog Days (film)|Diary of a Wimpy Kid:Dog Days]].  In 2013 after a legal battle with Wally Brandt, Seven Williams left We 3 Kings.
