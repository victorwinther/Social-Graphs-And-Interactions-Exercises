'''Ashton Delilah Shepherd''' (born August 16, 1986)<ref name="allmusic"/> is an American [[country music]] singer-songwriter. She was first signed to [[Universal Music Group Nashville]]'s MCA Nashville division in April 2007. Her 2008 debut album ''[[Sounds So Good]]'' produced two top 40 hits on the [[Hot Country Songs]] charts: "[[Takin' Off This Pain]]" and the [[Sounds So Good (song)|title track]]. In 2011, she released her second album, ''[[Where Country Grows]]'', which includes the top 20 hit "[[Look It Up (song)|Look It Up]]".

==Biography==
Ashton Shepherd was born in [[Coffeeville, Alabama]], United States. She began singing at an early age, and started writing songs at age five.<ref name="allmusic"/> By age eight, she had entered her first talent competition, where she sang [[Patsy Cline]] songs, and soon afterward, she began performing at various local venues as well. Her parents helped her finance a self-titled CD, which she released independently at age fifteen.<ref name="allmusic"/> In June 2006, she entered and won a talent contest in [[Gilbertown, Alabama]]. The prize was being the opening act for [[Lorrie Morgan]] in concert. Shepherd won the opening slot, and while performing as Morgan's opener, she was spotted by a representative of [[Universal Music Group Nashville|MCA Nashville Records]], who then signed her to the label in April 2007.<ref name="universal">{{cite web |url=http://www.countrystandardtime.com/news/newsitem.asp?xid=538 |title=Universal signs Ashton Shepherd to deal |accessdate=November 12, 2007 |date=April 1, 2007|work=[[Country Standard Time]]}}</ref>

Her debut single, "[[Takin' Off This Pain]]," was released in September 2007, the same month in which she made her debut on the [[Grand Ole Opry]].<ref name="alabama">{{cite web |url=http://www.southalabamian.com/news/2007/0927/Front_Page/002.html |title=Leroy resident to appear on Grand Ole' (sic) Opry |accessdate=November 12, 2007 |last=Pickard |first=Kathryn F. |year=2007 |work=The South Alabamian |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20071013094650/http://southalabamian.com/news/2007/0927/Front_Page/002.html |archivedate=October 13, 2007 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.clarkecountydemocrat.com/news/2007/1004/Community/059.html |title=Ashton wows Grand Ole Opry with her premier performance |work=The Clarke County Democrat |access-date=November 12, 2007 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081011090520/http://www.clarkecountydemocrat.com/news/2007/1004/community/059.html |archive-date=October 11, 2008 |url-status=dead }}</ref> The song, which peaked at No.&nbsp;20 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs]] charts, was the first single from her debut album ''[[Sounds So Good]]'', which was produced by Buddy Cannon and released on March 4, 2008.<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web |url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p988912|pure_url=yes}} |title=Ashton Shepherd biography |accessdate=April 8, 2008 |last=Leggett |first=Steve |work=[[AllMusic|Allmusic]]}}</ref> She also appeared on [[CMT (American TV channel)|CMT]]'s Unplugged at Studio 330 television program and subsequent interview March 5, 2008.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.countrymusicnewsblog.com/ashton-shepherd/ |title=Ashton Shepherd offers up CMT Exclusive interview |accessdate=March 27, 2008 |date=March 2, 2008|work=Country Music News Blog}}</ref> The album's second single, its [[Sounds So Good (song)|title track]], was released in May 2008 and peaked at No.&nbsp;21.

Shepherd's third single, "[[Look It Up (song)|Look It Up]]," was released to country radio on January 3, 2011, as the lead-off single to her second album for MCA Nashville, ''[[Where Country Grows]]''. It debuted at number 56 on the country charts for chart week ending January 15, 2011.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=http://www.billboard.com/#/column/chartbeat/chart-highlights-pop-rock-dance-club-play-1004137695.story|title=Chart Highlights: Pop, Rock, Dance/Club Play Songs & More|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|publisher=[[Prometheus Global Media]]|accessdate=January 3, 2011| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20110103102738/http://www.billboard.com/| archivedate= January 3, 2011 | url-status= live}}</ref> The song entered the Top 40 of the charts in its third week and became her second Top 20 hit on the chart, reaching a peak of number 19 in May 2011. The [[Where Country Grows (song)|title track]] was released as the second and final single from the album, and reached a peak of number 42 on the charts, becoming her first single to miss the Top 30. Shepherd parted ways with MCA Nashville in mid-2012.<ref name=split>{{cite web|url=http://social.entertainment.msn.com/music/blogs/one-country-blogpost.aspx?post=a9a20672-1f82-4c8d-8d4d-19edcf73fe9a |title=Label Roster Changes Involve Lee Ann Womack And Other Female Acts |work=[[MSN]] |accessdate=August 15, 2012 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120816024519/http://social.entertainment.msn.com/music/blogs/one-country-blogpost.aspx?post=a9a20672-1f82-4c8d-8d4d-19edcf73fe9a |archivedate=August 16, 2012 }}</ref>

Shepherd also performed backup vocals on "Tailgate Blues", a track on [[Luke Bryan]]'s 2011 album, ''[[Tailgates & Tanlines]]''.

After ending her relationship with MCA Nashville, Shepherd started her own company to pursue releasing music independently.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ashtonshepherd.altimarc.com/ |title=Welcome to Ashton Shepherd &#124; Ashton Shepherd |accessdate=May 26, 2013 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130527161439/http://ashtonshepherd.altimarc.com/ |archivedate=May 27, 2013 }}</ref> Her third studio album, ''This Is America'', was released on August 16, 2013, via Shepherd's own Pickin' Shed Records. The title track was released as the lead single in July 2013.

Shepherd released her fourth studio album, ''Out of My Pocket'', on June 14, 2016.
