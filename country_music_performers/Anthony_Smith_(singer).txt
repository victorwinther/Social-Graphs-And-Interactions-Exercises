'''Ralph Anthony Smith''' is an American singer, songwriter and record producer. As a Nashville recording artist, Smith's debut album, ''[[If That Ain't Country]]'', on [[Mercury Records Nashville]] . Produced three Top 40 singles on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' Hot Country Singles & Tracks (now [[Hot Country Songs]]) charts.
As a songwriter Smith has had over 250 songs recorded by other Nashville recording artist Such as George Strait, Blake Shelton, Tim McGraw, Rascal Flatts, Trace Adkins, Faith Hill and more.

==Biography==
Anthony Smith was raised in eastern Tennessee<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.countrystandardtime.com/d/article.asp?xid=760 |title=If that ain't country, that ain't Anthony Smith |accessdate=August 23, 2008 |last=MacIntosh |first=Dan |date=August 2002 |work=[[Country Standard Time]]}}</ref> His musical interests began at the early age of 5. At age nine, he had joined his first band, and by age twelve he was arranging music at his church.<ref name="guild">[http://www.songwritersguild.com/smith_interview.htm Smith, Writer of Hits for George Strait, Tim McGraw, Faith Hill, Trace Adkins, Montgomery Gentry, Rascal Flatts and Many More, Shares Advice With SGA Members] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070503220432/http://www.songwritersguild.com/smith_interview.htm |date=May 3, 2007 }}</ref> Smith moved to [[Kentucky]] in his early 20s and joined several bands. Luke Lewis, the boss of [[Mercury Nashville]] Records, received a demo tape of Smith's music, and signed the artist to a record deal.<ref name="media">[{{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p529055/biography|pure_url=yes}} allmusic ((( Anthony Smith > Biography )))]</ref> Meanwhile, Smith wrote songs for other artists, including "[[Run (George Strait song)|Run]] and "[[Cowboys Like Us]]" by [[George Strait]], "[[I'm Tryin']]" and "[[Chrome (Trace Adkins song)|Chrome]]" by [[Trace Adkins]], "Didn't I" and "[[What Do Ya Think About That]]" by [[Montgomery Gentry]], and "What Brothers Do" by [[Confederate Railroad]].<ref name="media"/> "My Worst Fear" for [[Rascal Flatts]] "[[Kristofferson (song)|Kristofferson]]" for [[Tim McGraw]] "Chasing Down a Good Time" by [[Randy Houser]]

Smith's debut album was released on Mercury Nashville, the same year that he was granted a songwriting deal from [[Broadcast Music Incorporated|BMI]].<ref>[http://www.bmi.com/news/entry/200028 BMI.com | Anthony Smith]</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://countrymusic.about.com/cs/productreviews/gr/aaprasitac.htm |title=Anthony Smith – If That Ain't Country |access-date=June 15, 2007 |archive-date=December 8, 2008 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081208062333/http://countrymusic.about.com/cs/productreviews/gr/aaprasitac.htm |url-status=dead }}</ref> The album, titled ''[[If That Ain't Country]]'', produced three Top 40 singles, of which the title track was the highest-charting. After exiting Mercury, Smith continued to write songs for other artists, including "[[Cowboys Like Us]]", another single release by George Strait. In 2007, two other singles that Smith co-wrote entered the country charts "Daisy" by [[Halfway to Hazard]], and "[[What Do Ya Think About That]]", another release by Montgomery Gentry. In 2008, [[Tim McGraw]] charted in the Top 20 with "[[Kristofferson (song)|Kristofferson]]", which Smith co-wrote with [[Reed Nielsen]]. In 2016, released the Houser single "Chasing Down a Good Time" which Smith co-wrote with [[Jeffrey Steele]]. Later that year, he signed to a second recording contract, this time with [[Stroudavarious Records]]. His first single for the label, "Bringin' Back the Sunshine", was released in July 2009, and debuted at No.&nbsp;60 on the Hot Country Songs chart. Smith also released an [[extended play]] for the label, then left in early 2010 to form his own label, Krankit Records. His first release on this label was "Love Is Love Is Love."<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.rodeoattitude.com/spur/countrymusic/headlines/anthony-smith-launches-ne-3827.shtml |title=Anthony Smith Launches New Label Based In Nashville |access-date=February 5, 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111005181343/http://www.rodeoattitude.com/spur/countrymusic/headlines/anthony-smith-launches-ne-3827.shtml |archive-date=October 5, 2011 |url-status=dead }}</ref>
