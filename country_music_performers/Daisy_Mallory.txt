'''Daisy Mallory''' (born May 19, 1993) is a [[country music]] singer-songwriter based in [[Nashville, Tennessee]].

==Early life==
Daisy Mallory grew up in [[Cave Creek, Arizona]].  She started playing guitar and writing songs at the age of nine.<ref>Johnson, Amanda. [http://www.digitalrodeo.com/articles/656 "CD Reviews"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100920015620/http://digitalrodeo.com/articles/656 |date=September 20, 2010 }}. Digital Rodeo. September 24, 2010.</ref> She performed for the first time at age twelve, at a benefit concert for [[Hurricane Katrina]] victims held in her hometown.<ref name="bio">[http://daisymallory.com/bio/ "Biography"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303200842/http://www.daisymallory.com/bio/ |date=March 3, 2016 }}. Daisy Mallory official site. September 23, 2010.</ref>

When she was thirteen, Mallory attended [[Seth Riggs]]'s Vocal Camp in Los Angeles.  She successfully auditioned to take part in a master class, and got positive feedback from the judges after her performance.  She began playing more shows in her area, and recorded her first EP in [[Phoenix, Arizona]].<ref name="bio" />

==Career==
Mallory began making connections with songwriters in her local chapter of [[Nashville Songwriters Association International|NSAI]], as well as making frequent trips to Nashville. She also recorded her second EP, "Just Sayin,'" which was released in June of that year.<ref name="bio" />

In May 2009, Mallory performed at her first showcase at The Rutledge in Nashville.

Her EP, "Girl Time," was released in early 2010.<ref name="cdbaby">[http://www.cdbaby.com/Artist/DaisyMallory "Artist Page: Daisy Mallory"]. CDBaby. September 24, 2010.</ref> Its debut single, "Do You Think Of Me," was produced by Jeff Dayton, who has also worked with [[George Strait]] and [[Glen Campbell]].<ref>[http://www.mi2n.com/press.php3?press_nb=132362 "Singer-Songwriter Daisy Mallory Releases Debut Single, "Do You Think Of Me?", To Country Radio"]. Music Industry News Network. September 23, 2010.</ref> The music video for "Do You Think of Me" was shot in March 2010.  It has received placement on CMT.com and GACtv.com,<ref>[http://thatnashvillesound.blogspot.com/2010/08/up-coming-new-nashville-daisy-mallory.html "Up & Coming New Nashville â€“ Daisy Mallory"]. That Nashville Sound. September 23, 2010.</ref> and has appeared on Yallwire's Top 100 charts in September.<ref>[http://www.yallwire.com/topvideos.php "Yallwire Top Videos"]. Yallwire. September 23, 2010.</ref>

Her notable performances in the spring of 2010 include performing at [[Muhammad Ali]]'s Celebrity Fight Night, alongside [[Reba McEntire]], [[David Foster]], and [[Kris Kristofferson]], as well as singing the national anthem for a [[Phoenix Coyotes]] game.<ref>Kodras, Marmy. [http://www.blueprintblog.org/content/what-daisy-mallory "What Is Daisy Mallory Up To?"]. Blueprint Education. September 23, 2010.</ref>

In April 2010, Mallory and her family moved to Nashville to continue pursuing her career.  In June, she was the first artist to be signed to new record label FrontWater Records.<ref>[http://www.allaccess.com/net-news/archive/story/78558/frontwater-records-launches-with-artist-daisy-mall "Frontwater Records Launches With Artist Daisy Mallory"]. AllAccess.com. September 23, 2010.</ref> She continues to perform in local venues and promote her current single.

She has had her songs cut by artists [[Jess Moskaluke]], [[Riana Nel]], [[Kenny Foster]], and more. In January 2017, Daisy began playing acoustic guitar and singing background vocals for country artist [[Lorrie Morgan]].
