'''Mica Roberts''' (born in [[Locust Grove, Oklahoma]]<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web |url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p397676|pure_url=yes}} |title=Mica Roberts biography |accessdate=2008-04-26 |last=Leggett |first=Steve |work=[[Allmusic]]}}</ref>) is an American [[country music]] artist signed to [[Show Dog Nashville]], a label owned by country singer [[Toby Keith]].

In 2008, she released her debut single, "Things a Mama Don't Know", a duet with [[Toby Keith]]. The song was the lead-off single to her debut EP, ''Days You Live For''. The song only managed to peak at No.&nbsp;55 on the U.S. ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs]] chart in 2008. It would be nearly a year before the release of her second single and album. In September 2009, the EP was released on September 15, via [[Show Dog Nashville]]. The second single "Days You Live For" was released in October 2009, where it debuted at No.&nbsp;60 for the week of October 17, 2009.

==Music career==
Roberts has performed background vocals for [[Faith Hill]], and has toured with [[Jessica Andrews]] and [[Martina McBride]].<ref name="allmusic"/> She also sang backing vocals on two tracks from Andrews' 2003 album ''[[Now (Jessica Andrews album)|Now]]''.<ref name="liner">{{Cite AV media notes |title=Now |others=Jessica Andrews |year=2003 |type=CD booklet |publisher=DreamWorks Records |id=50356}}</ref> Eventually, she caught the attention of [[Willie Nelson]], who recommended her to Toby Keith; she joined his backing band, Easy Money, as a guest musician.<ref name="allmusic"/> By 2008, Roberts was signed to Keith's Show Dog label, and released her debut single "Things a Mama Don't Know" that year.<ref name="allmusic"/> The song only managed to peak at No.&nbsp;55 on the U.S. ''Billboard'' [[Hot Country Songs]] chart.

She and Keith also sang the song "Let's Get Trashed" on the soundtrack to the 2008 film ''[[Beer for My Horses (film)|Beer for My Horses]]'', in which Keith starred.

On September 15, 2009, she released an EP entitled ''Day You Live For''.<ref name="Allmusic">{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=album|id=r1662927|pure_url=yes}}|title=''Days You Live For'' - Mica Roberts|publisher=[[Allmusic]]|accessdate=2009-11-01}}</ref> The EP included "Things a Mama Don't Know", as well as her second single, the title track. The latter song would debut at No.&nbsp;60 on the country charts for the week of October 17, 2009 before falling off. It later re-entered the chart at No.&nbsp;57 for the week of November 14, 2009.<ref name="Days You Live For">{{cite web|url={{BillboardURLbyName|artist=mica roberts|chart=all}}|title=Mica Roberts: "Days You Live For"|publisher=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|accessdate=2009-11-01}}</ref>

Roberts worked with [[Journey (band)|Journey]] band member [[Jonathan Cain]] in 2006 by performing a duet with him on the track "[[Faithfully (song)|Faithfully]]", which is a cover version of the song made famous by Journey in the 1980s. The track appears on Cain's solo album, "Where I Live".
