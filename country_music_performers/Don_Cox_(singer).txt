'''Don Cox''' (born January 14, 1964, in [[Belhaven, North Carolina]]<ref name="whitburn">{{cite book|last=Whitburn|first=Joel|title=Hot Country Songs 1944 to 2008|publisher=Record Research, Inc|page=106|year=2008|isbn=978-0-89820-177-2}}</ref>) is an American [[country music]] singer.

Cox began his career as a member of the [[Super Grit Cowboy Band]].<ref name="whitburn" /> Between 1994 and 1996, he released two albums on [[Step One Records]].<ref>{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p203718|pure_url=yes}}|title=Don Cox|work=[[Allmusic]]|accessdate=May 14, 2012}}</ref> His 1994 single "All Over Town" peaked at number 53 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs|Hot Country Singles & Tracks]] chart.<ref name="whitburn" />

Cox unsuccessfully ran for election as a [[Beaufort County, North Carolina|Beaufort County]] [[County commission|commissioner]] in 2014<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.witn.com/home/headlines/Deatherage-Upset-In-Beaufort-County-Commissioner-Race-258218451.html |title=Deatherage Upset In Beaufort County Commissioner Race |publisher=WITN |date=May 6, 2014 }}</ref> and 2016.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://beaufortncboe.org/article/march-15th-2016-republican-county-commissioner-recount-results |title=March 15th, 2016 Republican County Commissioner Recount Results |publisher=Beaufort County Board of Elections |date=March 23, 2016 }}</ref> In 2019 he ran unsuccessfully in the [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party]] primary for the North Carolina [[2019 North Carolina's 3rd congressional district special election|3rd congressional district special election.  Cox finished in a distant 15th place out of 17 candidates garnering 251 votes out of 42,175 votes cast or 0.6 of the vote]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2019/04/30/us/elections/results-north-carolina-house-3-special-primary.html |title=Election 2019: North Carolina Special Election Results: 2 Republicans, 1 Democrat Advance |newspaper=The New York Times |date=May 7, 2019 }}</ref>
