'''Regina Regina''' was an American [[country music]] duo formed in 1995. The duo consisted of female singers Regina Leigh and Regina Nicks, respectively natives of [[North Carolina]] and [[Lufkin, Texas]].<ref name="whitburn">{{cite book|last=Whitburn|first=Joel|title=Hot Country Songs 1944 to 2008|publisher=Record Research, Inc|date=2008|page=347|isbn=978-0-89820-177-2}}</ref> Leigh was formerly a backing vocalist in [[Reba McEntire]]'s road band, while Nicks was a personal assistant for McEntire and a former member of [[Dave & Sugar]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=2199&dat=19970305&id=ZZgyAAAAIBAJ&pg=6496,2485489|title=Regina Regina hopes pairing pays off|last=Patterson|first=Jim|date=5 March 1997|work=[[Lawrence Journal-World]]|accessdate=11 April 2013}}</ref> The two met through the assistance of producer [[Wally Wilson]], who wanted to include Nicks in a female duo. Nicks felt that she was unable to find a suitable singing partner until Leigh heard one of her demos and decided that she wanted to work with Nicks. The two were brought to [[Giant Records (Warner)|Giant Records]] then-president [[James Stroud]] in 1995.<ref>{{cite magazine|last=Flippo|first=Chet|date=December 14, 1996|title=Giant Squares Away Debut Album from Duo Regina Regina|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|pages=24, 26|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=tQkEAAAAMBAJ&dq=%22regina+regina%22+%22giant%22&pg=PA24}}</ref>

Regina Regina released its only album in 1997. The duo's only charting single in the U.S. was "More Than I Wanted to Know", which peaked at No.&nbsp;53 on ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''{{'}}s [[Hot Country Songs|Hot Country Singles & Tracks]].<ref name="whitburn"/>
