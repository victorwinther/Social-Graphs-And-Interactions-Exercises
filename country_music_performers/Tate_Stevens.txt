'''Stephen "Tater" Eatinger'''<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thexfactorusa.com/contestants/season_2/tate-stevens |title=Tate Stevens |accessdate=2012-12-04}}</ref><ref name=EngleOct>{{cite news|last=Engle|first=Tim|title=Thanks to 'X Factor,' Belton's Tate Stevens is on the road to fame|url=http://www.kansascity.com/2012/10/12/3864267/thanks-to-x-factor-tate-stevens.html|accessdate=22 December 2012|newspaper=The Kansas City Star|date=Oct 12, 2012}}</ref> (born March 1, 1975),<ref>{{cite web|title=Tate Stevens Bio|url=http://hollywoodlife.com/celeb/tate-stevens/|accessdate=22 December 2012}}</ref> known by his stage name '''Tate Stevens''', is an American [[country music]] artist, who won the [[The X Factor (U.S. season 2)|second season]] of the U.S. ''[[The X Factor (U.S. TV series)|The X Factor]]'' in 2012, securing a $5 million recording contract with [[Syco Music]] and [[RCA Records Nashville]].

After touring with a band for several years immediately after high school, Stevens returned to Missouri to settle down and raise his young family, working in the city of Raymore.  He joined a local band in 2005 and formed his own band in 2008, releasing an independent album and touring on weekends and holidays.  In early 2012, he auditioned for ''The X Factor'', progressing through the various stages of the competition, until winning on December 20, 2012. He released his debut album in 2013.

==Early life==
Stevens moved from Texas to [[Belton, Missouri]] as a child and grew up there, graduating from [[Belton High School (Belton, Missouri)|Belton High School]] in 1994. His father Steve had played the drums with a country band in Texas.<ref name=EngleOct /> He also was a construction worker.

== Music career ==

===1994–2000: Career beginnings and Dixie Cadillacs===
Stevens adopted his stage name when he was a young singer in the 1990s. He released a single in 1994 entitled "It Sure Looks Good On You".<ref>{{cite web|title=It Sure Looks Good On You - Single|url=https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/sure-looks-good-on-you/id577521463|work=[[iTunes]]|accessdate=22 December 2012}}</ref> Immediately after graduating high school, he began to tour as lead singer for a country band of five called the '''Dixie Cadillacs'''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Dixie Cadillacs|url=http://delafont.com/music_acts/dixie-cadillacs.htm|accessdate=22 December 2012|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130120111657/http://delafont.com/music_acts/dixie-cadillacs.htm|archivedate=20 January 2013}}</ref> The band released an album in June 1996 under Calf Creek Records under their band name.<ref>{{cite web|title=Dixie Cadillacs Audio CD|url=https://www.amazon.com/Dixie-Cadillacs/dp/B00000DQ5M|work=[[Amazon.com]]|accessdate=22 December 2012}}</ref> After touring for several years, Stevens returned to Missouri to settle down and raise his young family, working for the city of [[Belton, Missouri|Belton]], [[Missouri]] taking a construction job and later a job in the city's water department and later its street department repairing roads.<ref name=EngleOct/>

===2005–2008: Outlaw Junkies and Tate Stevens Band===
Around 2005, he became the lead singer of the band '''Outlaw Junkies''' with Steve Koken and Robert Harmon.<ref>{{cite news|last=Burke|first=Eric|title=Tate Stevens' Band Mates Reminisce About Honky Tonk Hay Day|url=http://fox4kc.com/2012/12/05/tate-stevens-band-mates-reminisce-about-honky-tonk-hay-days/|accessdate=22 December 2012|newspaper=FOX4 in Kansas City|date=December 5, 2012}}</ref> He left that band in 2008 to form the '''Tate Stevens Band''', a six-man ensemble that has toured extensively in the Midwest on weekends and vacations. He also wrote songs and released an independent album in 2008.<ref name=EngleOct/>

===2012: ''The X Factor''===
In 2012, Stevens auditioned  in [[Kansas City, Missouri]] for [[The X Factor (U.S. season 2)|season 2]] of ''The X Factor'' singing [[Randy Houser]]'s "[[Anything Goes (Randy Houser song)|Anything Goes]]" in front of judges [[L.A. Reid]], [[Demi Lovato]], [[Britney Spears]] and temporary judge [[Louis Walsh]], who was sitting in for [[Simon Cowell]]. He was selected to go to boot camp where he had to sing for survival singing "[[Nobody Knows (Tony Rich song)|Nobody Knows]]" against Willie Jones. He was selected to be part of the judges' houses stage competing in the top 24 in the "Over 25s" category and was mentored by L.A. Reid. He sang "Back At One" at Reid's home in front of him and guest judges [[Justin Bieber]] and [[Scooter Braun]]. Reid ultimately chose Stevens as a part of his top four.

On the first live show he sang "Tough", a Craig Morgan song and was saved by his mentor L.A. Reid. On week 2 of the live shows, he performed [[Bon Jovi]]'s song "[[Wanted Dead or Alive (Bon Jovi song)|Wanted Dead or Alive]]" and was declared the top of the popular vote in the competition. On Week 3 live show he performed "From This Moment" by [[Shania Twain]] again placing as top vote getter for a second week in a row. On week 4 live show he performed "[[I'm Already There]]" finishing second in popular vote to [[Carly Rose Sonenclar]]; he would remain in second place during the fifth week of live shows as well. During week 6 live show Stevens went "unplugged" with "[[Livin' on a Prayer]]" by [[Bon Jovi]] (for his acoustic song) and "[[If Tomorrow Never Comes]]" by [[Garth Brooks]] (chosen by the fans as his [[Pepsi Challenge]] song). His performance landed him back at the number one spot again. On the show's final week, Stevens performed "[[Tomorrow (Chris Young song)|Tomorrow]]" by [[Chris Young (singer)|Chris Young]] and was crowned the winner of ''The X Factor''. "Tomorrow" became the official winner's single.

===2013–present: ''Tate Stevens''===
Stevens was scheduled to move to [[Nashville]], [[Tennessee]] in early 2013 to begin working on his debut album, with work on the album reported to begin as soon as January 2, 2013. Stevens has stated that he would like to make a predominantly "fun" record, and hoped to co-write some of the songs. He stated he would like to work with [[Luke Laird]] and Kim Tribble.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/x-factor-winner-tate-stevens-406131 |title='X Factor' Winner Tate Stevens on L.A. Reid, Moving to Nashville and What's Next |work=The Hollywood Reporter |date=2012-12-21 |accessdate=2013-01-16}}</ref> Stevens was signed to [[RCA Records Nashville]] on January 15, 2013.<ref>[http://www.rcarecordslabel.com/press-releases/x-factor-winner-tate-stevens-signs-record-deal-syco-musicrca-records-nashville X Factor Winner Tate Stevens Signs Record Deal With Syco Music/RCA Records Nashville]</ref><ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/music/2013/01/15/x-factor-winner-stevens-signs-record-deal/1838367/ | title='X Factor' winner Tate Stevens signs record deal | work=USA TODAY | date=January 15, 2013 | accessdate=January 16, 2013}}</ref> He released his debut song "Holler If You're With Me" in a commercial produced by [[Pepsi]], which aired during the [[2013 Grammy Awards]] on February 10, 2013. The full-length music video for the song became available online immediately afterwards. The music video for the song used about 100 extras, and focuses on a "big bonfire party." The music video was filmed in [[Simi Valley]], [[California]].<ref>{{cite magazine|last1=Baltin|first1=Steve|title='X Factor' Winner Tate Stevens Set to Debut New Track|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/x-factor-winner-tate-stevens-set-to-debut-new-track-20130123|magazine=Rolling Stone|date=January 23, 2013}}</ref> "Power of a Love Song," which was released physically and digitally on March 12, 2013, with "Holler If You're With Me" as a [[B-side]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Power Of Love Song (Walmart Exclusive)|url=http://www.walmart.com/ip/23428133|website=Walmart}}</ref> served as the album's official lead single to country radio. Tate's [[Tate Stevens (album)|self-titled debut album]] was released on April 23, 2013. The album debuted at number 18 on the Billboard 200 with 17,000 copies in its first week, and received good reviews from critics.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Kaufman|first1=Gil|title=Fantasia, Phoenix And will.i.am Score Top 10 Debuts|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1706622/billboard-charts-fantasia-michael-buble.jhtml|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130504004848/http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1706622/billboard-charts-fantasia-michael-buble.jhtml|url-status=dead|archive-date=May 4, 2013|website=MTV|date=May 1, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Dukes|first1=Billy|title=Tate Stevens, 'Tate Stevens' – Album Review|url=http://tasteofcountry.com/tate-stevens/|website=Taste of Country|date=April 23, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Bjorke|first1=Matt|title=Album Review: Tate Stevens – Tate Stevens|url=http://roughstock.com/news/2013/04/15460-album-review-tate-stevens-tate-stevens/|website=Rough Stock|date=April 19, 2013}}</ref>

After "Power of a Love Song" reached a peak of number 49 on the ''Billboard'' Country Airplay chart, no further singles were released from the album. Subsequently, in August 2013, after winning a record deal with Sony (RCA Nashville), Stevens parted ways with the label.{{Citation needed|date=March 2021}}

==Personal life==

Stevens grew up in [[Belton, Missouri]] part of greater [[Kansas City, Missouri|Kansas City]] and now lives with his wife Ashlie, whom he met in high school and married in 1997, and their son Hayden and daughter Rylie in [[Peculiar, Missouri]].<ref name="EngleOct" />
