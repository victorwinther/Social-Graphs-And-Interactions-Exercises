'''Jonathan Adam "Jonny" Diaz''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|d|aɪ|.|æ|z}} {{respell|DEYE|az}}) (born March 30, 1984)<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.twitter.com/jonnydiaz|title=Jonny Diaz (jonnydiaz) on Twitter|publisher=|accessdate=September 27, 2014}}</ref> is an American [[Contemporary Christian music|contemporary Christian]] pop artist originally from [[Lakeland, Florida]]. His song "More Beautiful You" has charted on American Christian music charts. His major record label debut album of the same name has charted on ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' magazine's [[Top Heatseekers]] and [[Top Christian Albums]] charts.

== Career ==
Diaz released his first album ''Shades of White'' in August 2003 right before he began his second year of college; his second album, ''Everyday God'', was released during his fourth year of college.<ref name="CBNBio" /> He collaborated with producer Mitch Dane on his third CD ''They Need Love'' and the project came out in March 2007.<ref name="CBNBio" /> That album included the song "Hold Me", which got him attention when the song placed on the Top 40 charts.<ref name="NewManMagazine">{{cite web|url=http://www.newmanmag.com/e-magazine/051809/story6.php|title=Music Review: Jonny Diaz|last=DeVore|first=Leigh|date=May 18, 2009|publisher=New Man Magazine|accessdate=July 31, 2009}}</ref>

Diaz's major label debut album ''More Beautiful You'' album has appeared on several [[Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'']] magazine charts; on July 25, 2009 it was ranked No.&nbsp;32 on the [[Top Christian Albums]] chart and No.&nbsp;25 on the [[Top Heatseekers]] chart.<ref name="BillboardMoreBeautifulYouAlbum">{{cite magazine|url={{BillboardURLbyName|artist=jonny diaz|chart=all}}|title= More Beautiful You (2009) |date=July 25, 2009|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|accessdate=July 31, 2009}}</ref> The album's [[lead single]] "More Beautiful You" hit the American charts in mid-2009 and reached No.&nbsp;2 on ''Billboard'''s [[Hot Christian Songs]] chart by the end of August 2009.<ref>{{cite magazine |url={{BillboardURLbyName|artist=jonny diaz|chart=all}} |title=Jonny Diaz Music News & Info|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|accessdate=August 29, 2009}}</ref> The song tells young women that they are beautiful the way they are.<ref name="NewManMagazine" /> Diaz said "I hope to get the attention of females and tell them the truth found in God's Word, that there could never be a more beautiful you."<ref name="NewManMagazine" />

His music career was essentially started by leading worship for the youth group of the First Presbyterian Church in Lakeland, FL, playing with Brett Blondell and Vanessa Barbee.

== Sound ==

Jesus Freak Hideout says his style "nearly echoes the sound of [[Jason Mraz]]".<ref name="jfh20090502" /> Christian Manifesto says "Armed with a guitar and a clear message, his music is easily identifiable as that 'safe' genre 'acoustic pop'. But, at least, it is stellar acoustic pop, this time."<ref name="CCMMagazineCartwright" /> His sound has been compared to [[Dave Barnes]],<ref name="jfh20090502" /> [[Bebo Norman]],<ref name="CCMMagazineCartwright">{{cite web|url=http://www.ccmmagazine.com/reviews/music/11605983/|title=Promising Artist Dallies with Forgettable Fare|last=Cartwright|first=Grace S.|publisher=CCM Magazine|accessdate=July 31, 2009}}</ref> [[John Mayer]],<ref name="ChristianMusicTodayTheNeedLove" /> and [[Brandon Heath]].<ref name="ChristianManifesto">{{cite web|url=http://thechristianmanifesto.wordpress.com/2009/06/22/johnny-diazs-more-beautiful-you-a-review/|title=Johnny Diaz's "More Beautiful You": A Review|last=Akinola|first=Lydia|date=May 1, 2009|publisher=The Christian Manifesto|accessdate=July 31, 2009}}{{Dead link|date=March 2023 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

== Touring ==

Diaz has appeared as an opening act for [[Building 429]], [[Steven Curtis Chapman]], [[MercyMe]], [[Bebo Norman]], and [[Shane & Shane]].<ref name="CBNBio" /> He has performed at several Christian music festivals such as the [[Alive Festival]] and Atlanta Fest.<ref name="CBNBio" /> On his official website, he announce that he will be touring with [[Matthew West]] on the "Story of Your Life Tour".

==Personal life==
After graduating from high school, Diaz was offered a full [[athletic scholarship]] to play baseball for [[Florida State University]].<ref name="jfh20090502">{{cite web|url=http://www.jesusfreakhideout.com/cdreviews/MoreBeautifulYou.asp|title=Jonny Diaz: More Beautiful You|last=Leisure|first=Logan|date=May 2, 2009|publisher=Jesus Freak Hideout|accessdate=July 31, 2009}}</ref> Diaz was the fourth of four brothers to get a college baseball scholarship.<ref name="CBNBio" /> His brother, [[Matt Diaz]] was a professional baseball player in the major leagues, and another one of his brothers played baseball professionally.<ref name="CBNBio" /> He started college in the second half of 2002<ref name="CBNBio">{{cite web|url=http://www.cbn.com/cbnmusic/artists/Diaz_Jonny.aspx|title=Jonny Diaz Bio|publisher=[[Christian Broadcasting Network]]|accessdate=July 31, 2009}}</ref> and played on the team for one year.<ref name="ChristianMusicToday">{{cite web|url=http://www.christianitytoday.com/music/reviews/2009/morebeautifulyou-mini.html|title=Jonny Diaz: More Beautiful You |last=Greer|first=Andrew|date=May 2009|publisher=Christian Music Today|accessdate=July 31, 2009}}</ref> According to Jesus Freak Hideout, he "felt God calling him to pursue music".<ref name="jfh20090502" />
