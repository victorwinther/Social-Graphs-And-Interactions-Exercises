'''Thompson Square''' is an American [[country music]] duo composed of husband and wife Keifer and Shawna Thompson, who alternate as vocalists.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Country music duo Thompson Square to play the Weinberg stage |url=https://finance.yahoo.com/news/country-music-duo-thompson-square-154700252.html |access-date=2022-07-26 |website=finance.yahoo.com |date=March 24, 2022 |language=en-US}}</ref> They signed to Stoney Creek Records, a sister label of [[Broken Bow Records]], in 2010. The duo has released three albums, which have produced a combined ten chart singles on the [[Hot Country Songs]] and [[Country Airplay]] charts. Of these singles, two — "[[Are You Gonna Kiss Me or Not]]" and "[[If I Didn't Have You (Thompson Square song)|If I Didn't Have You]]" — reached No. 1.

== History ==
Keifer Thompson was born and raised in [[Miami, Oklahoma]]. His father is Darrion Thompson. He met Shawna, a native of [[Chatom, Alabama]], at a singing competition in [[Nashville, Tennessee]]. The two worked as solo artists before deciding to form a duo.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://allmusic.com/artist/thompson-square-p2313199/biography|title=Thompson Square biography|last=Leggett|first=Steve|work=[[Allmusic]]|access-date=February 7, 2011}}</ref> In 2007, they sang backing vocals on [[Ty Herndon]]'s ''[[Right About Now (Ty Herndon album)|Right About Now]]''.<ref name="liner">{{Cite AV media notes |title=Right About Now |others=Ty Herndon |year=2006 |type=CD booklet |publisher=Titan Records}}</ref>

=== 2010–2012: ''Thompson Square'' ===
Thompson Square signed to Stoney Creek Records, a division of [[Broken Bow Records]], in January 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.musicrow.com/2010/01/stoney-creek-adds-thompson-square/|title=Stoney Creek adds Thompson Square|last=Skates|first=Sarah|date=January 21, 2010|work=Music Row|access-date=June 16, 2010}}</ref> The duo released their debut single "Let's Fight" in mid-2010. Matt Bjorke of ''Roughstock'' gave the single four-and-a-half-stars out of five, with his review calling it "one of the catchiest, best-written singles of the 2010 summer season so far."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.roughstock.com/blog/thompson-square-let-s-fight-|title=Thompson Square — "Let's Fight"|last=Bjorke|first=Matt|date=May 15, 2010|work=Roughstock|access-date=June 16, 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100518230614/http://www.roughstock.com/blog/thompson-square-let-s-fight-|archive-date=May 18, 2010}}</ref> The song debuted on the [[Hot Country Songs]] chart for the week ending June 5, 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cmt.com/news/country-music/1640327/once-again-lady-antebellum-and-chris-young-are-chart-champs.jhtml|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100531125057/http://www.cmt.com/news/country-music/1640327/once-again-lady-antebellum-and-chris-young-are-chart-champs.jhtml|url-status=dead|archive-date=May 31, 2010|title=Once Again, Lady Antebellum and Chris Young Are Chart Champs|last=Morris|first=Edward|date=May 29, 2010|work=[[Country Music Television|CMT]]|access-date=June 16, 2010}}</ref>
[[File:Thompson Square2.JPG|thumb|left|Thompson Square perform "If I Didn't Have You."]]
On July 12, 2010, Thompson Square released their second single, "[[Are You Gonna Kiss Me or Not]]," written by [[David Lee Murphy]] and [[Jim Collins (singer)|Jim Collins]]. It became the duo's first number one single on the [[Hot Country Songs]] chart and has been certified double-platinum by the [[Recording Industry Association of America]]. It was the most played song of the year on [[Country music]] [[radio]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=AMERICAN MUSIC AWARDS NOMINEE THOMPSON SQUARE REACTS TO SPRINT NEW ARTIST OF THE YEAR NOMINATION - BBR Music Group |url=https://www.bbrmusicgroup.com/american-music-awards-nominee-thompson-square-reacts-to-sprint-new-artist-of-the-year-nomination/ |access-date=2022-07-26 |website=www.bbrmusicgroup.com}}</ref> Thompson Square received two nominations at the [[54th Grammy Awards]] for "Are You Gonna Kiss Me or Not" in [[Best Country Duo/Group Performance]] and [[Best Country Song]] categories.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2011-12-01 |title=THOMPSON SQUARE SCORES 2 GRAMMY NOMS |url=https://countrymusicnewsinternational.com/thompson-square-scores-2-grammy-noms/ |access-date=2022-07-26 |website=Country Music News International |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2011-12-05 |title=Thompson Square Reacts to GRAMMY Nominations |url=https://www.soundslikenashville.com/news/thompson-square-reacts-to-grammy-nominations/ |access-date=2022-07-26 |website=Sounds Like Nashville}}</ref>

Their [[Thompson Square (album)|self-titled debut album]] was released on February 8, 2011. It was produced by [[New Voice Entertainment]], a production team which includes members of labelmate [[Jason Aldean]]'s road band.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.roughstock.com/blog/new-artist-spotlight-thompson-square-|title=New Artist Spotlight: Thompson Square|last=Bjorke|first=Matt|date=January 25, 2011|work=Roughstock|access-date=January 31, 2011|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110128234938/http://www.roughstock.com/blog/new-artist-spotlight-thompson-square-|archive-date=January 28, 2011}}</ref> A third single, "[[I Got You (Thompson Square song)|I Got You]]," was released May 9, 2011, and reached a peak of number 8 on the country charts. Thompson Square wrote "I Got You" with [[Jason Sellers]] and Paul Jenkins. Their fourth single, "[[Glass (Thompson Square song)|Glass]]", written by [[Ross Copperman]] and [[Jon Nite]], was released on January 30, 2012. It peaked at number 15.

On April 1, 2012, Thompson Square received "Vocal Duo of the Year" award at the [[Academy of Country Music Awards]].<ref>[http://www.acmcountry.com/winners_search.html Winners search results] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141009181632/http://www.acmcountry.com/winners_search.html |date=October 9, 2014}}. [[Academy of Country Music]]. Retrieved October 8, 2013.</ref>

=== 2012–2014: ''Just Feels Good'' ===
[[File:Team Thompson Square backstage after Thompson Square 2013 CMA Vocal Duo of the Year win.jpg|thumb|289x289px|Thompson Square after winning 2013 CMA Vocal Duo of the Year award.]]
Thompson Square's fifth single, "[[If I Didn't Have You (Thompson Square song)|If I Didn't Have You]]", made its chart debut in late 2012 and reached number one on the [[Country Airplay]] chart in May 2013. The duo wrote this song with Sellers and Jenkins. It served as the lead single to their second Stoney Creek album, ''[[Just Feels Good]]'', which was released on March 26, 2013. The album's second single, "[[Everything I Shouldn't Be Thinking About]]", was released to country radio on June 10, 2013. Thompson Square and Murphy wrote this song with [[Brett James]], and in early 2014, it became the duo's fourth Top 10 hit. The album's third single, "Testing the Water", was released to country radio on April 21, 2014, although after only two months, "I Can't Outrun You", was released as the fourth single on June 23, 2014, and neither made the Top 40.

The duo won the 2013 Academy of Country Music Award for Vocal Duo of the Year.<ref>{{cite web|last=Moraski|first=Lauren|title=ACM Awards 2013: Eric Church, Miranda Lambert among winners| website=[[CBS News]] |url=http://www.cbsnews.com/8301-207_162-57578340/acm-awards-2013-eric-church-miranda-lambert-among-winners/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130408145233/http://www.cbsnews.com/8301-207_162-57578340/acm-awards-2013-eric-church-miranda-lambert-among-winners/|url-status=dead|archive-date=April 8, 2013|access-date=April 8, 2013}}</ref>

=== 2015–present: ''Masterpiece'' ===
Thompson Square's first single in nearly a year, "[[Trans Am (song)|Trans Am]]," was released in March 2015.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/premieres/hear-thompson-squares-vivacious-trans-am-20150323 | title=Hear Thompson Square's Vivacious 'Trans Am' | publisher=Rolling Stone Country | date=March 23, 2015 | access-date=September 5, 2015}}</ref> It peaked at number 44 on the Country Airplay charts. On August 4, 2015, the duo revealed they were expecting their first child, due in January.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://theboot.com/thompson-square-shawna-thompson-pregnant/ | title=Thompson Square Are Having a Baby | publisher=The Boot | date=August 4, 2015 | access-date=September 5, 2015}}</ref> Their son, Rigney Cooper, was born in January 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.eonline.com/fr/news/733294/thompson-square-s-shawna-husband-keifer-thompson-welcome-first-child-rigney-cooper|title=Thompson Square's Shawna & Keifer Thompson Welcome First Child, Rigney Cooper|date=January 24, 2016|website=Eonline.com|access-date=April 21, 2019}}</ref> The duo decided to leave Stoney Creek in 2016 after poor performance of singles.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/country/8459501/thompson-square-masterpiece-interview|title=Thompson Square Talk 'Masterpiece' & 5-Year Gap Between Albums: 'Some Things Don't Work Out Like You Want'|website=Billboard.com}}</ref> In November 2018, Thompson Square published a [[Children's literature|children's book]] titled ''Time To Get Dressed'', which teaches children how to get ready for their day.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Thompson Square believe it's "Time to Get Dressed" |url=https://www.countrystandardtime.com/news/newsitem.asp?xid=10311 |access-date=2022-07-22 |website=www.countrystandardtime.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last1=S |first1=Nicole |date=March 19, 2019 |title=Thompson Square's Keifer Thompson Thought Up New Children's Book While on Dad Duty |url=https://people.com/parents/thompson-square-childrens-book-time-to-get-dressed/ |access-date=2022-07-22 |website=PEOPLE.com |language=en}}</ref>

In 2018, they self-released their third album, ''[[Masterpiece (Thompson Square album)|Masterpiece]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.countrystandardtime.com/d/cdreview.asp?xid=6694|title=Thompson Square – Masterpiece|website=Countrystandardtime.com|access-date=April 21, 2019}}</ref> In March 2019, Thompson Square signed a recording deal with Reviver Entertainment, and the title track from their third album was released on July 22, 2019, as its first single. It debuted on the ''Billboard'' Country Airplay chart at number 60 in November 2019, making it their first appearance on the chart in three years.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://bulletins.billboard.com/country/country_update_1125.pdf|title=Billboard Country Update|website=Bulletins.billboard.com|access-date=April 3, 2020}}</ref>

On April 8, 2022, the duo released a new single "Country In My Soul,"<ref>{{Cite web |last=Dukes |first=Billy|title=WATCH: Thompson Square Prove They Have Country in Their Soul |url=https://tasteofcountry.com/thompson-square-country-in-my-soul-acoustic-video/ |access-date=2022-07-22 |website=Taste of Country |date=May 27, 2022 |language=en}}</ref> co-written by [[Lainey Wilson]], Daniel Ross and James McNair.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web |date=2022-07-06 |title=Thompson Square Celebrates Summer With New 'Country In My Soul Tour' |url=https://musicrow.com/2022/07/thompson-square-celebrates-summer-with-new-country-in-my-soul-tour1/ |access-date=2022-07-22 |website=MusicRow.com |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=May 20, 2022 |first=Tricia |last=Despres |title=Whatever You Do, Don't Ask Country Duo Thompson Square Where They've Been: 'We Never Stopped' |url=https://people.com/country/thompson-square-release-country-in-my-soul/ |access-date=2022-07-22 |website=PEOPLE.com |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Listen: Thompson Square's 'Country in My Soul' Is 100 Percent Country Pride |url=https://tasteofcountry.com/thompson-square-country-in-my-soul/ |access-date=2022-07-22 |website=Taste of Country |date=April 20, 2022 |language=en}}</ref> On April 28, 2022, ''Entertainment Focus'' reported that "Country In My Soul" was the second most added song of the week by radio stations nationwide.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Daykin |first=James |title=Thompson Square's New Single 'Country in My Soul' Has Big Impact at Country Radio |date=April 28, 2022 |url=https://entertainment-focus.com/2022/04/28/1326482/ |access-date=2022-07-22 |language=en-US}}</ref>

Thompson Square embarked on ''"''Country In My Soul Tour" from July to October 2022.<ref name=":0" /> On March 10, 2023, their new single, "Without You" was released.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Janes |first=Lesley |date=2023-03-10 |title=Thompson Square "Without You" Song Review |url=https://www.thenashnews.com/post/thompson-square-without-you-song-review |access-date=2023-04-10 |website=The Nash News |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Despres |first=Tricia|title=Listen: Thompson Square Return to Form With Romantic New Single 'Without You' |url=https://tasteofcountry.com/thompson-square-without-you/ |access-date=2023-04-10 |website=Taste of Country |date=March 10, 2023 |language=en}}</ref>
