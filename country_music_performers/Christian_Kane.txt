'''Christian Kane''' is an American actor and singer-songwriter. His television roles include [[Lindsey McDonald]] in ''[[Angel (1999 TV series)|Angel]]'', Eliot Spencer in ''[[Leverage (American TV series)|Leverage]]'' and its [[Spin-off (media)|spin-off]] ''[[Leverage: Redemption]]'', Jacob Stone in ''[[The Librarians (2014 TV series)|The Librarians]]'', and Abe "High Wolf" Wheeler in ''[[Into the West (miniseries)|Into the West]]''. His cinematic filmography includes ''[[Just Married]]'', ''[[Taxi (2004 film)|Taxi]]'', and ''[[Secondhand Lions]]''.

Kane is the lead singer of the [[country music|country]]-[[southern rock]] band [[Kane (American band)|Kane]]. On December 7, 2010, they released ''The House Rules'', their third album and their debut for [[record label]] [[Bigger Picture Music Group]]. The album reached no.&nbsp;25 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' Country Albums chart. The first single from the album, also titled "The House Rules", debuted at no.&nbsp;54 on the ''Billboard'' Country Songs chart. The second single, “Let Me Go”, was released on July 11, 2011.

==Early life==
Kane has stated that he is of Cherokee (Native American) ancestry.<ref name="oregonlive">{{cite news |last1=Baker |first1=Jeff |title=Christian Kane loves Portland and Oklahoma and his wolf-dog hybrid |url=https://www.oregonlive.com/movies/2014/07/christian_kane_loves_portland.html |access-date=12 September 2021 |work=The Oregonian/OregonLive |date=16 July 2014}}</ref> His parents participated in, and met at, the [[rodeo]]. The family moved around the [[Southern U.S.|South]] because his father was in the [[oil business]], and they finally settled in [[Norman, Oklahoma]], when Kane was in eighth grade. While growing up in [[Texas]] and [[Oklahoma]], Kane was a collegiate-style wrestler and played [[American Football|football]] (as a [[strong safety]]).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.starpulse.com/news/index.php/2009/07/22/christian_kane_of_leverage_discusses_his|title=CK discusses his Bad-Ass Character|website=Starpulse.com|access-date=2010-10-26|archive-date=2012-10-08|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121008191438/http://www.starpulse.com/news/index.php/2009/07/22/christian_kane_of_leverage_discusses_his|url-status=dead}}</ref> He studied [[art history]] at the [[University of Oklahoma]], but decided he wanted to be an actor and headed to [[Los Angeles]] before finishing the degree.<ref name="digitaljournal.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.digitaljournal.com/article/295118|title=Christian Kane is singing a totally different tune on 'Leverage'|website=Digitaljournal.com}}</ref> In Los Angeles, he worked at a [[talent management]] company, where he delivered scripts in exchange for getting acting jobs.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.intrepidmedia.com/column.asp?id=4138|title=Portrait of an artist: Christian Kane|website=Intrepidmedia.com|access-date=2010-10-26|archive-date=2017-11-07|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171107012210/http://www.intrepidmedia.com/column.asp?id=4138|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Career==
===Acting===
[[File:Christian Kane and Amy Acker Aug 2004.jpg|thumb|right|Kane with [[Amy Acker]] at a signing in Santa Barbara (2004)]]
Kane landed his first acting role in 1997 as one of the leads in [[MGM]]'s television series ''[[Fame L.A.]]'' playing a country singer who moved to Los Angeles from Kansas.  His television roles have included a recurring role in [[Joss Whedon]]’s ''[[Angel (1999 TV series)|Angel]]'' as the lawyer [[Lindsey MacDonald]], the [[Jerry Bruckheimer]]-produced series ''[[Close to Home (2005 TV series)|Close to Home]]'' on [[CBS]], the [[TNT (U.S. TV network)|TNT]] television film ''[[Crossfire Trail]]'' in which he appeared alongside [[Tom Selleck]], and the role of Abe Wheeler in the [[Steven Spielberg]]-produced 2005 miniseries ''[[Into the West (miniseries)|Into the West]]''. He starred as Billy Ryan in the [[MTV]] movie ''[[Love Song (2000 film)|Love Song]]'' in 2000, alongside [[R&B]] artist [[Monica (singer)|Monica]].

Kane’s appearances on the big screen include the [[20th Century Fox]] film ''[[Just Married]]'' and [[New Line Cinema]]'s ''[[Secondhand Lions]]'' (as the younger version of [[Robert Duvall]]'s Hub McCann, he has a scar on his upper lip from an injury he received while filming the movie).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.unclebarky.com/reviews_files/3f547e2c6a9a1f7c0197e39ec6383151-542.html|title=Redneck scrapper: Dallas-born Christian Kane fights for TNT's new Leverage|website=Unclebarky.com|access-date=January 7, 2017}}</ref> He also appeared in ''[[Taxi (2004 film)|Taxi]]'', ''[[Life or Something Like It]]'', the Peter Berg-directed ''[[Friday Night Lights (film)|Friday Night Lights]]'', and the Warner Bros. baseball film ''[[Summer Catch]]''.

In 2007, Kane was featured in the [[Carrie Underwood]] music video "So Small", playing the role of one of the three converging stories. In 2009, he appeared in the psychological thriller, ''Hide'', in which he played the lead role of Billy, and ''[[The Donner Party (2009 film)|The Donner Party]]'' which is based on the true story of the [[Donner Party]]. In 2010, he starred in the romantic drama, ''Not Since You''.

From 2008 to 2012, Kane appeared on the TNT television series ''[[Leverage (American TV series)|Leverage]]'' as [[Eliot Spencer]], a retrieval specialist and black ops soldier who teams up with four other specialists to undo the injustices perpetrated upon the helpless.  Kane performed all his own fight scenes and also choreographed fights for the series. Kane plays the same character in ''[[Leverage: Redemption|Leverage:Redemption]]'', which premiered in July 2021, streaming on [[IMDb TV]]. The show takes up the team's adventures 8 years after the last episode.

In 2013, Kane appeared as JT Maxwell, brother to [[Rebecca Romijn]]'s female lead Michelle Maxwell on TNT's ''[[King & Maxwell]]''. He appeared again with Romijn co-starring in the 2014 to 2018 TNT television series ''[[The Librarians (2014 TV series)|The Librarians]]'', based on ''[[The Librarian (franchise)|The Librarian]]'' film series.

In 2020, Kane starred as Alex Walker on the drama series, [[Almost Paradise (TV series)|Almost Paradise]]. The second season was released in July 2023. In an interview with ''[[Tell-Tale TV]]'', Kane described that character as being several of his previous roles on other series rolled into one.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-03-29|title=Almost Paradise: Christian Kane Says His New Series is Here to Take You on a Vacation [Exclusive Interview] {{!}} Tell-Tale TV|url=https://telltaletv.com/2020/03/christian-kane-almost-paradise-interview/|access-date=2021-07-07|website=telltaletv.com|language=en-US}}</ref>

===Music===

The band consists of Kane ([[lead vocals]], [[guitar]]), Carlson ([[rhythm guitar]], [[backing vocals]]), Jason Southard ([[lead guitar]]), Will Amend ([[bass guitar]]), and Ryan Baker ([[drums]])  and is signed to independent record label [[Bigger Picture Music Group]]. Their label debut, ''The House Rules'', was released December 7, 2010. It debuted at no.&nbsp;1 on the ''Billboard'' Heatseekers album chart and no.&nbsp;25 on the Country Albums chart.<ref>{{cite web|url={{BillboardURLbyName|artist=christian kane|chart=Heatseekers Albums}}|title=Billboard Heatseekers chart}}, Billboard.com</ref> The album was produced by [[Bob Ezrin]] and [[Jimmie Lee Sloas]].<ref name="allaccess.com">{{cite web|url=http://www.allaccess.com/country/artist-info/q/id/1451/christian-kane|title=AllAccess Artist profile: Christian Kane|website=Allaccess.com}}</ref>

The first single from the album, also titled "The House Rules", debuted at no.&nbsp;54 on the ''Billboard'' Country Songs chart<ref>{{cite magazine |url=http://www.billboard.com/column/chartbeat/chart-highlights-alternative-country-songs-1004124105.story |title=Billboard oct. 25th 2010 Chart Highlights |magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101029214755/http://www.billboard.com/column/chartbeat/chart-highlights-alternative-country-songs-1004124105.story |archive-date=October 29, 2010 }}</ref> and was the seventh-most added song on [[Mediabase]] Country stations on its official impact date.<ref name="allaccess.com"/> The video for the single was directed by [[Timothy Hutton]] and premiered on [[Country Music Television|CMT]]'s ''Big New Music Weekend'' October 1, 2010. "The House Rules" was featured in the video game ''NASCAR 09''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cmt.com/news/news-in-brief/1649107/eight-videos-debuting-during-cmt-big-new-music-weekend.jhtml|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101004020031/http://www.cmt.com/news/news-in-brief/1649107/eight-videos-debuting-during-cmt-big-new-music-weekend.jhtml|url-status=dead|archive-date=October 4, 2010|title=CMT: Eight Videos Debuting During CMT Big New Music Weekend|website=Cmt.com}}</ref> The second single, "Let Me Go", was released on July 11, 2011. The video for the single, directed by [[Roman White]], premiered on [[Country Music Television|CMT]] August 8, 2011 and reached no.&nbsp;1 on their Today's Top Videos chart .<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.musicnewsnashville.com/christian-kanes-let-me-go-blazes-to-no-1-on-cmt-com/|title=''Let Me Go'' blazes to nr. 1|website=Musicnewsnashville,cin|access-date=2011-11-14|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170620133808/http://www.musicnewsnashville.com/christian-kanes-let-me-go-blazes-to-no-1-on-cmt-com/|archive-date=2017-06-20|url-status=dead}}</ref>

[[Trace Adkins]] released a song written by Kane called "Happy Man" on his 2010 album, ''[[Cowboy's Back in Town]]''.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://m.lasvegassun.com/vegasdeluxe/2011/sep/14/actor-and-singer-christian-kane-likes-nashville-al/|title=Actor and singer Christian Kane likes Nashville, Alice in Chains and UFC|newspaper=[[Las Vegas Sun]]|access-date=January 7, 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170108094313/http://m.lasvegassun.com/vegasdeluxe/2011/sep/14/actor-and-singer-christian-kane-likes-nashville-al/|archive-date=January 8, 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Kane's co-writing efforts include songs written with Blair Daly, [[Brett James]], [[David Lee Murphy]], [[Casey Beathard]], and [[Jerrod Niemann]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://christiankane.com/?q=content/music |title=News - Updates |website=Christiankane.com |date=2017-01-22 |access-date=2020-04-04}}</ref> "Thinking of You", a song he co-wrote with Blair Daly, was featured in ''Leverage'' [[List of Leverage episodes#Season 3 (2010)|Season 3]] episode, "The Studio Job."<ref name="digitaljournal.com"/>
