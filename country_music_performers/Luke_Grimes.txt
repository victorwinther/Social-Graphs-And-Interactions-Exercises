'''Luke Timothy Grimes'''<ref>{{cite web |title=Luke Grimes Biography |url=https://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/luke-grimes/bio/3000615681/ |website=[[TV Guide]] |access-date=March 12, 2023}}</ref> (born January 21, 1984)<ref>{{Cite web |last=Rose |first=Mike |date=January 21, 2023|title=Today's famous birthdays list for January 21, 2023 includes celebrities Luke Grimes, Geena Davis|url=https://www.cleveland.com/entertainment/2023/01/todays-famous-birthdays-list-for-january-21-2023-includes-celebrities-luke-grimes-geena-davis.html |access-date=January 21, 2023 |website=[[Cleveland.com]]}}</ref> is an American actor and musician. He is known for his role as real life Navy SEAL [[Marc Alan Lee]] in the acclaimed film ''[[American Sniper]]''. He played Christian Grey's brother, Elliot, in the film ''[[Fifty Shades of Grey (film)|Fifty Shades of Grey]]'' (2015), and its sequels, ''[[Fifty Shades Darker (film)|Fifty Shades Darker]]'' (2017) and ''[[Fifty Shades Freed (film)|Fifty Shades Freed]]'' (2018). He currently stars as Kayce Dutton in the [[Paramount Network]] drama series ''[[Yellowstone (American TV series)|Yellowstone]]'' (2018–present).

==Early life==
Grimes was born in [[Dayton, Ohio]], the son of a Pentecostal pastor.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.eonline.com/ca/news/474475/luke-grimes-5-things-to-know-about-fifty-shades-of-grey-star-playing-elliot|title=Luke Grimes: 5 Things to Know About the Fifty Shades of Grey Star Playing Christian's Brother Elliot|date=October 25, 2013|website=E! News}}</ref> Grimes graduated from [[Dayton Christian High School]] in 2002.{{cn|date=January 2023}} He moved to [[New York City]] to study acting at the [[American Academy of Dramatic Arts]].{{cn|date=January 2023}}

==Career==
Grimes has appeared in ''[[All the Boys Love Mandy Lane]]'', ''[[War Eagle, Arkansas (film)|War Eagle, Arkansas]]'', and ''[[Assassination of a High School President]],'' which received a limited release in March 2009. He also appeared in the [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] drama ''[[Brothers & Sisters (2006 TV series)|Brothers & Sisters]]'', as Ryan Lafferty, the illegitimate son of William Walker, patriarch of the show's family. Beginning with Season 4, he became a series regular.<ref>{{cite web|last=Mitovich|first=Matt|title=Ryan Game Over: Brothers & Sisters Casts New Offspring|url=https://www.tvguide.com/News/Brothers-Sisters-casting-27381.aspx|work=[[TV Guide]]|access-date=January 12, 2009|date=October 20, 2008}}</ref>

He starred in the 2010 [[FX (TV channel)|FX]] movie pilot, ''Outlaw Country'', with [[Haley Bennett]] and [[Mary Steenburgen]]. Grimes played a cowboy named Eli Larkin.<ref>{{cite web|last=Abrams|first=Natalie|title=Luke Grimes, Haley Bennett Head for Outlaw Country|url=https://www.tvguide.com/News/Grimes-Bennett-Outlaw-1020213.aspx|work=TV Guide|access-date=July 8, 2010|date=July 6, 2010}}</ref> The series was not picked up by FX, but the pilot aired on August 23, 2012 as a television movie. In 2012, he appeared in the [[Liam Neeson]] thriller ''[[Taken 2]]'' as Neeson's daughter's boyfriend.<ref>{{cite web|last=Armitage|first=Hugh|title=Luke Grimes cast in Liam Neeson's 'Taken 2'|url=http://www.digitalspy.com/movies/news/a349929/luke-grimes-cast-in-liam-neesons-taken-2.html|publisher=[[Digital Spy]]|access-date=January 29, 2013|date=November 9, 2011}}</ref>

Grimes portrayed vampire James Kent in ''[[True Blood]]'' for six episodes during the show's [[True Blood (season 6)|sixth season]], but later left the show and was replaced by [[Nathan Parsons]]. Reports, confirmed by [[BuzzFeed]],<ref>{{cite web |last1=Peitzman |first1=Louis |title=Luke Grimes Left "True Blood" Because He Refused To Play Gay |url=https://www.buzzfeed.com/louispeitzman/luke-grimes-left-true-blood-because-he-refused-to-play-gay |publisher=BuzzFeed |access-date=24 January 2023}}</ref> indicated that he quit because he refused to participate in same-sex kissing or sex scenes or to play a character that was attracted to men.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/1875059/true-blood-luke-grimes-quit-anti-gay-nelsan-ellis-speaks-out/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140724214415/http://www.mtv.com/news/1875059/true-blood-luke-grimes-quit-anti-gay-nelsan-ellis-speaks-out/|url-status=dead|archive-date=July 24, 2014|title='True Blood' Actor Speaks Out On Ex-Costar's Anti-Gay Behavior: 'I'm Over Him'|first=Shaunna|last=Murphy|website=MTV News}}</ref><ref name=buzzfeed>{{Cite web|url=https://www.buzzfeed.com/louispeitzman/luke-grimes-left-true-blood-because-he-refused-to-play-gay|title = Luke Grimes Left "True Blood" Because He Refused to Play Gay| website=[[BuzzFeed]] }}</ref> Grimes's publicist stated that he departed to pursue other opportunities.<ref name=buzzfeed/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.pinknews.co.uk/2014/06/29/true-blood-star-denies-he-quit-show-over-planned-gay-storyline/|title = True Blood star denies he quit show over planned gay storyline|date=June 29, 2014}}</ref> 

Grimes played [[United States Navy SEALs|United States Navy SEAL]] [[Marc Alan Lee|Marc Lee]], who was killed in action in 2006, in ''[[American Sniper]]'' (2014). He played Christian Grey's brother, Elliot, in the film ''[[Fifty Shades of Grey (film)|Fifty Shades of Grey]]'' (2015), and its sequels, ''[[Fifty Shades Darker (film)|Fifty Shades Darker]]'' (2017) and ''[[Fifty Shades Freed (film)|Fifty Shades Freed]]'' (2018).<ref>{{cite web|last=Fleming|first=Mike|title=Another Grey Casting In 'Fifty Shades'|url=https://www.deadline.com/2013/10/another-grey-casting-in-fifty-shades/|website=[[Deadline Hollywood]]|publisher=[[PMC (company)|PMC]]|access-date=October 25, 2013|date=October 25, 2013}}</ref> Grimes played Todd Belkin in ''[[Freeheld (2015 film)|Freeheld]]'' (2015). Since 2018, he has starred alongside [[Kevin Costner]] in the [[Paramount Network]] drama series ''[[Yellowstone (American TV series)|Yellowstone]]'' in the role of Kayce Dutton.

==Music==
In January 2024, Grimes released “Burn – Live From Nashville” on [[Spotify]]. 

On March 8, 2024, Grimes released his self-titled debut album, ''Luke Grimes''.<ref name="Saving Country Music">{{cite web|title=Album Review – Luke Grimes – (Self-Titled) |url=https://www.savingcountrymusic.com/album-review-luke-grimes-self-titled/|website=Saving Country Music|access-date=April 4, 2024}}</ref>
