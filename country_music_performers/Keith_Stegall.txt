'''Robert Keith Stegall''' (born November 1, 1955<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web |url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p128262/biography|pure_url=yes}} |title=Keith Stegall biography |access-date=January 7, 2008 |last=Huey |first=Steve |work=[[Allmusic]]}}</ref>) is an American [[country music]] recording artist and record producer. Active since 1980, Stegall has recorded two major-label studio albums: 1985's ''Keith Stegall'' and 1996's ''Passages'', although he is mainly known for his production work.

==Musical career==
Robert Keith Stegall was born in [[Wichita Falls, Texas]], in 1955. He performed in local bands, spent a short time in northwestern Louisiana (in the mid-1970s) where he operated a small-time music recording business, then moved to [[Nashville, Tennessee]], at the persuasion of [[Kris Kristofferson]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.rama-info.com/home/producers/third/bio2_text.html |title=Keith Stegall |access-date=April 27, 2013 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130630055830/http://www.rama-info.com/home/producers/third/bio2_text.html |archive-date=June 30, 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Stegall charted thirteen singles on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs]] charts, with the highest-peaking being 1985's "Pretty Lady", a No.&nbsp;10 hit.<ref name="allmusic"/> Starting in the late 1980s, Stegall has been active primarily as a record producer for several recording acts, most notably [[Alan Jackson]], [[George Jones]], [[Zac Brown Band]], and [[Clay Walker]].<ref name="allmusic"/> Stegall has also written several of Jackson's singles, as well as [[George Strait]]'s Number one hit "[[I Hate Everything (song)|I Hate Everything]]" and [[Dr. Hook]]'s "[[Sexy Eyes (Dr. Hook song)|Sexy Eyes]]".

In 2008, Stegall co-founded the label [[Bigger Picture Music Group]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://biggerpicturegroup.com/updates/news/producer%E2%80%99s-chair-keith-stegall-189 |title=News: The Producer's Chair: Keith Stegall &#124; Bigger Picture Group Artist Services |access-date=May 9, 2014 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20140509040502/http://biggerpicturegroup.com/updates/news/producer%E2%80%99s-chair-keith-stegall-189 |archive-date=May 9, 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref> The label closed in 2014.

In 2016, Stegall launched a Nashville-based production, management, and publishing firm, Dreamlined Entertainment Group, with Scott Miller of Miller Investment Management.<ref>{{cite web | date=February 1, 2016 | title=Keith Stegall Launches Dreamlined Entertainment Group | url=https://musicrow.com/2016/02/keith-stegall-launches-dreamlined-entertainment-group/ | work=Jessica Nicholson }}</ref>

In October 2017, Fangate Music, a label run by Australian country music promoter Rob Potts, in conjunction with [[Sony Music Australia]], announced their partnership with Stegall's Dreamlined Entertainment Group.<ref>{{cite web | date=October 17, 2017 | title=Sony Music Australia & Fangate Music partner with Dreamlined Entertainment | url=https://www.theindustryobserver.com.au/sony-fangate-dreamlined-entertainment/ | work=Tyler Jenke }}</ref>
