'''Heather Myles''' (born July 31, 1962) is an American [[country music]] singer, with a [[honky tonk]] [[Bakersfield sound]].

==Early life==
Myles was born in [[Riverside, California]], United States, where her parents bred and trained horses for [[horse racing|racing]].  Heather had a job in the family business until the lure of the honky-tonks called her away.

While still in her teens, she joined a band, and within a year they had a contract with [[HighTone Records|HighTone]].<ref name=HM-nodep-2002>{{cite news|last1=Mazor|first1=Barry|title=Heather Myles - River deep|url=http://nodepression.com/article/heather-myles-river-deep|accessdate=5 April 2017|work=No Depression|date=30 June 2002}}</ref>

==Career==
Myles has released five studio albums, including two on HighTone, two on Rounder, and one on the Me and My Americana Roots label.

Her 1992 debut album ''Just Like Old Times'' contained mostly original compositions, along with songs from [[Jim Lauderdale]] and [[Robert Cray]], and was followed by ''Untamed'' in 1995.

Her third studio album ''Highways and Honkytonks'' was released in 1998, and featured a duet with [[Merle Haggard]] on "No One is Gonna Love You Better."<ref name=HM-countrystandardtime-1998>{{cite news|last1=Wooldridge|first1=Robert|title=Heather Myles: third time lucky|url=http://www.countrystandardtime.com/d/article.asp?xid=639|accessdate=5 April 2017|work=Country Standard Time|date=1 October 1998}}</ref>

2002's ''Sweet Talk & Good Lies'' included a duet with [[Dwight Yoakam]] on the song "Little Chapel."<ref name=HM-slipcue-2002>{{cite web 
| url=http://www.slipcue.com/music/country/countryartists/myles_heather_01.html| title=Heather Myles discography  | work=slipcue
| accessdate=5 April 2017| author= }}</ref>

Released in 2009, ''In The Wind'' was co-produced by Taras Prodaniuk ([[Dwight Yoakam]], [[Lucinda Williams]]) who also played bass. They were accompanied by Larry Mitchell and Jim Christie (drums), and Bob Gothar (guitar).<ref name=HM-rockabilly-2011>{{cite web|url=http://www.rockabillyhall.com/TBSFeatureHeather.html|title=Heather Myles Biography|work=Rockabilly Hall of Fame|accessdate=5 April 2017|author=|archive-date=6 April 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170406111404/http://www.rockabillyhall.com/TBSFeatureHeather.html|url-status=dead}}</ref>
