'''Mary Sarah Gross''' (born July 7, 1995), known professionally as her stage name '''Mary Sarah''', is a country music singer and songwriter. She started what would become her career with performances local to her region, before being picked up by [[Kidz Bop]] in 2007. After leaving them, she continued with local and regional performances before releasing ''Crazy Good'' in 2010. Her second album, ''Bridges'', followed in 2014, and included duets with several high-profile country music stars. A third album, ''Dress Up This Town'', followed a year later, and she has performed at the [[Grand Ole Opry]] and was featured on ''[[The Voice (U.S. TV series)|The Voice]]''.

==Early years==
Mary Sarah Gross<ref>[http://marysarah.com Mary Sarah]</ref> was born in Tulsa, Oklahoma on July 7, 1995, to Richard and Patricia Gross. She has one sister, Emilee Elizabeth (b. 1993), one brother, Christopher Thomas (b. 1989){{Citation needed|date=December 2022}}, and one step brother, Richard Tyler<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://rtylergross.wordpress.com/2010/07/01/new-music/ | title=New Music | date=July 1, 2010}}</ref> who resides in Santa Barbara, CA, and works as a photographer.<ref>[http://rtylergross.com RTYLERPHOTO]</ref> Christopher was born prematurely,<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://nashvillegab.com/2016/04/mary-sarah-boot-barn-the-voice.html | last=Swirsky | first=Jen | date=April 19, 2016 | title=Mary Sarah Helps Score "The Voice" Coaches Boot Barn Boots}}</ref> received a [[liver transplant]]<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://heavy.com/entertainment/2016/05/mary-sarah-on-the-voice-instagram-youtube-photos-pics/ | title=Mary Sarah on 'The Voice': Top 10 Instagram Photos | date=May 16, 2016 | publisher=[[Heavy.com|Heavy]] | last=Weigle | first=Lauren}}</ref> and is [[diabetes mellitus type 1|Type 1 diabetic]].<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://thecountrybuzzz.wordpress.com/2013/01/27/interview-with-artist-mary-sarah/ | title=Interview with Artist "Mary Sarah" | date=January 27, 2013 | access-date=February 10, 2017 | website=The Country Buzzz}}</ref> For this reason, she supports the [[Juvenile Diabetes Research Foundation]] (JDRF). She is also the National Ambassador for Caiden's Hope,<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.caidenshope.org/mary-sarah | title=Mary Sarah | access-date=February 10, 2017}}</ref> an organization that assists with non-medical expenses related to caring for an infant in [[neonatal intensive care]].

==Singing career==
Mary Sarah began performing in church at the age of 8, and began to appear on radio stations and at several regional Opry Theaters. She was signed to the Osbrink Talent Agency in Los Angeles at age 11, and in 2007, at the age of 12, toured as a featured lead vocalist and dancer in [[Kidz Bop]] for six months.<ref>{{Cite AV media | url=http://www.kidzbop.com/videos/watch/kidz-bop-presents/music/4140/Rockin-around-the-kidzbop-tree.html | title=Mary Sarah's Rockin Around The KidzBop Tree Video | publisher=Kidzbop | archiveurl=https://archive.today/20130411051905/http://www.kidzbop.com/videos/watch/kidz-bop-presents/music/4140/Rockin-around-the-kidzbop-tree.html | archivedate=April 11, 2013 | df= }}</ref> At the age of 14, she left the agency and returned to Texas, where she performed in local and regional events.

In 2010 she released her first album, ''Crazy Good''.

In January 2011, Mary Sarah was invited to meet [[The Oak Ridge Boys]] after [[Joe Bonsall]] saw a video of her performance of "Don't Stop Believing" at the Sugar Land (Texas) Idol competition.<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.heraldmailmedia.com/life/the-voice-s-mary-sarah-to-perform-in-three-concerts/article_bec3c157-e902-55f8-975a-64f9aa50300e.html | title=The Voice's Mary Sarah to perform in three concerts in area for charity | last=Schelle | first=Crystal | date=October 9, 2016 | access-date=February 8, 2017 | website=Herald Mail Media}}</ref> After visiting with them backstage before the show at the Galveston Grand Opera House, Joe and [[Duane Allen]] invited her onstage to sing "[[Where the Boys Are]]".<ref>{{Cite AV media | title=Where The Boys ~ Connie Francis ~ by Mary Sarah w/ @OakridgeBoys | url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-WrU6l9_71c | date=March 8, 2011 | publisher=Mary Sarah | website=YouTube | location= Galveston Grand Opera House }}</ref> She credits the support and love of The Oak Ridge Boys for helping her career move forward.<ref>{{Cite AV media | url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RrOiWxmZXIs | title=Mary Sarah on Why She Owes the Oak Ridge Boys | publisher=[[Associated Press]] | date=August 22, 2014 | website=YouTube }}</ref>

In 2011, she hosted a four-part series for the 2011 season of "OPRY on the Square – Country Now and Then" at the Sugar Land, TX, town square.<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://opryonthesquare.com/artists/mary-sarah/ | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110607033530/http://opryonthesquare.com/artists/mary-sarah/ | archive-date=June 7, 2011 | publisher=Opry on the Square | title=Mary Sarah: Houston 15-Year-Old Already Headed for the Top Ten | last=Sweetland | first=Phil }}</ref> In December 2011, the Houston Texans held a vote-in contest to see who would sing the National Anthem for their annual Home for the Holidays game. Mary Sarah was the winner over 7 other finalists.<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.houstontexans.com/fan-zone/national-anthem.html | title=Home for the Holidays National Anthem Contest | publisher=[[Houston Texans]] | access-date=February 8, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web | first=Joey | last=Guerra | url=http://www.29-95.com/music/story/mary-sarah-sing-national-anthem-texans | title=Mary Sarah to sing National Anthem for Texans | publisher=29-95.com | date=December 14, 2011 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120110165240/http://www.29-95.com/music/story/mary-sarah-sing-national-anthem-texans | archive-date=January 10, 2012}}</ref> In April 2014, Mary Sarah appeared on the "Opry Country Classics" with [[Lynn Anderson]] and The Oakridge Boys, honoring the late [[Ray Price (musician)|Ray Price]]<ref name="marysarah_billboard_chuckdauphin" />

On July 8, 2014, Mary Sarah released her second album, ''Bridges''.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/dolly-parton-merle-haggard-willie-nelson-duet-with-teen-prodigy-20140708 | title=Dolly Parton, Merle Haggard, Willie Nelson Duet With Teen Prodigy | publisher=Rolling Stone | date=July 8, 2014 | last=Leahey | first=Andrew | access-date=February 8, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.broadwayworld.com/bwwmusic/article/Cleopatra-Records-to-Release-Mary-Sarah-BRIDGES-78-Feat-Dolly-Parton-Willie-Nelson-Merle-Haggard-Oak-Ridge-Boys-More-20140701 | title=Cleopatra Records to Release Mary Sarah 'BRIDGES' Feat. Dolly Parton & More, 7/8 | publisher=BWW Music World | date=July 1, 2014 | access-date=February 8, 2017}}</ref> Included on the album is a rendition of "Where the Boys Are" with [[Neil Sedaka]]. [[The Oak Ridge Boys]] also appear on the album singing "Dream On" with her. Several major country stars also join Mary in duet.<ref name="marysarah_billboard_chuckdauphin">{{Cite web | url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-615/6055537/spotlight-mary-sarah-bridges-album-song-premiere | title=615 Spotlight: Mary Sarah Builds 'Bridges' With Country Icons (Song Premiere) | publisher=Billboard | last=Dauphin | first=Chuck | access-date=February 8, 2017 | date=April 16, 2014 }}</ref> On July 29, 2014, Mary Sarah appeared on [[Mike Huckabee]]'s show on [[Fox News]] to promote her new album. The Governor played bass in accompaniment as they performed "[[The Fightin' Side of Me]]".<ref>{{Cite AV media | url=http://video.foxnews.com/v/3703204679001/the-fighting-side-of-me/ | title='The Fighting Side of Me' | website=[[Fox News]] | date=July 29, 2014}}</ref>

On May 31, 2016, Mary Sarah made her Grand Ole Opry debut.<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://opry.com/artist/mary-sarah | title=Mary Sarah | website=Grand Ole Opry | access-date=February 8, 2017}}</ref>  Mary Sarah has appeared a total of 5 times since that date.

On October 31, 2017, ''[[Deadline Hollywood]]'' announced that Mary Sarah was selected to be a lead performer in ''[[The Voice: Neon Dreams]]''.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://deadline.com/2017/10/the-voice-neon-dreams-las-vegas-nbc-hard-rock-cafe-casino-alisan-porter-1202199010/ | title='The Voice: Neon Dreams' Set To Open In Las Vegas With Former Contestants | date=October 31, 2017 | author=Ramos, Dino-Ray | publisher=[[Deadline Hollywood]]}}</ref>

===''The Voice'' (2016)===
On February 29, 2016, Mary Sarah auditioned on [[The Voice (U.S. season 10)|season 10]] of ''[[The Voice (U.S. TV series)|The Voice]]'' with "[[Where the Boys Are (Connie Francis song)|Where the Boys Are]]". All four coaches turned their chairs for her, and she chose [[Blake Shelton]] as her coach. She defeated Justin Whisnant in the battles, and defeated Brittany Kennell in the knockouts.<ref>{{Cite web | last=Norwin | first=Alyssa | title='The Voice' Recap: Christina Aguilera Is Back With A Vengeance As Season 10 Begins | url=http://hollywoodlife.com/2016/02/29/the-voice-premiere-season-10-live-blog/ | publisher=Hollywoodlife.com | access-date=February 29, 2016 | date=February 29, 2016}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web | last=Bentley | first=Jean | title=The Voice's Tenth Season Kicks Off With Some Truly Outstanding Blind Auditions | url=http://www.eonline.com/news/744730/the-voice-s-tenth-season-kicks-off-with-some-truly-outstanding-blind-auditions | website=E! Online | access-date=February 29, 2016 | date=February 29, 2016 | publisher=Deadline}}</ref> She placed in the middle three in the semifinals and was then eliminated in the Instant Save performances, placing her in the Top Six. She lost a "twitter save" to [[Laith Al Saadi]], the bearded blues guitarist/rocker from Ann Arbor Michigan in a sing off, exiting the show in the 5th position.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://deadline.com/2017/10/the-voice-neon-dreams-las-vegas-nbc-hard-rock-cafe-casino-alisan-porter-1202199010/|title = 'The Voice: Neon Dreams' Set to Open in Las Vegas with Former Contestants|date = November 2017}}</ref>
