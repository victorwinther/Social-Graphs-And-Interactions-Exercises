'''The Reklaws''' are a Canadian country music duo from [[North Dumfries, Ontario]], formed in 2012.<ref name="emerging_artists"/>  The duo consists of siblings Stuart and Jenna Walker.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://globalnews.ca/news/5059194/reklaws-canadian-day-london/ |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190318022922/https://globalnews.ca/news/5059194/reklaws-canadian-day-london/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=18 March 2019 |title=Juno-nominated band The Reklaws spending the 'most Canadian day' in London |author=Liny Lamberink |date=15 March 2019 |website=globalnews.ca |publisher=[[Global News]] |access-date=17 March 2019 |quote=}}</ref> They have released three albums, ''[[Freshman Year (album)|Freshman Year]]'', ''[[Sophomore Slump (album)|Sophomore Slump]]'', and ''[[Good Ol' Days (album)|Good Ol' Days]]''.<ref>{{cite web|first=Nanci|last=Dagg|title=It was a twofold delivery day for platinum selling country music duo The Reklaws with the release of their album Sophomore Slump and video for "Not Gonna Not" |url=https://canadianbeats.ca/2020/10/19/it-was-a-twofold-delivery-day-for-platinum-selling-country-music-duo-the-reklaws-with-the-release-of-their-album-sophomore-slump-and-video-for-not-gonna-not/ |work=Canadian Beats Media|date=October 19, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|first=Melissa|last=Wiseman|title=The Reklaws share their new album, Good Ol' Days|url=https://canadianbeats.ca/2022/11/18/the-reklaws-share-their-new-album-good-ol-days|work=Canadian Beats Media|date=November 18, 2022|access-date=November 22, 2022}}</ref> The duo has achieved four number one hits with "[[Feels Like That]]",  "[[Can't Help Myself (Dean Brody and The Reklaws song)|Can't Help Myself]]", "[[11 Beers]]", and "[[Honky Tonkin' About]]" on the [[Canada Country]] chart, in addition to multiple gold and platinum certified singles.<ref name="CAN Country"/><ref name="MC certs"/>

==Origins==
The Walker siblings grew up in [[North Dumfries, Ontario|North Dumfries]], [[Ontario]] and are two in a family of seven.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.umusic.ca/2019/01/04/meet-country-duo-reklaws/ |title=MEET: Brother-Sister Country Duo, The Reklaws |author=<!--Not stated--> |date=4 January 2019 |website=umusic.ca |publisher=[[Universal Music Group|Umusic]] |access-date=18 March 2019 |quote=}}</ref>   Their parents owned and operated the Yee Haw Adventure Farm, where they used to perform for visitors. They attended Glenview Park Secondary School in Cambridge, Ontario. They would regularly perform at Talent Shows for the school. <ref>
{{cite web |url=https://www.therecord.com/news-story/9224413-reklaws-have-big-shoes-to-fill/ |title=Reklaws have big shoes to fill |author=Joel Rubinoff |date=17 March 2019 |website=therecord.ca |publisher=[[Metroland Media Group|Metroland Media]] |access-date=17 March 2019 |quote=}}</ref>

They got their band name from their mother, who suggested the Reklaws sounded more interesting than the Walkers, Sibling Rivalry, or Bro-Sis.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://theboot.com/who-are-the-reklaws/ |title=Who Are The Reklaws? 5 Things You Need to Know |author=Liv Stecker |date=7 November 2018 |website=theboot.com |publisher=[[Townsquare Media]] |access-date=18 March 2019 |quote=}}</ref>  Reklaw is Walker spelled backwards.

They were nominated for the CCMA Discovery Artist Award in 2013 and then won the Emerging Artist Showcase at the [[Boots and Hearts Music Festival]] in 2013.<ref name="seeing_stars">{{cite web |url=https://www.cambridgetimes.ca/whatson-story/4627957-the-reklaws-are-seeing-stars-with-first-ever-music-video/ |title=The Reklaws are Seeing Stars with first-ever music video |author=Richard Vivian |date=13 July 2014 |website=cambridgetimes.ca |publisher=[[Metroland Media Group|Metroland Media]] |access-date=17 March 2019 |quote=}}</ref> They met with several Nashville songwriters before being signed by [[Universal Music Group|Universal Music Canada]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://kawarthanow.com/2018/07/26/peterborough-musicfest-tebey-with-the-reklaws/ |title=Tebey with The Reklaws: a free double shot of Canadian country on July 28 |author=Paul Rellinger |date=26 July 2018 |website=kawarthanow.com |publisher=kawarthaNow |access-date=18 March 2019 |quote=}}</ref>

==Career==
===2017–2019: ''Feels Like That'' and ''Freshman Year''===
The duo scored their first national country music hit in 2017 with "[[Hometown Kids]]".<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.cambridgetimes.ca/whatson-story/8740185-cambridge-s-hometown-kids-make-strides-across-canada-with-three-ccma-nominations/ |title=Cambridge's 'Hometown Kids' make strides across Canada with three CCMA nominations |author=Bill Doucet |date=13 July 2018 |website=cambridgetimes.ca |publisher=[[Metroland Media Group|Metroland Media]] |access-date=17 March 2019 |quote=}}</ref>

In 2018, they released the EP ''[[Long Live the Night]]''. The EP's title track was released in both a "regular" version and a [[Canadian Football League]]-themed rewrite which served as a theme song for ''[[CFL on TSN]]'' games.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.reddeeradvocate.com/news/country-star-gord-bamford-and-the-reclaws-perform-free-games-concert-friday/ |title=Country star Gord Bamford and The Reklaws perform free Games concert Friday |author=Lana Michelin |date=20 February 2019 |website=reddeeradvocate.com |publisher=[[Black Press Media]] |access-date=17 March 2019 |quote=}}</ref> Their [[Extended Play|EP]] ''[[Feels Like That]]'' was released in 2018 and received a [[Juno Award]] nomination for [[Juno Award for Country Album of the Year|Country Album of the Year]].<ref name="2019_juno">{{cite web |url=https://www.cbc.ca/music/junos/news/shawn-mendes-and-the-weeknd-lead-the-2019-juno-nominations-1.4992669 |title=Shawn Mendes and the Weeknd lead the 2019 Juno nominations |author=<!--Not stated--> |date=29 January 2019 |website=cbc.ca |publisher=[[Canadian Broadcasting Corporation|CBC]] |access-date=17 March 2019 |quote=}}</ref> Later in the year they won the Rising Star Award at the [[Canadian Country Music Association]] Awards,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.cambridgetimes.ca/whatson-story/8894386-big-week-for-cambridge-s-the-reklaws-culminates-with-ccma-award/ |title=Big week for Cambridge's The Reklaws culminates with CCMA award |author=Bill Doucet |date=12 September 2018 |website=cambridgetimes.ca |publisher=[[Metroland Media Group|Metroland Media]] |access-date=17 March 2019 |quote=}}</ref> and performed the kickoff show at the [[2018 Grey Cup]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://globalnews.ca/news/4304076/reklaws-country-cfl-grey-cup-edmonton/ |title=The Reklaws performing at Grey Cup kickoff show in Edmonton |author=[[Canadian Press]] |date=28 June 2018 |website=globalnews.ca |publisher=[[Global News]] |accessdate=17 March 2019 |quote=}}</ref> The track "Feels Like That" became the duo's first Number One [[Canada Country]] hit in January 2019.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://arcadesongs.com/the-reklaws-feels-like-that-no-1-in-canada/|title=THE REKLAWS "FEELS LIKE THAT" NO. 1 IN CANADA|work=Arcade Songs|accessdate=January 1, 2021}}</ref>

In August 2019, the Reklaws released their debut full-length album, ''[[Freshman Year (album)|Freshman Year]]'' on [[Universal Music Canada]].<ref>{{cite web|title=BREAKOUT COUNTRY MUSIC STARS, THE REKLAWS, RELEASE HIGHLY ANTICIPATED ALBUM, FRESHMAN YEAR, OUT NOW|url=http://www.umusic.ca/press-releases/breakout-country-music-stars-the-reklaws-release-highly-anticipated-album-freshman-year-out-now/|work=[[Universal Music Canada|UMusic.ca]]|date=August 27, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|first=Taryn|last=McElheran|title=Review – The Reklaws|url=https://canadianbeats.ca/2019/09/07/review-the-reklaws-3/|work=Canadian Beats Media|date=September 7, 2019}}</ref> The album included the previously released singles "[[Hometown Kids]]", "[[Long Live the Night]]", and "[[Feels Like That]]", in addition to the top ten hits "[[I Do Too]]" and "[[Old Country Soul]]".<ref>{{cite web|title=THE REKLAWS RELEASE HIGHLY ANTICIPATED ALBUM 'FRESHMAN YEAR' |url=http://www.soundzine.ca/2019/08/the-reklaws-release-highly-anticipated.html|work=SoundZine|date=August 29, 2019}}</ref>

Their song "[[Roots (World Junior Song)|Roots]]" was selected as the official song for [[The Sports Network|TSN]]'s broadcast of the [[IIHF World U20 Championship|IIHF World Junior Hockey Championship]] in [[2019 World Junior Ice Hockey Championships|2019]] and [[2020 World Junior Ice Hockey Championships|2020]].<ref>{{cite web|first=John R|last=Kennedy|url=https://www.iheartradio.ca/news/listen-the-reklaws-debut-roots-at-world-juniors-1.8635215|title=LISTEN: The Reklaws Debut 'Roots' At World Juniors|work=[[IHeartRadio#Canada|iHeartRadio.ca]]|publisher=[[Bell Media]]|date=December 28, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|first=Shakiel|last=Mahjouri|title=The Reklaws Share Their Canadian 'Roots' With New Single|url=https://etcanada.com/news/563450/the-reklaws-share-their-canadian-roots-with-new-single/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210225213407/https://etcanada.com/news/563450/the-reklaws-share-their-canadian-roots-with-new-single/|url-status=dead|archive-date=February 25, 2021|work=[[Entertainment Tonight Canada|ET Canada]]|date=December 24, 2019}}</ref>

===2020–present: ''Sophomore Slump'', and ''Good Ol' Days''===
In February 2020, the Reklaws joined [[Dean Brody]] on the single "[[Can't Help Myself (Dean Brody and The Reklaws song)|Can't Help Myself]]".<ref>{{cite web |title= Dean Brody and The Reklaws on their new single 'Can't Help Myself' |url= https://globalnews.ca/video/6660244/dean-brody-and-the-reklaws-on-their-new-single-cant-help-myself |access-date=June 1, 2020|work=Global News|publisher=Corus Media}}</ref> The song would top the [[Canada Country]] chart and set the record for most played song ever at Canadian country radio in a single week on the Nielsen BDS charts with 1782 spins.<ref>{{cite web|url= https://www.fyimusicnews.ca/articles/2020/06/08/music-news-digest-june-8-2020 |title= Music News Digest, June 8, 2020 |work=FYI Music News|date=June 8, 2020|access-date=June 23, 2020}}</ref> In October 2020, the Reklaws released their second album, ''[[Sophomore Slump (album)|Sophomore Slump]]'', featuring their singles, "[[Where I'm From (The Reklaws song)|Where I'm From]]" and "[[Not Gonna Not]]".<ref>{{cite web|first=Katie|last=Colley|title=The Reklaws Debut New Album 'Sophomore Slump', Drop New Music Video For 'Not Gonna Not'|url=https://etcanada.com/news/703343/the-reklaws-debut-new-album-sophomore-slump-drop-new-music-video-for-not-gonna-not/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201020112401/https://etcanada.com/news/703343/the-reklaws-debut-new-album-sophomore-slump-drop-new-music-video-for-not-gonna-not/|url-status=dead|archive-date=October 20, 2020|work=[[Entertainment Tonight Canada|ET Canada]]|date=October 16, 2020}}</ref>

In May 2021, they independently released the promotional single "[[What the Truck]]" with fellow Canadian country artist [[Sacha (singer)|Sacha]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Premiere: The Reklaws, Sacha Take a Backroad in "What The Truck" Video|first=Jessica|last=Nicholson|url=http://www.cmt.com/news/1834962/premiere-the-reklaws-sacha-what-the-truck-video/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210614235310/http://www.cmt.com/news/1834962/premiere-the-reklaws-sacha-what-the-truck-video/|url-status=dead|archive-date=June 14, 2021|date=June 14, 2021|work=[[CMT (American TV channel)|CMT]]|access-date=June 22, 2021}}</ref> The song debuted with over 450,000 streams in its first week, setting the record for the most streamed Canadian country song in a single week. It then became the fastest Canadian country song to reach 1 Million domestic streams.<ref>{{Cite web|first=Nanci|last=Dagg|url=https://canadianbeats.ca/2021/06/20/the-reklaws-viral-tiktok-smash-hit-what-the-truck-feat-sacha-continues-to-break-records/|title=The Reklaws viral TikTok smash hit "What the Truck" feat. Sacha continues to break records|work=Canadian Beats Media|date=June 20, 2021|accessdate=June 21, 2021}}</ref> Alongside [[Brett Kissel]], they featured on the single "Somewhere to Drink" by their new labelmate [[Nate Haller]] in September 2021.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://onlineradiobox.com/track/522019625291452051/|title=Somewhere To Drink - Nate Haller (f. B.Kissel, Reklaws)|work=Online Radio Box|date=March 11, 2022}}</ref> In March 2022, they released the single "[[11 Beers]]" with American country artist [[Jake Owen]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.iheartradio.ca/purecountry/ottawa/video/the-reklaws-drop-new-song-with-jake-owen-1.17330653|title=The Reklaws Drop New Song with Jake Owen!|work=Pure Country 94|date=March 11, 2022|publisher=[[iHeartRadio Canada]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://canadianbeats.ca/2022/03/13/the-reklaws-drop-new-single-11-beers-featuring-jake-owen/|title=The Reklaws drop new single, "11 Beers" featuring Jake Owen|work=Canadian Beats Media|first=Nanci|last=Dagg|date=March 13, 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://etcanada.com/video/3ca32e5c-a17e-11ec-9cc7-0242ac110005/the-reklaws-talk-full-circle-moment-collaborating-with-jake-owen-on-11-beers/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220312183849/https://etcanada.com/video/3ca32e5c-a17e-11ec-9cc7-0242ac110005/the-reklaws-talk-full-circle-moment-collaborating-with-jake-owen-on-11-beers/|url-status=dead|archive-date=March 12, 2022|title=The Reklaws Talk 'Full Circle Moment' Collaborating With Jake Owen On '11 Beers'|date=March 11, 2022|work=[[Entertainment Tonight Canada|ET Canada]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=The Reklaws drop new single '11 Beers'|url=https://saskatoon.ctvnews.ca/video?clipId=2400568|date=March 11, 2022|work=[[CTV News]] Saskatoon}}</ref> They released their third studio album ''[[Good Ol' Days (album)|Good Ol' Days]]'' on Starseed Records on November 4, 2022.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://entertainment-focus.com/2022/11/04/the-reklaws-good-ol-days-review/|title=The Reklaws – 'Good Ol' Days' Review|first=James|last=Daykin|date=November 4, 2022|work=Entertainment Focus|access-date=November 14, 2022}}</ref>

In 2023, they participated in an all-star recording of [[Serena Ryder]]'s single "[[What I Wouldn't Do]]", which was released as a charity single to benefit [[Kids Help Phone]]'s Feel Out Loud campaign for youth mental health.<ref>Brent Furdyk, [https://web.archive.org/web/20230302170512/https://etcanada.com/news/972900/feel-out-loud-alessia-cara-serena-ryder-more-canadian-artists-collaborate-on-new-single-promoting-youth-mental-health-initiative/ "Feel Out Loud: Alessia Cara, Serena Ryder & More Canadian Artists Collaborate On New Single Promoting Youth Mental Health Initiative"]. ''[[Entertainment Tonight Canada]]'', March 2, 2023.</ref> Later that year, they collaborated with [[Drake Milligan]] on their single "[[Honky Tonkin' About]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://countrynow.com/new-country-songs-you-need-to-hear-right-now-the-reklaws-with-drake-millian-emily-ann-roberts-more/|title=New Country Songs You Need To Hear Right Now: The Reklaws with Drake Milligan, Emily Ann Roberts, & More|date=July 28, 2023|work=Country Now|first=Madeleine|last=O'Connell}}</ref> In 2024, they released the single "I Grew Up on a Farm".<ref>{{cite web|url= https://frontporchmusic.ca/reklaws-single-i-grew-up-on-a-farm/|title=The Reklaws Honour Their Country Roots in New Single "I Grew Up On A Farm"|first=Lily|last=Braendle|date=February 20, 2024|work=Front Porch Music|access-date=June 11, 2024}}</ref> That same year, they appeared on [[America's Got Talent season 19|season 19]] of the [[reality television]] show ''[[America's Got Talent]]'', where they reached the quarterfinals stage.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://toronto.citynews.ca/2024/08/28/canadian-country-duo-the-reklaws-americas-got-talent/|title=Canadian country duo The Reklaws fail to advance on ‘America’s Got Talent’|date=August 28, 2024|publisher=[[CityNews]]|author=[[Canadian Press]]|access-date=September 2, 2024}}</ref>
