'''John & Audrey Wiggins''' was an American [[country music]] duo formerly signed to [[Mercury Records]]. The duo consisted of John Wiggins and his sister Audrey, both of whom alternated as lead vocalists. They recorded two studio albums for [[PolyGram]]/[[Mercury Records]] between 1994 and 1997, in addition to charting four singles on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs]] charts. John has since become a Nashville songwriter, with cuts by [[Joe Nichols]], [[Blake Shelton]], [[Randy Houser]], and others.

==Biography==
John Wayne Wiggins was born on October 13, 1962, in [[Nashville, Tennessee]], and his sister, Audrey, was born December 26, 1967, in [[Asheville, North Carolina]].<ref name="my">{{cite web |url=http://www.mywire.com/a/Muze//2224293?extID=10051 |title=John & Audrey Wiggins biography |accessdate=2008-10-03 |date=2007-02-01 |work=MyWire}}</ref> They had been singers since childhood,<ref>[{{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p45017|pure_url=yes}} allmusic ((( John Wiggins > Overview)))]</ref> and their father, Johnny Wiggins, was the "Singing Bus Driver" on [[Ernest Tubb]]'s 1960s tours.<ref name="new">{{cite journal|last=Cannon|first=Bob|date=September 1994|title=Album reviews|journal=New Country|pages=57–58|issn=1074-536X}}</ref> John made his singing debut at age four, and for the next several years, they performed in their father's band.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://mountaingrownmusic.org/johnny-wiggins.html|title=Carrying on the dream|last=Callaway|first=Todd|work=Mountain Grown Music|accessdate=16 November 2010}}</ref> In the 1980s, the Wiggins siblings and [[Clinton Gregory]] joined the house band at a [[North Carolina]] venue called The Stompin' Ground.<ref name="my"/>

They signed to [[PolyGram]]/[[Mercury Records]] in 1994, shortly before their father died.<ref name="my"/> Working with producers Jim Cotton and Joe Scaife, they released their self-titled debut that year. The album produced three chart entries on the U.S. ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs]] charts, including the No.&nbsp;22 "Has Anybody Seen Amy". In 1995, 1996, and 1997, John & Audrey Wiggins received [[Country Music Association]] nominations for Duo of the Year.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cmt.com/news/country-music/1472579/the-vocal-duo-dilemma-they-also-serve-who-also-run.jhtml|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121022221601/http://www.cmt.com/news/country-music/1472579/the-vocal-duo-dilemma-they-also-serve-who-also-run.jhtml|url-status=dead|archive-date=October 22, 2012|title=The Vocal Duo Dilemma: They Also Serve Who Also Run|last=Morris|first=Edwards|date=10 August 2000|work=[[Country Music Television|CMT]].com|accessdate=16 November 2010}}</ref> A second album, ''The Dream'', followed in 1997 under the production of [[Dann Huff]]. and the only charting release from it was "Somewhere in Love" which peaked at No.&nbsp;49 on the country charts.

In the 2000s, John found work as a songwriter in [[Nashville, Tennessee]]. Among his cuts were the singles "[[Tequila Makes Her Clothes Fall Off]]" by [[Joe Nichols]];<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20070930014746/http://www.cmt.com/artists/news/1521116/20060118/nichols_joe.jhtml?headlines=true&rsspartner=rssyahoo CMT.com : Joe Nichols : Joe Nichols' Latest No. 1 Draws Big Crowd]</ref> "[[Anything Goes (Randy Houser song)|Anything Goes]]" by [[Randy Houser]];<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gactv.com/gac/nw_headlines/article/0,,GAC_26063_5942868,00.html |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120731131324/http://www.gactv.com/gac/nw_headlines/article/0,,GAC_26063_5942868,00.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=31 July 2012 |title=Randy Houser Finds All "Goes" Well |date=28 April 2009 |work=[[Great American Country]] |accessdate=11 April 2010 }}</ref> and "[[Who Are You When I'm Not Looking]]" by Nichols and by [[Blake Shelton]].
