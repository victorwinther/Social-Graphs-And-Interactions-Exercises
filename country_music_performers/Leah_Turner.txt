'''Leah Turner''' (born July 29, 1987) is a Mexican-American [[country music]] singer who was previously signed with [[Columbia Records|Columbia Nashville]].

== Early life and education ==
Turner was born on July 29, 1987, in California.<ref name='musicrow'>{{Cite web |url=http://www.musicrow.com/2013/07/columbia-nashville-adds-to-artist-roster/ |title=Columbia Nashville Adds To Artist Roster |last=Nicholson |first=Jessica |date=July 30, 2013 |website=[[MusicRow]] |access-date=July 29, 2016}}</ref><ref name='taste'>{{Cite web |url=http://tasteofcountry.com/leah-turner-grit-and-glamour/ |title=Leah Turner Interview: Rodeo-Raised Newcomer Is Equal Parts Grit and Glamour |last=Dukes |first=Billy |date=August 28, 2013 |website=TasteofCountry.com |access-date=July 29, 2016}}</ref> She was raised on a ranch with horses, goats, and chickens in [[Morongo Valley, California]].<ref name='ascap'>{{Cite web |url=http://www.ascap.com/playback/2013/08/faces-places/nashville/leah-turner.aspx |title=ASCAP New Member Profile: Leah Turner |date=September 12, 2013 |website=[[American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers]] |access-date=July 29, 2016}}</ref><ref name='taste'/><ref name='cmtbio'>{{Cite web |url=http://www.cmt.com/artists/leah-turner/biography/ |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140129042444/http://www.cmt.com/artists/leah-turner/biography/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=January 29, 2014 |title=Leah Turner Bio on CMT |website=[[CMT (U.S. TV channel)|CMT]] |access-date=July 29, 2016}}</ref> Turner attended the [[University of California, Santa Barbara]] and took music and songwriting classes.<ref name='ascap'/><ref name='cmtbio'/><ref name='oprybio'>{{Cite web |url=http://www.opry.com/artist/leah-turner |title=Leah Turner Bio at the Opry |website=[[Grand Ole Opry]] |access-date=July 29, 2016}}</ref> While at UCSB, she sang in front of [[Kenny Loggins]] who convinced her to move to Los Angeles, California to pursue her career.<ref name='ascap'/><ref name='cmtbio'/><ref name='oprybio'/>

== Career ==
Turner took Kenny Loggins' advice and moved to Los Angeles to pursue a singing career.  She worked with [[Humberto Gatica]] and [[David Foster]], but moved to [[Nashville, Tennessee]] to pursue a country career.<ref name='ascap'/><ref name='cmtbio'/><ref name='oprybio'/>

Turner was signed to [[Columbia Records|Columbia Nashville]] in July 2013.<ref name='musicrow'/><ref name='ascap'/><ref name='cmtbio'/><ref name='oprybio'/> Her debut single, "Take the Keys," was co-written with [[Cary Barlowe]] and [[Jesse Frasure]] and released on October 7, 2013.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://radio.com/2013/10/19/new-music-to-know-leah-turners-star-studded-journey-from-l-a-to-nashville/ |title=New Music To Know: Leah Turner's Star-Studded Journey From L.A. To Nashville |last=Wolff |first=Kurt |date=October 19, 2013 |website=Radio.com |publisher=[[CBS Radio]] |access-date=July 29, 2016}}</ref> The song debuted at number 60 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Country Airplay]] chart dated for the week ending October 19, 2013, and peaked at number 37 while spending 23 weeks total listed.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.cmt.com/news/country-music/1715500/billy-curringtons-hey-girl-docks-at-no-1-crash-my-party-still-top-album.jhtml |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131014144840/http://www.cmt.com/news/country-music/1715500/billy-curringtons-hey-girl-docks-at-no-1-crash-my-party-still-top-album.jhtml |url-status=dead |archive-date=October 14, 2013 |title=Billy Currington's "Hey Girl" Docks at No. 1, Crash My Party Still Top Album |last=Morris |first=Edward |date=October 11, 2013 |website=[[CMT (U.S. TV channel)|CMT]] |access-date=July 29, 2016}}</ref><ref name='billboardchart'>{{Cite magazine |url=http://www.billboard.com/artist/5880356/leah-turner/chart |title=Leah Turner Chart History |magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=July 29, 2016}}</ref><ref name='billboardsofter'>{{Cite magazine |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/columns/the-615/6049831/leah-turner-shows-softer-side-on-new-single-pull-me-back |title=Leah Turner Shows 'Softer Side' on New Single 'Pull Me Back' |last=Dauphin |first=Chuck |date=April 11, 2014 |magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]] |access-date=July 29, 2016}}</ref> Her second single, "Pull Me Back," spent one week on the ''Billboard'' Country Airplay chart at number 52.<ref name='billboardchart'/><ref name='billboardsofter'/>
