'''Noel Lee Haggard''' (born September 4, 1963) is an American [[country music]] artist.

Haggard is the son of country music legend [[Merle Haggard]].<ref>[http://www.truecountry.com/noelbio.shtml Noel Haggard Biography]</ref> He was signed to a recording deal with [[Atlantic Records]] and released his debut album, ''One Lifetime'', in 1997. The album produced two singles, both of which peaked at No.&nbsp;75 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs|Hot Country Singles & Tracks]] chart.<ref>[{{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p201703/charts-awards|pure_url=yes}} Noel Haggard Singles]</ref>

His first single, "Once You Learn," also peaked at No.&nbsp;72 on the ''[[RPM (magazine)|RPM]]'' Country Tracks chart in Canada. His second single, "[[Tell Me Something Bad About Tulsa]]," was covered by [[George Strait]] on his 2003 album ''[[Honkytonkville]]''. Strait's version of the song peaked at No.&nbsp;11 on the Hot Country Singles & Tracks chart.

Haggard was also featured on the [[Maverick (soundtrack)|soundtrack]] of the 1994 film ''[[Maverick (film)|Maverick]]'' as a member of the "Maverick Choir.".<ref>[https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0110478/soundtrack Maverick (1994) - Soundtracks]</ref> Noel was a member of the Strangers and opened up shows for Merle his whole professional life. He continues to perform with the remaining Strangers  and his brother Ben, since the passing of Merle.
