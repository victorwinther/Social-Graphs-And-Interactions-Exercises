'''Ingrid Elizabeth Andress''' (born September 21, 1991) is an American [[country music]] singer-songwriter. Her breakthrough single, "[[More Hearts Than Mine]]" (2019) peaked at number 30 on the [[Billboard Hot 100|''Billboard'' Hot 100]]. Her single "[[The Stranger (Ingrid Andress song)|The Stranger]]" (2020) peaked at number 49 on Country Airplay and her single "[[Wishful Drinking (song)|Wishful Drinking]]" (2021) became her second entry on the ''Billboard'' Hot 100.  She has released two albums: ''[[Lady Like]]'' (2020) and ''[[Good Person]]'' (2022). She has also cowritten songs recorded by other artists including "[[Boys (Charli XCX song)|Boys]]" (2017) by [[Charli XCX]].

==Early life==
Andress was born in [[Southfield, Michigan]],<ref name="Birthplace" /> and grew up in [[Highlands Ranch, Colorado]]. She has three sisters and a brother and was mostly homeschooled until high school. Her father is former major league strength and conditioning coach Brad Andress, who worked for the  [[Detroit Tigers]], [[Colorado Rockies]], and [[New York Mets]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/mlb/story/_/id/40575650/hr-derby-anthem-singer-ingrid-andress-says-was-drunk|title=HR Derby anthem singer Ingrid Andress says she was drunk|date=July 16, 2024|website=ESPN.com}}</ref> As a child, she learned to play piano and drums. In middle school, she frequently listened to [[Coldplay]] andÂ [[Evanescence]] and she started a [[heavy metal music]] band.<ref>{{Cite news | url=https://country.iheart.com/content/2019-04-29-ingrid-andress-once-in-metal-band-now-performs-own-sound-in-nashville/ | title=Ingrid Andress Once In Metal Band, Now Performs Own Sound In Nashville}}</ref> She attended [[Rock Canyon High School]], where she participated in high school choir competitions.

==Career==
===Education and early career===
Andress attended [[Berklee College of Music]] and majored in songwriting and performance.<ref name=ghost>{{Cite news | url=https://www.thelineofbestfit.com/features/interviews/ingid-andress-ghost-writing | title=Ghost Writing | first=Jen | last=Long | work=[[The Line of Best Fit]] | date=August 26, 2022}}</ref><ref name="bio">{{cite web | url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/ingrid-andress-mn0002596016/biography | title=Ingrid Andress biography | publisher=AllMusic | access-date=October 20, 2019 | first=Matt | last=Collar | archive-date=October 20, 2019 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191020221446/https://www.allmusic.com/artist/ingrid-andress-mn0002596016/biography | url-status=live }}</ref> She joined [[a cappella]] group Pitch Slapped and performed on the [[NBC]] singing competition ''[[The Sing-Off]]''. Andress later joined the group Delilah and placed sixth. After leaving the show, Andress finished her degree in 2013 and underwent mentorship with her former teacher at Berklee and pop songwriter [[Kara DioGuardi]]. She then moved to [[Nashville, Tennessee]], and signed a publishing deal with [[Sea Gayle Music]] and [[Arthouse Entertainment]] in 2014.<ref name="publishing"/> From there, she began co-writing songs for other artists: "[[Boys (Charli XCX song)|Boys]]" (2017), recorded by [[Charli XCX]]; "In Too Deep", recorded by [[Why Don't We]], "Conflicted" (2018) recorded by [[Halestorm]]; "Girl in the Mirror" (2019) recorded by [[Bebe Rexha]]; and several songs on the ''[[No Saint]]'' (2019) album by [[Lauren Jenkins]].<ref>{{Cite news | url=https://americansongwriter.com/7-songs-you-didnt-know-ingrid-andress-wrote-for-other-artists-halestorm-bebe-rexha-charli-xcx-and-more/ | title=7 Songs You Didn't Know Ingrid Andress Wrote for Other Artistsâ€”Halestorm, Bebe Rexha, Charli XCX, and More | work=[[American Songwriter]]}}</ref>

===2018â€“2020: Breakthrough with ''Lady Like''===
Andress signed a [[recording contract]] with [[Warner Records|Warner Nashville]] and [[Atlantic Records]] in July 2018.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://musicrow.com/2018/07/ingrid-andress-signs-with-warner-music-nashville-atlantic-records/ | title=Ingrid Andress Signs With Warner Music Nashville, Atlantic Records | work=MusicRow | date=July 27, 2018 | access-date=October 20, 2019 | first=Lorie | last=Hollabaugh | archive-date=November 28, 2020 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201128125547/https://musicrow.com/2018/07/ingrid-andress-signs-with-warner-music-nashville-atlantic-records/ | url-status=live }}</ref> In February 2019, her first song with the label, "Lady Like", was released to digital platforms and was a top ten debut on [[Top Country Albums]].<ref name="leading"/>

In April 2019, she released the single "[[More Hearts Than Mine]]", which reached the top five of the American and Canadian country airplay charts.<ref name="leading"/><ref>{{cite web | url=https://tasteofcountry.com/ingrid-andress-more-hearts-than-mine-interview/ | title=Ingrid Andress Parents' Perfect Reaction to More Hearts Than Mine | work=Taste of Country | date=August 26, 2019 archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190925091147/https://tasteofcountry.com/ingrid-andress-more-hearts-than-mine-interview/ | url-status=live }}</ref>  On March 27, 2020, she released her debut studio album ''[[Lady Like]]'' through Warner Music Nashville.<ref>{{Cite magazine |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/country/ingrid-andress-lady-like-album-breakdown-9343148/ |title=Ingrid Andress' 'Lady Like' Album: Stories Behind the Songs |date=March 26, 2020 |magazine=Billboard|access-date=April 2, 2020|archive-date=January 29, reached No. 1 on the ''Billboard'' Emerging Artists chart.<ref name="leading">{{Cite web |url=https://www.billboard.com/pro/ingrid-andress-tops-emerging-artists-chart/ |title=Leading 'Lady': Ingrid Andress Hits No. 1 on Emerging Artists Chart |date=April 8, 2020 |website=billboard.com |access-date=April 9, 2020 |archive-date=June 4, 2022 Best Country Albums of the year and set the record as the highest streaming country female debut album of all time upon release.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-03-03 |title=Ingrid Andress {{!}} Official Website |url=https://www.ingridandress.com/ |access-date=2024-07-03 |website=Ingrid Andress {{!}} Official Website |language=en}}</ref>

In July 2020, she released "[[The Stranger (Ingrid Andress song)|The Stranger]]", which peaked at number 49 on Country Airplay. The deluxe edition of ''Lady Like'' was released on October 2, 2020.<ref>{{cite web |title=Future Releases for Country Radio Stations |url=https://www.allaccess.com/country/future-releases |website=All Access |access-date=September 25, 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200922020516/https://www.allaccess.com/country/future-releases |archive-date=September 22, 2020}}</ref> At the [[63rd Annual Grammy Awards]], Andress received three [[Grammy Award]]s nominations: [[Grammy Award for Best Country Song|Best Country Song]] ("More Hearts Than Mine"), [[Grammy Award for Best Country Album|Best Country Album]] (''[[Lady Like]]''), and [[Grammy Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]]; these nominations made her the only country artist in a "Big Four" category.<ref name="grammy63"/>

===2021â€“present: ''Good Person''===
In August 2021, Andress released "[[Wishful Drinking (song)|Wishful Drinking]]", a collaboration with Sam Hunt. The song peaked at number 4 on Country Airplay. The song, though originally not intended for the album, was included as a bonus track and served as the lead single to her sophomore album ''[[Good Person]]''.<ref>{{Cite magazine |last=Nicholson |first=Jessica |date=2022-08-25 |title=Ingrid Andress Returns With 'Good Person': 'The Whole Point of the Album Is Dark Versus Light' |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/country/ingrid-andress-good-person-album-1235130952/ |access-date=2023-02-26 |magazine=Billboard}}</ref> "Wishful Drinking" earned Andress her 4th Grammy Award nomination, for Best Country Duo/Group Performance at the 65th annual ceremony.

Two more singles were released from the album; "Seeing Someone Else", which became her first cross-format single and peaked top 25 on the [[Adult Top 40|Adult Pop Airplay]] chart, and "Feel Like This", the album's second country single. The album charted on both the ''Billboard'' 200 and the Top Country Albums chart.<ref name="bb200"/><ref name="uscountry"/>

==Personal life==
Andress resides in [[Nashville, Tennessee]]. In 2020, during the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], Andress broke up with her boyfriend of six years and entered a new relationship.<ref>{{Cite news | url=https://people.com/country/ingrid-andress-releases-new-album-good-person/ | title=Ingrid Andress Bares Her Broken and Mended Heart in New Album: 'Just Flip My Whole Life Over' | first=Nancy | last=Kruh | work=[[People (magazine)|People]] | date=August 26, 2022}}</ref>
