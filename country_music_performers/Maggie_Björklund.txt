'''Margrethe "Maggie" Björklund''' is a [[Danes|Danish]] guitarist and singer-songwriter who composes, plays, and sings music in the [[folk music|folk]] and [[alternative country]] genres. She began her career playing electric guitar in the female band The Darleens and briefly for the experimental pop band Miss B Haven. Björklund then started playing [[pedal steel guitar]] as a studio musician and has released two solo recordings.<ref name=MB-3ImaginaryGirls-2011>{{cite news|last1=Estey|first1=Chris|title=Imaginary Interview with Maggie Bjorklund|url=http://threeimaginarygirls.com/interview-maggie-bjorklund-she-has-big-hootenanny-cobirds-unite-others-wed-817-triple-door/|accessdate=21 October 2016|work=Three Imaginary Girls|date=11 August 2011}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Maggie Björklund was born in [[Denmark]] and grew up near [[Copenhagen]]. She took piano lessons, but switched to guitar as soon as she could. She moved to [[Hollywood, Los Angeles|Hollywood]] and attended the [[Musicians Institute]], where she was introduced to [[country music]]. She returned to Denmark after a year.<ref name=MB-Tidal-2015>{{cite news|last1=Kleinschmidt|first1=Jonas|title=Blue Steel: Maggie Björklund's Long Road|url=http://read.tidal.com/article/blue-steel-maggie-bjorklund-us/|accessdate=21 October 2016|work=TIDAL|date=20 April 2015}}</ref>

== Career ==
Back in Denmark, she formed the all-female country band Darleens, and they recorded three albums for [[Sony Records]]. After Darleens split up, she took [[pedal steel guitar]] lessons from [[Jeff Newman (musician)|Jeff Newman]] in [[Nashville, Tennessee]].<ref name=MB-15questions-ScandWay-2012>{{cite news|last1=Fischer|first1=Tobias|title=15 Questions: Maggie Björklund: The Scandinavian Way|url=http://www.15questions.net/interview/fifteen-questions-maggie-bjorklund/|accessdate=21 October 2016
|work=15 Questions|date=4 December 2012}}</ref>

Her 2011 debut ''Coming Home'' was produced by Johnny Sangster ([[The Tripwires]]). The record featured members of [[Calexico (band)|Calexico]], and guest vocalists [[Mark Lanegan]] ([[Screaming Trees]]), [[Jon Auer]] ([[The Posies]]), and Rachel Flotard ([[Visqueen (band)|Visqueen]]).<ref name=MB-Uprooted-ComingHome-2011>{{cite news|last1=Mateer|first1=Chris|title=Introducing Bloodshot Records Recording Artist: Maggie Bjorklund, Pedal Steel Songstress|url=https://www.bloodshotrecords.com/artist/maggie-bjorklund
|accessdate=21 October 2016|work=Uprooted Music Revue|date=21 March 2011}}</ref>

After the album's release, she played pedal steel with [[Jack White]]'s all-female backing band, the Peacocks.<ref name=MB-Guitar-World-2012>{{cite news|last1=Whitmore|first1=Laura|title=Interview with Maggie Björklund: On Playing Pedal Steel and Backing Jack White|url=http://www.guitarworld.com/features-interviews-blogs/interview-maggie-björklund-playing-pedal-steel-and-backing-jack-white|accessdate=21 October 2016|work=Guitar World|date=1 May 2012}}</ref> In 2011 and 2012 she performed at [[South by Southwest]] in [[Austin, Texas]]. She also toured with [[John Doe (musician)|John Doe]] and with [[Exene Cervenka]] from the band [[X band|X]].<ref name=MB-ChicagoReader-2011>{{cite news|last1=Margasak|first1=Peter|title=John Doe's beguiling pedal-steel player, Maggie Bjorklund|url=http://chicagoreader.com/Bleader/archives/2011/11/25/john-does-beguiling-pedal-steel-player-maggie-bjorklund/|accessdate=21 October 2016|work=[[Chicago Reader]]|date=25 November 2011}}</ref>

For her sophomore album ''Shaken'', released in 2014, she enlisted [[John Convertino]] ([[Calexico (band)|Calexico]]), Barb Hunter ([[Afghan Whigs]]), and Jim Barr ([[Portishead (band)|Portishead]]). [[John Parish]] (producer for [[Sparklehorse]]) was producer, and [[Kurt Wagner (musician)|Kurt Wagner]] ([[Lambchop (band)|Lambchop]]) provided a guest vocal. The songs reflected the impact of the death of her mother.<ref name=MB-popmatters-shaken-2014>{{cite news|last1=Risch|first1=Eric|title=Maggie Björklund: Shaken|url=http://www.popmatters.com/review/188564-maggie-bjoerklund-shaken/|accessdate=21 October 2016|work=[[Popmatters]]|date=12 December 2014}}</ref><ref name=MB-Spin-2014>{{cite news|last1=Young|first1=Killian|title=Maggie Bjorklund, Jack White's Guitarist, Announces New Solo Album 'Shaken'|url=http://www.spin.com/2014/08/maggie-bjorklund-shaken-missing-at-sea-stream-jack-white/|accessdate=22 October 2016|work=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin Magazine]]|date=7 August 2014}}</ref>
