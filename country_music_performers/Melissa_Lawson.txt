'''Melissa Ann Lawson''' (born April 10, 1976) is an American country music singer. On August 4, 2008, she was declared the winner of the television program ''[[Nashville Star]]'', a country music singing competition which aired on [[NBC]].<ref name="dallas">{{cite web |url=http://www.dallasnews.com/sharedcontent/dws/ent/stories/DN-nashville_0805gl.ART.State.Edition1.4d99dbb.html |title=Arlington's Melissa Lawson wins 'Nashville Star' |accessdate=2008-08-05 |work=Dallas News }}</ref> Signed to [[Warner Bros. Records]] Nashville, she released her debut single "What If It All Goes Right" that year, and charted on [[Hot Country Songs]] with it.

==Biography==
Born in [[Dallas, Texas]], Melissa Lawson grew up in [[Arlington, Texas]]. In 2004, she tried out for the [[American Idol (season 4)|fourth season]] of [[American Idol]], and made it to the final day of Hollywood week, eventually being cut in the top 75.<ref name="idolpast">{{cite magazine |url=http://popwatch.ew.com/popwatch/2008/08/qa-nashville-st.html |title='Nashville Star' Melissa Lawson on her 'Idol' past, Katie Cook's cleavage, and that songwriting controversy |accessdate=2008-10-11 |magazine=[[Entertainment Weekly]]}}</ref> After this, she competed on and won the sixth and final season of ''[[Nashville Star]]'' in 2008.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.nbc.com/breakthrough-with-tony-robbins/video/rick-and-melissa-lawson/1243636/ |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2010-08-23 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100817165326/http://www.nbc.com/breakthrough-with-tony-robbins/video/rick-and-melissa-lawson/1243636/ |archivedate=2010-08-17 }}</ref>

==Music career==
===2008-present: "What If It All Goes Right" and label release===
Following her win on ''Nashville Star'', Lawsons' debut single, "What If It All Goes Right", was released on August 5, 2008 to radio. It debuted and peaked at No.&nbsp;49 on the U.S. ''Billboard'' [[Hot Country Songs]] chart as well as No.&nbsp;79 on the ''Billboard'' [[Billboard Hot 100|Hot 100]], due to legal [[music download]]s. It was then re-released in November 2008, where it re-entered the country charts at No.&nbsp;55, and eventually reached No.&nbsp;43.

==''Nashville Star''==
Melissa Lawson was the oldest contestant among the ten finalists.<ref name="ssn">{{cite web |url=http://www.monstersandcritics.com/smallscreen/news/article_1421491.php/Nashville_Star_picks_Texan_Melissa_Lawson_as_winner |title='Nashville Star' picks Texan Melissa Lawson as winner |accessdate=2008-08-05 |work=Smallscreen News |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080808035948/http://www.monstersandcritics.com/smallscreen/news/article_1421491.php/Nashville_Star_picks_Texan_Melissa_Lawson_as_winner |archivedate=2008-08-08 }}</ref>
