'''Ruby Lovett''' (born February 16, 1967, in [[Laurel, Mississippi]]<ref name="whitburn">{{cite book|last=Whitburn|first=Joel|title=Hot Country Songs 1944 to 2008|publisher=Record Research, Inc|page=248|year=2008|isbn=978-0-89820-177-2}}</ref>) is an American [[country music]] singer-songwriter. Lovett was put up for adoption as a baby.<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|title=Ruby Lovett – Music Biography|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=mn0000299032|pure_url=yes}}|work=[[Allmusic]]|accessdate=March 12, 2013}}</ref> She cut a [[gramophone record|45 rpm]] recording of "One Day at a Time" and "Me and Jesus" when she was 6 years old.<ref name="ledger">{{cite news|last=Rush|first=Diane Samms|title=Ruby Lovett Writes About Her Own Life|url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=Lr4wAAAAIBAJ&sjid=Mv0DAAAAIBAJ&pg=6528,4192799&dq=ruby-lovett+look-what-love-can-do&hl=en|accessdate=March 12, 2013|newspaper=[[The Ledger]]|date=March 24, 1998}}</ref> She later entered local talent contests and won the True Value Country Showdown three years in a row.<ref name="ledger" /> She started writing songs at age 10 and formed a band at age 13.<ref name="allmusic" />

Lovett's mother encouraged her to perform but also to get an education.<ref name="allmusic" /> She studied to become a [[Radiographer]] and was working at a hospital in [[Nashville, Tennessee|Nashville]] when she was offered a recording contract.<ref name="people">{{cite news|title=Picks and Pans Review: Ruby Lovett|url=http://www.people.com/people/archive/article/0,,20124766,00.html|accessdate=March 12, 2013|newspaper=[[People (magazine)|People]]|date=March 23, 1998}}</ref> Lovett signed with [[Curb Records]] who released her debut album, ''Ruby Lovett'', in January 1998.<ref name="ledger" /> Its only single, "Look What Love Can Do", peaked at number 73 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs|Hot Country Singles & Tracks]] chart.<ref name="whitburn" />

===Critical reception===
Thom Owens of [[Allmusic]] gave ''Ruby Lovett'' three stars out of five, writing that it is "a fine collection of urbane country music that emphasizes her nuanced vocals and skill at interpretation."<ref>{{cite web|last=Owens|first=Thom|title=Ruby Lovett – Ruby Lovett|url={{AllMusic|class=album|id=mw0000032124|pure_url=yes}}|work=[[Allmusic]]|accessdate=March 12, 2013}}</ref> Randy Lewis of the ''[[Los Angeles Times]]'' gave the album three stars out of four, saying that it "benefits further from songs, both her own and by other writers, that prize honest emotion over cliche."<ref name="latimes">{{cite news|last=Lewis|first=Randy|title=Album Reviews|url=https://www.latimes.com/archives/la-xpm-1998-mar-28-ca-33440-story.html|access-date=March 12, 2013|newspaper=[[Los Angeles Times]]|date=March 28, 1998}}</ref> The album received a favorable review from ''[[People (magazine)|People]]'', which wrote that Lovett is "an emotional country singer" with "an eclectic mix of songs."<ref name="people" /> Chet Flippo of ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' also gave the album a positive review, saying that Lovett is the "possessor of a big, expressive voice and a confident delivery" and the album's production is "beautiful, unobtrusive – and very country."<ref>{{cite news|last=Flippo|first=Chet|title=Reviews & Previews|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=ug0EAAAAMBAJ&q=%22ruby+lovett%22&pg=PA69|accessdate=March 12, 2013|newspaper=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=January 10, 1998}}</ref> Eric Zehnbauer of ''[[Country Standard Time]]'' praised Lovett's "rich and evocative" voice and wrote that "she infuses her songs with plenty of emotion."<ref>{{cite news|last=Zehnbauer|first=Eric|title=Ruby Lovett – Ruby Lovett|url=http://www.countrystandardtime.com/d/cdreview.asp?xid=2472|accessdate=March 12, 2013|newspaper=[[Country Standard Time]]}}</ref>
