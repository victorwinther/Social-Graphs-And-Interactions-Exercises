'''The Swon Brothers''' are an American [[country music]] duo from [[Muskogee, Oklahoma]], consisting of Zach Swon (born February 21, 1985) and Colton Swon (born August 17, 1988).<ref name=cambio/><ref name=OKVOTESZACHERY>{{cite web|url=http://oklavoters.com/51/0082057.html|title=ZACHERY BLUE SWON — 21 February 1985|author=OKLAVOTES|publisher=OKLAVOTES|access-date=June 16, 2013}}</ref> In 2013, they finished in third place on the [[The Voice (U.S. season 4)|fourth season]] of [[NBC]]'s [[The Voice (U.S. TV series)|''The Voice'']].<ref name="Tulsa World 3rd place">{{cite news|last=Chancellor|first=Jennifer|title=The Swon Brothers take 3rd place in 'The Voice' finale|url=http://www.tulsaworld.com/article.aspx/The_Swon_Brothers_take_3rd_place_in_The_Voice_finale/20130619_269_D1_ULNSTe25135 |access-date=June 22, 2013|newspaper=Tulsa World|date=June 19, 2013}}</ref> They were the first duo to make it from the Top 12 live shows to the season finale.<ref name="Tulsa World showdown">{{cite news|last=Chancellor|first=Jennifer|title=Muskogee's Swon Brothers ready for 'Voice' showdown|url=http://www.tulsaworld.com/article.aspx/Muskogees_Swon_Brothers_ready_for_Voice_showdown/20130616_269_D1_CUTLIN194911 |access-date=June 22, 2013|newspaper=Tulsa World|date=June 16, 2013}}</ref>

On November 1, 2013, the Swon Brothers signed a record deal with [[Arista Nashville]] which is part of the [[Sony Music]] group.<ref>{{cite web|last=Wyland |first=Sarah |url=http://blog.gactv.com/blog/2013/11/04/the-swon-brothers-land-record-deal-with-arista-nashville/ |title=The Swon Brothers Land Record Deal with Arista Nashville &#124; GAC News & Notes |publisher=Blog.gactv.com |date=2013-11-04 |access-date=2014-04-18}}</ref> They released their first official single, "[[Later On (The Swon Brothers song)|Later On]]", the following month. On October 14, 2014, the Swon Brothers released their self-titled debut album,<ref>[http://www.swonbrothers.com/the-swon-brothers-release-self-titled-album-today-debuted-new-single-on-mondays-today-show/ The Swon Brothers Release Self-Titled Album Today; Debuted New Single on Monday's Today Show], SwonBrothers, Retrieved December 8, 2014</ref> before parting ways with Arista the following year.

==Early life==
The Swon Brothers, Zach and Colton, were born in [[Muskogee, Oklahoma]], to Kelly and Tammy Swon.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://muskogeephoenix.com/features/x383273108/Swon-Brothers-to-perform-Friday-Saturday |title=Swon Brothers to perform Friday, Saturday |date= June 17, 2010 |work=Muskogee Phoenix }}</ref><ref name="SCT reach semifinals">{{cite news|last=Remer|first=Amie|title=Swon Brothers reach semifinals of 'The Voice'|url=http://www.sequoyahcountytimes.com/news/article_870b5782-d36d-11e2-a752-0019bb30f31a.html|access-date=June 22, 2013|newspaper=Sequoyah County Times|date=June 12, 2013}}</ref>  Colton Swon was born on August 17, 1988, while Zach was born on February 21, 1985.<ref name=cambio>{{cite web |url= http://www.cambio.com/2013/06/14/the-voice-do-the-swon-brothers-ever-argue-exclusive/ |title= 'The Voice': Do the Swon Brothers Ever Argue? (EXCLUSIVE!) |author=Jeanette Batur |work=Cambio |date=June 14, 2013 }}</ref><ref name=OKVOTESZACHERY /> Colton attended elementary school in Hilldale, Muskogee, and later attended school with his brother Zach in [[Oktaha, Oklahoma|Oktaha]], where he left after eighth grade to go to Hilldale High School.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://muskogeephoenix.com/highschoolsports/x2128946627/For-the-love-of-it |title=For the love of it -Football drove this pair to Hilldale |date=October 26, 2006 |author= Steve Schmidt |work=Muskogee Phoenix }}</ref>  Zach Swon studied at the [[Northeastern State University]] and worked at the [[Oklahoma Music Hall of Fame]] in Muskogee.<ref>{{cite news |url= http://muskogeephoenix.com/features/x2128969565/Mall-plans-Gimme-the-Mike-watch-party |title= Mall plans 'Gimme the Mike' watch party |date=August 10, 2007 |work=Muskogee Phoenix }}</ref>

The brothers started performing when they were children, touring with their parents' Southern gospel group Exodus, and performing in various local events and stages.<ref name="tulsaworld">{{cite news |url=http://www.tulsaworld.com/article.aspx/Muskogees_Swon_Brothers_The_Voice_like_a_dream_Blake/20130404_275_D1_CUTLIN615041 |title=Muskogee's Swon Brothers: 'The Voice' like a dream, Blake Shelton 'like a cousin' |author= Rita Sherro |work=Tulsa World |date=April 4, 2013 }}</ref><ref name="phoenix">{{cite news |url= http://muskogeephoenix.com/local/x331657789/Wagoner-claims-Swons-too |title=Wagoner claims Swons, too |author=Liz McMahan |work=Muskogee Phoenix |date=June 17, 2013 }}</ref> By the time Zach Swon was 9 or 10, he was playing drums for his parents’ band.<ref name="phonenix2"/>  In 1995, the Swon Brothers’ parents formed a family-style variety show in [[Wagoner, Oklahoma|Wagoner]] called Westwood Music Show, where the Brothers performed songs ranging from Elvis to Frank Sinatra, as well as starting to perform in the country music genre.<ref name="phoenix"/><ref name="phonenix2"/><ref>{{cite news |url=http://muskogeephoenix.com/local/x319990720/Swons-take-TV-stage-again-tonight |title=Swons take TV stage again tonight |author=Liz McMahan |work=Musskogee Phoenix |date=June 20, 2012 }}</ref>  In 2000, when they were 12 and 15, the brothers started calling themselves The Swon Brothers, and appeared in various venues.<ref>{{cite news |url= http://muskogeephoenix.com/local/x2128983509/Muskogee-teen-going-to-Hollywood |date=January 16, 2008 |title =Muskogee teen going to Hollywood |author= Liz McMahan |work=Muskogee Phoenix }}</ref><ref name=history>{{citation |url=http://muskogeephoenix.com/local/x331658739/A-history-of-the-Swon-Brothers |title=A history of the Swon Brothers |work= Muskogee Phoenix |date=June 18, 2013 }}</ref>

In 2007, Colton Swon auditioned for the [[American Idol (season 7)|seventh season]] of ''American Idol'' (aired 2008) and reached the Top 48.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://watch.accesshollywood.com/video/the-voice:-the-swon-brothers-dish-on-making-top-8/2413583858001 |title=The Voice: The Swon Brothers Dish On Making Top 8 |author=Laura Saltman |work=Access Hollywood }}</ref>  Both Zach and Colton also appeared as contestants in 2007 on a local TV show ''Gimme the Mike''.<ref>{{cite news |url= http://muskogeephoenix.com/entertainment/x2128968357/Swon-brothers-belt-it-out-on-TV-show |title=Swon brothers belt it out on TV show |date= July 26, 2007 |work=Muskogee Phoenix }}</ref> The Swon Brothers have released a couple of independent albums with songs including "Oklahoma Lovin’" and "This Close to Gone".<ref name="phonenix2">{{cite news |url=http://muskogeephoenix.com/features/x514653349/Little-country-little-rock-n-roll-Swon-Brothers-Signs-of-Warning-play-to-help-OMHOF |title=Little country, little rock 'n' roll: Swon Brothers, Signs of Warning play to help OMHOF |author= Mike Carrels |work=Musskogee Phoenix |date=June 14, 2012 }}</ref> Their first professionally recorded album ''Another Day'', which they described as modern country, was released on January 16, 2009.<ref>{{cite news |url= http://muskogeephoenix.com/features/x2129018119/Brothers-get-ready-to-release-first-pro-album |date= January 11, 2009 |title=Brothers get ready to release first pro album |author = Travina Coleman |work=Muskogee Phoenix }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=http://muskogeephoenix.com/features/x46894874/Swons-country-TV-star-to-play-together-tonight |date= August 27, 2009 |title=Swons, country TV star to play together tonight |author = Leilani Roberts Ott |work=Muskogee Phoenix }}</ref> They cited [[The Eagles]] as their musical influence.<ref name="tulsaworld" />

==''The Voice''==
{{Main|The Voice (U.S. season 4)}}
The Swon Brothers auditioned for The Voice on a whim when asked by their keyboard player, James Redden, who wanted them to audition with him.<ref>{{cite news |url= http://muskogeephoenix.com/local/x182741319/Swons-song-nod-to-their-band |title= Swons' song nod to their band |work=Muskogee Phoenix |author= Wendy Burton|date=June 16, 2013 }}</ref> During the blind auditions, The Swon Brothers performed [[Tom Petty and the Heartbreakers]]'s "[[American Girl (Tom Petty song)|American Girl]]", turning three chairs, [[Usher (entertainer)|Usher]], [[Blake Shelton]], and [[Shakira]] on April 1, 2013. They proceeded to choose Blake as their coach and remained on his team the whole season.<ref name="Newsday superstar">{{cite news|last=Gamboa|first=Glenn|title='The Voice': Can the show produce a superstar?|url=http://www.newsday.com/entertainment/tv/reality-tv/the-voice-can-the-show-produce-a-superstar-1.5478435|access-date=June 22, 2013|newspaper=Newsday|date=June 16, 2013}}</ref> During the Top 8 Live Performances, The Swon Brothers chose to sing [[Seven Bridges Road]] as a tribute to victims of the [[2013 Moore tornado]]. They finished in third place making them the farthest duo in the voice.<ref name="Tulsa World 3rd place"/>

==Post-''The Voice'' career==

The Swon Brothers signed a recording contract with Arista Nashville after appearing in ''The Voice''.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.roughstock.com/blog/the-swon-brothers-ink-new-record-deal-with-sony-music-nashville-s-arista-label |title=The Swon Brothers Ink New Record Deal With Sony Music Nashville's Arista Label|author= Alanna Conaway|date= November 4, 2013|work=Roughstock}}</ref>  They released a single "[[Later On (The Swon Brothers song)|Later On]]" on December 10, 2013, the first single from [[The Swon Brothers (album)|their self-titled album]] released on October 14, 2014.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://newsok.com/wednesday-video-spotlight-swon-brothers-debut-later-on-on-the-voice/article/3913760 |title=Wednesday Video Spotlight: Swon Brothers debut "Later On" on "The Voice" |author=Brandy McDonnell |date= December 12, 2013 |work=NewsOK }}</ref>  They debuted the single on ''The Voice'' the same day.  It sold 23,000 copies in its debut week.<ref name="Roughstock 12-18-2013">{{cite news |title=Country Chart News - The Top 30 Digital Singles - December 18, 2013: Luke Bryan, Florida Georgia Line, Scotty McCreery & Cassadee Pope News |url=http://www.roughstock.com/blog/country-chart-news-the-top-30-digital-singles-december-18-2013-luke-bryan-florida-georgia-line-scotty-mccreery-cassadee-pope-news |last=Bjorke |first=Matt |work=Roughstock |date=2013-12-18}}</ref> In October 2015, the duo parted ways with Arista due to creative differences. On January 29, 2016, they released the EP Timeless.<ref name="Recent History">{{cite web |url=http://www.swonbrothers.com/ |title=2015-2016 History |last=The Swon Brothers |access-date=19 July 2016}}</ref> They also opened for [[Carrie Underwood]]'s [[Storyteller Tour: Stories in the Round|Storyteller Tour]] in 2016.<ref name="Recent History"/>
