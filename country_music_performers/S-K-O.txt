'''S-K-O''', originally known as '''Schuyler, Knobloch and Overstreet''', was an American [[country music]] vocal group consisting of [[Thom Schuyler]], [[J. Fred Knobloch]], and [[Paul Overstreet]].<ref name="allmusic"/> The original line-up recorded one album for [[MTM Records]] and charted three country hits, including the number one "[[Baby's Got a New Baby]]".<ref name="allmusic"/> After Overstreet departed in 1987 for a solo career, he was replaced with [[Craig Bickhardt]], and the group was renamed '''Schuyler, Knobloch, and Bickhardt''', or S-K-B for short.<ref name="allmusic"/> The renamed group released one album and four singles before disbanding in 1989.<ref name="allmusic"/>

==Biography==
[[Thom Schuyler]], [[J. Fred Knobloch]], and [[Paul Overstreet]] were three [[Nashville, Tennessee]]-based songwriters who got together to form the group in 1986.<ref name="allmusic"/> After the release of their first single, they shortened their name simply to S-K-O.<ref name="whitburn"/> When Overstreet left the group in 1987 to pursue a solo career, he was replaced by [[Craig Bickhardt]] and they changed their name to Schuyler, Knobloch & Bickhardt or S-K-B for short.<ref name="allmusic"/> S-K-B disbanded when their record label, MTM Records, shut down in 1989.<ref name="bickhardt">{{Cite web|url=http://www.craigbickhardt.com/bio.html|title=Craig Bickhardt Bio|website=Craigbickhardt.com|access-date=August 10, 2021}}</ref> Since then, Thom Schuyler became the vice president of [[RCA Nashville]] (a position from which he retired in 1994), while Knobloch and Bickhardt continued their songwriting careers.<ref name="allmusic">{{Cite web|url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/s-k-o-mn0001009950|title=S-K-O &#124; Biography & History|website=[[AllMusic]]|access-date=August 10, 2021}}</ref>
