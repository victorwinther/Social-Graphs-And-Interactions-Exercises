'''Amanda Anne Platt & The Honeycutters'''<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.honeycutters.com/|title=The Honeycutters|access-date=3 July 2017|quote="After four albums I've decided to step out and start using my own name. It's something that a lot of people have encouraged me to do over the years, and I guess that 2017 just felt right." That name, Amanda Anne Platt & The Honeycutters, is also the title of the band's new album ...}}</ref> is an [[Americana (music)|Americana]] band based in [[Asheville, North Carolina]] in the [[United States]].

== Background ==
The Honeycutters was founded in 2007 by Amanda Anne Platt and former guitarist Peter James based around Platt's songwriting.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://archive.knoxnews.com/entertainment/music/the-honeycutters-find-a-little-more-sweetness-in-the-bands-new-songs-365d2127-546c-7c0b-e053-0100007-385061011.html|title=The Honeycutters find a little more sweetness in the band's new songs|access-date=2017-02-16|language=en}}</ref> Originally called the ''Bee's Knees'',<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://theashevillepost.com/entertainment/music/the-honeycutters-stay-sharp/|title=The Honeycutters Stay Sharp {{!}} The Asheville Post|website=theashevillepost.com|language=en-US|access-date=2017-02-16}}</ref> the band derived its current name from a [[Tow truck]] company in Asheville, NC.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://indexjournal.com/lifestyles/accent/Honeycutters--Cicada-Rhythm-to-play-at-Music-on-Maxwell-18759470|title=Honeycutters, Cicada Rhythm to play at Music on Maxwell|website=indexjournal.com|date=6 November 2016 |language=en|access-date=2017-02-16}}</ref> They released their first studio album, ''Irene'', in 2009. The band has performed throughout the United States, Canada, The Netherlands, Germany, and the UK, including the [[Vancouver Folk Music Festival]], [[Rochester International Jazz Festival]], [[Folk Alliance International]], AmericanaFest, [[MerleFest]], and [[Mountain Stage]]. In 2016, they were featured on [[NPR]]'s World Cafe: Sense of Place Asheville.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.npr.org/sections/world-cafe/2016/03/31/472541381/sense-of-place-asheville-the-honeycutters|title=Sense Of Place Asheville: The Honeycutters|newspaper=NPR.org|access-date=2017-02-16|language=en}}</ref>
