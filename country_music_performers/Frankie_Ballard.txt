'''Frank Robert Ballard IV''' (born December 16, 1982) is an American [[country music]] singer-songwriter and guitarist. He has released two albums each for [[Reprise Records]] and [[Warner Bros. Records]], and has charted eight singles on the [[Hot Country Songs]] charts.

==Personal life==
Ballard played college baseball at [[Mott Community College]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.wmubroncos.com/ViewArticle.dbml?ATCLID=205152275 |title=Bronco Baseball Alumni Note - Ballard Playing on the Big Stage |access-date=December 29, 2015 |date=May 17, 2011 |author=Keirns, Kristin|publisher=[[Western Michigan University]]}}</ref> He then continued to pursue college baseball by playing in the NCAA with [[Western Michigan University]].

On March 12, 2017, Ballard married his girlfriend, Christina Murphy, owner of Old Smokeys Boots, a Nashville-based shoe and accessory store. On November 7, 2019, the couple announced they were expecting their first child.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://people.com/parents/frankie-ballard-expecting-first-child-wife-christina-pregnant-exclusive/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191108002847/https://people.com/parents/frankie-ballard-expecting-first-child-wife-christina-pregnant-exclusive/|archive-date=November 8, 2019|title=Close}}</ref> Their daughter, Pepper Lynn, was born on February 8, 2020.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://theboot.com/frankie-ballard-baby-2020-2/ |title=Frankie Ballard and Wife Christina Welcome First Child|website=The Boot|date=February 21, 2020 }}</ref>

==Music career==
[[File:Frankie Ballard 09.jpg|thumb|left|Ballard performing at Great River Days, [[Muscatine, Iowa]], July 27, 2013]]
In the summer of 2008 he signed up for [[Kenny Chesney]]'s Next Big Star competition.<ref name=Bio>{{cite web|title=Frankie Ballard Biography|url=http://www.gactv.com/gac/ar_artists_a-z/article/0,,gac_26071_6032683,00.html|publisher=Great American Country|access-date=March 26, 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140407080411/http://www.gactv.com/gac/ar_artists_a-z/article/0,,gac_26071_6032683,00.html|archive-date=April 7, 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>  After winning the competition for Michigan in 2008,<ref name="mlive">{{cite web|url=http://www.mlive.com/entertainment/grand-rapids/index.ssf/2010/03/frankie_ballard_kicks_off_tour.html|title=Frankie Ballard kicks off tour with free show at The Intersection in Grand Rapids|date=March 31, 2010|work=Mlive.com|access-date=July 7, 2010}}</ref> Ballard also opened shows for Chesney at Michigan venues.

He signed to [[Reprise Records]] Nashville in 2010 and released his debut single "Tell Me You Get Lonely."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.warnermusicnashville.com/artist/frankie-ballard|title=Frankie Ballard biography|work=Warner Music Nashville|access-date=July 7, 2010}}</ref> It received a four-star review from Kyle Ward of Roughstock, who praised Ballard's voice and called the song "an above average debut effort."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.roughstock.com/blog/frankie-ballard-tell-me-you-get-lonely-|title=Frankie Ballard — "Tell Me You Get Lonely"|last=Ward|first=Kyle|date=June 15, 2010|work=Roughstock|access-date=July 7, 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101126120728/http://www.roughstock.com/blog/frankie-ballard-tell-me-you-get-lonely-|archive-date=November 26, 2010|url-status=dead}}</ref> The song debuted at Number 59 on the [[Hot Country Songs]] charts dated for the week ending June 12, 2010,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cmt.com/news/country-music/1640800/miranda-lamberts-house-claims-highest-address-on-country-songs-chart.jhtml|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100608083522/http://www.cmt.com/news/country-music/1640800/miranda-lamberts-house-claims-highest-address-on-country-songs-chart.jhtml|url-status=dead|archive-date=June 8, 2010|title=Miranda Lambert's "House" Claims Highest Address on Country Songs Chart|last=Morris|first=Edward|date=June 5, 2010|work=[[Country Music Television|CMT]]|access-date=July 7, 2010}}</ref> and peaked at number 33 in early 2011.

On January 7, 2011, Ballard made his debut appearance at the [[Grand Ole Opry]].

He released his second single, "[[A Buncha Girls]]", in early 2011. This song also made the top 40. Both of his singles are included on his [[Frankie Ballard (album)|self-titled debut album]], released in May 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://allmusic.com/artist/frankie-ballard-p2256728/biography|title=Frankie Ballard biography|last=Leggett|first=Steve|work=AllMusic|access-date=May 24, 2011}}</ref>

Also in 2011, he opened for [[Lady Antebellum]].

In 2013, Ballard moved to [[Warner Bros. Records]] and released his third single, "[[Helluva Life]]". This is the lead single to his second album, ''[[Sunshine & Whiskey]]''. It became his first number 1 single in early 2014.<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/music-country/frankie-ballard-revels-in-number-one-success-of-sunshine-whiskey-181844/|title=Frankie Ballard Talks Seger, Number One Song|last=Hudak|first=Joseph|date=November 21, 2014|magazine=Rolling Stone|access-date=October 10, 2018|language=en-US}}</ref> Later in the year, the album's [[Sunshine & Whiskey (song)|title track]] went to number 1. "[[Young & Crazy]]", the album's third single, also went to number 1.

The first single from Ballard's third album, "[[It All Started with a Beer]]", was released to country radio on November 16, 2015. The album, ''[[El Río (album)|El Rio]]'', was released on June 10, 2016. The album's second single, "Cigarette", released to country radio on August 8, 2016.

Ballard co-wrote "I Came to Git Down" on [[Big & Rich]]'s 2014 album ''[[Gravity (Big & Rich album)|Gravity]]''.
