'''Tyler Joe Miller''' is a Canadian [[country music|country]] singer. He was the first independent artist to debut with back-to-back No. 1 hits on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Canada Country]] chart with "[[Pillow Talkin'|Pillow Talkin{{'-}}]]" and "[[I Would Be Over Me Too]]".<ref name="JR"/> Miller released his debut full-length album ''[[Spillin' My Truth]]'' on August 25, 2023.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.surreynowleader.com/entertainment/miller-time-debut-album-for-surrey-country-musician-has-him-spillin-my-truth/|title=Miller time: Debut album for Surrey country musician has him 'Spillin' My Truth'|first=Tom|last=Zillich|date=August 25, 2023|work=[[Surrey Now-Leader]]|access-date=August 26, 2023}}</ref>

==Biography==
Miller was born in [[Surrey, British Columbia]].<ref name="CBM">{{Cite web|first=Nanci|last=Dagg|url=https://canadianbeats.ca/2020/11/07/tyler-joe-miller-released-his-debut-album-sometimes-i-dont-but-sometimes-i-do-with-two-songs-reaching-1-at-canadian-country-radio/|title=Tyler Joe Miller released his debut album Sometimes I Don't, But Sometimes I Do with two songs reaching No. 1 at Canadian country radio|work=Canadian Beats Media|date=November 7, 2020|accessdate=January 8, 2021}}</ref> He liked [[acting]] when he was younger, before he began to play the guitar.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.surreynowleader.com/entertainment/humbled-by-hit-song-pillow-talkin-surrey-musician-aims-to-build-on-humanitarian-work/|title=Humbled by hit song 'Pillow Talkin,' Surrey musician aims to build on humanitarian work|date=May 26, 2020|access-date=October 11, 2022|work=[[Surrey Now-Leader]]|first=Tom|last=Zillich}}</ref> In 2019, Miller won the BC Country Music Association's Ray McAuley Horizon Award.<ref name="TRA">{{cite news|title=Tyler Joe Miller – Pillow Talkin', Q&A and 5 Quick Questions|url=https://thereviewsarein.com/2019/11/19/tyler-joe-miller-pillow-talkin-qa-5-quick-questions/|work=The Reviews Are In|first=Joshua|last=Murray|date=November 19, 2019}}</ref> Miller cites [[Brad Paisley]], [[Alan Jackson]], [[Kenny Rogers]], [[George Strait]], and [[Garth Brooks]] as influences on his music.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.cmt.com/news/1824864/tyler-joe-miller-i-would-be-over-me-too/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200915112531/http://www.cmt.com/news/1824864/tyler-joe-miller-i-would-be-over-me-too/|url-status=dead|archive-date=September 15, 2020|title=Even the Audience Is Over Tyler Joe Miller in "I Would Be Over Me Too"|work=[[CMT (American TV channel)|CMT]]|date=September 15, 2020|accessdate=January 8, 2021}}</ref><ref name="MDM">{{cite news|title=Tyler Joe Miller - MDM Recordings Inc.|url=https://mdmrecordings.net/tyler-joe-miller/|work=[[MDM Recordings]]|accessdate=January 8, 2021}}</ref>

On [[Christmas]] Day 2019, Miller released his debut single "[[Pillow Talkin'|Pillow Talkin{{'-}}]]".<ref name="TRA"/><ref>{{cite web|title="Pillow Talkin'" – Tyler Joe Miller|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/pillow-talkin-mw0003344926|work=[[AllMusic]]|accessdate=January 8, 2021}}</ref> The song reached No. 1 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Canada Country]] chart, making Miller the first independently signed artist to top the chart with his debut single.<ref name="JR">{{cite web|title=How A Simple Thank-You Note Helped Make Canadian Country Music History|url=https://www.country933.com/2020/05/15/how-a-simple-thank-you-note-helped-make-canadian-country-music-history/|work=Country 93.3|publisher=[[Rogers Media]]|date=May 15, 2020}}</ref> It also became Miller's first Gold-certified single.<ref name="PT"/> In June 2020, Miller released his second single "[[I Would Be Over Me Too]]".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://mdmrecordings.net/tyler-joe-miller-releases-his-brand-new-single-i-would-be-over-me-too/|title=TYLER JOE MILLER RELEASES HIS BRAND NEW SINGLE, "I WOULD BE OVER ME TOO"|work=[[MDM Recordings]]|date=June 5, 2020 |accessdate=January 8, 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title="I Would Be Over Me Too" – Tyler Joe Miller|url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/i-would-be-over-me-too-mw0003397719|work=[[AllMusic]]|accessdate=January 8, 2021}}</ref> Miller was then selected as a semi-finalist in the [[SiriusXM Canada|SiriusXM]] Top of the Country competition.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.newswire.ca/news-releases/meet-the-eight-semi-finalists-competing-in-siriusxm-s-third-annual-top-of-the-country-contest-822865377.html|title=Meet the eight semi-finalists |work=NewsWire|publisher=[[Cision]]|date=July 17, 2020}}</ref> In October, "I Would Be Over Me Too" would also reach No. 1 on the Canada Country chart, handing Miller his second consecutive chart-topper, and first as a songwriter.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.surreynowleader.com/entertainment/record-setting-second-hit-song-for-surreys-tyler-joe-miller-whos-stoked-for-whats-next/|first=Tom|last=Zillich|title=Record-setting second hit song for Surrey's Tyler Joe Miller, who's 'stoked for what's next'|work=[[Surrey Now-Leader]]|date=October 27, 2020}}</ref> In November 2020, Miller released his debut extended play ''[[Sometimes I Don't, But Sometimes I Do]]'', which featured his first two singles as well as his the singles, "[[Fighting (Tyler Joe Miller song)|Fighting]]"<ref name="FPM"/><ref name="CBM"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.jrcountry.ca/2021/01/04/tyler-joe-miller-fighting/|work=937 JR Country|title=Tyler Joe Miller – Fighting|publisher=[[Jim Pattison Broadcast Group]]|date=January 4, 2021}}</ref> and "[[Sometimes I Do]]".<ref name="SID">{{Cite web|first=Nanci|last=Dagg|url=https://canadianbeats.ca/2021/06/15/tyler-joe-miller-releases-his-latest-single-sometimes-i-do/|title=Tyler Joe Miller releases his latest single "Sometimes I Do"|work=Canadian Beats Media|date=June 15, 2021|accessdate=June 21, 2021}}</ref> In November 2021, Miller was named the winner of the [[Sirius XM Canada|SiriusXM]] "Top of the Country" competition.<ref>{{cite web|title='Holy smokes!': B.C. musician reacts after winning $25K 'Top of the Country Contest'|url=https://www.abbynews.com/entertainment/holy-smokes-b-c-musician-reacts-after-winning-25k-top-of-the-country-contest/|first=Tom|last=Zillich|date=November 27, 2021|work=[[Abbotsford News]]|publisher=[[Black Press]]}}</ref> 

He followed that up with the release of "[[Never Met a Beer]]" in August 2022, a collaboration with fellow Canadian country artist [[Matt Lang]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://mdmrecordings.net/tyler-joe-miller-and-matt-lang-team-up-on-intoxicating-new-summer-single-never-met-a-beer/|title=TYLER JOE MILLER AND MATT LANG TEAM UP ON INTOXICATING NEW SUMMER SINGLE "NEVER MET A BEER"|work=[[MDM Recordings]]|date=August 8, 2022 |access-date=November 30, 2022}}</ref> In February 2023, he released the single "[[Back to Drinkin' Whiskey]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://canadianbeats.ca/2023/02/03/tyler-joe-miller-releases-his-single-back-to-drinkin-whiskey/|title=Tyler Joe Miller releases his single, "Back To Drinkin' Whiskey"|first=Nanci|last=Dagg|date=February 3, 2023|work=Canadian Beats Media|access-date=February 7, 2023}}</ref> In April 2023, Miller released the track "I Know Jack", which was later included on his acoustic extended play ''The Band's Packed Up'' that was released on May 19, 2023.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://mdmrecordings.net/4097-2/|title=TYLER JOE MILLER RELEASES LEAD SINGLE OFF UPCOMING ACOUSTIC EP!|date=April 7, 2023|work=[[MDM Recordings]]|access-date=April 19, 2023}}</ref><ref name="TBPU">{{cite web|url=https://canadianbeats.ca/2023/04/12/tyler-joe-miller-unveils-stripped-back-version-of-i-know-jack/|title=Tyler Joe Miller unveils stripped back version of "I Know Jack"|date=April 12, 2023|work=Canadian Beats Media|first=Nanci|last=Dagg|access-date=May 21, 2023}}</ref> The tracks from the extended play were included on Miller's album ''[[Spillin' My Truth]]'', released on August 25, 2023.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://519magazine.com/boots-hearts-and-tyler-joe-miller-a-festival-highlight/|title=Boots, Hearts and Tyler Joe Miller: A Festival Highlight|first=Dan|last=Savoie|date=August 17, 2023|work=519 Magazine|access-date=October 4, 2023}}</ref> The album also includes the single "[[Shoulda Known Better (song)|Shoulda Known Better]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cjvr.com/2023/07/12/45650/|title=New Song – Tyler Joe Miller – Shoulda Known Better|first=Gabby|last=Shukin|work=CJVR Today's Best Country|location=[[Melfort, Saskatchewan]]|date=July 12, 2023|access-date=November 7, 2023}}</ref> 

In January 2024, Miller signed a management deal with RLive, a division of Republic Live, as their flagship signing.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://musicrow.com/2024/01/republic-live-launches-new-management-division-rlive/|title=Republic Live Launches New Management Division, RLive|work=Music Row|first=Lorie|last=Hollabaugh|date=January 23, 2024|access-date=January 24, 2024}}</ref> In February 2024, he signed a distribution deal with [[The Orchard (company)|The Orchard]], and released the single "Broken Man" in March 2024.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://musicrow.com/2024/02/industry-ink-mitchell-tenpenny-raelynn-tyler-joe-miller-steve-fishell/|title=Industry Ink: Mitchell Tenpenny, RaeLynn, Tyler Joe Miller, Steve Fishell|first=Lorie|last=Hollabaugh|date=February 21, 2024|access-date=February 23, 2024|work=Music Row}}</ref> In the spring of 2024, Miller embarked on "The Country Mixtape Tour" across Canada as a co-headliner alongside [[Shawn Austin]] and [[Andrew Hyatt]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.soundcheckentertainment.ca/tyler-joe-miller-shawn-austin-and-andrew-hyatt-team-up-for-the-2024-country-mixtape-tour/|title=TYLER JOE MILLER, SHAWN AUSTIN AND ANDREW HYATT TEAM UP FOR THE 2024 COUNTRY MIXTAPE TOUR|date=December 12, 2023|work=Sound Check Entertainment|first=Hendrik|last=Pape|access-date=December 16, 2023}}</ref> In August 2024, he released the single "What Good Is a Memory".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://completecountry.ca/what-good-is-a-memory-new-single-from-tyler-joe-miller-out-today/|title=“What Good Is A Memory” New Single From TYLER JOE MILLER Out Today|work=Complete Country|date=August 9, 2024|access-date=September 8, 2024}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Miller is a [[carpenter]] and [[painter]] by trade. He owns a non-profit organization called The Climb Outreach Society, which provides housing, drinking water sources, as well as emergency food funds and healthcare for newborn babies in [[Central America|Central]] and [[South America]].<ref name="FPM">{{Cite web|url=http://frontporchmusic.ca/music/tyler-joe-miller-ep/|title=Tyler Joe Miller Talks About His Debut EP "Sometimes I Don't, But Sometimes I Do"|work=Front Porch Music|date=November 26, 2020|accessdate=January 8, 2021}}</ref>
