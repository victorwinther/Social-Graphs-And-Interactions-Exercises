'''Annie Bosko''' is an American [[country music]] singer-songwriter from [[Los Angeles, California]] signed to Stone Country Records.<ref name="Stone Country">{{cite web |url=https://www.anniebosko.com/news/2 |title=Annie Bosko News |date=August 10, 2023 |access-date=July 11, 2024}}</ref>

==Background==
Growing up in [[Thousand Oaks, California]], Bosko performed in various venues from a young age, and at the age of 14, sang on the soundtrack for [[The Little Mermaid II: Return to the Sea]] (2000), before relocating to [[Nashville, Tennessee]] in the mid-2000s.<ref name="Opry"/><ref name="Background">{{cite web |url=https://www.anniebosko.com/about |title=About Annie |access-date=July 11, 2024}}</ref> "Crooked Halo", her first solo effort, was released in November 2014<ref>{{cite web |url=https://open.spotify.com/album/1qSDZw1jlh1tpELsZi5dZo?si=Cqdp7hR4QuKn5TjCXIIqtQ |title="Crooked Halo" |website=[[Spotify]] |date=November 14, 2014 |access-date=July 11, 2024}}</ref> and received play on satellite radio and [[CMT (American TV channel)|CMT]].<ref name="Background"/> She found work recording jingles, cutting demos, and writing songs for other artists, touring as an opening act for many prominent country music artists, and eventually made her [[Grand Ole Opry]] debut on September 30, 2023.<ref name="Opry">{{cite web|url=https://www.opry.com/artists/annie-bosko|title=Annie Bosko Opry|website=Grand Ole Opry|access-date=July 11, 2024}}</ref> In August 2023, it was announced that Bosko had signed a recording contract with Stone Country Records,<ref name="Stone Country"/> and "Neon Baby" was released as her debut single to country radio on November 6, 2023.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://assets.countryaircheck.com/public/acd75299-89ea-4abe-beb32cbdba9ff7ed/Issue882-October302023.pdf|title=Country Aircheck Weekly, October 30, 2023, Issue 882|website=Country Aircheck|date=October 30, 2023|access-date=July 11, 2024}}</ref> It debuted on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Country Airplay]] chart at number 60 in July 2024.<ref name="BCU July 5">{{cite magazine|url=https://s3.amazonaws.com/bulletins.billboard.com/country/country_update_2024_0705.pdf|title=Billboard Country Update|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|date=July 5, 2024|access-date=July 11, 2024}}</ref> Her self-titled EP was produced by [[Derek George]] and [[Mickey Jack Cones]] and released on January 5, 2024.<ref name="EP"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://countrynow.com/annie-bosko-unapologetically-leans-into-her-country-roots-on-self-titled-debut-ep/|title=Annie Bosko Unapologetically Leans Into Her Country Roots On Self-Titled Debut EP|author=Madeleine O'Connell|date=January 8, 2024|website=Country Now|access-date=July 11, 2024}}</ref>
