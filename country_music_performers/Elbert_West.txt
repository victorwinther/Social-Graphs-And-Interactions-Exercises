'''Elbert Lee West''' (July 22, 1967 – May 18, 2015) was an American [[country music]] artist. Initially a session songwriter in [[Nashville, Tennessee]], West saw his first chart success in the 1990s as a co-writer on singles for country singer [[Tracy Lawrence]], including the Number Ones "[[Sticks and Stones (Tracy Lawrence song)|Sticks and Stones]]" and "[[Can't Break It to My Heart]]".<ref name="allmusic">{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p336780|pure_url=yes}}|title=Elbert West biography|accessdate=May 5, 2008|last=Hage|first=Erik|work=[[AllMusic]]}}</ref><ref name="about">{{cite web|url=http://countrymusic.about.com/library/blelbertwestrev.htm|title=Elbert West – Livin' the Life review|accessdate=May 5, 2008|last=Downs|first=Jolene|work=[[About.com]]|archive-date=May 23, 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080523193558/http://countrymusic.about.com/library/blelbertwestrev.htm|url-status=dead}}</ref> West co-wrote album tracks for other artists, including tracks for [[Tim McGraw]] and [[John Michael Montgomery]].<ref name="interview">{{cite web|url=http://www.countryinterviewsonline.net/Westinterview.html |title=Elbert West interview |accessdate=May 5, 2008 |last=Claffey |first=Laura |work=Country Interviews Online |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100925234523/http://countryinterviewsonline.net/Westinterview.html |archivedate=September 25, 2010 }}</ref>

==Biography==
Elbert West was born on July 22, 1967.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.familysearch.org/auth/familysearch/login?icid=hr-signin&returnUrl=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.familysearch.org%2Fsearch%2Fsearch%2Frecord%2Fresults%3Fcount%3D50%26query%3D%2Bgivenname%3A%2522Elbert%2BLee%2522~%2B%2Bsurname%3AWest~%2B%2Bbirth_year%3A1967-1969~|title=FamilySearch|website=www.familysearch.org}}</ref> Early in his musical career, West wrote songs for [[Tracy Lawrence]], [[Tim McGraw]], and [[John Michael Montgomery]].

By 2001, he had signed to [[Broken Bow Records]], then a newly formed independent label, and his debut album, ''Livin' the Life'', was released that year.<ref name="allmusic"/> West co-wrote ten of the album's thirteen tracks,<ref name="interview"/> while others – including "(This One's Gonna) Leave a Mark", previously recorded by John Michael Montgomery – were co-written by Randy Archer and Johnny Park, formerly of the duo [[Archer/Park]]. "Diddley", which peaked at No.&nbsp;56 on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Hot Country Songs|Hot Country Singles & Tracks]] charts, was the album's lead single and West's only single to chart. "Diddley" was also made into a music video, which aired on [[Country Music Television|CMT]].<ref name="about"/> Erik Hage of ''[[AllMusic]]'' gave the album four stars out of five, saying, "West has a big, husky baritone, a keen songwriting ability, a defiant attitude, and is a major country talent."<ref>{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=album|id=r534972|pure_url=yes}}|title=''Livin' the Life'' review|last=Hage|first=Erik|work=AllMusic|accessdate=February 17, 2010}}</ref> After a falling out over a record, West left Broken Bow Records.

In 2013, West started having seizures. He died in [[Carthage, Tennessee|Portland, Tennessee]], on May 18, 2015, at the age of 47 from a [[stomach]] [[aneurysm]]. West is survived by his wife Tammy and their three children.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Skates|first1=Sarah|title=Lifenotes: Songwriter Elbert West|url=http://www.musicrow.com/2015/05/lifenotes-songwriter-elbert-west|accessdate=May 19, 2015|work=[[MusicRow]]|date=May 19, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/40995569/elbert_lee_west/ |title=Elbert Lee West |work=[[The Tennessean]] |page=10A |date=May 20, 2015 |access-date=December 23, 2019 |via=Newspapers.com}}</ref>
