'''Ward Thomas''' are an English modern country-pop duo, composed of twin sisters Catherine and Lizzy Ward Thomas from [[Hampshire]].<ref name="Nott">{{cite news |last=Wilson |first=Simon |url=http://www.nottinghampost.com/Ward-Thomas-play-Rescue-Rooms/story-25972833-detail/story.html |title=Country music stars Ward Thomas head for the Rescue Rooms |newspaper=[[Nottingham Post]] |date=4 February 2015 |accessdate=19 February 2016 |archive-date=10 July 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150710010347/http://www.nottinghampost.com/Ward-Thomas-play-Rescue-Rooms/story-25972833-detail/story.html |url-status=live }}</ref> The band has been called "Britain's first country stars".<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.thenorthernecho.co.uk/entertainment/11611066.Ward_Thomas_export_style_from_our_country_to_USA/?ref=rss |title=Ward Thomas export style from our country to USA |newspaper=[[The Northern Echo]] |date=19 November 2014 |accessdate=19 February 2016 |archive-date=2 March 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160302044054/http://www.thenorthernecho.co.uk/entertainment/11611066.Ward_Thomas_export_style_from_our_country_to_USA/?ref=rss |url-status=live }}</ref, their music has been described as written "from a British perspective". While people have been able to appreciate the American sound, they can connect with the themes of British lyrics.</ref> ''[[Cartwheels (Ward Thomas album)|Cartwheels]]'', their second studio album, became the first album by a UK country act to reach number one on the [[UK Albums Chart]]. surpassing the previous record by [[the Shires (duo)|the Shires]]. It has since gone gold in the UK.

==Early life==
Catherine and Lizzy are twins, born two minutes apart,<ref name="theguardian.com">{{Cite web|url = https://www.theguardian.com/music/2016/sep/15/catherine-ward-thomas-mum-told-us-dont-swear-or-talk-politics-or-religion|title = Catherine Ward Thomas: 'Mum told us don't swear or talk politics or religion'|website = [[TheGuardian.com]]|date = 15 September 2016|access-date = 3 July 2020|archive-date = 3 July 2020|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20200703132926/https://www.theguardian.com/music/2016/sep/15/catherine-ward-thomas-mum-told-us-dont-swear-or-talk-politics-or-religion|url-status = live}}</ref> who grew up on a farm in rural Hampshire, England, and were educated at [[Alton School]], a [[Roman Catholic]] private day school, where they acquired the nicknames "Scruff 1" and "Scruff 2" because they were "so bad at being neat and tidy".<ref name="theguardian.com"/>

Their father is Anthony Ward-Thomas, who in 1985 founded his own eponymous removals business, now with an annual turnover of £22 million (2015).<ref>{{cite web |last1=Ward-Thomas |first1=Anthony |title=Who we are &#124; Anthony Ward Thomas &#124; UK and London Removals |url=https://ward-thomas.co.uk/who-we-are |access-date=21 February 2020 |archive-date=21 February 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200221083831/https://ward-thomas.co.uk/who-we-are |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ward-thomas.co.uk/who-we-are|title=Who we are &#124; Anthony Ward Thomas &#124; UK and London Removals|access-date=21 February 2020|archive-date=21 February 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200221083831/https://ward-thomas.co.uk/who-we-are|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>Natalie Graham, Financial Times, February 20, 2015, ''My First Million — Anthony Ward Thomas, A moving story of success from small beginnings''[https://www.ft.com/content/f11d7122-a545-11e4-ad35-00144feab7de] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200703113217/https://www.ft.com/content/f11d7122-a545-11e4-ad35-00144feab7de |date=3 July 2020 }}</ref> Their mother Amanda Ward Thomas is an artist.

==Career==
===2014–2015: ''From Where We Stand''===
{{Main|From Where We Stand}}
They co-wrote the songs for their debut album ''[[From Where We Stand]]'', which was released on 20 July 2014. It peaked at number 41 on the [[UK Albums Chart]], number 1 on the UK Country Chart, number 7 on the [[UK Independent Singles and Albums Charts|UK Indie Chart]] and number 36 in [[Scottish Singles and Albums Charts|Scotland]]. The album won UK Album of the Year at the British Country Music Awards and sold over 25,000 copies.<ref name="Nott"/> They have toured the UK multiple times, with shows at venues including [[Islington Assembly Hall]] and [[The New Adelphi Club]] in [[Kingston upon Hull|Hull]], [[East Riding of Yorkshire|East Yorkshire]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/events/1533175116949507/ |title=Under The Influence presents Ward Thomas |publisher=Facebook |accessdate=19 February 2016}}</ref>{{Primary source inline|date=January 2018}} In 2014, they performed on the [[BBC Introducing]] stage at the BBC Radio 2 Live in Hyde Park festival,<ref>{{cite web|title=Ward Thomas – Push For the Stride at Radio 2 Live in Hyde Park 2014|website=[[YouTube]]|date=15 September 2014|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=buEtZSwoevY|accessdate=12 June 2016|archive-date=16 October 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141016120031/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=buEtZSwoevY&gl=US&hl=en|url-status=live}}</ref> returning to the festival in 2015 to perform on the main stage alongside The Shires; a performance which was broadcast live on [[BBC Radio 2]].<ref>{{cite web|title=The Shires & Ward Thomas – Everywhere (Radio 2 Live in Hyde Park 2015)|website=[[YouTube]]|date=13 September 2015|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7DzoDdxjHso|accessdate=12 June 2016|archive-date=16 September 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150916171733/https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7DzoDdxjHso&gl=US&hl=en|url-status=live}}</ref>

===2016–2017: ''Cartwheels'' and ''A Shorter Story''===
{{Main|Cartwheels (Ward Thomas album)}}
In June 2016, Ward Thomas announced a joint venture with Sony Music and that their second album, ''[[Cartwheels (Ward Thomas album)|Cartwheels]]'', would be released in September 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://twitter.com/wardthomasmusic/status/740643172330704896|title=Ward Thomas on Twitter|website=Twitter|access-date=12 June 2016}}</ref>{{Primary source inline|date=January 2018}} Lead single "Carry You Home", described by ''The Times'' as "chirpy country pop" was released in June, followed by the singles "Guilty Flowers" and "Cartwheels", all of which were added to the Radio 2 playlist. Cartwheels reached number 1 in the UK Albums Chart, making Ward Thomas the first UK country act to top the charts and officially making them the most successful UK country act. It has since gone gold in the UK.<ref>{{Cite news|title = Country duo Ward Thomas top UK album chart|work = BBC News|date = 9 September 2016|url = https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-37319660/|access-date = 21 June 2018|archive-date = 4 November 2018|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20181104021355/https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-37319660|url-status = live}}</ref> In September 2016, Ward Thomas were confirmed to play at the 31st edition of [[Eurosonic Noorderslag]] in [[Groningen]]<ref>{{Cite web|title = First European Artists Confirmed for Eurosonic Noorderslag – Eurosonic Noorderslag|url = https://www.eurosonic-noorderslag.nl/en/news/festival/article/eerste-artiesten-voor-eurosonic-noorderslag-2017-bevestigd/|website = Eurosonic Noorderslag|accessdate = 11 October 2016|archive-date = 2 August 2017|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20170802210425/https://www.eurosonic-noorderslag.nl/en/news/festival/article/eerste-artiesten-voor-eurosonic-noorderslag-2017-bevestigd/|url-status = live}}</ref> and in March 2017, Ward Thomas were announced as the official opening act for the [[C2C: Country to Country|Country to Country]] 2017 festival.

On 28 October 2016, Ward Thomas released the single version of "Cartwheels", featuring a cover of [[Lenny Kravitz]]'s song "[[Fly Away (Lenny Kravitz song)|Fly Away]]" which was also featured on the TV show "[[Countrywise]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://celebmix.com/ward-thomas-release-lenny-kravitzs-fly-away-cover|title=Ward Thomas Release Lenny Kravitz's 'Fly Away' Cover|date=28 October 2016|publisher=|access-date=21 May 2017|archive-date=8 August 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170808074728/http://celebmix.com/ward-thomas-release-lenny-kravitzs-fly-away-cover/|url-status=live}}</ref> On 31 March 2017, Ward Thomas released their first EP, ''[[A Shorter Story]]'', which features the duo covering five of their favourite songs.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/a-shorter-story-ep/id1217842584|title=A Shorter Story - EP by Ward Thomas on Apple Music|website=[[iTunes]]|date=31 March 2017|publisher=|access-date=31 March 2017|archive-date=1 April 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170401060200/https://itunes.apple.com/gb/album/a-shorter-story-ep/id1217842584|url-status=live}}</ref> In 2017, they supported American country superstar [[Miranda Lambert]] on the UK leg of her [[Highway Vagabond Tour]].

===2017–present: ''Restless Minds''===
In July 2018, Ward Thomas announced that they will release their third album, ''[[Restless Minds]]''. The announcement coincided with the release of lead single "Lie Like Me", a biting indictment of lives faked online. During the 2019 [[C2C: Country to Country]] festival, the duo were surprised by [[Cam (singer)|Cam]] at the CMA Songwriter's Event with the [[Country Music Association Award for International Achievement|Jeff Walker Global Achievement Award]] from the [[Country Music Association]], making them only the second UK country act to receive the award.<ref>[https://bellesandgals.com/2019/03/07/ward-thomas-recieve-prestigious-cma-jeff-walker-global-artist-award/ Ward Thomas Receive Prestigious CMA Jeff Walker Global Artist Award] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191205200950/https://www.bellesandgals.com/2019/03/07/ward-thomas-recieve-prestigious-cma-jeff-walker-global-artist-award/ |date=5 December 2019 }}, Music News, March 7, 2019, by Laura Klonowski</ref>
