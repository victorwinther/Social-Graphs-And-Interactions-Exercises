'''Megan Redmond''' (born March 1996 in [[Portland, Oregon|Portland]], [[Oregon]]) is an American singer-songwriter living in [[Nashville, Tennessee]] and [[Denver, Colorado]], where she started her band in 2011.<ref>{{citation |url=http://www.mtv.com/artists/megan-redmond/biography/ |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130208121843/http://www.mtv.com/artists/megan-redmond/biography/ |url-status=dead |archive-date=February 8, 2013 |title=Megan Redmond MTV Biography |publisher=www.MTV.com}}</ref>

==Background==
Redmond’s first single, titled "Telephone," was released in 2011, followed by her first EP, ''Dreamland'', in 2012.<ref>{{citation |url=http://musicbrainz.org/artist/ebc5000b-eece-46cf-bbd1-369e5030af34 |title=Megan Redmond Discography |publisher=www.MusicBrainz.org}}</ref> The single "Falling," off of ''Dreamland'', was listed by [[Clear Channel Communications|Clear Channel]]’s Hot New Music program, peaking at number 87 on the BillBoard Hot New Country Top 100 charts.<ref>{{citation |url=http://thecoloradosound.com/2012/04/22/the-colorado-sound-vol-4-ep15-2012/ |title= The Colorado Sound - VOL 4 EP 15 2012 |date=April 22, 2012 |publisher=The Colorado Sound}}</ref> In 2009 Redmond received the Colorado Country Music Association’s Female Vocalist and Female Entertainer of the year award.<ref>{{Citation| year =2012| title =Megan Redmond| publisher =Colorado Connections Academy| url =http://www.connectionsacademy.com/colorado-school/our-school/students/megan-redmond.aspx| url-status =dead| archive-url =https://web.archive.org/web/20120929005050/http://www.connectionsacademy.com/colorado-school/our-school/students/megan-redmond.aspx| archive-date =2012-09-29}}</ref><ref>{{citation |url=http://www.steveclayton.com/blog/custom-guitar-picks/pick-of-the-week-megan-redmond/ |title=Pick of the week - Megan Redmond |author=Steve Clayton |date=May 4, 2011 |publisher=www.steveclayton.com}}</ref> She has performed at major music festivals, including the Bohemian Nights in [[Fort Collins]], Colorado.<ref>{{citation |url=http://www.bohemiannights.org/saturday-highlights-late/ |title=Bohemian Nights Highlights |publisher=Bohemian Nights at New West Fest}}</ref> Redmond has worked with the Grammy award-winning artist [[Linda Davis]] and is currently receiving training from Nashville vocal coach Kim Woods Sandusky.<ref>{{Citation | last =Sullivan | first =Charlie | year =2012 | title =UMX 2012 Review and Artists to Watch | publisher =Colorado Music Buzz | url =http://www.colomusicbuzz.com/cmb-events/umx-2012-review-and-artists-to-watch/ | url-status =dead | archive-url =https://web.archive.org/web/20120911074739/http://www.colomusicbuzz.com/cmb-events/umx-2012-review-and-artists-to-watch/ | archive-date =2012-09-11 }}</ref> Redmond was one of the top 25 finalists in the ''American Songwriter Coffeehouse Tour'' presented by Fishman Transducers.<ref>{{citation |url=http://www.americansongwriter.com/2012/09/the-american-songwriter-coffeehouse-tour-25-finalists/ |title=The American Songwriter Coffeehouse Tour: 25 finalists |author=John Ritchie |date=September 10, 2012 |publisher=American Songwriter}}</ref> In 2010, she was a local winner of the [[Texaco]] Country Music Showdown.<ref>{{citation |url=http://www.countryshowdown.com/contestant.php?id=25836 |title=Megan Redmond on the Web |year=2012 |publisher=Texaco Country Showdown |access-date=2012-10-05 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140407230044/https://www.countryshowdown.com/contestant.php?id=25836 |archive-date=2014-04-07 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Megan also has a number of cuts on other artist albums reflected her song writing and melodic talent.

In addition to Redmond’s role as a vocalist, she is also a multi-instrumentalist  playing the [[guitar]], [[piano]], [[mandolin]], and [[banjo]].<ref>{{citation |url=http://www.bohemiannights.org/megan-redmond/?doing_wp_cron=1342538745.4237499237060546875000 |title=Bohemian Nights |date=August 11, 2012 |publisher=Bohemian Nights}}</ref>
